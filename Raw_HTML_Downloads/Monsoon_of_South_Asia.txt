It affects the\n       <a href="/wiki/Indian_subcontinent" title="Indian subcontinent">\n        Indian subcontinent\n       </a>\n       , where it is one of the oldest and most anticipated\n       <a href="/wiki/Weather" title="Weather">\n        weather\n       </a>\n       phenomena and an economically important pattern every year from June through September, but it is only partly understood and notoriously difficult to predict. Several theories have been proposed to explain the origin, process, strength, variability, distribution, and general vagaries of the monsoon, but understanding and predictability are still evolving.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The unique\n       <a href="/wiki/Geography_of_India" title="Geography of India">\n        geographical features of the Indian subcontinent\n       </a>\n       , along with associated\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atmospheric" title="Atmospheric">\n        atmospheric\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Ocean" title="Ocean">\n        oceanic\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geophysical" title="Geophysical">\n        geophysical\n       </a>\n       factors, influence the behavior of the monsoon. Because of its effect on\n       <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">\n        agriculture\n       </a>\n       , on\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flora_and_fauna" title="Flora and fauna">\n        flora and fauna\n       </a>\n       , and on the climates of nations such as\n       <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">\n        India\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Nepal" title="Nepal">\n        Nepal\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">\n        Bangladesh\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Bhutan" title="Bhutan">\n        Bhutan\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">\n        Pakistan\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">\n        Sri Lanka\n       </a>\n       \xe2\x80\x94 among other\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Economic" title="Economic">\n        economic\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Social" title="Social">\n        social\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Natural_environment" title="Natural environment">\n        environmental\n       </a>\n       effects \xe2\x80\x94 the monsoon is one of the most anticipated, tracked,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Frater2005_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Frater2005-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and studied weather phenomena in the region.  (derived from the Arabic "mausam," meaning "season"), although generally defined as a system of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Winds" title="Winds">\n        winds\n       </a>\n       characterized by a seasonal reversal of direction,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMS_6-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-AMS-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       lacks a direction of winds along the shores of the\n        <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">\n         Indian Ocean\n        </a>\n        , especially in the Arabian Sea, which blow from the southwest for half of the year and from the northeast for the other half.\n        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IMDdef_8-0">\n         <a href="#cite_note-IMDdef-8">\n          [8]\n         </a>\n        </sup>\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Colin Stokes Ramage, in\n        <i>\n         Monsoon Meteorology\n        </i>\n        , defines the monsoon as a seasonal reversing wind accompanied by corresponding changes in precipitation.\n        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ramage1971_9-0">\n         <a href="#cite_note-Ramage1971-9">\n          [9]\n         </a>\n        </sup>\n       </li>\n      </ul>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Background">\n        Background\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Selin1997_10-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Selin1997-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       traveling between\n       <a href="/wiki/Africa" </p>\n      <ul>\n       <li>\n        Arabian Sea branch\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Bengal" title="Bay of Bengal">\n         Bay of Bengal\n        </a>\n    Alternatively, it can be categorized into two\n       <i>\n        segments\n       </i>\n       based on the direction of rain-bearing winds:\n      </p>\n      <ul>\n       <li>\n     </ul>\n      <p>\n       The complexity of the monsoon of South Asia is not completely understood, making it difficult to accurately predict the quantity, timing, and geographic distribution of the accompanying precipitation. These are the most monitored components of the monsoon, and they determine the water availability in India for any given year.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IMD1_12-0">\n        <a  </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Monsoon_of_South_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Mechanism of the monsoon">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main article:\n       <a href="/wiki/Monsoon#South_Asian_Monsoon" title="Monsoon">\n        Monsoon \xc2\xa7\xc2\xa0South Asian Monsoon\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:352px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Aug2011monsoon.JPG">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1721" data-file-width="1757" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/95/Aug2011monsoon.JPG/525px-Aug2011monsoon.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/95/Aug2011monsoon.JPG/700px-Aug2011monsoon.JPG 2x" width="350"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Aug2011monsoon.JPG" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Monsoon over India\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Monsoons typically occur in tropical areas.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_13-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-:0-13">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       One area that monsoons impact greatly is India.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_13-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-:0-13">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In India monsoons create an entire season in which the winds reverse completely.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_13-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-:0-13">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Various atmospheric conditions influence the monsoon winds. The first condition is the differential heating and cooling of land and water. This creates low pressure on the landmass, while high pressure is created over the seas around during daytime, but is reversed during the nighttime.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The second condition is the shift in the position of\n       <a href="/wiki/Intertropical_Convergence_Zone" title="Intertropical Convergence Zone">\n        Inter-Tropical Convergence Zone\n       </a>\n       (ITCZ). In summer, the equatorial trough normally positioned about 5\xc2\xb0N of the equator moves over the Ganga plain creating a monsoon trough during the monsoon season.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The third condition is the presence of the high-pressure area that develops east of Madagascar. It is approximately at 20\xc2\xb0S over the Indian Ocean. The intensity and position of this high-pressure area affects the Indian Monsoon.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The fourth condition develops during the summer. The Tibetan Plateau gets intensely heated resulting in strong vertical air currents and high pressure over the plateau about 9\xc2\xa0km above sea level. The fifth condition develops during the summer due to the movement of the westerly jet streams to the north of the Himalayas and the presence of the tropical easterly jet stream over the Indian Peninsula.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Changes in pressure over the southern oceans also affect the monsoons. In certain years, there is a reversal in the pressure conditions. This periodic change in pressure conditions is known as the Southern Oscillation, or SO.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The Southern Oscillation is connected to El Nino, which is a warm ocean current that flows past the Peruvian Coast. It flows every two to five years in place of the cold Peruvian current. The phenomenon is, referred to as ENSO (El Nino Southern Oscillations). In India, the monsoon lasts for 100 to 120 days from early June and to mid-September. The monsoon winds encounter various atmospheric conditions on their way and hence are pulsating in nature, and not steady.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The monsoon arrives with a sudden downpour of rainfall that continues for several days. This is known as the \xe2\x80\x98burst\xe2\x80\x99 of the monsoon.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The rainfall is a result of the convergence of wind flow from the\n       <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Bengal" title="Bay of Bengal">\n        Bay of Bengal\n       </a>\n       and reverse winds from the\n       <a href="/wiki/South_China_Sea" title="South China Sea">\n        South China Sea\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_14-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-:1-14">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <a class="external text" href="http://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/onset" rel="nofollow">\n        onset\n       </a>\n       of the monsoon occurs over the\n       <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Bengal" title="Bay of Bengal">\n        Bay of Bengal\n       </a>\n       in May,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_14-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-:1-14">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       arriving at the\n       <a href="/wiki/Indian_subcontinent" title="Indian subcontinent">\n        Indian Peninsula\n       </a>\n       by June,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_13-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-:0-13">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and then the winds move towards the\n       <a href="/wiki/South_China_Sea" title="South China Sea">\n        South China Sea\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_14-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-:1-14">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       By early September, the monsoon starts to withdraw or retreat and is a more gradual process. By mid-October, it withdraws completely from the northern half of the peninsula. The withdrawal takes place progressively from north to south from the first week of December to the first week of January. This is the start of the winter season.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The retreating monsoon winds move over the Arabian Sea and the Bay of Bengal, and collect moisture on the way. These monsoon winds reach the southern states of India by October, and are responsible for a second round of rainfall. These are called the winter monsoons. The winter monsoon is experienced in the states of Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Kerala and Andhra Pradesh in the first week of January.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Effect_of_geographical_relief_features">\n        Effect of geographical relief features\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Monsoon_of_South_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Effect of geographical relief features">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Although the southwest and northeast monsoon winds are seasonally reversible, they do not cause precipitation on their own.\n      </p>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main article:\n       <a href="/wiki/Rain#Formation" title="Rain">\n        Rain \xc2\xa7\xc2\xa0Formation\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Two factors are essential for\n       <a href="/wiki/Rain#Formation" title="Rain">\n        rain formation\n       </a>\n       :\n      </p>\n      <ol>\n       <li>\n        <a href="/wiki/Rain#Water-saturated_air" title="Rain">\n         Moisture-laden winds\n        </a>\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        <a href="/wiki/Rain#Coalescence" title="Rain">\n         Droplet formation\n        </a>\n       </li>\n      </ol>\n      <p>\n       Additionally, one of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Rain#Causes" title="Rain">\n        causes of rain\n       </a>\n       must happen. In the case of the monsoon, the cause is primarily\n       <a href="/wiki/Rain#Orographic_effects" title="Rain">\n        orographic\n       </a>\n       , due to the presence of highlands in the path of the winds. Orographic barriers force wind to rise. Precipitation then occurs on the windward side of the highlands because of\n       <a href="/wiki/Adiabatic_process#Adiabatic_heating_and_cooling" title="Adiabatic process">\n        adiabatic cooling\n       </a>\n       and condensation of the moist rising air.\n      </p>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main article:\n       <a href="/wiki/Orographic_lift" title="Orographic lift">\n        Orographic lift\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The unique\n       <a href="/wiki/Geography_of_India" title="Geography of India">\n        geographic relief\n       </a>\n       features of the Indian subcontinent come into play in allowing all of the above factors to occur simultaneously. The relevant features in explaining the monsoon mechanism are as follows:\n      </p>\n      <ol>\n       <li>\n        The presence of abundant water bodies around the subcontinent: the\n        <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Sea" title="Arabian Sea">\n         Arabian Sea\n        </a>\n        ,\n        <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Bengal" title="Bay of Bengal">\n         Bay of Bengal\n        </a>\n        , and\n        <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">\n         Indian Ocean\n        </a>\n        . These help moisture accumulate in the winds during the hot season.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        The presence of abundant highlands like the\n        <a href="/wiki/Western_Ghats" title="Western Ghats">\n         Western Ghats\n        </a>\n        and the\n        <a href="/wiki/Himalayas" title="Himalayas">\n         Himalayas\n        </a>\n        right across the path of the southwest monsoon winds. These are the main cause of the substantial\n        <a href="/wiki/Orographic_lift#Precipitation" title="Orographic lift">\n         orographic precipitation\n        </a>\n        throughout the subcontinent.\n        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">\n         <a href="#cite_note-15">\n          [Note 2]\n         </a>\n        </sup>\n        <ol>\n         <li>\n          The Western Ghats are the first highlands of India that the southwest monsoon winds encounter.\n          <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">\n           <a href="#cite_note-16">\n            [Note 3]\n           </a>\n          </sup>\n          The Western Ghats rise abruptly from the\n          <a href="/wiki/Western_Coastal_Plains" title="Western Coastal Plains">\n           Western Coastal Plains\n          </a>\n          of the subcontinent, making effective orographic barriers for the monsoon winds.\n         </li>\n         <li>\n          The Himalayas play more than the role of orographic barriers for the monsoon. <a href="/wiki/Afghanistan" title="Afghanistan">\n           Afghanistan\n          </a>\n          , and\n          <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">\n           Russia\n          </a>\n          without causing any rain.\n          <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">\n           <a href="#cite_note-17">\n            [Note 4]\n           </a>\n          </sup>\n         </li>\n         <li>\n          For the northeast monsoon, the highlands of the\n          <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Ghats" title="Eastern Ghats">\n           Eastern Ghats\n          </a>\n          play the role of orographic barrier.\n         </li>\n        </ol>\n       </li>\n      </ol>\n      <ul class="gallery mw-gallery-traditional">\n       <li class="gallerybox" style="width: 155px">\n        <div class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Monsoon_of_South_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Features of monsoon rains">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       There are some unique features of the rains that the monsoon brings to the Indian subcontinent.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span id=\'"Bursting"\'>\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id=".22Bursting.22">\n        <i>\n         "Bursting"\n        </i>\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Monsoon_of_South_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title=\'Edit section: "Bursting"\'>\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Mumbai_india_monsoon_clouds.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="428" data-file-width="572" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Mumbai_india_monsoon_clouds.jpg/220px-Mumbai_india_monsoon_clouds.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Mumbai_india_monsoon_clouds.jpg/330px-Mumbai_india_monsoon_clouds.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/Mumbai_india_monsoon_clouds.jpg/440px-Mumbai_india_monsoon_clouds.jpg 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Mumbai_india_monsoon_clouds.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         "Monsoon burst" over\n         <a href="/wiki/Mumbai" title="Mumbai">\n          Mumbai\n         </a>\n         .\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       <i>\n        Bursting of monsoon\n       </i>\n       refers to the sudden change in weather conditions in India (typically from hot and dry weather to wet and humid weather during the southwest monsoon), characterized by an abrupt rise in the mean daily rainfall.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Allaby2002_18-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Allaby2002-18">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hanif2005_19-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Hanif2005-19">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Similarly, the burst of the northeast monsoon refers to an abrupt increase in the mean daily rainfall over the affected regions.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wang2006_20-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Wang2006-20">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span id=\'Rain_variability_("vagaries")\'>\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Rain_variability_.28.22vagaries.22.29">\n        Rain variability (\n        <i>\n         "vagaries"\n        </i>\n        )\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Monsoon_of_South_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title=\'Edit section: Rain variability ("vagaries")\'>\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       One of the most commonly used words to describe the erratic nature of the monsoon is "vagaries," used in newspapers,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IEvag_21-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-IEvag-21">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       magazines,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Darpan2007_22-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Darpan2007-22">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       books,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Krishnamacharyulu2003_23-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Krishnamacharyulu2003-23">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <a href="/wiki/Web_portal" title="Web portal">\n        web portals\n       </a>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IWP_24-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-IWP-24">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       to\n       <a href="/wiki/Insurance" title="Insurance">\n        insurance\n       </a>\n       plans,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FT1_25-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-FT1-25">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and India\'s budget discussions.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MI1_26-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MI1-26">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In some years, it rains too much, causing floods in parts of India; in others, it rains too little or not at all, causing droughts. In some years, the rain quantity is sufficient but its timing arbitrary. Sometimes, despite average annual rainfall, the daily distribution or geographic distribution of the rain is substantially skewed. In the recent past, rainfall variability in short time periods (about a week) were attributed to desert dust over the Arabian Sea and\n       <a href="/wiki/Western_Asia" title="Western Asia">\n        Western Asia\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27">\n        <a href="#cite_note-27">\n         [23]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Ideal_and_normal_monsoon_rains">\n        Ideal and normal monsoon rains\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Monsoon_of_South_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Ideal and normal monsoon rains">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:India_annual_rainfall_map_en.svg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1852" data-file-width="1639" height="226" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/India_annual_rainfall_map_en.svg/200px-India_annual_rainfall_map_en.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/India_annual_rainfall_map_en.svg/300px-India_annual_rainfall_map_en.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/India_annual_rainfall_map_en.svg/400px-India_annual_rainfall_map_en.svg.png 2x" width="200"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:India_annual_rainfall_map_en.svg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Annual average rainfall in India.\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Normally, the southwest monsoon can be expected to "burst" onto the western coast of India (near\n       <a href="/wiki/Thiruvananthapuram" title="Thiruvananthapuram">\n        Thiruvananthapuram\n       </a>\n       ) at the beginning of June and to cover the entire country by mid-July.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IMD1_12-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-IMD1-12">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IMDMOK_28-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-IMDMOK-28">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IMDjpg_29-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-IMDjpg-29">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Its withdrawal from India typically starts at the beginning of September and finishes by the beginning of October.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IMDwith_30-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-IMDwith-30">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IMDwithjpg_31-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-IMDwithjpg-31">\n         [27]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The northeast monsoon usually "bursts" around the 20th of October and lasts for about 50 days before withdrawing.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wang2006_20-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Wang2006-20">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       However, a rainy monsoon is not necessarily a normal monsoon \xe2\x80\x94 that is, one that performs close to\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Statistical_average" title="Statistical average">\n        statistical averages\n       </a>\n       calculated over a long period. A normal monsoon is generally accepted to be one involving close to the\n       <i>\n        average quantity\n       </i>\n       of precipitation over all the geographical locations under its influence (\n       <i>\n        mean spatial distribution\n       </i>\n       ) and over the entire expected time period (\n       <i>\n        mean temporal distribution\n       </i>\n       ). Additionally, the\n       <i>\n        arrival date\n       </i>\n       and the\n       <i>\n        departure date\n       </i>\n       of both the southwest and northeast monsoon should be close to the mean dates. The exact criteria for a normal monsoon are defined by the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indian_Meteorological_Department" title="Indian Meteorological Department">\n        Indian Meteorological Department\n       </a>\n       with calculations for the mean and standard deviation of each of these variables.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IMDavgMonsoon_32-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-IMDavgMonsoon-32">\n         [28]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <br/>\n       A monsoon with excess rain can cause floods, and one with too little rain can lead to widespread drought,\n       <a href="/wiki/Famine" title="Famine">\n        famine\n       </a>\n       , and economic losses. Therefore, a normal monsoon with mean performance is the most desirable monsoon.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Theories_for_mechanism_of_monsoon">\n        Theories for mechanism of monsoon\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Monsoon_of_South_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Theories for mechanism of monsoon">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Theories of the mechanism of the monsoon primarily try to explain the reasons for the seasonal reversal of winds and the timing of their reversal.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Traditional_theory">\n        Traditional theory\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Monsoon_of_South_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Traditional theory">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main article:\n       <a href="/wiki/Sea_breeze" title="Sea breeze">\n        Sea breeze\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Because of differences in the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Specific_heat_capacity" title="Specific heat capacity">\n        specific heat capacity\n       </a>\n       of land and water,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Continents" title="Continents">\n        continents\n       </a>\n       heat up faster than\n       <a href="/wiki/Sea" title="Sea">\n        seas\n       </a>\n       . Consequently, the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Air" title="Air">\n        air\n       </a>\n       above\n       <a href="/wiki/Coast" title="Coast">\n        coastal\n       </a>\n       lands heats up faster than the air above seas. This creates areas of low air pressure above coastal lands compared with pressure over the seas, causing winds to flow from the seas onto the neighboring lands. This is known as\n       <a href="/wiki/Sea_breeze" title="Sea breeze">\n        sea breeze\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <h4>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Process_of_monsoon_creation">\n        Process of monsoon creation\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Monsoon_of_South_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Process of monsoon creation">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h4>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main article:\n       <a href="/wiki/Monsoon#Process" title="Monsoon">\n        Monsoon \xc2\xa7\xc2\xa0Process\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Diagrama_de_formacion_de_la_brisa-breeze.svg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="755" data-file-width="689" height="219" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9b/Diagrama_de_formacion_de_la_brisa-breeze.svg/200px-Diagrama_de_formacion_de_la_brisa-breeze.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9b/Diagrama_de_formacion_de_la_brisa-breeze.svg/300px-Diagrama_de_formacion_de_la_brisa-breeze.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9b/Diagrama_de_formacion_de_la_brisa-breeze.svg/400px-Diagrama_de_formacion_de_la_brisa-breeze.svg.png 2x" width="200"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Diagrama_de_formacion_de_la_brisa-breeze.svg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         A: sea breeze; B: land breeze.\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Also known as the\n       <i>\n        thermal theory\n       </i>\n       or the\n       <i>\n        differential heating of sea and land theory\n       </i>\n       , the traditional theory portrays the monsoon as a large-scale\n       <a href="/wiki/Sea_breeze" title="Sea breeze">\n        sea breeze\n       </a>\n       . It states that during the hot subtropical summers, the massive\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landmass" title="Landmass">\n        landmass\n       </a>\n       of the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indian_Peninsula" title="Indian Peninsula">\n        Indian Peninsula\n       </a>\n       heats up at a different rate than the surrounding seas, resulting in a\n       <a href="/wiki/Pressure_gradient" title="Pressure gradient">\n        pressure gradient\n       </a>\n       from south to north. This causes the flow of moisture-laden winds from sea to land. On reaching land, these winds rise because of the geographical relief, cooling\n       <a href="/wiki/Adiabatic_process#Adiabatic_heating_and_cooling" title="Adiabatic process">\n        adiabatically\n       </a>\n       and leading to orographic rains. This is the\n       <i>\n        southwest monsoon\n       </i>\n       . The reverse happens during the winter, when the land is colder than the sea, establishing a pressure gradient from land to sea. This causes the winds to blow over the Indian subcontinent toward the\n       <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">\n        Indian Ocean\n       </a>\n       in a northeasterly direction, causing the\n       <i>\n        northeast monsoon\n       </i>\n       . Because the southwest monsoon flows from sea to land, it carries more moisture, and therefore causes more rain, than the northeast monsoon. Only part of the northeast monsoon passing over the Bay of Bengal picks up moisture, causing rain in\n       <a href="/wiki/Andhra_Pradesh" title="Andhra Pradesh">\n        Andhra Pradesh\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Tamil_Nadu" title="Tamil Nadu">\n        Tamil Nadu\n       </a>\n       during the winter months.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       However, many meteorologists argue that the monsoon is not a local phenomenon as explained by the traditional theory, but a general weather phenomenon along the entire\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropics" title="Tropics">\n        tropical zone\n       </a>\n       of\n       <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">\n        Earth\n       </a>\n       . This criticism does not deny the role of differential heating of sea and land in generating monsoon winds, but casts it as one of several factors rather than the only one.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Dynamic_theory">\n        Dynamic theory\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Monsoon_of_South_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Dynamic theory">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Earth_Global_Circulation_-_en.svg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="835" data-file-width="1000" height="251" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Earth_Global_Circulation_-_en.svg/300px-Earth_Global_Circulation_-_en.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Earth_Global_Circulation_-_en.svg/450px-Earth_Global_Circulation_-_en.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Earth_Global_Circulation_-_en.svg/600px-Earth_Global_Circulation_-_en.svg.png 2x" width="300"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Earth_Global_Circulation_-_en.svg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         The atmospheric circulation system with associated pressure belts and latitudes.\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main article:\n       <a href="/wiki/Prevailing_winds" title="Prevailing winds">\n        Prevailing winds\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main article:\n       <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_circulation" title="Atmospheric circulation">\n        Atmospheric circulation\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <a href="/wiki/Prevailing_winds" title="Prevailing winds">\n        prevailing winds\n       </a>\n       of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_circulation" title="Atmospheric circulation">\n        atmospheric circulation\n       </a>\n       arise because of the difference in pressure at various\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latitudes" title="Latitudes">\n        latitudes\n       </a>\n       and act as means for distribution of\n       <a href="/wiki/Thermal_energy" title="Thermal energy">\n        thermal energy\n       </a>\n       on the planet. This pressure difference is because of the differences in\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solar_insolation" title="Solar insolation">\n        solar insolation\n       </a>\n       received at different latitudes and the resulting uneven heating of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Planet" title="Planet">\n        planet\n       </a>\n       . Alternating belts of high pressure and low pressure develop along the\n       <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">\n        equator\n       </a>\n       , the two\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropics" title="Tropics">\n        tropics\n       </a>\n       , the\n       <a href="/wiki/Arctic" title="Arctic">\n        Arctic\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Antarctic" title="Antarctic">\n        Antarctic\n       </a>\n       circles, and the two\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polar_region" title="Polar region">\n        polar regions\n       </a>\n       , giving rise to the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trade_wind" title="Trade wind">\n        trade winds\n       </a>\n       , the\n       <a href="/wiki/Westerlies" title="Westerlies">\n        westerlies\n       </a>\n       , and the\n       <a href="/wiki/Polar_easterlies" title="Polar easterlies">\n        polar easterlies\n       </a>\n       . However, geophysical factors like\n       <a href="/wiki/Earth%27s_orbit" title="Earth\'s orbit">\n        Earth\'s orbit\n       </a>\n       , its rotation, and its\n       <a href="/wiki/Axial_tilt" title="Axial tilt">\n        axial tilt\n       </a>\n       cause these belts to shift gradually north and south, following the\n       <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">\n        Sun\n       </a>\n       \'s seasonal shifts.\n      </p>\n      <h4>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Process_of_monsoon_creation_2">\n        Process of monsoon creation\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Monsoon_of_South_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Process of monsoon creation">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h4>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <i>\n        dynamic theory\n       </i>\n       explains the monsoon on the basis of the annual shifts in the position of global belts of pressure and winds. According to this theory, the monsoon is a result of the shift of the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inter_Tropical_Convergence_Zone" title="Inter Tropical Convergence Zone">\n        Inter Tropical Convergence Zone\n       </a>\n       (ITCZ) under the influence of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Solar_equator" title="Solar equator">\n        vertical sun\n       </a>\n       . Though the mean position of the ITCZ is taken as the equator, it shifts north and south with the migration of the vertical sun toward the Tropics of\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropic_of_Cancer" title="Tropic of Cancer">\n        Cancer\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropic_of_Capricorn" title="Tropic of Capricorn">\n        Capricorn\n       </a>\n       during the\n       <a href="/wiki/Summer" title="Summer">\n        summer\n       </a>\n       of the respective hemispheres (\n       <a href="/wiki/Northern_Hemisphere" title="Northern Hemisphere">\n        Northern\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Southern_Hemisphere" title="Southern Hemisphere">\n        Southern Hemisphere\n       </a>\n       ). As such, during the northern summer (May and June), the ITCZ moves north, along with the vertical sun, toward the Tropic of Cancer. The ITCZ, as the zone of lowest pressure in the tropical region, is the target destination for the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trade_wind" title="Trade wind">\n        trade winds\n       </a>\n       of both hemispheres. Consequently, with the ITCZ at the Tropic of Cancer, the southeast trade winds of the Southern Hemisphere have to cross the equator to reach it.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33">\n        <a href="#cite_note-33">\n         [Note 5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       However, because of the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coriolis_effect" title="Coriolis effect">\n        Coriolis effect\n       </a>\n       (which causes winds in the Northern Hemisphere to turn right, whereas winds in the Southern Hemisphere turn left), these southeast trade winds are deflected east in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Northern_Hemisphere" title="Northern Hemisphere">\n        Northern Hemisphere\n       </a>\n       , transforming into southwest trades.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34">\n        <a href="#cite_note-34">\n         [Note 6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       These pick up moisture while traveling from sea to land and cause orographic rain once they hit the highlands of the Indian Peninsula. This results in the southwest monsoon.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The dynamic theory explains the monsoon as a global weather phenomenon rather than just a local one. And when coupled with the traditional theory (based on the heating of sea and land), it enhances the explanation of the varying intensity of monsoon precipitation along the coastal regions with orographic barriers.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Jet_stream_theory">\n        Jet stream theory\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Monsoon_of_South_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Jet stream theory">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main article:\n       <a href="/wiki/Jet_stream" title="Jet stream">\n        Jet stream\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Jetcrosssection.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="240" data-file-width="600" height="120" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Jetcrosssection.jpg/300px-Jetcrosssection.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Jetcrosssection.jpg/450px-Jetcrosssection.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/3/3e/Jetcrosssection.jpg 2x" width="300"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Jetcrosssection.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Jet streams of Earth\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       This theory tries to explain the establishment of the northeast and southwest monsoons, as well as unique features like "bursting" and variability.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The jet streams are systems of upper-air westerlies. They give rise to slowly moving upper-air waves, with 250-\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Knots" title="Knots">\n        knot\n       </a>\n       winds in some air streams. First observed by\n       <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">\n        World War II\n       </a>\n       pilots, they develop just below the\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropopause" title="Tropopause">\n        tropopause\n       </a>\n       over areas of steep pressure gradient on the surface. The main types are the\n       <i>\n        polar jets\n       </i>\n       , the\n       <i>\n        subtropical westerly jets\n       </i>\n       , and the less common\n       <i>\n        tropical easterly jets\n       </i>\n       . They follow the principle of\n       <a href="/wiki/Geostrophic_wind" title="Geostrophic wind">\n        geostrophic winds\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35">\n        <a href="#cite_note-35">\n         [Note 7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h4>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Process_of_monsoon_creation_3">\n        Process of monsoon creation\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Monsoon_of_South_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Process of monsoon creation">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h4>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Tibet_Karte_Topograpisch.png">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1012" data-file-width="1500" height="169" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9e/Tibet_Karte_Topograpisch.png/250px-Tibet_Karte_Topograpisch.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9e/Tibet_Karte_Topograpisch.png/375px-Tibet_Karte_Topograpisch.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9e/Tibet_Karte_Topograpisch.png/500px-Tibet_Karte_Topograpisch.png 2x" width="250"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Tibet_Karte_Topograpisch.png" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         The\n         <a href="/wiki/Tibetan_Plateau" title="Tibetan Plateau">\n          Tibetan Plateau\n         </a>\n         lies north of the\n         <a href="/wiki/Himalayas" title="Himalayas">\n          Himalayas\n         </a>\n         .\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Over India, a subtropical westerly jet develops in the winter season and is replaced by the tropical easterly jet in the summer season. The high temperature during the summer over the\n       <a href="/wiki/Tibetan_Plateau" title="Tibetan Plateau">\n        Tibetan Plateau\n       </a>\n       , as well as over\n       <a href="/wiki/Central_Asia" title="Central Asia">\n        Central Asia\n       </a>\n       in general, is believed to be the critical factor leading to the formation of the tropical easterly jet over India.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The mechanism affecting the monsoon is that the westerly jet causes high pressure over northern parts of the subcontinent during the winter. This results in the north-to-south flow of the winds in the form of the northeast monsoon. With the northward shift of the vertical sun, this jet shifts north, too. The intense heat over the Tibetan Plateau, coupled with associated\n       <a href="/wiki/Terrain" title="Terrain">\n        terrain\n       </a>\n       features like the high altitude of the plateau, generate the tropical easterly jet over central India. This jet creates a low-pressure zone over the\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Northern_Indian_plains&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Northern Indian plains (page does not exist)">\n        northern Indian plains\n       </a>\n       , influencing the wind flow toward these plains and assisting the development of the southwest monsoon.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span id=\'Theories_for_"bursting"\'>\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Theories_for_.22bursting.22">\n        Theories for "bursting"\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Monsoon_of_South_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title=\'Edit section: Theories for "bursting"\'>\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       The "bursting"\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Allaby2002_18-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Allaby2002-18">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       of the monsoon is primarily explained by the jet stream theory and the dynamic theory.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Dynamic_theory_2">\n        Dynamic theory\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Monsoon_of_South_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Dynamic theory">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       According to this theory, during the summer months in the Northern Hemisphere, the ITCZ shifts north, pulling the southwest monsoon winds onto the land from the sea. However, the huge landmass of the Himalayas restricts the low-pressure zone onto the Himalayas themselves. It is only when the Tibetan Plateau heats up significantly more than the Himalayas that the ITCZ abruptly and swiftly shifts north, leading to the bursting of monsoon rains over the Indian subcontinent. The reverse shift takes place for the northeast monsoon winds, leading to a second, minor burst of rainfall over the eastern Indian Peninsula during the Northern Hemisphere winter months.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Jet_stream_theory_2">\n        Jet stream theory\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Monsoon_of_South_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Jet stream theory">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       According to this theory, the onset of the southwest monsoon is driven by the shift of the subtropical westerly jet north from over the plains of India toward the Tibetan Plateau. This shift is due to the intense heating of the plateau during the summer months. The northward shift is not a slow and gradual process, as expected for most changes in weather pattern. The primary cause is believed to be the height of the Himalayas. As the Tibetan Plateau heats up, the low pressure created over it pulls the westerly jet north. Because of the lofty Himalayas, the westerly jet\'s movement is inhibited. But with continuous dropping pressure, sufficient force is created for the movement of the westerly jet across the Himalayas after a significant period. As such, the shift of the jet is sudden and abrupt, causing the bursting of southwest monsoon rains onto the Indian plains. The reverse shift happens for the northeast monsoon.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Theories_for_monsoon_variability">\n        Theories for monsoon variability\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Monsoon_of_South_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Theories for monsoon variability">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="The_jet_stream_effect">\n        The jet stream effect\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Monsoon_of_South_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: The jet stream effect">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       The jet stream theory also explains the variability in timing and strength of the monsoon.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        Timing:\n       </b>\n       A timely northward shift of the subtropical westerly jet at the beginning of summer is critical to the onset of the southwest monsoon over India. If the shift is delayed, so is the southwest monsoon. An early shift results in an early monsoon.\n       <br/>\n       <b>\n        Strength:\n       </b>\n       The strength of the southwest monsoon is determined by the strength of the easterly tropical jet over central India. A strong easterly tropical jet results in a strong southwest monsoon over central India, and a weak jet results in a weak monsoon.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span id="El_Ni\xc3\xb1o-Southern_Oscillation_effect">\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="El_Ni.C3.B1o-Southern_Oscillation_effect">\n        El Ni\xc3\xb1o-Southern Oscillation effect\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Monsoon_of_South_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: El Ni\xc3\xb1o-Southern Oscillation effect">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main article:\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/El_Ni%C3%B1o-Southern_Oscillation" title="El Ni\xc3\xb1o-Southern Oscillation">\n        El Ni\xc3\xb1o-Southern Oscillation\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:El_Nino_regional_impacts.png">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="539" data-file-width="486" height="333" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/El_Nino_regional_impacts.png/300px-El_Nino_regional_impacts.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/El_Nino_regional_impacts.png/450px-El_Nino_regional_impacts.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/b/b7/El_Nino_regional_impacts.png 2x" width="300"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:El_Nino_regional_impacts.png" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Effects of\n         <a href="/wiki/El_Ni%C3%B1o" title="El Ni\xc3\xb1o">\n          El Ni\xc3\xb1o\n         </a>\n         on the weather of the subcontinent.\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/El_nino" title="El nino">\n        <i>\n         El Ni\xc3\xb1o\n        </i>\n       </a>\n       is a warm ocean current originating along the coast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">\n        Peru\n       </a>\n       that replaces the usual cold\n       <a href="/wiki/Humboldt_Current" title="Humboldt Current">\n        Humboldt Current\n       </a>\n       . The warm surface water moving toward the coast of Peru with El Ni\xc3\xb1o is pushed west by the trade winds, thereby raising the temperature of the southern Pacific Ocean. The reverse condition is known as\n       <a href="/wiki/La_Ni%C3%B1a" title="La Ni\xc3\xb1a">\n        La Ni\xc3\xb1a\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <i>\n        Southern oscillation\n       </i>\n       , a phenomenon first observed by\n       <a href="/wiki/Gilbert_Walker" title="Gilbert Walker">\n        Sir Gilbert Thomas Walker\n       </a>\n       , director general of observatories in India, refers to the seesaw relationship of atmospheric pressures between\n       <a href="/wiki/Tahiti" title="Tahiti">\n        Tahiti\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Darwin,_Northern_Territory" title="Darwin, Northern Territory">\n        Darwin\n       </a>\n       , Australia.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IODhindu_36-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-IODhindu-36">\n         [29]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Walker noticed that when pressure was high in Tahiti, it was low in Darwin, and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Latin_phrases:_V#vice_versa" title="List of Latin phrases: V">\n        vice versa\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IODhindu_36-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-IODhindu-36">\n         [29]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A\n       <i>\n        Southern Oscillation Index\n       </i>\n       (SOI), based on the pressure difference between Tahiti and Darwin, has been formulated by the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bureau_of_Meteorology_(Australia)" title="Bureau of Meteorology (Australia)">\n        Bureau of Meteorology (Australia)\n       </a>\n       to measure the strength of the oscillation.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Aus_37-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Aus-37">\n         [30]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Walker noticed that the quantity of rainfall in the Indian subcontinent was often negligible in years of high pressure over Darwin (and low pressure over Tahiti). Conversely, low pressure over Darwin bodes well for precipitation quantity in India. Thus, Walker established the relationship between southern oscillation and quantities of monsoon rains in India.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IODhindu_36-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-IODhindu-36">\n         [29]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Ultimately, the southern oscillation was found to be simply an atmospheric component of the El Ni\xc3\xb1o/La Ni\xc3\xb1a effect, which happens in the ocean.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IODhindu_36-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-IODhindu-36">\n         [29]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Therefore, in the context of the monsoon, the two together came to be known as the El Ni\xc3\xb1o-Southern Oscillation (ENSO) effect. The effect is known to have a pronounced influence on the strength of the southwest monsoon over India, with the monsoon being weak (causing droughts) during El Ni\xc3\xb1o years, while La Ni\xc3\xb1a years bring particularly strong monsoons.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IODhindu_36-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-IODhindu-36">\n         [29]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Indian_Ocean_dipole_effect">\n        Indian Ocean dipole effect\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Monsoon_of_South_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Indian Ocean dipole effect">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main article:\n       <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean_Dipole" title="Indian Ocean Dipole">\n        Indian Ocean Dipole\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Although the ENSO effect was statistically effective in explaining several past droughts in India, in recent decades, its relationship with the Indian monsoon seemed to weaken.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38">\n        <a href="#cite_note-38">\n         [31]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       For example, the strong ENSO of 1997 did not cause drought in India.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IODhindu_36-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-IODhindu-36">\n         [29]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       However, it was later discovered that, just like ENSO in the Pacific Ocean, a similar seesaw ocean-atmosphere system in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">\n        Indian Ocean\n       </a>\n       was also in play. This system was discovered in 1999 and named the\n       <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean_Dipole" title="Indian Ocean Dipole">\n        Indian Ocean Dipole\n       </a>\n       (IOD). An index to calculate it was also formulated. IOD develops in the equatorial region of the Indian Ocean from April to May and peaks in October.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IODhindu_36-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-IODhindu-36">\n         [29]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       With a positive IOD, winds over the Indian Ocean blow from east to west. This makes the Arabian Sea (the western Indian Ocean near the African coast) much warmer and the eastern Indian Ocean around Indonesia colder and drier.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IODhindu_36-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-IODhindu-36">\n         [29]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In negative dipole years, the reverse happens, making Indonesia much warmer and rainier.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       A positive IOD index often negates the effect of ENSO, resulting in increased monsoon rains in years such as 1983, 1994, and 1997.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IODhindu_36-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-IODhindu-36">\n         [29]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Further, the two poles of the IOD \xe2\x80\x94 the eastern pole (around Indonesia) and the western pole (off the African coast) \xe2\x80\x94 independently and cumulatively affect the quantity of monsoon rains.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IODhindu_36-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-IODhindu-36">\n         [29]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h4>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Equatorial_Indian_Ocean_oscillation">\n        Equatorial Indian Ocean oscillation\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Monsoon_of_South_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Equatorial Indian Ocean oscillation">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h4>\n      <p>\n       As with ENSO, the atmospheric component of the IOD was later discovered and the cumulative phenomenon named\n       <i>\n        Equatorial Indian Ocean oscillation\n       </i>\n       (EQUINOO).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IODhindu_36-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-IODhindu-36">\n         [29]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       When EQUINOO effects are factored in, certain failed forecasts, like the acute drought of 2002, can be further accounted for.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IODhindu_36-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-IODhindu-36">\n         [29]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The relationship between extremes of the Indian summer monsoon rainfall, along with ENSO and EQUINOO,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EQgadgil_39-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-EQgadgil-39">\n         [32]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       have been studied, and models to better predict the quantity of monsoon rains have been statistically derived.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EQgadgil_39-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-EQgadgil-39">\n         [32]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Impact_of_climate_change_on_the_monsoon">\n        Impact of climate change on the monsoon\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Monsoon_of_South_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Impact of climate change on the monsoon">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Since 1950s, the South Asian summer monsoon underwent a gradual decline, with a reduction of up to 10% over central India.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40">\n        <a href="#cite_note-40">\n         [33]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       This is primarily due to a weakening monsoon circulation as a result of the rapid warming in the Indian Ocean\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41">\n        <a href="#cite_note-41">\n         [34]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42">\n        <a href="#cite_note-42">\n         [35]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       , and changes in land use and land cover\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43">\n        <a href="#cite_note-43">\n         [36]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       . Since the strength of the monsoon is partially dependent on the temperature difference between the ocean and the land, higher ocean temperatures in the Indian Ocean have weakened the moisture bearing winds from the ocean to the land. The reduction in the summer monsoon rainfall have grave consequences over central India because at least 60% of the agriculture in this region is still largely\n       <a href="/wiki/Rainfed_agriculture" title="Rainfed agriculture">\n        rain-fed\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       A recent assessment of the monsoonal changes indicate that the land warming has increased during 2002-2014, possibly reviving the strength of the monsoon circulation and rainfall\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44">\n        <a href="#cite_note-44">\n         [37]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       . Future changes in the monsoon will depend on a competition between land and ocean\xe2\x80\x94on which is warming faster than the other.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Meanwhile, there has been a three-fold rise in widespread extreme rainfall events during the years 1950 to 2015, over the entire central belt of India, leading to a steady rise in the number of flash floods with significant socioeconomic losses\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45">\n        <a href="#cite_note-45">\n         [38]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46">\n        <a href="#cite_note-46">\n         [39]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       . Widespread extreme rainfall events are those rainfall events which are larger than 150 mm/day and spread over a region large enough to cause floods.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Monsoon_rain_prediction_models">\n        Monsoon rain prediction models\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Monsoon_of_South_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Monsoon rain prediction models">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Since the\n       <a href="/wiki/Great_Famine_of_1876%E2%80%9378" class="reference" id="cite_ref-MOL1_47-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MOL1-47">\n         [40]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At least five prediction models exist.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CSgadgil_48-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CSgadgil-48">\n         [41]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span distribution.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CSgadgil_48-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CSgadgil-48">\n         [41]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span In 2003, IMD substantially changed its forecast methodology, model,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rajeevan1_50-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Rajeevan1-50">\n         [43]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and administration.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IMDchiefChange_51-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-IMDchiefChange-51">\n         [44]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A sixteen-parameter monsoon forecasting model used since 1988 was replaced in 2003.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rajeevan1_50-1">\n        <a edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner"  <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:MatheranPanoramaPointMonsoon.JPG" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         <a href="/wiki/Western_Ghats" primary delivery mechanism for\n       <a href="/wiki/Fresh_water" title="Fresh water">\n        fresh water\n       </a>\n       in the Indian subcontinent. As such, it affects the\n       <a href="/wiki/Natural_environment" title="Natural environment">\n        environment\n       </a>\n       (and associated flora, fauna, and\n       <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem" title="Ecosystem">\n        ecosystems\n       </a>\n       ), agriculture, society, hydro-power production, and geography of the subcontinent (like the availability of fresh water in water bodies and the underground water table), with all of these factors cumulatively contributing to the health of the economy of affected countries.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The monsoon turns large parts of India from semi-deserts into green grasslands. See photos taken only three months apart in the Western Ghats.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span id="Geographical_(wettest_spots_on_Earth)">\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Geographical_.28wettest_spots_on_Earth.29">\n        Geographical (wettest spots on Earth)\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Monsoon_of_South_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=29" title="Edit section: Geographical (wettest spots on Earth)">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       <a href="/wiki/Mawsynram" title="Mawsynram">\n        Mawsynram\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Cherrapunji" title="Cherrapunji">\n        Cherrapunji\n       </a>\n       , both in the Indian state of\n       <a href="/wiki/Meghalaya" title="Meghalaya">\n        Meghalaya\n       </a>\n       , alternate as the wettest places on Earth given the quantity of their rainfall,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wettest_54-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Wettest-54">\n         [47]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       though there are other cities with similar claims. They receive more than 11,000 millimeters of rain each from the monsoon.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Agricultural">\n        Agricultural\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Monsoon_of_South_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=30" title="Edit section: Agricultural">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       In India, which has historically had a primarily agrarian economy, the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Services_sector" title="Services sector">\n        services sector\n       </a>\n       recently overtook the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Farm_sector" title="Farm sector">\n        farm sector\n       </a>\n       in terms of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GDP" title="GDP">\n        GDP\n       </a>\n       contribution. However, the\n       <a href="/wiki/Agriculture" title="Agriculture">\n        agriculture\n       </a>\n       sector still contributes 17-20% of GDP\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rediff1_55-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Rediff1-55">\n         [48]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and is the largest employer in the country, with about 60% of Indians dependent on it for employment and livelihood.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rediff1_55-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Rediff1-55">\n         [48]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       About 49% of India\'s land is agricultural; that number rises to 55% if associated\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wetlands" title="Wetlands">\n        wetlands\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Dryland_farming" title="Dryland farming">\n        dryland farming\n       </a>\n       areas, etc., are included. Because more than half of these farmlands are rain-fed, the monsoon is critical to food sufficiency and quality of life.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Despite progress in alternative forms of irrigation, agricultural dependence on the monsoon remains far from insignificant. Therefore, the agricultural calendar of India is governed by the monsoon. Any fluctuations in the time distribution, spatial distribution, or quantity of the monsoon rains may lead to floods or droughts, causing the agricultural sector to suffer. This has a cascading effect on the secondary economic sectors, the overall economy, food inflation, and therefore the general population\'s quality and cost of living.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Economic">\n        Economic\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Monsoon_of_South_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=31" title="Edit section: Economic">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       The economic significance of the monsoon is aptly described by\n       <a href="/wiki/Pranab_Mukherjee" title="Pranab Mukherjee">\n        Pranab Mukherjee\n       </a>\n       \'s remark that the monsoon is the "real finance minister of India".\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HT1_4-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HT1-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IBNlive1_5-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-IBNlive1-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A good monsoon results in better agricultural yields, which brings down prices of essential food commodities and reduces imports, thus reducing\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Food_inflation" title="Food inflation">\n        food inflation\n       </a>\n       overall.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rediff1_55-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Rediff1-55">\n         [48]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Better rains also result in increased hydroelectric production.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rediff1_55-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Rediff1-55">\n         [48]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       All of these factors have positive ripple effects throughout the economy of India.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rediff1_55-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Rediff1-55">\n         [48]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The down side however is that when monsoon rains are weak, crop production is low leading to higher food prices with limited supply.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:2_56-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-:2-56">\n         [49]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       As a result, the Indian government is actively working with farmers and the nation\'s meteorological department to produce more drought resistant crops.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:2_56-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-:2-56">\n         [49]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Social">\n        Social\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Monsoon_of_South_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=32" title="Edit section: Social">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/D._Subbarao" title="D. Subbarao">\n        D. Subbarao\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Governors_of_the_Reserve_Bank_of_India" title="List of Governors of the Reserve Bank of India">\n        governor of the Reserve Bank of India\n       </a>\n       , emphasized during a quarterly review of India\'s\n       <a href="/wiki/Monetary_policy" title="Monetary policy">\n        monetary policy\n       </a>\n       that the lives of Indians depend on the performance of the monsoon.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DS_57-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-DS-57">\n         [50]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       His own career prospects, his emotional well-being, and the performance of his monetary policy are all "a hostage" to the monsoon, he said, as is the case for most Indians.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DS_57-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-DS-57">\n         [50]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Additionally, farmers rendered jobless by failed monsoon rains tend to migrate to cities. This crowds city slums and aggravates the infrastructure and sustainability of city life.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IEtme_58-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-IEtme-58">\n         [51]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Travel">\n        Travel\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Monsoon_of_South_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=33" title="Edit section: Travel">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       In the past, Indians usually refrained from traveling during monsoons for practical as well as religious reasons. But with the advent of globalization, such travel is gaining popularity. Places like\n       <a href="/wiki/Kerala" title="Kerala">\n        Kerala\n       </a>\n       and the Western Ghats get a large number of tourists, both local and foreigners, during the monsoon season. Kerala is one of the top destinations for tourists interested in\n       <a href="/wiki/Ayurveda" title="Ayurveda">\n        Ayurvedic\n       </a>\n       treatments and massage therapy. One major drawback of traveling during the monsoon is that most wildlife sanctuaries are closed. Also, some mountainous areas, especially in Himalayan regions, get cut off when roads are damaged by landslides and floods during heavy rains.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ift_59-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ift-59">\n         [52]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Environmental">\n        Environmental\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Monsoon_of_South_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=34" title="Edit section: Environmental">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       The monsoon is the primary bearer of fresh water to the area. The\n       <a href="/wiki/Geography_of_South_India#Water_bodies" title="Geography of South India">\n        peninsular/Deccan rivers of India\n       </a>\n       are mostly rain-fed and non-perennial in nature, depending primarily on the monsoon for water supply.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IG_60-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-IG-60">\n         [53]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Most of the\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_rivers_of_India#Rivers_flowing_into_Arabian_Sea" title="List of rivers of India">\n        coastal rivers of Western India\n       </a>\n       are also rain-fed and monsoon-dependent.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IG_60-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-IG-60">\n         [53]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IBC_61-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-IBC-61">\n         [54]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       As such, the flora, fauna, and entire\n       <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem" title="Ecosystem">\n        ecosystems\n       </a>\n       of these areas rely heavily on the monsoon.\n       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">\n        [\n        <i>\n         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">\n          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. <b>\n           <a href="#cite_ref-11">\n            ^\n           </a>\n          </b>\n         </span>\n         <span class="reference-text">\n          The name of the wind is based on the Northeast winds flow from the northeast toward the southwest, onto the land.\n         </span>\n        </li>\n        <li id="cite_note-15">\n         <span class="mw-cite-backlink">\n          <b>\n direction of the path of the southwest winds and not\n          <i>\n           across\n          </i>\n          them, causing no\n          <a href="/wiki/Orographic_lift" title="Orographic lift">\n           orographic lift\n          </a>\n          of the winds.\n         </span>\n        </li>\n        <li id="cite_note-16">\n         <span class="mw-cite-backlink">\n          <b>\n
