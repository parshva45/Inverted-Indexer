</tr>\n       <tr style="display:none">\n        <td colspan="2">\n        </td>\n       </tr>\n      </table>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        Hurricane Frances\n       </b>\n       was the sixth named\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        storm\n       </a>\n       , the fourth\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        hurricane\n       </a>\n       , and the third major hurricane, of the\n       <a href="/wiki/2004_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2004 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        2004 Atlantic hurricane season\n       </a>\n       . The system crossed the open Atlantic in mid and late August, moving to the north of the Lesser Antilles while strengthening. Its outer bands struck\n       <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">\n        Puerto Rico\n       </a>\n       and the\n       <a href="/wiki/British_Virgin_Islands" title="British Virgin Islands">\n        British Virgin Islands\n       </a>\n       while passing north of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">\n        Caribbean Sea\n       </a>\n       . The storm\'s\n       <a href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_wind" title="Maximum sustained wind">\n        maximum sustained wind\n       </a>\n       peaked at 145 miles per hour (233\xc2\xa0km/h), achieving Category 4 on the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">\n        Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale\n       </a>\n       . As the system\'s forward motion slowed, the\n       <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">\n        eye\n       </a>\n       passed over\n       <a href="/wiki/San_Salvador_Island" title="San Salvador Island">\n        San Salvador Island\n       </a>\n       and very close to\n       <a href="/wiki/Cat_Island,_Bahamas" title="Cat Island, Bahamas">\n        Cat Island\n       </a>\n       in the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">\n        Bahamas\n       </a>\n       . Frances was the first hurricane to impact the entire Bahamian archipelago since 1928, and almost completely destroyed the agricultural economy there.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Frances then passed over the central sections of\n       <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">\n        Florida\n       </a>\n       , in the\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">\n        United States\n       </a>\n       , three weeks after\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Charley" title="Hurricane Charley">\n        Hurricane Charley\n       </a>\n       , causing significant damage to the state\'s citrus crop, closing major airports and schools, and forcing the cancellation of a collegiate football game. The storm then moved briefly offshore from Florida, into the northeast\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">\n        Gulf of Mexico\n       </a>\n       , and made a second U.S. landfall, at the\n       <a href="/wiki/Florida_Panhandle" title="Florida Panhandle">\n        Florida Panhandle\n       </a>\n       , before accelerating northeast through the eastern\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">\n        United States\n       </a>\n       near the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Appalachians" title="Appalachians">\n        Appalachians\n       </a>\n       and into\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Canada" title="Atlantic Canada">\n        Atlantic Canada\n       </a>\n       while weakening. A significant tornado outbreak accompanied the storm across the eastern United States, nearly equaling the outbreak from\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Beulah" title="Hurricane Beulah">\n        Hurricane Beulah\n       </a>\n       . Very heavy rains fell in association with this slow-moving and relatively large hurricane, which caused floods in Florida and North Carolina. Some 49 people died from the\n       <a href="/wiki/Cyclone" title="Cyclone">\n        cyclone\n       </a>\n       . Damages totaled US$12\xc2\xa0billion (2004 dollars).\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="toc">\n       <div class="toctitle">\n        <h2>\n         Contents\n </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       A strong tropical wave moved off the west coast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">\n        Africa\n       </a>\n       on August 21.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TWO1_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TWO1-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Moving under the base of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_ridge" title="Subtropical ridge">\n        subtropical ridge\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tropdis1_2-0">\n        <a id="cite_ref-TWO3_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TWO3-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Thunderstorms associated with the wave finally began organizing early on August 24,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TWO2_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TWO2-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and the system became a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="#cite_note-pubadv1-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Good upper-level outflow was observed in all but the eastern quadrants as the depression continued on its path,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tropdis2_6-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tropdis2-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and the depression strengthened to tropical storm status on August 25, approximately 1,420 miles (2,290\xc2\xa0km) east of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">\n        Lesser Antilles\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pubadv4_7-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-pubadv4-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The tropical storm, now named Frances, further intensified on August 26 in an environment of low vertical\n       <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">\n        wind shear\n       </a>\n       as its track bent to the west-northwest.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tropdis4_8-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tropdis4-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Frances rapidly intensified, developing an\n       <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">\n        eye\n       </a>\n       and reaching hurricane strength late that afternoon.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tropdis5-1_9-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tropdis5-1-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       An approaching upper level trough caused Frances to move more northwesterly on August 27.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tropdis6_10-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tropdis6-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The cyclone reached its primary peak intensity of 135 miles per hour (217\xc2\xa0km/h) on August 28.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tropdis5-2_11-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tropdis5-2-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The hurricane turned back to its original westward motion on August 29, as the upper trough moved away the region and the subtropical ridge strengthened to Frances\' north.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tropdis7_12-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tropdis7-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb 27, 2004\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Over the next day, the hurricane underwent an\n       <a href="/wiki/Eyewall_replacement_cycle" title="Eyewall replacement cycle">\n        eyewall replacement cycle\n       </a>\n       , during which the maximum sustained winds decreased to 115 miles per hour (185\xc2\xa0km/h).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_13-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       This weakening trend was short lived, and the storm reintensified during the afternoon of August 30, as vertical wind shear remained low.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tropdis8_14-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tropdis8-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The storm continued strengthening as it turned west-northwestward, reaching its peak intensity of 145 miles per hour (233\xc2\xa0km/h) on September 2 while 555 miles (893\xc2\xa0km) east-southeast of\n       <a href="/wiki/West_Palm_Beach,_Florida" title="West Palm Beach, Florida">\n        West Palm Beach, Florida\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tropdis9_15-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tropdis9-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On September 2, Frances entered the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">\n        Bahamas\n       </a>\n       , passing directly over\n       <a href="/wiki/San_Salvador_Island" title="San Salvador Island">\n        San Salvador Island\n       </a>\n       and very close to\n       <a href="/wiki/Cat_Island,_Bahamas" title="Cat Island, Bahamas">\n        Cat Island\n       </a>\n       . The storm weakened to a Category\xc2\xa03 hurricane by 2\xc2\xa0pm, which was initially attributed to inner core processes,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">\n        <a href="#cite_note-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       but increasing westerly winds aloft, and the resultant vertical wind shear, was later determined to be the cause.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_13-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On September 3, Frances passed into the vicinity of\n       <a href="/wiki/Abaco_Islands" title="Abaco Islands">\n        Abaco Island\n       </a>\n       and directly over\n       <a href="/wiki/Grand_Bahama" title="Grand Bahama">\n        Grand Bahama\n       </a>\n       while continuing to slowly weaken. The storm regained Category\xc2\xa02 hurricane intensity prior to passing over Grand Bahama Island and also slowed in forward speed due to a weakness in the subtropical ridge to its north. Parts of\n       <a href="/wiki/South_Florida" title="South Florida">\n        South Florida\n       </a>\n       began to be affected by\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Squalls" title="Squalls">\n        squalls\n       </a>\n       and the outer\n       <a href="/wiki/Rainband" title="Rainband">\n        rainbands\n       </a>\n       of the hurricane at this time. Gusts from 40 miles per hour (64\xc2\xa0km/h) to as high as 87 miles per hour (140\xc2\xa0km/h) were reported from Jupiter Inlet to Miami.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_13-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Frances moved slowly, between 5 to 10 miles per hour (8.0 to 16.1\xc2\xa0km/h), as it crossed the warm\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Stream" title="Gulf Stream">\n        Gulf Stream\n       </a>\n       between the Bahamas and Florida, leading to the concern that it could restrengthen. However, Frances remained stable at Category\xc2\xa02 intensity with 105 miles per hour (169\xc2\xa0km/h)\n       <a href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_wind" title="Maximum sustained wind">\n        maximum sustained winds\n       </a>\n       while it battered the east coast of Florida between\n       <a href="/wiki/Fort_Pierce,_Florida" title="Fort Pierce, Florida">\n        Fort Pierce\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/West_Palm_Beach,_Florida" title="West Palm Beach, Florida">\n        West Palm Beach\n       </a>\n       for much of September 4. At 11\xc2\xa0pm, the western edge of Frances\' eyewall began moving onshore. Because of Frances\' large eye, which was roughly 80 miles (130\xc2\xa0km) across, and its slow forward motion, the center of circulation remained offshore for several more hours. At 1\xc2\xa0am EDT on September 5 (0500 UTC), the center of the broad eye of Frances made\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">\n        landfall\n       </a>\n       along the Florida coast, at the southern end of\n       <a href="/wiki/Hutchinson_Island_(Florida)" title="Hutchinson Island (Florida)">\n        Hutchinson Island\n       </a>\n       , near\n       <a href="/wiki/Sewall%27s_Point,_Florida" title="Sewall\'s Point, Florida">\n        Sewall\'s Point\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Jensen_Beach,_Florida" title="Jensen Beach, Florida">\n        Jensen Beach\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Port_Salerno,_Florida" title="Port Salerno, Florida">\n        Port Salerno, Florida\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_13-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Late on September 5, Frances picked up speed due to a strengthening high pressure system to its north and crossed the\n       <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">\n        Florida Peninsula\n       </a>\n       , emerging over the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">\n        Gulf of Mexico\n       </a>\n       near\n       <a href="/wiki/Tampa,_Florida" title="Tampa, Florida">\n        Tampa\n       </a>\n       as a tropical storm. After a short trip over the Gulf of Mexico, Frances made a second landfall near\n       <a href="/wiki/St._Marks,_Florida" title="St. Marks, Florida">\n        St. Marks, Florida\n       </a>\n       . Frances headed inland, weakening to a tropical depression and causing heavy rainfall over the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Southern_states" title="U.S. Southern states">\n        southern\n       </a>\n       and eastern United States. As Tropical Depression Frances turned northeast,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_13-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">\n        United States\n       </a>\n       meteorologists at the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hydrometeorological_Prediction_Center" title="Hydrometeorological Prediction Center">\n        Hydrometeorological Prediction Center\n       </a>\n       continued issuing advisories on the system until it crossed the\n       <a href="/wiki/Canada%E2%80%93United_States_border" title="Canada\xe2\x80\x93United States border">\n        Canada\xe2\x80\x93United States border\n       </a>\n       into\n       <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">\n        Quebec\n       </a>\n       , where heavy rainfall also fell.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">\n        <a href="#cite_note-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">\n        Preparations\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n Frances at 12:57 am EDT, 5 September.\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       A tropical storm watch was issued for Frances for the Leeward Islands during the afternoon of August 29, which was upgraded to a warning that night and expanded to include the islands of\n       <a href="/wiki/Anguilla" title="Anguilla">\n        Anguilla\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Antigua" title="Antigua">\n        Antigua\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Barbuda" title="Barbuda">\n        Barbuda\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Nevis" title="Nevis">\n        Nevis\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Saba" title="Saba">\n        Saba\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Saint_Kitts" title="Saint Kitts">\n        Saint Kitts\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Sint_Eustatius" title="Sint Eustatius">\n        Sint Eustatius\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Sint_Maarten" title="Sint Maarten">\n        Sint Maarten\n       </a>\n       . A hurricane watch was issued during the night of August 29 for the northern\n       <a href="/wiki/British_Virgin_Islands" title="British Virgin Islands">\n        British Virgin Islands\n       </a>\n       , the northern\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Virgin_Islands" title="United States Virgin Islands">\n        United States Virgin Islands\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Culebra,_Puerto_Rico" title="Culebra, Puerto Rico">\n        Culebra\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Vieques" title="Vieques">\n        Vieques\n       </a>\n       . On the morning of August 30, the hurricane watch for Vieques was downgraded to a tropical storm watch. That afternoon, hurricane watches were changed to tropical storm warnings across Puerto Rico, Culebra, Vieques, British Virgin Islands, and the northern U. S. Virgin Islands while a tropical storm watch was issued for St. Croix while all remaining hurricane watches were dropped. That night, tropical storm watches were issued for eastern portions of the northern coast of the Dominican Republic while a tropical storm warning was issued for Guadeloupe.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_13-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Early on the morning of August 31, tropical storm warnings were dropped for Antigua, Barbuda, Nevis, and St. Kitts while hurricane watches were issued for the southeast Bahamas as well as the\n       <a href="/wiki/Turks_and_Caicos_Islands" title="Turks and Caicos Islands">\n        Turks and Caicos Islands\n       </a>\n       . Hurricane watches were upgraded to hurricane warnings later that morning. Toward noon, tropical storm warnings were issued for the remainder of the northern coast of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">\n        Dominican Republic\n       </a>\n       , a hurricane watch was issued for the central Bahamas, while all watches and warnings were dropped for northeast portion of the Dominican Republic and portions of the Lesser Antilles south of the British Virgin Islands. That afternoon, tropical storm warnings were dropped from Puerto Rico eastward. On the morning of September 1, a hurricane watch was issued for the northwest Bahamas while the watch for the central Bahamas was upgraded to a warning. That afternoon, warnings were dropped for the Dominican Republic.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_13-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Frances_05_sept_2004_1815Z.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="6800" data-file-width="5200" height="288" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0a/Hurricane_Frances_05_sept_2004_1815Z.jpg/220px-Hurricane_Frances_05_sept_2004_1815Z.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0a/Hurricane_Frances_05_sept_2004_1815Z.jpg/330px-Hurricane_Frances_05_sept_2004_1815Z.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0a/Hurricane_Frances_05_sept_2004_1815Z.jpg/440px-Hurricane_Frances_05_sept_2004_1815Z.jpg 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Frances_05_sept_2004_1815Z.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Hurricane Frances making landfall on\n         <a href="/wiki/Hutchinson_Island_(Florida)" title="Hutchinson Island (Florida)">\n          Hutchinson Island\n         </a>\n         near\n         <a href="/wiki/Port_St._Lucie,_Florida" title="Port St. Lucie, Florida">\n          Port St. Lucie, Florida\n         </a>\n         , on September 5\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       On the evening of September 1, hurricane warnings were issued for the northwest Bahamas while hurricane watches were issued for the lower east coast of Florida and tropical storm watches were issued for the Florida Keys. There was the potential for catastrophic damage along Florida\'s heavily populated east coast, with warnings that damages from Frances could exceed the insured losses of\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Andrew" title="Hurricane Andrew">\n        Hurricane Andrew\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">\n        <a href="#cite_note-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       These damage estimates were in anticipation that Frances would strike Florida as a strong Category Four hurricane. Preparations for the storm were stepped up in Florida on September 1. Governor\n       <a href="/wiki/Jeb_Bush" title="Jeb Bush">\n        Jeb Bush\n       </a>\n       declared a\n       <a href="/wiki/State_of_emergency" title="State of emergency">\n        state of emergency\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">\n        <a href="#cite_note-19">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy_Space_Center" title="John F. Kennedy Space Center">\n        Kennedy Space Center\n       </a>\n       closed down,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">\n        <a href="#cite_note-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and evacuations of 500,000\xc2\xa0people were initially ordered. Eventually 41 counties received evacuation orders, covering 2.8\xc2\xa0million residents, the largest evacuation in Florida\'s history.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">\n        <a href="#cite_note-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The state education system also responded to the pending crisis. Many universities across Florida canceled classes. Both the\n       <a href="/wiki/University_of_Central_Florida" title="University of Central Florida">\n        University of Central Florida\n       </a>\n       and the\n       <a href="/wiki/University_of_North_Florida" title="University of North Florida">\n        University of North Florida\n       </a>\n       told all students to leave their dorms. Evacuation at the\n       <a href="/wiki/University_of_South_Florida" title="University of South Florida">\n        University of South Florida\n       </a>\n       was performed on a dorm-by-dorm basis.\n       <a href="/wiki/Florida_Atlantic_University" title="Florida Atlantic University">\n        Florida Atlantic University\n       </a>\n       was closed for a week and a half. Most schools were shut down from southern\n       <a href="/wiki/Miami-Dade_County,_Florida" title="Miami-Dade County, Florida">\n        Miami-Dade County\n       </a>\n       to just south of\n       <a href="/wiki/Melbourne,_Florida" title="Melbourne, Florida">\n        Melbourne\n       </a>\n       two days before the hurricane. The annual\n       <a href="/wiki/Florida_State_University" title="Florida State University">\n        Florida State University\n       </a>\n       -\n       <a href="/wiki/University_of_Miami" title="University of Miami">\n        University of Miami\n       </a>\n       college football game was rescheduled for the following week.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">\n        <a href="#cite_note-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Early during the morning of September 2, hurricane watches were extended southward to Craig Key. Later that morning, hurricane watches were upgraded to hurricane warnings for the lower east coast of Florida while a hurricane watch and tropical storm warning was raised for most of the Florida Keys and Florida Bay. Hurricane warnings were dropped for the Turks and Caicos Islands late on the morning of September 1 and for the Southeast Bahamas late that night. Late on the morning of September 3, hurricane watches were issued for the northeast coast of Florida, while early that afternoon tropical storm warnings were issued for the same area. Hurricane warnings were dropped for the central Bahamas that afternoon. That night, tropical storm warnings were issued for the southwest coast of the Florida peninsula with watches issued for the northwest Florida peninsula. On the morning of September 4, tropical storm warnings were extended northward to\n       <a href="/wiki/Anna_Maria_Island" title="Anna Maria Island">\n        Anna Maria Island\n       </a>\n       and along the\n       <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">\n        Georgia\n       </a>\n       coast. Tropical storm watches were extended northward to\n       <a href="/wiki/St._Marks,_Florida" title="St. Marks, Florida">\n        St. Marks, Florida\n       </a>\n       . That afternoon, hurricane watches were dropped for most of the northwest Bahamas while warnings were extended up the coast to St. Marks, and watches were extended westward to\n       <a href="/wiki/Panama_City,_Florida" title="Panama City, Florida">\n        Panama City, Florida\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_13-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Early on morning of September 5, hurricane warnings were downgraded to tropical storm warnings south of\n       <a href="/wiki/Deerfield_Beach,_Florida" title="Deerfield Beach, Florida">\n        Deerfield Beach, Florida\n       </a>\n       , while tropical storm warnings were extended westward through the western Florida Keys. Later that morning, hurricane warnings were issued for most of the northwest Florida coast while hurricane watches were lowered for northeast Florida and hurricane warnings were dropped for the remainder of the northwest Bahamas. That afternoon, all warnings were dropped for southeast Florida south of\n       <a href="/wiki/Jupiter,_Florida" title="Jupiter, Florida">\n        Jupiter Inlet\n       </a>\n       , while the remaining hurricane warnings along the east Florida coast were downgraded to tropical storm warnings. Hurricane warnings along the coast of western Florida were extended southward to Anna Maria Island. Late that night, tropical storm warnings were dropped south of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bonita_Beach,_Florida" title="Bonita Beach, Florida">\n        Bonita Beach\n       </a>\n       including all the Florida Keys. On September 4, teams from the Ministry of Works, the Department of Environmental Health Services, the Royal Bahamas Defence Force and the Royal Bahamas Police Force were used to clear roadways of tree debris while utilities attempted to restore power and water in New Providence. The Family Islands were surveyed on September 5 and 6, when it was noted that major damage occurred within the island group. Telecommunications were restored to most of the island chain within 24 hours. As of September 21, electricity had been restored to half of the Bahamians who had lost power. Western sections of Grand Bahama Island remained without power into Hurricane Jeanne, which was the most significantly impacted island. The onion crop was expected to be late in 2005 due to the loss of seedbeds and seedlings during the storm.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bahamasaftermath_30-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-bahamasaftermath-30">\n         [30]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Florida_2">\n        Florida\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Frances&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Florida">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:20040908-12_florida1-4-515h.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="343" data-file-width="515" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/20040908-12_florida1-4-515h.jpg/220px-20040908-12_florida1-4-515h.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/20040908-12_florida1-4-515h.jpg/330px-20040908-12_florida1-4-515h.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/20040908-12_florida1-4-515h.jpg/440px-20040908-12_florida1-4-515h.jpg 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:20040908-12_florida1-4-515h.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         President\n         <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Pierce, Florida">\n          Ft. Pierce, Florida\n         </a>\n         .\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       In the aftermath of the storm, many colleges and school districts across Florida remained closed. President\n       <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">\n        George W. Bush\n       </a>\n       declared all of Florida a federal disaster area.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47">\n        <a href="#cite_note-47">\n         [47]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Kennedy Space Center did not restore its complete work force until September 13 due to relief operations, as well as a lack of gasoline, ice, and water in the area.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48">\n        <a href="#cite_note-48">\n         [48]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A total of 8000 members of the\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Guard_of_the_United_States" title="National Guard of the United States">\n        National Guard\n       </a>\n       helped out with recovery efforts soon after the storm left the Florida peninsula. Residents in the areas of impact after the storm were under a boil water order, because of the lack of electricity to area water systems.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-newshour_36-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-newshour-36">\n         [36]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Churches prepared meals for those without power and food themselves.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-49">\n        <a href="#cite_note-49">\n         [49]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Federal employees were granted excused absences if they helped with law enforcement and the cleanup.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50">\n        <a href="#cite_note-50">\n         [50]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Thousands of portable generators were sent to the state by\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Home_Depot" title="Home Depot">\n        Home Depot\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Lowe%27s" title="Lowe\'s">\n        Lowe\'s\n       </a>\n       home improvement stores.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51">\n        <a href="#cite_note-51">\n         [51]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Corps_of_Engineers" title="United States Army Corps of Engineers">\n        United States Army Corps of Engineers\n       </a>\n       installed blue\n       <a href="/wiki/Tarpaulin" title="Tarpaulin">\n        tarpaulins\n       </a>\n       on 41,556 damaged roofs statewide.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52">\n        <a href="#cite_note-52">\n         [52]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Damage to the Florida citrus crop caused orange futures to rise four cents a pound due to Frances.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53">\n        <a href="#cite_note-53">\n         [53]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Georgia_and_the_Carolinas">\n        Georgia and the Carolinas\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Frances&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Georgia and the Carolinas">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       On September 24, the southern two-thirds of Georgia was declared a disaster by President Bush.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54">\n        <a href="#cite_note-54">\n         [54]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The state lost 50\xc2\xa0percent of its\n       <a href="/wiki/Pecan" title="Pecan">\n        pecan\n       </a>\n       crop due to Frances, which led to a price rise in pecans by late October.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55">\n        <a href="#cite_note-55">\n         [55]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Lost peach trees were expected to lower output during 2005, and increase peach prices.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56">\n        <a href="#cite_note-56">\n         [56]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A disaster declaration was made for the northeast section of South Carolina on October 7.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57">\n        <a href="#cite_note-57">\n         [57]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On September 10, President Bush declared 34 counties within North Carolina a disaster area, making them eligible for US$6.5\xc2\xa0million (2004 dollars) in public assistance. A Wildlife Commission removed its\n       <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina_Mountain_State_Fair" title="North Carolina Mountain State Fair">\n        North Carolina Mountain State Fair\n       </a>\n       exhibit due to Frances midway through the fair. Over 100,000 trout were lost due to the storm in the Pisgah, Table Rock, Marion, and Armstrong state fish hatcheries.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-58">\n        <a href="#cite_note-58">\n         [58]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Red Cross volunteers distributed over 200,000 gallons of water by its 600 volunteers in four days. After Frances and\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Ivan" title="Hurricane Ivan">\n        Hurricane Ivan\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Asheville,_North_Carolina" title="Asheville, North Carolina">\n        Asheville\n       </a>\n       determined that it needed $14\xc2\xa0million in order to buy out willing businesses and homes within the floodplain.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59">\n        <a href="#cite_note-59">\n         [59]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Retirement">\n        Retirement\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Frances&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Retirement">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       See also:\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricane names">\n        List of retired Atlantic hurricane names\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Because of its effects in the\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">\n        United States\n       </a>\n       , the name Frances was retired in the spring of 2005 by the\n       <a href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organization" title="World Meteorological Organization">\n        World Meteorological Organization\n       </a>\n       , and will never again be used for an\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_tropical_cyclone" title="North Atlantic tropical cyclone">\n        Atlantic hurricane\n       </a>\n       . It was replaced with\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_Storm_Fiona_(2010)" title="Tropical Storm Fiona (2010)">\n        Fiona\n       </a>\n       for the 2010 season. Due to a request by France in the 2003 WMO Meeting, the name Frances was to be removed from the rotating cyclone lists after the 2004 hurricane season, even if Frances wasn\'t as destructive,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60">\n        <a href="#cite_note-60">\n         [60]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       but the destruction caused by Frances in the United States was enough to warrant retirement on its own merit.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">\n        See also\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span
