   Hurricane Michelle\n       </b>\n       was the fifth costliest\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        tropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       in Cuban history and strongest of the\n       <a href="/wiki/2001_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2001 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        2001 Atlantic hurricane season\n       </a>\n       . The thirteenth\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_naming" title="Tropical cyclone naming">\n        named storm\n       </a>\n       and seventh hurricane that year, Michelle developed from a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">\n        tropical wave\n       </a>\n       that had traversed into the western\n       <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">\n        Caribbean Sea\n       </a>\n       on October\xc2\xa029; the wave had initially moved off the coast of Africa 13\xc2\xa0days prior. In its early\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclogenesis" title="Tropical cyclogenesis">\n        developmental stages\n       </a>\n       , the depression meandered over\n       <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">\n        Nicaragua\n       </a>\n       , later paralleling the\n       <a href="/wiki/Mosquito_Coast" title="Mosquito Coast">\n        Mosquito Coast\n       </a>\n       before intensifying into\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_storm" title="Tropical storm">\n        tropical storm\n       </a>\n       intensity on November\xc2\xa01; Michelle was upgraded to hurricane strength the following day. Shortly after,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" title="Rapid deepening">\n        rapid intensification\n       </a>\n       ensued within favorable conditions, with the storm\'s central\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barometric_pressure" title="Barometric pressure">\n        barometric pressure\n       </a>\n       dropping 51\xc2\xa0mbar (hPa; 1.51\xc2\xa0inHg) in 29\xc2\xa0hours. After a slight fluctuation in strength, Michelle reached its peak intensity as a\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_Category_4_Atlantic_hurricanes" title="List of Category 4 Atlantic hurricanes">\n        Category\xc2\xa04 hurricane\n       </a>\n       with winds of 140\xc2\xa0mph (225\xc2\xa0km/h) and a minimum pressure of 933\xc2\xa0mbar (hPa; 27.55\xc2\xa0inHg).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-1">\n         [nb 1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At roughly the same time, the hurricane began to accelerate northeastward; this brought the intense hurricane to a Cuban\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">\n        landfall\n       </a>\n       within the\n       <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Pigs" title="Bay of Pigs">\n        Bay of Pigs\n       </a>\n       later that day. Crossing over the island, Michelle was weakened significantly, and was only a Category\xc2\xa01 hurricane upon reentry into the Atlantic Ocean. The hurricane later transitioned into an\n       <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">\n        extratropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       over\n       <a href="/wiki/The_Bahamas" title="The Bahamas">\n        The Bahamas\n       </a>\n       on November\xc2\xa05, before being absorbed by a\n       <a href="/wiki/Cold_front" title="Cold front">\n        cold front\n       </a>\n       the following day.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Hurricane Michelle caused widespread devastation across the western Caribbean during its eight-day trek. As a tropical wave, torrential rainfall occurred across\n       <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">\n        Jamaica\n       </a>\n       , causing mudslides and killing two people. Damage in the island country was estimated at $18\xc2\xa0million.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [nb 2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       When the storm drifted over areas of\n       <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">\n        Central America\n       </a>\n       early in its existence, heavy rains isolated numerous villages and damaged infrastructure, affecting areas affected by\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Mitch" title="Hurricane Mitch">\n        Hurricane Mitch\n       </a>\n       roughly three years prior. In\n       <a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">\n        Honduras\n       </a>\n       and Nicaragua, 14\xc2\xa0people were killed, with an additional 62\xc2\xa0people unaccounted for. In the\n       <a href="/wiki/Cayman_Islands" title="Cayman Islands">\n        Cayman Islands\n       </a>\n       , areas were affected by strong\n       <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">\n        storm surge\n       </a>\n       and flooding, particularly in\n       <a href="/wiki/Grand_Cayman" title="Grand Cayman">\n        Grand Cayman\n       </a>\n       , where damage was totaled $28\xc2\xa0million.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The majority of damage caused by Michelle occurred in Cuba, where the storm was the strongest tropical cyclone to make landfall on the island in over\n       <a href="/wiki/1952_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1952 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        49\xc2\xa0years\n       </a>\n       . Strong storm surge and heavy rainfall disrupted communication networks across the country. High winds and rain also destroyed buildings and damaged the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sugar_cane" title="Sugar cane">\n        sugar cane\n       </a>\n       crop. At the time, Michelle was the costliest hurricane in Cuban history with an estimated $1.8\xc2\xa0billion in damage; this figure was greatly surpassed by\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Ike" title="Hurricane Ike">\n        Hurricane Ike\n       </a>\n       nearly seven years later. As a weakening system, Michelle moved past\n       <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">\n        Florida\n       </a>\n       and the Bahamas. Strong waves caused severe\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beach_erosion" title="Beach erosion">\n        beach erosion\n       </a>\n       and winds damaged property. Throughout the entirety of Michelle\'s track, 22\xc2\xa0people were killed, and damage was estimated at roughly $2\xc2\xa0billion (2001 USD; $2.71\xc2\xa0billion 2017\xc2\xa0USD). After the season, the name Michelle was\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricane names">\n        retired\n       </a>\n       and was replaced with Melissa for the\n       <a href="/wiki/2007_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2007 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        2007 Atlantic hurricane season\n       </a>\n       .\n </li>\n       </ul>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Meteorological_history">\n        Meteorological history\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Michelle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Michelle_2001_track.png">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/22/Michelle_2001_track.png/220px-Michelle_2001_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/22/Michelle_2001_track.png/330px-Michelle_2001_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/22/Michelle_2001_track.png/440px-Michelle_2001_track.png 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Michelle_2001_track.png" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Map plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the\n         <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">\n          Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale\n         </a>\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       On October\xc2\xa016, 2001, a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">\n        tropical wave\n       </a>\n       moved westward off the coast of Africa and moved into the Atlantic Ocean. At the time, the wave produced minimal convective and thunderstorm activity. On October\xc2\xa023, the disturbance crossed the\n       <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">\n        Lesser Antilles\n       </a>\n       before moving into the Caribbean Sea while showing minimal signs of\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclogenesis" title="Tropical cyclogenesis">\n        tropical development\n       </a>\n       . Three days later, the wave moved into the western Caribbean, spawning a broad\n       <a href="/wiki/Low-pressure_area" title="Low-pressure area">\n        low-pressure area\n       </a>\n       off the coast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">\n        Nicaragua\n       </a>\n       . At 1800\n       <a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">\n        UTC\n       </a>\n       on October\xc2\xa029,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-3">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hunter" title="Hurricane Hunter">\n        reconnaissance flight\n       </a>\n       confirmed the development of a tropical depression 40\xc2\xa0mi (60\xc2\xa0km) south-southwest of\n       <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Cabezas" title="Puerto Cabezas">\n        Puerto Cabezas, Nicaragua\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ADV1_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ADV1-4">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At the time, convection was displaced primarily to the north of the center of circulation, and the storm was situated in a weak steering pattern; as such, initial forecasts predicted for the depression, designated as\n       <i>\n        Tropical Depression Fifteen\n       </i>\n       , to slowly move generally northward.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DISC1_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-DISC1-5">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Later that day, a burst of convection occurred over the circulation center of the depression, indicating an increase in organization.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DISC2_6-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-DISC2-6">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The disturbance meandered over eastern Nicaragua for 36\xc2\xa0hours before emerging into the Caribbean Sea near\n       <a href="/wiki/Cabo_Gracias_a_Dios" title="Cabo Gracias a Dios">\n        Cabo Gracias a Dios\n       </a>\n       by 2100\xc2\xa0UTC on October\xc2\xa031.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_3-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-3">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DISC9_7-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-DISC9-7">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Upon emerging into the Caribbean Sea, the depression quickly organized, and the\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">\n        National Hurricane Center\n       </a>\n       (NHC) upgraded the depression to\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_storm" title="Tropical storm">\n        tropical storm\n       </a>\n       strength at 0000\xc2\xa0UTC the following day, based on information from a reconnaissance flight prior. As such, the tropical storm was designated with the name\n       <i>\n        Michelle\n       </i>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DISC10_8-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-DISC10-8">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_9-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-9">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Gradual strengthening occurred throughout November\xc2\xa01, as Michelle\'s cloud tops began to cool as it drifted northward, signifying strengthening. At the time, the NHC predicted that there was a roughly 12\xc2\xa0percent chance for the tropical storm to\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" title="Rapid deepening">\n        rapidly intensify\n       </a>\n       ; by that time the storm had met three of the organization\'s five criterion for rapid intensification.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DISC11_10-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-DISC11-10">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       During the day, fluctuations in convective activity occurred and there were some signs of\n       <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">\n        wind shear\n       </a>\n       affecting the cyclone, but there was a general strengthening trend.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DISC14_11-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-DISC14-11">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Two\n       <a href="/wiki/Dropsonde" title="Dropsonde">\n        dropsondes\n       </a>\n       deployed within the system early on November\xc2\xa02 observed\n       <a href="/wiki/Beaufort_scale" title="Beaufort scale">\n        hurricane-force\n       </a>\n       winds within Michelle; based on this data the NHC upgraded Michelle to hurricane intensity at 1200\xc2\xa0UTC that day,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_9-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-9">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       while the storm was situated 290\xc2\xa0mi (460\xc2\xa0km) south-southeast of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Guanahacabibes_Peninsula" title="Guanahacabibes Peninsula">\n        Guanahacabibes Peninsula\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ADV15A_12-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ADV15A-12">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Michelle_5_Nov_2001_1515z.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="765" data-file-width="1035" height="163" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Hurricane_Michelle_5_Nov_2001_1515z.jpg/220px-Hurricane_Michelle_5_Nov_2001_1515z.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Hurricane_Michelle_5_Nov_2001_1515z.jpg/330px-Hurricane_Michelle_5_Nov_2001_1515z.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/Hurricane_Michelle_5_Nov_2001_1515z.jpg/440px-Hurricane_Michelle_5_Nov_2001_1515z.jpg 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Michelle_5_Nov_2001_1515z.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Hurricane Michelle undergoing\n         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extratropical_transition" title="Extratropical transition">\n          extratropical transition\n         </a>\n         over the Bahamas on November\xc2\xa05\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       A period of rapid intensification began shortly after as Michelle\'s satellite presentation improved, with the cyclone\'s minimum\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barometric_pressure" title="Barometric pressure">\n        barometric pressure\n       </a>\n       falling 51\xc2\xa0mbar (hPa; 1.51\xc2\xa0inHg) in 29\xc2\xa0hours.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_3-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-3">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At 0000\xc2\xa0UTC on November\xc2\xa03, the hurricane attained Category\xc2\xa02 hurricane intensity. At roughly the same time, the hurricane\'s\n       <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">\n        eye\n       </a>\n       became more apparent on satellite imagery.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DISC18_13-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-DISC18-13">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       By 1200\xc2\xa0UTC later that day, Michelle had intensified to a\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_Category_4_Atlantic_hurricanes" title="List of Category 4 Atlantic hurricanes">\n        Category\xc2\xa04 hurricane\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_9-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-9">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Six hours later, the hurricane weakened to Category\xc2\xa03 intensity, but reached its minimum barometric pressure of 934\xc2\xa0mbar (hPa; 27.58\xc2\xa0inHg). Afterwards, Michelle\'s central pressure began to rise; despite the trend, its\n       <a href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_wind" title="Maximum sustained wind">\n        maximum sustained winds\n       </a>\n       also strengthened as it curved northeastward.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_9-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-9">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Despite having its convective organization disrupted and its eye no longer as apparent on November\xc2\xa04,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DISC14-2_14-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-DISC14-2-14">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Michelle attained winds of 140\xc2\xa0mph (220\xc2\xa0km/h) by 0600\xc2\xa0UTC that day; these would be the highest sustained winds estimated for the system throughout its existence.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_9-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-9">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       At 1800\xc2\xa0UTC on November\xc2\xa04, Michelle made\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">\n        landfall\n       </a>\n       on\n       <a href="/wiki/Cayo_Largo_del_Sur" title="Cayo Largo del Sur">\n        Cayo Largo del Sur\n       </a>\n       as a Category\xc2\xa04 hurricane with winds of 140\xc2\xa0mph (220\xc2\xa0km/h); this was followed shortly after with a landfall on the\n       <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Pigs" title="Bay of Pigs">\n        Bay of Pigs\n       </a>\n       five hours later as a slightly weaker storm. When Michelle made landfall on the Cuban mainland, it was the strongest landfalling hurricane in Cuban history since the landfall of\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Fox_(1952)" title="Hurricane Fox (1952)">\n        Hurricane Fox\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/1952_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1952 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1952\n       </a>\n       . At the time, the hurricane\'s minimum barometric pressure was 950\xc2\xa0mbar (hPa; 28.06\xc2\xa0inHg). Over Cuba, Michelle\'s center of circulation was significantly disrupted, and as a result the hurricane decreased in intensity;\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_3-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-3">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       by the time it had emerged into the Atlantic Ocean on November\xc2\xa05, winds were only estimated at 90\xc2\xa0mph (150\xc2\xa0km/h).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_9-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-9">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Southwesterly wind shear further inhibited any additional tropical development, and as such Michelle began to transition into an\n       <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">\n        extratropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DISC27_15-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-DISC27-15">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       That day, the weakening hurricane made landfalls over\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Andros_Island" title="Andros Island">\n        Andros Island\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eleuthera_Island" title="Eleuthera Island">\n        Eleuthera Island\n       </a>\n       before becoming fully extratropical by 0000\xc2\xa0UTC on November\xc2\xa06, while still maintaining hurricane-force winds. The extratropical system persisted for roughly 18\xc2\xa0more hours before it was absorbed by an approaching\n       <a href="/wiki/Cold_front" title="Cold front">\n        cold front\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_3-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-3">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">\n        Preparations\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Michelle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Cuba">\n        Cuba\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Michelle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cuba">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Michelle_Warnings_4_Nov_2001_Adv_25.GIF">\n         <img alt="Map showing areas under the effect of tropical cyclone watches and warnings as denoted by highlighted borders. <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Michelle_Warnings_4_Nov_2001_Adv_25.GIF" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Warnings and watches associated with Hurricane Michelle as it made landfall on Cuba on November\xc2\xa04\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       As Hurricane Michelle approached the Cuban coast, the government of Cuba issued a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_watch" title="Hurricane watch">\n        hurricane watch\n       </a>\n       for the western Cuban\n       <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Cuba" title="Provinces of Cuba">\n        provinces\n       </a>\n       of\n       <a href="/wiki/Pinar_del_R%C3%ADo_Province" title="Pinar del R\xc3\xado Province">\n        Pinar del R\xc3\xado\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/La_Habana_Province" title="La Habana Province">\n        La Habana\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Matanzas_Province" title="Matanzas Province">\n        Matanzas\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Isla_de_la_Juventud" title="Isla de la Juventud">\n        Isla de la Juventud\n       </a>\n       at 2100\xc2\xa0UTC on November\xc2\xa01.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FCST13_16-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-FCST13-16">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The following day, the same hurricane watch was extended eastward to include all provinces from Pinar del R\xc3\xado to\n       <a href="/wiki/Ciego_de_%C3%81vila" title="Ciego de \xc3\x81vila">\n        Ciego de \xc3\x81vila\n       </a>\n       ;\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_3-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-3">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FCST18_17-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-FCST18-17">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       all hurricane watches in Cuba were upgraded to\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_warning" title="Hurricane warning">\n        hurricane warnings\n       </a>\n       at 1100\xc2\xa0UTC on November\xc2\xa03.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FCST19_18-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-FCST19-18">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       All warnings in Cuba were discontinued once Michelle began to accelerate out to sea at 1200\xc2\xa0UTC on November\xc2\xa05.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_3-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-3">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Prior to reaching the coast, Michelle was expected to be the strongest Cuban tropical cyclone in at least 75\xc2\xa0years. The\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_Red_Cross_and_Red_Crescent_Societies" title="List of Red Cross and Red Crescent Societies">\n        Cuban Red Cross\n       </a>\n       deployed 24,500\xc2\xa0volunteers across Cuba to assist civil defense teams in evacuation procedures for potentially affected populations. By November\xc2\xa04, an estimated 590,000\xc2\xa0people were evacuated across the country, including 80\xc2\xa0percent of the population of the province of Pinar del R\xc3\xado.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-590K_19-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-590K-19">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Havana,_Cuba" title="Havana, Cuba">\n        Havana\n       </a>\n       , authorities evacuated 150,000\xc2\xa0people. Coastal tourist resorts were cleared, and 35,500\xc2\xa0students were evacuated out of camps hosted on the\n       <a href="/wiki/Zapata_Peninsula" title="Zapata Peninsula">\n        Zapata Peninsula\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ThousandsEvacuatedMonster_20-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ThousandsEvacuatedMonster-20">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On Cuba\'s southwestern islands including\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cayo_Largo" title="Cayo Largo">\n        Cayo Largo\n       </a>\n       and Isla de la Juventud, 500\xc2\xa0tourists were evacuated. The entire population of\n       <a href="/wiki/Bataban%C3%B3,_Cuba" title="Bataban\xc3\xb3, Cuba">\n        Surgidero de Bataban\xc3\xb3\n       </a>\n       was evacuated.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BracesForImpact_21-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-BracesForImpact-21">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In addition to the evacuations, the government sent food, water, and medicine supplies to areas south of the capital. All domestic and international flights to and from the country were cancelled.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ThousandsEvacuatedMonster_20-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ThousandsEvacuatedMonster-20">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Elsewhere">\n        Elsewhere\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Michelle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Elsewhere">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       At 1700\xc2\xa0UTC on November\xc2\xa01, a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_storm_watch" title="Tropical storm watch">\n        tropical storm watch\n       </a>\n       was issued for\n       <a href="/wiki/Grand_Cayman" title="Grand Cayman">\n        Grand Cayman Island\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_3-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-3">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FCST18_17-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-FCST18-17">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Three hours prior, the National Hurricane Center issued a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_storm_warning" title="Tropical storm warning">\n        tropical storm warning\n       </a>\n       in conjunction with a hurricane watch for areas of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Florida_Keys" title="Florida Keys">\n        Florida Keys\n       </a>\n       from\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ocean_Reef,_Florida" title="Ocean Reef, Florida">\n        Ocean Reef, Florida\n       </a>\n       to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Dry_Tortugas" title="Dry Tortugas">\n        Dry Tortugas\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FCST19_18-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-FCST19-18">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At 0300\xc2\xa0UTC on November\xc2\xa04, a hurricane watch was issued for areas of the northwestern and central Bahamas. At the same time, a new tropical storm warning was issued for the east coast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">\n        Florida\n       </a>\n       between\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jupiter_Inlet" title="Jupiter Inlet">\n        Jupiter Inlet\n       </a>\n       and Ocean Reef, and for the state\'s western coast south of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bonita_Beach,_Florida" title="Bonita Beach, Florida">\n        Bonita Beach\n       </a>\n       . At 0600\xc2\xa0UTC, the tropical storm watch for Grand Cayman was upgraded and extended to include the entirety of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Cayman_Islands" title="Cayman Islands">\n        Cayman Islands\n       </a>\n       . Six hours later, the hurricane watch for the Florida Keys was upgraded to warning status. Early the next day, a hurricane watch was issued for\n       <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">\n        Bermuda\n       </a>\n       after Michelle made its final Cuban landfall. Meanwhile, the tropical storm warning issued for the Cayman Islands was discontinued. As Michelle accelerated away from landmasses, warnings and watches were discontinued. All tropical cyclone warnings in the United States were discontinued by 2100\xc2\xa0UTC that day. At the same time, however, the hurricane watch for Bermuda was changed to a tropical storm warning; this warning was sustained for a day before being discontinued, shortly after all warnings in the <p>\n       Hurricane Michelle brought torrential rains along its path through the western Caribbean Sea, killing 22 people and causing extensive damage in\n       <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">\n        Central America\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">\n        Cuba\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Jamaica">\n        Jamaica\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Michelle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Jamaica">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       As the precursor\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">\n        tropical wave\n       </a>\n       to Michelle drifted through the western Caribbean Sea, it produced torrential rainfall on the island of\n       <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">\n        Jamaica\n       </a>\n       , causing widespread flooding.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_3-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-3">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Several residences on\n       <a href="/wiki/Annotto_Bay" title="Annotto Bay">\n        Annotto Bay\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Port_Maria" title="Port Maria">\n        Port Maria\n       </a>\n       were flooded. As a result, 163\xc2\xa0evacuees sought shelter in locations in Annotto Bay. In\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Portland,_Jamaica" title="Portland, Jamaica">\n        Portland\n       </a>\n       , a shelter was opened for effected populations;\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FFJamaica7_23-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-FFJamaica7-23">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       in the same parish, six homes were swept away from their foundations.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FFJamaica9_24-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-FFJamaica9-24">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In the parishes of\n       <a href="/wiki/Saint_Catherine_Parish" title="Saint Catherine Parish">\n        Saint Catherine\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Saint_Andrew_Parish,_Jamaica" title="Saint Andrew Parish, Jamaica">\n        Saint Andrew\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Saint_Ann_Parish" title="Saint Ann Parish">\n        Saint Ann\n       </a>\n       , several roads were blocked by debris. The Jamaican National Works Agency\xc2\xa0(NWA) worked to clear the road debris.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FFJamaica7_23-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-FFJamaica7-23">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The blocked roads isolated seven Jamaican villages for an extended period of time.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FFJamaica10_25-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-FFJamaica10-25">\n         [23]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       After tropical cyclogenesis, Michelle passed to the northwest of Jamaica, producing additional rainfall. In a 48-hour period beginning on October\xc2\xa029, a location measured 41.65 inches (1,058\xc2\xa0mm), denoting an average rainfall rate of 0.86 inches (22\xc2\xa0mm) per hour.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uwi_26-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-uwi-26">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Many other locations reported high rainfall totals over a ten-day period throughout Michelle\'s passage near Jamaica.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_3-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-3">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Near Spur Tree Hill, an\n       <a href="/wiki/Oil_spill" title="Oil spill">\n        oil spill\n       </a>\n       resulted from the flooding. Roads that were previously cleaned of debris a few days prior were covered again by the additional rainfall. The rains also disrupted the country\'s communication and electrical grid, leaving 6,000\xc2\xa0homes without power in affected regions. The widespread\n       <a href="/wiki/Power_outage" title="Power outage">\n        power outages\n       </a>\n       in conjunction with invasive floodwaters also caused the cessation of 31\xc2\xa0water plants run by the National Water Commission. Overall, 30\xc2\xa0homes were destroyed by flooding caused by Michelle as it passed to the northwest. Extensive damage was reported to small livestock, bananas, plantains, vegetables, and coffee.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FFJamaicaSR1_27-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-FFJamaicaSR1-27">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Coffee crops were severely affected in Portland and Saint Andrew Parishes, with losses calculated at\n       <a href="/wiki/Jamaican_dollar" title="Jamaican dollar">\n        J$\n       </a>\n       102.6\xc2\xa0million (US$2.2\xc2\xa0million). Roughly 400\xc2\xa0ac (200\xc2\xa0ha) of banana plantations were damaged over four parishes, amounting to J$18.2\xc2\xa0million (US$400,000) in damage. The plantain crop, mostly grown for domestic usage, suffered extensive damage valued at J$22\xc2\xa0million (US$480,000). Losses sustained to livestock were estimated at J$30.8\xc2\xa0million (US$670,000).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JamaicaReport_28-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-JamaicaReport-28">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Overall, floods caused widespread damage throughout Jamaica. The watersheds of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Spanish_River_(Jamaica)" title="Spanish River (Jamaica)">\n        Spanish\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Swift_River_(Jamaica)" title="Swift River (Jamaica)">\n        Swift Rivers\n       </a>\n       were the worst affected areas. Near the coast, the rivers began to overflow due to excess floodwater, eroding beach roads and three major highways. In Portland Parish, the rains caused numerous landslides. In the community of Bybrook alone, it was estimated that roughly 7,000,000\xc2\xa0ft\n       <sup>\n        3\n       </sup>\n       (200,000\xc2\xa0m\n       <sup>\n        3\n       </sup>\n       ) of material was deposited in the village. An assessment of the damage concluded that 500\xc2\xa0homes were destroyed beyond repair, while an additional 561\xc2\xa0homes were at least damaged by the floods and heavy rain. While one school was damaged solely by the floods, six other schools were damaged due to their use as shelters.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JamaicaReport_28-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-JamaicaReport-28">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Damage on Jamaica due to Michelle totaled $18\xc2\xa0million,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2001Floods_29-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2001Floods-29">\n         [27]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       of which $11.8\xc2\xa0million was due to crop and livestock losses. Damage to bridges alone totaled J$143.7\xc2\xa0million (US$3.1\xc2\xa0million). Five fatalities were also confirmed,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JamaicaReport_28-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-JamaicaReport-28">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and 340\xc2\xa0people were displaced from their homes.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2001Floods_29-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2001Floods-29">\n         [27]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Central_America">\n        Central America\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Michelle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Central America">\n       Tropical Depression Fifteen affecting\n         <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">\n          Central America\n         </a>\n         on October\xc2\xa030\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       In its early developmental stages as a tropical depression, Michelle drifted over areas of\n       <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">\n        Central America\n       </a>\n       for roughly 36\xc2\xa0hours, dropping torrential rainfall and causing widespread flooding in areas affected by\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Mitch" title="Hurricane Mitch">\n        Hurricane Mitch\n       </a>\n       almost exactly three years prior. The hardest hit region was\n       <a href="/wiki/Gracias_a_Dios_Department" title="Gracias a Dios Department">\n        Gracias a Dios Department\n       </a>\n       in northeastern Honduras, where at one point 100\xc2\xa0villages were isolated by the floods. The torrential rainfall caused the displacement of at least 100,000\xc2\xa0people from their residences.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_3-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-3">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Across Central America, over 27,300\xc2\xa0people were evacuated from flooded areas.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EightDead19Missing_30-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-EightDead19Missing-30">\n         [28]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In Honduras, the floods damaged 245\xc2\xa0homes, resulting in the displacement of 4,393\xc2\xa0people across the country. Six bridges sustained at least partial damage, of which three were destroyed.  <a href="/wiki/Departments_of_Honduras" title="Departments of Honduras">\n        departments\n       </a>\n       \xe2\x80\x93\n       <a href="/wiki/Col%C3%B3n_Department_(Honduras)" (department)">\n        Atl\xc3\xa1ntida\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yoro_(department)" title="Yoro (department)">\n        Yoro\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cort%C3%A9s_(department)" title="Cort\xc3\xa9s (department)">\n        Cort\xc3\xa9s\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Santa_B%C3%A1rbara_Department,_Honduras" title="Santa B\xc3\xa1rbara Department, Honduras">\n        Santa B\xc3\xa1rbara\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" exacerbated by a\n       <a href="/wiki/Cold_front" title="Cold front">\n        cold front\n       </a>\n       which lingered over the region, dropping additional rainfall.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EightDead19Missing_30-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-EightDead19Missing-30">\n         [28]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Of the affected departments, Yoro was the most affected, with no supply of drinkable water and electricity following the flooding.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OCHA1Honduras_32-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-OCHA1Honduras-32">\n         [30]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Near Yoro, a bridge crossing the\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cuyamapa_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cuyamapa River (page does not exist)">\n        Cuyamapa River\n       </a>\n       collapsed due to the floodwaters.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UMCORHotline_33-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-UMCORHotline-33">\n         [31]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Despite the heavy rainfall, areas of Honduras suffering from a prolonged drought did not receive beneficial rainfall.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WFPODM31_34-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-WFPODM31-34">\n         [32]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Overall, the floods in Honduras killed 21\xc2\xa0people and caused $5\xc2\xa0million in damage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EMDAT_35-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-EMDAT-35">\n         [33]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Thirty-five of the country\'s water systems were destroyed. The rains also caused the loss of 70\xc2\xa0percent of bean crops.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TripleMitch_36-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TripleMitch-36">\n         [34]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Although the precursor to Michelle remained stationary primarily over Nicaragua, damage was not as significant as in Honduras due to the storm\'s displacement of convection to the north.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_3-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-3">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Nonetheless, floods still isolated villages and other communities in the Nicaraguan districts of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bonanza,_Regi%C3%B3n_Aut%C3%B3noma_del_Atl%C3%A1ntico_Norte" title="Bonanza, Regi\xc3\xb3n Aut\xc3\xb3noma del Atl\xc3\xa1ntico Norte">\n        Bonaza\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rosita,_Regi%C3%B3n_Aut%C3%B3noma_del_Atl%C3%A1ntico_Norte" title="Rosita, Regi\xc3\xb3n Aut\xc3\xb3noma del Atl\xc3\xa1ntico Norte">\n        Rosita\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Siuna" title="Siuna">\n        Siuna\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Cabezas" title="Puerto Cabezas">\n        Puerto Cabezas\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Waspam" title="Waspam">\n        Waspam\n       </a>\n       , and to a lesser extent,\n       <a href="/wiki/Prinzapolka" title="Prinzapolka">\n        Prinzapolka\n       </a>\n       . In those areas, roads and bridges were damaged, and in some cases, destroyed. The heavy rains also damaged crops.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OCHA1Honduras_32-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-OCHA1Honduras-32">\n         [30]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Due to the rains, an estimated 6,000\xc2\xa0people evacuated from their homes. The port city of Puerto Cabezas was partially flooded.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ARCMichelleReport_37-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ARCMichelleReport-37">\n         [35]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       As a result of Michelle, a total of 16\xc2\xa0deaths were confirmed, and damage in the country was estimated at $1\xc2\xa0million.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EMDAT_35-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-EMDAT-35">\n         [33]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In Costa Rica, Michelle caused anomalously high rainfall, resulting in flooding.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CostaRicaFlood_38-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CostaRicaFlood-38">\n         [36]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Overall, damage in Costa Rica totaled $1\xc2\xa0million, though no deaths were reported.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EMDAT_35-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-EMDAT-35">\n         [33]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Cayman_Islands">\n        Cayman Islands\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Michelle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Cayman Islands">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       In early November, Hurricane Michelle brought heavy surf,\n       <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">\n        storm surge\n       </a>\n       , and flooding to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Cayman_Islands" title="Cayman Islands">\n        Cayman Islands\n       </a>\n       . </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">\n        Cuba\n       </a>\n       , about 750,000 people and 741,000 animals were evacuated prior to the hurricane\'s arrival.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cuba_46-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-cuba-46">\n         [44]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Hurricane Michelle quickly crossed the island as a Category\xc2\xa04 hurricane, the strongest since 1952\'s Hurricane Fox. To the south of Cuba,\n       <a href="/wiki/Cayo_Largo_del_Sur" title="Cayo Largo del Sur">\n        Cayo Largo del Sur\n       </a>\n       received a 9\xe2\x80\x9310\xc2\xa0foot storm surge, inundating the entire island with water. Closer to Cuba, the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Isle_of_Youth" title="Isle of Youth">\n        Isle of Youth\n       </a>\n       experienced 11.83 inches (300\xc2\xa0mm) of rain with 15-foot (4.6\xc2\xa0m) waves, causing extensive power outages and flooding.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_3-15">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-3">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       On the coast of western and southern Cuba, Michelle produced 4\xe2\x80\x935\xc2\xa0foot waves, along with a heavy storm surge. Rainfall amounts up to 754\xc2\xa0mm/29.69\xc2\xa0inches were recorded across the island.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CUBARAIN_44-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CUBARAIN-44">\n         [42]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In addition, 300\xc2\xa0mm/11.83\xc2\xa0inches was reported at Punta del Este. The provinces of\n       <a href="/wiki/Matanzas" title="Matanzas">\n        Matanzas\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Villa_Clara_Province" title="Villa Clara Province">\n        Villa Clara\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Cienfuegos" title="Cienfuegos">\n        Cienfuegos\n       </a>\n       were hardest hit, where 10,000 homes were destroyed and another 100,000 damaged. Severe damage was also reported to the sugar cane\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_3-16">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-3">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and in the tourist town of\n       <a href="/wiki/Varadero" title="Varadero">\n        Varadero\n       </a>\n       . In\n       <a href="/wiki/Havana" title="Havana">\n        Havana\n       </a>\n       , winds and rain destroyed 23 buildings, with many others damaged. Due to well-executed warnings and evacuations, only 5 people were killed in Cuba.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cuba_46-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-cuba-46">\n         [44]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The Category\xc2\xa04 hurricane caused\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US$" title="US$">\n        US$\n       </a>\n       1.8\xc2\xa0billion in damage.\n Florida\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Florida">\n        Florida\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Michelle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Florida">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Severe beach erosion due to increased swells over several days took place from Hollywood Beach to Hallandale Beach. At high tide on November\xc2\xa04, water topped the seawall, damaging it and nearby structures leaving $20,000 in damages. The cost to restore the beaches was estimated at $10\xc2\xa0million.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56">\n        <a href="#cite_note-56">\n         [54]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The highest winds in relation to the storm were recorded in\n       <a href="/wiki/Sombrero_Key" title="Sombrero Key">\n        Sombrero Key\n       </a>\n       ; sustained winds reached 49\xc2\xa0mph (79\xc2\xa0km/h) and gusts reached 60\xc2\xa0mph (95\xc2\xa0km/h).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57">\n        <a href="#cite_note-57">\n         [55]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A\n       <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">\n        storm surge\n       </a>\n       of 1 to 2\xc2\xa0ft (0.30 to 0.61\xc2\xa0m) was recorded along the southeast coastline of Florida. Damages from the hurricane amounted to $50,000.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-damage_FL_NCDC_58-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-damage_FL_NCDC-58">\n         [56]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Two tornadoes touched down in the state within the\n       <a href="/wiki/Rainband" title="Rainband">\n        outer bands\n       </a>\n       of the hurricane.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-damage_FL_NCDC_58-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-damage_FL_NCDC-58">\n         [56]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The first formed as a\n       <a href="/wiki/Waterspout" title="Waterspout">\n        waterspout\n       </a>\n       and moved onshore near Bill Baggs Beach and destroyed two Chikee huts, costing $6,000, warranting F0 intensity, before dissipating.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59">\n        <a href="#cite_note-59">\n         [57]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The second and stronger of the two tornadoes, rated F1, tracked for 2\xc2\xa0mi (3.2\xc2\xa0km) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Palm_Beach_County,_Florida" title="Palm Beach County, Florida">\n        Palm Beach County\n       </a>\n       . Windows were blown out of vehicles and buildings, trees and signs were downed and a small area of sugar cane was leveled by the tornado. Damages from the tornado amounted to $10,000.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60">\n        <a href="#cite_note-60">\n         [58]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Oceanic_and_Atmospheric_Administration" title="National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration">\n        NOAA\n       </a>\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/WP-3D_Orion" title="WP-3D Orion">\n        WP-3D Orion\n       </a>\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hunters" title="Hurricane Hunters">\n        hurricane hunter\n       </a>\n       aircraft was damaged during a mission into the storm.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_3-17">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-3">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Bahamas">\n        Bahamas\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Michelle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Bahamas">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Because Hurricane Michelle was weakened and moving rapidly as it moved through the Bahamas, no deaths or damage were reported. The hurricane still retained some of its strength, and caused 12.64 inches (321\xc2\xa0mm) of rain in\n       <a href="/wiki/Nassau,_Bahamas" title="Nassau, Bahamas">\n        Nassau\n       </a>\n       , while\n       <a href="/wiki/New_Providence" title="New Providence">\n        New Providence\n       </a>\n       received a storm surge of 5\xe2\x80\x938\xc2\xa0feet.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_3-18">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-3">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Flooding was reported throughout the archipelago and high winds downed numerous trees, resulting in 200,000 power outages. A few homes sustained roof damage and the roof a shopping center in Nassau was blown into a nearby funeral home.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61">\n        <a href="#cite_note-61">\n         [59]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The radio tower of the station MORE FM was snapped in half by high winds, resulting in a severe disruption of radio broadcasts.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62">\n        <a href="#cite_note-62">\n         [60]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">\n        Aftermath\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Michelle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Aftermath">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Ships carrying tons of frozen chicken legs and corn arrived in Havana\'s harbor on December 16, to aid in the recovery of Cuba from Hurricane Michelle. The shipments are part of a one-time $30\xc2\xa0million purchase of U.S. food by the Cuban government to assist in the country\'s present food shortage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reliefaid_63-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-reliefaid-63">\n         [61]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The Cuban government refused an initial U.S. offer of humanitarian assistance, but accepted a proposal to purchase the goods instead. Although the United States has maintained an economic embargo on Cuba for decades, U.S. law does allow shipments of food and medicine to the communist-controlled island. But until now the Cuban government has refused to buy American food because of the restrictions, including a ban on direct U.S. financing of food sales.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reliefaid_63-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-reliefaid-63">\n         [61]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Other aid from the international community is helping Cuba survive Michelle\'s aftermath. Cuba was promised $600,000 in aid from Chinese leader Li Peng, who was visiting when the hurricane struck. Venezuela also sent humanitarian assistance.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reliefaid_63-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-reliefaid-63">\n         [61]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Retirement">\n        Retirement\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Michelle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Retirement">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       See also:\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricane names">\n        List of retired Atlantic hurricane names\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Because of its effects, the name\n       <b>\n        Michelle\n       </b>\n       was retired in the spring of 2002 and will never again be used for an\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_tropical_cyclone" title="North Atlantic tropical cyclone">\n        Atlantic hurricane\n       </a>\n       . The name was replaced with\n       <b>\n        Melissa\n       </b>\n       in the\n       <a href="/wiki/2007_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2007 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        2007 season\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">\n        See also\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span
