International Space Station\n       </a>\n       (ISS). </a>\n         </div>\n         Canadarm2 removing unpressurised payload from HTV-2.\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The HTV is about 9.8\xc2\xa0m long (including maneuvering thrusters at one end) and 4.4\xc2\xa0m in diameter. Total mass when empty is 10.5 tonnes (11.6 short tons), with a maximum total payload of 6,000 kilograms (13,000\xc2\xa0lb; 6.0\xc2\xa0t; 6.6 short tons), for a maximum launch weight of 16.5 tonnes (18.2 short tons).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jaxa2007_1-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-jaxa2007-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The HTV is comparable in function to the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_Space_Agency" title="Russian Space Agency">\n        Russian\n       </a>\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Progress_spacecraft" title="Progress spacecraft">\n        Progress\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/European_Space_Agency" title="European Space Agency">\n        European\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Automated_Transfer_Vehicle" title="Automated Transfer Vehicle">\n        ATV\n       </a>\n       , commercial\n       <a href="/wiki/SpaceX_Dragon" title="SpaceX Dragon">\n        Dragon\n       </a>\n       , and commercial\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cygnus_spacecraft" title="Cygnus spacecraft">\n        Cygnus\n       </a>\n       spacecraft, all of which bring or are planned to bring supplies to the ISS. Like the ATV, the HTV carries more than twice the payload of the Progress, but is launched less than half as often. Unlike Progress capsules and ATVs, which dock automatically, HTVs and American\n       <a href="/wiki/Commercial_Orbital_Transportation_Services#Commercial_Resupply_Services" title="Commercial Orbital Transportation Services">\n        commercial spacecraft\n       </a>\n       approach the ISS in stages, and are signaled by ISS crew or ground control to continue from one holding point to the next. Once they reach their closest parking orbit to the ISS, crew\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grapple_Fixture" title="Grapple Fixture">\n        grapple\n       </a>\n       them using the robotic arm\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadarm2" title="Canadarm2">\n        Canadarm2\n       </a>\n       and berth them to an open berthing port on the\n       <a href="/wiki/Harmony_(ISS_module)" title="Harmony (ISS module)">\n        <i>\n         Harmony\n        </i>\n       </a>\n       module.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The HTV has an external payload bay which is accessed by robotic arm after it has been berthed to the ISS. New payloads can be moved directly from the HTV to\n       <i>\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kib%C5%8D" title="Kib\xc5\x8d">\n         Kib\xc5\x8d\n        </a>\n        \'\n       </i>\n       s exposed facility. Internally, it has eight\n       <a href="/wiki/International_Standard_Payload_Rack" title="International Standard Payload Rack">\n        International Standard Payload Racks\n       </a>\n       (ISPRs) in total which can be unloaded by the crew in a\n       <a href="/wiki/Shirt-sleeve_environment" title="Shirt-sleeve environment">\n        shirt-sleeve environment\n       </a>\n       . After the retirement of\n       <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">\n        NASA\n       </a>\n       \'s\n       <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle" title="Space Shuttle">\n        Space Shuttle\n       </a>\n       in 2011, HTVs became the only spacecraft capable of transporting ISPRs to the ISS. The\n       <a href="/wiki/SpaceX" title="SpaceX">\n        SpaceX\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/SpaceX_Dragon" title="SpaceX Dragon">\n        Dragon\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Orbital_Sciences_Corporation" title="Orbital Sciences Corporation">\n        Orbital Sciences\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Cygnus_(spacecraft)" title="Cygnus (spacecraft)">\n        Cygnus\n       </a>\n       can carry resupply cargo bags but not ISPRs.\n       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">\n        [\n        <i>\n         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">\n          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">\n           citation needed\n          </span>\n         </a>\n        </i>\n        ]\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:HTV-1_close-up_view.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2850" data-file-width="4288" height="146" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/HTV-1_close-up_view.jpg/220px-HTV-1_close-up_view.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/HTV-1_close-up_view.jpg/330px-HTV-1_close-up_view.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/HTV-1_close-up_view.jpg/440px-HTV-1_close-up_view.jpg 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:HTV-1_close-up_view.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         The four main thrusters. Smaller attitude control thrusters can be seen at the right side of this view of HTV-1.\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The intention of HTV\'s modularized design was to use different module configuration to match the mission requirement.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-miki2010_10-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-miki2010-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       However, to reduce the development cost it was decided to fly the mixed PLC/ULC configuration only.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-miki2010_10-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-miki2010-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       To control the HTV\'s attitude and to perform the orbital maneuvers such as rendezvous and re-entry, the craft has four 500 N class main thrusters and twenty-eight 110 N class attitude control thrusters. Both use bipropellant, namely\n       <a href="/wiki/Monomethylhydrazine" title="Monomethylhydrazine">\n        monomethylhydrazine\n       </a>\n       (MMH) as fuel and\n       <a (rocket engine)">\n        HBT-5\n       </a>\n       thrusters and 120 N class HBT-1 thrusters made by Japanese manufacturer\n       <a href="/wiki/IHI_Corporation" title="IHI Corporation">\n        IHI Aerospace Co., Ltd\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The HTV carries about 2400\xc2\xa0kg of propellant in four tanks.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IAC_11-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-IAC-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=H-II_Transfer_Vehicle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Planned successor">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="HTV-X">\n        HTV-X\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=H-II_Transfer_Vehicle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: HTV-X">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       In May 2015,\n       <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_Education,_Culture,_Sports,_Science_and_Technology" title="Ministry of Education, Culture, Sports, Science and Technology">\n        Ministry of Education, Culture, Sports, Science and Technology\n       </a>\n       announced a proposal to replace HTV with an improved, cost-reduced version preliminary called HTV-X.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mext20150520_13-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mext20150520-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">\n        <a href="#cite_note-24">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Proposed concept of HTV-X as of July 2015\n       <sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;">\n        <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=H-II_Transfer_Vehicle&amp;action=edit">\n         [update]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       is:\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jaxa20150702_25-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-jaxa20150702-25">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <ul>\n       <li>\n        To re-use the design of HTV\'s Pressurized Logistics Carrier (PLC) as much as possible, except adding a side hatch for late access cargo,\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        To replace the Unpressurized Logistics Carrier, Avionics Module, and Propulsion Module with a new Service Module\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Instead of loading the unpressurized cargo inside the spacecraft, load them on top of the Service Module.\n       </li>\n      </ul>\n      <p>\n       Re-using the PLC design will allow minimizing the development cost and risk. Concentrating the Reaction Control System (RCS) and the solar panels to Service Module will allow simplifying the wiring and piping, to reduce the weight and the manufacturing cost. Loading the unpressurized cargo outside the spacecraft allows larger cargo, only limited by the launch vehicle fairing. The aim is to cut the cost in half, while keeping or extending the capability of existing HTV.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jaxa20150702_25-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-jaxa20150702-25">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The simplification of overall structure will allow the launch mass of HTV-X to be dropped to 15.5 t (planned) from HTV\'s 16.5 t, while the maximum weight of cargo will be increased to 7.2 t (net weight 5.85 t excluding support structure weight) from HTV\'s 6.0 t (net 4.0 t).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jaxa20160714_26-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-jaxa20160714-26">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In December 2015, the plan to develop HTV-X was approved by the Strategic Headquarters for Space Policy of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Cabinet_Office_(Japan)" title="Cabinet Office (Japan)">\n        Cabinet Office\n
