   <a href="/wiki/Washington_Metropolitan_Area_Transit_Authority" title="Washington Metropolitan Area Transit Authority">\n        Washington Metropolitan Area Transit Authority\n       </a>\n       (WMATA). Its fleet consists of 1,480 buses covering an area of 1,500 square miles (3,900\xc2\xa0km\n       <sup>\n        2\n       </sup>\n       ) in Washington, D.C.,\n       <a href="/wiki/Maryland" title="Maryland">\n        Maryland\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">\n        Virginia\n       </a>\n       . There are more than 300 bus routes serving 12,216 stops, including 2,398 bus shelters. In fiscal year 2009 Metrobus provided more than 133 million trips.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On a typical weekday, it provides more than 424,000 trips.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-APTA-2015-Q4_3-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-APTA-2015-Q4-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n deduct the correct fare from a rider\'s SmarTrip card (including transfer credit). Formerly, Metrobus issued paper transfers which gave the rider free transfers to any regular bus for two hours. Effective January 4, 2009, when a rider uses a SmarTrip card, they will automatically receive rail-to-bus transfer credit and bus-to-bus free transfer time for regular buses is increased to three hours. Express routes have different requirements. This section discusses the transfer policy to and from Metrobus and other regional bus operators. The routes can be Metrobus routes or routes of other regional operators. Note that as of January 4, 2009, transfer credit is issued exclusively on a SmarTrip card; riders using cash payment cannot obtain a paper transfer and must pay full fare upon boarding the bus. The first batch of 85 NABI diesel-electric hybrid 42-foot buses is already on order, with delivery expected within a year. The new buses will replace some of Metro\xe2\x80\x99s older Orion V buses, which were delivered in 1997 and 2000. Going forward, the contract gives Metro the option to order up to 498 additional 42-foot buses and up to 71 60-foot buses over the five-year period.\n       <i>\n        Red/Silver\n       </i>\n       painted buses will be used on local routes and\n       <i>\n        Blue/Silver\n       </i>\n       buses will be used on Express routes. These buses will have either\n       <i>\n        <b>\n         "Local"\n        </b>\n       </i>\n       or\n       <i>\n        <b>\n         "MetroExtra"\n        </b>\n       </i>\n       on the top of each side of the bus for easy identification.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wmata_7-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-wmata-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The numbering represents its region of operation. To differ the regions numbering system, most Maryland letters is "prefix" to the route number and Virginia letters is "suffix" to the "number" of the route. For example:\n       <b>\n        C22\n       </b>\n       (Maryland) and\n       <b>\n        15L\n       </b>\n       (Virginia). WMATA is adding a total of 533 hybrid buses to replace its diesel bus fleet. Each new "New Flyer Xcelsior XDE40" bus costs $571,737 and is expected to break down less frequently as well as offer greater fuel economy. With the latest purchase of 152 hybrid buses for $89.3 million from New Flyer of America, WMATA\'s Metrobus fleet will consist of 297 diesel buses, 800 hybrid buses and 458 natural gas fueled buses.\n </div>\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        <a href="/wiki/Metroway" title="Metroway">\n         Metroway\n        </a>\n       </b>\n       is a\n       <a href="/wiki/Bus_rapid_transit" title="Bus rapid transit">\n        bus rapid transit\n       </a>\n       (BRT) service that began on August 24, 2014. The first phase is the\n       <a href="/wiki/Crystal_City,_Arlington,_Virginia" title="Crystal City, Arlington, Virginia">\n        Crystal City\n       </a>\n       /\n       <a href="/wiki/Potomac_Yard" title="Potomac Yard">\n        Potomac Yard\n       </a>\n       Transitway, which operates on\n       <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_1_in_Virginia" title="U.S. Route 1 in Virginia">\n        Route 1\n       </a>\n       in Arlington and Alexandria, Virginia. It is a 5-mile corridor with 33 platforms and 20 station located between\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pentagon_City_(WMATA_station)" title="Pentagon City (WMATA station)">\n        Pentagon City\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Braddock_Road_(WMATA_station)" title="Braddock Road (WMATA station)">\n        Braddock Road\n       </a>\n       . The first 0.8 mile segment in Alexandria runs on a transit lane only. The Arlington County segment began construction in the summer of 2014 and opened April 17, 2016. Metroway originally operated between Braddock Road Station and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crystal_City_(WMATA_station)" title="Crystal City (WMATA station)">\n        Crystal City\n       </a>\n       and was expanded to Pentagon City in April 2016. Thirteen 2016 New Flyer Xcelsior XN40 CNG buses (2981-2993) operate with the blue-and-white Metroway livery. The original Metroway fleet consisted of thirteen 2014 NABI 42 BRT diesel-electric hybrid buses (8002-8014) until they were all repainted in December 2016. The Metroway service, which is operated by Metrobus\' Four Mile Run bus division, features dedicated bus lanes, transit signal priority, off-board fare collection, real-time information, boarding at all doors, custom designed shelters and stations, as well as near-level boarding at station platforms. A Metroway fare costs the same as Metrobus, which is\n       <b>\n        $2.00\n       </b>\n       (using cash &amp; SmarTrip).\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline"
