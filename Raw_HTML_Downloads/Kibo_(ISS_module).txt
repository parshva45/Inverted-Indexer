<p>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Components">\n        Components\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span     </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       <i>\n        Kib\xc5\x8d\n       </i>\n       consists of six major elements: 1) Pressurized Module (PM), 2) Exposed Facility (EF), 3) Experiment Logistics Module-Pressurized Section (ELM-PS), 4) Experiment Logistics Module-Exposed Section (ELM-ES), 5) Japanese Experiment Module Remote Manipulator System (JEMRMS), and 6) Inter-orbit Communication System (ICS).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [2]\n     edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner"      </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <i>\n        Pressurized Module\n       </i>\n       (PM) is the core component connected to the port hatch of the Node 2 Module. It is cylindrical in shape and contains twenty-three\n       <a href="/wiki/International_Standard_Payload_Rack" title="International Standard Payload Rack">\n        International Standard Payload Racks\n       </a>\n       (ISPRs), ten of which are dedicated to science experiments while the remaining 13 are dedicated to Kibo\xe2\x80\x99s systems and storage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The racks are placed 6-6-6-5 along the four walls of the module. The end of the JEM-PM has an airlock and two window hatches. The Exposed Facility, Experiment Logistics Module and the Remote Manipulator System all connect to the pressurized module. Kibo is also the location for many of the press conferences that take place on board the station.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Exposed_Facility">\n        Exposed Facility\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/35/Japanese_Experiment_Module_Exposed_Facility_%28ISS050-E-011668%29.jpg/440px-Japanese_Experiment_Module_Exposed_Facility_%28ISS050-E-011668%29.jpg </div>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <i>\n        Exposed Facility\n       </i>\n       (EF), also known as "Terrace", is located outside the port cone of the PM (which is equipped with an airlock). The EF has 12 EFU (Exposed Facility Unit) ports that attach to PIU (Payload Interface Unit) connectors on EF-EEUs (EF-Equipment Exchange Units). All experiment payloads are fully exposed to the space environment. For proper functioning of these experiments, the payload requires an ORU (Orbital Replacement Unit) which consists of the EPS (Electrical Power System), CT (Communications &amp; Tracking) and the TCS (\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thermal_Control_System" title="Thermal Control System">\n        Thermal Control System\n       </a>\n       ). Of the 12 ORUs, eight are replaceable by the JEMRMS while the other four are\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extra-vehicular_activity" title="Extra-vehicular activity">\n        EVA\n       </a>\n       -replaceable.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Experiment_Logistics_Module">\n        Experiment Logistics Module\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kibo_(ISS_module)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit       </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <i>\n        Experiment Logistics Module\n       </i>\n       (ELM) includes two sections:\n      </p>\n      <ul>\n       <li>\n        The Japanese Experiment Logistics Module, Pressurized Section (ELM-PS)\xc2\xa0\xe2\x80\x93- also called the JLP\xc2\xa0\xe2\x80\x93- is a pressurized addition to the PM. The module is a storage facility that provides storage space for experiment payloads, samples and spare items.\n        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n         <a href="#cite_note-4">\n          [4]\n         </a>\n        </sup>\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        The unpressurized (external) section (ELM-ES) serves the EF as a storage and transportation module.\n       </li>\n      </ul>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Remote_Manipulator_System">\n        Remote Manipulator System\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kibo_(ISS_module)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Remote Manipulator System">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <i>\n        Remote Manipulator System\n       </i>\n       (JEMRMS) is a 10m long robotic arm, mounted at the port cone of the PM, intended to service the EF and to move equipment from and to the ELM. The RMS control console was launched while inside the ELM-PS. The main arm was launched with the PM. The "Small Fine Arm", is 2m long and attaches to the end effector of the main arm, was launched aboard.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-5">\n         [5]\n
