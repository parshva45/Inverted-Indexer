The second was to jettison the\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Floating_Potential_Probe&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Floating Potential Probe (page does not exist)">\n        Floating Potential Probe\n       </a>\n       which was a failed instrument, designed to measure the station\'s electrical potential and compare it to the surrounding\n       <a href="/wiki/Plasma_(physics)" title="Plasma (physics)">\n        plasma\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="EVA_2">\n        EVA 2\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_12&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: EVA 2">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       The second spacewalk took place on 3 February 2006 and lasted 5 hours and 43 minutes. The astronauts jettisoned an old Russian\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orlan_space_suits" title="Orlan space suits">\n        Orlan spacesuit\n       </a>\n       , named\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SuitSat-1" title="SuitSat-1">\n        SuitSat-1\n       </a>\n       , that was equipped with a radio for broadcasts to students around the world. The suit reached the end of its operation life in 2004. They also retrieved the Biorisk experiment, photographed a sensor for a micrometeoroid experiment, and tied off the surviving\n       <a href="/wiki/Umbilical_cable" title="Umbilical cable">\n        umbilical\n       </a>\n       of the mobile transporter.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n
