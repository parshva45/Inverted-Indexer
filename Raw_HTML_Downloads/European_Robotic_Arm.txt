Robotic Arm\n       </b>\n       (\n       <b>\n        ERA\n       </b>\n       ) is a\n       <a href="/wiki/Robotic_arm" title="Robotic arm">\n        robotic arm\n       </a>\n       to be Station">\n        International Space Station\n       </a>\n       . The ERA is designed and assembled by Airbus Defence and Space Netherlands (formerly Dutch Space).\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="toc">\n       <div class="toctitle">\n </span>\n         </a>\n        </li>\n       </ul>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Major_features_and_tasks">\n        Major features and tasks\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=European_Robotic_Arm&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Major features and tasks">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       The ERA has several interesting features. Most prominent are its ability to \'walk\' around the exterior of the Russian segments of the station under its own control, moving hand-over-hand between pre-fixed basepoints, and its ability to perform many tasks automatically or semi-automatically, thereby freeing its operators to do other work. Specific tasks of ERA include:\n      </p>\n      <ul>\n       <li>\n        Installation and deployment of\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solar_array" title="Solar array">\n         solar arrays\n        </a>\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Replacement of solar arrays\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Inspection of the station\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Handling of (external) payloads\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Support of\n        <a href="/wiki/Astronaut" title="Astronaut">\n         astronauts\n        </a>\n        during\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Space_walk" title="Space walk">\n         space walks\n        </a>\n       </li>\n      </ul>\n      <p>\n       The International Space Station already features three robotic arms, the first being the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadarm2" title="Canadarm2">\n        Canadarm2\n       </a>\n       , but because the Russian\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grapple_Fixture" title="Grapple Fixture">\n        grapple fixtures\n       </a>\n       are different, that arm and\n       <a href="/wiki/Dextre" title="Dextre">\n        Dextre\n       </a>\n       can only be used on the Zarya module of the Russian segment.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The third arm is fixed on the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_Experiment_Module" title="Japanese Experiment Module">\n        Japanese Experiment Module\n       </a>\n       , the\n       <a href="/wiki/Kibo_(ISS_module)#Remote_Manipulator_System" title="Kibo (ISS module)">\n        Remote Manipulator System\n       </a>\n       (JEM-RMS) uses a similar grapple fixture to Canadarm2. Because all Russian and European spacecraft dock automatically there is no need to manipulate spacecraft on the Russian segment so the European arm is half the length and less powerful than Canadarm2.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Developed for the\n       <a href="/wiki/European_Space_Agency" title="European Space Agency">\n        European Space Agency\n       </a>\n       (ESA) by a number of European space companies, with\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dutch_Space&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dutch Space (page does not exist)">\n        Dutch Space\n       </a>\n       as prime contractor and subcontractors in 8 countries, the robot arm will be launched by a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Proton_(rocket)" title="Proton (rocket)">\n        Proton rocket\n       </a>\n       , along with the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Multipurpose_Laboratory_Module" title="Multipurpose Laboratory Module">\n        MLM\n       </a>\n       , in 2017 (previously planned: 2015) to be put to work in space by the ISS crew. In 2010, a spare elbow joint for the arm was launched preemptively, attached to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Rassvet_(ISS_module)" title="Rassvet (ISS module)">\n        Mini-Research Module 1\n       </a>\n       (MRM-1). The\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Multipurpose_Laboratory_Module" title="Multipurpose Laboratory Module">\n        MLM\n       </a>\n       will also serve as home base for ERA; originally, the arm was going to be attached to the cancelled\n       <a href="/wiki/Science_Power_Platform" title="Science Power Platform">\n        Science Power Platform\n       </a>\n       which would have had eight solar arrays.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span ERA\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       <a href="/wiki/Astronaut" title="Astronaut">\n        Astronauts\n       </a>\n       can control the robot from both inside and
