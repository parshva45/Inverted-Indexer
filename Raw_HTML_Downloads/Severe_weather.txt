Regional and seasonal severe weather phenomena include\n       <a href="/wiki/Blizzard" title="Blizzard">\n        blizzards\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Snowstorm" title="Snowstorm">\n        snowstorms\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ice_storms" title="Ice storms">\n        ice storms\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Duststorm" title="Duststorm">\n        duststorms\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMS1_2-0">\n        <a     </a>\n        </li>\n       </ul>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Terminology">\n        Terminology\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Severe_weather&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Terminology">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Meteorologists generally define\n       <b>\n        severe weather\n       </b>\n       as any aspect of the weather that poses risks to life, property or requires the intervention of authorities. A narrower definition of\n       <i>\n        severe weather\n       </i>\n       is any weather phenomena relating to\n       <a href="/wiki/Thunderstorm" title="Thunderstorm">\n        severe thunderstorms\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMS1_2-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-AMS1-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Glossary2009_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Glossary2009-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       According to the\n       <a href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organization" title="World Meteorological Organization">\n        World Meteorological Organization\n       </a>\n       (WMO), severe weather can be categorized into two groups: general severe weather and localized severe weather.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WMO_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-WMO-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <a href="/wiki/Nor%27easter" title="Nor\'easter">\n        Nor\'easters\n       </a>\n       , European wind storms, and the phenomena that accompany them form over wide geographic areas. These occurrences are classified as\n       <i>\n        general severe weather\n       </i>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WMO_1-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-WMO-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Downbursts and tornadoes are more localized and therefore have a more limited geographic effect. These forms of weather are classified as\n       <i>\n        localized severe weather\n       </i>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WMO_1-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-WMO-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The term\n       <i>\n        severe weather\n       </i>\n       is technically not the same phenomenon as\n       <a href="/wiki/Extreme_weather" title="Extreme weather">\n        extreme weather\n       </a>\n       . Extreme weather describes unusual weather events that are at the extremes of the historical distribution for a given area.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Causes">\n        Causes\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Severe_weather&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Causes">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:CAPE_vs_SHEAR.png">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="456" data-file-width="777" height="129" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6a/CAPE_vs_SHEAR.png/220px-CAPE_vs_SHEAR.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6a/CAPE_vs_SHEAR.png/330px-CAPE_vs_SHEAR.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6a/CAPE_vs_SHEAR.png/440px-CAPE_vs_SHEAR.png 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:CAPE_vs_SHEAR.png" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         This graphic shows the conditions favorable for certain organized thunderstorm complexes, based upon\n         <a href="/wiki/Convective_available_potential_energy" title="Convective available potential energy">\n          CAPE\n         </a>\n         and vertical\n         <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">\n          wind shear\n         </a>\n         values.\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Organized severe weather occurs from the same conditions that generate ordinary thunderstorms: atmospheric moisture, lift (often from\n       <a href="/wiki/Thermal" title="Thermal">\n        thermals\n       </a>\n       ), and\n       <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_instability" title="Atmospheric instability">\n        instability\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SPC_FAQ_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SPC_FAQ-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A wide variety of conditions cause severe weather. Several factors can convert thunderstorms into severe weather. For example, a pool of cold air aloft may aid in the development of large hail from an otherwise innocuous appearing thunderstorm. However, the most severe hail and tornadoes are produced by\n       <a href="/wiki/Supercell" title="Supercell">\n        supercell\n       </a>\n       thunderstorms, and the worst\n       <a href="/wiki/Downburst" title="Downburst">\n        downbursts\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Derecho" title="Derecho">\n        derechos\n       </a>\n       (straight-line winds) are produced by\n       <a href="/wiki/Bow_echo" title="Bow echo">\n        bow echoes\n       </a>\n       . Both of these types of storms tend to form in environments high in\n       <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">\n        wind shear\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SPC_FAQ_5-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SPC_FAQ-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Floods, hurricanes, tornadoes, and thunderstorms are considered to be the most destructive weather-related\n       <a href="/wiki/Natural_disaster" title="Natural disaster">\n        natural disasters\n       </a>\n       . Although these weather phenomena are all related to\n       <a href="/wiki/Cumulonimbus_cloud" title="Cumulonimbus cloud">\n        cumulonimbus clouds\n       </a>\n       , they form and develop under different conditions and geographic locations. The relationship between these weather events and their formation requirements are used to develop\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weather_model" title="Weather model">\n        models\n       </a>\n       to predict the most frequent and possible locations. This information is used to notify affected areas and save lives.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Categories">\n        Categories\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Severe_weather&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Categories">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Comp-rcm.png">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="496" data-file-width="540" height="202" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d0/Comp-rcm.png/220px-Comp-rcm.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d0/Comp-rcm.png/330px-Comp-rcm.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d0/Comp-rcm.png/440px-Comp-rcm.png 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Comp-rcm.png" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Diagram showing ingredients needed for severe weather. The red arrow shows the position of the low level jet stream, while the blue arrow shows the location of the upper level jet stream\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Severe thunderstorms can be assessed in three different categories. These are "approaching severe", "severe", and "significantly severe".\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        Approaching severe\n       </b>\n       is defined as hail between\n       <span class="frac nowrap">\n        <sup>\n         1\n        </sup>\n        \xe2\x81\x84\n        <sub>\n         2\n        </sub>\n       </span>\n       to 1 inch (13 to 25\xc2\xa0mm) diameter or winds between 50 and 58 M.P.H. (50 knots, 80\xe2\x80\x9393\xc2\xa0km/h). In the United States, such storms will usually warrant a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Significant_Weather_Alert" title="Significant Weather Alert">\n        Significant Weather Alert\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        Severe\n       </b>\n       is defined as hail 1 inch (25\xc2\xa0mm) diameter or larger, winds 58 M.P.H (93\xc2\xa0km/h). or stronger, or a tornado.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WhyOne_7-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-WhyOne-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        Significant severe\n       </b>\n       is defined as hail 2 inches (51\xc2\xa0mm) in diameter or larger, winds 75 M.P.H. (65 knots, 120\xc2\xa0km/h) or stronger, a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tornado" title="Tornado">\n        tornado\n       </a>\n       of strength EF2 or stronger, the occurrence of\n       <a href="/wiki/Flash_flood" title="Flash flood">\n        flash flood\n       </a>\n       phenomena by heavy\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Precipitation_(meteorology)" title="Precipitation (meteorology)">\n        precipitation\n       </a>\n       , or extreme temperatures.\n       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">\n        [\n        <i>\n         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources">\n          <span title="Statement needs to be more specific about the content to which it refers. (May 2012)">\n           specify\n          </span>\n         </a>\n        </i>\n        ]\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WMO_1-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-WMO-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OnlineSevere2007_8-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-OnlineSevere2007-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Both\n       <i>\n        severe\n       </i>\n       and\n       <i>\n        significant severe\n       </i>\n       events warrant a\n       <a href="/wiki/Severe_thunderstorm_warning" title="Severe thunderstorm warning">\n        severe thunderstorm warning\n       </a>\n       from the United States\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Weather_Service" title="National Weather Service">\n        National Weather Service\n       </a>\n       (excludes flash floods), the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Environment_Canada" title="Environment Canada">\n        Environment Canada\n       </a>\n       , the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Australian_Bureau_of_Meteorology" title="Australian Bureau of Meteorology">\n        Australian Bureau of Meteorology\n       </a>\n       , or the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meteorological_Service_of_New_Zealand" title="Meteorological Service of New Zealand">\n        Meteorological Service of New Zealand\n       </a>\n       if the event occurs in those countries. If a tornado is occurring (a tornado has been seen by\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weather_spotter" title="Weather spotter">\n        spotters\n       </a>\n       ) or is imminent (\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Doppler_weather_radar" title="Doppler weather radar">\n        Doppler weather radar\n       </a>\n       has observed\n       <a href="/wiki/Tornado_vortex_signature" title="Tornado vortex signature">\n        strong rotation in a storm\n       </a>\n       , indicating an incipient tornado), the severe thunderstorm warning will be superseded by a\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Tornado_warning" title="Tornado warning">\n         tornado warning\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       in the United States and Canada.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spc-faq_9-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-spc-faq-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       A severe weather outbreak is typically considered to be when ten or more tornadoes, some of which will likely be long-tracked and violent, and\n       <i>\n        many\n       </i>\n       large hail or damaging wind reports occur within one or more consecutive days. Severity is also dependent on the size of the geographic area affected, whether it covers hundreds or thousands of square kilometers.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div style="clear:both;">\n      </div>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="High_winds">\n        High winds\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Severe_weather&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: High winds">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main articles:\n       <a href="/wiki/Wind" title="Wind">\n        Wind\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Beaufort_scale" title="Beaufort scale">\n        Beaufort scale\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Thunderstorm_panorama.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="542" data-file-width="2500" height="48" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/Thunderstorm_panorama.jpg/220px-Thunderstorm_panorama.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/Thunderstorm_panorama.jpg/330px-Thunderstorm_panorama.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/Thunderstorm_panorama.jpg/440px-Thunderstorm_panorama.jpg 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Thunderstorm_panorama.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Panorama of a strong\n         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shelf_cloud" title="Shelf cloud">\n          shelf cloud\n         </a>\n         , which can precede the onset of high winds\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       High winds are known to cause damage, depending upon their strength.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Wind speeds as low as 23 knots (43\xc2\xa0km/h) may lead to power outages when tree branches fall and disrupt power lines.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LightningPrinciples2009_11-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-LightningPrinciples2009-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Some species of trees are more vulnerable to winds. Trees with shallow roots are more prone to uproot, and brittle trees such as\n       <a href="/wiki/Eucalyptus" title="Eucalyptus">\n        eucalyptus\n       </a>\n       , sea\n       <a href="/wiki/Hibiscus" title="Hibiscus">\n        hibiscus\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Avocado" title="Avocado">\n        avocado\n       </a>\n       are more prone to branch damage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Derek2006_12-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Derek2006-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Wind gusts may cause poorly designed\n       <a href="/wiki/Suspension_bridge" title="Suspension bridge">\n        suspension bridges\n       </a>\n       to sway. When wind gusts\n       <a href="/wiki/Harmony" title="Harmony">\n        harmonize\n       </a>\n       with the frequency of the swaying bridge, the bridge may fail as occurred with the\n       <a href="/wiki/Tacoma_Narrows_Bridge_(1940)" title="Tacoma Narrows Bridge (1940)">\n        Tacoma Narrows Bridge\n       </a>\n       in 1940.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grazulis2001_13-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Grazulis2001-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Hurricane-force winds, caused by individual thunderstorms, thunderstorm complexes, derechos, tornadoes, extratropical cyclones, or tropical cyclones can destroy mobile homes and structurally damage buildings with foundations. Winds of this strength due to downslope winds off terrain have been known to shatter windows and sandblast paint from cars.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-boulder_14-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-boulder-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Once winds exceed 135 knots (250\xc2\xa0km/h) within strong tropical cyclones and tornadoes, homes completely collapse, and significant damage is done to larger buildings. Total destruction to man-made structures occurs when winds reach 175 knots (324\xc2\xa0km/h). The\n       <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">\n        Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale\n       </a>\n       for cyclones and\n       <a href="/wiki/Enhanced_Fujita_scale" title="Enhanced Fujita scale">\n        Enhanced Fujita scale\n       </a>\n       (\n       <a href="/wiki/TORRO_scale" title="TORRO scale">\n        TORRO scale\n       </a>\n       in Europe) for tornados were developed to help estimate wind speed from the damage they cause.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NHC_SSHS_15-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NHC_SSHS-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EF_SPC_16-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-EF_SPC-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Tornado">\n        Tornado\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Severe_weather&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Tornado">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main article:\n       <a href="/wiki/Tornado" title="Tornado">\n        Tornado\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a    <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:F5_tornado_Elie_Manitoba_2007.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         The F5 tornado that struck\n         <a href="/wiki/Elie,_Manitoba" title="Elie, Manitoba">\n          Elie, Manitoba\n         </a>\n         in 2007.\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       A dangerous rotating column of air in contact with both the surface of the earth and the base of a\n       <a href="/wiki/Cumulonimbus_cloud" title="Cumulonimbus cloud">\n        cumulonimbus cloud\n       </a>\n       (thundercloud) or a\n       <a href="/wiki/Cumulus_cloud" title="Cumulus cloud">\n        cumulus cloud\n       </a>\n       , in rare cases. Tornadoes come in many sizes but typically form a visible\n       <a href="/wiki/Funnel_cloud" title="Funnel cloud">\n        condensation funnel\n       </a>\n       whose narrowest end reaches the earth and surrounded by a cloud of\n       <a href="/wiki/Debris" title="Debris">\n        debris\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Dust" title="Dust">\n        dust\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Renno_17-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Renno-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Tornadoes\' wind speeds generally average between 40 miles per hour (64\xc2\xa0km/h) and 110 miles per hour (180\xc2\xa0km/h). They are approximately 250 feet (76\xc2\xa0m) across and travel a few miles (kilometers) before dissipating. Some attain wind speeds in excess of 300 miles per hour (480\xc2\xa0km/h), may stretch more than two miles (3.2\xc2\xa0km) across, and maintain contact with the ground for dozens of miles (more than 100\xc2\xa0km).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated2_18-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-autogenerated2-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fastest_wind_19-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-fastest_wind-19">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-widest_tornado_20-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-widest_tornado-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Tornadoes, despite being one of the most destructive weather phenomena, are generally short-lived. A long-lived tornado generally lasts no more than an hour, but some have been known to last for 2 hours or longer (for example, the\n       <a href="/wiki/Tri-State_Tornado" title="Tri-State Tornado">\n        Tri-State Tornado\n       </a>\n       ). Due to their relatively short duration, less information is known about the development and formation of tornadoes.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Tornadoes2008_21-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Tornadoes2008-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Downburst_and_derecho">\n        Downburst and derecho\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Severe_weather&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Downburst and derecho">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main articles:\n       <a href="/wiki/Downburst" title="Downburst">\n        Downburst\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Derecho" title="Derecho">\n        Derecho\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Downbursts are created within thunderstorms by significantly rain-cooled air, which, upon reaching ground level, spreads out in all directions and produce strong winds. Unlike winds in a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tornado" title="Tornado">\n        tornado\n       </a>\n       , winds in a downburst are not rotational but are directed outwards from the point where they strike land or water.\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Microburstnasa.JPG">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="496" data-file-width="641" height="170" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/Microburstnasa.JPG/220px-Microburstnasa.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/Microburstnasa.JPG/330px-Microburstnasa.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/Microburstnasa.JPG/440px-Microburstnasa.JPG 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Microburstnasa.JPG" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Illustration of a microburst. The air moves in a downward motion until it hits ground level. It then spreads outward in all directions.\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       "Dry downbursts" are associated with\n       <a href="/wiki/Thunderstorm" title="Thunderstorm">\n        thunderstorms\n       </a>\n       with very little precipitation,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-micro_22-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-micro-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       while\n       <b>\n        wet downbursts\n       </b>\n       are generated by thunderstorms with large amounts of rainfall.\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Microbursts" title="Microbursts">\n        Microbursts\n       </a>\n       are very small downbursts with winds that extend up to 2.5 miles (4\xc2\xa0km) from their source, while\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Macroburst" title="Macroburst">\n        macrobursts\n       </a>\n       are large-scale\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Downbursts" title="Downbursts">\n        downbursts\n       </a>\n       with winds that extend in excess of 2.5 miles (4\xc2\xa0km).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Microburst_23-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Microburst-23">\n         [23]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The\n       <b>\n        heat burst\n       </b>\n       is created by vertical currents on the backside of old\n       <a href="/wiki/Outflow_boundary" title="Outflow boundary">\n        outflow boundaries\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Squall_line" title="Squall line">\n        squall lines\n       </a>\n       where rainfall is lacking. Heat bursts generate significantly higher temperatures due to the lack of rain-cooled air in their formation.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Oklahomaheat1999_24-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Oklahomaheat1999-24">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <b>\n        <a href="/wiki/Derecho" title="Derecho">\n         Derechos\n        </a>\n       </b>\n       are longer, usually stronger, forms of downburst winds characterized by straight-lined windstorms.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-noaa_25-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-noaa-25">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EW_26-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-EW-26">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Downbursts create vertical\n       <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">\n        wind shear\n       </a>\n       or\n       <a href="/wiki/Microburst" title="Microburst">\n        microbursts\n       </a>\n       , which are dangerous to aviation.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-National1992_27-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-National1992-27">\n         [27]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       These\n       <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_convection" title="Atmospheric convection">\n        convective\n       </a>\n       <b>\n        downbursts\n       </b>\n       can produce damaging winds, lasting 5 to 30 minutes, with wind speeds as high as 168\xc2\xa0mph (75\xc2\xa0m/s), and cause tornado-like damage on the ground. Downbursts also occur much more frequently than tornadoes, with ten downburst damage reports for every one tornado.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-columbia_28-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-columbia-28">\n         [28]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Squall_line">\n        Squall line\n       </span>\n  <i>\n        hurricanes\n       </i>\n       in the United States and Canada and\n       <i>\n        typhoons\n       </i>\n       in eastern Asia). A tropical cyclone\'s heavy\n       <a href="/wiki/Breaking_wave" title="Breaking wave">\n        surf\n       </a>\n       created by such winds may cause harm to marine life either close to or upon the surface of the water, such as\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coral_reefs" title="Coral reefs">\n        coral reefs\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dan2004_36-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Dan2004-36">\n         [36]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Coastal regions may receive significant damage from a tropical cyclone while inland regions are relatively safe from the strong winds, due to their rapid dissipation over land. Lakes region.\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Waterspouts are generally defined as tornadoes or non-\n       <a href="/wiki/Supercell" title="Supercell">\n        supercell\n       </a>\n       tornadoes that develop over bodies of water.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Glossary_37-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Glossary-37">\n         [37]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Waterspouts are not known for inflicting much damage because they are not commonly exposed to land, but they are capable of traveling over land. Some waterspouts are known to produce\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        hurricane\n       </a>\n       -strength winds and are capable of producing equivalent damage. Vegetation, weakly constructed buildings, and other infrastructure may be destroyed by waterspouts. Automobiles may be lifted by advancing waterspouts. Heavy precipitation may be noted, developed from the water raised by the wind currents. Waterspouts do not generally last long over terrestrial environments as the friction produced easily dissipates the winds. Strong horizontal winds cause waterspouts to dissipate,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-erh.noaa.gov_38-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-erh.noaa.gov-38">\n         [38]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       destroying the concentration of the updrafts.      (FROS) when burning through dense, uninterrupted fuels.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45">\n        <a href="#cite_note-45">\n         [45]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       They href="#cite_note-Glossary_b-53">\n         [53]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Hailstorms are generally capable of developing in any geographic area where\n       <a class="mw-redirect" </sup>\n       Due to their larger density, these hailstones become heavy enough to overcome the density of the cloud and fall towards the ground. The term "hailstorm" is usually used to describe the existence of significant quantities or size of hailstones.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Hailstones can cause serious damage, notably to\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Automobile" title="Automobile">\n        automobiles\n       </a>\n       , aircraft, skylights, glass-roofed structures,\n       <a href="/wiki/Livestock" title="Livestock">\n        livestock\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crops" title="Crops">\n        crops\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nolanhail_56-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Nolanhail-56">\n         [56]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Rarely, massive hailstones have been known to cause\n       <a href="/wiki/Concussion" title="Concussion">\n        concussions\n       </a>\n       or fatal head\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Physical_trauma" title="Physical trauma">\n        trauma\n       </a>\n       . href="#cite_note-58">\n         [58]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div style="clear:both;">\n      </div>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Kings_Christian_Church_carpark_Flooded.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         A flash flood caused by a severe thunderstorm\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main articles:\n       <a href="/wiki/Rain" title="Rain">\n        Rain\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Flood" title="Flood">\n        Flood\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Flash_flood" title="Flash flood">\n        Flash flood\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Heavy rainfall can lead to a number of hazards, most of which are floods or hazards resulting from floods. Flooding is the inundation of areas that are not normally under water. It is typically divided into three classes: River flooding, which relates to rivers rising outside their normals banks; flash flooding, which is the process where a landscape, often in urban and arid environments, is subjected to rapid floods;\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Glossary2009f_59-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Glossary2009f-59">\n         [59]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and coastal flooding, which can be caused by strong winds from tropical or non-tropical cyclones.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60">\n        <a href="#cite_note-60">\n         [60]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <a href="/wiki/Meteorology" title="Meteorology">\n        Meteorologically\n       </a>\n       , excessive rains occur within a plume of air with high amounts of moisture (also known as an\n       <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_river" title="Atmospheric river">\n        atmospheric river\n       </a>\n       ) which is directed around an upper level\n       <a href="/wiki/Cold-core_low" title="Cold-core low">\n        cold-core low\n       </a>\n       or a tropical cyclone.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Boniface2010_61-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Boniface2010-61">\n         [61]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Flash flooding can frequently occur in slow-moving thunderstorms and are usually caused by the heavy liquid precipitation that accompanies it. Flash floods are most common in dense populated urban environments, where less plants and bodies of water are presented to absorb and contain the extra water. Flash flooding can be hazardous to small infrastructure, such as bridges, and weakly constructed buildings. Plants and crops in agricultural areas can be destroyed and devastated by the force of raging water. Automobiles parked within experiencing areas can also be displaced.\n       <a href="/wiki/Soil" title="Soil">\n        Soil\n       </a>\n       erosion can occur as well, exposing risks of\n       <a href="/wiki/Landslide" title="Landslide">\n        landslide\n       </a>\n       phenomena. Like all forms of flooding phenomenon, flash flooding can also spread and produce\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Waterborne_disease" title="Waterborne disease">\n        waterborne\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Insect-borne_disease" title="Insect-borne disease">\n        insect-borne\n       </a>\n       diseases cause by microorganisms. Flash flooding can be caused by extensive rainfall released by tropical cyclones of any strength or the sudden thawing effect of\n       <a href="/wiki/Ice_dam" title="Ice dam">\n        ice dams\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WeatherEye2007_62-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-WeatherEye2007-62">\n         [62]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-srh.noaa.gov_63-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-srh.noaa.gov-63">\n         [63]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Monsoons">\n        Monsoons\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Severe_weather&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Monsoons">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main article:\n       <a href="/wiki/Monsoon" title="Monsoon">\n        Monsoon\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Seasonal wind shifts lead to long-lasting\n       <a href="/wiki/Wet_season" title="Wet season">\n        wet seasons\n       </a>\n       which produce the bulk of annual\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Precipitation_(meteorology)" title="Precipitation (meteorology)">\n        precipitation\n       </a>\n       in areas such as Southeast Asia, Australia, Western Africa, eastern South America, Mexico, and the Philippines. Widespread flooding occurs if rainfall is excessive,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64">\n        <a href="#cite_note-64">\n         [64]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       which can lead to landslides and mudflows in mountainous areas.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65">\n        <a href="#cite_note-65">\n         [65]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Floods cause rivers to exceed their capacity with nearby buildings becoming submerged.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66">\n        <a href="#cite_note-66">\n         [66]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Flooding may be exacerbated if there are fires during the previous dry season. This may cause soils which are sandy or composed of\n       <a href="/wiki/Loam" title="Loam">\n        loam\n       </a>\n       to become hydrophobic and repel water.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67">\n        <a href="#cite_note-67">\n         [67]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Government organizations help their residents deal with wet-season floods though\n       <a href="/wiki/Floodplain" title="Floodplain">\n        floodplain\n       </a>\n       mapping and information on erosion control. Mapping is conducted to help determine areas that may be more prone to flooding.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68">\n        <a href="#cite_note-68">\n         [68]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Erosion control instructions are provided through outreach over the telephone or the internet.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69">\n        <a href="#cite_note-69">\n         [69]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Flood waters that occur during monsoon seasons can often host numerous\n       <a href="/wiki/Protist" title="Protist">\n        protozoa\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Bacteria" title="Bacteria">\n        bacterial\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Virus" title="Virus">\n        viral\n       </a>\n       microorganisms.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PreventingDisease_70-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-PreventingDisease-70">\n         [70]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Mosquitoes and flies will lay their eggs within the contaminated bodies of water. These disease agents may cause infections of foodborne and waterborne diseases. Diseases associated with exposure to flood waters include\n       <a href="/wiki/Malaria" title="Malaria">\n        malaria\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Cholera" title="Cholera">\n        cholera\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Typhoid" title="Typhoid">\n        typhoid\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Hepatitis_A" title="Hepatitis A">\n        hepatitis A\n       </a>\n       , and the\n       <a href="/wiki/Common_cold" title="Common cold">\n        common cold\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Monsoondiseases2009_71-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Monsoondiseases2009-71">\n         [71]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Possible\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trenchfoot" title="Trenchfoot">\n        trenchfoot\n       </a>\n       infections may also occur when personnel are exposed for extended periods of time within flooded areas.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72">\n        <a href="#cite_note-72">\n         [72]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main article:\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Hurricane_andrew_fema_2563.jpg/330px-Hurricane_andrew_fema_2563.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Hurricane_andrew_fema_2563.jpg/440px-Hurricane_andrew_fema_2563.jpg 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_andrew_fema_2563.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         The damage caused by\n         <a     </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       A tropical cyclone is a storm system characterized by a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Low-pressure_system" title="Low-pressure system">\n        low-pressure\n       </a>\n       center and numerous\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Thunderstorms" title="Thunderstorms">\n        thunderstorms\n       </a>\n       that produce strong winds and flooding rain. A tropical cyclone feeds on heat released when moist air rises, resulting in\n       <a href="/wiki/Condensation" title="Condensation">\n        condensation\n       </a>\n       of\n       <a href="/wiki/Water_vapor" title="Water vapor">\n        water vapor\n       </a>\n       contained in the moist air. Tropical cyclones may produce torrential rain, high waves, and damaging\n       <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">\n        storm surge\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oxfo_73-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-oxfo-73">\n         [73]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Heavy rains produce significant inland flooding. Storm surges may produce extensive coastal flooding up to 40 kilometres (25\xc2\xa0mi) from the coastline.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Although\n       <a href="/wiki/Cyclone" title="Cyclone">\n        cyclones\n       </a>\n       take an enormous toll in lives and personal property, they are also important factors in the precipitation regimes of areas they affect. They bring much-needed precipitation to otherwise dry regions.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2005_EPac_outlook_74-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2005_EPac_outlook-74">\n         [74]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Areas in their path can receive a year\'s worth of rainfall from a tropical cyclone passage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jack2005_75-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Jack2005-75">\n         [75]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Tropical cyclones can also relieve\n       <a href="/wiki/Drought" title="Drought">\n        drought\n       </a>\n       conditions.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-autogenerated1_76-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-autogenerated1-76">\n         [76]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       They also carry heat and energy away from the tropics and transport it toward\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Temperate" title="Temperate">\n        temperate\n       </a>\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latitudes" title="Latitudes">\n        latitudes\n       </a>\n       , which makes them an important part of the global\n       <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_circulation" title="Atmospheric circulation">\n        atmospheric circulation\n       </a>\n       mechanism. As a result, tropical cyclones help to maintain equilibrium in the Earth\'s\n       <a href="/wiki/Troposphere" title="Troposphere">\n        troposphere\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n When extratropical cyclones deposit heavy, wet snow with a snow-water equivalent (SWE) ratio of between 6:1 and 12:1 and a weight in excess of 10 pounds per square foot (~50\xc2\xa0kg/m\n usually associated with strong tropical cyclones.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Historic_Lake_Effect_Snow_Storm_of_October_12.E2.80.9313.2C_2006_78-0">\n        <a    avalanche\n       </a>\n       can occur with a sudden thermal or mechanical impact on snow that has accumulated on a mountain, which causes the snow to rush downhill suddenly. Preceding an avalanche is a phenomenon known as an avalanche wind caused by the approaching avalanche itself, which adds to its destructive potential.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Glossary2009g_79-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Glossary2009g-79">\n         [79]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Large amounts of snow which accumulate on top of man-made structures can lead to structural failure.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gershon2009_80-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Gershon2009-80">\n         [80]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       During snowmelt, acidic precipitation which previously fell in the snow pack is released and harms marine life.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Samuel1995_81-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Samuel1995-81">\n         [81]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Lake-effect snow is produced in the winter in the shape of one or more elongated bands. Nor\'easters can cause heavy rain or snow, either within their comma-head precipitation pattern or along their trailing cold or stationary front. Nor\'easters can occur at any time of the year but are mostly known for their presence in the winter season.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc3_84-0">\n        <a </sup>\n       The accumulations of ice during the storm can be extremely destructive. </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main article:\n       <a href="/wiki/Drought" title="Drought">\n        Drought\n of precipitation).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-drought_88-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-drought-88">\n         [88]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Although droughts do not     </sup>\n       their effects can be just as deadly; in fact, droughts are classified and measured based upon these effects.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-drought_88-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-drought-88">\n         [88]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Droughts have a variety of severe effects; they can cause crops to fail,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-creeping_89-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-creeping-89">\n         [89]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and they can severely deplete water resources, sometimes interfering with the\n       <a href="/wiki/Dust_Bowl" title="Dust Bowl">\n        Dust Bowl\n       </a>\n       affected 50 million acres of farmland in the central United States.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-drought_88-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-drought-88">\n         [88]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In economic terms, they can cost many billions of dollars: a drought in the United States in 1988 caused over $40 billion in losses,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-90">\n        <a href="#cite_note-90">\n         [90]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       exceeding the economic totals of\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Andrew" title="Hurricane Andrew">\n        Hurricane Andrew\n       </a>\n       , the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Great_Mississippi_and_Missouri_Rivers_Flood_of_1993" title="Great Mississippi and Missouri Rivers Flood of 1993">\n        Great Flood of 1993\n       </a>\n       , and the\n       <a href="/wiki/1989_Loma_Prieta_earthquake" title="1989 Loma Prieta earthquake">\n        1989 Loma Prieta earthquake\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-creeping_89-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-creeping-89">\n         [89]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In addition to the other severe effects, the dry conditions caused by droughts also significantly increase the risk of wildfires.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-drought_88-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-drought-88">\n         [88]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div style="clear:both;">\n      </div>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Heat_waves">\n        Heat waves\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Severe_weather&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Heat waves">\n         edit\n Europe in 2003\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Although official definitions vary, a heat wave is generally defined as a prolonged period with excessive heat.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aussie1_91-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-aussie1-91">\n         [91]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Although heat waves do not cause as much economic damage as other types of severe weather, they are extremely dangerous to humans and animals: according to the United States National Weather Service, the average total number of heat-related fatalities each year is higher than the combined total fatalities for floods, tornadoes, lightning strikes, and hurricanes.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-heatwave_92-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-heatwave-92">\n         [92]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In Australia, heat waves cause more fatalities than any other type of severe weather.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aussie1_91-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-aussie1-91">\n         [91]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       As in droughts, plants can also be severely affected by heat waves (which are often accompanied by dry conditions) can cause plants to lose their moisture and die.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aussie2_93-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-aussie2-93">\n         [93]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Heat waves are often more severe when combined with high humidity.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-heatwave_92-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-heatwave-92">\n         [92]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div style="clear:both;">\n      </div>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">\n
