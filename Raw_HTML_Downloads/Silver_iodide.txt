   </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Structure">\n        Structure\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Silver_iodide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Structure">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       The structure adopted by silver iodide is temperature dependent:\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <ul>\n       <li>\n        Below 420\xc2\xa0K, the \xce\xb2-phase of AgI, with the\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wurtzite_(crystal_structure)" title="Wurtzite (crystal structure)">\n         wurtzite\n        </a>\n        structure, is most stable. This phase is encountered in nature as the mineral\n        <a href="/wiki/Iodargyrite" title="Iodargyrite">\n         iodargyrite\n        </a>\n        .\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Above 420\xc2\xa0K, the \xce\xb1-phase becomes more stable. This motif is a\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Body-centered_cubic" title="Body-centered cubic">\n         body-centered cubic\n        </a>\n        structure which has the silver centers distributed randomly between 6 octahedral, 12 tetrahedral and 24 trigonal sites.\n        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n         <a href="#cite_note-3">\n          [3]\n         </a>\n        </sup>\n        At this temperature, Ag\n        <sup>\n         +\n        </sup>\n        ions can move rapidly through the solid, allowing\n        <a href="/wiki/Fast_ion_conductor" title="Fast ion conductor">\n         fast ion conduction\n        </a>\n        . The transition between the \xce\xb2 and \xce\xb1 forms represents the melting of the silver (cation) sublattice. The\n        <a href="/wiki/Entropy_of_fusion" title="Entropy of fusion">\n         entropy of fusion\n        </a>\n        for \xce\xb1-AgI is approximately half that for\n        <a href="/wiki/Sodium_chloride" title="Sodium chloride">\n         sodium chloride\n        </a>\n        (a typical ionic solid). This can be rationalized by considering the AgI crystalline lattice to have already "partly melted" in the transition between \xce\xb1 and \xce\xb2 polymorphs.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        A metastable \xce\xb3-phase also exists below 420\xc2\xa0K with the\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Zincblende_(crystal_structure)" title="Zincblende (crystal structure)">\n         zinc blende structure\n        </a>\n        .\n       </li>\n      </ul>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:242px;">\n        <a class="image" </div>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Preparation_and_properties">\n        Preparation and properties\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Silver_iodide&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparation and properties">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Silver iodide is prepared by reaction of an iodide solution (e.g.,\n       <a href="/wiki/Potassium_iodide" title="Potassium iodide">\n        potassium iodide\n       </a>\n       ) with a solution of silver ions (e.g.,\n       <a href="/wiki/Silver_nitrate" title="Silver nitrate">\n        silver nitrate\n       </a>\n       ). A yellowish solid quickly\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Precipitate" title="Precipitate">\n        precipitates\n       </a>\n       . The solid is a mixture of the two principal phases. Dissolution of the AgI in\n       <a href="/wiki/Hydroiodic_acid" title="Hydroiodic acid">\n        hydroiodic acid\n       </a>\n       , followed by dilution with water precipitates \xce\xb2-AgI. Alternatively, dissolution of AgI in a solution of concentrated silver nitrate followed by dilution affords \xce\xb1-AgI.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       If the preparation is not conducted in the absence of sunlight, the solid darkens rapidly, the light causing the reduction of ionic silver to metallic.
