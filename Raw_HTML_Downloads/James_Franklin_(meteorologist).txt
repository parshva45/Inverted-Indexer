Franklin\n       </b>\n       is a former\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weather_forecaster" title="Weather forecaster">\n        weather forecaster\n       </a>\n       who served with the\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">\n        National Hurricane Center\n       </a>\n       as its branch chief.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       He is a graduate of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts_Institute_of_Technology" title="Massachusetts Institute of Technology">\n        Massachusetts Institute of Technology\n       </a>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and of\n       <a href="/wiki/Ransom_Everglades_School" title="Ransom Everglades School">\n        Ransom Everglades School\n       </a>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       in 1976 in\n       <a href="/wiki/Miami" title="Miami">\n        Miami\n       </a>\n       , where he was a co-\n       <a href="/wiki/Valedictorian" title="Valedictorian">\n        valedictorian\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Franklin liked to add his wit to many of his forecasts, much like his counterpart\n       <a href="/wiki/Lixion_Avila" title="Lixion Avila">\n        Lixion Avila\n       </a>\n       and in contrast with the often dry commentary of other forecasters. For example:\n      </p>\n      <ul>\n       <li>\n        In forecasting\n        <a href="/wiki/2002_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2002 Atlantic hurricane season">\n         2002 Atlantic hurricane season\n        </a>\n        \'s\n        <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Kyle_(2002)" title="Hurricane Kyle (2002)">\n         Hurricane Kyle\n        </a>\n        , Franklin noted "for fun, [a] long-range run of the\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_prediction_model" title="Tropical cyclone prediction model">\n         GFS [model]\n        </a>\n        has Kyle \xe2\x80\x94 its decayed remnants actually \xe2\x80\x94 reaching south\n        <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">\n         Florida\n        </a>\n        just in time for the kickoff of the\n        <a href="/wiki/University_of_Miami" title="University of Miami">\n         Miami\n        </a>\n        /\n        <a href="/wiki/Florida_State_University" title="Florida State University">\n         Florida State\n        </a>\n        <a href="/wiki/College_football" title="College football">\n         game\n        </a>\n        , one week from tomorrow."\n        <sup class="reference"     </a>\n        .\n        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">\n         <a href="#cite_note-5">\n          [5]\n         </a>\n        </sup>\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        When forecasting\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_2005_Atlantic_hurricane_season_storms#Tropical_Storm_Franklin" title="List of 2005 Atlantic hurricane season storms">\n         Tropical Storm Franklin\n        </a>\n        in 2005, Franklin said "Franklin \xe2\x80\x94 the storm, not the forecaster \xe2\x80\x94 has become a little better organized overnight. [...] It is quite possible that little or nothing will be left of Franklin \xe2\x80\x94 the storm, not the forecaster \xe2\x80\x94 in 2\xe2\x80\x933 days."\n        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">\n         <a href="#cite_note-6">\n          [6]\n         </a>\n        </sup>\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        In the Tropical Cyclone Report for\n        <a class="mw-redirect" title="My Fair Lady">\n          My Fair Lady\n         </a>\n        </i>\n        when he quipped "\n        <a href="/wiki/The_Rain_in_Spain" title="The Rain in Spain">\n         The rain in      [7]\n         </a>\n        </sup>\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        In a discussion for\n        <a href="/wiki/Tropical_Storm_Ana_(2009)" title="Tropical Storm Ana (2009)">\n         Tropical
