In contrast,\n       <a href="/wiki/International_Union_of_Pure_and_Applied_Chemistry" title="International Union of Pure and Applied Chemistry">\n        IUPAC\n       </a>\n       recommends the use of 100 kPa as a standard pressure when reporting the properties of substances.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n kilopascal (kPa) as a unit of pressure measurement is widely used throughout the world and has largely replaced the\n       <a href="/wiki/Pounds_per_square_inch" title="Pounds per square inch">\n        pounds per square inch\n       </a>\n       (psi) unit, except in some countries that still use the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Imperial_measurement_system" title="Imperial measurement system">\n        imperial measurement system\n       </a>\n       or the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_customary_system" title="US customary system">\n        US customary system\n       </a>\n       , including the United States.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <a href="/wiki/Geophysics" title="Geophysics">\n        Geophysicists\n       </a>\n       use the gigapascal (GPa) in measuring or calculating tectonic stresses and pressures within the\n       <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">\n        Earth\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Medical\n       <a href="/wiki/Elastography" title="Elastography">\n        elastography\n       </a>\n       measures tissue stiffness non-invasively with\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Medical_ultrasonography" title="Medical ultrasonography">\n        ultrasound\n       </a>\n       or\n       <a href="/wiki/Magnetic_resonance_imaging" title="Magnetic resonance imaging">\n        magnetic resonance imaging\n       </a>\n       , and often displays the\n       <a href="/wiki/Young%27s_modulus" title="Young\'s modulus">\n        Young\'s modulus\n       </a>\n       or\n       <a href="/wiki/Shear_modulus" title="Shear modulus">\n        shear modulus\n       </a>\n       of tissue in kilopascals.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Materials_science" title="Materials science">\n        materials science\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">\n        engineering\n       </a>\n       , the pascal measures the\n       <a href="/wiki/Young%27s_modulus" title="Young\'s modulus">\n        stiffness\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tensile_strength" title="Tensile strength">\n        tensile strength\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Compressive_strength" title="Compressive strength">\n        compressive strength\n       </a>\n       of materials. In engineering use, because the pascal represents a very small quantity, the megapascal (MPa) is the preferred unit for these uses.\n      </p>\n      <table class="wikitable">\n       <caption>\n pascal is also equivalent to the SI unit of\n       <a href="/wiki/Energy_density" title="Energy density">\n        energy density\n       </a>\n       , J/m\n       <sup>\n        3\n       </sup>\n       . This applies not only to the thermodynamics of pressurised gases, but also to the energy density of\n       <a href="/wiki/Electric_potential_energy" title="Electric potential energy">\n        electric\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Magnetic_field#Energy_stored_in_magnetic_fields" title="Magnetic field">\n        magnetic\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Gravitational_energy" title="Gravitational energy">\n        gravitational\n       </a>\n       fields.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In measurements of\n       <a href="/wiki/Sound_pressure" title="Sound pressure">\n        sound pressure\n       </a>\n       or loudness of sound, one pascal is equal to 94 decibels\n       <a href="/wiki/Sound_pressure#Sound_pressure_level" title="Sound pressure">\n        SPL\n       </a>\n       . The quietest sound a human can hear, known as the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Threshold_of_hearing" title="Threshold of hearing">\n        threshold of hearing\n       </a>\n       , is 0\xc2\xa0dB\n       <a href="/wiki/Sound_pressure#Sound_pressure_level" title="Sound pressure">\n        SPL\n       </a>\n       , or 20\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb5Pa.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <a href="/wiki/Blower_door" title="Blower door">\n        The airtightness of buildings\n       </a>\n       is measured at 50\xc2\xa0Pa.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Hectopascal_and_millibar_units">\n        Hectopascal and millibar units\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pascal_(unit)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Hectopascal and millibar units">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main article:\n       <a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">\n        Bar (unit)\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The units of atmospheric pressure commonly used in\n       <a href="/wiki/Meteorology" title="Meteorology">\n        meteorology\n       </a>\n       were formerly the\n       <a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">\n        bar\n       </a>\n       , which was close to the average air pressure on Earth, and the millibar. Since the introduction of\n       <a href="/wiki/International_System_of_Units" title="International System of Units">\n        SI units\n       </a>\n       , meteorologists generally measure pressures in hectopascals (hPa) unit, equal to 100 pascals or 1 millibar.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">\n        <a href="#cite_note-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">\n        <a href="#cite_note-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">\n        <a href="#cite_note-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">\n        <a href="#cite_note-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">\n        <a href="#cite_note-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Exceptions include Canada, which use kilopascals (kPa). In many other fields of science, the SI is preferred, which means Pa with a prefix (in multiples of 1000) is preferred.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">\n        <a href="#cite_note-19">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">\n        <a href="#cite_note-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Many countries also use the millibar or hectopascal to give aviation\n       <a href="/wiki/Altimeter_setting" title="Altimeter setting">\n        altimeter settings\n       </a>\n       . In practically all other fields, the kilopascal (1000 pascals) is used instead.\n       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">\n        [\n        <i>\n         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">\n          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources.
