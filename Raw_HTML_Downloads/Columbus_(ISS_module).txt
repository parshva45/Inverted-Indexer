The functional architecture (including software) of the lab was designed by\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EADS" title="EADS">\n        EADS\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/Bremen" title="Bremen">\n        Bremen\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">\n        Germany\n       </a>\n       where it was also integrated before being flown to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Kennedy_Space_Center" title="Kennedy Space Center">\n        Kennedy Space Center\n       </a>\n       (KSC) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">\n        Florida\n       </a>\n       in an\n       <a href="/wiki/Airbus_Beluga" title="Airbus Beluga">\n        Airbus Beluga\n       </a>\n       .      edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       The laboratory is a cylindrical module with two end cones. It is 4,477\xc2\xa0mm (15\xc2\xa0ft) in external diameter and 6,871\xc2\xa0mm (23\xc2\xa0ft) in overall length, excluding the projecting external experiment racks. Its shape is very similar to that of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Multi-Purpose_Logistics_Module" title="Multi-Purpose Logistics Module">\n        Multi-Purpose Logistics Modules\n       </a>\n       (MPLMs), since both were designed to fit in the cargo bay of a Space Shuttle orbiter. The starboard end cone contains most of the laboratory\'s on-board computers. </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       During cryo-filling of the space shuttle\n on December 6, 2007, two of four LH2 ECO sensors failed a test. Mission rules called for at least three of the four sensors to be in working order for a launch attempt to proceed. As a result of the failure, the Launch Director Doug Lyons postponed the launch, initially for 24 hours. This was later revised into a 72-hour delay, resulting in a next launch attempt set for Sunday December 9, 2007. This launch attempt was scrubbed when one of the ECO sensors again failed during fuelling.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The ECO sensors external connector was changed on the space shuttle external tank, causing a two-month delay in the launch. Columbus was finally launched successfully on the third attempt at 2:45pm EST, February 7, 2008.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-release_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-release-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Once at the station,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadarm2" title="Canadarm2">\n        Canadarm2\n       </a>\n       removed Columbus from the docked shuttle\'s cargo bay and attached it to the starboard hatch of\n       <i>\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Harmony_Module" title="Harmony Module">\n         Harmony\n Four active rack locations are on the forward side of the deck, four on the aft side, and two are in overhead locations. Three of the deck racks are filled with life support and cooling systems. The remaining deck rack and the two remaining overhead racks are storage racks.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In addition, four un-pressurized payload platforms can be attached outside the starboard cone, on the Columbus External Payload Facility (CEPF).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Each <a href="#cite_note-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">\n        History\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Columbus_(ISS_module)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main article:\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Columbus_program" title="Columbus program">\n        Columbus program\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Columbus_logo.svg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="438" data-file-width="412" height="234" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/8/88/Columbus_logo.svg/220px-Columbus_logo.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/8/88/Columbus_logo.svg/330px-Columbus_logo.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/8/88/Columbus_logo.svg/440px-Columbus_logo.svg.png 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Columbus_logo.svg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         The Columbus logo.\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       <i>\n        Columbus\n       </i>\n       was originally planned as part of the Columbus program, an\n       <a href="/wiki/European_Space_Agency" title="European Space Agency">\n        ESA\n       </a>\n       program to develop an autonomous manned\n       <a href="/wiki/Space_station" title="Space station">\n        space station\n       </a>\n       that could be used for a variety of microgravity experiments. The program ran from 1986 to 1991 and included three components: a\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Columbus_Man-Tended_Free_Flyer" title="Columbus Man-Tended Free Flyer">\n         Man-Tended Free Flyer\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       (MTFF) element serviced by the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hermes_(shuttle)" title="Hermes (shuttle)">\n        Hermes\n       </a>\n       shuttle, an\n       <i>\n        Attached Pressurized Module\n       </i>\n       (APM), and a Polar Platform (\n       <i>\n        PPF\n       </i>\n       ). After several budget cuts and cancellation of Hermes shuttle, all that remained in the Columbus program was the APM, finally renamed to\n       <i>\n        Columbus\n       </i>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       When only the APM was left in the program there were not enough tasks for the two main contributors Germany and Italy represented by\n       <a href="/wiki/Messerschmitt-B%C3%B6lkow-Blohm" title="Messerschmitt-B\xc3\xb6lkow-Blohm">\n        MBB\n       </a>\n       -\n       <a href="/wiki/Entwicklungsring_Nord" title="Entwicklungsring Nord">\n        ERNO\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Thales_Alenia_Space" title="Thales Alenia Space">\n        Alenia\n       </a>\n       respectively. As compromise the PICA (Pre Integrated Columbus APM) \xe2\x80\x93 Principle was invented meaning a split responsibility where Alenia as a co-prime is responsible for the overall Columbus configuration, the mechanical and thermal/life support systems, HFE and harness design/manufacturing whereas\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/EADS_Astrium_Space_Transportation" title="EADS Astrium Space Transportation">\n        EADS Astrium Space Transportation\n       </a>\n       is responsible for the overall Columbus design and all Avionics systems including electrical harness design and software.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Splitting off systems engineering responsibility and harness design under separate fixed-price contracts was found not to be advantageous with respect to efficiency and fast decision making as financial reasonings were pre-dominant in the last phase of development and verification.\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The structure used for Columbus is based on the MPLM module built for\n       <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">\n        NASA\n       </a>\n       by Thales Alenia Space. In 2000 the pre-integrated module (structure including harness and tubing) was delivered to Bremen in Germany by the Co-prime contractor Alenia. The final integration and system testing was performed by the overall prime contractor EADS Astrium Space Transportation, after that the initial Payload was integrated and the overall complement checked-out.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       On May 27, 2006 Columbus was flown from\n       <a href="/wiki/Bremen" title="Bremen">\n        Bremen\n       </a>\n       , Germany to\n       <a href="/wiki/Kennedy_Space_Center" title="Kennedy Space Center">\n        Kennedy Space Center\n       </a>\n       on board an Airbus Beluga.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The final schedule was much longer than originally planned due to development problems (several caused by the complex responsibility splitting between the Co-prime and the Overall prime contractor) and design changes introduced by ESA but being affordable due to the Shuttle problems delaying the Columbus launch for several years. The main design change was the addition of the\n       <i>\n        External Payload Facility\n       </i>\n       (EPF), which was driven by the different European Payload organizations being more interested in outer space than internal experiments. Also the addition of a terminal for direct communications to/from ground, which could have been used also as back-up for the\n       <i>\n        ISS\n       </i>\n       system, was studied but not implemented for cost reasons.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Specifications">\n        Specifications\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n
