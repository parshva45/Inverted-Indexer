    <p>\n       <b>\n        Boss\n       </b>\n       is the internal name for a family of large-displacement\n       <a href="/wiki/V8_engine" title="V8 engine">\n        V8 engines\n       </a>\n       from\n       <a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">\n        Ford Motor Company\n       </a>\n       intended to compete with\n       <a href="/wiki/Chrysler" title="Chrysler">\n        Chrysler\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Chrysler_Hemi_engine#Modern_Hemi" title="Chrysler Hemi engine">\n        Hemi\n       </a>\n       engines and\n       <a href="/wiki/General_Motors" title="General Motors">\n        General Motors\n       </a>\n       \' 6.2-L\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GM_Vortec_engine#6200" title="GM Vortec engine">\n        Vortec\n       </a>\n       engines. Originally, Ford developed the engine architecture under the name\n       <b>\n        Hurricane\n       </b>\n       ; however, development of the engine was delayed due to its temporary cancellation in 2005. It was revived in early 2006 by\n       <a href="/wiki/Mark_Fields_(businessman)" title="Mark Fields (businessman)">\n        Mark Fields\n       </a>\n       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">\n        [\n        <i>\n         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">\n          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2012)">\n           citation needed\n          </span>\n         </a>\n        </i>\n        ]\n       </sup>\n       and was given the new name of\n       <b>\n        Boss\n       </b>\n       in light of the devastation caused by\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Katrina" title="Hurricane Katrina">\n        Hurricane Katrina\n       </a>\n       in 2005.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hotrod1_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-hotrod1-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In spite of this change, Ford has yet to officially market the engines with the Boss name in any production vehicle where they are to be used, instead referring to the engines by their displacement.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The first (modern) Boss engine since the late 60\'s Boss 289ci, a 6.2-L V8, is produced at Ford\'s Romeo Engine Plant in\n       <a href="/wiki/Romeo,_Michigan" title="Romeo, Michigan">\n        Romeo, Michigan\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6.2_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-6.2-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <a href="/wiki/Ford_Australia" title="Ford Australia">\n        Ford Australia\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Ford_Performance_Vehicles" title="Ford Performance Vehicles">\n        Ford Performance Vehicles\n       </a>\n       used the "Boss" name for V8 engines from 2002, but these are\n       <a href="/wiki/Ford_Modular_engine#Ford_of_Australia" title="Ford Modular engine">\n        variations\n       </a>\n       of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Ford_Modular_engine" title="Ford Modular engine">\n        Ford Modular V8\n       </a>\n       with locally produced parts.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="6.2-L">\n        6.2-L\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ford_Boss_engine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: 6.2-L">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       The 6.2\xc2\xa0L (379\xc2\xa0cu\xc2\xa0in) V8 is the main variant of the Boss engine. The V8 shares design similarities with the\n       <a href="/wiki/Ford_Modular_engine" title="Ford Modular engine">\n        modular engine family\n       </a>\n       such as a deep-skirt block with cross-bolted main caps, crankshaft-driven gerotor oil pump,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Overhead_cam" title="Overhead cam">\n        overhead cam\n       </a>\n       valve train arrangement, and bellhousing bolt pattern. In particular, the 6.2-L features a two-valve per cylinder SOHC valve train with\n       <a href="/wiki/Roller_rocker" title="Roller rocker">\n        roller-rocker\n       </a>\n       shafts and two spark plugs per cylinder, as well as dual-equal\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Variable_cam_timing" title="Variable cam timing">\n        variable cam timing\n       </a>\n       . Just as notable is that they use a much wider 4.53\xc2\xa0in (115\xc2\xa0mm) bore spacing (compared to the Modular\'s 3.937\xc2\xa0in (100.0\xc2\xa0mm)), allowing for the use of larger bore diameters and valves. The 6.2-L V8 has a bore diameter of 4.0150\xc2\xa0in (101.98\xc2\xa0mm) with a 3.740\xc2\xa0in (95.0\xc2\xa0mm) stroke. It has lightweight aluminum cylinder heads and pistons, but makes use of a cast-iron cylinder block for extra durability since most applications for the engine will be trucks.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6.2_2-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-6.2-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       This V8 went into production in early 2010 and debuted in the 2010\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ford_F-150_SVT_Raptor" title="Ford F-150 SVT Raptor">\n        Ford F-150 SVT Raptor\n       </a>\n       as a late-availability option. A limited-edition version of the Raptor from Ford Racing called the Raptor XT features a high-output version of the 6.2-L V8 with about 500 horsepower (370\xc2\xa0kW).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       For the 2011 model year, the 6.2-L V8 was introduced in Ford\'s\n       <a href="/wiki/Ford_Super_Duty" title="Ford Super Duty">\n        Super Duty\n       </a>\n       pickups as a replacement for both the 5.4-L\n       <a href="/wiki/Ford_Modular_engine" title="Ford Modular engine">\n        Triton\n       </a>\n       V8 and the 6.8-L\n       <a href="/wiki/Ford_Modular_engine" title="Ford Modular engine">\n        Triton\n       </a>\n       V10, and in the F-150 as the premium engine option, though it is not available in all configurations.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6.2_2-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-6.2-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       For 2017 the 6.2 L V8 in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Ford_Super_Duty" title="Ford Super Duty">\n        Super Duty\n       </a>\n       received new tuning and modified camshafts to bump torque to 430 lb-ft, while horsepower remained at 385hp. This gives the 6.2 L V8 the most torque of any gasoline V8 in the full-size pickups segment over 8,500 lbs GVWR. The 6.2 L V8 is also now mated to Ford\'s TorqShift G 6 speed transmission, Ford\'s Live-Drive Power Takeoff (PTO) Provision with Mobile Mode is also optional on 6.2 L equipped trucks.\n      </p>\n
