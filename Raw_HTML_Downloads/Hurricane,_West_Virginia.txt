<p>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">\n        History\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane,_West_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Hurricane was named after Hurricane Creek, which was, in turn, named after a group of trees at the arm of the river bent in one direction. A party of surveyors commissioned by\n       <a href="/wiki/George_Washington" title="George Washington">\n        George Washington\n       </a>\n       noted the site appeared to have been struck by a hurricane,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       giving rise to the name. Locals pronounce the town (HURR-i-KINN) not (HURR-i-CANE).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Less than 17 miles (27\xc2\xa0km) southeast of Hurricane is a post office named\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Upper_Falls,_West_Virginia#Post_Offices" title="Upper Falls, West Virginia">\n        Tornado\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       A town named Hurricane Bridge was located where\n       <a href="/wiki/West_Virginia_Route_34" title="West Virginia Route 34">\n        West Virginia Route 34\n       </a>\n       now crosses the creek, near current-day Hurricane. The town can be traced back to an 1811\n       <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">\n        Virginia\n       </a>\n       map. The town primarily consisted of a large Inn. On March 28, 1863,\n       <a href="/wiki/Union_Army" title="Union Army">\n        Union\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Confederate_States_Army" title="Confederate States Army">\n        Confederate\n       </a>\n       forces fought a\n       <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">\n        Civil War\n       </a>\n       battle at Hurricane Bridge, now known as the skirmish at Hurricane Bridge.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Hurricane itself can be traced back to 1873, when a railroad track was laid through the town and a small depot was constructed. It was during this period that Main Street developed, along with other businesses in the area. Hurricane was a trading and residential town for\n       <a href="/wiki/Tobacco" title="Tobacco">\n        tobacco\n       </a>\n       growers and other farmers in the early 1900s. The fire department was established March 2, 1936. It was incorporated as a town in 1889, and as a city in the 1970s.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The three oldest continuously operating businesses are:\n      </p>\n      <ul>\n       <li>\n        The\n        <i>\n         Hurricane Breeze\n        </i>\n        , a weekly newspaper established October 1900.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Putnam County Bank, established October 25, 1901.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Rappold\'s Barber Shop, established June 1906.\n       </li>\n      </ul>\n      <p>\n       In the early 1990s, the old depot, which once served as the catalyst for the town\'s development, was torn down and replaced with a small\n       <a href="/wiki/Gazebo" title="Gazebo">\n        gazebo\n       </a>\n       . It was also during the 1990s that the city began to see a surge in urban growth as several subdivisions were established in the city limits. The population increase resulted in the widening of Route 34, a connecting road to\n       <a href="/wiki/Teays_Valley,_West_Virginia" title="Teays Valley, West Virginia">\n        Teays Valley\n       </a>\n       , and an expansion of\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_High_School_(West_Virginia)" title="Hurricane High School (West Virginia)">\n        Hurricane High School\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In 2002, a winning multi-state\n       <a href="/wiki/Powerball" title="Powerball">\n        Powerball\n       </a>\n       ticket was sold at a convenience store in Teays Valley near the city of Hurricane. The winning ticketholder was\n       <a href="/wiki/Jack_Whittaker_(lottery_winner)" title="Jack Whittaker (lottery winner)">\n        Jack Whittaker\n       </a>\n       .
