   <p>\n       The\n       <b>\n        Cygnus spacecraft\n       </b>\n       is an American\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Automated_cargo_spacecraft" title="Automated cargo spacecraft">\n        automated cargo spacecraft\n       </a>\n       developed by\n       <a href="/wiki/Orbital_ATK" title="Orbital ATK">\n        Orbital ATK\n       </a>\n       as part of\n       <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">\n        NASA\n       </a>\n       \'s\n       <a href="/wiki/Commercial_Orbital_Transportation_Services" title="Commercial Orbital Transportation Services">\n        Commercial Orbital Transportation Services\n       </a>\n       (COTS) developmental program. It is launched by Orbital\'s\n       <a href="/wiki/Antares_(rocket)" title="Antares (rocket)">\n        Antares rocket\n       </a>\n       or\n       <a href="/wiki/United_Launch_Alliance" title="United Launch Alliance">\n        ULA\n       </a>\n       \'s\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlas_V" title="Atlas V">\n        Atlas V\n       </a>\n       (powered by the Russian\n       <a href="/wiki/RD-180" title="RD-180">\n        RD-180\n       </a>\n       engine) and is designed to transport supplies to the\n       <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">\n        International Space Station\n       </a>\n       (ISS) following the retirement of the American\n       <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle" title="Space Shuttle">\n        Space Shuttle\n       </a>\n       . Since August 2000\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_unmanned_spaceflights_to_the_ISS" title="List of unmanned spaceflights to the ISS">\n        ISS resupply missions\n       </a>\n       have been regularly flown by\n       <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">\n        Russian\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Progress_(spacecraft)" title="Progress (spacecraft)">\n        Progress (spacecraft)\n       </a>\n       , as well as by the\n       <a href="/wiki/European_Space_Agency" title="European Space Agency">\n        European\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Automated_Transfer_Vehicle" title="Automated Transfer Vehicle">\n        Automated Transfer Vehicle\n       </a>\n       , and the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japan_Aerospace_Exploration_Agency" title="Japan Aerospace Exploration Agency">\n        Japanese\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/H-II_Transfer_Vehicle" title="H-II Transfer Vehicle">\n        H-II Transfer Vehicle\n       </a>\n       . commercial aviation and aeronautics industry.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-space-081223_7-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-space-081223-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n Under this contract, Orbital Sciences will deliver up to 20 tons of cargo to the ISS through 2016 in eight Cygnus spacecraft flights.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-space-081223_7-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-space-081223-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       By April 2010 Orbital had displayed a full-scale model of the Cygnus cargo delivery spacecraft at the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Space_Symposium" title="National Space Symposium">\n        National Space Symposium\n       </a>\n       (NSS) in Colorado Springs, CO.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-orbital-2010CRS_10-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-orbital-2010CRS-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Launched on an\n       <a href="/wiki/Antares_(rocket)" title="Antares (rocket)">\n        Antares\n       </a>\n       (renamed from\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Taurus_II" title="Taurus II">\n        Taurus II\n       </a>\n       ) medium-class launch vehicle or\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlas_V" title="Atlas V">\n        Atlas V\n       </a>\n       , the first Cygnus flight was originally planned to occur in December 2010.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nasa-ssa20080227_11-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nasa-ssa20080227-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aw-200802_12-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-aw-200802-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The Cygnus demonstration mission was successfully launched on September 18, 2013.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nasa-pr20130919_13-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nasa-pr20130919-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On January 12, 2014, the first scheduled Cygnus resupply mission arrived at the space station; the capsule carried Christmas presents and fresh fruit for the astronauts. Its arrival was delayed, first by the need to repair the station, and then by frigid weather at the launch site and solar flares that forced postponements.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nasa-pr20130919_13-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nasa-pr20130919-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-yahoonews-20140112_14-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-yahoonews-20140112-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       With the December 2015 launch of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cygnus_CRS_Orb-4" title="Cygnus CRS Orb-4">\n        Orb CRS-4\n       </a>\n       on\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlas_V" title="Atlas V">\n        Atlas V\n       </a>\n       , the Enhanced version made its debut. While it was planned from the beginning to fly on the fifth mission, the\n       <a href="/wiki/Cygnus_CRS_Orb-3" title="Cygnus CRS Orb-3">\n        Orb CRS-3\n       </a>\n       failure and subsequent move to Atlas V meant a delay. On the other hand, the lessons learned on packing and the extra capabilities of the Atlas allowed payload to be increased to 3,500\xc2\xa0kg (7,700\xc2\xa0lb).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nsf-20150831_15-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nsf-20150831-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Design">\n        Design\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cygnus_(spacecraft)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Design">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Standard_Cygnus_vs_Enhanced_Cygnus.png">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1060" data-file-width="2284" height="139" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/61/Standard_Cygnus_vs_Enhanced_Cygnus.png/300px-Standard_Cygnus_vs_Enhanced_Cygnus.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/61/Standard_Cygnus_vs_Enhanced_Cygnus.png/450px-Standard_Cygnus_vs_Enhanced_Cygnus.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/61/Standard_Cygnus_vs_Enhanced_Cygnus.png/600px-Standard_Cygnus_vs_Enhanced_Cygnus.png 2x" width="300"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Standard_Cygnus_vs_Enhanced_Cygnus.png" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Scale drawing of the Standard (left) and Enhanced (right) Cygnus\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The Cygnus spacecraft consists of two basic components: the Pressurized Cargo Module (PCM) and the Service Module (SM). The PCM is manufactured by\n       <a href="/wiki/Thales_Alenia_Space" title="Thales Alenia Space">\n        Thales Alenia Space\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/Turin" title="Turin">\n        Turin\n       </a>\n       (\n       <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">\n        Italy\n       </a>\n       ), the initial PCMs have a volume of 18\xc2\xa0m\n       <sup>\n        3\n       </sup>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-orbital-payload_3-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-orbital-payload-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The service module is built by\n       <a href="/wiki/Orbital_ATK" title="Orbital ATK">\n        Orbital ATK\n       </a>\n       and is based on their\n       <a href="/wiki/GEOStar" title="GEOStar">\n        GEOStar\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LEOStar" title="LEOStar">\n        LEOStar\n       </a>\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spacecraft_bus" title="Spacecraft bus">\n        spacecraft buses\n       </a>\n       as well as components from the development of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Dawn_(spacecraft)" title="Dawn (spacecraft)">\n        Dawn spacecraft\n       </a>\n       . It is currently expected to have a gross mass of 1,800\xc2\xa0kg with propulsion provided by thrusters using the\n       <a href="/wiki/Hypergolic_propellant" title="Hypergolic propellant">\n        hypergolic\n       </a>\n       propellants\n       <a href="/wiki/Hydrazine" title="Hydrazine">\n        hydrazine\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Dinitrogen_tetroxide" title="Dinitrogen tetroxide">\n        nitrogen tetroxide\n       </a>\n       and is capable of producing up to 4\xc2\xa0kW of electrical power via two\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solar_cells#Gallium_arsenide_.28GaAs.29_multijunction" title="Solar cells">\n        gallium arsenide solar arrays\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-orbital-payload_3-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-orbital-payload-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On November 12, 2009,\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Dutch_Space&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Dutch Space (page does not exist)">\n        Dutch Space\n       </a>\n       announced it will provide the solar arrays for the initial Cygnus spacecraft.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sn-091112_16-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-sn-091112-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Orb_CRS-1_unberthing_-_crop.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2904" data-file-width="2832" height="226" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Orb_CRS-1_unberthing_-_crop.jpg/220px-Orb_CRS-1_unberthing_-_crop.jpg" <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Orb_CRS-1_unberthing_-_crop.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Standard Cygnus being unberthed from the\n         <a href="/wiki/Harmony_(ISS_module)" title="Harmony (ISS module)">\n          Harmony module\n         </a>\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The fourth and all subsequent Cygnus spacecraft are planned to be of the "enhanced" variant.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-orbital-2011CRSBlog_17-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-orbital-2011CRSBlog-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       These will use a stretched PCM which increases the interior volume to 27\xc2\xa0m\n       <sup>\n        3\n       </sup>\n       and the service module will use\n       <a href="/wiki/Orbital_ATK" title="Orbital ATK">\n        Orbital ATK\n       </a>\n       Ultraflex\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solar_array" title="Solar array">\n        solar arrays\n       </a>\n       which will provide the same amount of power as the previous solar arrays but at a lower mass.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-orbital-payload_3-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-orbital-payload-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-orbital-2011CRSBlog_17-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-orbital-2011CRSBlog-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A new upper stage built by\n       <a href="/wiki/Orbital_ATK" title="Orbital ATK">\n        Orbital ATK\n       </a>\n       , the Castor 30XL, will be used in conjunction with the enhanced Cygnus; because of the more powerful upper stage and the lighter solar arrays, the payload that Cygnus can deliver to the ISS will be increased by 700\xc2\xa0kg.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nsf-20120222_18-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nsf-20120222-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       During nominal CRS missions, Cygnus maneuvers close to the International Space Station, where the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadarm2" title="Canadarm2">\n        Canadarm2\n       </a>\n       robotic arm\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grapple_Fixture" title="Grapple Fixture">\n        grapples\n       </a>\n       the spacecraft and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spacecraft_Docking_and_Berthing_Mechanisms" title="Spacecraft Docking and Berthing Mechanisms">\n        berths\n       </a>\n       it to a\n       <a href="/wiki/Common_Berthing_Mechanism" title="Common Berthing Mechanism">\n        Common Berthing Mechanism\n       </a>\n       on the\n       <a href="/wiki/Harmony_(ISS_module)" title="Harmony (ISS module)">\n        Harmony module\n       </a>\n       in a similar fashion to the Japanese\n       <a href="/wiki/H-II_Transfer_Vehicle" title="H-II Transfer Vehicle">\n        H-II Transfer Vehicle\n       </a>\n       and the other American CRS vehicle, the\n       <a href="/wiki/SpaceX_Dragon" title="SpaceX Dragon">\n        SpaceX Dragon\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-orbital-payload_3-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-orbital-payload-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       For typical missions, Cygnus is planned to remain berthed for about 30 days.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nasa-report201007_19-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nasa-report201007-19">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-orbital-aonepreflight_20-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-orbital-aonepreflight-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Cygnus does not provide return capability, but can be loaded with obsolete equipment and trash for destructive reentry similar to the Russian\n       <a href="/wiki/Progress_(spacecraft)" title="Progress (spacecraft)">\n        Progress vehicles\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-esa-hsocou029_21-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-esa-hsocou029-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       A formerly planned variant of Cygnus would have replaced the PCM with the Unpressurized Cargo Module (UCM), based on NASA\'s\n       <a href="/wiki/ExPRESS_Logistics_Carrier" title="ExPRESS Logistics Carrier">\n        ExPRESS Logistics Carrier\n       </a>\n       , and would have been used to transport unpressurized cargo, such as\n       <a href="/wiki/Orbital_replacement_unit" title="Orbital replacement unit">\n        ISS Orbital Replacement Units\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nasa-ssa20080227_11-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nasa-ssa20080227-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wired-20080202_22-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-wired-20080202-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Another proposed variant would have replaced the PCM with the Return Cargo Module (RCM), which would have allowed Cygnus to return cargo to Earth.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nasa-ssa20080227_11-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nasa-ssa20080227-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div style="clear:both;">\n      </div>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline"
