External links\n          </span>\n         </a>\n        </li>\n       </ul>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Background">\n        Background\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Vostok_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Background">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main article:\n       <a href="/wiki/Vostok_programme" title="Vostok programme">\n        Vostok programme\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <a href="/wiki/Space_Race" title="Space Race">\n        Space Race\n       </a>\n       between the Soviet Union and the United States, the two\n       <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">\n        Cold War\n       </a>\n       superpowers, began just before the Soviet Union launched the world\'s first artificial\n       <a href="/wiki/Satellite" title="Satellite">\n        satellite\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Sputnik_1" title="Sputnik 1">\n        Sputnik 1\n       </a>\n       , in 1957. Both countries wanted to develop spaceflight technology quickly, particularly by launching the first successful human spaceflight. The Soviet Union secretly pursued the\n       <a href="/wiki/Vostok_programme" title="Vostok programme">\n        Vostok programme\n       </a>\n       in competition with the United States\n       <a href="/wiki/Project_Mercury" title="Project Mercury">\n        Project Mercury\n       </a>\n       . Vostok launched several precursor unmanned missions between May 1960 and March 1961, to test and develop the\n       <a href="/wiki/Vostok_(rocket_family)" title="Vostok (rocket family)">\n        Vostok rocket family\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Vostok_(spacecraft)" title="Vostok (spacecraft)">\n        space capsule\n       </a>\n       . These missions had varied degrees of success, but the final two\xe2\x80\x94\n       <a href="/wiki/Korabl-Sputnik_4" title="Korabl-Sputnik 4">\n        Korabl-Sputnik 4\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Korabl-Sputnik_5" title="Korabl-Sputnik 5">\n        Korabl-Sputnik 5\n       </a>\n       \xe2\x80\x94were complete successes, allowing the first manned flight.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Crew">\n        Crew\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Vostok_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Crew">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <dl>\n       <dd>\n        <i>\n         See also\n         <a href="/wiki/Vostok_programme#Cosmonaut_selection_and_training" title="Vostok programme">\n          Selection and training of the Vostok programme\n         </a>\n        </i>\n       </dd>\n      </dl>\n      <p>\n       The Vostok 1 capsule was designed to carry a single cosmonaut. Yuri Gagarin, 27, was chosen as the prime pilot of Vostok 1, with\n       <a href="/wiki/Gherman_Titov" title="Gherman Titov">\n        Gherman Titov\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Grigori_Nelyubov" title="Grigori Nelyubov">\n        Grigori Nelyubov\n       </a>\n       as backups. These assignments were formally made on April 8, four days before the mission, but Gagarin had been a favourite among the cosmonaut candidates for at least several months.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-siddiqi2000_7-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-siddiqi2000-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">\n        :262,272\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The final decision of who would fly the mission relied heavily on the opinion of the head of cosmonaut training,\n       <a href="/wiki/Nikolai_Kamanin" title="Nikolai Kamanin">\n        Nikolai Kamanin\n       </a>\n       . In an April 5 diary entry, Kamanin wrote that he was still undecided between Gagarin and Titov.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       "The only thing that keeps me from picking [Titov] is the need to have the stronger person for the one day flight."\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Kamanin was referring to the second mission,\n       <a href="/wiki/Vostok_2" title="Vostok 2">\n        Vostok 2\n       </a>\n       , compared to the relatively short single-orbit mission of Vostok 1. When Gagarin and Titov were informed of the decision during a meeting on April 9, Gagarin was very happy, and Titov was disappointed.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On April 10, this meeting was reenacted in front of television cameras, so there would be official footage of the event. This included an acceptance speech by Gagarin.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       As an indication of the level of secrecy involved, one of the other cosmonaut candidates,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alexei_Leonov" title="Alexei Leonov">\n        Alexei Leonov\n       </a>\n       , later recalled that he did not know who was chosen for the mission until after the spaceflight had begun.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Burgess_and_Hall.2C_p.151_12-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Burgess_and_Hall.2C_p.151-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Medical_exam">\n        Medical exam\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Vostok_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Medical exam">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Gagarin was examined by a team of doctors prior to his flight. One doctor gave her recollection of the events in an interview with\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russia_Today" title="Russia Today">\n        Russia Today\n       </a>\n       in April 2011: "Gagarin looked more pale than usual. He was unsociable and quiet, which was not like him at all. He would answer by nodding or a short \'yes\' to all questions. Sometimes he would start humming some tunes. This was a different Gagarin. We geared him up, and hugged. And I said, \'Yuri, everything will be fine.\' And he nodded back."\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">\n        Preparations\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Vostok_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Preparations">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Vostok_spacecraft.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="942" data-file-width="1304" height="159" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/Vostok_spacecraft.jpg/220px-Vostok_spacecraft.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/Vostok_spacecraft.jpg/330px-Vostok_spacecraft.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/Vostok_spacecraft.jpg/440px-Vostok_spacecraft.jpg 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Vostok_spacecraft.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Model of the Vostok spacecraft with its upper stage, on display in\n         <a href="/wiki/Frankfurt_Airport" title="Frankfurt Airport">\n          Frankfurt Airport\n         </a>\n         \'s "Russia in Space" exhibition\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Unlike later Vostok missions, there were no dedicated\n       <a href="/wiki/Tracking_ship" title="Tracking ship">\n        tracking ships\n       </a>\n       available to receive signals from the spacecraft. Instead they relied on the network of ground stations, also called Command Points, to communicate with the spacecraft; all of these Command Points were located within the\n       <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">\n        Soviet Union\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">\n        <a href="#cite_note-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Because of weight constraints, there was no backup\n       <a href="/wiki/Retrorocket" title="Retrorocket">\n        retrorocket\n       </a>\n       engine. The spacecraft carried 10 days of provisions to allow for survival and natural\n       <a href="/wiki/Orbital_decay" title="Orbital decay">\n        orbital decay\n       </a>\n       in the event the retrorockets failed.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The letters "CCCP" were hand-painted onto Gagarin\'s helmet by engineer Sergeevich Lebedev during transfer to the launch site. As it had been less than a year since\n       <a href="/wiki/1960_U-2_incident" title="1960 U-2 incident">\n        U2 pilot Gary Powers was shot down\n       </a>\n       , Lebedev reasoned that without some country identification, there was a small chance the cosmonaut might be mistaken for a spy on landing.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sssr_15-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-sssr-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Automatic_control">\n        Automatic control\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Vostok_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Automatic control">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Vostokpanel.JPG">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2064" data-file-width="2786" height="163" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c7/Vostokpanel.JPG/220px-Vostokpanel.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c7/Vostokpanel.JPG/330px-Vostokpanel.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c7/Vostokpanel.JPG/440px-Vostokpanel.JPG 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Vostokpanel.JPG" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Part of the Vostok 1 instrument panel prominently displaying the\n         <a href="/wiki/Voskhod_Spacecraft_%22Globus%22_IMP_navigation_instrument" title=\'Voskhod Spacecraft "Globus" IMP navigation instrument\'>\n          "Globus" navigation instrument\n         </a>\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The entire mission would be controlled by either automatic systems or by\n       <a href="/wiki/Mission_control_center" title="Mission control center">\n        ground control\n       </a>\n       . This was because medical staff and spacecraft engineers were unsure how a human might react to weightlessness, and therefore it was decided to lock the pilot\'s manual controls. In an unusual move, a code to unlock the controls was placed in an onboard envelope, for Gagarin\'s use in case of emergency.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-siddiqi2000_7-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-siddiqi2000-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">\n        :278\n       </sup>\n       Prior to the flight, Kamanin and others told Gagarin the code anyway.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oleg_16-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-oleg-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">\n        <a href="#cite_note-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span id="April_11,_1961">\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="April_11.2C_1961">\n        April 11, 1961\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Vostok_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: April 11, 1961">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Electrocardiogram_of_Gagarin.JPG">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1652" data-file-width="2416" height="150" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Electrocardiogram_of_Gagarin.JPG/220px-Electrocardiogram_of_Gagarin.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Electrocardiogram_of_Gagarin.JPG/330px-Electrocardiogram_of_Gagarin.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/59/Electrocardiogram_of_Gagarin.JPG/440px-Electrocardiogram_of_Gagarin.JPG 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Electrocardiogram_of_Gagarin.JPG" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Electrocardiogram of\n         <a href="/wiki/Yuri_Gagarin" title="Yuri Gagarin">\n          Gagarin\n         </a>\n         recorded April 11, 1961, at 19 hours and 35 minutes. Exhibited at the\n         <a href="/wiki/Memorial_Museum_of_Cosmonautics" title="Memorial Museum of Cosmonautics">\n          Memorial Museum of Cosmonautics\n         </a>\n         in Moscow.\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       At Baikonur Cosmodrome on the morning of April 11, 1961, the\n       <a href="/wiki/Vostok-K" title="Vostok-K">\n        Vostok-K\n       </a>\n       rocket, together with the attached\n       <i>\n        Vostok 3KA\n       </i>\n       space capsule, were transported several kilometers to the launch pad, in a horizontal position. Once they arrived at the launch pad, a quick examination of the booster was conducted by technicians to make sure everything was in order. When no visible problems were found, the booster was erected on LC-1.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Burgess_and_Hall.2C_p.150_18-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Burgess_and_Hall.2C_p.150-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At 10:00 (\n       <a href="/wiki/Moscow_Time" title="Moscow Time">\n        Moscow Time\n       </a>\n       ), Gagarin and Titov were given a final review of the flight plan.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Burgess_and_Hall.2C_p.150_18-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Burgess_and_Hall.2C_p.150-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       They were informed that launch was scheduled to occur the following day, at 09:07 Moscow Time. This time was chosen so that when the capsule started to fly over Africa, which was when the retrorockets would need to fire for reentry, the solar illumination would be ideal for the orientation system\'s sensors.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Siddiqi.2C_p.273_19-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Siddiqi.2C_p.273-19">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       At 18:00, once various physiological readings had been taken, the doctors instructed the cosmonauts not to discuss the upcoming missions. That evening Gagarin and Titov relaxed by listening to music, playing pool, and chatting about their childhoods.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Burgess_and_Hall.2C_p.151_12-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Burgess_and_Hall.2C_p.151-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At 21:50, both men were offered sleeping pills, to ensure a good night\'s sleep, but they both declined.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">\n        <a href="#cite_note-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Physicians had attached sensors to the cosmonauts, to monitor their condition throughout the night, and they believed that both had slept well.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">\n        <a href="#cite_note-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Gagarin\'s biographers Doran and Bizony say that neither Gagarin nor Titov slept that night.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">\n        <a href="#cite_note-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Korolev didn\'t sleep that night, due to anxiety caused by the imminent spaceflight.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Siddiqi.2C_p.273_19-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Siddiqi.2C_p.273-19">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Mission">\n        Mission\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Vostok_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Mission">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       At 05:30 Moscow time, on the morning of April 12, 1961, both Gagarin and his backup Titov were woken.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">\n        <a href="#cite_note-23">\n         [23]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       They were given breakfast, assisted into their spacesuits, and then were transported to the launch pad.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-p274_24-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-p274-24">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Gagarin entered the\n       <i>\n        Vostok 1\n       </i>\n       spacecraft, and at 07:10 local time (04:10 UTC), the radio communication system was turned on.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-p274_24-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-p274-24">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Once Gagarin was in the spacecraft, his picture appeared on television screens in the launch control room from an onboard camera. Launch would not occur for another two hours, and during the time Gagarin chatted with the mission\'s main\n       <a href="/wiki/Flight_controller#Capsule_Communicator_.28CAPCOM.29" title="Flight controller">\n        CapCom\n       </a>\n       , as well as Chief Designer\n       <a href="/wiki/Sergei_Korolev" title="Sergei Korolev">\n        Sergei Korolev\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Nikolai_Kamanin" title="Nikolai Kamanin">\n        Nikolai Kamanin\n       </a>\n       , and a few others.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-p274_24-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-p274-24">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Following a series of tests and checks, about forty minutes after Gagarin entered the spacecraft, its hatch was closed. The orbit\'s\n       <a href="/wiki/Apsis" title="Apsis">\n        perigee and apogee\n       </a>\n       had been selected to cause reentry due to\n       <a href="/wiki/Orbital_decay" title="Orbital decay">\n        orbital decay\n       </a>\n       within 10 days (the limit of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Life_support_system" title="Life support system">\n        life support system\n       </a>\n       function) in the event of retrorocket malfunction. At around 07:35 UT, the two parts of the spacecraft began reentry and went through strong gyrations as Vostok 1 neared Egypt. At this point the wires broke, the two modules separated, and the descent module settled into the proper reentry attitude. Gagarin telegraphed "Everything is OK" despite continuing gyrations; he later reported that he did not want to "make noise" as he had (correctly) reasoned that the gyrations did not endanger the mission (and were apparently caused by the spherical shape of the reentry module). At 2.5\xc2\xa0km (8,200\xc2\xa0ft) altitude, the main parachute was deployed from the Vostok spacecraft. Two schoolgirls witnessed the Vostok landing and described the scene: "It was a huge ball, about two or three meters high. It fell, then it bounced and then it fell again. There was a huge hole where it hit the first time."\n       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">\n        [\n        <i>\n         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">\n          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. Both he and the spacecraft landed via parachute 26\xc2\xa0km (16\xc2\xa0mi) south west of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Engels_(city)" title="Engels (city)">\n        Engels\n       </a>\n       , in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Saratov_Oblast" title="Saratov Oblast">\n        Saratov region\n       </a>\n       at\n       <span class="plainlinks nourlexpansion">\n        <a class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Vostok_1&amp;params=51.270682_N_45.99727_E_">\n         <span class="geo-nondefault">\n          <span class="geo-dms" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">\n           <span class="latitude">\n            51\xc2\xb016\xe2\x80\xb214\xe2\x80\xb3N\n           </span>\n           <span class="longitude">\n            45\xc2\xb059\xe2\x80\xb250\xe2\x80\xb3E\n           </span>\n          </span>\n         </span>\n         <span class="geo-multi-punct">\n          \xef\xbb\xbf / \xef\xbb\xbf\n         </span>\n         <span class="geo-default">\n          <span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">\n           51.270682\xc2\xb0N 45.99727\xc2\xb0E\n          </span>\n          <span style="display:none">\n           \xef\xbb\xbf /\n           <span class="geo">\n            51.270682; 45.99727\n           </span>\n          </span>\n         </span>\n        </a>\n       </span>\n       . It was 2800\xc2\xa0km to the west of the planned landing site (near Baikonur).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-.D0.A0.D1.83.D0.B4.D0.B5.D0.BD.D0.BA.D0.BE_.D0.9C._.D0.98_31-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-.D0.A0.D1.83.D0.B4.D0.B5.D0.BD.D0.BA.D0.BE_.D0.9C._.D0.98-31">\n         [31]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       A farmer and her daughter observed the strange scene of a figure in a bright orange suit with a large white helmet landing near them by parachute. Gagarin later recalled, "When they saw me in my space suit and the parachute dragging alongside as I walked, they started to back away in fear. I told them, don\'t be afraid, I am a Soviet citizen like you, who has descended from space and I must find a telephone to call Moscow!"\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Reactions_and_legacy">\n        Reactions and legacy\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Vostok_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Reactions and legacy">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Soviet_reaction">\n        Soviet reaction\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Vostok_1&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Soviet reaction">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Gagarin\'s flight was announced on the Soviet radio by\n       <a href="/wiki/Yuri_Levitan" title="Yuri Levitan">\n        Yuri Levitan\n       </a>\n       , the speaker who had announced all major events in the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Great_Patriotic_War" title="Great Patriotic War">\n        Great Patriotic War\n       </a>\n       . As with all previous and most subsequent Soviet rocket launches, the flight preparation was\n       <a href="/wiki/Soviet_space_program#Program_Secrecy" title="Soviet space program">\n        kept secret\n       </a>\n       and the news was aired only\n       <i>\n        post-factum\n       </i>\n       . The flight was celebrated as a great triumph of the Soviet science and technology demonstrating the superiority of the socialist system over capitalism. Moscow and other cities in the USSR held mass demonstrations, the scale of which was comparable to\n       <a href="/wiki/Moscow_Victory_Parade_of_1945" title="Moscow Victory Parade of 1945">\n        World War II Victory Parades\n       </a>\n       . Gagarin was awarded the title of\n       <a href="/wiki/Hero_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Hero of the Soviet Union">\n        Hero of the Soviet Union\n       </a>\n       , the nation\'s highest honour. He also became an international celebrity with\n       <a href="/wiki/Yuri_Gagarin#Legacy_and_tributes" title="Yuri Gagarin">\n        numerous honours and awards\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32">\n        <a href="#cite_note-32">\n         [32]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       April 12 was declared\n       <a href="/wiki/Cosmonautics_Day" title="Cosmonautics Day">\n        Cosmonautics Day\n       </a>\n       in the USSR, and is celebrated today in Russia as one of the official "Commemorative Dates of Russia."\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32FZ_33-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-32FZ-33">\n         [33]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In 2011, it was declared the International Day of Human Space Flight by the\n       <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">\n        United Nations\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34">\n        <a href="#cite_note-34">\n         [34]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Gagarin\'s informal reply\n       <i>\n        poyekhali!\n       </i>\n       became a historical phrase used to refer to the arrival of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Space_Age" title="Space Age">\n        Space Age\n       </a>\n       in human history.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35">\n        <a href="#cite_note-35">\n         [35]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Later it was included in the refrain of a Soviet patriotic song written by\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alexandra_Pakhmutova" title="Alexandra Pakhmutova">\n        Alexandra Pakhmutova\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Nikolai_Dobronravov" title="Nikolai Dobronravov">\n        Nikolai Dobronravov\n       </a>\n       (\n       <i>\n        He said "let\'s go!" He waved his hand\n       </i>\n       ).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36">\n        <a href="#cite_note-36">\n         [36]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The Soviet press later reported that, minutes before boarding the spacecraft, Gagarin made a speech: "Dear friends, you who are close to me, and you whom I do not know, fellow Russians, and people of all countries and all continents: in a few minutes a powerful space vehicle will carry me into the distant realm of space. What can I tell you in these last minutes before the launch? My whole life appears to me as one beautiful moment. All that I previously lived through and did, was lived through and done for the sake of this moment." According to historian\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asif_Siddiqi" title="Asif Siddiqi">\n        Asif Siddiqi\n       </a>\n       , Gagarin actually "was essentially forced to utter a stream of banalities prepared by anonymous speechwriters" taped much earlier in Moscow.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-siddiqi2000_7-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-siddiqi2000-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">\n        :274\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline"
