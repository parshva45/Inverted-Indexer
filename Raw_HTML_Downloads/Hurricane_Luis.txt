<tr>\n        <td colspan="2" style="text-align:center;padding:0px 2px">\n        </td>\n       </tr>\n       <tr>\n        <td colspan="2" style="text-align:center; border-top:#aaa 1px solid;">\n </b>\n        </td>\n       </tr>\n       <tr style="display:none">\n        <td colspan="2">\n        </td>\n       </tr>\n      </table>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        Hurricane Luis\n       </b>\n       was a long-lived and an extremely destructive\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cape_Verde-type_hurricane" title="Cape Verde-type hurricane">\n        Cape Verde-type hurricane\n       </a>\n       as well as one of the strongest, deadliest and most notable hurricanes of the\n       <a href="/wiki/1995_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1995 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1995 Atlantic hurricane season\n       </a>\n       , with maximum sustained winds of 140\xc2\xa0mph (220\xc2\xa0km/h). Luis was also the strongest hurricane to make landfall, and the third-most intense\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone#Hurricane_or_typhoon" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        hurricane\n       </a>\n       recorded during the extremely active season. The storm was the twelfth tropical storm, sixth hurricane, and second major hurricane of the season. At one point, the storm was one of four simultaneous tropical systems in the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlantic" title="Atlantic">\n        Atlantic basin\n       </a>\n       , along with\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Humberto_(1995)" title="Hurricane Humberto (1995)">\n        Humberto\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Iris_(1995)" title="Hurricane Iris (1995)">\n        Iris\n       </a>\n       , and Karen. The storm lasted for 14\xc2\xa0days as a tropical storm between late August and nearly middle September.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The system initially formed from a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">\n        tropical wave\n       </a>\n       south of\n       <a href="/wiki/Cape_Verde" title="Cape Verde">\n        Cape Verde\n       </a>\n       islands, west of\n       <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">\n        Africa\n       </a>\n       on August 27 then subsequently attained tropical storm status on August 29. Two days later that day, the storm reached hurricane status; shortly thereafter, it rapidly strengthened into a 140\xc2\xa0mph (220\xc2\xa0km/h) category 4 hurricane.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At this strength, it affected much of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Leeward_Islands" title="Leeward Islands">\n        Leeward Islands\n       </a>\n       on September 4 to September 6. Throughout the following days, Luis gradually weakened as it accelerated safely to the west of\n       <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">\n        Bermuda\n       </a>\n       . As a category 1 hurricane, Luis made landfall on\n       <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="Newfoundland and Labrador">\n        Newfoundland\n       </a>\n       before it became extratropical on September 11.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       It caused catastrophic damage in\n       <a href="/wiki/Antigua" title="Antigua">\n        Antigua\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Barbuda" title="Barbuda">\n        Barbuda\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Barthelemy" title="St. Barthelemy">\n        St. Barthelemy\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Saint_Martin" title="Saint Martin">\n        St Martin\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Anguilla" title="Anguilla">\n        Anguilla\n       </a>\n       as a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_Hurricane_Scale#Category_4" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson Hurricane Scale">\n        Category 4\n       </a>\n       with winds of 130\xc2\xa0mph (209\xc2\xa0km/h). The storm accounted for 19 deaths, left between 20,000 and 70,000 homeless (mostly in Antigua, Barbuda and Saint Martin) and caused roughly $3 billion (1995\xc2\xa0USD) in damage across the affected areas. Hurricane Luis was the most devastating hurricane to strike the northern Leeward Islands in the 20th century and one of the strongest storms to hit the Leeward Islands at the time, along with\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_David" title="Hurricane David">\n        Hurricane David\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/1979_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1979 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1979\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hugo" title="Hurricane Hugo">\n        Hurricane Hugo\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/1989_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1989 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1989\n       </a>\n       . Additionally, Luis was the second of three\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        tropical cyclones\n       </a>\n       to affect\n       <a href="/wiki/Guadeloupe" title="Guadeloupe">\n        Guadeloupe\n       </a>\n       in a short period of time, the first being\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Iris_(1995)" title="Hurricane Iris (1995)">\n        Hurricane Iris\n       </a>\n       a week before and the last being\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Marilyn" title="Hurricane Marilyn">\n        Hurricane Marilyn\n       </a>\n       only ten days afterward. The next year, the Leeward Islands would be struck by\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Bertha_(1996)" title="Hurricane Bertha (1996)">\n        Hurricane Bertha\n       </a>\n       , while still repairing from Luis and Marilyn, then successively hit by\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hortense" title="Hurricane Hortense">\n        Hortense\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Erika_(1997)" title="Hurricane Erika (1997)">\n        Erika\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Georges" title="Hurricane Georges">\n        Georges\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Jose_(1999)" title="Hurricane Jose (1999)">\n        Jose\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Lenny" title="Hurricane Lenny">\n        Lenny\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Debby_(2000)" title="Hurricane Debby (2000)">\n        Debby\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Luis was also responsible for an intense\n       <a href="/wiki/Rogue_wave" title="Rogue wave">\n        rogue wave\n       </a>\n       which struck\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RMS_Queen_Elizabeth_2" title="RMS Queen Elizabeth 2">\n        RMS\n        <i>\n         Queen Elizabeth 2\n        </i>\n       </a>\n       on Monday, September 11, though the ship pulled through with little damage.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="toc">\n       <div class="toctitle">\n        <h2>\n         Contents\n        </h2>\n       </div>\n id="Meteorological_history">\n        Meteorological history\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Luis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main article:\n       <a href="/wiki/Meteorological_history_of_Hurricane_Luis" title="Meteorological history of Hurricane Luis">\n        Meteorological history of Hurricane Luis\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Luis_1995_track.png">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2700" data-file-width="2700" height="220" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Luis_1995_track.png/220px-Luis_1995_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Luis_1995_track.png/330px-Luis_1995_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/Luis_1995_track.png/440px-Luis_1995_track.png 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Luis_1995_track.png" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Map plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the\n         <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">\n          Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale\n         </a>\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The origins of Hurricane Luis trace back to an area of disturbed weather associated with a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">\n        tropical wave\n       </a>\n       over the eastern\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">\n        Atlantic Ocean\n       </a>\n       on August 26. A low-level circulation center formed and moved westward until it developed a weak\n       <a href="/wiki/Surface_weather_analysis" title="Surface weather analysis">\n        surface low\n       </a>\n       on August 27; consequently, a tropical depression was designated at 1200 UTC that day. The depression attained 36 hours later into a Tropical Storm named "Luis" on August 29. Though\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Convection_(meteorology)" title="Convection (meteorology)">\n        convective activity\n       </a>\n       fluctuated for the next two days as a result of nearby\n       <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">\n        wind shear\n       </a>\n       , the storm slowly intensified while pressure rose. The shear relented the next day, allowing an\n       <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">\n        eye\n       </a>\n       to gradually form, and the system attained hurricane status thereafter on early August 31 then a Category 3 major hurricane 18 hours later.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Tropical_Storm_Luis_on_August_29.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="308" data-file-width="364" height="186" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/Tropical_Storm_Luis_on_August_29.jpg/220px-Tropical_Storm_Luis_on_August_29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/Tropical_Storm_Luis_on_August_29.jpg/330px-Tropical_Storm_Luis_on_August_29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/6/6e/Tropical_Storm_Luis_on_August_29.jpg 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Tropical_Storm_Luis_on_August_29.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Tropical Storms Karen (left) and Luis (right) on August\xc2\xa029\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Hurricane Luis continued to strengthen as it tracked west-northwestward into a Category 4 on the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">\n        Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale\n       </a>\n       on September 2 and the cyclone turned more towards the west earlier that day as it maintained a rectilinear slow motion. on September 3, a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hunters" title="Hurricane Hunters">\n        reconnaissance aircraft\n       </a>\n       confirmed that the storm, that reached Category 4 status earlier, peaked with 140\xc2\xa0mph (230\xc2\xa0km/h) and maintained it for two days and a half. At this time, it was located approximately 540 miles (870\xc2\xa0km) to the east of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">\n        Lesser Antilles\n       </a>\n       . As a result, the storm began to slightly accelerate as it moved along a\n       <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_ridge" title="Subtropical ridge">\n        subtropical ridge\n       </a>\n       due to the absorption of\n       <a href="/wiki/1995_Atlantic_hurricane_season#Tropical_Storm_Karen" title="1995 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        Karen\n       </a>\n       by the stronger\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Iris_(1995)" title="Hurricane Iris (1995)">\n        Iris\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       By the day of September 4, Luis sustained a large wind field as it approach the Lesser Antilles, its tropical storm-force winds measured from 345\xc2\xa0mi (555\xc2\xa0km) to 365\xc2\xa0mi (587\xc2\xa0km) in diameter as it begin to affected in the night\n       <a href="/wiki/Antigua" title="Antigua">\n        Antigua\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Barbuda" title="Barbuda">\n        Barbuda\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Dominica" title="Dominica">\n        Dominica\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Guadeloupe" title="Guadeloupe">\n        Guadeloupe\n       </a>\n       . On the morning of September 5, islands such as Dominica and even the islands of Guadeloupe, where it experienced hurricane-force winds on the northeastern coast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Grande-Terre" title="Grande-Terre">\n        Grande-Terre\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/La_D%C3%A9sirade" title="La D\xc3\xa9sirade">\n        D\xc3\xa9sirade\n       </a>\n       , has been relatively spared by the storm. However the eyewall of the hurricane skimed Antigua and directly passing over Barbuda while weakened slightly. During the time, it proceeded slowly northwestward affecting\n       <a href="/wiki/Montserrat" title="Montserrat">\n        Montserrat\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Saint_Kitts_and_Nevis" title="Saint Kitts and Nevis">\n        Saint Kitts and Nevis\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Sint_Eustatius" title="Sint Eustatius">\n        Sint Eustatius\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Saba" title="Saba">\n        Saba\n       </a>\n       causing moderate damage. Later, Luis moved along\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Barthelemy" title="St. Barthelemy">\n        St. Barthelemy\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Martin" title="St. Martin">\n        St. Martin\n       </a>\n       and ultimately crossed Anguilla, where maximum sustained winds within the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eyewall" title="Eyewall">\n        eyewall\n       </a>\n       were estimated to have reached 135\xc2\xa0mph (217\xc2\xa0km/h), and its central pressure had dropped from 945 to 942\n       <a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">\n        mbar\n       </a>\n       (27.9 to 27.8\n       <a href="/wiki/Inch_of_mercury" title="Inch of mercury">\n        inHg\n       </a>\n       ).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       A large and powerful hurricane, Luis retained Category 4 intensity until September 7, situated about 150\xc2\xa0mi (240\xc2\xa0km) to the north of\n       <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">\n        Puerto Rico\n       </a>\n       . The storm gradually re-curved over the northern Atlantic as a Category 2 after having spent 7 consecutive days as a major hurricane with\n       <a href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_wind" title="Maximum sustained wind">\n        maximum sustained winds\n       </a>\n       of at least 115\xc2\xa0mph (185\xc2\xa0km/h) from September 1 to 8. From there on, the center of the storm passed 200 miles (320\xc2\xa0km) to the west of\n       <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">\n        Bermuda\n       </a>\n       on September 9 causing minor damage, and then later weakened to a Category\xc2\xa02 hurricane, with winds of 110\xc2\xa0mph (165\xc2\xa0km/h).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Several hours later, the hurricane made its closest approach to Bermuda, passing about 200\xc2\xa0mi (320\xc2\xa0km) west of the island.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Later that day, the storm began to accelerate as it traveled northeast ahead of a strong trough located to the northwest of Luis.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       During the afternoon of September\xc2\xa010, Hurricane Luis began to undergo an\n       <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">\n        extratropical transition\n       </a>\n       as it rapidly approached the Canadian coastline.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CHC_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CHC-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Due to the rapid movement of the storm, significant weakening did not occur until Luis was at an unusually high latitude as the central pressure of the storm decreased to 961\xc2\xa0mbar (28.38\xc2\xa0inHg) but sustained winds did not exceed 90\xc2\xa0mph (140\xc2\xa0km/h) according to the NHC.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Luis maintained this intensity until it made landfall on the\n       <a href="/wiki/Avalon_Peninsula" title="Avalon Peninsula">\n        Avalon Peninsula\n       </a>\n       in eastern Newfoundland early on September\xc2\xa011.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       While tracking over Newfoundland, cold, dry air became entrenched in Luis\'s circulation and the system began to merge with the approaching trough. The NHC issued their final advisory on Hurricane Luis at 0900\xc2\xa0UTC on September\xc2\xa011 as it tracked nearly 65\xc2\xa0mph (100\xc2\xa0km/h) to the northeast and transitioned into an\n       <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">\n        extratropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       on September 11 as it moved ashore on eastern\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Newfoundland_(Island)" title="Newfoundland (Island)">\n        Newfoundland\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The extratropical remnants persisted for 30\xc2\xa0more hours over the North Atlantic Ocean before being absorbed by the trough near the southern coast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">\n        Greenland\n       </a>\n       late on September\xc2\xa012.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">\n        Preparations\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Luis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Luis_on_September_6_1995.png">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="684" data-file-width="650" height="232" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2a/Hurricane_Luis_on_September_6_1995.png/220px-Hurricane_Luis_on_September_6_1995.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2a/Hurricane_Luis_on_September_6_1995.png/330px-Hurricane_Luis_on_September_6_1995.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2a/Hurricane_Luis_on_September_6_1995.png/440px-Hurricane_Luis_on_September_6_1995.png 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Luis_on_September_6_1995.png" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Hurricane Luis on September\xc2\xa06 to the north of the Leeward Islands\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       3 days before devastation occurred in the northern Lesser Antilles, Hurricane Luis became a Category 4 hurricane and forecasted to avoid those area well to the north, following Humberto and Karen. However, as Karen neared Iris, it weakened and absorbed by a stronger Iris as influenced by the subtropical ridge, steering Luis to the west.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Leeward_Islands_and_Puerto_Rico">\n        Leeward Islands and Puerto Rico\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Luis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Leeward Islands and Puerto Rico">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Due to its slow motion, giving local officials ample time to prepare. In advance of the storm,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_watches_and_warnings" title="Tropical cyclone watches and warnings">\n        tropical cyclone watches and warnings\n       </a>\n       were declared in several areas throughout the\n       <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">\n        Caribbean\n       </a>\n       . Seventeen watches and/or warnings were issued in association with Luis. The first two were a hurricane watch issued on Antigua, Barbuda and Guadeloupe on September\xc2\xa02 at 2200\xc2\xa0UTC. Five hours later, a tropical storm watch was issued for Dominica. At 2100\xc2\xa0UTC, a hurricane warning was issued for Antigua, Barbuda and a hurricane watch for Guadeloupe. At 1500\xc2\xa0UTC, Puerto Rico was put under a tropical storm watch. At 1700\xc2\xa0UTC, Guadeloupe, St. Barthelemy, and French portion of St. Martin were put under a hurricane warning. Four hours later, Puerto Rico, U.S. and British Virgin Islands were put under a tropical storm warning, this is including\n       <a href="/wiki/Martinique" title="Martinique">\n        Martinique\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Saint_Lucia" title="Saint Lucia">\n        Saint Lucia\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Bermuda_and_Canada">\n        Bermuda and Canada\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Luis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Bermuda and Canada">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Additionally, a tropical storm warning was also issued for Bermuda on September 7, all of which were canceled by September 10. However, there were no tropical storm watches or warnings issued for any part of\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Canada" title="Atlantic Canada">\n        Atlantic Canada\n       </a>\n       .\n According to the islands\' Prime Minister, a great number of houses were damaged or destroyed in Barbuda, and nearly 45% of the residences on Antigua were eradicated by the hurricane as it passed 25 miles (40\xc2\xa0km) to the north of the island.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-18">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Across the islands, numerous inhabitants experienced power outages and disrupted water systems. The storm ultimately accounted for three deaths and injured 165 locals; 32.000 inhabitants on both islands were greatly affected, forced 1,700 people to take shelter and left approximately 3,200 others homeless. Throughout the country, the total damage from the storm was estimated $350\xc2\xa0million or 60% of the country\'s\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gross_Domestic_Product" title="Gross Domestic Product">\n        GDP\n       </a>\n       worth of damage was assessed.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-caribbean_12-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-caribbean-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       International aid was sought after the storm had made its passing, with shelter and food supplies being the most needed.\n      </p>\n broadcast from sites off the island. Sea-level Airport readings showed 86\xc2\xa0mph (139\xc2\xa0km/h) sustained winds and winds gusting to 114\xc2\xa0mph (183\xc2\xa0km/h).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AHSof1995_21-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-AHSof1995-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Whereas measurements on some hillside homes and especially the airport on the north of the French side, between two mountains, showed wind gusts between 170 and 205\xc2\xa0mph (275 and 330\xc2\xa0km/h). An sailor, that been sheltered in the lagoon recorded a 115\xc2\xa0mph (185\xc2\xa0km/h) winds gusting and a minimal pressure reading to 965\xc2\xa0mbar (28.5\xc2\xa0inHg).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42">\n        <a href="#cite_note-42">\n         [42]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Records_and_retirement">\n        Records and retirement\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Luis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Records and retirement">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       See also:\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricane names">\n        List of retired Atlantic hurricane names\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:1995_Parade_of_Storms.png">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="450" data-file-width="720" height="138" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/1995_Parade_of_Storms.png/220px-1995_Parade_of_Storms.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/1995_Parade_of_Storms.png/330px-1995_Parade_of_Storms.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/1995_Parade_of_Storms.png/440px-1995_Parade_of_Storms.png 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:1995_Parade_of_Storms.png" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         A satellite image of the Atlantic Ocean on August 24 including\n         <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Humberto_(1995)" title="Hurricane Humberto (1995)">\n          Humberto\n         </a>\n         , Iris,\n         <a href="/wiki/Tropical_Storm_Jerry_(1995)" title="Tropical Storm Jerry (1995)">\n          Jerry\n         </a>\n         , and two waves that would soon become Karen and Luis\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       When Tropical Depression Thirteen strengthened into Tropical Storm Luis on August\xc2\xa029, it marked the earliest date on which the twelfth named storm of any season formed, surpassing the previous record set by\n       <a href="/wiki/1933_Treasure_Coast_hurricane" title="1933 Treasure Coast hurricane">\n        Tropical Storm Twelve\n       </a>\n       of the\n       <a href="/wiki/1933_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1933 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1933 season\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Shortly before becoming\n       <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">\n        extratropical\n       </a>\n       , Hurricane Luis was moving at 65\xc2\xa0mph (105\xc2\xa0km/h), becoming one of the fastest moving Atlantic hurricanes in recorded history.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_43-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-43">\n         [43]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       On September\xc2\xa011, a 98 feet (30\xc2\xa0m) wave struck an ocean liner. This wave is the largest wave ever recorded;\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44">\n        <a href="#cite_note-44">\n         [44]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       however,\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Ivan" title="Hurricane Ivan">\n        Hurricane Ivan\n       </a>\n       in 2004 may have produced a wave up to 130 feet (40\xc2\xa0m) high off the coast of Mexico.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45">\n        <a href="#cite_note-45">\n         [45]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Since 1950, there have been very few Atlantic hurricanes that have had an\n       <a href="/wiki/Accumulated_cyclone_energy" title="Accumulated cyclone energy">\n        Accumulated cyclone energy\n       </a>\n       (ACE) index over 50. As it spent nearly fourteen days as a named storm, Luis achieved and ACE value of 53.5, which was the highest since\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Inez" title="Hurricane Inez">\n        Inez\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/1966_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1966 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1966\n       </a>\n       and remained the highest until being surpassed by hurricanes\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Isabel" title="Hurricane Isabel">\n        Isabel\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/2003_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2003 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        2003\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Ivan" title="Hurricane Ivan">\n        Ivan\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/2004_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2004 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        2004\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Irma" title="Hurricane Irma">\n        Irma\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/2017_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2017 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        2017\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Due to the severe damage and loss of life caused by the storm in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Leeward_Islands" title="Leeward Islands">\n        Leeward Islands\n       </a>\n       , the name "Luis" was\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricanes" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricanes">\n        retired\n       </a>\n       in the spring of 1996, and will never again be used for an\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_tropical_cyclone" title="North Atlantic tropical cyclone">\n        Atlantic hurricane\n       </a>\n       . It was replaced with "Lorenzo" in the\n       <a href="/wiki/2001_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2001 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        2001 season\n       </a>\n       . Luis was the first Atlantic hurricane name beginning with "L" and the first hurricane to be retired in three years since\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Andrew" title="Hurricane Andrew">\n        Hurricane Andrew\n       </a>\n       in 1992.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-names_46-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-names-46">\n         [46]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n
