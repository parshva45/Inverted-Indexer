<span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a       ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Smith was born in\n       <a href="/wiki/Edmonton,_London" title="Edmonton, London">\n        Edmonton\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Middlesex" title="Middlesex">\n        Middlesex\n       </a>\n       , and served as an\n       <a href="/wiki/Royal_Air_Force" title="Royal Air Force">\n        RAF\n After an unsuccessful career as a\n       <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">\n        jazz\n       </a>\n       musician, Smith joined\n       <a href="/wiki/EMI" title="EMI">\n        EMI\n       </a>\n       as an apprentice sound engineer in 1959.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Career_as_producer_and_engineer">\n        Career as producer and engineer\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Norman_Smith_(record_producer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career as producer and engineer">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       He was the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Audio_engineering" title="Audio engineering">\n        engineer\n       </a>\n       on all of the EMI studio\n       <a href="/wiki/Sound_recording_and_reproduction" title="Sound recording and reproduction">\n        recordings\n       </a>\n       by\n       <a href="/wiki/The_Beatles" title="The Beatles">\n        the Beatles\n       </a>\n       until 1965,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dead_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Dead-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       when EMI promoted him from engineer to producer. The last Beatles album he recorded was\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Rubber_Soul" title="Rubber Soul">\n         Rubber Soul\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and Smith engineered the sound for almost 100 Beatles songs in total.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <a href="/wiki/John_Lennon" title="John Lennon">\n        John Lennon\n       </a>\n       first bestowed upon Smith the nickname of "Normal", and it was quickly picked up by the other Beatles. (November 2015)">\n           citation needed\n          </span>\n         </a>\n        </i>\n        ]\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       While working with The Beatles on 17 June 1965, he was offered \xc2\xa315,000 by the band\'s music publishing company,\n       <a href="/wiki/Dick_James" title="Dick James">\n        Dick James\n       </a>\n       Music, to buy outright a song he had written.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In early 1967, he began working with a new group,\n       <a href="/wiki/Pink_Floyd" title="Pink Floyd">\n        Pink Floyd\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dead_1-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Dead-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       producing their first, second, and fourth studio albums\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/The_Piper_at_the_Gates_of_Dawn" title="The Piper at the Gates of Dawn">\n         The Piper at the Gates of Dawn\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       ,\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/A_Saucerful_of_Secrets" title="A Saucerful of Secrets">\n         A Saucerful of Secrets\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       , and\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Ummagumma" title="Ummagumma">\n         Ummagumma\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       During the sessions for the song "\n       <a href="/wiki/Remember_a_Day" title="Remember a Day">\n        Remember a Day\n       </a>\n       ", drummer\n       <a href="/wiki/Nick_Mason" title="Nick Mason">\n        Nick Mason\n       </a>\n       became agitated that he could not come up with the right drum part for the song. Smith, however, knew what he wanted with the drums, so he played the part himself.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In 1968, Smith produced one of the first rock\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Concept_albums" title="Concept albums">\n        concept albums\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Pretty_Things" title="The Pretty Things">\n        the Pretty Things\n       </a>\n       \'\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/S.F._Sorrow" title="S.F. Sorrow">\n         S.F. Sorrow\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dead_1-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Dead-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       He produced early recordings by\n       <a href="/wiki/Barclay_James_Harvest" title="Barclay James Harvest">\n        Barclay James Harvest\n       </a>\n       , including their album\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Once_Again_(Barclay_James_Harvest_album)" title="Once Again (Barclay James Harvest album)">\n         Once Again\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       , and many years later was name-checked in\n       <a href="/wiki/John_Lees_(musician)" title="John Lees (musician)">\n        John Lees\n       </a>\n       \' song "John Lennon\'s Guitar".\n       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">\n        [\n        <i>\n         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">\n          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2015)">\n           citation needed\n          </span>\n         </a>\n        </i>\n        ]\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span id=\'Recording_career_as_"Hurricane_Smith"\'>\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Recording_career_as_.22Hurricane_Smith.22">\n        Recording career as "Hurricane Smith"\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Norman_Smith_(record_producer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title=\'Edit section: Recording career as "Hurricane Smith"\'>\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       In 1971, Smith, using a\n       <a href="/wiki/Musician" title="Musician">\n        recording artist\n       </a>\n       pseudonym of\n       <b>\n        Hurricane Smith\n       </b>\n       , had a UK No. 2\n       <a href="/wiki/Hit_record" title="Hit record">\n        hit\n       </a>\n       with "\n       <a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Let_It_Die" title="Don\'t Let It Die">\n        Don\'t Let It Die\n       </a>\n       ".\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-British_Hit_Singles_.26_Albums_7-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-British_Hit_Singles_.26_Albums-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       This recording was a demo of a song that he had written with the hope that John Lennon would record it. When he played it for fellow record producer\n       <a href="/wiki/Mickie_Most" title="Mickie Most">\n        Mickie Most\n       </a>\n       , Most was impressed enough to tell him to release it as it was.\n       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">\n        [\n        <i>\n         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">\n          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (UK #23).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-British_Hit_Singles_.26_Albums_7-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-British_Hit_Singles_.26_Albums-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Some recordings followed, such as "My Mother Was Her Name" (1972), "Beautiful Day, Beautiful Night" (1973) and "To Make You My Baby" (1974). However, his subsequent attempts at producing successful recordings proved elusive. Capitalising on his solo recording efforts, Smith undertook two tours of the then thriving north of England cabaret circuit, complete with band and dancers. Smith enlisted the help of session drummer Peter Boita who "fixed" a band for Smith which was mostly made up of the temporarily disbanded "\n       <a href="/wiki/Bob_Miller_and_the_Millermen" title="Bob Miller and the Millermen">\n        Bob Miller and the Millermen\n       </a>\n       " band. This line-up went on to record the last album Smith made for EMI Records,\n       <i>\n        Razzmahtazz Shall Inherit The Earth\n       </i>\n       , which was released in 1973.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Smith also recorded an\n       <a href="/wiki/Instrumental" title="Instrumental">\n        instrumental\n       </a>\n       track, entitled "Theme From an Unmade Silent Movie", which the\n       <a href="/wiki/West_Midlands_(region)" title="West Midlands (region)">\n        West Midlands\n       </a>\n       based radio presenter,\n       <a href="/wiki/Tony_Butler" title="Tony Butler">\n        Tony Butler\n       </a>\n       , adopted as his\n       <a href="/wiki/Theme_music" title="Theme music">\n        theme music\n       </a>\n       , playing it frequently on his sports show in an attempt, often successful, to encourage the region\'s local football teams to score a goal. Fans of\n       <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">\n        Aston Villa F.C.\n       </a>\n       also consider this tune as their unofficial club theme, and it can often be heard played at Villa Park during the pre-match and half-time intervals. It was performed by the\n       <a href="/wiki/City_of_Birmingham_Symphony_Orchestra" title="City of Birmingham Symphony Orchestra">\n        City of Birmingham Symphony Orchestra\n       </a>\n       on 6 June 2008.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In 2004, Smith released a new CD,\n       <i>\n        From Me To You\n       </i>\n       (SFMCD030), featuring new recordings of his biggest self-penned hits, "Don\'t Let It Die" and "Oh, Babe, What Would You Say?". Included in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Liner_notes" title="Liner notes">\n        liner notes\n       </a>\n       were messages from Sir\n       <a href="/wiki/Paul_McCartney" title="Paul McCartney">\n        Paul McCartney\n       </a>\n       and members of Pink Floyd.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In 2011, a snippet of Smith\'s "Don\'t Let It Die" was included in the soundtrack of Tomas Alfredson\'s 2011\n       <a href="/wiki/Tinker_Tailor_Soldier_Spy_(film)" title="Tinker Tailor Soldier Spy (film)">\n        film adaptation\n       </a>\n       of\n       <a href="/wiki/John_le_Carr%C3%A9" title="John le Carr\xc3\xa9">\n        John le Carr\xc3\xa9\n       </a>\n       \'s\n       <i>\n        Tinker Tailor Soldier Spy\n       </i>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Memoir">\n        Memoir\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Norman_Smith_(record_producer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Memoir">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Smith wrote a memoir, entitled\n       <i>\n        John Lennon Called Me Normal\n       </i>\n       . It debuted on 16 March 2007 as a\n       <a href="/wiki/Special_edition" title="Special edition">\n        limited edition\n       </a>\n       at\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Fest_For_Beatles_Fans" title="The Fest For Beatles Fans">\n        The Fest For Beatles Fans\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/Secaucus,_New_Jersey" title="Secaucus, New Jersey">\n        Secaucus, New Jersey\n       </a>\n       . There, Smith appeared and sang "Oh, Babe". The book contains never-before published pictures, newly revealed historical facts about the Beatles and Pink Floyd at\n       <a href="/wiki/Abbey_Road_Studios" title="Abbey Road Studios">\n        Abbey Road Studios\n       </a>\n       , as well as details of Smith\'s life as an RAF glider pilot.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Death">\n        Death\n
