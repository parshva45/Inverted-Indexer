      </tr>\n       <tr style="display:none">\n        <td colspan="2">\n        </td>\n       </tr>\n      </table>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        Hurricane Carla\n       </b>\n       ranks as the most intense U.S.\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        tropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">\n        landfall\n       </a>\n       on the\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Severity_Index" title="Hurricane Severity Index">\n        Hurricane Severity Index\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The third named storm and first\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Category_5_hurricane" title="Category 5 hurricane">\n        Category\xc2\xa05 hurricane\n       </a>\n       of the\n       <a href="/wiki/1961_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1961 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1961 Atlantic hurricane season\n       </a>\n       , Carla developed from an area of\n       <a href="/wiki/Squall" title="Squall">\n        squally weather\n       </a>\n       in the southwestern\n       <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">\n        Caribbean Sea\n       </a>\n       on September\xc2\xa03. Initially a tropical depression, it strengthened slowly while heading northwestward, and by September\xc2\xa05, the system was upgraded to Tropical Storm Carla. About 24\xc2\xa0hours later, Carla was upgraded to a hurricane. Shortly thereafter, the storm curved northward while approaching the\n       <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Channel" title="Yucat\xc3\xa1n Channel">\n        Yucat\xc3\xa1n Channel\n       </a>\n       . Late on September\xc2\xa07, Carla entered the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">\n        Gulf of Mexico\n       </a>\n       while passing just northeast of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula" title="Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula">\n        Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula\n       </a>\n       . By early on the following day, the storm became a major hurricane after reaching Category\xc2\xa03 intensity. Resuming its northwestward course, Carla continued intensification and on September\xc2\xa011, it was upgraded to a Category\xc2\xa05 hurricane. Later that day, Carla weakened slightly, but was still a large and intense hurricane when the storm made landfall near\n       <a href="/wiki/Port_O%27Connor,_Texas" title="Port O\'Connor, Texas">\n        Port O\'Connor, Texas\n       </a>\n       . It weakened quickly inland and was reduced to a tropical storm on September\xc2\xa012. Heading generally northward, Carla transitioned into an\n       <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">\n        extratropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       on September\xc2\xa013, while centered over southern\n       <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma" title="Oklahoma">\n        Oklahoma\n       </a>\n       . Rapidly moving northeastward, Carla\'s remnants reached the\n       <a href="/wiki/Labrador_Sea" title="Labrador Sea">\n        Labrador Sea\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">\n        Canada\n       </a>\n       and dissipated on September\xc2\xa017,\xc2\xa01961.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       While crossing the Yucat\xc3\xa1n Channel, the\n       <a href="/wiki/Rainband" title="Rainband">\n        outer bands\n       </a>\n       of Carla brought gusty winds and severe local\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flooding" title="Flooding">\n        flooding\n       </a>\n       in western\n       <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">\n        Cuba\n       </a>\n       and the Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula, though no damage or fatalities were reported. Although initially considered a significant threat to\n       <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">\n        Florida\n       </a>\n       , the storm brought only light winds and small amounts of precipitation, reaching no more than 3.15\xc2\xa0in (80\xc2\xa0mm). In Texas, wind gusts as high as 170\xc2\xa0mph (280\xc2\xa0km/h) were observed in\n       <a href="/wiki/Port_Lavaca,_Texas" title="Port Lavaca, Texas">\n        Port Lavaca\n       </a>\n       . Additionally, several\n       <a href="/wiki/Tornado" title="Tornado">\n        tornadoes\n       </a>\n       spawned in the state caused notable impacts, with the most destructive twister, an F4 near\n       <a href="/wiki/Galveston,_Texas" title="Galveston, Texas">\n        Galveston, Texas\n       </a>\n       , resulting in 200\xc2\xa0buildings severely damaged, of which at least 60\xc2\xa0were destroyed, and 8\xc2\xa0deaths and 55\xc2\xa0injuries. The aforementioned tornado remains the strongest hurricane-spawned tornado ever recorded, with wind speeds in the tornado nearly 50% greater than Carla\'s peak intensity. Throughout the state, Carla destroyed 1,915\xc2\xa0homes, 568\xc2\xa0farm buildings, and 415\xc2\xa0other buildings. Additionally, 50,723\xc2\xa0homes, 5,620\xc2\xa0farm buildings, and 10,487\xc2\xa0other buildings suffered damage. There were 34\xc2\xa0fatalities and at least $300\xc2\xa0million (1961\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">\n        USD\n       </a>\n       ) in losses in Texas alone. Several tornadoes also touched down in\n       <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">\n        Louisiana\n       </a>\n       , causing the destruction of 140\xc2\xa0homes and 11\xc2\xa0farms and other buildings, and major damage to 231\xc2\xa0additional homes and 11\xc2\xa0farm and other buildings. Minor to moderate damage was also reported to 748\xc2\xa0homes and 75\xc2\xa0farm and other buildings. Six deaths and $25\xc2\xa0million in losses in Louisiana were attributed to Carla. Heavy rainfall occurred in several other states, especially in Kansas, where flash flooding severely damaged crops and drowned 5\xc2\xa0people. Overall, Carla resulted in $325.74\xc2\xa0million in losses and 43\xc2\xa0fatalities. There were apparently 100 more undocumented deaths.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="toc">\n       <div class="toctitle">\n        <h2>\n         Contents\n </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       As early as September\xc2\xa01, a tropical disturbance \xe2\x80\x93 an area of\n       <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_convection" title="Atmospheric convection">\n        convective activity\n       </a>\n       \xe2\x80\x93 was observed tracking westward across the\n       <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">\n        Caribbean Sea\n       </a>\n       within the\n       <a href="/wiki/Intertropical_Convergence_Zone" title="Intertropical Convergence Zone">\n        Intertropical Convergence Zone\n       </a>\n       (ITCZ). Around that time, an\n       <a href="/wiki/Anticyclone" title="Anticyclone">\n        anticyclone\n       </a>\n       was situated over the western Caribbean Sea at the\n       <a href="/wiki/Troposphere" title="Troposphere">\n        upper-tropospheric levels\n       </a>\n       .\n       <a href="/wiki/Surface_weather_analysis" title="Surface weather analysis">\n        Surface charts\n       </a>\n       indicate that a low-level circulation was developing early on September\xc2\xa03.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-new12_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-new12-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Thus, it is estimated that a tropical depression \xe2\x80\x93 a tropical cyclone with\n       <a href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_wind" title="Maximum sustained wind">\n        sustained winds\n       </a>\n       with winds of less than 39\n       <a href="/wiki/Miles_per_hour" title="Miles per hour">\n        mph\n       </a>\n       (63\n       <a href="/wiki/Kilometres_per_hour" title="Kilometres per hour">\n        km/h\n       </a>\n       ) \xe2\x80\x93 developed about 175 miles (282\xc2\xa0km) northwest of\n       <a href="/wiki/Barranquilla" title="Barranquilla">\n        Barranquilla\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">\n        Colombia\n       </a>\n       at 1200\xc2\xa0UTC.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Initially, the center of circulation remained difficult to locate on surface charts due to lack of data. At\n       <a href="/wiki/San_Andr%C3%A9s_(island)" title="San Andr\xc3\xa9s (island)">\n        San Andr\xc3\xa9s\n       </a>\n       , winds shifted west at about 12\xc2\xa0mph (19\xc2\xa0km/h), while\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barometric_pressure" title="Barometric pressure">\n        barometric pressures\n       </a>\n       dropped to 1,007\n       <a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">\n        mbar\n       </a>\n       (29.7\n       <a href="/wiki/Inch_of_mercury" title="Inch of mercury">\n        inHg\n       </a>\n       ). The\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Weather_Service" title="National Weather Service">\n        Weather Bureau\n       </a>\n       Office in\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Miami,_Florida" title="Miami, Florida">\n        Miami, Florida\n       </a>\n       issued its first bulletin at 1600\xc2\xa0UTC on September\xc2\xa04, while the depression was centered about 250 miles (400\xc2\xa0km) east-southeast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Cabo_Gracias_a_Dios" title="Cabo Gracias a Dios">\n        Cabo Gracias a Dios\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">\n        Nicaragua\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-new12_2-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-new12-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       It is estimated that sustained winds reached 45\xc2\xa0mph (75\xc2\xa0km/h) at 1200\xc2\xa0UTC on September\xc2\xa05,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-new13_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-new13-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       thus the depression strengthened into a tropical storm and was named\n       <i>\n        Carla\n       </i>\n       , while located just northeast of Cabo Gracias a Dios.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A reconnaissance aircraft flight reported that Carla was continuing to intensify, with surface winds of 50\xc2\xa0mph (85\xc2\xa0km/h). Radars reported an unusually large tropical cyclone, with convective bands extending about 520 miles (840\xc2\xa0km) outward from the center. Around 0000\xc2\xa0UTC on September\xc2\xa06, Carla passed near\n       <a href="/wiki/Swan_Islands,_Honduras" title="Swan Islands, Honduras">\n        Swan Island\n       </a>\n       , which reported a barometric pressure of about 995\xc2\xa0mbar (29.4\xc2\xa0inHg) and wind gusts of 60\xc2\xa0mph (95\xc2\xa0km/h) from the southwest.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-new13_4-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-new13-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Based on observations obtained by a reconnaissance aircraft at 1100\xc2\xa0UTC on September\xc2\xa06 \xe2\x80\x93 namely a barometric pressure of 982\xc2\xa0mbar (29.0\xc2\xa0inHg) \xe2\x80\x93 a bulletin issued by the Weather Bureau an hour later indicated that the storm had "probably reached hurricane intensity".\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-new14_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-new14-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       According to post-season analysis, Carla reached hurricane status at that time.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_carla_radar.jpg">\n         <img alt="" Texas\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Shortly after becoming a hurricane on September\xc2\xa06, Carla curved northward in the northwestern Caribbean Sea. At 1200\xc2\xa0UTC on September\xc2\xa07, the storm intensified into a Category\xc2\xa02 hurricane while located east-northeast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Cozumel" title="Cozumel">\n        Cozumel\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Quintana_Roo" title="Quintana Roo">\n        Quintana Roo\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">\n        Mexico\n       </a>\n       (actual use of the Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Wind Scale, which was developed in 1971, was not in effect). Shortly thereafter, Carla crossed the Yucat\xc3\xa1n Channel and entered the Gulf of Mexico. A strong\n       <a href="/wiki/High-pressure_area" title="High-pressure area">\n        high pressure area\n       </a>\n       forced Carla to resume its original northwesterly course. Early on September\xc2\xa08, the storm strengthened into a Category\xc2\xa03 hurricane while located just north of the Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula. Thus, Carla was the third major hurricane of the season. Carla was upgraded to a Category\xc2\xa04 hurricane early on September\xc2\xa010, while approaching the Gulf Coast of the United States. At 0000\xc2\xa0UTC on September\xc2\xa011, Carla strengthened into a Category\xc2\xa05 while located in the northwestern Gulf of Mexico. Six hours later, the storm reached its maximum sustained wind speed of 175\xc2\xa0mph (280\xc2\xa0km/h).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Although it weakened back to a Category\xc2\xa04 hurricane late on September\xc2\xa011, Carla reached its minimum barometric pressure of 931\xc2\xa0mbar (27.5\xc2\xa0inHg). Later that day, the storm struck\n       <a href="/wiki/Matagorda_Island" title="Matagorda Island">\n        Matagorda Island\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">\n        Texas\n       </a>\n       (seven miles south of\n       <a href="/wiki/Port_O%27Connor,_Texas" title="Port O\'Connor, Texas">\n        Port O\'Connor\n       </a>\n       ) with winds of 145\xc2\xa0mph (230\xc2\xa0km/h). Carla rapidly weakened inland, and degenerated to a Category\xc2\xa02 hurricane on September\xc2\xa012, while passing just east of Port Lavaca. Six hours later, the storm was downgraded to a Category\xc2\xa01 hurricane near\n       <a href="/wiki/Shiner,_Texas" title="Shiner, Texas">\n        Shiner\n       </a>\n       , shortly before being downgraded to a tropical storm between\n       <a href="/wiki/Taylor,_Texas" title="Taylor, Texas">\n        Taylor\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Coupland,_Texas" title="Coupland, Texas">\n        Coupland\n       </a>\n       . Beginning late on September\xc2\xa012, the storm curved just east of due north. After reaching\n       <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma" title="Oklahoma">\n        Oklahoma\n       </a>\n       early on September\xc2\xa013, Carla transitioned into an extratropical cyclone while located over rural\n       <a href="/wiki/Johnston_County,_Oklahoma" title="Johnston County, Oklahoma">\n        Johnston County\n       </a>\n       . The extratropical remnants of Carla moved rapidly northeastward and brought heavy rainfall to some areas of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Great_Plains" title="Great Plains">\n        Great Plains\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Midwestern_United_States" title="Midwestern United States">\n        Midwestern United States\n       </a>\n       . By September\xc2\xa014, it entered\n       <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">\n        Canada\n       </a>\n       near\n       <a href="/wiki/Sault_Ste._Marie,_Ontario" title="Sault Ste. Marie, Ontario">\n        Sault Ste. Marie, Ontario\n       </a>\n       . It then continued swiftly northeastward across\n       <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">\n        Ontario\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">\n        Quebec\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Labrador" title="Labrador">\n        Labrador\n       </a>\n       , before the remnants dissipated over the\n       <a href="/wiki/Labrador_Sea" title="Labrador Sea">\n        Labrador Sea\n       </a>\n       on September\xc2\xa017.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">\n        Preparations\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Carla&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Occurring a year after\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Donna" title="Hurricane Donna">\n        Hurricane Donna\n       </a>\n       , officials in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Florida_Keys" title="Florida Keys">\n        Florida Keys\n       </a>\n       took precautions to brace for potential impact from the storm. The\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">\n        United States Navy\n       </a>\n       flew their planes out of\n       <a href="/wiki/Key_West" title="Key West">\n        Key West\n       </a>\n       , while\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">\n        Coast Guardsmen\n       </a>\n       evacuated\n       <a href="/wiki/Alligator_Reef_Light" title="Alligator Reef Light">\n        Alligator Reef Light\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/American_Shoal_Light" title="American Shoal Light">\n        American Shoal Light\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Sombrero_Key_Light" title="Sombrero Key Light">\n        Sombrero Key Light\n       </a>\n       . Ships docked at Key West sailed out to sea to ride out the squalls. On the morning of September\xc2\xa07, a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">\n        hurricane watch\n       </a>\n       was issued from the entire coast of Louisiana eastward to\n       <a href="/wiki/Apalachicola,_Florida" title="Apalachicola, Florida">\n        Apalachicola, Florida\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On September\xc2\xa08, a hurricane watch was issued along the southwest coast of the state from\n       <a href="/wiki/Vermilion_Bay_(Louisiana)" title="Vermilion Bay (Louisiana)">\n        Vermilion Bay\n       </a>\n       westward.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       It was later extended westward to include the entire coast of Texas, and eastward to Apalachicola, Florida.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A portion of the hurricane watch from\n       <a href="/wiki/Aransas_Pass,_Texas" title="Aransas Pass, Texas">\n        Aransas Pass, Texas\n       </a>\n       to\n       <a href="/wiki/Grand_Isle,_Louisiana" title="Grand Isle, Louisiana">\n        Grand Isle, Louisiana\n       </a>\n       was upgraded to a hurricane warning at 1600\xc2\xa0UTC on September\xc2\xa09.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       An estimated 500,000\xc2\xa0people fled the coasts of Texas and Louisiana, making it the largest evacuation in the history of the United States, at the time.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-new03_10-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-new03-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Further, at least half of those people were from Texas. In small towns along the coast of Texas, 90\xe2\x80\x93100% of their populations evacuated, while 20% of people left the larger cities. Many in\n       <a href="/wiki/Galveston,_Texas" title="Galveston, Texas">\n        Galveston\n       </a>\n       stayed due to protection from the\n       <a href="/wiki/Galveston_Seawall" title="Galveston Seawall">\n        Galveston Seawall\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pshaus1_11-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-pshaus1-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">\n        Impact\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Carla&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Impact">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Carla spawned the largest hurricane-related tornado outbreak on record at the time, when 26\xc2\xa0tornadoes touched down within its circulation. However, it was overwhelmingly surpassed by\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Beulah" title="Hurricane Beulah">\n        Hurricane Beulah\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/1967_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1967 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1967\n       </a>\n       , which spawned at least 115\xc2\xa0tornadoes.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-beulah_12-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-beulah-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Throughout its path, 43\xc2\xa0fatalities and about $325.74\xc2\xa0million in damage were attributed to Carla. Most of the impact occurred in Texas, where the storm made landfall as a large and strong Category\xc2\xa04 hurricane.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata961_13-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-stormdata961-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata1261_14-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-stormdata1261-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr2_15-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr2-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n and\n       <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">\n        storm surge\n       </a>\n       was reported along the coast of Texas, reaching at least 10 feet (3.0\xc2\xa0m) above mean sea level at many coastal areas between\n       <a href="/wiki/Sabine_Pass" title="Sabine Pass">\n        Sabine Pass\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Port_Aransas,_Texas" title="Port Aransas, Texas">\n        Port Aransas\n       </a>\n       . The highest tide reported was 18.5 feet (5.6\xc2\xa0m) at Port Lavaca, with large wave heights including 12.6 feet (3.8\xc2\xa0m) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Matagorda,_Texas" title="Matagorda, Texas">\n        Matagorda\n       </a>\n       , 10.9 feet (3.3\xc2\xa0m) in Port Aransas, 10.8 feet (3.3\xc2\xa0m) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Freeport,_Texas" title="Freeport, Texas">\n        Freeport\n       </a>\n       , 10.1 feet (3.1\xc2\xa0m) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Texas_City,_Texas" title="Texas City, Texas">\n        Texas City\n       </a>\n       , and 10 feet (3.0\xc2\xa0m) in Galveston and\n       <a href="/wiki/Sabine_Pass,_Port_Arthur,_Texas" title="Sabine Pass, Port Arthur, Texas">\n        Sabine\n       </a>\n       . Because the storm was a Category\xc2\xa04 hurricane at landfall, Carla produced strong winds, especially near its path. The strongest sustained winds include 115\xc2\xa0mph (185\xc2\xa0km/h) in Matagorda, 110\xc2\xa0mph (180\xc2\xa0km/h) in Victoria, and 88\xc2\xa0mph (142\xc2\xa0km/h). Additionally, the highest wind gust observations include 175\xc2\xa0mph (282\xc2\xa0km/h) in Port Lavaca, 160\xc2\xa0mph (260\xc2\xa0km/h) in Matagorda, and 150\xc2\xa0mph (240\xc2\xa0km/h) in Aransas Pass,\n       <a href="/wiki/Austwell,_Texas" title="Austwell, Texas">\n        Austwell\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Edna,_Texas" title="Edna, Texas">\n        Edna\n       </a>\n       , Port Aransas, and Victoria. Carla produced heavy rainfall in Texas, peaking at 17.48 inches (444\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Bay_City,_Texas" title="Bay City, Texas">\n        Bay City\n       </a>\n       . Other significant precipitation totals include 16.49 inches (419\xc2\xa0mm) at\n       <a href="/wiki/Scholes_International_Airport_at_Galveston" title="Scholes International Airport at Galveston">\n        Scholes International Airport at Galveston\n       </a>\n       , 14.94 inches (379\xc2\xa0mm) in Downtown Galveston, 13.05 inches (331\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Wharton,_Texas" title="Wharton, Texas">\n        Wharton\n       </a>\n       , 12.55 inches (319\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Liberty,_Texas" title="Liberty, Texas">\n        Liberty\n       </a>\n       , 12.47 inches (317\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Dickinson,_Texas" title="Dickinson, Texas">\n        Dickinson\n       </a>\n       , 11.81 inches (300\xc2\xa0mm) in Flatonia, 10.59 inches (269\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Columbus,_Texas" title="Columbus, Texas">\n        Columbus\n       </a>\n       , 8.75 inches (222\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Hallettsville,_Texas" title="Hallettsville, Texas">\n        Hallettsville\n       </a>\n       , and 8.9 inches (230\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Smithville,_Texas" title="Smithville, Texas">\n        Smithville\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pshaus1_11-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-pshaus1-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div </div>\n      <p>\n       The most significant damage to property occurred between\n       <a href="/wiki/Port_Arthur,_Texas" title="Port Arthur, Texas">\n        Port Arthur\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Corpus_Christi,_Texas" title="Corpus Christi, Texas">\n        Corpus Christi\n       </a>\n       . Port O\'Connor, lying nearest to the location of Carla\'s landfall, was virtually destroyed. In\n       <a href="/wiki/Victoria,_Texas" title="Victoria, Texas">\n        Victoria\n       </a>\n       , the highest sustained wind speed was 110\xc2\xa0mph (180\xc2\xa0km/h), while gusts reached 150\xc2\xa0mph (240\xc2\xa0km/h). About 4,260\xc2\xa0homes were damaged, with around 500 severely damaged or destroyed. 43\xc2\xa0businesses and 26\xc2\xa0public buildings were also significantly impacted. Damage in the city of Victoria reached $10\xc2\xa0million.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">\n        <a href="#cite_note-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Of the 26\xc2\xa0tornadoes spawned by Carla,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-beulah_12-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-beulah-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       eight of them in Texas caused significant impact. A tornado near\n       <a href="/wiki/Bay_City,_Texas" title="Bay City, Texas">\n        Bay City\n       </a>\n       destroyed two radio towers and damaged several buildings. Near\n       <a href="/wiki/Jacksonville,_Texas" title="Jacksonville, Texas">\n        Jacksonville\n       </a>\n       , a tornado injured three people, caused the destruction of one house and impacted 3\xc2\xa0others, resulting in $25,000\xc2\xa0in damage. Twenty-two people were injured, 18\xc2\xa0homes and 6\xc2\xa0commercial buildings were destroyed, and 40\xc2\xa0additional homes were damaged in a tornado in Channelview, located near\n       <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">\n        Houston\n       </a>\n       ; losses reached $200,000. In the early morning hours of September\xc2\xa013, an F4 tornado moved across\n       <a href="/wiki/Galveston_Island" title="Galveston Island">\n        Galveston Island\n       </a>\n       along a 15-mile (24-kilometer) long, 230-yard-wide path (it crossed into Galveston Bay as well), severely damaging 200\xc2\xa0buildings, of which at least 60\xc2\xa0were destroyed, and causing eight deaths and 55\xc2\xa0injuries. A few hours later, another twister in the area resulted in the destruction of six houses and extensive lesser damage. In\n       <a href="/wiki/Hardin,_Texas" title="Hardin, Texas">\n        Hardin\n       </a>\n       , a tornado damaged six\xc2\xa0homes. Several structures sustained impact during a tornado in Fulbright. The final twister occurred during the late afternoon hours in\n       <a href="/wiki/Latex,_Texas" title="Latex, Texas">\n        Latex\n       </a>\n       . It caused two\xc2\xa0injuries, and resulted in $5,000\xc2\xa0in losses, after damaging two\xc2\xa0houses and three\xc2\xa0garages.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata961_13-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-stormdata961-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Galvestonstormmarkers.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3264" data-file-width="2448" height="293" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e1/Galvestonstormmarkers.jpg/220px-Galvestonstormmarkers.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e1/Galvestonstormmarkers.jpg/330px-Galvestonstormmarkers.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e1/Galvestonstormmarkers.jpg/440px-Galvestonstormmarkers.jpg 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Galvestonstormmarkers.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Sign showing the highest water level of Hurricane Carla in\n         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Galveston" title="Galveston">\n          Galveston\n         </a>\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Then little-known newsman\n       <a href="/wiki/Dan_Rather" title="Dan Rather">\n        Dan Rather\n       </a>\n       reported live from the second floor of a building in Texas City during the storm, an act that would be imitated by later reporters. This marked the first live\n       <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">\n        television\n       </a>\n       broadcast of a hurricane. Rather also alerted the public of the size of Carla in a way that "literally changed the way the world sees hurricanes", according to a fellow reporter. Broadcasting live at the Weather Bureau Office in Galveston, Rather asked a meteorologist to draw an outline of the Gulf of Mexico on a transparent sheet of plastic. He then held the map over the black and white radar screen, which put the size of Carla into perspective, saying that Carla was the size of the Gulf of Mexico.\n       <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">\n        CBS\n       </a>\n       was so impressed with Rather\'s work that he was offered the position of correspondent.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">\n        <a href="#cite_note-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Throughout Texas, Carla destroyed 1,915\xc2\xa0homes, 568\xc2\xa0farm buildings, and 415\xc2\xa0other buildings. Additionally, 50,723\xc2\xa0homes, 5,620\xc2\xa0farm buildings, and 10,487\xc2\xa0other buildings suffered damage. There were 460\xc2\xa0injuries according to the\n       <a href="/wiki/American_Red_Cross" title="American Red Cross">\n        American Red Cross\n       </a>\n       , though the\n       <a href="/wiki/Monthly_Weather_Review" title="Monthly Weather Review">\n        Monthly Weather Review\n       </a>\n       listed a slightly higher number, 465. The storm caused 34\xc2\xa0fatalities in Texas. Causes of death include 20\xc2\xa0people drowning, eight from tornadoes, four electrocutions, and one heart attack.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">\n        <a href="#cite_note-19">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Overall, damage in the state was "conservatively" estimated at $300\xc2\xa0million.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata961_13-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-stormdata961-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A breakdown of damage indicates $200\xc2\xa0million incurred to property and $100\xc2\xa0million to crops, mostly from unharvested rice and lesser impact to cotton and citrus.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata961_13-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-stormdata961-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h4>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Louisiana">\n        Louisiana\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Carla&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Louisiana">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h4>\n      <p>\n       In southeastern Louisiana, abnormally high tides lashed the coast, though no beach erosion was reported. Rainfall was heavy, peaking at 13.9 inches (350\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Many,_Louisiana" title="Many, Louisiana">\n        Many\n       </a>\n       . Other observed precipitation totals include 5.6 inches (140\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Baton_Rouge,_Louisiana" title="Baton Rouge, Louisiana">\n        Baton Rouge\n       </a>\n       , 5.57 inches (141\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Morgan_City,_Louisiana" title="Morgan City, Louisiana">\n        Morgan City\n       </a>\n       , 4.25 inches (108\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Shreveport,_Louisiana" title="Shreveport, Louisiana">\n        Shreveport\n       </a>\n       , 3.16 inches (80\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Orleans,_Louisiana" title="New Orleans, Louisiana">\n        New Orleans\n       </a>\n       , 2.25 inches (57\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Lafayette,_Louisiana" title="Lafayette, Louisiana">\n        Lafayette\n       </a>\n       , 2.19 inches (56\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Alexandria,_Louisiana" title="Alexandria, Louisiana">\n        Alexandria\n       </a>\n       , 2.1 inches (53\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Jonesville,_Louisiana" title="Jonesville, Louisiana">\n        Jonesville\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/West_Monroe,_Louisiana" title="West Monroe, Louisiana">\n        West Monroe\n       </a>\n       , and 1.37 inches (35\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Lake_Charles,_Louisiana" title="Lake Charles, Louisiana">\n        Lake Charles\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pshnew_20-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-pshnew-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In the western portions of Louisiana, sustained winds peaked at 44\xc2\xa0mph (71\xc2\xa0km/h) and gusts reached 55\xc2\xa0mph (89\xc2\xa0km/h). Only minor damage occurred, limited to roofs, glass, and downed tree limbs. Winds caused a local river to reach 1 foot (0.30\xc2\xa0m) above\n       <a href="/wiki/Flood_stage" title="Flood stage">\n        flood stage\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">\n        <a href="#cite_note-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The 11\xc2\xa0tornadoes spawned by Carla in Louisiana destroyed 140\xc2\xa0homes and 11\xc2\xa0farm and other buildings, while causing major damage to 231\xc2\xa0additional homes and 11\xc2\xa0farm and other buildings. Additionally, 748\xc2\xa0homes and 75\xc2\xa0farm and other buildings suffered minor to moderate damage. Six fatalities and 199\xc2\xa0injuries occurred, mostly related to the tornadoes.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata1261_14-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-stormdata1261-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Damage in Louisiana totaled to about $25\xc2\xa0million. A breakdown of losses include $6\xc2\xa0million to agriculture, $5\xc2\xa0million to buildings, $4\xc2\xa0million to boats and beach property, $3\xc2\xa0million to roads and bridges, $5\xc2\xa0million to\n       <a href="/wiki/Oil_platform" title="Oil platform">\n        oil platforms\n       </a>\n       just offshore, and $2\xc2\xa0million in miscellaneous damage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr2_15-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr2-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h4>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Oklahoma">\n        Oklahoma\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Carla&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Oklahoma">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h4>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Carla_1961_rainfall.png">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="916" data-file-width="871" height="263" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/Carla_1961_rainfall.png/250px-Carla_1961_rainfall.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/Carla_1961_rainfall.png/375px-Carla_1961_rainfall.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/Carla_1961_rainfall.png/500px-Carla_1961_rainfall.png 2x" width="250"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Carla_1961_rainfall.png" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Rainfall from Carla in the United States\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Carla and its remnants brought heavy rainfall and strong winds to portions of Oklahoma. About 2\xe2\x80\x938 inches (51\xe2\x80\x93203\xc2\xa0mm) of rain fell in\n       <a href="/wiki/Grant_County,_Oklahoma" title="Grant County, Oklahoma">\n        Grant\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Tillman_County,_Oklahoma" title="Tillman County, Oklahoma">\n        Tillman\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Washita_County,_Oklahoma" title="Washita County, Oklahoma">\n        Washita Counties\n       </a>\n       . Heavier amounts of at least 5 inches (130\xc2\xa0mm) fell in\n       <a href="/wiki/Atoka_County,_Oklahoma" title="Atoka County, Oklahoma">\n        Atoka\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Pushmataha_County,_Oklahoma" title="Pushmataha County, Oklahoma">\n        Pushmataha County\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata1261_14-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-stormdata1261-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Precipitation in the state peaked at 7.68 inches (195\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Kingfisher,_Oklahoma" title="Kingfisher, Oklahoma">\n        Kingfisher\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcmidwest_22-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcmidwest-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       More than 140\xc2\xa0city blocks were flooded with up to 6 feet (1.8\xc2\xa0m) in residential areas of Kingfisher and up to 3 feet (0.91\xc2\xa0m) in business areas. 225\xc2\xa0homes and buildings in the city suffered water damaged. Throughout the state, damage in other areas included 90\xc2\xa0homes and buildings in\n       <a href="/wiki/Tulsa,_Oklahoma" title="Tulsa, Oklahoma">\n        Tulsa\n       </a>\n       , 50\xc2\xa0homes each in El Reno and\n       <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma_City" title="Oklahoma City">\n        Oklahoma City\n       </a>\n       , and 10\xc2\xa0homes in\n       <a href="/wiki/Pawnee,_Oklahoma" title="Pawnee, Oklahoma">\n        Pawnee\n       </a>\n       . Additionally, water inundation washed out several bridges and highways in northeastern parts of the state, leaving roads impassable. Sustained winds between 30 and 55\xc2\xa0mph (48 and 89\xc2\xa0km/h) and gusts up to 70\xc2\xa0mph (110\xc2\xa0km/h) were recorded in Oklahoma. Winds injured two people due to airborne debris. Winds and rain combined severely disrupted electrical and telephone services and caused $670,000\xc2\xa0in damage, with $600,000\xc2\xa0to property and $70,000\xc2\xa0to crops.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata1261_14-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-stormdata1261-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h4>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Illinois">\n        Illinois\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Carla&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Illinois">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h4>\n      <p>\n       The storm dropped precipitation amounts of 7 inches (180\xc2\xa0mm) throughout northwestern\n       <a href="/wiki/Illinois" title="Illinois">\n        Illinois\n       </a>\n       , with a peak of 8.18 inches (208\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Mount_Carroll,_Illinois" title="Mount Carroll, Illinois">\n        Mount Carroll\n       </a>\n       . Thus, Carla is the\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_wettest_tropical_cyclones_in_the_United_States#Illinois" title="List of wettest tropical cyclones in the United States">\n        second-wettest tropical cyclone in Illinois\n       </a>\n       , behind only\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Audrey" title="Hurricane Audrey">\n        Hurricane Audrey\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/1957_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1957 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1957\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcmidwest_22-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcmidwest-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Additionally, up to 6.29 inches (160\xc2\xa0mm) fell in 24\xc2\xa0hours. Severe local flooding occurred, especially in the northern portions of the state. Heavy property and agricultural damage occurred. Streets and basements were reported to have flooded, while bridge approaches and a few small bridges were washed out. Precipitation of 2.5\xe2\x80\x933.5 inches (64\xe2\x80\x9389\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Chicago" title="Chicago">\n        Chicago\n       </a>\n       flooded about 60\xc2\xa0viaducts and 1,000\xc2\xa0basements. After the\n       <a href="/wiki/Chicago_River" title="Chicago River">\n        Chicago River\n       </a>\n       rose 5 feet (1.5\xc2\xa0m), the lock gate to\n       <a href="/wiki/Lake_Michigan" title="Lake Michigan">\n        Lake Michigan\n       </a>\n       was opened to release excess water. However, the abrupt onrush of water resulted in about $75,000\xc2\xa0in damage to boats at\n       <a href="/wiki/Wilmette,_Illinois" title="Wilmette, Illinois">\n        Wilmette Harbor\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata961_13-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-stormdata961-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr2_15-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr2-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h4>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Elsewhere_in_the_United_States">\n        Elsewhere in the United States\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Carla&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Elsewhere in the United States">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h4>\n      <p>\n       Winds in Key West reached 30\xc2\xa0mph (48\xc2\xa0km/h), while much of\n       <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">\n        Florida\n       </a>\n       reported rainfall from the outer bands of Carla.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">\n        <a href="#cite_note-23">\n         [23]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In Kansas, 4 to 7 inches (100 to 180\xc2\xa0mm) of rain in only 6\xc2\xa0hours caused flash flooding. With a peak amount of 8.52 inches (216\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Haddam,_Kansas" title="Haddam, Kansas">\n        Haddam\n       </a>\n       , Carla was the rainiest tropical cyclone in Kansas, until being surpassed by\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Paine_(1986)" title="Hurricane Paine (1986)">\n        Hurricane Paine\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/1986_Pacific_hurricane_season" title="1986 Pacific hurricane season">\n        1986\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_Storm_Frances_(1998)" title="Tropical Storm Frances (1998)">\n        Tropical Storm Frances\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/1998_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1998 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1998\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcmidwest_22-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcmidwest-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Severe crop damage occurred, especially in areas of\n       <a href="/wiki/Chautauqua_County,_Kansas" title="Chautauqua County, Kansas">\n        Chautauqua\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Shawnee_County,_Kansas" title="Shawnee County, Kansas">\n        Shawnee Counties\n       </a>\n       and to the south of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Kansas_River" title="Kansas River">\n        Kansas River\n       </a>\n       . Many highways were left impassable due to water inundation. A family of 4\xc2\xa0drowned after their car was swept off a road near\n       <a href="/wiki/Waverly,_Kansas" title="Waverly, Kansas">\n        Waverly\n       </a>\n       . A fifth fatality occurred after a car with one occupant was swept into a tributary of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Marmaton_River" title="Marmaton River">\n        Marmaton River\n       </a>\n       near\n       <a href="/wiki/Fort_Scott,_Kansas" title="Fort Scott, Kansas">\n        Fort Scott\n       </a>\n       . The storm persistently produced winds of 35 to 50\xc2\xa0mph (56 to 80\xc2\xa0km/h), causing further agricultural damage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata961_13-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-stormdata961-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Nebraska" title="Nebraska">\n        Nebraska\n       </a>\n       , rainfall peaked at 5.68 inches (144\xc2\xa0mm) in Hubbrell, making Carla the rainiest tropical cyclone in the state.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcmidwest_22-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcmidwest-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Heavy precipitation also fell in\n       <a href="/wiki/Missouri" title="Missouri">\n        Missouri\n       </a>\n       , especially in\n       <a href="/wiki/Brookfield,_Missouri" title="Brookfield, Missouri">\n        Brookfield\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Lee%27s_Summit,_Missouri" title="Lee\'s Summit, Missouri">\n        Lee\'s Summit\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Pleasant_Hill,_Missouri" title="Pleasant Hill, Missouri">\n        Pleasant Hill\n       </a>\n       . The maximum rainfall total in Missouri was 9.34 inches (237\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Concordia,_Missouri" title="Concordia, Missouri">\n        Concordia\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcmidwest_22-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcmidwest-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       One fatality was reported in Missouri, though the cause of death is unknown. Rainfall in\n       <a href="/wiki/Iowa" title="Iowa">\n        Iowa\n       </a>\n       peaked at 9.03 inches (229\xc2\xa0mm) near\n       <a href="/wiki/Chariton,_Iowa" title="Chariton, Iowa">\n        Chariton\n       </a>\n       , making Carla the rainiest tropical cyclone in the state.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcmidwest_22-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcmidwest-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Up to 8 inches (200\xc2\xa0mm) of rain fell in a 12-hour period in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Lower_Peninsula_of_Michigan" title="Lower Peninsula of Michigan">\n        Lower Peninsula of Michigan\n       </a>\n       , mostly between\n       <a href="/wiki/Cheboygan,_Michigan" title="Cheboygan, Michigan">\n        Cheboygan\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Grand_Rapids,_Michigan" title="Grand Rapids, Michigan">\n        Grand Rapids\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata961_13-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-stormdata961-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       However, the\n       <a href="/wiki/Weather_Prediction_Center" title="Weather Prediction Center">\n        Weather Prediction Center\n       </a>\n       indicated precipitation amounts of 6.07 inches (154\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Boyne_Falls,_Michigan" title="Boyne Falls, Michigan">\n        Boyne Falls\n       </a>\n       . Regardless, Carla is the wettest tropical cyclone in the history of Michigan.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcmidwest_22-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcmidwest-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Despite, the rainfall, flooding in\n       <a href="/wiki/Michigan" title="Michigan">\n        Michigan\n       </a>\n       was relatively minimal, limited to an washed out dam in\n       <a href="/wiki/Traverse_City,_Michigan" title="Traverse City, Michigan">\n        Traverse City\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata961_13-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-stormdata961-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Precipitation in\n       <a href="/wiki/Wisconsin" title="Wisconsin">\n        Wisconsin\n       </a>\n       reached 7.58 inches (193\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Brodhead,_Wisconsin" title="Brodhead, Wisconsin">\n        Brodhead\n       </a>\n       , causing Carla to rank as the wettest tropical cyclone in the state.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcmidwest_22-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcmidwest-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Precipitation peaks in other states include 4.44 inches (113\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">\n        Alabama\n       </a>\n       , 5.48 inches (139\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Arkansas" title="Arkansas">\n        Arkansas\n       </a>\n       , 6.37 inches (162\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">\n        Mississippi\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcgulfcoast_24-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcgulfcoast-24">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and 3.6 inches (91\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Indiana" title="Indiana">\n        Indiana\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcmidwest_22-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcmidwest-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Elsewhere">\n        Elsewhere\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Carla&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Elsewhere">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       In Cuba, broadcasts reported severe flooding near\n       <a href="/wiki/Pinar_del_R%C3%ADo" title="Pinar del R\xc3\xado">\n        Pinar del R\xc3\xado\n       </a>\n       , and in Los Colomas and Punta de Cartas. Offshore\n       <a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">\n        Honduras\n       </a>\n       on Swan Island, a rainfall amount of 3.89 inches (99\xc2\xa0mm) was reported along with wind gusts up to 60\xc2\xa0mph (95\xc2\xa0km/h). The remnants of Carla brought tropical storm force winds to some areas of Canada. In\n       <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">\n        Ontario\n       </a>\n       , gusty winds caused power outages in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Clarkson,_Mississauga" title="Clarkson, Mississauga">\n        Clarkson\n       </a>\n       \xe2\x80\x93\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Port_Credit,_Ontario" title="Port Credit, Ontario">\n        Port Credit\n       </a>\n       area. Electrical services were are disrupted in\n       <a href="/wiki/New_Brunswick" title="New Brunswick">\n        New Brunswick\n       </a>\n       , due to winds up to 80\xc2\xa0mph (130\xc2\xa0km/h) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Saint_John,_New_Brunswick" title="Saint John, New Brunswick">\n        Saint John\n       </a>\n       . Winds of 57\xc2\xa0mph (92\xc2\xa0km/h) lashed\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Halifax_Regional_Municipality" title="Halifax Regional Municipality">\n        Halifax\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">\n        Nova Scotia\n       </a>\n       , thus the\n       <a href="/wiki/Halifax_Public_Gardens" title="Halifax Public Gardens">\n        Halifax Public Gardens\n       </a>\n       closed for threat of falling trees and branches.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">\n        <a href="#cite_note-25">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">\n        Aftermath\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Carla&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Aftermath">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       See also:\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricane names">\n        List of retired Atlantic hurricane names\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Shortly after the storm,\n       <a href="/wiki/Vice_President_of_the_United_States" title="Vice President of the United States">\n        Vice President\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Lyndon_B._Johnson" title="Lyndon B. Johnson">\n        Lyndon B. Johnson\n       </a>\n       , a lifelong resident of Texas, toured the devastation in the state and met with officials from 18\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_counties_in_Texas" title="List of counties in Texas">\n        different counties\n       </a>\n       , remarking that "as always, Texans are arising to a challenge... We have suffered hurricane, tornado, and floods, but we in Texas are a bold and brave people."\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26">\n        <a href="#cite_note-26">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       After his report to\n       <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">\n        President\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/John_F._Kennedy" title="John F. Kennedy">\n        John F. Kennedy\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress" title="United States Congress">\n        Congress\n       </a>\n       , $55\xc2\xa0million was approved in aid for Texas, though Kennedy later requested an additional $65\xc2\xa0million.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tmn_27-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tmn-27">\n         [27]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       President Kennedy issued a disaster declaration for Texas, allowing all jurisdictions in the state to apply for public assistance.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28">\n        <a href="#cite_note-28">\n         [28]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The president also declared 6\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_parishes_in_Louisiana" title="List of parishes in Louisiana">\n        parishes in Louisiana\n       </a>\n       a disaster area.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tmn_27-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tmn-27">\n         [27]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Helicopters deployed 88\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Marine_Corps" title="United States Marine Corps">\n        United States Marines\n       </a>\n       to Galveston to patrol devastated areas. A\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">\n        United States Navy\n       </a>\n       task force composed of medical and construction work teams was also sent to the area. Members of the\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Guard_of_the_United_States" title="National Guard of the United States">\n        United States National Guard\n       </a>\n       assisted refugees in returning to their homes and worked to prevent looting.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29">\n        <a href="#cite_note-29">\n         [29]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Owing the hurricane\'s intensity and destruction, the name Carla was retired and will never again be used for an\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_tropical_cyclone" title="North Atlantic tropical cyclone">\n        Atlantic hurricane\n       </a>\n       . It was replaced by Carol in the\n       <a href="/wiki/1965_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1965 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1965 season\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30">\n        <a href="#cite_note-30">\n         [30]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">\n        See
