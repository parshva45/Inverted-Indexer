<p>\n       <b>\n        Progress M-48\n       </b>\n       , identified by\n       <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">\n        NASA\n       </a>\n       as\n       <b>\n        Progress 12\n       </b>\n       or\n       <b>\n        12P\n       </b>\n       , was a\n       <a href="/wiki/Progress_(spacecraft)" title="Progress (spacecraft)">\n        Progress\n       </a>\n       spacecraft used to resupply the\n       <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">\n        International Space Station\n       </a>\n       . It was a\n       <a href="/wiki/Progress-M" title="Progress-M">\n        Progress-M\n       </a>\n       11F615A55 spacecraft, with the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Serial_number" title="Serial number">\n        serial number\n       </a>\n       248.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JSR-LL_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-JSR-LL-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Progress M-48 was launched by a\n       <a href="/wiki/Soyuz-U" title="Soyuz-U">\n        Soyuz-U\n       </a>\n       carrier rocket from\n       <a href="/wiki/Gagarin%27s_Start" title="Gagarin\'s Start">\n        Site 1/5\n       </a>\n       at the\n       <a href="/wiki/Baikonur_Cosmodrome" title="Baikonur Cosmodrome">\n        Baikonur Cosmodrome\n       </a>\n       . Launch occurred at 01:47:59 GMT on 29 August 2003.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JSR-LL_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-JSR-LL-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The spacecraft docked with the Aft port of the\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Zvezda_(ISS_module)" title="Zvezda (ISS module)">\n         Zvezda\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       module at 03:40:45 GMT on 31 August.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SKN_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SKN-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EA_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-EA-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       It remained docked for five months before undocking at 08:35:56 GMT on 28 January 2004\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SKN_2-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SKN-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       to make way for\n       <a href="/wiki/Progress_M1-11" title="Progress M1-11">\n        Progress M1-11\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RSW_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-RSW-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       It was deorbited at 13:11 GMT on the same day.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SKN_2-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SKN-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The spacecraft burned up in the atmosphere over the\n       <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">\n        Pacific Ocean\n       </a>\n       , with any remaining debris landing in the ocean at around 13:57:12 GMT.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SKN_2-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SKN-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JSR-SC_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-JSR-SC-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Progress M-48 carried supplies to the International Space
