a\n       <b>\n        cold snap\n       </b>\n       ) is a weather phenomenon that is distinguished by a cooling of the air. Specifically, as used by the U.S.\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Weather_Service" title="National Weather Service">\n        National Weather Service\n       </a>\n       , a cold wave is a rapid fall in temperature within a 24-hour period requiring substantially increased protection to agriculture, industry, commerce, and social activities. The precise criterion for a cold wave is determined by the rate at which the temperature falls, and the minimum to which it falls. This minimum temperature is dependent on the geographical region and time of year.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In the United States, a\n       <i>\n        cold spell\n       </i>\n       is defined as the national average high temperature dropping below 20\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0F (\xe2\x88\x927\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0C).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n id="Effects">\n        Effects\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cold_wave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Effects">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       A cold wave can cause death and injury to livestock and wildlife. Exposure to cold mandates greater\n       <a href="/wiki/Food_energy" title="Food energy">\n        caloric intake\n       </a>\n       for all animals, including humans, and if a cold wave is accompanied by heavy and persistent snow, grazing animals may be unable to reach needed food and die of\n       <a href="/wiki/Hypothermia" title="Hypothermia">\n        hypothermia\n       </a>\n       or starvation. They often necessitate the purchase of foodstuffs to feed livestock at considerable cost to farmers.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Cold spells are associated with increased mortality rates in populations around the world.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JaakkolaGuo2015_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-JaakkolaGuo2015-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Both cold waves and heat waves cause deaths, though different groups of people may be susceptible to different weather events.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JoacimBertil2014_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-JoacimBertil2014-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Developed_countries" title="Developed countries">\n        developed countries\n       </a>\n       , more deaths occur during a heat wave than in a cold snap, though the mortality rate is higher in undeveloped regions of the world. Globally, more people die of cold weather than hot weather, due to the rise in diseases like cold, flu, and pneumonia.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Extreme winter cold often causes poorly\n       <a href="/wiki/Thermal_insulation" title="Thermal insulation">\n        insulated\n       </a>\n       water\n       <a href="/wiki/Pipeline_transport" title="Pipeline transport">\n        pipelines\n       </a>\n       and mains to freeze. Even some poorly protected indoor\n       <a href="/wiki/Plumbing" title="Plumbing">\n        plumbing\n       </a>\n       ruptures as water expands within them, causing much damage to property and costly insurance claims. Demand for electrical power and\n       <a href="/wiki/Fuel" title="Fuel">\n        fuels\n       </a>\n       rises dramatically during such times, even though the generation of electrical power may fail due to the freezing of water necessary for the generation of\n       <a href="/wiki/Hydroelectricity" title="Hydroelectricity">\n        hydroelectricity\n       </a>\n       . Some metals may become brittle at low temperatures. Motor vehicles may fail when\n       <a href="/wiki/Antifreeze" title="Antifreeze">\n        antifreeze\n       </a>\n       fails or motor oil gels, producing a failure of the transportation system. To be sure, such is more likely in places like\n       <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">\n        Siberia\n       </a>\n       and much of Canada that customarily get very cold weather.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Fires become even more of a hazard during extreme cold. Water mains may break and water supplies may become unreliable, making\n       <a href="/wiki/Firefighting" title="Firefighting">\n        firefighting\n       </a>\n       more difficult. The air during a cold wave is typically denser and thus contains more oxygen, so when air that a fire draws in becomes unusually cold it is likely to cause a more intense fire.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Winter cold waves that aren\xe2\x80\x99t considered cold in some areas, but cause temperatures significantly below average for an area, are also destructive. Areas with subtropical climates may recognize unusual cold, perhaps barely freezing, temperatures, as a cold wave. In such places, plant and animal life is less tolerant of such cold as may appear rarely. The same winter temperatures that one associates with the norm for\n       <a href="/wiki/Colorado" title="Colorado">\n        Colorado\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Ohio" title="Ohio">\n        Ohio\n       </a>\n       , or\n       <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">\n        Bavaria\n       </a>\n       are catastrophic to winter crops in places like\n       <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">\n        Florida\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">\n        California\n       </a>\n       , or parts of\n       <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">\n        South America\n       </a>\n       that grow fruit and vegetables in winter.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Cold waves that bring unexpected freezes and frosts during the growing season in mid-latitude zones can kill plants during the early and most vulnerable stages of growth, resulting in crop failure as plants are killed before they can be\n       <a href="/wiki/Harvest" title="Harvest">\n        harvested\n       </a>\n       economically. Such cold waves have caused\n       <a href="/wiki/Famine" title="Famine">\n        famines\n       </a>\n       . At times as deadly to plants as\n       <a href="/wiki/Drought" title="Drought">\n        drought\n       </a>\n       , cold waves can leave a land in danger of later brush and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Forest_fire" title="Forest fire">\n        forest fires\n       </a>\n       that consume dead biomass. One extreme was the so-called\n       <a href="/wiki/Year_Without_a_Summer" title="Year Without a Summer">\n        Year Without a Summer\n       </a>\n       of 1816, one of several years during the 1810s in which numerous crops failed during freakish summer cold snaps after\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Volcanic_eruption" title="Volcanic eruption">\n        volcanic eruptions\n       </a>\n       that reduced incoming sunlight.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Countermeasures">\n        Countermeasures\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cold_wave&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Countermeasures">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       In some places, such as\n       <a href="/wiki/Siberia" title="Siberia">\n        Siberia\n       </a>\n       , extreme cold requires that fuel-powered machinery to be used even part-time must be run continuously. Internal plumbing can be wrapped, and persons can often run water continuously through pipes. Energy conservation, difficult as it is in a cold wave, may require such measures as collecting people (especially the poor and elderly) in communal shelters. Even the\n       <a href="/wiki/Homelessness" title="Homelessness">\n        homeless\n       </a>\n       may be\n       <a href="/wiki/Arrest" title="Arrest">\n        arrested\n       </a>\n       and taken to shelters, only to be released when the hazard abates.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-paducah_nws_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-paducah_nws-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Hospitals can prepare for the admission of victims of frostbite and hypothermia; schools and other public buildings can be converted into shelters.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       People can stock up on food, water, and other necessities before a cold wave. Some may even choose to migrate to places of milder climates, at least during the winter. Suitable stocks of forage can be secured before cold waves for livestock, and livestock in vulnerable areas might be shipped from affected areas or even slaughtered.\n       <a href="/wiki/Smudge_pot" title="Smudge pot">\n        Smudge pots\n       </a>\n       can bring smoke that prevents hard freezes on a farm or grove. Vulnerable crops may be sprayed with water that will paradoxically protect the plants by freezing and absorbing the cold from surrounding air.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Most people can dress appropriately and can even layer their clothing should they need to go outside or should their heating fail. They can also stock candles, matches, flashlights, and portable fuel for cooking and wood for fireplaces or wood stoves, as necessary. However caution should be taken as the use of charcoal fires for cooking or heating within an enclosed dwelling is extremely dangerous due to\n       <a href="/wiki/Carbon_monoxide_poisoning" title="Carbon monoxide poisoning">\n        carbon monoxide poisoning\n       </a>\n       . It was \xe2\x88\x929 degrees at kickoff. The Sunday of the following week (January 17, 1982) is also known as\n        <a href="/wiki/Cold_Sunday" title="Cold Sunday">\n         Cold Sunday\n        </a>\n        .\n       </li>\n      </ul>\n      <ul>\n       <li>\n        Late 1970s (1977, 1978, 1979) \xe2\x80\x93 In the last three years of the 1970s, almost all of the conterminous United States had at least one winter with a memorable cold wave, and the winter of 1978\xe2\x80\x9379 was the coldest on record in the lower 48, with everywhere, except normally frigid upstate Maine, experiencing well below average temperatures.\n       </li>\n      </ul>\n      <ul>\n       <li>\n        Cold wave of 1979 \xe2\x80\x93 widespread cold across the country.    1979 Chicago Blizzard\n        </a>\n        occurred during the cold wave in January.\n       </li>\n      </ul>\n      <ul>\n       <li>\n        <a href="/wiki/Cold_wave_of_1978" title="Cold </ul>\n      <ul>\n       <li>\n        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Winter_of_1968%E2%80%9369_in_Central_Asia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Winter of 1968\xe2\x80\x9369 in Central \xe2\x80\x99s only month where temperatures remained below 32\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0F (0\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0C) throughout.\n       </li>\n      </ul>\n      <ul>\n       <li>\n        <a class="new"
