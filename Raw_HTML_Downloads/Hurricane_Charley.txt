</tr>\n       <tr style="display:none">\n        <td colspan="2">\n        </td>\n       </tr>\n      </table>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        Hurricane Charley\n       </b>\n       was the first of four individual hurricanes to produce winds of at least 75 mph in\n       <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">\n        Florida\n       </a>\n       in a single season\xe2\x80\x94\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Frances" title="Hurricane Frances">\n        Frances\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Ivan" title="Hurricane Ivan">\n        Ivan\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Jeanne" title="Hurricane Jeanne">\n        Jeanne\n       </a>\n       . It was the third named storm, the second\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane" title="Hurricane">\n        hurricane\n       </a>\n       , and the second major hurricane of the\n       <a href="/wiki/2004_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2004 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        2004 Atlantic hurricane season\n       </a>\n       . Charley lasted from August 9 to August 15, and at its peak intensity it attained 150\xc2\xa0mph (240\xc2\xa0km/h) winds, making it a strong Category\xc2\xa04 hurricane on the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">\n        Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale\n       </a>\n       . It made\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">\n        landfall\n       </a>\n       in southwestern\n       <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">\n        Florida\n       </a>\n       at maximum strength, making it the strongest hurricane to hit the United States since\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Andrew" title="Hurricane Andrew">\n        Hurricane Andrew\n       </a>\n       struck Florida in\n       <a href="/wiki/1992_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1992 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1992\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       After moving slowly through the\n       <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">\n        Caribbean Sea\n       </a>\n       , Charley crossed\n       <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">\n        Cuba\n       </a>\n       on Friday, August 13 as a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale#Category_3" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">\n        Category 3\n       </a>\n       hurricane, causing heavy damage and four deaths. That same day, it crossed over the\n       <a href="/wiki/Dry_Tortugas" title="Dry Tortugas">\n        Dry Tortugas\n       </a>\n       , just 22\xc2\xa0hours after\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_Storm_Bonnie_(2004)" title="Tropical Storm Bonnie (2004)">\n        Tropical Storm Bonnie\n       </a>\n       had struck northwestern\n       <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">\n        Florida\n       </a>\n       . It was the first time in history that two tropical cyclones struck the same state in a 24-hour period.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-space.com_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-space.com-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At its peak intensity of 150\xc2\xa0mph (240\xc2\xa0km/h), Hurricane Charley struck the northern tip of\n       <a href="/wiki/Captiva_Island" title="Captiva Island">\n        Captiva Island\n       </a>\n       and the southern tip of North Captiva Island, before crossing over\n       <a href="/wiki/Bokeelia,_Florida" title="Bokeelia, Florida">\n        Bokeelia\n       </a>\n       causing severe damage\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       . Charley, the strongest hurricane to hit southwest Florida since\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Donna" title="Hurricane Donna">\n        Hurricane Donna\n       </a>\n       in 1960, then continued to produce severe damage as it made landfall on the\n       <a href="/wiki/Southwest_Florida" title="Southwest Florida">\n        peninsula\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/Punta_Gorda,_Florida" title="Punta Gorda, Florida">\n        Punta Gorda\n       </a>\n       . It continued to the north-northeast along the Peace River corridor, devastating\n       <a href="/wiki/Punta_Gorda,_Florida" title="Punta Gorda, Florida">\n        Punta Gorda\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Port_Charlotte,_Florida" title="Port Charlotte, Florida">\n        Port Charlotte\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Cleveland,_Florida" title="Cleveland, Florida">\n        Cleveland\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Fort_Ogden,_Florida" title="Fort Ogden, Florida">\n        Fort Ogden\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Nocatee,_DeSoto_County,_Florida" title="Nocatee, DeSoto County, Florida">\n        Nocatee\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Arcadia,_Florida" title="Arcadia, Florida">\n        Arcadia\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Zolfo_Springs,_Florida" title="Zolfo Springs, Florida">\n        Zolfo Springs\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Sebring,_Florida" title="Sebring, Florida">\n        Sebring\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Wauchula,_Florida" title="Wauchula, Florida">\n        Wauchula\n       </a>\n       . Zolfo Springs was isolated for nearly two days as masses of large trees, power poles, power lines, transformers, and debris filled the streets. Wauchula sustained gusts to 147\xc2\xa0mph (237\xc2\xa0km/h); buildings in the downtown areas caved onto Main Street. Ultimately, the storm passed through the central and eastern parts of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Orlando,_Florida" title="Orlando, Florida">\n        Orlando\n       </a>\n       metropolitan area, still carrying winds gusting up to 106\xc2\xa0mph (171\xc2\xa0km/h). The city of Winter Park, north of Orlando, also sustained considerable damage since its many old, large oak trees had not experienced high winds. Falling trees tore down power utilities and smashed cars, and their huge roots lifted underground water and sewer utilities. The storm slowed as it exited the state over Ormond Beach just north of Daytona Beach. The storm was ultimately absorbed by a front in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">\n        Atlantic Ocean\n       </a>\n       shortly after sunrise on August 15, near southeastern\n       <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">\n        Massachusetts\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hpc_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-hpc-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Damage in the state totaled to over $13\xc2\xa0billion (2004\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">\n        USD\n       </a>\n       ). Charley initially was expected to hit further north in\n       <a href="/wiki/Tampa,_Florida" title="Tampa, Florida">\n        Tampa\n       </a>\n       , and caught many Floridians off-guard due to a sudden change in the storm\'s track as it approached the state. Throughout the United States, Charley caused 10\xc2\xa0deaths and $15.4\xc2\xa0billion in damage to insured residential property, making it the second costliest hurricane in United States history at the time (it has since dropped to ninth).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Charley was a compact, fast-moving storm, which limited the scope and severity of the damage.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="toc">\n       <div class="toctitle">\n        <h2>\n  <span class="mw-headline" id="Meteorological_history">\n        Meteorological history\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Charley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span according to the\n         <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">\n          Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale\n         </a>\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Charley began as a tropical wave that moved off the west coast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">\n        Africa\n       </a>\n       on August 4.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_3-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       It moved quickly westward and steadily organized over the open Atlantic Ocean, with\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Convection_(meteorology)" title="Convection (meteorology)">\n        convection\n       </a>\n       developing in curved bands.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_3-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The wave continued to develop as it approached the\n       <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">\n        Lesser Antilles\n       </a>\n       , and became Tropical Depression Three on August 9 while 115\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Statute_mile" title="Statute mile">\n        mi\n       </a>\n       (185\xc2\xa0km) south-southeast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Barbados" title="Barbados">\n        Barbados\n       </a>\n       , near the island of\n       <a href="/wiki/Grenada" title="Grenada">\n        Grenada\n       </a>\n       , however, the threat to Barbados was short-lived.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_3-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2004wmo_6-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2004wmo-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Low upper-level\n       <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">\n        wind shear\n       </a>\n       and well-defined outflow contributed to further intensification, and the depression strengthened on August 10, despite being located in the eastern\n       <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">\n        Caribbean Sea\n       </a>\n       , which is an area not particularly suited to\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclogenesis" title="Tropical cyclogenesis">\n        tropical cyclogenesis\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hpc_4-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-hpc-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At this time, the\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">\n        National Hurricane Center\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Miami,_Florida" title="Miami, Florida">\n        Miami\n       </a>\n       designated the name "Charley."\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_3-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Charley_12_aug_2004_1555Z.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="5200" data-file-width="6600" height="173" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Hurricane_Charley_12_aug_2004_1555Z.jpg/220px-Hurricane_Charley_12_aug_2004_1555Z.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Hurricane_Charley_12_aug_2004_1555Z.jpg/330px-Hurricane_Charley_12_aug_2004_1555Z.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Hurricane_Charley_12_aug_2004_1555Z.jpg/440px-Hurricane_Charley_12_aug_2004_1555Z.jpg 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Charley_12_aug_2004_1555Z.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Hurricane Charley approaching Cuba on August 12\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       A strong\n       <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">\n        ridge\n       </a>\n       of high pressure to the system\'s north forced Charley to change track quickly to the west-northwest. It continued to strengthen and became a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale#Category_1" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">\n        Category 1 hurricane\n       </a>\n       on August 11, while 90\xc2\xa0mi (140\xc2\xa0km) south of\n       <a href="/wiki/Kingston,_Jamaica" title="Kingston, Jamaica">\n        Kingston, Jamaica\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_3-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2004wmo_6-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2004wmo-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The storm was being steered around the periphery of the high pressure area, and as a result, Charley changed direction toward the northwest. The following day, the core passed 40\xc2\xa0mi (64\xc2\xa0km) southwest of\n       <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">\n        Jamaica\n       </a>\n       , affecting the island on August 11 and 12.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hpc_4-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-hpc-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2004wmo_6-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2004wmo-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The storm then passed 15\xc2\xa0mi (24\xc2\xa0km) northeast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Grand_Cayman" title="Grand Cayman">\n        Grand Cayman\n       </a>\n       , reaching Category\xc2\xa02 status just after passing the island.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_3-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hpc_4-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-hpc-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The hurricane continued to strengthen as it turned to the northwest and rounded the southwest portion of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_ridge" title="Subtropical ridge">\n        subtropical ridge\n       </a>\n       , becoming a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Major_hurricane" title="Major hurricane">\n        major hurricane\n       </a>\n       \xe2\x80\x94a storm classified as a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale#Category_3" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">\n        Category 3 hurricane\n       </a>\n       or higher\xe2\x80\x94just before making landfall on southern\n       <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">\n        Cuba\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_3-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Charley came ashore near\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Punta_Cayamas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Punta Cayamas (page does not exist)">\n        Punta Cayamas\n       </a>\n       with maximum sustained winds of 120\xc2\xa0mph (190\xc2\xa0km/h) and gusts of up to 133\xc2\xa0mph (214\xc2\xa0km/h), at about 0430\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UTC" title="UTC">\n        UTC\n       </a>\n       on August 13.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2004wmo_6-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2004wmo-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       It weakened while crossing the island, passing about 15\xc2\xa0mi (24\xc2\xa0km) west of downtown\n       <a href="/wiki/Havana" title="Havana">\n        Havana\n       </a>\n       before weakening to 110\xc2\xa0mph (180\xc2\xa0km/h).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_3-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       After crossing Cuba near\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Menelao_Mora&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Menelao Mora (page does not exist)">\n        Menelao Mora\n       </a>\n       , Hurricane Charley accelerated to the north-northeast, toward the southwest coast of Florida in response to the approach of an unseasonal mid-tropospheric\n       <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">\n        trough\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2004wmo_6-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2004wmo-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Charley passed over the\n       <a href="/wiki/Dry_Tortugas" title="Dry Tortugas">\n        Dry Tortugas\n       </a>\n       at 1200 UTC on August 13, with maximum winds of about 110\xc2\xa0mph (180\xc2\xa0km/h).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_3-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hpc_4-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-hpc-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The strike occurred only 22\xc2\xa0hours after\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_Storm_Bonnie_(2004)" title="Tropical Storm Bonnie (2004)">\n        Tropical Storm Bonnie\n       </a>\n       made landfall on\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Vincent_Island,_Florida" title="St. Vincent Island, Florida">\n        St. Vincent Island\n       </a>\n       , marking the first time two tropical cyclones hit the same state within a 24-hour period.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-space.com_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-space.com-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Then Charley\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" title="Rapid deepening">\n        rapidly intensified\n       </a>\n       , strengthening from a 110\xc2\xa0mph (180\xc2\xa0km/h) hurricane with a\n       <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_pressure" title="Atmospheric pressure">\n        minimum central barometric pressure\n       </a>\n       of 965\n       <a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">\n        mbar\n       </a>\n       (\n       <a href="/wiki/Pascal_(unit)" title="Pascal (unit)">\n        hPa\n       </a>\n       ; 28.50\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/InHg" title="InHg">\n        inHg\n       </a>\n       ) to a 145\xc2\xa0mph (233\xc2\xa0km/h) hurricane with a pressure of 947\xc2\xa0mbar (hPa; 27.96\xc2\xa0inHg) in just three\xc2\xa0hours. It continued to strengthen as it turned more to the northeast, and made\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">\n        landfall\n       </a>\n       near the island of\n       <a href="/wiki/Cayo_Costa_State_Park" title="Cayo Costa State Park">\n        Cayo Costa, Florida\n       </a>\n       as a 150\xc2\xa0mph (240\xc2\xa0km/h)\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale#Category_4" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">\n        Category 4 hurricane\n       </a>\n       with a pressure of 941\xc2\xa0mbar (hPa; 27.79\xc2\xa0inHg) at approximately 1945 UTC on August 13.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hpc_4-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-hpc-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fsem_7-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-fsem-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       An hour later, the hurricane struck\n       <a href="/wiki/Punta_Gorda,_Florida" title="Punta Gorda, Florida">\n        Punta Gorda\n       </a>\n       as a 145\xc2\xa0mph (233\xc2\xa0km/h) storm and then passed up through\n       <a href="/wiki/Port_Charlotte,_Florida" title="Port Charlotte, Florida">\n        Port Charlotte\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_3-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       However, the eye had shrunk before landfall, limiting the most powerful winds to an area within 7\xc2\xa0mi (11\xc2\xa0km) of the center.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_3-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Charley weakened considerably due to its passage over land, but still retained sustained winds of about 85\xc2\xa0mph (137\xc2\xa0km/h) as it passed directly over\n       <a href="/wiki/Orlando,_Florida" title="Orlando, Florida">\n        Orlando\n       </a>\n       between 0020 and 0140 UTC August 14,\xc2\xa0; gusts of up to 106\xc2\xa0mph (171\xc2\xa0km/h) were recorded at\n       <a href="/wiki/Orlando_International_Airport" title="Orlando International Airport">\n        Orlando International Airport\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hpc_4-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-hpc-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       It cut a swath of destruction across Florida, also passing near\n       <a href="/wiki/Kissimmee,_Florida" title="Kissimmee, Florida">\n        Kissimmee\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_3-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The hurricane reemerged into the Atlantic Ocean after crossing directly over\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Smyrna_Beach" title="New Smyrna Beach">\n        New Smyrna Beach\n       </a>\n       as a Category\xc2\xa01 hurricane, but restrengthened slightly over open waters.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_3-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hpc_4-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-hpc-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Continuing to move rapidly to the north-northeast, Charley struck near\n       <a href="/wiki/Cape_Romain_National_Wildlife_Refuge" title="Cape Romain National Wildlife Refuge">\n        Cape Romain National Wildlife Refuge\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina" title="South Carolina">\n        South Carolina\n       </a>\n       as an 80\xc2\xa0mph (130\xc2\xa0km/h) hurricane, moved offshore briefly, and made its final landfall near\n       <a href="/wiki/North_Myrtle_Beach,_South_Carolina" title="North Myrtle Beach, South Carolina">\n        North Myrtle Beach\n       </a>\n       as a minimal hurricane, with winds of 75\xc2\xa0mph (121\xc2\xa0km/h).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hpc_4-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-hpc-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Charley then began interacting with an approaching\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Front_(meteorology)" title="Front (meteorology)">\n        frontal boundary\n       </a>\n       , becoming a tropical storm over southeastern\n       <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">\n        North Carolina\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hpc_4-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-hpc-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       After moving back into the Atlantic Ocean near\n       <a href="/wiki/Virginia_Beach,_Virginia" title="Virginia Beach, Virginia">\n        Virginia Beach\n       </a>\n       on August 15, the storm became\n       <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">\n        extratropical\n       </a>\n       and became embedded in the frontal zone.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_3-14">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hpc_4-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-hpc-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The extratropical storm continued to move rapidly to the northeast, and was completely absorbed by the front shortly after sunrise on August 15, near southeastern\n       <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">\n        Massachusetts\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_3-15">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hpc_4-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-hpc-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n      hurricane warning\n       </a>\n       a day later.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_3-16">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">\n        Cuban\n       </a>\n       government officials issued a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Watch" In addition, the power grid in southern Cuba was turned off to avoid accidents.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cubevac_9-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-cubevac-9">\n         [9]\n    <a href="#cite_note-VoA_evacs-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       instead, these residents boarded up their homes and bought supplies to ride out the storm.\n       <sup     </a>\n       as a 105\xc2\xa0mph (169\xc2\xa0km/h) Category\xc2\xa02 hurricane on the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">\n        Saffir-Simpson scale\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-char15_35-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-char15-35">\n         [34]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In post-hurricane-season analysis, Charley was determined to have struck southern Cuba as a 120\xc2\xa0mph (190\xc2\xa0km/h) hurricane; the original estimate was revised based on a report of a 118\xc2\xa0mph (190\xc2\xa0km/h) sustained wind measurement in\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Playa_Baracoa" title="Playa Baracoa">\n        Playa Baracoa\n       </a>\n       , and meant that Charley was a major hurricane at landfall.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2004wmo_6-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2004wmo-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The hurricane produced a storm surge of up to 13.1\xc2\xa0ft (4.0\xc2\xa0m) in\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Playa_Cajio&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Playa Cajio (page does not exist)">\n        Playa Cajio\n       </a>\n       ;\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_3-25">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       on the other hand, Charley\'s quick passage caused\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Precipitation_(meteorology)" title="Precipitation (meteorology)">\n        precipitation\n       </a>\n       amounts to be small, with the largest total, 5.87 inches (149\xc2\xa0mm) occurring in\n       <a href="/wiki/Mariel,_Cuba" title="Mariel, Cuba">\n        Mariel\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2004wmo_6-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2004wmo-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Strong wind gusts downed nearly 1,500\xc2\xa0power lines and knocked over 28 large high tension wire towers at a power plant in Mariel. As a result, more than half of the electricity customers in\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Havana_Province" title="Havana Province">\n        Havana Province\n       </a>\n       were left without power for 12\xc2\xa0days after the storm, and all of\n       <a href="/wiki/Pinar_del_R%C3%ADo_Province" title="Pinar del R\xc3\xado Province">\n        Pinar del R\xc3\xado Province\n       </a>\n       was without power for over 11\xc2\xa0days. Blackouts continued in areas where power returned. The power outages resulted in lack of drinking water for numerous people, including no\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Potable_water" title="Potable water">\n        potable water\n       </a>\n       in the city of\n       <a href="/wiki/Havana" title="Havana">\n        Havana\n       </a>\n       for four days. As a result, the Cuban government sent water tanks to satisfy the short term need. Similarly, there was a lack of gas for cooking for over a week. However, one Cuban government official stated that it could take up to two months for basic utilities to be returned to many isolated villages.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cubass_10-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-cubass-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Near its landfall location, Charley destroyed 290 of the 300\xc2\xa0houses in the village, while over 70,000\xc2\xa0homes in\n       <a href="/wiki/Havana" title="Havana">\n        Havana\n       </a>\n       were either damaged or destroyed. Numerous hotels reported damage, potentially impacting the important tourism industry in the country. Agricultural damage was heavy, with the hurricane damaging more than 3,000 agricultural institutions. Citrus officials estimated a loss of 15,000\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metric_ton" title="Metric ton">\n        metric tons\n       </a>\n       of\n       <a href="/wiki/Grapefruit" title="Grapefruit">\n        grapefruit\n       </a>\n       on the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Isle_of_Youth" title="Isle of Youth">\n        Isle of Youth\n       </a>\n       , while strong winds ruined 66,000\xc2\xa0metric tons of citrus trees in the Havana area. Charley also destroyed around 57,000\n       <a href="/wiki/Acre" title="Acre">\n        acres\n       </a>\n       (230\xc2\xa0km\xc2\xb2) of fruit trees in the Havana area.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cubass_10-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-cubass-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Approximately 95% of the sugar cane, bean, and banana crops were affected in Cuban territory.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reliefweb1year_36-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-reliefweb1year-36">\n         [35]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In all, Charley was directly responsible for four deaths in Cuba, and was responsible for $923\xc2\xa0million in property damage, primarily from agricultural losses.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2004wmo_6-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2004wmo-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Florida">\n        Florida\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Charley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Florida">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Hurricane Charley severely affected the state of Florida. There were nine direct fatalities, 20\xc2\xa0indirect fatalities, and numerous injuries attributed to the storm.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_3-26">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Property damage was estimated at $5.4\xc2\xa0billion, and approximately $285\xc2\xa0million in agricultural damage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-noaa01_17-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-noaa01-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       However, due to Charley\'s speed (it crossed the Florida peninsula in approximately seven hours) and small size, rainfall along the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eyewall" title="Eyewall">\n        eyewall\n       </a>\n       was mostly limited to 4\xe2\x80\x936 inches (10\xe2\x80\x9315 centimetres).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-noaa01_17-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-noaa01-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       While moving northward to the west of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Florida_Keys" title="Florida Keys">\n        Florida Keys\n       </a>\n       , Charley produced moderate winds of 48\xc2\xa0mph (77\xc2\xa0km/h) with gusts to 60\xc2\xa0mph (97\xc2\xa0km/h) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Key_West,_Florida" title="Key West, Florida">\n        Key West\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_3-27">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The winds toppled a few trees, power lines, and unreinforced signs. A boat, knocked loose by strong waves, struck a power transmission line, causing widespread power outages from\n       <a href="/wiki/Marathon,_Florida" title="Marathon, Florida">\n        Marathon\n       </a>\n       to Key West. On\n       <a href="/wiki/Dry_Tortugas_National_Park" title="Dry Tortugas National Park">\n        Fort Jefferson\n       </a>\n       in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Dry_Tortugas" title="Dry Tortugas">\n        Dry Tortugas\n       </a>\n       , the hurricane produced an estimated storm surge of up to 6\xc2\xa0ft (1.8\xc2\xa0m). The surge, combined with incoming waves, caused extensive flooding in the park and damaged numerous docks. In spite of this, property damage was minimal in the area, totaling $160,000.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-flkeydam_12-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-flkeydam-12">\n         [12]\n passed directly over\n       <a href="/wiki/Captiva_Island" title="Captiva Island">\n        Captiva Island\n       </a>\n       near\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cayo_Costa,_Florida&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cayo Costa, Florida (page does not exist)">\n        Cayo Costa\n       </a>\n       with peak winds of 150\xc2\xa0mph (240\xc2\xa0km/h).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_3-28">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The Category\xc2\xa04\xc2\xa0hurricane produced an estimated storm surge of up to 6.5\xc2\xa0ft (2.0\xc2\xa0m) on the island, which is lower than expected for a storm of its intensity. The decrease in the height of the surge was due to the hurricane\'s small size and its rapid intensification just prior to landfall. Furthermore, the storm surge, combined with the strong pressure gradient, produced a \xc2\xbc\xc2\xa0mi (450\xc2\xa0m) inlet on\n       <a href="/wiki/North_Captiva_Island" title="North Captiva Island">\n        North Captiva Island\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-surge_37-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-surge-37">\n         [36]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       known as Charley\'s Cut. Strong waves and storm surge caused severe beach erosion and dune damage at various locations. The storm severely damaged five houses, lightly damaged many others, and downed many trees on\n       <a href="/wiki/Gasparilla_Island" title="Gasparilla Island">\n        Gasparilla Island\n       </a>\n       . At least half of the 300\xc2\xa0homes on North Captiva Island were substantially damaged, including ten that were destroyed. On Captiva Island, the strong winds severely damaged most houses, as well as several recreational buildings.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-flchisl_38-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-flchisl-38">\n         [37]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The city of\n       <a href="/wiki/Arcadia,_Florida" title="Arcadia, Florida">\n        Arcadia\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/DeSoto_County,_Florida" title="DeSoto County, Florida">\n        DeSoto County\n       </a>\n       saw extreme damage, in spite of being relatively further inland.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39">\n        <a href="#cite_note-39">\n         [38]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       About 95% of the buildings in the downtown area saw some sort of damage. The only shelter in the town had its roof torn open by the wind, leaving 3,500 evacuees inside unprotected from the onslaught of the storm.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40">\n        <a href="#cite_note-40">\n         [39]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:CharleyDamageInOrlando.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1050" data-file-width="1600" height="144" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/CharleyDamageInOrlando.jpg/220px-CharleyDamageInOrlando.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/CharleyDamageInOrlando.jpg/330px-CharleyDamageInOrlando.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/CharleyDamageInOrlando.jpg/440px-CharleyDamageInOrlando.jpg 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:CharleyDamageInOrlando.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Damage caused to a gas station by Hurricane Charley in\n         <a href="/wiki/Kissimmee,_Florida" title="Kissimmee, Florida">\n          Kissimmee, Florida\n         </a>\n         .\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       <a href="/wiki/Hardee_County,_Florida" title="Hardee County, Florida">\n        Hardee County\n       </a>\n       saw property damage estimated at $750\xc2\xa0million, along with six injuries, but no deaths were reported. Charley caused blackouts in the entire county, as well as damage to\xc2\xa03,600\xc2\xa0homes and the destruction of\xc2\xa01,400. A radio tower near\n       <a href="/wiki/Sebring,_Florida" title="Sebring, Florida">\n        Sebring\n       </a>\n       was toppled, along with numerous trees and power poles along the north and east side of\n       <a href="/wiki/Highlands_County,_Florida" title="Highlands County, Florida">\n        Highlands County\n       </a>\n       . Additionally, there were several reports of severely damaged homes in Polk County near Babson Park and\n       <a href="/wiki/Avon_Park,_Florida" title="Avon Park, Florida">\n        Avon Park\n       </a>\n       . In Lake Wales, Florida, a sand mine lake encroached into State Road 60 due to wave action and swallowed a car. Additionally, Lake Wales saw 23,000\xc2\xa0buildings damaged, as well as the destruction of 739\xc2\xa0structures. Seven deaths were reported in the county, one of them determined to be direct.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC_SW_FL_41-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NCDC_SW_FL-41">\n         [40]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Throughout the rest of the islands in\n       <a href="/wiki/Sarasota_County,_Florida" title="Sarasota County, Florida">\n        Sarasota\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Charlotte_County,_Florida" title="Charlotte County, Florida">\n        Charlotte\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Lee_County,_Florida" title="Lee County, Florida">\n        Lee\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Collier_County,_Florida" title="Collier County, Florida">\n        Collier counties\n       </a>\n       , strong winds from Hurricane Charley caused severe damage to hundreds of buildings and trees.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-flchisl_38-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-flchisl-38">\n         [37]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Lee County also endured an 8-foot (2.4\xc2\xa0m)\n       <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">\n        storm surge\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-noaa01_17-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-noaa01-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       These counties were exposed to Charley\'s\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eyewall" title="Eyewall">\n        eyewall\n       </a>\n       , so they saw the most damage. Due to its small size, the area of most intense damage was located within a 10-mi (16-km) band centered on Charley\'s track, with additional heavy damage forming an outer band extending 7.5\xc2\xa0mi (12.1\xc2\xa0km) to each side of the inner swath of damage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC_landfall_area_42-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NCDC_landfall_area-42">\n         [41]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In Charlotte County, 80% of buildings were damaged.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fema0814_43-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-fema0814-43">\n         [42]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Charley_damage.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="343" data-file-width="515" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bc/Hurricane_Charley_damage.jpg/220px-Hurricane_Charley_damage.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bc/Hurricane_Charley_damage.jpg/330px-Hurricane_Charley_damage.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bc/Hurricane_Charley_damage.jpg/440px-Hurricane_Charley_damage.jpg 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Charley_damage.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         President\n         <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Many towns such as\n       <a href="/wiki/Punta_Gorda,_Florida" title="Punta Gorda, Florida">\n        Punta Gorda\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Port_Charlotte,_Florida" title="Port Charlotte, Florida">\n        Port Charlotte\n       </a>\n       were leveled by the hurricane. Several possible tornadoes occurred, with severe thunderstorms during the duration of the storm. </a>\n       , while winds at Orlando International Airport topped out at 105\xc2\xa0mph (169\xc2\xa0km/h) in a gust.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-47">\n        <a Rainfall amounts in the state were moderate, ranging from 4 to 6\xc2\xa0in (100 to 150\xc2\xa0mm), but still caused flooding across seven North Carolina counties. The hurricane spawned five weak tornadoes across the state,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdam_55-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ncdam-55">\n         [54]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       including an\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fujita_Scale" title="Fujita Scale">\n        F1\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/Nags_Head,_North_Carolina" title="Nags Head, North Carolina">\n        Nags Head\n       </a>\n       that damaged twenty structures.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nctorn_56-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nctorn-56">\n         [55]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Charley destroyed 40\xc2\xa0houses and damaged 2,231, 231 severely, including 221 damaged beach homes in\n       <a href="/wiki/Sunset_Beach,_North_Carolina" title="Sunset Beach, North Carolina">\n        Sunset Beach\n       </a>\n       .
