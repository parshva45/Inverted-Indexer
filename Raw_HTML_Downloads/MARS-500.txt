</div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <b>\n        Mars-500\n       </b>\n       mission was a\n       <a href="/wiki/Psychosocial" title="Psychosocial">\n        psychosocial\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Remote_and_isolated_community" title="Remote and isolated community">\n        isolation\n       </a>\n       experiment conducted between 2007 and 2011 by Russia, the\n       <a href="/wiki/European_Space_Agency" title="European Space Agency">\n        European Space Agency\n       </a>\n       and China, in preparation for an unspecified future manned spaceflight to the planet\n       <a href="/wiki/Mars" title="Mars">\n        Mars\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lenta.ru_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-lenta.ru-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The experiment\'s facility was located at the\n       <a href="/wiki/Russian_Academy_of_Sciences" title="Russian Academy of Sciences">\n        Russian Problems\n       </a>\n       (IBMP) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">\n        Moscow\n       </a>\n       , Russia.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lenta.ru_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-lenta.ru-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Between 2007 and 2011, three different crews of volunteers lived and worked in a mock-up spacecraft at IBMP. The final stage of the experiment, which was intended to simulate a 520-day manned mission, was conducted by an all-male crew consisting of three Russians (Alexey Sitev, Sukhrob Kamolov, Alexander Smoleevskij), a Frenchman (Romain Charles), an Italian (Diego Urbina) and a Chinese citizen (Yue Wang). The mock-up facility simulated an Earth-Mars shuttle spacecraft, an ascent-descent craft, and the Martian surface. The volunteers who participated in the three stages included professionals with experience in engineering, medicine, biology, and human spaceflight. The experiment yielded important data on the physiological, social and psychological effects of long-term close-quarters isolation.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="Project_overview">\n        Project overview\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=MARS-500&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Project overview">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Purpose">\n        Purpose\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=MARS-500&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Purpose">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       The experiment was intended to yield valuable psychological and medical data on the effects of the planned long-term deep space mission. The experiment permitted the study of the technical challenges, work capability of crew and management of long-distance spaceflight. Communications lag, autonomy, resource rationing, health, conditions of isolation and hermetically closed, confined environment are the main peculiarities of the martian flight.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Scientific_objectives">\n        Scientific objectives\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=MARS-500&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Scientific objectives">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       The experiment was designed to allow planning the methods and means of control and monitoring of the habitat during lengthy crew stays in confined and cramped conditions. Planning diagnostic, forecast and treatment of medical support and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prophylaxis" title="Prophylaxis">\n        prophylaxis\n       </a>\n       , the use of\n       <a href="/wiki/Telemedicine" title="Telemedicine">\n        telemedicine\n       </a>\n       technologies, countermeasures against adverse effect of the "flight" factors on the human body, psychological support, and the creation of a reporting system for activity of the crew and electronic transfer of information. Methods of psychological support and approbation, using the\n       <a href="/wiki/Sociomapping" title="Sociomapping">\n        sociomapping\n       </a>\n       method.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Experiment_stages">\n        Experiment stages\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=MARS-500&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Experiment stages">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       A total of 640 experiment days were scheduled between 2007 and 2011, divided into three stages of differing length. During each stage, the crew of volunteers lived and worked in a mockup spacecraft. Communication with the outside world was limited, and was conducted with a realistic time delay of up to 25 minutes, to simulate the real-life communications lag between Mars and Earth. Similarly, a realistically limited supply of on-board consumables was provided for the volunteers.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-esa-mars-study_overview_overview_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-esa-mars-study_overview_overview-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Some conditions, such as\n       <a href="/wiki/Weightlessness" title="Weightlessness">\n        weightlessness\n       </a>\n       and cosmic radiation, could not be simulated.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The first fifteen-day stage of the Mars-500 experiment took place from 15 November 2007 to 27 November 2007.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rian.ru_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rian.ru-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The purpose of this stage was to test the technical equipment, facilities, and operating procedures for the voyage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-esa-quickfacts_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-esa-quickfacts-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The second, 105-day stage of the Mars-500 experiment began on 31 March 2009 when six volunteers started living in the experiment\'s isolated living complex.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On 14 July 2009, this stage of the experiment was completed.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The 520-day final stage of the experiment, which was intended to simulate a full-length manned mission, began on 3 June 2010 and ended on 4 November 2011.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBCNov2011_9-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-BBCNov2011-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       This stage was conducted by a six-man international crew, consisting of three Russians, a Frenchman, an Italian/ The complex consisted of the isolation facility, the mission operations room, technical facilities, and offices.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The isolation facility consisted of five different modules. Three of the modules \xe2\x80\x93 the habitat, utility, and medical modules \xe2\x80\x93 simulated the main spacecraft. The fourth module simulated the Martian-lander ship and was connected to the main spacecraft. The fifth module was a simulator of the Martian surface, and is connected to the Martian-lander. The combined volume of the modules was 550\xc2\xa0m\n       <sup>\n        3\n       </sup>\n       (19,000\xc2\xa0cu\xc2\xa0ft).\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The facility included all the necessary equipment for running the experiment. These included communications and control systems, ventilation systems, air and water supplies, electrical installations, sewage systems, air and water quality monitoring and partial recycling systems, medical equipment, fire and other safety monitoring systems, and emergency equipment. \xd0\xa1\xd0\xbc\xd0\xbe\xd0\xbb\xd0\xb5\xd0\xb5\xd0\xb2\xd1\x81\xd0\xba\xd0\xb8\xd0\xb9), Russian physiologist\n        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Businessweek_19-3">\n         <a member of the crew, a replacement, was Mikhail Sinelnikov (\xd0\x9c\xd0\xb8\xd1\x85\xd0\xb0\xd0\xb8\xd0\xbb \xd0\x9e\xd0\xbb\xd0\xb5\xd0\xb3\xd0\xbe\xd0\xb2\xd0\xb8\xd1\x87     [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28">\n        <a href="#cite_note-28">\n         [28]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Satellite_experiments">\n        Satellite experiments\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=MARS-500&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Satellite experiments">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       During the MARS 500 experiment related additional experiments aimed at studying the effects of\n       <a href="/wiki/Radiation" title="Radiation">\n        radiation\n       </a>\n       , health problems associated with weightlessness, the impact of fire on board the ship, and others are conducted.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SE2008_29-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SE2008-29">\n         [29]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Cardiac_experiments">\n        Cardiac experiments\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=MARS-500&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Cardiac experiments">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Crew experiments included monthly operational research, consisting of recording\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electrocardiogram" title="Electrocardiogram">\n        electrocardiogram\n       </a>\n       , respiratory samples and\n       <a href="/wiki/Blood_pressure" title="Blood pressure">\n        blood pressure\n       </a>\n       , and a questionnaire about lifestyle, stress and possible complaints over the past month.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Quarterly dynamic complex studies included measurement and recording of blood pressure and complex cardiorespiratory parameters during the performance of functional tests with physical, mental and orthostatic load. Detailed questionnaires conducting psychological testing and standard outpatient dispensary studies were conducted before and after the series of studies.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Immersion_experiments">\n        Immersion experiments\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=MARS-500&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Immersion experiments">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Because of the long stay in weightlessness, the person gets hypokinetic disorders. To study this phenomenon, IBMP has been conducting research in this area for many years, what allowed to construct a detailed picture of the make hypokinetic disorders. The experimental results show that the main cause of violations was a change in the gravity-dependent mechanisms that are responsible for motoric activity under the influence of gravity on the body. The changes begin to occur because of the disruption of coordinated work of sensory systems, in particular, support and proprioceptive ones. Data obtained during the experiments suggest that the support afferentation in humans acts as a mechanism of activation and regulation of activity of the postural-tonic system, and that the lack of support is the reason for discharge of the physiological and morphological changes that are common in conditions of weightlessness and microgravity.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SE2008_29-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SE2008-29">\n         [29]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The main purpose of the immersion experiments are to study effects of the flight, including unloading, on the support mechanisms of the body (spinal, supraspinal), the state of the central nervous systems, motor function and hand-eye co-ordination.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mars500-immersion_30-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mars500-immersion-30">\n         [30]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Hyperbaric_experiments">\n        Hyperbaric experiments\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=MARS-500&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Hyperbaric experiments">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       During the flight, there is a risk of fire in the spacecraft, and\n       <a href="/wiki/Argon" title="Argon">\n        argon\n       </a>\n       is planned as an inhibitor in the ships atmosphere to reduce this risk. The use of argon can significantly reduce the concentration of oxygen in the atmosphere of a spacecraft without any harm to the crew and to create a so-called\n       <a href="/wiki/Hypoxic_air_technology_for_fire_prevention" title="Hypoxic air technology for fire prevention">\n        hypoxic environment\n       </a>\n       .\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hyperbaric" title="Hyperbaric">\n        Hyperbaric\n       </a>\n       experiments will complement the knowledge on the effect of fire-resistant oxygen-nitrogen-argon mixture on the human body with the help of an integrated assessment of the subject\'s body during a long stay in fire-resistant mixture. The tests will determine the level of mental and physical performance, assess the state of the cardiorespiratory system, hematological, metabolic and immunological parameters in blood, and conduct microbiological research and research that will improve existing support systems.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mars500-Hyperbaric_31-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mars500-Hyperbaric-31">\n         [31]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Radiological_experiments">\n        Radiological experiments\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=MARS-500&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Radiological experiments">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       To avoid health problems caused by\n       <a href="/wiki/Irradiation" title="Irradiation">\n        irradiation\n       </a>\n       during the flight to Mars, it is necessary to create a prediction model of radiation risk. The model should describe the risk of radiation sickness depending on the total dose received, decreased performance which causes an acute reaction, and the possible reduction of the overall immune system resistance to the other health risks of interplanetary flight.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The prediction model is created by exposing a group of monkeys to a radiation source (\n       <a href="/wiki/Caesium-137" title="Caesium-137">\n        caesium-137\n       </a>\n       ). Radiological experiments are then conducted to study the radiobiological reactions of the main regulatory body systems (nervous, endocrine, immune, cardiovascular, hematopoietic), and spermatozoa and cytogenetic response to irradiation and analysis of long-term effects of exposure (life expectancy and carcinogenesis). The experiment uses male\n       <a href="/wiki/Rhesus_macaque" title="Rhesus macaque">\n        rhesus macaque\n       </a>\n       aged 3\xe2\x80\x935 years. They are divided into groups of 10-15 monkeys in each group. The experiments were designed so that mimic the real exposure of crew during the flight to Mars, including acute and chronic phases of disease.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mars500-Radiological_32-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mars500-Radiological-32">\n         [32]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Study_of_gastrointestinal_tract">\n        Study of gastrointestinal tract\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=MARS-500&amp;action=edit&amp;section=20" title="Edit section: Study of gastrointestinal tract">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Among the space experiments on the medical-biological section of "The long-term program of scientific and applied research and experiments planned for the Russian segment of\n       <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">\n        ISS\n       </a>\n       " planned and put into effect an experiment Splankh: "Study of the structural-functional state of various parts of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gastrointestinal_tract" title="Gastrointestinal tract">\n        gastrointestinal tract\n       </a>\n       to identify the specific changes in the digestive system in a space flight". Within the MARS-500 project the crew did a 24-h\n       <a href="/wiki/Electrogastrogram" title="Electrogastrogram">\n        electrogastroenterography\n       </a>\n       study electrical activity of gastrointestinal tract using on-board gastroenterograph Splankh-1, developed by IBMP with RPC "Istok-Sistema" based on the mass-produced gastroenterograph\n       <a href="/wiki/Gastroscan" title="Gastroscan">\n        Gastroscan-GEM\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IBMPGas_33-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-IBMPGas-33">\n         [33]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34">\n        <a href="#cite_note-34">\n         [34]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35">\n        <a href="#cite_note-35">\n         [35]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Psychological_effects">\n        Psychological effects\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=MARS-500&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Psychological effects">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       According to official results, the crew of 520-day isolation underwent the trial as the single unit. There were no conflict situations noted, nor any situations requiring interference of the ground-based services.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36">\n        <a href="#cite_note-36">\n         [36]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The difficulties encountered during the performance of some complicated activities were overcome by the crew together. It is worth saying that cultural differences and language difficulties did not bear any significant influence. Friendly and constructive communication is said to have prevailed throughout the experiment. The crew spent enough time together, watching films in different languages, as an opportunity to see and discuss the seen films together. The crew prepared surprises for birthdays, major state holidays and informal holidays (on October 31, they celebrated Halloween). It is possible to notice that the crew members somehow increased the time spent on individual activities, which did not hamper communication. There were no division of the crew along language lines or other interests to be noted. The commander exercised his authority as both a formal and informal leader.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">\n        See also\n       </span>\n       <span
