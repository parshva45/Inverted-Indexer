   </table>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <b>\n        2017\xe2\x80\x9318 South Pacific cyclone season\n       </b>\n       is the period of the year when most\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclones" title="Tropical cyclones">\n        tropical cyclones\n       </a>\n       form within the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/South_Pacific_Ocean" title="South Pacific Ocean">\n        South Pacific Ocean\n       </a>\n       to the east of 160\xc2\xb0E. The season officially runs from November 1, 2017 to April 30, 2018, however a tropical cyclone could form at any time between July 1, 2017 and June 30, 2018 and would count towards the season total. During the season, tropical cyclones will be officially monitored by the\n       <a href="/wiki/Regional_Specialized_Meteorological_Center" title="Regional Specialized Meteorological Center">\n        Regional Specialized Meteorological Center\n       </a>\n       (RSMC) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Fiji_Meteorological_Service" title="Fiji Meteorological Service">\n        Nadi, Fiji\n       </a>\n       and the Tropical Cyclone Warning Centers in\n       <a href="/wiki/Bureau_of_Meteorology" title="Bureau of Meteorology">\n        Brisbane, Australia\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meteorological_Service_of_New_Zealand_Limited" title="Meteorological Service of New Zealand Limited">\n        Wellington, New Zealand\n       </a>\n       . The\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Armed_Forces" title="United States Armed Forces">\n        United States Armed Forces\n       </a>\n       through the\n       <a href="/wiki/Joint_Typhoon_Warning_Center" title="Joint Typhoon Warning Center">\n        Joint Typhoon Warning Center\n       </a>\n       <small>\n        (JTWC)\n       </small>\n       will also monitor the basin and issue unofficial warnings for American interests. RSMC Nadi attaches a number and an F suffix to tropical disturbances that form in or move into the basin while the JTWC designates significant tropical cyclones with a number and a P suffix. RSMC Nadi, TCWC Wellington and TCWC Brisbane all use the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Australian_Tropical_Cyclone_Intensity_Scale" title="Australian Tropical Cyclone Intensity Scale">\n        Australian Tropical Cyclone Intensity Scale\n       </a>\n       and estimate windspeeds over a period of ten minutes, while the JTWC estimated sustained winds over a 1-minute period, which are subsequently compared to the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_hurricane_wind_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson hurricane wind scale">\n        Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson hurricane wind scale\n       </a>\n       <small>\n        (SSHWS)\n       </small>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="toc">\n </table>\n      <p>\n       Ahead of the cyclone season, the Fiji Meteorological Service\n       <small>\n        (FMS)\n       </small>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Bureau_of_Meteorology" title="Bureau of Meteorology">\n        Australian Bureau of Meteorology\n       </a>\n       <small>\n        (BoM)\n       </small>\n       , New Zealand\'s MetService and\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Institute_of_Water_and_Atmospheric_Research" title="National Institute of Water and Atmospheric Research">\n        National Institute of Water and Atmospheric Research\n       </a>\n       <small>\n        (NIWA)\n       </small>\n       and various other Pacific Meteorological services, all contributed towards the Island Climate Update tropical cyclone outlook that was released during October 2016.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NIWA_3-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NIWA-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The outlook took into account the ENSO neutral conditions that had been observed across the Pacific and analogue seasons that had ENSO neutral and weak La Nina conditions occurring during the season.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NIWA_3-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NIWA-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The outlook called for a near average number of tropical cyclones for the 2017\xe2\x80\x9318 season, with eight to ten named tropical cyclones, to occur between 135\xc2\xb0E and 120\xc2\xb0W compared to an average of about 10.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NIWA_3-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NIWA-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At least three of the tropical cyclones were expected to become Category 3 severe tropical cyclones, while two could become Category 4 severe tropical cyclones; they also noted that a Category 5 severe tropical cyclone was not likely to occur during the season.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NIWA_3-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NIWA-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In addition to contributing towards the Island Climate Update outlook, the FMS and the BoM issued their own seasonal forecasts for the South Pacific region.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2017-18_SO_2-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2017-18_SO-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SPOutlook_4-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SPOutlook-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The BoM issued two seasonal forecasts for the Southern Pacific Ocean, for their self-defined eastern and western regions of the South Pacific Ocean.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SPOutlook_4-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SPOutlook-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       They predicted that the Western region between 142.5\xc2\xb0E and 165\xc2\xb0E, had a 48% chance of seeing activity above its average of 7 tropical cyclones. The BoM also predicted that the Eastern Region between 165\xc2\xb0E and 120\xc2\xb0W, had a 55% chance of seeing activity above its average of 4 tropical cyclones.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SPOutlook_4-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SPOutlook-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Within their outlook the FMS predicted that between four and six tropical cyclones, would occur within the basin compared to an average of around 7.1 cyclones.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2017-18_SO_2-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2017-18_SO-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At least one of the tropical cyclones was expected to intensify into a Category 3 or higher severe tropical cyclone.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2017-18_SO_2-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2017-18_SO-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       They also predicted that the main area for tropical cyclogenesis would be, within the Coral Sea to the west of the International Dateline.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2017-18_SO_2-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2017-18_SO-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Both the Island Climate Update and the FMS tropical cyclone outlooks assessed the risk of a tropical cyclone affecting a certain island or territory.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2017-18_SO_2-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2017-18_SO-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NIWA_3-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NIWA-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The Island Climate Update Outlook predicted that New Caledonia, Tonga, New Zealand and Papua New Guinea had an above average chance of being impacted by a tropical cyclone or the.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NIWA_3-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NIWA-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       They also predicted that the Solomon Islands, Fiji and Vanuatu had a near normal to normal risk of being impacted by one or more tropical cyclones.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NIWA_3-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NIWA-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The Southern\n       <a href="/wiki/Cook_Islands" title="Cook Islands">\n        Cook Islands\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/American_Samoa" title="American Samoa">\n        American Samoa\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Samoa" title="Samoa">\n        Samoa\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Niue" title="Niue">\n        Niue\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Tokelau" title="Tokelau">\n        Tokelau\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Tuvalu" title="Tuvalu">\n        Tuvalu\n       </a>\n       , as well as\n       <a href="/wiki/Wallis_and_Futuna" title="Wallis and Futuna">\n        Wallis and Futuna\n       </a>\n       were thought to have a below average chance of being impacted. It was also considered unlikley that the Northern Cook Islands, French Polynesia,\n       <a href="/wiki/Kiribati" title="Kiribati">\n        Kiribati\n       </a>\n       and the\n       <a href="/wiki/Pitcairn_Islands" title="Pitcairn Islands">\n        Pitcairn Islands\n       </a>\n       would be affected by a tropical cyclone.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NIWA_3-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NIWA-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The FMS\'s outlook predicted that Vanuatu, New Caledonia, Fiji as well as\n       <a href="/wiki/Wallis_and_Futuna" title="Wallis and Futuna">\n        Wallis and Futuna\n       </a>\n       had a normal chance of being impacted by a tropical cyclone.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2017-18_SO_2-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2017-18_SO-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The outlook also predicted that the Solomon Islands, Tokelau, Samoa, Tonga, Niue, Cook Islands and French Polynesia had a reduced chance of being affected by a tropical cyclone.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2017-18_SO_2-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2017-18_SO-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       It was thought that there was a normal risk of Vanuatu, New Caledonia, Fiji and Samoa being impacted by at least one severe tropical cyclone, while other areas such as the Solomon Islands, Tokelau, Samoa, Tonga, Niue and French Polynesia, had a reduced chance of being impacted by a severe tropical cyclone.\n
