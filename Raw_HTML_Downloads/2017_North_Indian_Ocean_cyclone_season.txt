</tr>\n      </table>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <b>\n        2017 North Indian Ocean cyclone season\n       </b>\n       is a current event in the annual cycle of\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        tropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       formation. North Indian Ocean cyclone season has no official bounds, but cyclones tend to form between April and December, with the two peaks in May and November. These dates conventionally delimit the period of each year when most tropical cyclones form in the northern\n       <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">\n        Indian Ocean\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The scope of this article is limited to the Indian Ocean in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Northern_Hemisphere" title="Northern Hemisphere">\n        Northern Hemisphere\n       </a>\n       , east of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Horn_of_Africa" title="Horn of Africa">\n        Horn of Africa\n       </a>\n       and west of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Malay_Peninsula" title="Malay Peninsula">\n        Malay Peninsula\n       </a>\n       . There are two main\n       <a href="/wiki/Sea" title="Sea">\n        seas\n       </a>\n       in the North Indian Ocean\xc2\xa0\xe2\x80\x94 the\n       <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Sea" title="Arabian Sea">\n        Arabian Sea\n       </a>\n       to the west of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Indian_subcontinent" title="Indian subcontinent">\n        Indian subcontinent\n       </a>\n       , abbreviated\n       <i>\n        ARB\n       </i>\n       by the\n       <a href="/wiki/India_Meteorological_Department" title="India Meteorological Department">\n        India Meteorological Department\n       </a>\n       (IMD); and the\n       <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Bengal" title="Bay of Bengal">\n        Bay of Bengal\n       </a>\n       to the east, abbreviated\n       <i>\n        BOB\n       </i>\n       by the IMD. The official\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Regional_Specialized_Meteorological_Centre" title="Regional Specialized Meteorological Centre">\n        Regional Specialized Meteorological Centre\n       </a>\n       in this basin is the IMD, while the\n       <a href="/wiki/Joint_Typhoon_Warning_Center" title="Joint Typhoon Warning Center">\n        Joint Typhoon Warning Center\n       </a>\n       (JTWC) releases unofficial advisories. On average, three to four cyclonic storms form in this basin every season.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n The system made landfall in\n       <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">\n        Bangladesh\n       </a>\n       with three-minute sustained winds of 110\xc2\xa0km/h (70\xc2\xa0mph), and one-minute sustained winds of 120\xc2\xa0km/h (75\xc2\xa0mph). At this strength, its peak intensity, it was equivalent to a marginal Category 1 hurricane on the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_scale" title="Saffir-Simpson scale">\n        Saffir-Simpson hurricane wind scale\n       </a>\n       . The cyclone produced severe flooding across\n       <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">\n        Sri Lanka\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">\n        India\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Myanmar" title="Myanmar">\n        Myanmar\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">\n        Bangladesh\n       </a>\n       and caused 15 deaths directly and 203 deaths indirectly. The\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Floods" title="Floods">\n        floods\n       </a>\n       persisted in Bangladesh since a Deep Depression over Bay of Bengal made landfall and killed 156 people in Bangladesh. A depression formed in northwestern\n       <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Bengal" title="Bay of Bengal">\n        Bay of Bengal\n       </a>\n       and produced torrential rainfall. It was followed by a depression over\n       <a href="/wiki/Jharkhand" title="Jharkhand">\n        Jharkhand\n       </a>\n       which killed 70 people in\n       <a href="/wiki/West_Bengal" title="West Bengal">\n        West Bengal\n       </a>\n       . Under the influence of strong\n       <a href="/wiki/Monsoon" title="Monsoon">\n        monsoon\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">\n        surge\n       </a>\n       a disturbance developed over\n       <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Bengal" title="Bay of Bengal">\n        Bay of Bengal\n       </a>\n       travelled westwards and intensified to a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Depression_(weather)" title="Depression (weather)">\n        depression\n       </a>\n       . Cyclone Maarutha\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       On April 13, an area of low pressure formed in the South Bay of Bengal, under the influence of a persistent area of convection, in a span of six hours.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Under favorable conditions, rapid deepening took place, and the system was classified as a depression on April 15.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Later on the same day, it further intensified into a Deep Depression, and then into Cyclonic Storm Maarutha.The system moved very fast under the influence of mid-latitude trough in westerlies lying over India in the middle and upper tropospheric levels.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       However, strong vertical wind shear and unfavourable MJO inhibited rapid intensification or further intensification of the system. Moving northeastwards, it reached its peak intensity in the early hours of 16th. The system maintained its peak intensity till landfall near Sandoway (Thandwe) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Myanmar" title="Myanmar">\n        Myanmar\n       </a>\n       in the midnight. After landfall, the system weakened into a Deep Depression in early hours of 17th, into a Depression in the morning and well marked low pressure area over central\n       <a href="/wiki/Myanmar" title="Myanmar">\n        Myanmar\n       </a>\n       and neighbourhood in the forenoon of 17th.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Maarutha had already triggered heavy rainfall as a depression in Sri Lanka, as well as the Andaman and Nicobar Islands (India). Due to a heavy swell in the sea around the Andaman Islands, all ferries to Havelock Island from Port Blair were suspended on leaving nearly 1,500 tourists stranded.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Maarutha_89_10-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Maarutha_89-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Kyaukpyu" title="Kyaukpyu">\n        Kyaukpyu\n       </a>\n       , Maarutha destroyed more than 81 households and total damages amounted to\n       <a href="/wiki/Burmese_kyat" title="Burmese kyat">\n        Ks\n       </a>\n       31.8 million (US$23,400) as of April 18.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Maarutha1_11-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Maarutha1-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Four people were reported to be killed in the Irrawady division of Myanmar.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Maarutha2_12-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Maarutha2-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Maarutha_76_13-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Maarutha_76-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Maarutha developed as a depression in the first fortnight of April. Climatologically, the formation of tropical cyclones in the Bay of Bengal at this time of the year is rare.
