   </div>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        External stowage platforms\n       </b>\n       (\n       <b>\n        ESPs\n       </b>\n       ) are key components of the\n       <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">\n        International Space Station\n       </a>\n       (ISS). Each platform is an external pallet that can hold spare parts, also known as\n       <a href="/wiki/Orbital_replacement_unit" title="Orbital replacement unit">\n        orbital replacement units\n       </a>\n       (ORUs), for the space station. As a platform it is not pressurized, but does require electricity to power the heaters of some of the stored equipment. ORUs are attached to the ESP via\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flight_Releasable_Attachment_Mechanism" title="Flight Releasable Attachment Mechanism">\n        Flight Releasable Attachment Mechanisms\n       </a>\n       (FRAMs), matching witness plates that mate the ORU to the platform.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       While ESP-1 is unique in shape, ESP-2 and ESP-3 were based on the deployable version of the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Integrated_Cargo_Carrier" title="Integrated Cargo Carrier">\n        Integrated Cargo Carriers\n       </a>\n       (ICC), which were designed to transport unpressurized cargo inside the Space Shuttle\'s cargo bay. ESP-1 was transported to the\n       <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">\n        International Space Station\n       </a>\n       on\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-102" title="STS-102">\n        STS-102\n       </a>\n       , ESP-2 flew on mission\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-114" title="STS-114">\n        STS-114\n       </a>\n       \'Return to Flight\' and ESP-3 on mission\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-118" title="STS-118">\n        STS-118\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" (ISS module)">\n        <i>\n         Destiny\n        </i>\n        Laboratory Module\n       </a>\n       on March 13, 2001 during the second\n       <a class="mw-redirect" points to store ORUs.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       ESP-1 was carried into orbit on the underside of an\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Integrated_Cargo_Carrier" title="Integrated Cargo 38\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       ESP-3 was detached from its Keel Yoke Assembly (which remained in the Orbiter) and installed on the P3 Truss at UCCAS-1 on August 14, 2007 during the Space Shuttle\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-118" title="STS-118">\n        STS-118\n       </a>\n       mission. It has seven attachment sites for ISS spare parts and assemblies, called\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orbital_Replacement_Unit" title="Orbital Replacement Unit">\n        Orbital Replacement Units\n       </a>\n       (ORUs). The platform also has handrails and attachment points for tethers and foot restraints that astronauts can use while working with the ORUs on the ESP-3. ESP-3, as with ESP-2 are deployable versions of the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Integrated_Cargo_Carrier" title="Integrated Cargo Carrier">\n        Integrated Cargo Carrier\n       </a>\n       and have the same dimensions, approximately 8.5 feet long and 14 feet wide. ESP-3 has two\n       <a href="/wiki/Grapple_fixture" title="Grapple fixture">\n        grapple fixtures\n       </a>\n       to aid deployment.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       ESP-3 was the first major station element to be installed completely by robotics, using only the shuttle and station\xe2\x80\x99s robotic arms, an external berthing camera system (BCS) and a\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Photovoltaic_Radiator_Grapple_Fixture&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Photovoltaic Radiator Grapple Fixture (page does not exist)">\n        Photovoltaic Radiator Grapple Fixture\n       </a>\n       (PVRGF). Astronauts robotically installed the platform onto the station\'s\n       <a href="/wiki/Integrated_Truss_Structure" title="Integrated Truss Structure">\n        P3 truss segment\n       </a>\n       during the\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-118" title="STS-118">\n        STS-118\n       </a>\n       mission\xe2\x80\x99s seventh day.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       On January 12, 2010, the station\'s robotic arm was used again to move ESP-3 from the P3 truss segment UCCAS-1 site. It was grappled by the arm and then transferred down the station\'s backbone on the mobile transporter. ESP-3 was then attached to its new location on the lower part of\n       <a href="/wiki/Integrated_Truss_Structure" title="Integrated Truss Structure">\n        S3 truss segment\n       </a>\n       at the PAS-3 site. Moving the storage platform cleared the way for\n       <a href="/wiki/ExPRESS_Logistics_Carrier" title="ExPRESS Logistics Carrier">\n        ExPRESS Logistics Carrier\n       </a>\n       -3 to be installed during\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-134" title="STS-134">\n        STS-134\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The ORUs (currently) installed on ESP-3 are:\n      </p>\n
