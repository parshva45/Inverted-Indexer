For other meanings of the word catastrophe, including\n         <a href="/wiki/Catastrophe_theory" title="Catastrophe theory">\n          catastrophe theory\n         </a>\n         in mathematics, see\n         <a class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Catastrophe_(disambiguation)" title="Catastrophe (disambiguation)">\n          catastrophe (disambiguation)\n         </a>\n         .\n        </i>\n       </dd>\n      </dl>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        Catastrophe modeling\n       </b>\n       (also known as\n       <b>\n        cat modeling\n       </b>\n       ) is the process of using\n       <a href="/wiki/Computer" title="Computer">\n        computer\n       </a>\n       -assisted calculations to estimate the losses that could be sustained due to a catastrophic event such as a hurricane or earthquake. Cat modeling is especially applicable to analyzing risks in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Insurance" title="Insurance">\n        insurance\n       </a>\n       industry and is at the confluence of\n       <a href="/wiki/Actuarial_science" title="Actuarial science">\n        actuarial science\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">\n        engineering\n <p>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Perils_analyzed">\n        Perils analyzed\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Catastrophe_modeling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Perils analyzed">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Natural catastrophes (sometimes referred to as "nat cat") include:\n      </p>\n      <ul>\n       <li>\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane" title="Hurricane">\n         hurricane\n        </a>\n        (main peril is wind damage; some models can also include storm surge)\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        <a href="/wiki/Earthquake" title="Earthquake">\n         earthquake\n        </a>\n        (main peril is ground shaking; some models can also include\n        <a href="/wiki/Tsunami" title="Tsunami">\n         tsunami\n        </a>\n        , fire following earthquakes, and sprinkler leakage damage)\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        severe\n        <a href="/wiki/Thunderstorm" title="Thunderstorm">\n         thunderstorm\n        </a>\n        (main perils are\n        <a href="/wiki/Tornado" title="Tornado">\n         tornado\n        </a>\n        and\n        <a href="/wiki/Hail" title="Hail">\n         hail\n        </a>\n        )\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        <a href="/wiki/Flood" title="Flood">\n         flood\n        </a>\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">\n         extratropical cyclone\n        </a>\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        <a href="/wiki/Wildfire" title="Wildfire">\n         wildfire\n        </a>\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        <a href="/wiki/Winter_storm" title="Winter storm">\n         winter storm\n        </a>\n       </li>\n      </ul>\n      <p>\n       Human catastrophes include:\n      </p>\n      <ul>\n       <li>\n        <a href="/wiki/Terrorism" title="Terrorism">\n         terrorism\n        </a>\n        events\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        <a href="/wiki/War" title="War">\n         warfare\n        </a>\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Casualty/liability events\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Displacement Crises\n        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">\n         <a href="#cite_note-1">\n          [1]\n         </a>\n        </sup>\n       </li>\n      </ul>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Lines_of_business_modeled">\n        Lines of business modeled\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Catastrophe_modeling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Lines of business modeled">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <ul>\n       <li>\n        Personal property\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Commercial property\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Workers\' compensation\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Automobile physical damage\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Limited liabilities\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Product liability\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Business Interruption\n       </li>\n      </ul>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Input">\n        Input\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Catastrophe_modeling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Input">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       The input into a typical cat modeling software package is information on the exposures being analyzed that are vulnerable to catastrophe risk. The exposure data can be categorized into three basic groups:\n      </p>\n      <ul>\n       <li>\n        information on the site locations, referred to as geocoding data (street address, postal code, county/CRESTA zone, et cetera)\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        information on the physical characteristics of the exposures (construction, occupation/occupancy, year built, number of stories, number of employees, et cetera)\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        information on the financial terms of the insurance coverage (coverage value, limit, deductible, et cetera)\n       </li>\n      </ul>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Output">\n        Output\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Catastrophe_modeling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Output">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       The output is an estimate of the losses that the model predicts would be associated with a particular event or set of events. When running a\n       <a href="/wiki/Mathematical_model#Model_classifications_in_mathematics" title="Mathematical model">\n        <i>\n         probabilistic\n        </i>\n        model\n       </a>\n       , the output is either a probabilistic loss distribution or a set of events that could be used to create a loss distribution; probable maximum losses (PMLs) and average annual losses (AALs) are calculated from the loss distribution. When running a\n       <i>\n        deterministic\n       </i>\n       model, losses caused by a specific event are calculated; for example,\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Katrina" title="Hurricane Katrina">\n        Hurricane Katrina\n       </a>\n       or "a magnitude 8.0 earthquake in downtown San Francisco" could be analyzed against the portfolio of exposures.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Uses">\n        Uses\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Catastrophe_modeling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Uses">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <ul>\n       <li>\n        Insurers and risk managers use cat modeling to assess the risk in a portfolio of exposures. This might help guide an insurer\'s underwriting strategy or help them decide how much\n        <a href="/wiki/Reinsurance" title="Reinsurance">\n         reinsurance\n        </a>\n        to purchase.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Some state departments of insurance allow insurers to use cat modeling in their rate filings to help determine how much premium their policyholders are charged in catastrophe-prone areas.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Insurance rating agencies such as\n        <a href="/wiki/A._M._Best" title="A. M. Best">\n         A. M. Best\n        </a>\n        and\n        <a href="/wiki/Standard_%26_Poor%27s" title="Standard &amp; Poor\'s">\n         Standard &amp; Poor\'s\n        </a>\n        use cat modeling to assess the financial strength of insurers that take on catastrophe risk.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Reinsurers and reinsurance brokers use cat modeling in the pricing and structuring of reinsurance treaties.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        European insurers use cat models to derive the required regulatory capital under the Solvency II regime. Cat models are used to derive catastrophe loss probability distributions which are components of many Solvency II internal capital models.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Likewise, cat bond investors, investment banks, and bond rating agencies use cat modeling in the pricing and structuring of\n        <a href="/wiki/Catastrophe_bond" title="Catastrophe bond">\n         catastrophe bond\n        </a>\n        .\n       </li>\n      </ul>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Open_catastrophe_modeling">\n        Open catastrophe modeling\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Catastrophe_modeling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Open catastrophe modeling">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Recently, an effort to create and disseminate open multi-hazard cat risk modeling tools was initiated by the\n       <a class="external text" href="http://www.risk-agora.org/" rel="nofollow">\n        Alliance for Global Open Risk Analysis (AGORA)\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Also, Oasis Loss Modelling Framework (LMF)\n       <a class="external free" href="http://www.oasislmf.org" rel="nofollow">\n        http://www.oasislmf.org\n       </a>\n       has been created. It has been founded as a not for profit organisation funded and owned by the Insurance Industry to promote open access to models and to promote transparency. Open source code to be available.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Operating on the Oasis LMF,\n       <b>\n        <a class="external text" href="http://www.simplitium.com/modex" rel="nofollow">\n         ModEx\n        </a>\n       </b>\n       , the cat risk modelling platform, has been brought to market by\n       <a class="external text" href="http://www.simplitium.com" rel="nofollow">\n        Simplitium\n       </a>\n       to provide the (re)insurance industry with a deeper understanding of risk. The ModEx platform allows model providers from all over the world to make their models available to the industry using the Oasis framework. By creating a marketplace where model developers can make their models available via a single user interface, the platform lowers the barriers to entry in the cat model marketplace and encourages the development of new and improved cat risk models.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Additionally, the insurance industry is currently working with the\n       <a class="external text" href="http://www.acord.org/" rel="nofollow">\n        Association for Cooperative Operations Research and Development (ACORD)\n       </a>\n       to develop an industry standard for collecting and sharing exposure data. To date, the industry has been operating on closed, proprietary data formats.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">\n        See also\n       </span>\n       <span
