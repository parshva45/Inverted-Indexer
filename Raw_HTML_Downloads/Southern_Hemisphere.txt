    </b>\n       is the half\n       <a href="/wiki/Sphere" title="Sphere">\n        sphere\n       </a>\n       of\n       <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">\n        Earth\n       </a>\n It contains all or parts of five\n       <a href="/wiki/Continent" title="Continent">\n        continents\n       </a>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       (\n       <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">\n        Antarctica\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Australia_(continent)" title="Australia (continent)">\n        Australia\n       </a>\n       , about 90% of\n       <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">\n        South America\n       </a>\n       , the southern third of\n       <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">\n        Africa\n       </a>\n       , and several southern islands off the continental\n       <a href="/wiki/Mainland" title="Mainland">\n        mainland\n       </a>\n       of\n       <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">\n        Asia\n       </a>\n       ), four\n       <a href="/wiki/Ocean" title="Ocean">\n        oceans\n       </a>\n       (\n       <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">\n        Indian\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">\n        South Atlantic\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Southern_Ocean" title="Southern Ocean">\n        Southern\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">\n        South Pacific\n       </a>\n       ) and most of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Islands" title="Pacific Islands">\n        Pacific Islands\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/Oceania" title="Oceania">\n        Oceania\n       </a>\n       . Its surface is 80.9% water, compared with 60.7% water in the case of the Northern Hemisphere, and it contains 32.7% of Earth\'s land.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Owing to the tilt of\n       <a href="/wiki/Earth%27s_rotation" title="Earth\'s rotation">\n        Earth\'s rotation\n       </a>\n       relative to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">\n        Sun\n       </a>\n       and the\n       <a href="/wiki/Ecliptic" title="Ecliptic">\n        ecliptic plane\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Summer" title="Summer">\n        summer\n       </a>\n       is from December to March and\n       <a href="/wiki/Winter" title="Winter">\n        winter\n       </a>\n       is from June to September. <p>\n       In the Southern Hemisphere the sun passes from east to west through the north, although north of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropic_of_Capricorn" title="Tropic of Capricorn">\n        Tropic of Capricorn\n       </a>\n       the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mean_solar_time" title="Mean solar time">\n        mean sun\n       </a>\n       can be directly overhead or due south at midday. The Sun rotating through the north causes an apparent right-left trajectory through the sky unlike the left-right motion of the Sun when seen from the Northern Hemisphere as it passes through the southern sky. Sun-cast shadows turn anticlockwise throughout the day and\n       <a href="/wiki/Sundial" title="Sundial">\n        sundials\n       </a>\n       have the hours increasing in the anticlockwise direction. During\n       <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse" title="Solar eclipse">\n        solar eclipses\n       </a>\n       viewed from a point to the south of the Tropic of Capricorn, the Moon moves from left to right on the disc of the Sun (see, for example, photos with timings of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Solar_eclipse_of_November_13,_2012" title="Solar eclipse of November 13, 2012">\n        solar eclipse of November 13, 2012\n       </a>\n       ), while viewed from a point to the north of the Tropic of Cancer (i.e.,\xc2\xa0in the Northern Hemisphere), the Moon moves from right to left during solar eclipses.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Cyclones and tropical storms spin clockwise in the Southern Hemisphere (as opposed to\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anticlockwise" title="Anticlockwise">\n        anticlockwise\n       </a>\n       in the Northern Hemisphere) due to the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coriolis_effect" title="Coriolis effect">\n        Coriolis effect\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The southern\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Temperateness" title="Temperateness">\n        temperate zone\n       </a>\n       , a subsection of the Southern Hemisphere, is nearly all oceanic. This zone includes the southern tip of\n       <a href="/wiki/Uruguay" title="Uruguay">\n        Uruguay\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">\n        South Africa\n       </a>\n       ; the southern half of\n       <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">\n        Chile\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">\n        Argentina\n       </a>\n       ; parts of\n       <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">\n        Australia\n       </a>\n       , going south from\n       <a href="/wiki/Adelaide" title="Adelaide">\n        Adelaide\n       </a>\n       , and all of\n       <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">\n        New Zealand\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <a href="/wiki/Sagittarius_(constellation)" title="Sagittarius (constellation)">\n        Sagittarius\n       </a>\n       constellation that includes the\n       <a href="/wiki/Milky_Way" title="Milky Way">\n        galactic centre\n       </a>\n       is a southern constellation and this, combined with clearer skies, makes for excellent viewing of the night sky from the Southern Hemisphere with brighter and more numerous stars.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Forests in the Southern Hemisphere have special features which set them apart from those in the Northern Hemisphere. Both\n       <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">\n        Chile\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">\n        Australia\n       </a>\n       share, for example, unique\n       <a href="/wiki/Beech" title="Beech">\n        beech\n       </a>\n       species or\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Nothofagus" title="Nothofagus">\n         Nothofagus\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">\n        New Zealand\n       </a>\n       has members of the closely related genera\n       <a href="/wiki/Lophozonia" title="Lophozonia">\n        Lophozonia\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Fuscospora" title="Fuscospora">\n        Fuscospora\n       </a>\n       . The\n       <a href="/wiki/Eucalyptus" title="Eucalyptus">\n        eucalyptus\n       </a>\n       is native to\n       <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">\n        Australia\n       </a>\n       but is now also planted in\n       <a href="/wiki/Southern_Africa" title="Southern Africa">\n        Southern Africa\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">\n        Latin America\n       </a>\n       for pulp production and, increasingly,\n       <a href="/wiki/Biofuel" title="Biofuel">\n        biofuel\n       </a>\n       uses.\n      </p>\n
