William and Samuel Abernethy by an act of the Virginia General Assembly,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Springfield was named in commemoration of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Springfield_(1780)" title="Battle of Springfield (1780)">\n        Battle of Springfield (1780)\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <a href="/wiki/George_Washington" title="George Washington">\n        George Washington\n       </a>\n       first visited the Springfield area in 1748 as a member of a party that surveyed the land holdings of\n       <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Fairfax,_6th_Lord_Fairfax_of_Cameron" title="Thomas Fairfax, 6th Lord Fairfax of Cameron">\n        Thomas Fairfax, 6th Lord Fairfax of Cameron\n       </a>\n       in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Potomac_River#South_Branch_Potomac_River" title="Potomac River">\n        South Branch Potomac River\n       </a>\n       Valley.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In June 1861, the town organized a\n       <a href="/wiki/Company_(military_unit)" title="Company (military unit)">\n        company\n       </a>\n       of men known as the "Potomac Guards" in support of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Confederate_States_of_America" title="Confederate States of America">\n        Confederacy\n       </a>\n       . The company was under the command of Captain Philip T. Grace.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       On August 23, 1861, Springfield played host to an\n       <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">\n        American Civil War\n       </a>\n       skirmish between the Unionists and the Confederates.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Historic_Sites">\n        Historic Sites\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Springfield,_West_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Historic Sites">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Today, Springfield is the site of a number of historic private residences dating from the 18th and 19th Centuries.\n      </p>\n      <ul>\n       <li>\n        <b>\n         63 Springfield Pike\n        </b>\n        (1860),\n        <a href="/wiki/Springfield_Grade_Road" title="Springfield Grade Road">\n         Springfield Pike\n        </a>\n        (CR 3)\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        <b>\n         <a href="/wiki/Washington_Bottom_Farm" title="Washington Bottom Farm">\n          Ridgedale (George W.
