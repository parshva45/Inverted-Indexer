Stormfury\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        Project Stormfury\n       </b>\n       was an attempt to weaken\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        tropical cyclones\n       </a>\n       by flying aircraft into them and\n       <a href="/wiki/Cloud_seeding" title="Cloud seeding">\n        seeding\n       </a>\n       with\n       <a href="/wiki/Silver_iodide" title="Silver iodide">\n        silver iodide\n       </a>\n       . The project was run by the\n       <a href="/wiki/Federal_government_of_the_United_States" title="Federal government of the United States">\n        United States Government\n       </a>\n       from 1962 to 1983.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The hypothesis was that the silver iodide would cause\n       <a href="/wiki/Supercooling" title="Supercooling">\n        supercooled\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">\n        water\n       </a>\n       in the storm to freeze, disrupting the inner structure of the hurricane. This led to the seeding of several Atlantic hurricanes. However, it was later shown that this hypothesis was incorrect. It was determined that most hurricanes do not contain enough supercooled water for cloud seeding to be effective. Additionally, researchers found that unseeded hurricanes often undergo the same\n       <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)#Eyewall_replacement_cycles" title="Eye (cyclone)">\n        structural changes\n       </a>\n       that were expected from seeded hurricanes. This finding called Stormfury\'s successes into question, as the changes reported now had a natural explanation.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The last experimental flight was flown in 1971, due to a lack of candidate storms and a changeover in\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NOAA" title="NOAA">\n        NOAA\n       </a>\n       \'s fleet. More than a decade after the last modification experiment, Project Stormfury was officially canceled. Although a failure in its goal of reducing the destructiveness of hurricanes, Project Stormfury was not without merit. The observational data and storm lifecycle research generated by Stormfury helped improve meteorologists\' ability to\n       <a href="/wiki/Weather_forecasting" title="Weather forecasting">\n        forecast\n       </a>\n       the movement and intensity of future hurricanes.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="toc">\n       <div class="toctitle">\n        <h2>\n id="Hypothesis">\n        Hypothesis\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Project_Stormfury&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Hypothesis">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Project_stormfury_hypothesis.png">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="384" data-file-width="300" height="256" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/Project_stormfury_hypothesis.png/200px-Project_stormfury_hypothesis.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/15/Project_stormfury_hypothesis.png 1.5x" width="200"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Project_stormfury_hypothesis.png" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         The working hypothesis of Project Stormfury\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Cloud seeding was first attempted by\n       <a href="/wiki/Vincent_Schaefer" title="Vincent Schaefer">\n        Vincent Schaefer\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Irving_Langmuir" title="Irving Langmuir">\n        Irving Langmuir\n       </a>\n       . After witnessing the artificial creation of ice crystals, Langmuir became an enthusiastic proponent of\n       <a href="/wiki/Weather_modification" title="Weather modification">\n        weather modification\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Davies_p._85_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Davies_p._85-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Schaefer found that when he dumped crushed\n       <a href="/wiki/Dry_ice" title="Dry ice">\n        dry ice\n       </a>\n       into a cloud, precipitation in the form of\n       <a href="/wiki/Snow" title="Snow">\n        snow\n       </a>\n       resulted.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Whipple_p._150_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Whipple_p._150-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       With regard to hurricanes, it was hypothesized that by seeding the area around the\n       <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">\n        eyewall\n       </a>\n       with\n       <a href="/wiki/Silver_iodide" title="Silver iodide">\n        silver iodide\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Latent_heat" title="Latent heat">\n        latent heat\n       </a>\n       would be released. This would promote the formation of a new eyewall. As this new eyewall was larger than the old eyewall, the winds of the tropical cyclone would be weaker due to a reduced\n       <a href="/wiki/Pressure_gradient" title="Pressure gradient">\n        pressure gradient\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Landsea_D9_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Landsea_D9-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Even a small reduction in the speed of a hurricane\'s winds would be beneficial: since the damage potential of a hurricane increased as the square of the wind speed,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Landsea_D5_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Landsea_D5-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       a slight lowering of wind speed would have a large reduction in destructiveness.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Landsea_D5_4-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Landsea_D5-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Due to Langmuir\'s efforts, and the research of Schaefer at\n       <a href="/wiki/General_Electric" title="General Electric">\n        General Electric\n       </a>\n       , the concept of using cloud seeding to weaken hurricanes gathered momentum. Indeed, Schaefer had caused a major snowstorm on December 20, 1946 by seeding a cloud.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Whipple_p._150_2-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Whipple_p._150-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       This caused GE to drop out for legal reasons. Schaefer and Langmuir assisted the U.S. military as advisors for Project Cirrus, the first large study of\n       <a href="/wiki/Cloud_physics" title="Cloud physics">\n        cloud physics\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Weather_modification" title="Weather modification">\n        weather modification\n       </a>\n       . Its most important goal was to try to weaken\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        hurricanes\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Whipple_p._151_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Whipple_p._151-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Project_Cirrus">\n        Project Cirrus\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Project_Stormfury&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Project Cirrus">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Project Cirrus was the first attempt to modify a hurricane. It was a collaboration of the\n       <a href="/wiki/General_Electric" title="General Electric">\n        General Electric\n       </a>\n       Corporation, the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_Army_Signal_Corps" title="US Army Signal Corps">\n        US Army Signal Corps\n       </a>\n       , the\n       <a href="/wiki/Office_of_Naval_Research" title="Office of Naval Research">\n        Office of Naval Research\n       </a>\n       , and the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US_Air_Force" title="US Air Force">\n        US Air Force\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Davies_p._85_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Davies_p._85-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       After several preparations, and initial skepticism by government scientists,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bulletin_p._505_6-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Bulletin_p._505-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       the first attempt to modify a hurricane began on October 13, 1947 on a\n       <a href="/wiki/1947_Cape_Sable_hurricane" title="1947 Cape Sable hurricane">\n        hurricane\n       </a>\n       that was heading\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boxing_the_compass" title="Boxing the compass">\n        west to east\n       </a>\n       and out to sea.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Whipple_p._151_5-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Whipple_p._151-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       An airplane flew along the rainbands of the hurricane, and dropped nearly 180 pounds (82 kilograms) of crushed\n       <a href="/wiki/Dry_ice" title="Dry ice">\n        dry ice\n       </a>\n       into the clouds.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Davies_p._85_1-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Davies_p._85-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The crew reported "Pronounced modification of the cloud deck seeded".\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Whipple_p._151_5-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Whipple_p._151-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       It is not known if that was due to the seeding. Next, the hurricane changed direction and made landfall near\n       <a href="/wiki/Savannah,_Georgia" title="Savannah, Georgia">\n        Savannah, Georgia\n       </a>\n       . The public blamed the seeding, and\n       <a href="/wiki/Irving_Langmuir" title="Irving Langmuir">\n        Irving Langmuir\n       </a>\n       claimed that the reversal had been caused by human intervention.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bulletin_p._505_6-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Bulletin_p._505-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Cirrus was canceled,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Whipple_p._151_5-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Whipple_p._151-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Lawsuit" title="Lawsuit">\n        lawsuits\n       </a>\n       were threatened. Only the fact that\n       <a href="/wiki/1906_Florida_Keys_hurricane" title="1906 Florida Keys hurricane">\n        a system in 1906\n       </a>\n       had taken a similar path, as well as evidence showing that the storm had already begun to turn when seeding began, ended the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Litigation" title="Litigation">\n        litigation\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Whipple_p._151_5-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Whipple_p._151-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       This disaster set back the cause of seeding hurricanes for eleven years.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       At first the seeding was officially denied and it took years before the government admitted it. According to the Sept. 12, 1965 edition of the Fort Lauderdale News and Sun-Sentinel, in 1947 a hurricane "went whacky" and "Twelve years later it was admitted the storm\n       <i>\n        had in fact been seeded.\n       </i>\n       "\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Between_the_projects">\n        Between the projects\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Project_Stormfury&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Between the projects">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Fly00716.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1225" data-file-width="1800" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/Fly00716.jpg/200px-Fly00716.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/Fly00716.jpg/300px-Fly00716.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/Fly00716.jpg/400px-Fly00716.jpg 2x" width="200"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Fly00716.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Eye of\n         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Esther_(1961)" title="Hurricane Esther (1961)">\n          Hurricane Esther\n         </a>\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Weather_Bureau" title="United States Weather Bureau">\n        United States Weather Bureau\n       </a>\n       \'s\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Research_Project" title="National Hurricane Research Project">\n        National Hurricane Research Project\n       </a>\n       , founded in 1955, had as one of its objectives to investigate the scientific validity of hurricane modification methods. To this end, silver iodide dispensers were tested in\n       <a href="/wiki/1958_Atlantic_hurricane_season#Hurricane_Daisy" title="1958 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        Hurricane Daisy\n       </a>\n       in August 1958. The flares were deployed outside of the hurricane eyewall, so this was an equipment test rather than a modification experiment. The equipment malfunctioned in all but one of the flights, and no conclusive data was acquired.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Whipple_p._151_5-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Whipple_p._151-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The first seeding experiment since the Cirrus disaster was attempted on September 16, 1961, into\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Esther_(1961)" title="Hurricane Esther (1961)">\n        Hurricane Esther\n       </a>\n       by NHRP and the\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">\n        United States Navy\n       </a>\n       aircraft. Eight cylinders of silver iodide were dropped into Esther\'s eyewall, and winds were recorded as weakening by 10 percent.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Davies_p._89_8-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Davies_p._89-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The next day, more seeding flights were made. This time, the silver iodide did not fall into the eyewall, and no reduction in windspeed was observed. These two results were interpreted as making the experiment a "success".\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Davies_p._90_9-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Davies_p._90-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The seedings into Hurricane Esther led to the establishment of Project Stormfury in 1962. Project Stormfury was a joint venture of the\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Commerce" title="United States Department of Commerce">\n        United States Department of Commerce\n       </a>\n       and the\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">\n        United States Navy\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Davies_p._90_9-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Davies_p._90-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Project_BATON">\n        Project BATON\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Project_Stormfury&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Project BATON">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       The objective of Project BATON was the analysis of the life history of thunderstorms. A Department of Defense research activity supported by the Advanced Research Project Agency, Project BATON sought to expand understanding of storm physics as an aid to weather forecasting, fire prevention, and, possibly, for artificially controlling the weather. Dr. Helmut Weickmann, as an employee of the U.S, Army Signal Research and Development Laboratory, and Dr. Paul McReady of Meteorology Research, Inc., were joint leaders of the Project BATON team.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Army_10-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Army-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       During the 1962 July\xe2\x80\x93August storm season in Flagstaff, Arizona, the scientists, selected "guinea pig" storms, and seeded them with chemicals. Effects were thoroughly analyzed from the ground and from the air with time-lapse motion picture cameras, stereo still cameras, storm radar, lightning detectors, and airborne heat sensors. Among the agents inserted in selected clouds were "condensation nuclei" which temporarily increased the number of water droplets in the cloud, and pulverized dry ice, which turns a portion of the cloud to fine snow crystals that remain aloft. The utilization of these agents facilitated study of a storm\'s characteristics.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Army_10-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Army-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Project_STORMFURY_begins">\n        Project STORMFURY begins\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Project_Stormfury&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Project STORMFURY begins">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       <a href="/wiki/Robert_Simpson_(meteorologist)" title="Robert Simpson (meteorologist)">\n        Robert Simpson\n       </a>\n       became its first director, serving in this capacity until 1965.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fury_11-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-fury-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       There were several guidelines used in selecting which storms to seed. The hurricane had to have a less than 10 percent chance of approaching inhabited land within a day;\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Whipple_p._153_12-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Whipple_p._153-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       it had to be within range of the seeding aircraft; and it had to be a fairly intense storm with a well-formed\n       <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">\n        eye\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Davies_p._89_8-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Davies_p._89-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The primary effect of these criteria was to make possible seeding targets extremely rare.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Whipple_p._154_13-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Whipple_p._154-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       No suitable storms formed in the\n       <a href="/wiki/1962_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1962 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1962 season\n       </a>\n       . Next year, Stormfury began by conducting\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scientific_experiment" title="Scientific experiment">\n        experiments\n       </a>\n       on\n       <a href="/wiki/Cumulus_cloud" title="Cumulus cloud">\n        cumulus clouds\n       </a>\n       . From August 17 to 20 of that year, experiments were conducted in 11 clouds, of which six were seeded and five were\n       <a href="/wiki/Scientific_control" title="Scientific control">\n        controls\n       </a>\n       . In five of the six seeded clouds, changes consistent with the working hypothesis were observed.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bulletin_p._507_14-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Bulletin_p._507-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       On August 23, 1963,\n       <a href="/wiki/1963_Atlantic_hurricane_season#Hurricane_Beulah" title="1963 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        Hurricane Beulah\n       </a>\n       was the site of the next seeding attempt. It had an indistinct eyewall. In addition, mistakes were made, as the seedings of silver iodide were dropped in the wrong places. As a consequence, nothing happened.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Davies_p._90_9-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Davies_p._90-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The next day, another attempt was made, and the seeders hit their targets. The eyewall was observed to fall apart and be replaced by another eyewall with a larger radius.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bulletin_p._507_14-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Bulletin_p._507-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sustained_winds&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sustained winds (page does not exist)">\n        sustained winds\n       </a>\n       also fell by twenty percent.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bulletin_p._507_14-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Bulletin_p._507-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       All in all, the results of the experiments on Beulah were "encouraging but inconclusive."\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Davies_p._90_quoted_15-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Davies_p._90_quoted-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In the six years after Beulah, no seedings were conducted for several different reasons. In 1964, measurement and observation equipment was not ready to be used.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bulletin_p._507_14-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Bulletin_p._507-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The year after that, all flights were used for additional experimentation in non-hurricane clouds.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bulletin_p._507_14-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Bulletin_p._507-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <a href="/wiki/Joanne_Simpson" title="Joanne Simpson">\n        Joanne Simpson\n       </a>\n       became its director beginning in 1965.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fury_11-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-fury-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">\n        <a href="#cite_note-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       While out to sea in August of the\n       <a href="/wiki/1965_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1965 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1965 Atlantic hurricane season\n       </a>\n       , Stormfury meteorologists decided that\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Betsy" title="Hurricane Betsy">\n        Hurricane Betsy\n       </a>\n       was a good candidate for seeding.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Whipple_p._153_12-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Whipple_p._153-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       However, the storm immediately swung towards land, and on September 1, the planned flights were canceled. For some reason, the press was not notified that there were no seedings, and several newspapers reported that it had begun.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Whipple_p._153_12-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Whipple_p._153-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       As Betsy passed close to\n       <a href="/wiki/The_Bahamas" title="The Bahamas">\n        the Bahamas\n       </a>\n       and smashed into southern\n       <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">\n        Florida\n       </a>\n       , the public and\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress" title="United States Congress">\n        Congress\n       </a>\n       thought that seeding was underway and blamed Stormfury.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Whipple_p._153_12-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Whipple_p._153-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       It took two months for Stormfury officials to convince Congress that Betsy was not seeded, and the project was allowed to continue.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Whipple_p._153_12-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Whipple_p._153-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A second candidate,\n       <a href="/wiki/1965_Atlantic_hurricane_season#Hurricane_Elena" title="1965 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        Hurricane Elena\n       </a>\n       , stayed too far out to sea.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bulletin_p._507_14-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Bulletin_p._507-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       After Betsy, two other hurricanes came close to being seeded.\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Faith" title="Hurricane Faith">\n        Hurricane Faith\n       </a>\n       was considered a likely candidate, but it stayed out of range of the seeding planes.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bulletin_p._507_14-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Bulletin_p._507-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       That same year, recon flights were conducted into\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Inez" title="Hurricane Inez">\n        Hurricane Inez\n       </a>\n       , but there were no seedings.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bulletin_p._507_14-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Bulletin_p._507-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Both the\n       <a href="/wiki/1967_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1967 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1967\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/1968_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1968 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1968 seasons\n       </a>\n       were inactive. Because of that, there were no suitable seeding targets in either of those two seasons.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bulletin_p._507_14-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Bulletin_p._507-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Eye_of_hurricane_debbie_(1969).jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="460" data-file-width="700" height="131" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Eye_of_hurricane_debbie_%281969%29.jpg/200px-Eye_of_hurricane_debbie_%281969%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Eye_of_hurricane_debbie_%281969%29.jpg/300px-Eye_of_hurricane_debbie_%281969%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Eye_of_hurricane_debbie_%281969%29.jpg/400px-Eye_of_hurricane_debbie_%281969%29.jpg 2x" width="200"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Eye_of_hurricane_debbie_(1969).jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         The eye of\n         <a href="/wiki/1969_Atlantic_hurricane_season#Hurricane_Debbie" title="1969 Atlantic hurricane season">\n          Hurricane Debbie\n         </a>\n         on August 20\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Dr. R. Cecil Gentry became the director of Stormfury in 1968.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">\n        <a href="#cite_note-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       There were no more near-seedings until 1969. In the interim, equipment was improved. What once was the primitive method of hand-dumping dry ice was replaced with rocket canisters loaded with silver iodide, and then gun-like devices mounted on the wings of the airplanes that fired silver iodide into the clouds. Observation equipment was improved.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Whipple_p._153_12-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Whipple_p._153-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Additional reconnaissance data was utilized to modify the working hypothesis. The new theory took cumulus towers outside the\n       <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">\n        eyewall\n       </a>\n       into account. According to the revised theory, by seeding the towers,\n       <a href="/wiki/Latent_heat" title="Latent heat">\n        latent heat\n       </a>\n       would be released. This would trigger the start of new convection, which would then cause a new eyewall. Since the new eyewall was outside the original one, the first eyewall would be choked of energy and fall apart. In addition, since the new eyewall was broader than the old one, the winds would be lower due to a less sharp pressure difference.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Whipple_p._153_12-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Whipple_p._153-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Debbie_(1969)" title="Hurricane Debbie (1969)">\n        Hurricane Debbie\n       </a>\n       in 1969 provided the best opportunity to test the underpinnings of Project Stormfury. In many ways it was the perfect storm for seeding: it did not threaten any land; it passed within range of seeding aircraft; and was intense with a distinct eye.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Whipple_p._153-154_18-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Whipple_p._153-154-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On August 18 and again on August 20, thirteen planes flew out to the storm to monitor and seed it. On the first day, windspeeds fell by 31%.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bulletin_p._507_14-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Bulletin_p._507-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On the second day, windspeeds fell by 18%.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bulletin_p._507_14-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Bulletin_p._507-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Both changes were consistent with Stormfury\'s working hypothesis. Indeed, the results were so encouraging that "a greatly expanded research program was planned."\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Davies_._91_quote_19-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Davies_._91_quote-19">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Among other conclusions was the need for frequent seeding at close to hourly intervals.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_1972_p_216_20-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR_1972_p_216-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <a href="/wiki/1970_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1970 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1970\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/1971_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1971 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1971 seasons\n       </a>\n       provided no suitable seeding candidates.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bulletin_p._507_14-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Bulletin_p._507-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Despite this, flights were conducted into\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Ginger" title="Hurricane Ginger">\n        Hurricane Ginger\n       </a>\n       . Ginger was not a suitable storm for seeding, due to its diffuse, indistinct nature. The seeding had no effect. Ginger was the last seeding done by Project Stormfury.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bulletin_p._507_14-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Bulletin_p._507-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="After_the_seedings">\n        After the seedings\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Tropical_storm_dorothy_recon_(1970).jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1280" data-file-width="1800" height="178" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Tropical_storm_dorothy_recon_%281970%29.jpg/250px-Tropical_storm_dorothy_recon_%281970%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Tropical_storm_dorothy_recon_%281970%29.jpg/375px-Tropical_storm_dorothy_recon_%281970%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e2/Tropical_storm_dorothy_recon_%281970%29.jpg/500px-Tropical_storm_dorothy_recon_%281970%29.jpg 2x" width="250"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Tropical_storm_dorothy_recon_(1970).jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Stormfury inside\n         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_Storm_Dorothy_(1970)" title="Tropical Storm Dorothy (1970)">\n          Tropical Storm Dorothy\n         </a>\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Atlantic hurricanes meeting all of the criteria were extremely rare, which made duplication of the "success" reached with Hurricane Debbie extremely difficult. Meanwhile, developments outside of meteorology hindered the cause of hurricane modification.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In the early 1970s, the Navy withdrew from the project.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Davies_p._91_21-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Davies_p._91-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Stormfury began to refocus its efforts on understanding, rather than modifying, tropical cyclones.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Williams_22-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Williams-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At the same time, the Project\'s aircraft were nearing the end of their operational lifetimes. At the cost of $30\xc2\xa0million (year unknown)\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Davies_p._91_21-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Davies_p._91-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       two\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lockheed_P-3" title="Lockheed P-3">\n        Lockheed P-3\n       </a>\n       \'s were acquired. Due to the rarity of Atlantic hurricanes meeting the safety requirements, plans were made to move Stormfury to the Pacific and experiment on the large number of typhoons there.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Whipple_p._154_13-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Whipple_p._154-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       This action required many of the same safety requirements as in the Atlantic, but had the advantage of a much higher number of potential subjects.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Davies_p._91_21-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Davies_p._91-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The plan was to begin again in 1976, and seed typhoons by flying out of\n       <a href="/wiki/Guam" title="Guam">\n        Guam\n       </a>\n       . However, political issues blocked the plan. The\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China" title="People\'s Republic of China">\n        People\'s Republic of China\n       </a>\n       announced that it would not be happy if a seeded typhoon changed course and made landfall on its shores,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Whipple_p._154_13-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Whipple_p._154-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       while\n       <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">\n        Japan\n       </a>\n       declared itself willing to put up with difficulties caused by typhoons because that country got more than half of its rainfall from tropical cyclones.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Whipple_p._154_13-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Whipple_p._154-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Similar plans to operate Stormfury in the eastern north Pacific or in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">\n        Australian\n       </a>\n       region also collapsed.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bulletin_p._508_23-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Bulletin_p._508-23">\n         [23]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Failure_of_the_working_hypothesis">\n        Failure of the working hypothesis\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Project_Stormfury&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Failure of the working hypothesis">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Multiple eyewalls had been detected in very strong hurricanes before, including\n       <a href="/wiki/1958_Pacific_typhoon_season" title="1958 Pacific typhoon season">\n        Typhoon Sarah\n       </a>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_1972_p_210_24-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR_1972_p_210-24">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Donna" title="Hurricane Donna">\n        Hurricane Donna\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jordan_and_Schatzle_25-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Jordan_and_Schatzle-25">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       although the double eyes were usually seen in very intense systems. Double eyewalls were also seen post-seeding in some of the seeded storms. At the time however, the only known times that rapid changes in eyewall diameter, other than during presumably successful seedings, was during rapid changes in intensity.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_1972_p_213_26-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR_1972_p_213-26">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       It remained controversial whether the seedings caused the secondary eyewalls or whether it was just a natural cycle.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Journal_p._396_27-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Journal_p._396-27">\n         [27]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Basically, if eyewall changes similar to those observed in seeded hurricanes were rare in unseeded tropical cyclones, it would provide powerful evidence that Project Stormfury was successful. Conversely, if such changes were common in unseeded systems, it would throw doubt on the very hypothesis and assumptions driving Project Stormfury.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bulletin_p._511_28-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Bulletin_p._511-28">\n         [28]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Data and observations began to accumulate that debunked Stormfury\'s working hypothesis. Beginning with Hurricanes\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Anita" title="Hurricane Anita">\n        Anita\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_David" title="Hurricane David">\n        David\n       </a>\n       , flights by\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hunter" title="Hurricane Hunter">\n        Hurricane Hunter\n       </a>\n       aircraft encountered events similar to what happened in "successfully" seeded storms.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bulletin_p._511_28-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Bulletin_p._511-28">\n         [28]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Anita itself had a weak example of a concentric eyewall cycle, and David a more dramatic one.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Journal_p._396_27-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Journal_p._396-27">\n         [27]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In August 1980, Hurricane Allen passed through the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlantic" title="Atlantic">\n        Atlantic\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Caribbean" title="Caribbean">\n        Caribbean\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">\n        Gulf of Mexico\n       </a>\n       . It also underwent changes in the diameter of its eye and developed multiple eyewalls. All this was consistent with the behavior that would have been expected of Allen had it been seeded. Thus, what Stormfury was accomplishing by seeding was also happening on its own.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Goldenberg_29-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Goldenberg-29">\n         [29]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Other observations in Hurricanes Anita, David,\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Frederic" title="Hurricane Frederic">\n        Frederic\n       </a>\n       , and Allen\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bulletin_p._509_30-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Bulletin_p._509-30">\n         [30]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       also discovered that tropical cyclones have very little supercooled water and a great deal of ice crystals.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HRD_31-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HRD-31">\n         [31]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The reason that tropical cyclones have little supercooled water is that the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Updraft" title="Updraft">\n        updrafts\n       </a>\n       within such a system are too weak to prevent water from either falling as rain or freezing.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Landsea_C4_32-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Landsea_C4-32">\n         [32]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       As cloud seeding needed supercooled water to function, the lack of supercooled water meant that seeding would have no effect.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Those observations called the basis for Project Stormfury into question. In the middle of 1983, Stormfury was finally canceled after the hypothesis guiding its efforts was invalidated.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bulletin_p._513_33-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Bulletin_p._513-33">\n         [33]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">\n        Legacy\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Project_Stormfury&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Legacy">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       In the sense of weakening hurricanes to reduce their destructiveness, Project Stormfury was a complete failure because it did not distinguish between natural phenomena in tropical cyclones and the impact of human intervention.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HRD_31-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HRD-31">\n         [31]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Millions of dollars had been spent trying to do the impossible. In the end, "[Project] STORMFURY had two fatal flaws: it was neither microphysically nor statistically feasible."\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bulletin_p._513_33-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Bulletin_p._513-33">\n         [33]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In addition, Stormfury had been a primary generator of funding for the\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Research_Division" title="Hurricane Research Division">\n        Hurricane Research Division\n       </a>\n       . While the project was operational, the HRD\'s budget had been around $4\xc2\xa0million (1975\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Dollar" title="United States Dollar">\n        USD\n       </a>\n       ; $16\xc2\xa0million 2008\xc2\xa0USD), with a staff of approximately 100\xc2\xa0people.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Davies_p._92_34-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Davies_p._92-34">\n         [34]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Today, the HRD employs 30\xc2\xa0people and has a budget of roughly $2.6\xc2\xa0million each year.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Davies_p._93_35-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Davies_p._93-35">\n         [35]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       However, Project Stormfury had positive results as well. Knowledge gained during flights proved invaluable in debunking its hypotheses.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Davies_p._93_35-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Davies_p._93-35">\n         [35]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Other science resulted in a greater understanding of tropical cyclones. In addition, the\n       <a href="/wiki/Lockheed_P-3_Orion" title="Lockheed P-3 Orion">\n        Lockheed P-3\'s\n       </a>\n       were perfectly suitable for gathering data on tropical cyclones, allowing improved forecasting of these monstrous storms.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Davies_p._93_35-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Davies_p._93-35">\n         [35]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Those planes are still used by the NOAA today.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Swanson_and_Williams_36-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Swanson_and_Williams-36">\n         [36]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Former\n       <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">\n        Cuban\n       </a>\n       president\n       <a href="/wiki/Fidel_Castro" title="Fidel Castro">\n        Fidel Castro\n       </a>\n       alleged that Project Stormfury was an attempt to weaponize hurricanes.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37">\n        <a href="#cite_note-37">\n         [37]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">\n        See also\n       </span>\n       <span
