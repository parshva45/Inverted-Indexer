 </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Eastern Visayas lies on the east central section of the Philippine archipelago. It consists of three main islands, Leyte, Biliran and Samar, which form the easternmost coasts of the archipelago. It is bounded on the east and north by the\n       <a href="/wiki/Philippine_Sea" title="Philippine Sea">\n        Philippine Sea\n       </a>\n       with the\n       <a href="/wiki/San_Bernardino_Strait" title="San Bernardino Strait">\n        San Bernardino Strait\n       </a>\n       separating Samar from southeastern\n       <a href="/wiki/Luzon" title="Luzon">\n        Luzon\n       </a>\n       ; on the west by the\n       <a href="/wiki/Camotes_Sea" title="Camotes Sea">\n        Camotes\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Visayan_Sea" title="Visayan Sea">\n        Visayan\n       </a>\n       seas, and on the south by the\n       <a href="/wiki/Bohol_Sea" title="Bohol Sea">\n        Bohol Sea\n       </a>\n       with the\n       <a href="/wiki/Surigao_Strait" title="Surigao Strait">\n        Surigao Strait\n       </a>\n       separating Leyte from northwestern\n       <a href="/wiki/Mindanao" title="Mindanao">\n        Mindanao\n       </a>\n       . It has a total land area of 2,156,285 hectares (5,328,300 acres) or 7.2% of the country\xe2\x80\x99s total land area.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       52% of its total land area are classified as forestland and 48% as alienable and disposable land.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span    </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       There are two types of climate prevailing in the region under the Corona system of classification: Type II and Type IV. Type II climate is characterized by having no dry season but a pronounced maximum rainfall from November to January. Samar Island and the eastern part of Leyte Island fall under this type of climate. Type IV on the other hand has an even distribution of rainfall the year round and a short period of dry season that can be observed starting February up to May. This type of climate is well exhibited at the western half of Leyte island and some portion of Samar which covers the municipality of Motiong up to San Isidro of Northern Samar.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In November 2013, the region was struck with the highest death toll in the nation by\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Super_Typhoon_Haiyan" title="Super Typhoon Haiyan">\n        Super Typhoon Haiyan\n       </a>\n       , the second deadliest typhoon ever to violently hit the Philippines. Tacloban is the hub of investment, trade and development in the region.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Other industries include coconut oil extraction, alcohol distilling, beverage manufacture and forest products. Home industries include hat and basket weaving, metal craft, needlecraft, pottery, ceramics, woodcraft, shell craft and bamboo craft.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Education">\n        Education\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Eastern_Visayas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Education">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Eastern Visayas is home to several state universities, including the prestigious\n       <a href="/wiki/University_of_the_Philippines_Visayas_Tacloban_College" title="University of the Philippines Visayas Tacloban College">\n        University of the Philippines Visayas Tacloban College\n       </a>\n       . The region is also home to the\n       <a href="/wiki/University_of_Eastern_Philippines" title="University of Eastern Philippines">\n        University of Eastern Philippines\n       </a>\n       (UEP), located in\n       <a href="/wiki/Catarman,_Northern_Samar" title="Catarman, Northern Samar">\n        Catarman\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Northern_Samar" title="Northern Samar">\n        Northern Samar\n       </a>\n       , which holds the most number of baccalaureate and post-baccalaureate courses among universities in the region.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The Zonal Agricultural University for the Visayas under the National Agriculture Education System concept,\n       <a href="/wiki/Visayas_State_University" title="Visayas State University">\n        Visayas State University\n       </a>\n       (VSU) is also in the region, located in\n       <a href="/wiki/Baybay" title="Baybay">\n        Baybay\n       </a>\n       . Also, the region is home to\n       <a href="/wiki/Palompon_Institute_of_Technology" title="Palompon Institute of Technology">\n        Palompon Institute of Technology\n       </a>\n       , a maritime school in the Philippines providing deck and engine cadet. Its main campus is located in the municipality of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Palompon" title="Palompon">\n        Palompon\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Leyte" title="Leyte">\n        Leyte\n       </a>\n       province.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Visayas_State_University" title="Eastern Visayas State University">\n        Eastern Visayas State University\n       </a>\n       is\n       <a href="/wiki/Leyte_(province)" title="Leyte (province)">\n        Leyte\n       </a>\n       \'s state university with five extension campuses.\n       <a href="/wiki/Southern_Leyte_State_University" title="Southern Leyte State University">\n        Southern Leyte State University\n       </a>\n       with five extension campuses, is the only state university in the province of\n       <a href="/wiki/Southern_Leyte" title="Southern Leyte">\n        Southern Leyte\n       </a>\n       . In\n       <a href="/wiki/Biliran" title="Biliran">\n        Biliran\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Naval_State_University" title="Naval State University">\n        Naval State University\n       </a>\n       is the province state university. For\n       <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Samar" title="Eastern Samar">\n        Eastern Samar\n       </a>\n       , the\n       <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Samar_State_University" title="Eastern Samar State University">\n        Eastern Samar State University\n       </a>\n       is the only state university of the province with four extension campus while\n       <a href="/wiki/Samar_State_University" title="Samar State University">\n        Samar State University\n       </a>\n       is Samar\'s state university with two extension campuses.\n       <a href="/wiki/Northwest_Samar_State_University" title="Northwest Samar State University">\n        Northwest Samar State University\n       </a>\n       serves Samar Province\'s first district. For teacher education, the\n       <a href="/wiki/Leyte_Normal_University" title="Leyte Normal University">\n        Leyte Normal University\n       </a>\n       specializes in education courses.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Infrastructure">\n        Infrastructure\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Eastern_Visayas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Infrastructure">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation">\n        Transportation\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Eastern_Visayas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Transportation">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       The region\'s\n       <a href="/wiki/Leyte" title="Leyte">\n        Leyte\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Samar" title="Samar">\n        Samar\n       </a>\n       islands serve as main link between Luzon and Mindanao by land transport. A total of nine airports, are strategically located in different parts of the six provinces that define the region.\n       <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Z._Romualdez_Airport" title="Daniel Z. Romualdez Airport">\n        Daniel Z. Romualdez Airport\n       </a>\n       in Tacloban is the main gateway by air to the region. There are seaports in\n       <a href="/wiki/Tacloban" title="Tacloban">\n        Tacloban\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Catbalogan" title="Catbalogan">\n        Catbalogan\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Calbayog" title="Calbayog">\n        Calbayog\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Borongan" title="Borongan">\n        Borongan\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Allen,_Northern_Samar" title="Allen, Northern Samar">\n        Allen\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Ormoc" title="Ormoc">\n        Ormoc\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Bato,_Leyte" title="Bato, Leyte">\n        Bato\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hilongos" title="Hilongos">\n        Hilongos\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Maasin" title="Maasin">\n        Maasin\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Sogod,_Southern_Leyte" title="Sogod, Southern Leyte">\n        Sogod\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Naval" title="Naval">\n        Naval\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Power_and_energy">\n        Power and energy\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Eastern_Visayas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Power and energy">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       The region is the top producer of geothermal energy supply in the country. The province of\n       <a href="/wiki/Leyte_(province)" title="Leyte (province)">\n        Leyte\n       </a>\n       hosts the biggest geothermal plant in the Philippines. Still, geothermal exploration is ongoing in the nearby province of\n       <a href="/wiki/Biliran" title="Biliran">\n        Biliran\n       </a>\n       . With abundance of river system, the region has potential in hydroelectric production. The\n       <a href="/wiki/San_Juanico_Strait" title="San Juanico Strait">\n        strait of San Juanico\n       </a>\n       between Leyte and Samar islands has been declared as potential source for water current and tidal energy sources.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Culture">\n        Culture\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Eastern_Visayas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Culture">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Dances">\n        Dances\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Eastern_Visayas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Dances">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Tinikling" title="Tinikling">\n         Tinikling\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       , the Philippines\' national dance is folkdance that originated from the region. But the most popular cultural dance among Warays is the\n       <i>\n        Kuratsa\n       </i>\n       , danced during feast celebrations and special gatherings. The\n       <a href="/wiki/Leyte_Kalipayan_Dance_Company" title="Leyte Kalipayan Dance Company">\n        Leyte Kalipayan Dance Company\n       </a>\n       , a local cultural group, held highly successful performances around the world.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Music">\n        Music\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Eastern_Visayas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Music">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Waray people are music lovers whose folkloric music are mostly ballads in form, famous of which is\n       <i>\n        Dandansoy\n       </i>\n       while\n       <i>\n        Iroy nga Tuna\n       </i>\n       (Motherland) is a patriotic song.\n      </p>\n
