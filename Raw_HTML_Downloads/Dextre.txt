href="/wiki/Expedition_27" title="Expedition 27">\n          Expedition 27\n         </a>\n         crew member\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        Dextre\n       </b>\n       , also known as the\n       <b>\n        Special Purpose Dexterous Manipulator\n       </b>\n       (\n       <b>\n        SPDM\n       </b>\n       ), is a two armed robot, or\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Telemanipulator" title="Telemanipulator">\n        telemanipulator\n       </a>\n       , which is part of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Mobile_Servicing_System" title="Mobile Servicing System">\n        Mobile Servicing System\n       </a>\n       on the\n       <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">\n        International Space Station\n       </a>\n       (ISS), and does repairs otherwise requiring\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extra-vehicular_activity" title="Extra-vehicular activity">\n        spacewalks\n       </a>\n       . It was launched March 11, 2008 on mission\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-123" title="STS-123">\n        STS-123\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Dextre is part of\n       <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">\n        Canada\n       </a>\n       \'s contribution to the ISS and is named to represent its\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dexterity" title="Dexterity">\n        dexterous\n       </a>\n       nature. Dextre is the third Canadian robotic arm used on the ISS, preceded by the\n       <a href="/wiki/Canadarm" title="Canadarm">\n        Space Shuttle\'s Canadarm\n       </a>\n       and the large\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadarm2" title="Canadarm2">\n        Canadarm2\n       </a>\n       . Dextre was designed and manufactured by\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MacDonald_Dettwiler" title="MacDonald Dettwiler">\n        MacDonald Dettwiler\n       </a>\n       (MDA).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CWNS-2006-03-15_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CWNS-2006-03-15-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In the early morning of February 4, 2011, Dextre completed its first official assignment which consists of unpacking two pieces for\n       <a href="/wiki/Kounotori_2" title="Kounotori 2">\n        Kounotori 2\n       </a>\n       while the on-board crew was sleeping.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n <p>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Structure">\n        Structure\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Dextre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Structure">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Dextre resembles a gigantic torso fitted with two extremely agile, 3.5 metres (11\xc2\xa0ft) arms. Total mass approx 3,664 pounds.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nasa-dextre_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nasa-dextre-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The 3.5 metre long body\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nasa-dextre_3-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nasa-dextre-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       pivots at the "waist". The body has a Power Data\n       <a href="/wiki/Grapple_fixture" title="Grapple fixture">\n        grapple fixture\n       </a>\n       at the \'head\' end that can be grasped by the larger Space Station Arm,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadarm2" title="Canadarm2">\n        Canadarm2\n       </a>\n       so that Dextre can be positioned at the various\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orbital_Replacement_Unit" title="Orbital Replacement Unit">\n        Orbital Replacement Unit\n       </a>\n       (ORU) worksites around the Space Station. The other end of the body has a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latching_End_Effector" title="Latching End Effector">\n        Latching End Effector\n       </a>\n       virtually identical to that of Canadarm2, so that Dextre can also be attached to Space Station\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grapple_Fixture" title="Grapple Fixture">\n        grapple fixtures\n       </a>\n       or the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mobile_Base_System" title="Mobile Base System">\n        Mobile Base System\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Dextre can also be operated whilst it is attached to the end of Canadarm2.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Apr2009_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Apr2009-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Each arm is somewhat like a shortened Canadarm2 (in that it has 7 joints) but is fixed to Dextre at one end.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       At the end of Dextre\'s arms are\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=ORU/Tool_Changeout_Mechanism&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="ORU/Tool Changeout Mechanism (page does not exist)">\n        ORU/Tool Changeout Mechanisms\n       </a>\n       (OTCM). The OTCM has built-in grasping jaws, a retractable socket drive, a monochrome TV camera, lights, and an umbilical connector that can provide power, data, and video to/from a payload.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OTCM_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-OTCM-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Dextre moves one arm at a time, while one arm may hold onto the station (using specially provided standard H or Micro interfaces\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SARAH2004_6-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SARAH2004-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">\n        :5.1\n       </sup>\n       ) for stability and ease of control\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SARAH2004_6-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SARAH2004-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">\n        :2.1\n       </sup>\n       the other is available to perform tasks.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Tools">\n        Tools\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Dextre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Tools">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       The lower body of Dextre has a pair of orientable colour TV cameras with lights, a platform for stowing ORUs, and a tool holster. The tool holster is equipped with two Robotic Micro Conical Tools (RMCTs), which allow an arm to grasp additional types of ORU fixtures. The Socket Extension Tool (SET) extends the length of the grasping socket on an arm, and the Robotic Off-Set Tool (ROST) allows an arm to grasp difficult to reach targets.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Several new tools were added as part of the 2011 Robotic Refueling Mission. A Wire Cutter, Safety Cap Removal Tool, EVR Nozzle Tool and a Multifunction Tool with several adapters.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In 2013 the H-II Transport Vehicle brought the Robotic On-orbit Transfer Cage (ROTC), which is a sliding table within the Japanese airlock platform from which to retrieve and subsequently install new hardware.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Robotic Refueling Mission \xe2\x80\x94 Phase 2 will use the Visual Inspection Poseable Invertebrate Robot VIPIR borescope camera with a 34 inch long flexible tube.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       SARAH (Self-Adaptive Robotic Auxiliary Hand) is a three fingered hand that is designed to attach to the end of Dextre\'s arm. It has not been delivered to the ISS.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SARAH2004_6-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SARAH2004-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Design_and_delivery">\n        Design and delivery\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Dextre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Design and delivery">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Dextre was designed and manufactured by\n       <a href="/wiki/MDA_Space_Missions" title="MDA Space Missions">\n        MDA Space Missions\n       </a>\n       as part of the $200-million contracted by the\n       <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Space_Agency" title="Canadian Space Agency">\n        Canadian Space Agency\n       </a>\n       , which will oversee its future operations and the necessary training of station crews.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       It completed all necessary testing and was delivered to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Kennedy_Space_Center" title="Kennedy Space Center">\n        Kennedy Space Center\n       </a>\n       (KSC) in Florida, in mid-June 2007. Once at KSC, it underwent flight verification testing followed by\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Space_shuttle" title="Space shuttle">\n        shuttle\n       </a>\n       integration.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Installation">\n        Installation\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Dextre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Installation">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Dextre was launched to the ISS on March 11, 2008 aboard\n       <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Endeavour" title="Space Shuttle Endeavour">\n        <i>\n         Endeavour\n        </i>\n       </a>\n       on mission\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-123" title="STS-123">\n        STS-123\n       </a>\n       . It "woke up" and activated heaters needed for keeping its joints and electronics warm after receiving power from the space station\'s Canadarm2 on March 14. During the mission\'s second spacewalk on March 16, spacewalking astronauts attached the two 3.35 metres (11\xc2\xa0ft) arms to the robot\'s main body and further prepared the machine for its handyman job on the station. After the spacewalk, crew members hooked Dextre back up to the station\'s robotic arm to keep it warm and to allow NASA to perform tests to ensure all of Dextre\'s electronics were working properly. Later that day, the crew tested all of its joints and brakes.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnn_11-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-cnn-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Astronauts finished outfitting the robot during a third spacewalk on March 17, 2008.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnn080317_12-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-cnn080317-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Purpose">\n        Purpose\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Dextre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Purpose">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Dextre is designed to handle\n       <a href="/wiki/Orbital_replacement_unit" title="Orbital replacement unit">\n        Orbital replacement units\n       </a>\n       , many spares are stored on the ISS and Dextre is able to carry them to and from worksites and install replacements when failures occur. Before Dextre arrived astronauts were required to perform space walks to carry out this work.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Operation_use">\n        Operation use\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Dextre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Operation use">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       After testing and trials the first use planned in the removal and replacement of the RPCM (Remote Power Control Module) in 2009.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Apr2009_4-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Apr2009-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Additional_usage">\n        Additional usage\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Dextre&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Additional usage">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       In early August 2004\n       <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">\n        NASA\n       </a>\n       declared its intention to use Dextre (or a close copy of it) as the robotic component for the\n       <a href="/wiki/Hubble_Space_Telescope" title="Hubble Space Telescope">\n        Hubble Space Telescope\n       </a>\n       rescue mission. Months after awarding a contract to\n       <a href="/wiki/MDA_Space_Missions" title="MDA Space Missions">\n        MDA Space Missions\n       </a>\n       to provide an SPDM copy for the Hubble repair mission,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       NASA then canceled the mission in favour of flying a Shuttle mission to perform the repairs/upgrades. NASA cited excessive risks and new-found confidence in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_external_tank" title="Space Shuttle external tank">\n        Space Shuttle external tank\n       </a>\n       as reasons for the cancellation.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Dextre was the subject of an April Fool\'s Day joke article on April 1, 2008 on NASA\'s APOD website.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">\n        <a href="#cite_note-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Dextre was also featured in the new $5 Canadian Polymer bank note together with\n       <a href="/wiki/Mobile_Servicing_System" title="Mobile Servicing System">\n        Canadarm2\n       </a>\n       , which Commander Chris Hadfield helped reveal aboard the International Space Station.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">\n        <a href="#cite_note-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span
