</tr>\n       <tr style="display:none">\n        <td colspan="2">\n        </td>\n       </tr>\n      </table>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        Hurricane Dora\n       </b>\n       was the first\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        tropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       on record to make\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">\n        landfall\n       </a>\n       over the\n       <a href="/wiki/First_Coast" title="First Coast">\n        First Coast\n       </a>\n       of\n       <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">\n        Florida\n       </a>\n       . The sixth tropical storm and second hurricane of the\n       <a href="/wiki/1964_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1964 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1964 season\n       </a>\n       , Dora developed from a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">\n        tropical wave\n       </a>\n       near the coast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Senegal" title="Senegal">\n        Senegal\n       </a>\n       on August\xc2\xa028. After a reconnaissance aircraft flight on September\xc2\xa01, the depression was upgraded to Tropical Storm Dora. It then curved northeastward and continued to strengthen. By early on September\xc2\xa03, Dora became a Category\xc2\xa01 hurricane. Six hours later, the storm reached Category\xc2\xa02 intensity. Intensification slowed somewhat, though Dora became a Category\xc2\xa03 on September\xc2\xa05. Deepening further, the storm peaked as a Category\xc2\xa04 with maximum sustained winds of 130\xc2\xa0mph (215\xc2\xa0km/h) early the following day. At 18:00\xc2\xa0UTC on September\xc2\xa06, Dora weakened to a Category\xc2\xa03 hurricane, then a Category\xc2\xa02 hurricane while curving westward early the following day.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Later on September\xc2\xa07, Dora re-strengthened into a Category\xc2\xa03 hurricane. Approaching the east coast of Florida, Dora\'s motion became erratic, making a few cyclonic loops. Around 04:00\xc2\xa0UTC on September\xc2\xa010, the hurricane made landfall near\n       <a href="/wiki/St._Augustine,_Florida" title="St. Augustine, Florida">\n        St. Augustine, Florida\n       </a>\n       , with winds of 115\xc2\xa0mph (185\xc2\xa0km/h). Two hours later, Dora weakened to a Category\xc2\xa02 hurricane. However, deterioration then slowed, with the hurricane falling to tropical storm intensity over\n       <a href="/wiki/Jefferson_County,_Florida" title="Jefferson County, Florida">\n        Jefferson County\n       </a>\n       on September\xc2\xa011. Dora then briefly drifted over southwestern\n       <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">\n        Georgia\n       </a>\n       , until turned east-northeastward late the next day. Early on September\xc2\xa014, the storm re-emerged into the Atlantic Ocean near\n       <a href="/wiki/Cape_Hatteras" title="Cape Hatteras">\n        Cape Hatteras\n       </a>\n       , North Carolina, and transitioned into an\n       <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">\n        extratropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       several hours later. The remnants of Dora moved rapidly northeastward and dissipated offshore Newfoundland early on September\xc2\xa016.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Along the coast, tides reached up to 10\xc2\xa0ft (3\xc2\xa0m) above mean sea level. Strong winds lashed North Florida, with sustained wind of 125\xc2\xa0mph (205\xc2\xa0km/h) observed in St. Augustine. In\n       <a href="/wiki/Jacksonville,_Florida" title="Jacksonville, Florida">\n        Jacksonville\n       </a>\n       , approximately 156,000\xc2\xa0customers were left without electricity, while about 19% of phones in\n       <a href="/wiki/Duval_County,_Florida" title="Duval County, Florida">\n        Duval County\n       </a>\n       were out of service. Much of the damage in the Jacksonville area occurred to older buildings and those located in coastal areas. Additionally, sections of the city experienced wind-induced river flooding in the vicinity of the\n       <a href="/wiki/St._Johns_River" title="St. Johns River">\n        St. Johns River\n       </a>\n       . Heavy rainfall damaged many unharvested crops and inundated numerous of roads and bridges, isolated some communities for several days. Throughout Florida, 74\xc2\xa0dwellings were flattened and 9,374\xc2\xa0received damage, while 14\xc2\xa0mobile homes were destroyed and 218\xc2\xa0others suffered severe impact. About 50\xc2\xa0farm buildings and 423\xc2\xa0small businesses were severely damaged or demolished. Three deaths and at least $230\xc2\xa0million in damage occurred. In Georgia, the storm damaged about 1,135\xc2\xa0homes and obliterated five others. Additionally, 18\xc2\xa0trailers suffered major impact, while 43\xc2\xa0small businesses were destroyed or experienced severe damage. There was one death in the state and at least $9\xc2\xa0million in damage. A few other states were affected by the storm, though impact there was much lesser. One death occurred in Virginia. Overall, Dora caused $280\xc2\xa0million in damage and five deaths.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="toc">\n       <div class="toctitle">\n        <h2>\n <p>\n       Hurricane Dora was first identified as a broad\n       <a href="/wiki/Low-pressure_area" title="Low-pressure area">\n        area of low pressure\n       </a>\n       on August\xc2\xa028, 1964, as it moved off the west coast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">\n        Africa\n       </a>\n       into the\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">\n        Atlantic Ocean\n       </a>\n       near\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dakar,_Senegal" title="Dakar, Senegal">\n        Dakar, Senegal\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DoraPrelimReport_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-DoraPrelimReport-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Traveling west-southwestward,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       the system brushed the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cape_Verde_Islands" title="Cape Verde Islands">\n        Cape Verde Islands\n       </a>\n       the following day. By August\xc2\xa031, images from the eighth\n       <a href="/wiki/Television_Infrared_Observation_Satellite" title="Television Infrared Observation Satellite">\n        Television Infrared Observation Satellite\n       </a>\n       (TIROS VIII) depicted a developing storm with a\n       <a href="/wiki/Central_dense_overcast" title="Central dense overcast">\n        central dense overcast\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Rainband" title="Rainband">\n        banding features\n       </a>\n       and cirrus\n       <a href="/wiki/Outflow_(meteorology)" title="Outflow (meteorology)">\n        outflow\n       </a>\n       . Observations from ships in the vicinity of the storm indicated decreasing\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barometric_pressure" title="Barometric pressure">\n        barometric pressures\n       </a>\n       and wind gusts up to 40\xc2\xa0mph (65\xc2\xa0km/h). On September\xc2\xa01,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_hunter" title="Hurricane hunter">\n        reconnaissance aircraft\n       </a>\n       flew into the system and determined that it had already become a tropical storm, with sustained winds reaching 60\xc2\xa0mph (95\xc2\xa0km/h). Shortly thereafter, the first advisory was issued on Tropical Storm Dora and the center was estimated to be roughly 850\xc2\xa0mi (1,370\xc2\xa0km) east of\n       <a href="/wiki/Trinidad" title="Trinidad">\n        Trinidad\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DoraPrelimReport_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-DoraPrelimReport-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Upon being classified on September\xc2\xa01, Dora turned towards the northwest and intensified.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_2-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Several reconnaissance missions into the storm indicated that it attained hurricane status during the afternoon of September\xc2\xa02;\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DoraPrelimReport_1-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-DoraPrelimReport-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       however, in the official\n       <a href="/wiki/HURDAT" title="HURDAT">\n        Atlantic hurricane database\n       </a>\n       , it is not listed as reaching this intensity until the nighttime hours.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_2-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Increasing in size and strength,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DoraPrelimReport_1-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-DoraPrelimReport-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Dora attained winds of 100\xc2\xa0mph (155\xc2\xa0km/h) early on September\xc2\xa03, the equivalent of a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SSHS#Category_2" title="SSHS">\n        Category\xc2\xa02\n       </a>\n       on the modern-day\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson Hurricane Scale">\n        Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson Hurricane Scale\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_2-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At the time,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meteorologist" title="Meteorologist">\n        meteorologists\n       </a>\n       expected the storm to maintain a northwesterly course and be steered over open waters by a\n       <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">\n        trough\n       </a>\n       associated with\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Cleo" title="Hurricane Cleo">\n        Hurricane Cleo\n       </a>\n       to the west.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_3-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       However, Dora "missed" the trough and gradually turned towards the west on September\xc2\xa06.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_3-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       That day, the hurricane attained its peak intensity as a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SSHS#Category_4" title="SSHS">\n        Category\xc2\xa04 equivalent\n       </a>\n       storm with winds estimated at 130\xc2\xa0mph (215\xc2\xa0km/h) and a central pressure of 942\xc2\xa0mbar (hPa; 27.82\xc2\xa0inHg).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_2-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Dora_1964_Florida_track.png">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2057" data-file-width="2599" height="174" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Hurricane_Dora_1964_Florida_track.png/220px-Hurricane_Dora_1964_Florida_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Hurricane_Dora_1964_Florida_track.png/330px-Hurricane_Dora_1964_Florida_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Hurricane_Dora_1964_Florida_track.png/440px-Hurricane_Dora_1964_Florida_track.png 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Dora_1964_Florida_track.png" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Enlarged track of Hurricane Dora detailing its erratic track prior to landfall\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       While executing the turn, Dora steadily weakened as its low-level\n       <a href="/wiki/Inflow_(meteorology)" title="Inflow (meteorology)">\n        inflow\n       </a>\n       was disrupted. By September\xc2\xa08, the storm restrengthened slightly and attained winds of 115\xc2\xa0mph (185\xc2\xa0km/h).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DoraPrelimReport_1-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-DoraPrelimReport-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_3-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_2-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Tracking westward towards\n       <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">\n        Florida\n       </a>\n       , Dora\'s forward movement decreased and became erratic as it neared the coast. Early on September\xc2\xa09, the storm abruptly turned southeastward before moving north for several hours. Throughout the remainder of September\xc2\xa09, the hurricane executed three distinct cyclonic loops while maintaining a general westward motion.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DoraPrelimReport_1-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-DoraPrelimReport-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       During the afternoon hours, Dora passed over the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Stream" title="Gulf Stream">\n        Gulf Stream\n       </a>\n       , resulting in its central pressure decreasing 9\xc2\xa0mbar (hPa; 0.27\xc2\xa0inHg) in a few hours.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Around 12:20\xc2\xa0a.m.\n       <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Time_Zone" title="Eastern Time Zone">\n        EST\n       </a>\n       on September\xc2\xa010, Hurricane Dora made\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">\n        landfall\n       </a>\n       about 6\xc2\xa0mi (9.7\xc2\xa0km) north of\n       <a href="/wiki/St._Augustine,_Florida" title="St. Augustine, Florida">\n        St. Augustine, Florida\n       </a>\n       , with sustained winds between 115 and 125\xc2\xa0mph (185 and 205\xc2\xa0km/h).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DoraPrelimReport_1-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-DoraPrelimReport-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Although estimated to have been a Category\xc2\xa03 at landfall, the highest winds onshore were believed to have been in the Category\xc2\xa02 range.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Striking northeastern Florida, Dora became the first\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        tropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       on record to make landfall in the region.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DoraPrelimReport_1-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-DoraPrelimReport-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Once onshore the storm gradually weakened, losing hurricane status about 24\xc2\xa0hours later, and began a gradual turn towards the northeast. During the morning of September\xc2\xa012, Dora became almost stationary over the southern border between\n       <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">\n        Alabama\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">\n        Georgia\n       </a>\n       . However, the storm rapidly accelerated and re-emerged into the Atlantic Ocean on September\xc2\xa014 near the\n       <a href="/wiki/Outer_Banks" title="Outer Banks">\n        Outer Banks\n       </a>\n       of\n       <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">\n        North Carolina\n       </a>\n       . Hours after moving over water, the system transitioned into an\n       <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">\n        extratropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       . The remnants of Dora were last mentioned on September\xc2\xa016 off the northeast coast of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Newfoundland_(Island)" title="Newfoundland (Island)">\n        Newfoundland\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_2-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">\n        Preparations\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Dora&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Caribbean">\n        Caribbean\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Dora&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Caribbean">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Upon Dora\'s Windward Islands\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_3-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">\n        United States\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Dora&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: United States">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       As Dora approached\n       <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">\n        Florida\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Gale_warning" title="Gale warning">\n        gale warnings\n       </a>\n       were issued for the northeast section of the coastline.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc3_7-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-disc3-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       As Dora moved inland, gale warnings were issued from\n       <a href="/wiki/Sarasota,_Florida" title="Sarasota, Florida">\n        Sarasota\n       </a>\n       to\n       <a href="/wiki/Pensacola,_Florida" title="Pensacola, Florida">\n        Pensacola\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc4_8-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-disc4-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In addition, small craft for much of the Gulf Coast to the west coast of Florida, and later in the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mid-Atlantic_States" title="Mid-Atlantic States">\n        Mid-Atlantic\n       </a>\n       were advised to stay in port until the storm subsided.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc4_8-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-disc4-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">\n        Impact\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Dora&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Impact">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Overall, Hurricane Dora was responsible for five deaths and $280\xc2\xa0million in damage, much of which occurred in Florida.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Bahamas_and_Florida">\n        Bahamas and Florida\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Dora&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Bahamas and Florida">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Doraatlanticbeach.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="463" data-file-width="600" height="170" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/Doraatlanticbeach.jpg/220px-Doraatlanticbeach.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/Doraatlanticbeach.jpg/330px-Doraatlanticbeach.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/Doraatlanticbeach.jpg/440px-Doraatlanticbeach.jpg 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Doraatlanticbeach.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Damage caused by storm surge in Atlantic Beach\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       In the Bahamas, Dora brought heavy rains and high winds to Nassau.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Along the coast of Florida, tides reached up to 10\xc2\xa0ft (3.0\xc2\xa0m) above mean sea level.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_3-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The hurricane also produced storm surge and abnormally high tides on the Gulf Coast of Florida, especially from\n       <a href="/wiki/Tampa_Bay" title="Tampa Bay">\n        Tampa Bay\n       </a>\n       to\n       <a href="/wiki/St._Marks,_Florida" title="St. Marks, Florida">\n        St. Marks\n       </a>\n       , where tides between 2 and 6\xc2\xa0ft (0.61 and 1.83\xc2\xa0m) were observed. Residents were forced to evacuate their homes. Strong winds lashed northeastern Florida, with sustained wind of 125\xc2\xa0mph (205\xc2\xa0km/h) observed in St. Augustine.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_3-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Many locations north of\n       <a href="/wiki/Daytona_Beach,_Florida" title="Daytona Beach, Florida">\n        Daytona Beach\n       </a>\n       received sustained winds of at least 100\xc2\xa0mph (160\xc2\xa0km/h).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-damage_11-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-damage-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Because of the slow movement of Dora, some places experienced the worst of the storm for as much as 24\xc2\xa0hours.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_3-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Many areas of North Florida received at least 10\xc2\xa0in (250\xc2\xa0mm) of rainfall, damaging many unharvested crops and inundating numerous roads and bridges, which isolated some communities for several days.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_3-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Throughout Florida, 74\xc2\xa0dwellings were flattened and 9,374\xc2\xa0received damage, while 14\xc2\xa0mobile homes were destroyed and 218\xc2\xa0others suffered severe impact. About 50\xc2\xa0farm buildings and 423\xc2\xa0small businesses were severely damaged or demolished.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ddamage1_12-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Ddamage1-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h4>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="First_Coast">\n        First Coast\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Dora&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: First Coast">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h4>\n      <p>\n       Jacksonville was one of the most severely impacted cities. Approximately 156,000\xc2\xa0customers were left without electricity, while about 19% of phones in\n       <a href="/wiki/Duval_County,_Florida" title="Duval County, Florida">\n        Duval County\n       </a>\n       were out of service. The power supply for Jacksonville and surrounding towns was lost for six days.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Much of the damage in the Jacksonville area occurred to older buildings and those located in coastal areas. Additionally, sections of the city experienced wind-induced river flooding in the vicinity of the\n       <a href="/wiki/St._Johns_River" title="St. Johns River">\n        St. Johns River\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_3-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Along the coast,\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Beach,_Florida" title="Atlantic Beach, Florida">\n        Atlantic Beach\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Jacksonville_Beach,_Florida" title="Jacksonville Beach, Florida">\n        Jacksonville Beach\n       </a>\n       were lashed by storm surge, which flooded low-lying areas and swept away houses and roads. One street in the latter was inundated with about 6\xc2\xa0ft (1.8\xc2\xa0m) of water. Three homes were destroyed and 3,992\xc2\xa0suffered damage, while 5\xc2\xa0mobile homes were demolished and 25\xc2\xa0experienced impact, overall in Duval County.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ddamage3_14-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Ddamage3-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Thebeatleskeywest.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="426" data-file-width="600" height="156" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Thebeatleskeywest.jpg/220px-Thebeatleskeywest.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Thebeatleskeywest.jpg/330px-Thebeatleskeywest.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Thebeatleskeywest.jpg/440px-Thebeatleskeywest.jpg 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Thebeatleskeywest.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         <a href="/wiki/The_Beatles" title="The Beatles">\n          The Beatles\n         </a>\n         at their hotel in Key West\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The storm was also one of the factors that nearly led to the cancellation of a\n       <a href="/wiki/The_Beatles" title="The Beatles">\n        Beatles\n       </a>\n       concert at\n       <a href="/wiki/Gator_Bowl_Stadium" title="Gator Bowl Stadium">\n        Gator Bowl Stadium\n       </a>\n       in Jacksonville on September\xc2\xa011; other reasons included that the Beatles would not perform with a\n       <a href="/wiki/Racial_segregation" title="Racial segregation">\n        segregated\n       </a>\n       audience, the\n       <a href="/wiki/American_Guild_of_Variety_Artists" title="American Guild of Variety Artists">\n        American Guild of Variety Artists\n       </a>\n       forced the Fab Four to pay union dues, and that a group of filmmakers attempted to create\n       <a href="/wiki/Bootleg_recording" title="Bootleg recording">\n        bootleg footage\n       </a>\n       of the concert. Those issues were eventually resolved. Initially, the Beatles planned to fly to Jacksonville after their concert in\n       <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">\n        Montreal\n       </a>\n       on September\xc2\xa08, but the storm forced their plane to be diverted to\n       <a href="/wiki/Key_West,_Florida" title="Key West, Florida">\n        Key West\n       </a>\n       . Just hours before the concert began, the Beatles arrived at\n       <a href="/wiki/Imeson_Field" title="Imeson Field">\n        Imeson Field\n       </a>\n       . More than 20,000\xc2\xa0fans attended the concert, though thousands of others could not attend due to power outages in the city. More than 200\xc2\xa0ft (61\xc2\xa0m) of sand was lost at the Coast Guard station.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-barnes_24-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-barnes-24">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Strong winds downed power lines and trees, which struck six homes.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-city-by-city_38-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-city-by-city-38">\n         [38]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Sixty-three dwellings in\n       <a href="/wiki/Daytona_Beach,_Florida" title="Daytona Beach, Florida">\n        Daytona Beach\n       </a>\n       were damaged and over 1,000\xc2\xa0sq\xc2\xa0ft (93\xc2\xa0m\n       <sup>\n        2\n       </sup>\n       ) of the roof at\n       <a href="/wiki/Daytona_Beach_International_Airport" title="Daytona Beach International Airport">\n        Daytona Beach International Airport\n       </a>\n       was lost.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-barnes_24-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-barnes-24">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       One house burned down in Glenwood, a community between\n       <a href="/wiki/DeLeon_Springs,_Florida" title="DeLeon Springs, Florida">\n        DeLeon Springs\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/DeLand,_Florida" title="DeLand, Florida">\n        DeLand\n       </a>\n       , after the owner forgot to extinguish a kerosene lantern before falling asleep.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39">\n        <a href="#cite_note-39">\n         [39]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In DeLand, winds uprooted many trees and caused power and telephone service outages. The winds and rainfall ranging from 6 to 7\xc2\xa0in (150 to 180\xc2\xa0mm) caused some losses of oranges and grapefruits.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-city-by-city_38-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-city-by-city-38">\n         [38]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In western\n       <a href="/wiki/Volusia_County,_Florida" title="Volusia County, Florida">\n        Volusia County\n       </a>\n       , one dwelling was destroyed and twenty-nine suffered major impact, while one hundred twenty-seven homes experienced minor damage. Additionally, 15\xc2\xa0trailers were significantly impacted.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ddamage7_35-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Ddamage7-35">\n         [35]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Seminole_County,_Florida" title="Seminole County, Florida">\n        Seminole County\n       </a>\n       , wind damage was mainly limited a to downed power lines, electrical poles, and trees, some of which fell on homes and blocked streets, particularly in\n       <a href="/wiki/Altamonte_Springs,_Florida" title="Altamonte Springs, Florida">\n        Altamonte Springs\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Geneva,_Florida" title="Geneva, Florida">\n        Geneva\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Sanford,_Florida" title="Sanford, Florida">\n        Sanford\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40">\n        <a href="#cite_note-40">\n         [40]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Two deaths occurred on September\xc2\xa09 when a helicopter being evacuated from the storm crashed near\n       <a href="/wiki/Sanford,_Florida" title="Sanford, Florida">\n        Sanford\n       </a>\n       , killing two Navy personnel.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_3-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Farther south, storm surge and above normal tides caused the loss of about 10 to 12\xc2\xa0ft (3.0 to 3.7\xc2\xa0m) of sand in\n       <a href="/wiki/Fort_Pierce,_Florida" title="Fort Pierce, Florida">\n        Fort Pierce\n       </a>\n       . Winds in the area left some power outages and toppled a steel, concrete sign at a courthouse.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-city-by-city_38-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-city-by-city-38">\n         [38]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Elsewhere_in_North_America">\n        Elsewhere in North America\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Dora&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Elsewhere in North America">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Dora_1964_rainfall.png">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="929" data-file-width="719" height="284" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Dora_1964_rainfall.png/220px-Dora_1964_rainfall.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Dora_1964_rainfall.png/330px-Dora_1964_rainfall.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Dora_1964_rainfall.png/440px-Dora_1964_rainfall.png 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Dora_1964_rainfall.png" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Map of rainfall from Hurricane Dora in the\n         <a href="/wiki/Southeastern_United_States" title="Southeastern United States">\n          Southeastern United States\n         </a>\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Some locations in Georgia also experienced hurricane-force winds and heavy rainfall from the storm, particularly in the southeastern portion of the state. Winds estimated at 90\xc2\xa0mph (140\xc2\xa0km/h) were experienced on the coastal islands, while gale-force winds were observed as far north as\n       <a href="/wiki/Savannah,_Georgia" title="Savannah, Georgia">\n        Savannah\n       </a>\n       and as far west as\n       <a href="/wiki/Waycross,_Georgia" title="Waycross, Georgia">\n        Waycross\n       </a>\n       . Hundreds of trees were toppled, which downed trees and power lines, with some areas experiencing a near complete lost of electricity. The storm also brought heavy rainfall, with some areas observing more than 10\xc2\xa0in (250\xc2\xa0mm) of rain, while much of the southern half of Georgia experienced more than 3\xc2\xa0in (76\xc2\xa0mm) of precipitation. Storm surge and higher tides resulted in erosion and coastal flooding.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata_41-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-stormdata-41">\n         [41]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On\n       <a href="/wiki/Jekyll_Island" title="Jekyll Island">\n        Jekyll Island\n       </a>\n       , two motels lost portions of their roofs and a ferris wheel was destroyed. Extensive erosion also occurred on the island.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-carter_42-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-carter-42">\n         [42]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Damage on Jekyll Island was conservatively estimated at $1\xc2\xa0million.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-additions_43-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-additions-43">\n         [43]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At least five homes were swept away on\n       <a href="/wiki/St._Simons,_Georgia" title="St. Simons, Georgia">\n        St. Simons Island\n       </a>\n       , while sections of the pier and seawall were demolished. The causeway connecting\n       <a href="/wiki/Sea_Island,_Georgia" title="Sea Island, Georgia">\n        Sea Island\n       </a>\n       and St. Simons Island to\n       <a href="/wiki/Brunswick,_Georgia" title="Brunswick, Georgia">\n        Brunswick\n       </a>\n       was flooded.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-carter_42-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-carter-42">\n         [42]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Many streets in Brunswick were covered with 1 to 3\xc2\xa0ft (0.30 to 0.91\xc2\xa0m) of water, leaving them impassible. In one section of the city, about 100\xc2\xa0homes were deroofed, while 25\xc2\xa0other homes in another section were severely damaged. An estimated 35%-40% of dwellings were damaged to some degree in Brunswick. Many trees, and power lines were downed, limiting communication between the islands and the mainland to radio networks. Portions of\n       <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_17_in_Georgia" title="U.S. Route 17 in Georgia">\n        Route 17\n       </a>\n       south of Brunswick was washed out.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-carter_42-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-carter-42">\n         [42]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       One man drowned in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Little_Satilla_River_(Atlantic_Ocean)" title="Little Satilla River (Atlantic Ocean)">\n        Little Satilla River\n       </a>\n       near Brunswick while attempting to secure his boat.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-carter2_44-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-carter2-44">\n         [44]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <a href="/wiki/Glynn_County,_Georgia" title="Glynn County, Georgia">\n        Glynn County\n       </a>\n       suffered over $3.6\xc2\xa0million in damage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-additions_43-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-additions-43">\n         [43]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Soil erosion caused by heavy rainfall in Waycross resulted in extensive damage to buildings and streets.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-carter_42-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-carter-42">\n         [42]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Throughout Georgia, the storm structurally impacted about 1,135\xc2\xa0homes and obliterated five others. Additionally, 18\xc2\xa0trailers suffered major impact, while 43\xc2\xa0small businesses were destroyed or experienced severe damage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ddamage1_12-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Ddamage1-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Although many pastures were flooded,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata_41-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-stormdata-41">\n         [41]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       impact on agriculture was minor.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-carter2_44-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-carter2-44">\n         [44]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Overall, the storm left at least $9\xc2\xa0million in damage in Georgia.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_3-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       A few other states were affected by the storm, though impact there was much lesser. One death occurred in Virginia.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_3-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="Newfoundland and Labrador">\n        Newfoundland\n       </a>\n       , over 100\xc2\xa0ships sought shelter at the\n       <a href="/wiki/St._John%27s,_Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="St. John\'s, Newfoundland and Labrador">\n        St. John\'s\n       </a>\n       harbor. The central portions of the province experienced heavy rainfall and winds up to 59\xc2\xa0mph (95\xc2\xa0km/h).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45">\n        <a href="#cite_note-45">\n         [45]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">\n        Aftermath\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Dora&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Aftermath">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       See also:\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricane names">\n        List of retired Atlantic hurricane names\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Following the storm,\n       <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">\n        President\n       </a>\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lyndon_Johnson" title="Lyndon Johnson">\n        Lyndon Johnson\n       </a>\n       toured the devastated area with\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Governor_of_Florida" title="Governor of Florida">\n        Florida governor\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/C._Farris_Bryant" title="C. Farris Bryant">\n        Farris Bryant\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Senate" title="United States Senate">\n        U.S. senators\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Spessard_Holland" title="Spessard Holland">\n        Spessard Holland\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/George_Smathers" title="George Smathers">\n        George Smathers\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_mayors_of_Jacksonville,_Florida" title="List of mayors of Jacksonville, Florida">\n        Jacksonville mayor\n       </a>\n       and Democratic\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_gubernatorial_elections,_1964" title="United States gubernatorial elections, 1964">\n        nominee for governor\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/W._Haydon_Burns" title="W. Haydon Burns">\n        Haydon Burns\n       </a>\n       . President Johnson also personally assessed the damage in coastal Georgia, including at Brunswick and St. Simons Island.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46">\n        <a href="#cite_note-46">\n         [46]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In the flooded areas, the\n       <a href="/wiki/American_Red_Cross" title="American Red Cross">\n        Red Cross\n       </a>\n       delivered\n       <a href="/wiki/Typhoid_fever" title="Typhoid fever">\n        typhoid\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Nitrazepam" title="Nitrazepam">\n        serum\n       </a>\n       by helicopter.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-barnes_24-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-barnes-24">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The name "Dora" had replaced "\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Donna" title="Hurricane Donna">\n        Donna\n       </a>\n       " on the hurricane lists, and it was retired from the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_tropical_cyclone" title="North Atlantic tropical cyclone">\n        Atlantic hurricane\n       </a>\n       lists and replaced with "Dolly" for the\n       <a href="/wiki/1968_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1968 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1968 season\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline"
