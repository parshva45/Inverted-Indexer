        <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Cleo_1964_track.png" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Map plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the\n         <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">\n          Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale\n         </a>\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       A tropical wave that exited the coast of Africa on August 15, 1964, moved westward, not organizing into a tropical depression until around 890\xc2\xa0miles (1432.3\xc2\xa0km) east of\n       <a href="/wiki/Barbados" title="Barbados">\n        Barbados\n       </a>\n       on August 20\xe2\x80\x93as reported by a Navy reconnaissance plane. It continued west-northwestward, quickly strengthening to a hurricane the next day with a minimum central pressure of 993\n       <a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">\n        mb\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Early in the afternoon of August 22, Cleo crossed\n       <a href="/wiki/Guadeloupe" title="Guadeloupe">\n        Guadeloupe\n       </a>\n       as a 115\xc2\xa0mph (185\xc2\xa0km/h) Category\xc2\xa03 hurricane. The hurricane continued to strengthen as it moved through the Caribbean Sea and reached its peak intensity of 155\xc2\xa0mph (250\xc2\xa0km/h) on the August 23 while south of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">\n        Dominican Republic\n       </a>\n       . It maintained that intensity for a day, bringing heavy rain and winds to\n       <a href="/wiki/Hispaniola" title="Hispaniola">\n        Hispaniola\n       </a>\n       . As Cleo passed south of\n       <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">\n        Haiti\n       </a>\n       on August 24, it veered northward momentarily, enough to move on to the Southwest Peninsula of Haiti. The circulation of the hurricane was greatly disrupted by the mountainous terrain of the island, quickly weakening the hurricane.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Cleo weakened to a Category\xc2\xa01 hurricane before hitting southern\n       <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">\n        Cuba\n       </a>\n       on the August 26. It crossed the island quickly. Shortly after emerging from the north coast of Cuba, Cleo restrengthened to a hurricane, having weakened to a tropical storm while over Cuba. Cleo managed to intensify to a 100\xc2\xa0mph (160\xc2\xa0km/h), Category\xc2\xa02 hurricane before hitting the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Miami,_Florida" title="Miami, Florida">\n        Miami, Florida\n       </a>\n       area on August 27.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pg4_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-pg4-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       It weakened to a tropical storm while over\n       <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">\n        Florida\n       </a>\n       on the 28th. The center moved offshore between\n       <a href="/wiki/Jacksonville,_Florida" title="Jacksonville, Florida">\n        Jacksonville\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/St._Augustine,_Florida" title="St. Augustine, Florida">\n        St. Augustine, Florida\n       </a>\n       , before moving back onshore near\n       <a href="/wiki/Savannah,_Georgia" title="Savannah, Georgia">\n        Savannah, Georgia\n       </a>\n       on August 29 without any increase in intensity. Its northward path along the Florida coast was unusual for the month of August.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Cleo continued to weaken as it moved through the Carolinas, drifting through as a tropical depression. After bringing heavy rain through the area, Cleo exited into the Atlantic Ocean near\n       <a href="/wiki/Norfolk,_Virginia" title="Norfolk, Virginia">\n        Norfolk, Virginia\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pg5_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-pg5-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and quickly intensified to a tropical storm again on the September 1. The following day, Cleo became a hurricane again, but it remained well offshore and did not cause any further damage. Cleo was last noted on September 5 northeast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_(island)" title="Newfoundland (island)">\n        Newfoundland\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Preparation">\n        Preparation\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n   [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Cleo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Guadeloupe">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Official reports from\n       <a href="/wiki/Guadeloupe" title="Guadeloupe">\n        Guadeloupe\n       </a>\n       indicated 14 dead, 40 injured, 1,000 homes destroyed and extensive damage to the island\'s infrastructure. The hurricane devastated sugar and banana plantations.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-time_16-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-time-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       One C-124 aircraft delivered seven tons of relief supplies to the island.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">\n        <a href="#cite_note-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Greater_Antilles">\n        Greater Antilles\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Cleo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Greater Antilles">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Moderate to heavy rains fell across\n       <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">\n        Puerto Rico\n       </a>\n       , peaking at 4.95\xc2\xa0inches (125.7\xc2\xa0mm) at Matrullas Dam.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rain_18-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rain-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The outer\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone#Physical_structure" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        bands\n       </a>\n       of Cleo produced peak sustained winds of 52\xc2\xa0mph (85\xc2\xa0km/h) at Point Tuna, Puerto Rico.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Offshore Puerto Rico on its initial penetration of Cleo on August 23, a Lockheed WC-121N\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Super_Constellation" title="Super Constellation">\n        Super Constellation\n       </a>\n       used as a reconnaissance aircraft experienced its port wing tip fuel tank and portion of wing torn away by extreme updraft turbulence, which injured six of its crew. While trying to exit the storm, the starboard tip tank and larger portion of wing were torn away by extreme down draft turbulence. The aircraft was damaged beyond repair.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">\n        <a href="#cite_note-19">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Cleo led to the wettest known 24\xe2\x80\x91hour period recorded for any site within the\n       <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">\n        Dominican Republic\n       </a>\n       for any month, with 19.99\xc2\xa0inches (507.8\xc2\xa0mm) falling at Polo.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ONAAGO_20-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ONAAGO-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Les Cayes,\n       <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">\n        Haiti\n       </a>\n       was leveled as the storm struck the town.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-time_16-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-time-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The aircraft carrier U.S.S. Boxer arrived off the coast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Hispaniola" title="Hispaniola">\n        Hispaniola\n       </a>\n       on August 29 to provide medical aid and evacuation services to those in the worst impacted areas of Haiti and the Dominican Republic. href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Cleo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Florida">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Cleo_1964_rainfall.png">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="918" data-file-width="973" height="189" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/Cleo_1964_rainfall.png/200px-Cleo_1964_rainfall.png" class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Cleo_1964_rainfall.png" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Cleo\'s rainfall in the United States\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The hurricane was the first to directly strike Miami since\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_King" title="Hurricane King">\n        Hurricane King\n       </a>\n       in the\n       <a href="/wiki/1950_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1950 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1950 season\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_1-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Cleo intensified rapidly just prior to landfall, bringing sustained winds of 100\xe2\x80\x93105\xc2\xa0mph (160 \xc2\xa0km/h\xe2\x80\x93170\xc2\xa0km/h) with gusts to 135\xc2\xa0mph (215\xc2\xa0km/h) to the Miami area, due to moving over water temperatures of 30 to 32\xc2\xa0degrees Celsius and its compact size. Lightning was observed within the eyewall at the\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">\n        National Hurricane Center\n       </a>\n       . The pressure fell to 967.6\xc2\xa0mb (28.57\xc2\xa0inches) in North Miami. Major damage was constrained to a 20\xe2\x80\x9335\xc2\xa0mile (30\xc2\xa0km\xe2\x80\x9350\xc2\xa0km) wide strip from Miami to\n       <a href="/wiki/Melbourne,_Florida" title="Melbourne, Florida">\n        Melbourne\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pg4_3-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-pg4-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       in the form of broken glass, interior flooding, uprooted trees, overturned aircraft, power failures, and agriculture. Cleo cut power to 620,000 homes and businesses in southeast Florida.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sun_21-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-sun-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In Miami Shores the electricity was out for five days. At least two dozen fires blazed across Miami.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-time_16-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-time-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       About a quarter of the grapefruit crop was lost within the Indian River citrus producing region. The storm surge reached 4\xe2\x80\x936\xc2\xa0feet (1.2\xc2\xa0m\xe2\x80\x931.8\xc2\xa0m) between Miami and\n       <a href="/wiki/Pompano_Beach,_Florida" title="Pompano Beach, Florida">\n        Pompano Beach\n       </a>\n       . The highest rainfall total measured within Florida was 9.37\xc2\xa0inches (240\xc2\xa0mm) at Stuart.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Florida_Rain_22-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Florida_Rain-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The Sebastian River Baptist Church had its roof torn off during the cyclone.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">\n        <a href="#cite_note-23">\n         [23]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Minor damage occurred north of Melbourne.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pg5_5-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-pg5-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Heavy rains fell along the east coast of the state, along and east of its track.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rain_18-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rain-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Three tornadoes were reported with the storm within the state borders.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pg6_24-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-pg6-24">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Cleo caused the\n       <i>\n        Fort Lauderdale News\n       </i>\n       , one of South Florida\'s biggest newspapers, to miss publishing, the only time that happened in its history. Storyland, a popular children\'s theme park in Pompano Beach, was destroyed and never rebuilt.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sun_21-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-sun-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A disaster declaration for Florida was made on September 8.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">\n        <a href="#cite_note-25">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <a href="/wiki/Florida_Atlantic_University" title="Florida Atlantic University">\n        Florida Atlantic University\n       </a>\n       \'s grand opening to students was delayed six days due to Cleo.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26">\n        <a href="#cite_note-26">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Southeast_United_States">\n        Southeast United States\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Cleo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Southeast United States">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Heavy rains spread up along the Georgia coast into the Carolinas and southern Virginia in association with the weakening tropical storm. Interaction with a frontal boundary to its north led to significant rains across extreme southeast Virginia to the left of its track exceeding 14\xc2\xa0inches (356\xc2\xa0mm) in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Norfolk,_Virginia" title="Norfolk, Virginia">\n        Norfolk\n       </a>\n       metropolitan area.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rain_18-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rain-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       This helped lead to double the average rainfall for the month of September for southeast Virginia.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27">\n        <a href="#cite_note-27">\n         [27]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Seven tornadoes were reported within\n       <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina" title="South Carolina">\n        South Carolina\n       </a>\n       , while\n       <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">\n        North Carolina\n       </a>\n       witnessed three tornado touchdowns.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pg6_24-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-pg6-24">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span      </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       After surviving Cleo\'s wrath in the Bahamas, a survivor came up with the idea of a floating hospital designed to help out areas after a catastrophe. A husband-wife pair who survived Cleo bought a retired luxury liner for such a purpose 14\xc2\xa0years later, and it was put into service in 1982.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28">\n        <a href="#cite_note-28">\n         [28]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <a href="/wiki/Virginia_Beach,_Virginia" title="Virginia Beach, Virginia">\n        Virginia Beach, Virginia\n       </a>\n       fought to build a drainage canal to help drain the Lynnhaven flood zone after Cleo\'s flooding rains struck the region. Over the objection of those in North Carolina, the canal was built.
