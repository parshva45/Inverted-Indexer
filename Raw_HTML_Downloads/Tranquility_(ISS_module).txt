</i>\n       , also known as\n       <b>\n        Node 3\n       </b>\n       , is a module of the\n       <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">\n        International Space Station\n       </a>\n       (ISS).\n       <a href="/wiki/European_Space_Agency" title="European Space Agency">\n        ESA\n       </a>\n       and the\n       <a href="/wiki/Italian_Space_Agency" title="Italian Space Agency">\n        Italian Space Agency\n       </a>\n       had Tranquility built by\n       <a href="/wiki/Thales_Alenia_Space" title="Thales Alenia Space">\n        Thales Alenia Space\n       </a>\n       . A ceremony on November 20, 2009 transferred ownership of the module to\n       <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">\n        NASA\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On February 8, 2010, NASA launched the module on the\n       <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle" title="Space Shuttle">\n        Space Shuttle\n       </a>\n       \'s\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-130" title="STS-130">\n        STS-130\n       </a>\n       mission.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The module provides six berthing locations, but one of the locations is disabled, as modules originally planned to be attached to\n       <i>\n        Tranquility\n       </i>\n       were canceled. STS-130 also brought the\n       <a href="/wiki/Cupola_(ISS_module)" title="Cupola (ISS module)">\n        Cupola\n       </a>\n       , a large window module and robotics work station to the ISS, which was then attached to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Nadir" title="Nadir">\n        nadir\n       </a>\n       -side of\n       <i>\n        Tranquility\n       </i>\n       . The module also includes various ISS systems, including additional\n       <a href="/wiki/Life_support_system" title="Life support system">\n        life support systems\n       </a>\n       .\n </div>\n      <p>\n       <i>\n        Tranquility\n       </i>\n       was built within the ESA-NASA ISS bartering system. ESA committed to build and fund both\n       <a href="/wiki/Harmony_(ISS_module)" title="Harmony (ISS module)">\n        <i>\n         Harmony\n        </i>\n       </a>\n       and\n       <i>\n        Tranquility\n       </i>\n       as well as the\n       <a href="/wiki/Automated_Transfer_Vehicle" title="Automated Transfer Vehicle">\n        ATV\n       </a>\n       in order to use NASA ISS facitilies, fly astronauts on the Shuttle and for other ISS services. ESA teamed up with the Italian Space Agency ASI to build both\n       <i>\n        Harmony\n       </i>\n       and\n       <i>\n        Tranquility\n       </i>\n       at\n       <a href="/wiki/Thales_Alenia_Space" title="Thales Alenia Space">\n        Thales Alenia Space\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/Turin" title="Turin">\n        Turin\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <i>\n        Tranquility\n       </i>\n       provides six\n       <a href="/wiki/Common_Berthing_Mechanism" title="Common Berthing Mechanism">\n        berthing locations\n       </a>\n       with power, data and commanding, thermal and environmental control, and crew access for more attached habitable volumes or for crew transportation vehicles or stowage, or an appropriate combination of all of these. One of the berthing locations is used by\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Cupola_(ISS_module)" title="Cupola (ISS module)">\n         Cupola\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       , which houses a Robotic Work Station inside it to assist in the assembly/maintenance of the ISS, and offers a window for Earth observations.\n       <i>\n        Tranquility\n       </i>\n       was launched with the\n       <i>\n        Cupola\n       </i>\n       attached to its port facing\n       <a href="/wiki/Common_Berthing_Mechanism" title="Common Berthing Mechanism">\n        Common Berthing Mechanism\n       </a>\n       (CBM). After mating\n       <i>\n        Tranquility\n       </i>\n       with the port CBM of\n       <a href="/wiki/Unity_(ISS_module)" title="Unity (ISS module)">\n        <i>\n         Unity\n        </i>\n       </a>\n       , the\n       <i>\n        Cupola\n       </i>\n       was transferred to the nadir facing port of\n       <i>\n        Tranquility\n       </i>\n       where it will stay.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The module has three redundant berthing ports that were not scheduled to be used prior to the end of the Space Shuttle program, although there is a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Power_Data_Grapple_Fixture" title="Power Data Grapple Fixture">\n        Power Data Grapple Fixture\n       </a>\n       reserved for the\n       <a href="/wiki/Dextre" title="Dextre">\n        Special Purpose Dexterous Manipulator\n       </a>\n       (Dextre), which is located on the zenith berthing location. The current ISS configuration requires\n       <i>\n        Tranquility\n       </i>\n       to be docked to the port berthing location of\n       <a href="/wiki/Unity_(ISS_module)" title="Unity (ISS module)">\n        <i>\n         Unity\n        </i>\n       </a>\n       . As such, the three unused berthing locations of\n       <i>\n        Tranquility\n       </i>\n       were disabled as the node\'s close proximity to other segments would prohibit the port\'s direct use for cargo spacecraft or using the docking module\n       <a href="/wiki/Pressurized_Mating_Adapter" title="Pressurized Mating Adapter">\n        PMA-3\n       </a>\n       , which was relocated from\n       <i>\n        Harmony\n       </i>\n       to the port berthing location of\n       <i>\n        Tranquility\n       </i>\n       for storage. At the time, the move of PMA-3 to the port location of\n       <i>\n        Tranquility\n       </i>\n       was required because NASA decided to leave the\n       <a href="/wiki/Multi-Purpose_Logistics_Module" title="Multi-Purpose Logistics Module">\n        Multi-Purpose Logistics Module\n       </a>\n       (MPLM) Leonardo permanently attached to the ISS, which will be located at the nadir side of\n       <i>\n        Unity\n       </i>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In 2001, NASA considered changing the design of the module. This idea for an extended or "stretched" module,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       was a result of the deferral/deletion of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Habitation_Module" title="Habitation Module">\n        Habitation Module\n       </a>\n       . The stretched module would have held 16 racks, compared with the baseline capacity of eight racks. This modification was not funded and the plans were abandoned.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Purpose">\n        Purpose\n       </span>\n       <span life support system\n       </a>\n       recycles waste water for crew use and generates\n       <a href="/wiki/Oxygen" title="Oxygen">\n        oxygen\n       </a>\n       for the crew to breathe. class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tranquility_(ISS_module)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" an online poll to name Node 3. Users were allowed to choose from among four provided names (Earthrise, Legacy, Serenity, and Venture), or to suggest their own.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rules_11-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rules-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In early voting, fans of the science fiction TV series\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Firefly_(TV_series)" title="Firefly (TV series)">\n         Firefly\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       boosted "Serenity", also the name of the show\'s\n       <a href="/wiki/Serenity_(film)" title="Serenity (film)">\n        film\n       </a>\n       and eponymous\n       <a href="/wiki/Serenity_(Firefly_vessel)" title="Serenity (Firefly vessel)">\n        spacecraft\n       </a>\n       , to the top with 86%.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On the March 3, 2009, episode of\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/The_Colbert_Report" title="The Colbert Report">\n         The Colbert Report\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       , host\n       <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Colbert_(character)" title="Stephen Colbert (character)">\n        Stephen Colbert\n       </a>\n       instructed his viewers to suggest "Colbert" as the name for Node 3 in the online poll.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Following Colbert\'s call to have the node named after him, several other groups attempted to influence the vote. For example, a number of different environmental groups promoted the name "Amazonia", after the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amazon_Rainforest" title="Amazon Rainforest">\n        Amazon Rainforest\n       </a>\n       . They argued that the name was more appropriate given that Node 3 will include the station\'s environmental control systems.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">\n        <a href="#cite_note-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Humorist\n       <a href="/wiki/Dave_Barry" title="Dave Barry">\n        Dave Barry\n       </a>\n       urged readers of his blog to name the node "Buddy", which finished as the sixth most popular user-suggested name.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-results_15-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-results-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <a href="/wiki/Gaia_Online" title="Gaia Online">\n        Gaia Online\n       </a>\n       asked its users to "Send Gaia to Space" by naming the node "\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gaia_mission" title="Gaia mission">\n        Gaia\n       </a>\n       ", referring to the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Greek_goddess" title="Greek goddess">\n        Greek goddess\n       </a>\n       of the planet\n       <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">\n        Earth\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">\n        <a href="#cite_note-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and "Gaia" finished third among the user-suggested names.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-results_15-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-results-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Other popular user suggestions included "\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MyYearbook" title="MyYearbook">\n        myYearbook\n       </a>\n       ", "\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SocialVibe" title="SocialVibe">\n        SocialVibe\n       </a>\n       ", "\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ubuntu_(philosophy)" title="Ubuntu (philosophy)">\n        Ubuntu\n       </a>\n       ", and the name of\n       <a href="/wiki/Church_of_Scientology" title="Church of Scientology">\n        Scientology\n       </a>\n       \'s galactic overlord: "\n       <a href="/wiki/Xenu" title="Xenu">\n        Xenu\n       </a>\n       ".\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-results_15-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-results-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       "Serenity" was the top choice among the NASA-provided names, with 70% of the vote, but finished second overall, losing to "Colbert" by more than 40,000 votes.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-results_15-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-results-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The naming contest rules, however, state that although the poll results will be taken into account, NASA has ultimate discretion in choosing an appropriate name for the node.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rules_11-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rules-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On April 6, 2009, Stephen Colbert, in jest, threatened a lawsuit if the node was not named after him.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">\n        <a href="#cite_note-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In addition,\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">\n        United States\n       </a>\n       Congressman\n       <a href="/wiki/Chaka_Fattah" title="Chaka Fattah">\n        Chaka Fattah\n       </a>\n       stated that he believes that paying attention to democracy and voting results should not be limited to earthbound organizations so he planned to use congressional power to force NASA to honor the winning Colbert write-in votes.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">\n        <a href="#cite_note-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       On April 14, 2009, astronaut\n       <a href="/wiki/Sunita_Williams" title="Sunita Williams">\n        Suni Williams\n       </a>\n       appeared on\n       <i>\n        The Colbert Report\n       </i>\n       , and announced the name of the node would be\n       <i>\n        Tranquility\n       </i>\n       .
