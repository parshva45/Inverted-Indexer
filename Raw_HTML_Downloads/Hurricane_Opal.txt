Opal\n       </b>\n       was the most intense\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_hurricane_wind_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson hurricane wind scale">\n        Category\xc2\xa04 hurricane\n       </a>\n       that did not reach\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_hurricane_wind_scale" title="Saffir-Simpson hurricane wind scale">\n  <a href="#cite_note-Environmental-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Continuing to track westward, the disturbance showed little signs of organization before entering the western\n       <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">\n        Caribbean Sea\n       </a>\n       on September\xc2\xa023. There, the wave became entangled with a broad\n       <a href="/wiki/Low-pressure_area" title="Low-pressure area">\n        area of low-pressure\n       </a>\n       east of\n       <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">\n        Nicaragua\n       </a>\n       , and the combined system drifted west-northwestward toward the\n       <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula" title="Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula">\n        Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula\n       </a>\n       . Even then, the disturbance lacked any significant development. However, a burst of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Convection_(meteorology)" title="Convection (meteorology)">\n        thunderstorm activity\n       </a>\n       occurred near the storm\'s center on September\xc2\xa027, prompting the\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">\n        National Hurricane Center\n       </a>\n       (NHC) to declare the system a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_depression" title="Tropical depression">\n        tropical depression\n       </a>\n       at 18:00\n       <a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">\n        UTC\n       </a>\n       that day. At the time, the depression was centered 80\xc2\xa0mi (130\xc2\xa0km) south-southeast of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cozumel,_Mexico" title="Cozumel, Mexico">\n        Cozumel, Mexico\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The primordial depression meandered across the Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula during the three days following\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclogenesis" title="Tropical cyclogenesis">\n        tropical cyclogenesis\n       </a>\n       due to the lack of dominant\n       <a href="/wiki/Prevailing_winds" title="Prevailing winds">\n        steering currents\n       </a>\n       . Despite remaining over land for an extended period, the depression developed organized\n       <a href="/wiki/Rainband" title="Rainband">\n        rainbands\n       </a>\n       , and ships in the region reported weather conditions that were suggestive of a stronger system. As a result, the NHC upgraded the disturbance to\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_storm" title="Tropical storm">\n        tropical storm\n       </a>\n       intensity at 12:00\xc2\xa0UTC on September\xc2\xa030 while the storm was over the north-central coast of Yucat\xc3\xa1n; this classification resulted in the tropical cyclone\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_naming" title="Tropical cyclone naming">\n        being named\n       </a>\n       <i>\n        Opal\n       </i>\n       , which also made it the first Atlantic storm to be given a name starting with the letter O.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OName_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-OName-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Over the next two days, Opal would enter the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">\n        Gulf of Mexico\n       </a>\n       and track slowly west-southwestward into the\n       <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Campeche" title="Bay of Campeche">\n        Bay of Campeche\n       </a>\n       . There, the storm strengthened into a hurricane at 12:00\xc2\xa0UTC on October\xc2\xa02. Shortly afterward, a primitive\n       <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">\n        eye\n       </a>\n       began to form. At the same time, a strong\n       <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">\n        trough\n       </a>\n       tracking across the United States caused Opal to slowly turn northeastward.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       After clearing the Bay of Campeche, Opal accelerated towards the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Gulf_Coast" title="United States Gulf Coast">\n        United States Gulf Coast\n       </a>\n       . The combination of warm\n       <a href="/wiki/Sea_surface_temperature" title="Sea surface temperature">\n        sea surface temperatures\n       </a>\n       associated with an unusually warm pocket of warm ocean waters and an upper-level\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/High_pressure_area" title="High pressure area">\n        high pressure area\n       </a>\n       over the Gulf of Mexico resulted in a highly conductive environment for intensification.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WCR_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-WCR-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       After a significant reorganization in Opal\'s internal structure, the hurricane was able to\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" title="Rapid deepening">\n        rapidly intensify\n       </a>\n       in these favorable conditions. At 12:00\xc2\xa0UTC on October\xc2\xa04, Opal reached peak intensity with\n       <a href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_wind" title="Maximum sustained wind">\n        maximum sustained winds\n       </a>\n       of 150\xc2\xa0mph (240\xc2\xa0km/h) and a minimum\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barometric_pressure" title="Barometric pressure">\n        barometric pressure\n       </a>\n       of 916\n       <a href="/wiki/Pascal_(unit)" title="Pascal (unit)">\n        hPa\n       </a>\n       (27.05\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inches_of_mercury" title="Inches of mercury">\n        inHg\n       </a>\n       ), making it a\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_Category_4_Atlantic_hurricanes" title="List of Category 4 Atlantic hurricanes">\n        Category\xc2\xa04\n       </a>\n       on the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_hurricane_wind_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson hurricane wind scale">\n        Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson hurricane wind scale\n       </a>\n       . The tropical cyclone\'s eye measured 12\xc2\xa0mi (19\xc2\xa0km) at peak intensity as the storm was beginning an\n       <a href="/wiki/Eyewall_replacement_cycle" title="Eyewall replacement cycle">\n        eyewall replacement cycle\n       </a>\n       . The progression of this cycle resulted in Opal\'s gradual weakening thereafter. At 22:00\xc2\xa0UTC that day, Opal made\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">\n        landfall\n       </a>\n       between\n       <a href="/wiki/Pensacola_Beach,_Florida" title="Pensacola Beach, Florida">\n        Pensacola Beach, Florida\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Cape_San_Blas" title="Cape San Blas">\n        Cape San Blas\n       </a>\n       as a Category\xc2\xa03 hurricane with winds of 115\xc2\xa0mph (185\xc2\xa0km/h). Weakening quickened as Opal moved further inland, degenerating into a tropical depression over\n       <a href="/wiki/Tennessee" title="Tennessee">\n        Tennessee\n       </a>\n       less than a day after landfall. The diffusing cyclone transitioned into an\n       <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">\n        extratropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       shortly afterwards; these extratropical remnants tracked towards the northeast before they were last noted along the northern coast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Lake_Ontario" title="Lake Ontario">\n        Lake Ontario\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">\n        Quebec\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div style="clear:both;">\n      </div>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">\n        Preparations\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Opal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Opal_04_Oct_1995_0815Z.jpg">\n         <img alt="Grayscale image of a well-organized tropical cyclone near the lower-left corner of the image. class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Opal_04_Oct_1995_0815Z.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Hurricane Opal near peak intensity on October\xc2\xa04\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Although Opal was a weak system located over the Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula at the time, 350\xc2\xa0people evacuated from\n       <a href="/wiki/Grand_Isle,_Louisiana" title="Grand Isle, Louisiana">\n        Grand Isle, Louisiana\n       </a>\n       on September\xc2\xa028 after rough surf associated with the distant cyclone began to threaten\n       <a href="/wiki/Louisiana_Highway_1" title="Louisiana Highway 1">\n        Louisiana Highway 1\n       </a>\n       \xe2\x80\x94the only thoroughfare connecting Grand Isle with the Louisianan mainland.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Edge_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Edge-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The following day, oil companies operating\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Offshore_oil_drilling_platform" title="Offshore oil drilling platform">\n        offshore oil drilling platforms\n       </a>\n       in the Gulf of Mexico began evacuating workers from the rigs to land.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SevenDead_6-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SevenDead-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On October\xc2\xa01, a coastal flood watch was issued for portions of the United States Coast due to the risk of\n       <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">\n        storm surge\n       </a>\n       from Opal. The\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">\n        United States Coast Guard\n       </a>\n       issued an advisory for boaters in the Gulf of Mexico to use caution or remain in port where possible. The next day, the\n       <a href="/wiki/Galveston,_Texas" title="Galveston, Texas">\n        Galveston, Texas\n       </a>\n       , emergency operations center opened to monitor the tropical cyclone and deliberate upon potential preparations for the city.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IsleOfficials_7-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-IsleOfficials-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Meanwhile, the launch\n       <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Columbia" title="Space Shuttle Columbia">\n        Space Shuttle Columbia\n       </a>\n       \'s\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-73" title="STS-73">\n        STS-73\n       </a>\n       mission set for October\xc2\xa05 was postponed due to Opal\'s anticipated effects.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-STS73_8-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-STS73-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       On October 3, a voluntary evacuation order was placed on\n       <a href="/wiki/Plaquemines_Parish,_Louisiana" title="Plaquemines Parish, Louisiana">\n        Plaquemines Parish, Louisiana\n       </a>\n       , due to the risks of potential storm surge. Schools were also closed by noon that day across the parish.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EvacuationOrdered_9-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-EvacuationOrdered-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Voluntary evacuation orders for the Florida panhandle, which would ultimately be the most heavily affected area, also began the same day with several counties giving evacuation orders. Farther west, a mandatory evacuation order was placed on\n       <a href="/wiki/Mobile_County,_Alabama" title="Mobile County, Alabama">\n        Mobile County, Alabama\n       </a>\n       , by then-\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_Governors_of_Alabama" title="List of Governors of Alabama">\n        Governor\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Fob_James" title="Fob James">\n        Fob James\n       </a>\n       . Just before noon October 4 with the hurricane near landfall,\n       <a href="/wiki/Escambia_County,_Florida" title="Escambia County, Florida">\n        Escambia\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Okaloosa_County,_Florida" title="Okaloosa County, Florida">\n        Okaloosa\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Santa_Rosa_County,_Florida" title="Santa Rosa County, Florida">\n        Santa Rosa counties\n       </a>\n       halted evacuations, instead ordering those still within vulnerable areas to seek shelter instead. However, the large amount of evacuees led to severe\n       <a href="/wiki/Traffic_congestion" title="Traffic congestion">\n        traffic congestion\n       </a>\n       ; Escambia County officials estimated that "tens of thousands" were still on evacuation routes within the county only a few hours before Opal made landfall. Mass evacuations in some other counties concluded during the afternoon of October 4, though many evacuees in other locations were unable to reach safe areas prior to the onset of gale-force winds.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OpalAssessment_10-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-OpalAssessment-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       An estimated 100,000\xc2\xa0people evacuated from the United States Gulf Coast ahead of Opal, with 5 percent of evacuees seeking public shelters and over half of evacuees seeking shelter out of their home counties; in their post-storm assessment the\n       <a href="/wiki/Federal_Emergency_Management_Agency" title="Federal Emergency Management Agency">\n        Federal Emergency Management Agency\n       </a>\n       (FEMA) noted that the landfalls of\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Allison_(1995)" title="Hurricane Allison (1995)">\n        Hurricane Allison\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Erin_(1995)" title="Hurricane Erin (1995)">\n        Hurricane Erin\n       </a>\n       on the Florida panhandle earlier in the year produced a dichotomous mood toward preparations for Hurricane Opal\xe2\x80\x94some people may have taken a "wait and see" approach that resulted in complacency, while others affected by the widespread power outages caused by Erin may have acquired a greater sense of readiness toward the approaching hurricane. Regardless, the overall evacuation process was described as chaotic, and the failure to clear\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_evacuation_route" title="Hurricane evacuation route">\n        hurricane evacuation routes\n       </a>\n       of casual or communal traffic was blamed for the relative unease during evacuations for Opal. Traffic congestion on these routes caused some evacuees to return to vulnerable areas along the coast while raising fears that stranded evacuees on highways could be subjected to potentially fatal hurricane-force winds.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OpalAssessment_10-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-OpalAssessment-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The post-landfall watches and warnings released in accordance with Opal were a flash flood warning released on October 5 for portions of\n       <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">\n        Alabama\n       </a>\n       , northern\n       <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">\n        Georgia\n       </a>\n       , and the western parts of\n       <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">\n        North Carolina\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina" title="South Carolina">\n        South Carolina\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HPCAdvisory1_11-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HPCAdvisory1-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The warning also included eastern\n       <a href="/wiki/Tennessee" title="Tennessee">\n        Tennessee\n       </a>\n       . A flash flood watch was also in effect for portions of the\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Upper_Ohio_Valley&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Upper Ohio Valley (page does not exist)">\n        Upper Ohio Valley\n       </a>\n       , the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mid-Atlantic_States" title="Mid-Atlantic States">\n        Mid-Atlantic\n       </a>\n       region, the central\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Appalachians" title="Appalachians">\n        Appalachians\n       </a>\n       , and the lower\n       <a href="/wiki/Great_Lakes" title="Great Lakes">\n        Great Lakes\n       </a>\n       . Wind warnings were in effect for northwestern South Carolina all the way to western\n       <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">\n        New York\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HPCAdvisory1_11-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HPCAdvisory1-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A gale watch was also in effect for\n       <a href="/wiki/Lake_Erie" title="Lake Erie">\n        Lake Erie\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Lake_Ontario" title="Lake Ontario">\n        Lake Ontario\n       </a>\n       , and the southern sections of the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Lawrence_Seaway" title="St. Lawrence Seaway">\n        St. Lawrence Seaway\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Six hours later, the gale warnings over\n       <a href="/wiki/Lake_Erie" title="Lake Erie">\n        Lake Erie\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Lake_Ontario" title="Lake Ontario">\n        Lake Ontario\n       </a>\n       , and the southern sections of the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Lawrence_Seaway" title="St. Lawrence Seaway">\n        St. Lawrence Seaway\n       </a>\n       were upgraded into a storm warning for Erie alone. The gale warning for the other two sections remained. The shoreline of Lake Erie was under a beach erosion warning from\n       <a href="/wiki/Buffalo,_New_York" title="Buffalo, New York">\n        Buffalo, New York\n       </a>\n       to\n       <a href="/wiki/Ripley,_New_York" title="Ripley, New York">\n        Ripley, New York\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HPCAdvisory2_12-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HPCAdvisory2-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The Storm Prediction Center released a tornado watch for northern and central\n       <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">\n        New Jersey\n       </a>\n       and portions of\n       <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">\n        New York\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Connecticut" title="Connecticut">\n        Connecticut\n       </a>\n       on October 6.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HPCAdvisory3_13-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HPCAdvisory3-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Significant non-surge areas of\n       <a href="/wiki/Escambia_County,_Florida" title="Escambia County, Florida">\n        Escambia County, Florida\n       </a>\n       , south of\n       <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_98_in_Florida" title="U.S. Route 98 in Florida">\n        US 98\n       </a>\n       were included in evacuation areas because of the potential for isolation by flooding.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Evac1_14-0">\n        <a America\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Opal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Mexico and Central America">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Although Opal was only a weak tropical storm when it affected the Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula, its slow movement led to prolonged, flooding rains over much of the region.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-News2_15-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-News2-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <a href="/wiki/Tabasco" title="Tabasco">\n        Tabasco\n       </a>\n       received about 20 percent of its annual rainfall in four days.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">\n        <a href="#cite_note-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Across\n       <a href="/wiki/Campeche" title="Campeche">\n        Campeche\n       </a>\n       and Tabasco, more than 42,000 people were forced to evacuate as rivers overtopped their banks.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MXAP1_17-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MXAP1-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       According to\n       <a href="/wiki/Notimex" title="Notimex">\n        Notimex\n       </a>\n       , an estimated 100,000 people evacuated due to the storm.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">\n        <a href="#cite_note-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       By October\xc2\xa01, an estimated 500,000\xc2\xa0acres of crops had been destroyed by the floods.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MXAP1_17-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MXAP1-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Campeche,_Campeche" title="Campeche, Campeche">\n        San Francisco de Campeche\n       </a>\n       , flood waters reached a depth of 6\xc2\xa0ft (1.8\xc2\xa0m). Governor Jorge Salomon ordered the closure of all government offices, businesses, and schools by October\xc2\xa02 in light of the extensive flooding.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">\n        <a href="#cite_note-19">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At least 60\xc2\xa0towns across the state were isolated by flooding. By October 3, agricultural losses in Campeche alone were estimated in the billions of pesos (hundreds of millions of dollars).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">\n        <a href="#cite_note-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At least 19 people perished across Mexico while another 31 were killed in Guatemala.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-News2_15-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-News2-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Florida">\n        Florida\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Opal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Florida">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       About 200 miles (320\xc2\xa0km) of\n       <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">\n        Florida\n       </a>\n       \xe2\x80\x99s coastline felt effects from Hurricane Opal.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Geocities_21-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Geocities-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Rainfalls in Florida peaked at 15.45 inches (392\xc2\xa0mm) in Ellyson, 7.27 inches (185\xc2\xa0mm) at Pensacola, and 6.64 inches (169\xc2\xa0mm) at Hurlburt Field. The highest gust recorded was a 145\xc2\xa0mph (233\xc2\xa0km/h) gust at Hurlburt Field. Lower gusts were 115 miles per hour (185\xc2\xa0km/h) at\n       <a href="/wiki/Eglin_Air_Force_Base" title="Eglin Air Force Base">\n        Eglin Air Force Base\n       </a>\n       and an 88\xc2\xa0mph (142\xc2\xa0km/h) gust at Pensacola P.N.S. The highest sustained winds were 84\xc2\xa0mph (135\xc2\xa0km/h) at Hurlburt Field and 80\xc2\xa0mph (130\xc2\xa0km/h) at\n       <a href="/wiki/Eglin_Air_Force_Base" title="Eglin Air Force Base">\n        Eglin Air Force Base\n       </a>\n       . The two reported storm surges were 5 feet (1.5\xc2\xa0m) to 6 feet (1.8\xc2\xa0m) above normal at\n       <a href="/wiki/Apalachicola,_Florida" title="Apalachicola, Florida">\n        Apalachicola\n       </a>\n       and 2 feet (0.61\xc2\xa0m) to 4 feet (1.2\xc2\xa0m) above normal at\n       <a href="/wiki/Sarasota,_Florida" title="Sarasota, Florida">\n        Sarasota\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Opal brought heavy surge to the area, 8 feet (2.4\xc2\xa0m) to 15 feet (4.6\xc2\xa0m) in some areas, comparing itself to\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Eloise" title="Hurricane Eloise">\n        Hurricane Eloise\n       </a>\n       , which struck the same area at near equal strength in\n       <a href="/wiki/1975_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1975 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1975\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Buildings_damaged_by_Hurricane_Opal.png">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="228" data-file-width="360" height="158" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/61/Buildings_damaged_by_Hurricane_Opal.png/250px-Buildings_damaged_by_Hurricane_Opal.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/6/61/Buildings_damaged_by_Hurricane_Opal.png 1.5x" width="250"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Buildings_damaged_by_Hurricane_Opal.png" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Damage from Hurricane Opal\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Opal caused about US$5.1\xc2\xa0billion (1995\xc2\xa0dollars) in damage, making it the third costliest hurricane at the time. Most of the structural damage occurred near the coastline on the Florida Panhandle, due to intense surge. Amazingly enough, nearly a mile of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Highway_98_(Florida)" title="U.S. Highway 98 (Florida)">\n        U.S. Highway 98\n       </a>\n       near\n       <a href="/wiki/Eglin_Air_Force_Base" title="Eglin Air Force Base">\n        Eglin Air Force Base\n       </a>\n       was completely destroyed. The pavement was nearly replaced by mounds of sand left behind after storm surge.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">\n        <a href="#cite_note-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Opal also spawned an\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fujita_Scale" title="Fujita Scale">\n        F2\n       </a>\n       tornado that killed a young woman in Florida. None died as a direct result of storm surge. This is quite unusual, considering the strength and landfall location of Opal.\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Okaloosa_Island" title="Okaloosa Island">\n        Okaloosa Island\n       </a>\n       , Fort Walton Beach, was overwhelmed by storm surge. Numerous homes were under 3 feet (0.91\xc2\xa0m) to 10 feet (3.0\xc2\xa0m) of water. Residents were not allowed to return to the island until the homes could be secured. A Humver tour was arranged by the National Guard so home owners could \xe2\x80\x98see\xe2\x80\x99 the damage, but the homeowners were not allowed off the bus. Sand dunes along the stretch of US 98, normally 25 feet (7.6\xc2\xa0m) high, were removed by wind and surge. Where once the ocean was obscured from view by the dunes for miles, a flat open space opened up along U.S. Highway 98.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="U.S._Gulf_Coast">\n        U.S. Gulf Coast\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Opal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: U.S. Gulf Coast">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       The peak rainfall from Opal in\n       <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">\n        Alabama\n       </a>\n       was 19.42 inches (493\xc2\xa0mm) 3 miles (4.8\xc2\xa0km) east-northeast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Brewton,_Alabama" title="Brewton, Alabama">\n        Brewton, Alabama\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HPC_23-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HPC-23">\n         [23]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Lesser amounts include 7.48 inches (190\xc2\xa0mm) in Mobile and 6.1 inches (150\xc2\xa0mm) in Anniston. The highest gust reported was a 95\xc2\xa0mph (153\xc2\xa0km/h) gust in Fort Rucker and a secondary one at\n       <a href="/wiki/Maxwell_Air_Force_Base" title="Maxwell Air Force Base">\n        Maxwell Air Force Base\n       </a>\n       with a gust of 90\xc2\xa0mph (140\xc2\xa0km/h). The highest sustained winds reported from Opal was 75\xc2\xa0mph (121\xc2\xa0km/h) at Fort Rucker, 55\xc2\xa0mph (89\xc2\xa0km/h) in downtown Mobile and 47\xc2\xa0mph (76\xc2\xa0km/h) at\n       <a href="/wiki/Maxwell_Air_Force_Base" title="Maxwell Air Force Base">\n        Maxwell Air Force Base\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Montgomery,_Alabama" title="Montgomery, Alabama">\n        Montgomery\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Numerous downed trees across much of the southeastern United States left over 2\xc2\xa0million without power. Alabama reported that 476,000\xc2\xa0people were without power, which was a record at the time. This record number of power outages was beaten by\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Ivan" title="Hurricane Ivan">\n        Hurricane Ivan\n       </a>\n       in 2004.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Power_24-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Power-24">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Damage was heavy all the way inland to\n       <a href="/wiki/Montgomery,_Alabama" title="Montgomery, Alabama">\n        Montgomery\n       </a>\n       where winds reached 90\xc2\xa0mph (140\xc2\xa0km/h).\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Highest rainfalls in\n       <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">\n        Mississippi\n       </a>\n       were 5 inches (130\xc2\xa0mm) throughout the eastern side of the state with lower amounts going westward.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HPC_23-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HPC-23">\n         [23]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Tropical storm winds were reported along the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gulf_Coast" title="Gulf Coast">\n        Gulf Coast\n       </a>\n       during the afternoon and early evening of October 4. Wind damage was mainly limited to downed tree limbs, power lines, and signs. Property damage cost estimated. One minor injury was reported in Harrison County due to flying debris. Damage in Mississippi totaled up to $75 thousand.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC5_25-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NCDC5-25">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">\n        Louisiana\n       </a>\n       , the only significant wind damage occurred in extreme south\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Plaquemines_Parish" title="Plaquemines Parish">\n        Plaquemines Parish\n       </a>\n       where winds were estimated around 60 miles per hour (97\xc2\xa0km/h) with gusts to hurricane force, with wind damage reported to some mobile homes and roofs of a few other structures. Tropical storm force winds, were reported in extreme south\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lafourche_Parish" title="Lafourche Parish">\n        Lafourche Parish\n       </a>\n       and Jefferson Parish, as well as extreme east\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Bernard_Parish" title="St. Bernard Parish">\n        St. Bernard Parish\n       </a>\n       . Property damage cost estimated. Tides were generally 2 feet (0.61\xc2\xa0m) above normal in Lake Pontchartrain, and three to five feet above normal along the southeast Louisiana coast from\n       <a href="/wiki/Grand_Isle,_Louisiana" title="Grand Isle, Louisiana">\n        Grand Isle\n       </a>\n       eastward. Some low-lying coastal roads were flooded.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC29_26-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NCDC29-26">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Approximately 10,000\xc2\xa0people evacuated from the southern, or lower portions, of Plaquemines, St. Bernard,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lafourche_Parish" title="Lafourche Parish">\n        Lafourche\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jefferson_Parish" title="Jefferson Parish">\n        Jefferson Parishes\n       </a>\n       . The only significant gathering of persons in public shelters occurred in\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Plaquemines_Parish" title="Plaquemines Parish">\n        Plaquemines Parish\n       </a>\n       , where 1,600\xc2\xa0people were placed in public shelters.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC29_26-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NCDC29-26">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Although no direct injuries occurred from the hurricane, an indirect injury is attributed to the hurricane in a freak accident. A\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jefferson_Parish" title="Jefferson Parish">\n        Jefferson Parish\n       </a>\n       employee was injured while attempting to lower a large flag on October 4. The employee, who was holding onto a rope attached a flag, was tossed high into the air, and suffered severe injuries when he fell back to the ground. Total damage in Louisiana totaled out to US$200,000 (1995\xc2\xa0dollars).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC29_26-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NCDC29-26">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Breaking swells from Opal in\n       <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">\n        Texas\n       </a>\n       caused water to spill across at the usual wash-over points which damaged several vehicles.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC26_27-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NCDC26-27">\n         [27]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Southeastern_United_States">\n        Southeastern United States\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Opal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Southeastern United States">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       The peak rainfall in\n       <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">\n        Georgia\n       </a>\n       was 8.66 inches (220\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Marietta,_Georgia" title="Marietta, Georgia">\n        Marietta\n       </a>\n       , 18.08 inches (459\xc2\xa0mm) in Peachtree City, and 7.17\xc2\xa0in (182\xc2\xa0mm) in west\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">\n        Atlanta\n       </a>\n       . Southern Georgia only reported 1\xe2\x80\x933 inches (25\xe2\x80\x9376\xc2\xa0mm) of rain, while the northern part of the state reached about 5\xe2\x80\x937 inches (130\xe2\x80\x93180\xc2\xa0mm). The peak wind gust in Georgia was a 79\xc2\xa0mph (127\xc2\xa0km/h) gust in Marietta, a 70\xc2\xa0mph (113\xc2\xa0km/h) gust in\n       <a href="/wiki/Columbus,_Georgia" title="Columbus, Georgia">\n        Columbus\n       </a>\n       , and a 56\xc2\xa0mph (90\xc2\xa0km/h) gust in the Atlanta-Hartsfield area.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HPC_23-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HPC-23">\n         [23]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR2_28-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR2-28">\n         [28]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       High winds in\n       <a href="/wiki/Rabun_County,_Georgia" title="Rabun County, Georgia">\n        Rabun County\n       </a>\n       caused $5\xc2\xa0million (1995\xc2\xa0USD) from the approach of Opal on October 5. The damage was worst in Rabun County where numerous trees were blown down. The wind damage was described as being worse than the March\n       <a href="/wiki/1993_Storm_of_the_Century" title="1993 Storm of the Century">\n        Superstorm of 1993\n       </a>\n       . Power was out for some people for at least a week.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC_29-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NCDC-29">\n         [29]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       More than 4000 trees were knocked down within the city of Atlanta alone. These trees fell across roads, and on power lines, homes, mobile homes, and automobiles. More than a half a dozen people were injured from falling trees in the early morning hours of October 5. There were more than 1200 telephone poles knocked down and almost 5,000 power lines snapped. Power crews from surrounding states helped to restore power to many, however, thousands of residences remained without power through the weekend.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       An 80\xc2\xa0foot (24\xc2\xa0m) gash was torn out of\n       <a href="/wiki/Interstate_285" title="Interstate 285">\n        Interstate 285\n       </a>\n       between Roswell Road and the\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Glenridge_Connector&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Glenridge Connector (page does not exist)">\n        Glenridge Connector\n       </a>\n       in Atlanta. Schools were closed on October 5 and October 6 throughout the cities of\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlanta" title="Atlanta">\n        Atlanta\n       </a>\n       , Marietta, and in Fulton, Coweta, Carroll and Douglas counties. A total of 47 of 101 schools were closed in\n       <a href="/wiki/DeKalb_County,_Georgia" title="DeKalb County, Georgia">\n        Dekalb County\n       </a>\n       alone. Four state parks were closed after Opal:\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Moccasin_Creek_Park&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Moccasin Creek Park (page does not exist)">\n        Moccasin Creek Park\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Black_Rock_Mountain" title="Black Rock Mountain">\n        Black Rock Mountain\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Vogel_State_Park" title="Vogel State Park">\n        Vogel State Park\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Fort_Mountain_State_Park" title="Fort Mountain State Park">\n        Fort Mountain State Park\n       </a>\n       . 273 stations reported many falling traffic lights. Agricultural experts estimated that damage to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Pecan" title="Pecan">\n        pecan\n       </a>\n       crop was about $50\xc2\xa0million. class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC4_31-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NCDC4-31">\n         [31]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A tornado in Chesterfield, South Carolina caused many trees to be blown down in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Carolina_Sandhills_National_Wildlife_Refuge" title="Carolina Sandhills National Wildlife Refuge">\n        Carolina Sandhills National Wildlife Refuge\n       </a>\n       . Trees were blown down in Orangeburg, one of those trees fell onto a car and totaled it. An F0 tornado spawned by one of Opal\'s bands downed a number of trees and power lines. Campers, vehicles, structures and boats were damaged in Greenville.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SC_32-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SC-32">\n         [32]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">\n        North Carolina\n       </a>\n       , over 17 inches (430\xc2\xa0mm) of rain fell. The rainfall included 9.89 inches (251\xc2\xa0mm) in Robinson Creek and 8.95 inches (227\xc2\xa0mm) in Highlands. The Robinson Creek rains spawned flash flooding.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NC2_33-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NC2-33">\n         [33]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Officials in the state had the citizens boil their water before drinking it because of a possibility that floodwater may have entered purification plants.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-News1_34-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-News1-34">\n         [34]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A landslide triggered by Opal and damaged the\n       <a href="/wiki/Blue_Ridge_Parkway" title="Blue Ridge Parkway">\n        Blue Ridge Parkway\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NC_35-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NC-35">\n         [35]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Opal triggered a debris flow in the Poplar Cove area of Macon County.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NC3_36-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NC3-36">\n         [36]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A flash flood from rainfall amounts typically ranged from four to six inches (152\xc2\xa0mm) and closed roads and bridges were the result. The most serious flooding apparently occurred in Avery County where evacuations were required and tanks of propane were found floating in the\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Banner_Elk_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Banner Elk River (page does not exist)">\n        Banner Elk River\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC28_37-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NCDC28-37">\n         [37]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Three deaths also occurred in the state. A man in Candler was killed when a falling tree destroyed his mobile home. Another man was killed near Marshall when a tree was blown onto him while he was helping cut other trees out of the roadway. 10\xc2\xa0people were also injured by wind blown debris and from falling trees. Damage from high winds totaled up to $15\xc2\xa0million.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC3_38-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NCDC3-38">\n         [38]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Opal_1995_rainfall.gif">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="916" data-file-width="848" height="238" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Opal_1995_rainfall.gif/220px-Opal_1995_rainfall.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Opal_1995_rainfall.gif/330px-Opal_1995_rainfall.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Opal_1995_rainfall.gif/440px-Opal_1995_rainfall.gif 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Opal_1995_rainfall.gif" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Rainfall Totals\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Mid-Atlantic_United_States">\n        Mid-Atlantic United States\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Opal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Mid-Atlantic United States">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">\n        Virginia\n       </a>\n       , trees in the Shenandoah Valley and along the Allegheny Plateau were blown down by 40\xc2\xa0mph (64\xc2\xa0km/h) winds at higher elevations. Over 7000\xc2\xa0people were without power and damage in Virginia totaled to $5000. The National Park Service reported dozens of trees blown down along Skyline Drive in two counties.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC6_39-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NCDC6-39">\n         [39]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In the\n       <a href="/wiki/Great_Smoky_Mountains" title="Great Smoky Mountains">\n        Great Smoky Mountains\n       </a>\n       , power and phone service were out in many areas of the park.\n       <a href="/wiki/Newfound_Gap" title="Newfound Gap">\n        Newfound Gap\n       </a>\n       road closed for several days due to trees and a rockslide that were lying across it. Campers were asked to leave\n       <a href="/wiki/Elkmont,_Tennessee" title="Elkmont, Tennessee">\n        Elkmont Campground\n       </a>\n       near Gatlinburg because of high water.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GSM-BR_40-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-GSM-BR-40">\n         [40]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Many sections of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Blue_Ridge_Parkway" title="Blue Ridge Parkway">\n        Blue Ridge Parkway\n       </a>\n       were closed because of trees that fell across the road. Flooding occurred in the northern portion of the parkway. Linville Falls was evacuated; the Asheville and Gillespie Gap Districts were closed. Rockslides and mudslides had also been reported due to Opal.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GSM-BR_40-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-GSM-BR-40">\n         [40]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/West_Virginia" title="West Virginia">\n        West Virginia\n       </a>\n       , 0.5\xe2\x80\x931.5 inches (13\xe2\x80\x9338\xc2\xa0mm) of rain were reported causing some schools to let out early.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC15_41-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NCDC15-41">\n         [41]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       High winds associated with the remnants of Opal moved through southeast West Virginia on the 5th. High winds ranged between 35 and 45\xc2\xa0mph (72\xc2\xa0km/h) with some gusts to near 55\xc2\xa0mph (89\xc2\xa0km/h). A numerous amount of trees, large branches and power lines and ripped shingles off the roof of homes were ripped off in the wind. The vast majority of damage occurred at elevations above two thousand feet. Damage from the wind totaled out to $2000.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC16_42-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NCDC16-42">\n         [42]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Large limbs were downed by high wind across from the remnants of Hurricane Opal in Preston County. Damage there totaled out to $1000.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC17_43-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NCDC17-43">\n         [43]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Damage in West Virginia totaled out to only $5,000.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Maryland" title="Maryland">\n        Maryland\n       </a>\n       , a large tree and its limbs along\n       <a href="/wiki/Maryland_Route_495" title="Maryland Route 495">\n        Maryland Route 495\n       </a>\n       near\n       <a href="/wiki/Bittinger,_Maryland" title="Bittinger, Maryland">\n        Bittinger\n       </a>\n       were downed by high winds associated with the remnants of Opal. The damage from the fallen tree and its limbs totaled out to one thousand dollars.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC11_44-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NCDC11-44">\n         [44]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <a href="/wiki/Mesocyclone" title="Mesocyclone">\n        Mesocyclones\n       </a>\n       moving around Opal\'s eastern periphery within Maryland spawned three tornadoes in\n       <a href="/wiki/Charles_County,_Maryland" title="Charles County, Maryland">\n        Charles\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Prince_George%27s_County,_Maryland" title="Prince George\'s County, Maryland">\n        Prince George\'s\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Anne_Arundel_County,_Maryland" title="Anne Arundel County, Maryland">\n        Anne Arundel County\n       </a>\n       . The first tornado tracked along\n       <a href="/wiki/Maryland_Route_425" title="Maryland Route 425">\n        State Route 425\n       </a>\n       between the towns of Ironside and Grayton. Along the tornado\'s path, several trees were uprooted or snapped; two sheds were destroyed and two others sustained roof damage. Windows were blown out of a barn and several vehicles. Ten thousand dollars in damage occurred.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC12_45-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NCDC12-45">\n         [45]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The second and strongest tornado hit ground in Temple Hills, injuring three\xc2\xa0people after reaching a peak wind of 150\xc2\xa0mph (240\xc2\xa0km/h). 100 homes were damaged with 15 being condemned. The Potomac Electric Power reported 9000\xc2\xa0people without power. Damage from the second tornado totaled out to $5\xc2\xa0million.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC13_46-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NCDC13-46">\n         [46]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The third and final tornado touched down in Odenton, became an F1 tornado and doing $250,000 in damage to the area. Eleven houses were damaged and about 10,000\xc2\xa0people were without power in the whole district that the Baltimore Gas and Electric serves.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC14_47-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NCDC14-47">\n         [47]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Central_United_States">\n        Central United States\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Opal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Central United States">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Tennessee" title="Tennessee">\n        Tennessee\n       </a>\n       , rainfalls included about 3 to 5 inches (76 to 127\xc2\xa0mm) in the central part of the state, one inch (25\xc2\xa0mm) at the western part of the state and 3 to 5\xc2\xa0in (76 to 127\xc2\xa0mm) in the eastern part of the state.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HPC_23-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HPC-23">\n         [23]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Wind speeds at the higher elevations of the Appalachian Mountains were recorded at 70\xc2\xa0mph (110\xc2\xa0km/h) whereas 40\xe2\x80\x9350\xc2\xa0mph (64\xe2\x80\x9380\xc2\xa0km/h) gusts were more common at the lower elevations. Trees and power lines were down over much of the region. Over 70 miles (110\xc2\xa0km) of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Appalachian_Trail" title="Appalachian Trail">\n        Appalachian Trail\n       </a>\n       were closed due to trees being down. A total of over 20,000\xc2\xa0people were without power from Opal\'s wrath. The most damage occurred in\n       <a href="/wiki/Hamilton_County,_Tennessee" title="Hamilton County, Tennessee">\n        Hamilton County\n       </a>\n       , which had damage was estimated over a total of $1\xc2\xa0million. Damage in Hamilton County include a circus was left stranded at a campground and needed to be evacuated. A number of residences and businesses were also surrounded by water and occupants were to be evacuated. Total damage in Tennessee totaled out at $2.02\xc2\xa0million.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC7_48-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NCDC7-48">\n         [48]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Kentucky" title="Kentucky">\n        Kentucky\n       </a>\n       , 1 to 5 inches (25 to 127\xc2\xa0mm) of rain was reported throughout the state from Opal.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HPC_23-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HPC-23">\n         [23]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Total rainfall across\n       <a href="/wiki/Jefferson_County,_Kentucky" title="Jefferson County, Kentucky">\n        Jefferson County\n       </a>\n       , where\n       <a href="/wiki/Louisville,_Kentucky" title="Louisville, Kentucky">\n        Louisville\n       </a>\n       is located ranged from 2.4 inches (61\xc2\xa0mm) at the Louisville International Airport to 3.5 inches (89\xc2\xa0mm) at Fern Creek Road south of the Gene Snyder Freeway. Several trees were knocked down and soils were saturated after Opal passed through.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC8_49-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NCDC8-49">\n         [49]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A bridge washed out over Sulphur Creek and minor flooding was reported across Kentucky Route 80.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC9_50-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NCDC9-50">\n         [50]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Elizabethtown and the Fort Knox area had several roads closed after Opal washed them out.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC10_51-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NCDC10-51">\n         [51]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Michigan" title="Michigan">\n        Michigan\n       </a>\n       , Opal produced 2 to 3 inches (51 to 76\xc2\xa0mm) of rain over the\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Middle_Rouge_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Middle Rouge River (page does not exist)">\n        Middle Rouge River\n       </a>\n       Basin from late afternoon through the evening on the 5th. As a result, the Middle Rouge River crested one foot over flood stage, causing the Edward Hines Drive to be closed off to traffic.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC19_52-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NCDC19-52">\n         [52]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       High winds associated with the remains of Hurricane Opal affected the area during the late afternoon and early evening on the 5th. Strong northeast winds destroyed a new 200-foot (61-m), two-story pole barn on the Marine City Highway in\n       <a href="/wiki/Marine_City,_Michigan" title="Marine City, Michigan">\n        Marine City\n       </a>\n       . The storm also cut power to several areas, resulting in some school closings. The maximum wind gust at\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Detroit_Metro_Airport" title="Detroit Metro Airport">\n        Detroit Metro Airport\n       </a>\n       was a 38-mph (61-km/h) gust, which was from the northeast. The damage from this incident totaled out to $15,000.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC18_53-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NCDC18-53">\n         [53]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The remnants of Opal passed across northeast\n       <a href="/wiki/Ohio" title="Ohio">\n        Ohio\n       </a>\n       and caused wind gusts up to 45\xc2\xa0mph (72\xc2\xa0km/h) and sustained winds of 20 to 30\xc2\xa0mph (32 to 48\xc2\xa0km/h) all across northern Ohio. Several automobiles were damaged by falling trees or limbs. Crops were damaged from the strong winds. A number of farms reported fields of corn blown over and ripe apples and other fruit being stripped from trees.\n       <a href="/wiki/Rain" title="Rain">\n        Rainfall\n       </a>\n       of 3.4 inches (86\xc2\xa0mm) in less than 24\xc2\xa0hours was measured at\n       <a href="/wiki/Mansfield,_Ohio" title="Mansfield, Ohio">\n        Mansfield\n       </a>\n       , and most areas averaged 1.5 to 2.5 inches (38 to 64\xc2\xa0mm) during the same period. Flooding was localized and not significant since very dry conditions preceded the storm.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC21_54-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NCDC21-54">\n         [54]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Sustained northeast\n       <a href="/wiki/Wind" title="Wind">\n        winds\n       </a>\n       ahead of the storm reached 55\xc2\xa0mph (89\xc2\xa0km/h) all across the lake with gusts to 70\xc2\xa0mph (110\xc2\xa0km/h) producing\n       <a href="/wiki/Wave" title="Wave">\n        waves\n       </a>\n       of 10 to 14 feet (3.0 to 4.3\xc2\xa0m). Boats were also grounded. </td>\n        <td align="center">\n         33\n        </td>\n       </tr>\n      </table>\n      <p>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">\n        New Jersey\n       </a>\n       , thunderstorms with heavy rain, averaging around 3.5 inches (89\xc2\xa0mm) countywide, caused flooding of small streams and roadways including United States Route 46. The heavy rain was represented as the first significant dent in the drought that had affected northern New Jersey since September 1994. Storm totals included 6.7 inches (170\xc2\xa0mm) in Wawayanda, 5.3 (135\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Hackettstown,_New_Jersey" title="Hackettstown, New Jersey">\n        Hackettstown\n       </a>\n       , 4.5 inches (110\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Oak_Ridge,_New_Jersey" title="Oak Ridge, New Jersey">\n        Oak Ridge\n       </a>\n       , 4.2 inches (110\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Clinton,_New_Jersey" title="Clinton, New Jersey">\n        Clinton\n       </a>\n       and 4.10 inches (104\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Pequannock_Township,_New_Jersey" title="Pequannock Township, New Jersey">\n        Pequannock\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The remnants of Opal caused severe thunderstorms that uprooted trees near\n       <a href="/wiki/Belvidere,_New_Jersey" title="Belvidere, New Jersey">\n        Belvidere\n       </a>\n       . Trees and wires were down in scattered parts of the county including Route 57 near the Tri-county Firehouse. Downed wires caused power outages in\n       <a href="/wiki/Hackettstown,_New_Jersey" title="Hackettstown, New Jersey">\n        Hackettstown\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Mansfield_Township,_Warren_County,_New_Jersey" title="Mansfield Township, Warren County, New Jersey">\n        Mansfield Township\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC20_57-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NCDC20-57">\n         [57]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The remnants of Hurricane Opal passed over northwest\n       <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">\n        Pennsylvania\n       </a>\n       on Thursday night (5th/6th) and caused wind gusts up to 50\xc2\xa0mph (80\xc2\xa0km/h) and sustained winds of 20 to 30\xc2\xa0mph (32 to 48\xc2\xa0km/h). Heavy rains accompanied the storm and averaged 1.5 to 2.5 inches (38 to 64\xc2\xa0mm). Flooding was localized and not significant since drought conditions preceded the storm. The prolonged period of strong winds brought down trees and limbs along with some power lines. At least one automobile was damaged by a downed tree in Erie. Several farms reported fields of corn blown down and apples and other fruit being stripped from trees. The actual crop damage estimate was unknown.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC23_58-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NCDC23-58">\n         [58]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The remnants of Opal passed just to the west of\n       <a href="/wiki/Buffalo,_New_York" title="Buffalo, New York">\n        Buffalo\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">\n        New York\n       </a>\n       on October 5 and 6. Two to 3\xc2\xa0inches (38\xe2\x80\x9364\xc2\xa0mm) of rain fell over much of the area with isolated amounts of near four inches (102\xc2\xa0mm) over parts of the western Southern Tier. Sustained winds were estimated between 35 and 40\xc2\xa0mph (56 and 64\xc2\xa0km/h), but the easterly winds did down some trees and power lines. In\n       <a href="/wiki/Oneida_County,_New_York" title="Oneida County, New York">\n        Oneida County\n       </a>\n       , the high winds downed trees and wires in New York Mills, Waterville, Sylvan Beach, North Bay, Lee Center, Rome, McConnellsville and Verona. In\n       <a href="/wiki/Saratoga_County,_New_York" title="Saratoga County, New York">\n        Saratoga County\n       </a>\n       a large tree limb was downed in\n       <a href="/wiki/Saratoga_Springs,_New_York" title="Saratoga Springs, New York">\n        Saratoga Springs\n       </a>\n       which damaged four cars. Total damage in New York totaled to $35,000.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC30_59-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NCDC30-59">\n         [59]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC31_60-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NCDC31-60">\n         [60]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       A low pressure area which used to be Opal moved across western and northern New York late and into\n       <a href="/wiki/Vermont" title="Vermont">\n        Vermont\n       </a>\n       on the night of October 5 and the morning October 6. Damaging winds occurred across parts of central and northern Vermont but especially along the western slopes of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Green_Mountains" title="Green Mountains">\n        Green Mountains\n       </a>\n       . Damaging winds downed trees and power lines across Essex, Orleans, Addison, Caledonia and Rutland counties. In\n       <a href="/wiki/Essex_County,_Vermont" title="Essex County, Vermont">\n        Essex County\n       </a>\n       damage occurred in Canaan and Concord. Damage was also reported in\n       <a href="/wiki/Caledonia_County,_Vermont" title="Caledonia County, Vermont">\n        Caledonia County\n       </a>\n       , in\n       <a href="/wiki/Rutland_County,_Vermont" title="Rutland County, Vermont">\n        Rutland County\n       </a>\n       , in Clarendon and Chittenden and in\n       <a href="/wiki/Orleans_County,_Vermont" title="Orleans County, Vermont">\n        Orleans County\n       </a>\n       in Derby Center. Total damage in Vermont totaled out to $135,000.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC27_61-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NCDC27-61">\n         [61]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Heavy winds and rain associated with the remnants of Opal brought down trees and knocked out power in southwestern and northern\n       <a href="/wiki/New_Hampshire" title="New Hampshire">\n        New Hampshire\n       </a>\n       . One person was injured in\n       <a href="/wiki/Marlborough,_New_Hampshire" title="Marlborough, New Hampshire">\n        Marlborough\n       </a>\n       when a large tree blew onto his moving pickup truck.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC25_62-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NCDC25-62">\n         [62]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Maine" title="Maine">\n        Maine\n       </a>\n       , heavy winds and rain associated with the remnants of Opal brought down trees and knocked out power in coastal areas of southern Maine. Some beach erosion occurred in\n       <a href="/wiki/Saco,_Maine" title="Saco, Maine">\n        Saco\n       </a>\n       . Strong winds ripped away boats from their moorings in the Midcoast towns of\n       <a href="/wiki/Camden,_Maine" title="Camden, Maine">\n        Camden\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Rockland,_Maine" title="Rockland, Maine">\n        Rockland\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC24_63-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NCDC24-63">\n         [63]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Canada">\n        Canada\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Opal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Canada">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Wind and gale warnings were issued in southern\n       <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">\n        Ontario\n       </a>\n       and the upper\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Lawrence_River" title="St. Lawrence River">\n        St. Lawrence River\n       </a>\n       in accordance with the remnants of Opal by the\n       <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Hurricane_Centre" title="Canadian Hurricane Centre">\n        Canadian Hurricane Centre\n       </a>\n       on October 5. The remnants also spawned a heavy rainfall warning by the Ontario Hurricane Center and the Quebec Hurricane Center for both provinces. The anticipated rain would not exist as the same as\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hazel" title="Hurricane Hazel">\n        Hurricane Hazel\n       </a>\n       41\xc2\xa0years before. The leftover system of Opal spawned a gale warning for\n       <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">\n        Nova Scotia\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CHCAdvisory1_64-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CHCAdvisory1-64">\n         [64]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Rainfalls equaled out to 0.5 inches (13\xc2\xa0mm) in Northwestern\n       <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">\n        Ontario\n       </a>\n       , 0.5 inches (13\xc2\xa0mm) in southern parts of\n       <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">\n        Quebec\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/New_Brunswick" title="New Brunswick">\n        New Brunswick\n       </a>\n       . Rainfall stretched out to the area of\n       <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">\n        Nova Scotia\n       </a>\n       , but only 0.5 inches (13\xc2\xa0mm) was reported.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RainCan_65-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-RainCan-65">\n         [65]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Toronto had recorded winds of at least 52\xc2\xa0mph (84\xc2\xa0km/h) and rainfall of about 3.09 inches (78\xc2\xa0mm).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RainCan2_66-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-RainCan2-66">\n         [66]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Retirement">\n        Retirement\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Opal&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Retirement">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       See also:\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricane names">\n        List of retired Atlantic hurricane names\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Owing to the extreme destruction in the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southeast_United_States" title="Southeast United States">\n        Southeast United States\n       </a>\n       and the death toll in\n       <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">\n        Mexico\n       </a>\n       , the name "Opal" was retired in the spring of 1996, and will never again be used in the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlantic_basin" title="Atlantic basin">\n        Atlantic basin\n       </a>\n       . It was replaced with "\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Olga_(2001)" title="Hurricane Olga (2001)">\n        Olga\n       </a>\n       " in the\n       <a href="/wiki/2001_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2001 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        2001 season\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Opal was the first storm to be assigned a name beginning with letter \'O\' since hurricane naming began in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">\n        Atlantic\n       </a>\n       in 1950.\n      </p>\n
