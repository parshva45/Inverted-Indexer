</div>\n         The Ligurian Sea\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       A\n       <b>\n        Genoa low\n       </b>\n       (also known as\n       <b>\n        Genoa Genoa">\n        Gulf of Genoa\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Ligurian_Sea" title="Ligurian Sea">\n        Ligurian Sea\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Po_Valley" </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Genoa_low&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Cyclogenesis">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Alpenrelief_01.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="937" data-file-width="1200" height="234" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/Alpenrelief_01.jpg/300px-Alpenrelief_01.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/Alpenrelief_01.jpg/450px-Alpenrelief_01.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/Alpenrelief_01.jpg/600px-Alpenrelief_01.jpg 2x" width="300"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Alpenrelief_01.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Relief map of Alpine region of Europe\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The northwestern Mediterranean and the Gulf of Genoa in particular, are not only a transition area for passing cyclones, but are frequently areas of cyclogenesis.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kreienkamp_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Kreienkamp-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Low pressure areas move into or are formed as a result of North Atlantic air entering the Mediterranean Sea between the Alps and the\n       <a href="/wiki/Massif_Central" title="Massif Central">\n        Massif Central\n       </a>\n       , via the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rhone_Valley" title="Rhone Valley">\n        Rhone Valley\n       </a>\n       , or via the\n       <a href="/wiki/Carcassonne" title="Carcassonne">\n        Carcassonne\n       </a>\n       gap between the\n       <a href="/wiki/Pyrenees" title="Pyrenees">\n        Pyrenees\n       </a>\n       and Massif Central. This cold and moist air enters the Mediterranean basin, and is deflected by the high mountains of northwest\n       <a href="/wiki/Corsica" title="Corsica">\n        Corsica\n       </a>\n       , which divert the air mass to the northeast, triggering cool and wet\n       <a href="/wiki/Libeccio" title="Libeccio">\n        Libeccio\n       </a>\n       winds in response into to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Ligurian_Sea" title="Ligurian Sea">\n        Ligurian Sea\n       </a>\n       , which in turn hit the western\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Apennines" title="Apennines">\n        Apennines\n       </a>\n       located in the immediate vicinity of the sea.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Several factors that have special relevance in the development of depressions south of the Alps are:\n      </p>\n      <ul>\n       <li>\n        The thermal contrast between land and sea.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Interaction between the polar front jet stream and the subtropical jet stream.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Effect of northerly flow over the Alps, enhancing cyclogenetic activity along the southern slopes.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Effect of the concave curvature of the southern slopes of the Alps, enhancing cyclonic formation.\n        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nrlmil_1-1">\n         <a href="#cite_note-nrlmil-1">\n          [1]\n         </a>\n        </sup>\n       </li>\n      </ul>\n      <p>\n       A complex interaction is established between the\n       <a href="/wiki/Orography" title="Orography">\n        orography\n       </a>\n       of Liguria and the contrast between the cold and humid air mass and the warmer water of the Ligurian Sea, the process ends with the formation of a low pressure area over the Ligurian Sea, just near the city of\n       <a href="/wiki/Genoa" title="Genoa">\n        Genoa\n       </a>\n       . Genoa low cyclogenesis can occur at any time of year, though usually situated further south during the summer.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nrlmil_1-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nrlmil-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Cyclogenesis is shifted to the east depending on the amount of cold air entering the Po Valley, and generally shifts to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Venice" title="Gulf of Venice">\n        Gulf of Venice\n       </a>\n       when little enters the valley.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nrlmil_1-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nrlmil-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Effects_on_climate">\n        Effects on climate\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Genoa_low&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Effects on climate">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       The depressions bear rain, often intense, on the Ligurian coast and hills of\n       <a href="/wiki/Tuscany" title="Tuscany">\n        Tuscany\n       </a>\n       , due to\n       <a href="/wiki/Orographic_lift" title="Orographic lift">\n        orographic lift\n       </a>\n       which affects the southern side of the Apennines. The area of low pressure is slow moving, and may follow a trajectory from west to east, then going on to affect the regions of the Adriatic, or move from the north-west to south-east down along the\n       <a href="/wiki/Tyrrhenian_Sea" title="Tyrrhenian Sea">\n        Tyrrhenian Sea\n       </a>\n       : in this last case, the structure will reach the same cyclonic area of formation of Tyrrhenian depressions, although not related to the latter.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Van_Bebber_cyclone_tracks">\n        Van Bebber cyclone tracks\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Genoa_low&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Van Bebber cyclone tracks">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Zugstrassen_der_barometrischen_Minima.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="777" data-file-width="824" height="283" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/70/Zugstrassen_der_barometrischen_Minima.jpg/300px-Zugstrassen_der_barometrischen_Minima.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/70/Zugstrassen_der_barometrischen_Minima.jpg/450px-Zugstrassen_der_barometrischen_Minima.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/70/Zugstrassen_der_barometrischen_Minima.jpg/600px-Zugstrassen_der_barometrischen_Minima.jpg 2x" width="300"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Zugstrassen_der_barometrischen_Minima.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Map of cyclone tracks over Europe, showing divergence of track V over the Ligurian sea and north Adriatic\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Most Genoa lows remain stationary or leave a residual trough to the south of the Alps.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nrlmil_1-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nrlmil-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Three principal tracks which they typically follow were identified by Wilhelm Jakob van Bebber who classified\n       <a href="/wiki/European_windstorm" title="European windstorm">\n        European windstorm\n       </a>\n       tracks ("Zyklonenbahnen" in German) in 1891.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kreienkamp_3-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Kreienkamp-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       To this date, track V of the latter group has remained in common use, unlike the large majority of van Bebber\'s tracks.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Kreienkamp_3-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Kreienkamp-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The V track is linked to flooding events in central and eastern Europe, low pressure areas (south of the Alps) can track across France into the Mediterranean Sea where they pick up additional moisture, or form, and then move into central and eastern or southern Europe.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       the tracks diverge from the Genoa low formation area along the following pathways.\n      </p>\n      <h4>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Vb_track">\n        Vb track\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Genoa_low&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Vb track">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h4>\n      <p>\n       A strong southwesterly flow in the upper atmosphere leads the lows to the northeast and northnortheast, ("Zugstrasse Vb" Van Bebber) towards the\n       <a href="/wiki/Vienna_Basin" title="Vienna Basin">\n        Vienna Basin\n       </a>\n       . The lows then glide over colder and denser air from the northwest and are lifted orographically by the\n       <a href="/wiki/Bohemian_Massif" title="Bohemian Massif">\n        Bohemian Massif\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Ore_Mountains" title="Ore Mountains">\n        Ore Mountains\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Sudetes" title="Sudetes">\n        Sudetes\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Beskids" title="Beskids">\n        Beskids\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Tatra_Mountains" title="Tatra Mountains">\n        Tatra Mountains\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mudelsee_6-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Mudelsee-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The warm and moist air masses cause prolonged and abundant precipitation during slow\n       <a href="/wiki/Meridional_flow" title="Meridional flow">\n        Meridional flow\n       </a>\n       over the upper catchments of both the southern and northern\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/European_Watershed" title="European Watershed">\n        European Watershed\n       </a>\n       . Flooding then progresses down their major rivers of central Europe.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       This \'Vb-track\' displays a high potential for large summer floods in Europe.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Donat_8-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Donat-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Although the link between large summer floods and the Vb track have also been described as having a significant but weak correlation.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mudelsee_6-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Mudelsee-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Examples of flooding events which follow this pattern are:\n      </p>\n      <ul>\n       <li>\n        <a href="/wiki/St._Mary_Magdalene%27s_flood" title="St. Mary Magdalene\'s flood">\n         St. Mary Magdalene\'s flood\n        </a>\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        <a href="/wiki/1997_Central_European_flood" title="1997 Central European flood">\n         1997 Central European flood\n        </a>\n        from the lows "Xolska" and "Zoe".\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        <a href="/wiki/2002_European_floods" title="2002 European floods">\n         2002 European floods\n        </a>\n        from the lows "Hanne" and "Ilse".\n        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">\n         <a href="#cite_note-9">\n          [9]\n         </a>\n        </sup>\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        <a href="/wiki/2005_European_floods" title="2005 European floods">\n         2005 European floods\n        </a>\n        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">\n         <a href="#cite_note-10">\n          [10]\n         </a>\n        </sup>\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        <a href="/wiki/2009_European_floods" title="2009 European floods">\n         2009 European floods\n        </a>\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        <a         </a>\n        </sup>\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        <a href="/wiki/2013_European_floods" title="2013 European floods">\n         2013 European floods\n        </a>\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        <a href="/wiki/2014_Southeast_Europe_floods" title="2014 Southeast Europe floods">\n         2014 Southeast Europe floods\n        </a>\n        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">\n         <a href="#cite_note-12">\n          [12]\n         </a>\n        </sup>\n       </li>\n      </ul>\n      <h4>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Vc_track">\n        Vc track\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a </span>\n       </span>\n      </h4>\n      <p>\n       The Vc track draws the lows across the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Panonian_plain" title="Panonian plain">\n        Panonian plain\n       </a>\n       towards the\n       <a href="/wiki/Carpathian_Mountains" title="Carpathian Mountains">\n        Carpathian Mountains\n       </a>\n       and on towards western\n       <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">\n        Ukraine\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Moldova" title="Moldova">\n        Moldova\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <h4>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Vd_track">\n        Vd track\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Genoa_low&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Vd track">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h4>\n      <p>\n       If there is a strong anticyclone over the\n       <a href="/wiki/Balkans" title="Balkans">\n        Balkans\n       </a>\n       , Turkey and the\n       <a href="/wiki/Black_Sea" title="Black Sea">\n        Black Sea\n       </a>\n       , the usual track of the low is southeasterly, skirting the northern coast of the Mediterranean Sea. It is this track that moves the low across the Tyrrhenian Sea. Even after the primary low has moved out of the Tyrrhenian Sea-central Mediterranean area, if a residual trough remains south of the Alps, as is often the case, new centers can develop and occasionally move southeastward along the west coast of Italy.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nrlmil_1-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nrlmil-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       It is also common that a Genoa Low that has moved to the southwest will stall and become stationary, just to the west of the foot of the Italian boot, and this often will be associated with new low centers developing to the east over the\n       <a href="/wiki/Ionian_Sea" title="Ionian Sea">\n        Ionian Sea\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nrlmil_1-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nrlmil-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In this case, gale force\n       <a href="/wiki/Bora_(wind)" title="Bora (wind)">\n        Bora\n       </a>\n       are typically generated by the time the depression moves into the Ionian Sea.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-13">\n
