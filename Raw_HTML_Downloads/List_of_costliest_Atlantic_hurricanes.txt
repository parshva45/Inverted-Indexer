From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia\n    </div>\n    <div id="contentSub">\n    </div>\n    <div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">\n     Jump to:\n     <a href="#mw-head">\n      navigation\n <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_katrina_damage_gulfport_mississippi.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n <b>\n        list of costliest Atlantic hurricanes\n       </b>\n       .\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        Hurricanes\n       </a>\n       , as defined by the\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Oceanic_and_Atmospheric_Administration" title="National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration">\n        National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration\n       </a>\n       (NOAA), are\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        tropical cyclones\n       </a>\n       \xe2\x80\x94a warm-core, non-frontal synoptic-scale cyclone, originating over tropical or subtropical waters with organized deep convection and a closed surface wind circulation about a well-defined center\xe2\x80\x94in the Atlantic, Caribbean Sea, Gulf of Mexico, or eastern Pacific, in which the maximum 1-minute sustained surface wind exceeds 64\n       <a href="/wiki/Knot_(unit)" title="Knot (unit)">\n        knots\n       </a>\n       (74\n       <a href="/wiki/Miles_per_hour" title="Miles per hour">\n        mph\n       </a>\n       ; 119\n       <a href="/wiki/Kilometres_per_hour" title="Kilometres per hour">\n        km/h\n       </a>\n       ) or greater. The hurricanes on the list below depict the severity of the damage the system has caused. Typically, if a hurricane has caused significant damage to a particular location, its name is\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricane names">\n        retired\n       </a>\n       so that it will not be re-used as a name for any future hurricanes. The costliest tropical cyclone ever recorded was\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Harvey" title="Hurricane Harvey">\n        Hurricane Harvey\n       </a>\n       , which caused catastrophic flooding in southeastern Texas, in August\xc2\xa02017, causing $198.6 billion (2017\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">\n        USD\n       </a>\n       ) in property damage. The costliest season on record was\n       <a href="/wiki/2017_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2017 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        2017\n       </a>\n       , with damage estimated in excess of $300\xc2\xa0billion.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       A majority of the costliest Atlantic hurricanes in recorded history have peaked as major hurricanes. However, weaker tropical cyclones can still cause widespread damage. Both tropical storms\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_Storm_Allison" title="Tropical Storm Allison">\n        Allison\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/2001_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2001 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        2001\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_Storm_Lee_(2011)" title="Tropical Storm Lee (2011)">\n        Lee\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/2011_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2011 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        2011\n       </a>\n       have caused over a billion in damage; the former of which accounted for a higher damage total. Due to their excessive damage, the\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_naming" title="Tropical cyclone naming">\n        names\n       </a>\n       of tropical cyclones accruing at least $1\xc2\xa0billion in damage are often\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricane names">\n        retired\n       </a>\n       by the\n       <a href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organization" title="World Meteorological Organization">\n        World Meteorological Organization\n       </a>\n       . However, this is not always the case.\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Juan_(1985)" title="Hurricane Juan (1985)">\n        Hurricane Juan\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/1985_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1985 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1985\n       </a>\n       was the first hurricane to cause at least a billion in damage and not be retired; its name was retired on a\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Juan" title="Hurricane Juan">\n        later usage\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/2003_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2003 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        2003\n       </a>\n       that did not cause over a billion in damage. Since Juan, seven tropical cyclones that caused at least a billion in damage were not retired, including\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Emily_(2005)" title="Hurricane Emily (2005)">\n        Hurricane Emily\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/2005_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2005 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        2005\n       </a>\n       , and the most recent of which being\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Isaac_(2012)" title="Hurricane Isaac (2012)">\n        Hurricane Isaac\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/2012_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2012 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        2012\n       </a>\n       . All other 47\xc2\xa0Atlantic hurricanes to have caused as much damage have had their names retired, excluding recent hurricanes\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Harvey" title="Hurricane Harvey">\n        Harvey\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Irma" title="Hurricane Irma">\n        Irma\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Maria" title="Hurricane Maria">\n        Maria\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Ophelia_(2017)" title="Hurricane Ophelia (2017)">\n        Ophelia\n       </a>\n       of the\n       <a href="/wiki/2017_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2017 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        2017 season\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The first hurricane to cause at least $1\xc2\xa0billion in damage was\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Betsy" title="Hurricane Betsy">\n        Hurricane Betsy\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/1965_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1965 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1965\n       </a>\n       , which caused much of its damage in southeastern\n       <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">\n        Louisiana\n       </a>\n       . Between two and four years later, Hurricanes\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Beulah" title="Hurricane Beulah">\n        Beulah\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Camille" title="Hurricane Camille">\n        Camille\n       </a>\n       both caused at least $1\xc2\xa0billion in damage. After the 1960s, each decade saw an increase in tropical cyclones causing at least a billion in damage over the last, due to increasing urban development and population. In the 1970s, four hurricanes caused at least a billion in damage; the costliest of which was\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Frederic" title="Hurricane Frederic">\n        Hurricane Frederic\n       </a>\n       , which caused $2.3\xc2\xa0billion in damage, particularly in Mississippi and Alabama. The following decade featured seven hurricanes causing at least a billion in damage. In the\n       <a href="/wiki/1989_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1989 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1989 season\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hugo" title="Hurricane Hugo">\n        Hurricane Hugo\n       </a>\n       was the first storm to hit $10 billion U.S. dollars in cost. In the 1990s, nine tropical cyclones accrued at least a billion in damage.\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Andrew" title="Hurricane Andrew">\n        Hurricane Andrew\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/1992_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1992 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1992\n       </a>\n       greatly exceeded the damage figure of any preceding tropical cyclone after causing $26.5\xc2\xa0billion in damage, mostly in\n       <a href="/wiki/South_Florida" title="South Florida">\n        South Florida\n       </a>\n       . Seventeen tropical cyclones in the 2000s caused at least $1\xc2\xa0billion in damage. The\n       <a href="/wiki/2005_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2005 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        2005\n       </a>\n       season had six billion-dollar hurricanes, the most of any season on record.\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Ivan" title="Hurricane Ivan">\n        Hurricane Ivan\n       </a>\n       caused at least $1\xc2\xa0billion in damage in four separate countries, and according to estimates,\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Irma" title="Hurricane Irma">\n        Hurricane Irma\n       </a>\n       caused at least $1\xc2\xa0billion in damage in five separate countries. Thus far in the 2010s, eleven storms have caused at least $1\xc2\xa0billion in damage, with the costliest so far being\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Harvey" title="Hurricane Harvey">\n        Hurricane Harvey\n       </a>\n       which caused $198.6\xc2\xa0billion in damage.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Overall_costliest">\n        Overall costliest\n
