</a>\n    </div>\n    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">\n     <div class="mw-parser-output">\n      <p>\n       An\n       <b>\n        ExPRESS logistics carrier\n       </b>\n       (\n       <b>\n        ELC\n       </b>\n       ) is an\n       <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station#Unpressurised_elements" title="International Space Station">\n        unpressurized attached payload\n       </a>\n       platform for the\n       <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">\n        International Space Station\n       </a>\n       (ISS) that provides mechanical mounting surfaces, electrical power, and command and data handling services for\n       <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station#Unpressurised_elements" title="International Space Station">\n        Orbital Replacement Units\n       </a>\n       (ORUs) as well as science experiments on the ISS. ("ExPRESS" stands for\n       <i>\n        Expedite the Processing of Experiments to the Space Station\n       </i>\n       .) The ELCs were developed primarily at the\n       <a href="/wiki/Goddard_Space_Flight_Center" title="Goddard Space Flight Center">\n        Goddard Space Flight Center\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/Greenbelt,_Maryland" title="Greenbelt, Maryland">\n        Greenbelt, Maryland\n       </a>\n       , with support from\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lyndon_B._Johnson_Space_Center" title="Lyndon B. Johnson Space Center">\n        JSC\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Kennedy_Space_Center" title="Kennedy Space Center">\n        KSC\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Marshall_Space_Flight_Center" title="Marshall Space Flight Center">\n        MSFC\n       </a>\n       . ELC was formerly called "Express Pallet" and is the unpressurized counterpart to the pressurized ExPRESS Rack. An ELC provides scientists with a platform and infrastructure to deploy experiments in the vacuum of space without requiring a separate dedicated\n       <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">\n        Earth\n       </a>\n       -orbiting\n       <a href="/wiki/Satellite" title="Satellite">\n        satellite\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       ELCs interface directly with the ISS integrated truss common attach system (CAS).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SSP52055_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SSP52055-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The P3 Truss has two such attach points called Unpressurised Cargo Carrier Attachment System (UCCAS) mechanisms, one facing zenith (space facing) called UCCAS-1, the other facing nadir (earth facing) called UCCAS-2. Whereas the S3 Truss has four similar locations called Payload Attachment System (PAS) mechanisms, two facing Zenith (PAS-1 and PAS-2), and two facing Nadir (PAS-3 and PAS-4).\n      </p>\n      <p>\n     </div>\n      </div>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:402222main_Techs_working_on_ELC_1019.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="761" data-file-width="1019" height="164" class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:402222main_Techs_working_on_ELC_1019.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         ELC during final fabrication at\n         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GSFC" title="GSFC">\n          GSFC\n         </a>\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The ELC is an un-pressurized attached payload project for the International Space Station (ISS) that provides mechanical mounting surfaces, electrical power, and command and data handling services for science experiments on the ISS. ("ExPRESS" stands for \xe2\x80\x9cexpedite the processing of experiments to the Space Station\xe2\x80\x9d.) The ELCs have a deck size of about 14 feet by 16 feet and spans the width of the space shuttle\xe2\x80\x99s payload bay. Each one is capable of providing scientists with a platform and infrastructure to deploy experiments in the vacuum of space without requiring a separate dedicated Earth-orbiting satellite. Each carrier is capable of carrying 9,800\xc2\xa0lbs. to orbit and will also serve as parking fixtures for spare ISS hardware (\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orbital_Replacement_Unit" title="Orbital Replacement Unit">\n        ORUs\n       </a>\n       ) which can be retrieved when needed.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Experiments are mounted on ExPRESS payload adapters (ExPA) which are about the same size as the FRAMs that hold ORUs.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span id="Electrical_subsystem_ExPRESS_carrier_avionics_(ExPCA)">\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Electrical_subsystem_ExPRESS_carrier_avionics_.28ExPCA.29">\n        Electrical subsystem ExPRESS carrier avionics (ExPCA)\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=ExPRESS_Logistics_Carrier&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Electrical subsystem ExPRESS carrier avionics (ExPCA)">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Within the electrical subsystem of the ELC, the ExPRESS carrier avionics (ExPCA) provides electrical power distribution to experiments, and data interfaces to the ISS. Within the ExPCA, the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Freescale_ColdFire" title="Freescale ColdFire">\n        ColdFire\n       </a>\n       -based flight computer, software, and related electronics comprise its "flight controller unit" (FCU). The FCU runs the free\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Open-source" title="Open-source">\n        open-source\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Real-time_operating_system" title="Real-time operating system">\n        real-time operating system\n       </a>\n       (RTOS)\n       <a href="/wiki/RTEMS" title="RTEMS">\n        RTEMS\n       </a>\n       and provides the computing and communication resources as an ELC Command and Data Handling (C&amp;DH) system with the following major goals:\n      </p>\n      <ul>\n       <li>\n        Provide a low-rate data link (LRDL) interface to ISS to accept commands for the ELC and the resident experiments. The ExPCA is implemented as a\n        <a href="/wiki/MIL-STD-1553#The_Remote_Terminals" title="MIL-STD-1553">\n         remote terminal\n        </a>\n        (RT) on the\n        <a href="/wiki/MIL-STD-1553" title="MIL-STD-1553">\n         MIL-STD-1553\n        </a>\n        "ISS local bus." This interface also returns housekeeping\n        <a href="/wiki/Telemetry" title="Telemetry">\n         telemetry\n        </a>\n        from the ExPCA and resident experiments to the ISS.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Provide an LRDL from the ExPCA to the experiments resident on the ELC to forward commands from the ISS to the experiments and to receive telemetry from the experiments for transmission to the ISS. This is another MIL-STD-1553 interface, with the ExPCA acting as the\n        <a href="/wiki/MIL-STD-1553#The_Bus_Controller" title="MIL-STD-1553">\n         Bus Controller\n        </a>\n        (BC).\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Provide a high-rate data link (HRDL) between the ELC and the ISS. This interface is implemented as a fiber optical data bus with a capacity of up to 95.0 Megabits per second (Mbit/s). The primary function of this interface is the return on high-volume experiment Science data from the resident experiments to the ISS.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Provide an\n        <a href="/wiki/Ethernet" title="Ethernet">\n         Ethernet\n        </a>\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Local_Area_Network" title="Local Area Network">\n         Local Area Network\n        </a>\n        (LAN) between the ELC and the resident experiments up to 6.0 Mbit/s per experiment. The primary function of this interface is the return of science experiment data from to the ISS, relayed through the HRDL.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Support six analog input channels at each ExPA (ExPRESS payload adapter) location.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Support six\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Discrete_device" title="Discrete device">\n         discrete\n        </a>\n        command\n        <a href="/wiki/Channel_(programming)" title="Channel (programming)">\n         channels\n        </a>\n        at each ExPA location.\n       </li>\n      </ul>\n      <p>\n       Manifested on ELC-2 was the first ELC-based payload,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MISSE#MISSE-7" title="MISSE">\n        Materials for ISS Experiment (MISSE-7)\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       mounted on an ExPA.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="ELC_launch_schedule">\n        ELC launch schedule\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=ExPRESS_Logistics_Carrier&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: ELC launch schedule">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       ELC-1 and ELC-2 were transported to the International Space Station by\n       <span class="nowrap">\n        <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle" title="Space Shuttle">\n         Space Shuttle\n        </a>\n       </span>\n       <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Atlantis" title="Space Shuttle Atlantis">\n        <i>\n         Atlantis\n        </i>\n       </a>\n       on mission\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-129" title="STS-129">\n        STS-129\n       </a>\n       in November 2009. ELC-4 launched on mission\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-133" title="STS-133">\n        STS-133\n        <i>\n         Discovery\n        </i>\n       </a>\n       on 24 February 2011 and was installed on the station on 27 February. ELC-3 launched on mission\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-134" title="STS-134">\n        STS-134\n        <i>\n         Endeavour\n        </i>\n       </a>\n       on 16 May 2011 and was installed on the station on 18 May.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <a href="/wiki/Alpha_Magnetic_Spectrometer" title="Alpha Magnetic Spectrometer">\n        Alpha Magnetic Spectrometer\n       </a>\n
