Chiao was also the first astronaut to vote in a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Presidential_election" title="U.S. Presidential election">\n        U.S. Presidential election\n       </a>\n       from space.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_events">\n        Notable events\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_10&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Notable events">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <div class="PopUpMediaTransform" id="mwe_player_0" style="width:220px;" videopayload=\'&lt;div class="mediaContainer" style="width:352px"&gt;&lt;video id="mwe_player_1" poster="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/Eclipse_from_ISS.ogv/352px--Eclipse_from_ISS.ogv.jpg" controls="" preload="none" autoplay="" style="width:352px;height:240px" class="kskin" data-durationhint="30.933333333333" data-startoffset="0" data-mwtitle="Eclipse_from_ISS.ogv" data-mwprovider="wikimediacommons"&gt;&lt;source src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/51/Eclipse_from_ISS.ogv" type="video/ogg; During preflight testing, an explosive bolt was accidentally activated on the\n       <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-5" title="Soyuz TMA-5">\n        Soyuz TMA-5\n       </a>\n       spacecraft. The resulting damage caused was repaired and the mission launched from\n       <a href="/wiki/Baikonur_Cosmodrome" title="Baikonur Cosmodrome">\n        Baikonur Cosmodrome\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">\n        Kazakhstan\n       </a>\n       on 14 October 2004.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       During the Expedition 10 mission, two\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Progress_spacecraft" title="Progress spacecraft">\n        Progress supply spacecraft\n       </a>\n       docked with the International Space Station. The first (Progress 51) docked on 25 December 2004 followed by the second (Progress 52) on 28 February 2005.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <a href="/wiki/European_Space_Agency" title="European Space Agency">\n        European Space Agency\n       </a>\n       Astronaut\n       <a href="/wiki/Roberto_Vittori" title="Roberto Vittori">\n        Roberto Vittori\n       </a>\n       of Italy launched to the Station with the\n       <a href="/wiki/Expedition_11" title="Expedition 11">\n        Expedition 11\n       </a>\n       crew and spent eight days onboard performing scientific experiments. Vittori returned to Earth with the Expedition 10 crew. He was aboard under a contract between ESA and the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_Federal_Space_Agency" title="Russian Federal Space Agency">\n        Russian Federal Space Agency\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The re-entry of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-5" title="Soyuz TMA-5">\n        Soyuz TMA-5\n       </a>\n       spacecraft was perfect, returning the astronauts to\n       <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">\n        Earth\n       </a>\n       53 miles northeast of the town of Arkalyk after 192 days, 19 hours and 2 minutes in space. The recovery team reached the capsule in minutes.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Crew_2">\n        Crew\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_10&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Crew">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Exp_10_crew_in_PIRS.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2165" data-file-width="2000" height="238" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Exp_10_crew_in_PIRS.jpg/220px-Exp_10_crew_in_PIRS.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Exp_10_crew_in_PIRS.jpg/330px-Exp_10_crew_in_PIRS.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Exp_10_crew_in_PIRS.jpg/440px-Exp_10_crew_in_PIRS.jpg 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Exp_10_crew_in_PIRS.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Salizhan Sharipov (left) and Leroy Chiao (right) work with their Russian Orlan spacesuits in the Pirs Docking Compartment of the International Space Station (ISS). (NASA)\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Chiao is a veteran of three\n       <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle" title="Space Shuttle">\n        Space Shuttle\n       </a>\n       flights, and spent 13 hours on two spacewalks in 2000 as part of a construction mission at the Station. Sharipov has logged over 211 hours in space. He served as a mission specialist on the Shuttle during the eighth\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shuttle-Mir" title="Shuttle-Mir">\n        Shuttle-Mir\n       </a>\n       docking mission in 1998.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Spacewalks">\n        Spacewalks\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_10&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Spacewalks">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       The Expedition 10 crew completed two\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extra-vehicular_activity" title="Extra-vehicular activity">\n        spacewalks\n       </a>\n       , including experiment installation and tasks to prepare the Station for the arrival of the new European\n       <a href="/wiki/Automated_Transfer_Vehicle" title="Automated Transfer Vehicle">\n        Automated Transfer Vehicle\n       </a>\n       in 2006. Instead, the Automated Transfer Vehicle came in 2008.
