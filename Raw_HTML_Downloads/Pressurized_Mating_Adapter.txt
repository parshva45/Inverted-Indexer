Shuttle Endeavour">\n          Endeavour\n         </a>\n         via PMA-2, docking with Zarya via PMA-1 (NASA)\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       All the PMAs on the ISS are identical\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       but used slightly differently, and all three perform the same basic function of connecting a Common Berthing Mechanism port of an ISS module to an APAS-95 docking port of another module or visiting spacecraft.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       For this the PMAs carry a passive CBM port and a passive APAS port. They are pressurized and heated from the inside, and through docking rings as well as external connections allow for power and data communications transfer.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="PMA-1">\n        PMA-1\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pressurized_Mating_Adapter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: PMA-1">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       This was one of the first components of the International Space Station and joins the Russian side of the station with the US side. On\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-88" title="STS-88">\n        STS-88\n       </a>\n       the crew used the shuttle\'s\n       <a href="/wiki/Canadarm" title="Canadarm">\n        robotic arm\n       </a>\n       to attach the\n       <a href="/wiki/Zarya" title="Zarya">\n        Zarya\n       </a>\n       control module to PMA-1, which was already connected to the aft berthing port of\n       <i>\n        Unity\n       </i>\n       . PMA-1 now permanently connects these first two station components.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="PMA-2">\n        PMA-2\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pressurized_Mating_Adapter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: PMA-2">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       PMA-2 is currently mounted on the forward port of the\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Harmony_(ISS_module)" title="Harmony (ISS module)">\n         Harmony\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       connecting node and was used when\n       <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle" title="Space Shuttle">\n        Space Shuttle\n       </a>\n       orbiters docked at the station. It is the only PMA that has been outfitted with\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Station-to-Shuttle_Power_Transfer_System" title="Station-to-Shuttle Power Transfer System">\n        Station-to-Shuttle Power Transfer System\n       </a>\n       (SSPTS) hardware, which allowed shuttles to stay docked longer to the space station.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Due to\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ISS_assembly_sequence" title="ISS assembly sequence">\n        space station assembly\n       </a>\n       PMA-2 has been moved several times. It was originally connected to the forward hatch of\n       <a href="/wiki/Unity_(ISS_module)" title="Unity (ISS module)">\n        <i>\n         Unity\n        </i>\n       </a>\n       , but when\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-98" title="STS-98">\n        STS-98\n       </a>\n       delivered the\n       <i>\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Destiny_Laboratory_Module" title="Destiny Laboratory Module">\n         Destiny\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       in February 2001,\n       <i>\n        Destiny\n       </i>\n       was berthed to that hatch while PMA-2 was moved to the forward hatch of the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Z1_truss" title="Z1 truss">\n        Z1 truss\n       </a>\n       . It was finally moved to the forward hatch of "Destiny".\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-STS-98_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-STS-98-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       (The removal of PMA-2 from\n       <i>\n        Unity\n       </i>\n       was the first time the\n       <a href="/wiki/Common_Berthing_Mechanism" title="Common Berthing Mechanism">\n        Common Berthing Mechanism\n       </a>\n       (CBM) was used to disconnect two ISS components.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       ) After\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-120" title="STS-120">\n        STS-120\n       </a>\n       had delivered\n       <i>\n        Harmony\n       </i>\n       at the station in October 2007,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadarm2" title="Canadarm2">\n        Canadarm2\n       </a>\n       relocated PMA-2 to its final location at the forward hatch of\n       <i>\n        Harmony\n       </i>\n       on November 12, 2007. Two days later, the combined package of\n       <i>\n        Harmony\n       </i>\n       and PMA-2 was moved to its final location, the forward hatch of\n       <i>\n        Destiny\n       </i>\n       . PMA-2 will stay berthed at the forward port of\n       <i>\n        Harmony\n       </i>\n       for the remaining duration of the ISS.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       When a shuttle docked with the station, its "final approach [was] at a relative velocity of one-tenth of a foot per second. [As it made] contact with Pressurized Mating Adapter 2 [latches] automatically attached the two spacecraft together. Once relative motion between the spacecraft stopped, [a shuttle astronaut retracted] the docking ring on [the shuttle\'s] mechanism, closing latches to firmly secure the shuttle to the station."\n -2 was launched on\n       <a href="/wiki/SpaceX_CRS-9" title="SpaceX CRS-9">\n        SpaceX CRS-9\n       </a>\n       on 18 July 2016.\n       <sup class="reference"     </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="PMA-3">\n        PMA-3\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pressurized_Mating_Adapter&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: PMA-3">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span     </a>\n       (bottom, or Earth-facing) hatch of\n       <i>\n        Unity\n       </i>\n       . One and a half months later, when\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-97" title="STS-97">\n        STS-97\n       </a>\n       delivered the\n       <a href="/wiki/Integrated_Truss_Structure#P6.2C_S6_trusses" moved PMA-2 from\n       <i>\n        Unity\n       </i>\n       to\n       <i>\n        Destiny\n       </i>\n       via Z1,\n       <i>\n        Atlantis\n       </i>\n       was docked at PMA-3.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-STS-98_5-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-STS-98-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       PMA-3 was not used for Shuttle dockings for the rest of
