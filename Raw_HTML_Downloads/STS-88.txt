     </div>\n         Illustration of the International Space Station after STS-88.\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Node 1, named\n       <i>\n        Unity\n       </i>\n       , was the first space station hardware delivered by the\n       <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle" title="Space Shuttle">\n        space shuttle\n       </a>\n       . It has two\n       <a href="/wiki/Pressurized_Mating_Adapter" title="Pressurized Mating Adapter">\n        Pressurized Mating Adapters\n       </a>\n       (PMA), one attached to either end. One PMA is permanently mated to\n       <i>\n        Zarya\n       </i>\n       , and the other is used for orbiter dockings and crew access to the station.\n       <i>\n        Unity\n       </i>\n       also contains an International Standard Payload Rack used to support on-orbit activities, which was activated after the fifth Shuttle/Station assembly flight.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-launch_1-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-launch-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       To begin the assembly sequence, the crew conducted a series of rendezvous maneuvers similar to those conducted on other Shuttle missions to reach the orbiting FGB. On the way, Currie used the Shuttle\'s robot arm to place Node 1 atop the Orbiter Docking System.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FD3Highlights_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-FD3Highlights-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SR6_6-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SR6-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Cabana completed the rendezvous by flying\n       <i>\n        Endeavour\n       </i>\n       to within 10 metres (33\xc2\xa0ft) of the FGB, allowing Currie to capture the FGB with the robot arm and place it on the Node\'s Pressurized Mating Adapter.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SR9_7-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SR9-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Once the two elements were docked, Ross and Newman conducted two scheduled spacewalks to connect power and data cables between the Node, PMAs and the FGB.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FD5Highlights_8-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-FD5Highlights-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The day following the spacewalks,\n       <i>\n        Endeavour\n       </i>\n       undocked from the two components, completing the first Space Station assembly mission.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SR10_9-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SR10-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SR13_10-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SR13-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Endeavour\'s astronauts toured the new International Space Station on 11 December 1998, entering the Unity and Zarya modules for the first time, and establishing an S-band communications system that enables U.S. flight controllers to monitor the outpost\'s systems.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FD8Highlights_11-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-FD8Highlights-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SR17_12-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SR17-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Reflecting the international cooperation involved in building the largest space complex in history, Commander\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Robert_Cabana" title="Robert Cabana">\n        Robert Cabana\n       </a>\n       and Russian Cosmonaut\n       <a href="/wiki/Sergei_Krikalev" title="Sergei Krikalev">\n        Sergei Krikalev\n       </a>\n       opened the hatch to the U.S.-built Unity connecting module and floated into the new station together.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SR17_12-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SR17-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The rest of the crew followed and began turning on lights and unstowing gear in the roomy hub to which other modules would be connected in the future. Each passageway within Unity was marked by a sign leading the way into tunnels to which new modules would later be connected.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SR17_12-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SR17-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       About an hour later,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Robert_Cabana" title="Robert Cabana">\n        Robert Cabana\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Sergei_Krikalev" title="Sergei Krikalev">\n        Sergei Krikalev\n       </a>\n       opened the hatch to the Russian-built\n       <a href="/wiki/Zarya" title="Zarya">\n        Zarya\n       </a>\n       control module, which was the nerve center for the station in its embryonic stage. Joined by Pilot\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frederick_Sturckow" title="Frederick Sturckow">\n        Frederick Sturckow\n       </a>\n       and Mission Specialists\n       <a href="/wiki/Jerry_L._Ross" title="Jerry L. Ross">\n        Jerry Ross\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/James_H._Newman" title="James H. Newman">\n        James H. Newman\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nancy_Currie" title="Nancy Currie">\n        Nancy Currie\n       </a>\n       , Cabana and Krikalev hailed the historic entrance into the International Space Station and said the hatch opening signified the start of a new era in space exploration.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SR17_12-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SR17-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" right to work in Unity, completing the assembly of an early S-band communications system that allows flight controllers in\n       <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">\n        Houston\n       </a>\n       to send commands to Unity\'s systems and to keep tabs on the health of the station with a more extensive communications capability than exists through Russian ground stations.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SR17_12-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SR17-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The astronauts also conducted a successful test of the videoconferencing capability of the early communications system, which was used by the first crew to permanently occupy the station in November 2000 (\n       <a href="/wiki/Expedition_1" title="Expedition 1">\n        Expedition 1\n       </a>\n       ). Newman downlinked greetings to controllers in the station flight control room in Houston and to astronaut\n       <a href="/wiki/William_Shepherd" title="William Shepherd">\n        Bill Shepherd\n       </a>\n       , who commanded the first crew and lived aboard the station with Krikalev and Cosmonaut\n       <a href="/wiki/Yuri_Gidzenko" title="Yuri Gidzenko">\n        Yuri Gidzenko\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">\n        [\n        <i>\n         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">\n          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2011)">\n           citation needed\n          </span>\n         </a>\n        </i>\n        ]\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Krikalev and Currie replaced a faulty unit in Zarya which controlled the discharging of stored energy from one of the module\'s six batteries. The battery had not been working properly in its automatic configuration, but the new unit was functioning normally shortly after it was installed.\n       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">\n        [\n        <i>\n         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">\n          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2011)">\n           citation needed\n          </span>\n         </a>\n        </i>\n        ]\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The astronauts also unstowed hardware and logistical supplies stored behind panels in Zarya, relocating the items for use by the Shuttle crew that was to visit the station in May 1999 and by Shepherd\'s expedition crew. The astronauts also completed their initial outfitting of the station.\n       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">\n        [\n        <i>\n         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">\n          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2011)">\n           citation needed\n          </span>\n         </a>\n        </i>\n        ]\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The hatches to Zarya and\n       <a href="/wiki/Unity_(ISS_module)" title="Unity (ISS module)">\n        Unity\n       </a>\n       were closed before\n       <i>\n        Endeavour\n       </i>\n       undocked from the new station, leaving the new complex to orbit the Earth unpiloted.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SR18_13-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SR18-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n The crew prepared for the mission\xe2\x80\x99s third and final spacewalk on Flight Day 10.\n       <i>\n        Endeavour\n       </i>\n       \xe2\x80\x99s crew also conducted interviews with\n       <a href="/wiki/CNN" title="CNN">\n        CNN\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/CBS_News" title="CBS News">\n        CBS News\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FD9Highlights_23-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-FD9Highlights-23">\n         [23]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span id="12_December_(Flight_Day_10,_EVA_3)">\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="12_December_.28Flight_Day_10.2C_EVA_3.29">\n        12 December (Flight Day 10, EVA 3)\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=STS-88&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: 12 December (Flight Day 10, EVA 3)">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       At 3:33\xc2\xa0pm\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eastern_Standard_Time_Zone" title="Eastern Standard Time Zone">\n        EST\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Astronaut" title="Astronaut">\n        astronauts\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Jerry_L._Ross" title="Jerry L. Ross">\n        Jerry Ross\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/James_H._Newman" title="James H. Newman">\n        Jim Newman\n       </a>\n       commenced on a 6-hour, 59 minute\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extra-vehicular_activity" title="Extra-vehicular activity">\n        EVA\n       </a>\n       to finish the installation of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Unity_(ISS_module)" title="Unity (ISS module)">\n        <i>\n         Unity Node\n        </i>\n       </a>\n       to\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Zarya" title="Zarya">\n         Zarya\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       . The two stowed tools on the side of\n       <i>\n        Unity\n       </i>\n       to prepare for the spacewalks on\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-96" title="STS-96">\n        STS-96\n       </a>\n       , the next mission to the\n       <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">\n        ISS\n       </a>\n       . Ross also freed another antenna on\n       <i>\n        Zarya\n       </i>\n       , similar to the one Newman freed on EVA-2. The spacewalkers also tested the redesigned\n       <a href="/wiki/Simplified_Aid_For_EVA_Rescue" title="Simplified Aid For EVA Rescue">\n        SAFER\n       </a>\n       jet packs, to be used in the event an astronaut became separated from the spacecraft during a spacewalk. After the EVA, Pilot\n       <a href="/wiki/Frederick_W._Sturckow" title="Frederick W. Sturckow">\n        Rick Sturckow\n       </a>\n       depressurized the vestibule between\n       <i>\n        Endeavour\n       </i>\n       and PMA-2 to prepare for the undocking of the two spacecraft on Flight Day 11.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FD10Highlights_24-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-FD10Highlights-24">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span id="13_December_(Flight_Day_11,_Undocking)">\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="13_December_.28Flight_Day_11.2C_Undocking.29">\n        13 December (Flight Day 11, Undocking)\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=STS-88&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: 13 December (Flight Day 11, Undocking)">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       On Flight Day 11,\n       <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle" title="Space Shuttle">\n        Space Shuttle\n       </a>\n       <i>\n        Endeavour\n       </i>\n       undocked from the\n       <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">\n        International Space Station\n       </a>\n       . At 3:25\xc2\xa0pm\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eastern_Standard_Time_Zone" title="Eastern Standard Time Zone">\n        EST\n       </a>\n       , Pilot\n       <a href="/wiki/Frederick_W._Sturckow" title="Frederick W. Sturckow">\n        Rick Sturckow\n       </a>\n       backed\n       <i>\n        Endeavour\n       </i>\n       450 feet (140\xc2\xa0m) away from the station and started a nose-forward fly-around of the station, so that\n       <i>\n        Endeavour\n       </i>\n       \xe2\x80\x99s crew could take pictures of the space station. At 4:49\xc2\xa0pm\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eastern_Standard_Time_Zone" title="Eastern Standard Time Zone">\n        EST\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Frederick_W._Sturckow" title="Frederick W. Sturckow">\n        Sturckow\n       </a>\n       performed a final separation burn. Later, the crew deployed SAC-A, a small satellite for the Argentinean National Commission of Space Activities.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FD11Highlights_25-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-FD11Highlights-25">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span id="14_December_(Flight_Day_12)">\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="14_December_.28Flight_Day_12.29">\n        14 December (Flight Day 12)\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=STS-88&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: 14 December (Flight Day 12)">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       On the last full day on orbit, Endeavour\xe2\x80\x99s crew deployed a small\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Air_Force" title="United States Air Force">\n        Air Force\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Satellite" title="Satellite">\n        satellite\n       </a>\n       called\n       <a href="/wiki/MightySat-1" title="MightySat-1">\n        MightySat-1\n       </a>\n       . The crew tested\n       <i>\n        Endeavour\n       </i>\n       \xe2\x80\x99s shuttle\'s aerosurfaces and steering jets to be used on landing day and stowed equipment.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FD12Highlights_26-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-FD12Highlights-26">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span id="15_December_(Flight_Day_13,_Landing)">\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="15_December_.28Flight_Day_13.2C_Landing.29">\n        15 December (Flight Day 13, Landing)\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=STS-88&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: 15 December (Flight Day 13, Landing)">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Flight Day 13 was landing day for\n       <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle" title="Space Shuttle">\n        Space Shuttle\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Endeavour" title="Space Shuttle Endeavour">\n        Endeavour\n       </a>\n       and its crew of six. At 7:07\xc2\xa0pm\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eastern_Standard_Time_Zone" title="Eastern Standard Time Zone">\n        EST\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <i>\n        Endeavour\n       </i>\n       \xe2\x80\x99s payload bay doors were closed for entry.\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flight_Director" title="Flight Director">\n        Flight Director\n       </a>\n       John Shannon gave the go for\n       <i>\n        Endeavour\n       </i>\n       \xe2\x80\x99s crew to fire the\n       <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Orbital_Maneuvering_System" title="Space Shuttle Orbital Maneuvering System">\n        orbital maneuvering system engines\n       </a>\n       for the deorbit burn at 9:46\xc2\xa0pm\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eastern_Standard_Time_Zone" title="Eastern Standard Time Zone">\n        EST\n       </a>\n       so that\n       <i>\n        Endeavour\n       </i>\n       could slow down to enter the\n       <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">\n        Earth\n       </a>\n       \xe2\x80\x99s atmosphere. At 10:54\xc2\xa0pm\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eastern_Standard_Time_Zone" title="Eastern Standard Time Zone">\n        EST\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <i>\n        Endeavour\n       </i>\n       and crew landed on the\n       <a href="/wiki/Kennedy_Space_Center" title="Kennedy Space Center">\n        Kennedy Space Center\n       </a>\n       \xe2\x80\x99s Runway 15.\n       <i>\n        Endeavour\n       </i>\n       wrapped up a 4.7\xc2\xa0million mile mission, and the first to the\n       <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">\n        International Space Station\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FD13Highlights_27-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-FD13Highlights-27">\n         [27]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Extra-vehicular_activity">\n        Extra-vehicular activity\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=STS-88&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Extra-vehicular activity">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Three\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extra-vehicular_activity" title="Extra-vehicular activity">\n        extra-vehicular activity\n       </a>\n       (EVA) spacewalks were scheduled and completed during STS-88.\n       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">\n        [\n        <i>\n         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">\n          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources.
