id="Mission_highlights">\n        Mission highlights\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Soyuz_TM-31&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Mission highlights">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <b>\n        Soyuz\n       </b>\n       carried a crew of three to dock it with the Zvezda module of the\n       <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">\n        International Space Station\n       </a>\n       (ISS) at about 09:21 UT on November 2. The Progress M1-3 cargo craft that was docked with Zvezda was released to make way for the Soyuz. The crew of two Russian and one American spent over three months on the ISS, and returned to Earth in an American shuttle (\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-102" title="STS-102">\n        STS-102\n       </a>\n       ) in February 2001. In the initial days, the crew brought a variety of life support systems on-line, and created a laptop computer network that helped run all systems in the ISS. The remaining months were allotted for exercise and space endurance practice. The crew was first group of a planned decade-long "permanent inhabitation" of the ISS.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="References">\n        References\n       </span>\n
