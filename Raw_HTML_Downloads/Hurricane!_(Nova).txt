You can purchase it on ShopPBS.org\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        Hurricane!\n       </b>\n       (episode: 1616 (308)) is a\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Nova_(TV_series)" title="Nova (TV series)">\n         Nova\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       episode that\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_NOVA_episodes" title="List of NOVA episodes">\n        aired on\n       </a>\n       November 7, 1989 on\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Public_Broadcasting_Service" title="Public Broadcasting Service">\n        PBS\n       </a>\n       . The episode describes the fury of a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane" title="Hurricane">\n        hurricane\n       </a>\n       and the history of hurricane forecasting. The episode features footage of\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Camille" title="Hurricane Camille">\n        Hurricane Camille\n       </a>\n       of 1969 and\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Gilbert" title="Hurricane Gilbert">\n        Hurricane Gilbert\n       </a>\n       of 1988 and behind the scenes footage at the\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">\n        National Hurricane Center\n       </a>\n       as forecasters tracked Hurricane Gilbert from its formation to its landfall in northern\n       <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">\n        Mexico\n       </a>\n       . Meteorologists,\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Hugh_Willoughby_(meteorologist)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Hugh Willoughby (meteorologist) (page does not exist)">\n        Hugh Willoughby\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Bob_Sheets" title="Bob Sheets">\n        Bob Sheets\n       </a>\n       (then director of the\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">\n        National Hurricane Center\n       </a>\n       ) and Jeff Masters were shown in the episode.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-a_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-a-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The episode was released on\n       <a href="/wiki/VHS" title="VHS">\n        VHS\n       </a>\n       home video under the same episode title and distributed by\n       <a href="/wiki/WGBH-TV" title="WGBH-TV">\n        WGBH\n       </a>\n       Boston video.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In 2004, the\n       <i>\n        Nova\n       </i>\n       episode was released on\n       <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">\n        DVD\n       </a>\n       and featured a different cover picture and title style.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In 1997,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UAV_Corp." title="UAV Corp.">\n        UAV Corp.\n       </a>\n       also distributed the\n       <i>\n        Nova\n       </i>\n       episode under the title Deadly Hurricanes which contains additional footage of\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Andrew" title="Hurricane Andrew">\n        Hurricane Andrew\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Iniki" title="Hurricane Iniki">\n        Hurricane Iniki\n       </a>\n       of 1992. The UAV version of the episode has since been discontinued.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n
