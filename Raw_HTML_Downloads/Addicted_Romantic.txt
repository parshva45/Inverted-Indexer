href="/wiki/Capitol_Records" title="Capitol Records">\n        Capitol Records\n       </a>\n       released the album on 13 June 2005. Four singles were released from the album; "Quarter to Three", "The Familiar"/"Enough", "Love for Sale", and "Hurricane". Of these, "Hurricane" was the most successful; it reached number 21 on the\n       <a href="/wiki/Triple_J_Hottest_100" title="Triple J Hottest 100">\n        Triple J Hottest 100\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Triple_J_Hottest_100,_2005" title="Triple J Hottest 100, 2005">\n        2005\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The album spent one week on the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ARIA_Albums_Chart" title="ARIA Albums Chart">\n        ARIA Albums Chart\n       </a>\n       at number 44, on 26 June 2005.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The album was recorded by Faker with Lindsay Gravina, of\n       <a href="/wiki/Magic_Dirt" title="Magic Dirt">\n        Magic Dirt\n       </a>\n       fame, at\n       <a href="/wiki/Melbourne" title="Melbourne">\n        Melbourne\n       </a>\n       \'s Birdland Studios.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JAward_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-JAward-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMO_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-AMO-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The inspiration for "Enough", the first song written from the album, came to\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lead_singer" title="Lead singer">\n        lead singer\n       </a>\n       Nathan Hudson from a\n       <a href="/wiki/Patti_Smith" title="Patti Smith">\n        Patti Smith\n       </a>\n       song in 1997. Eight years later, the band worked with Gravina to produce "the winning sound" on the album.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMO_4-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-AMO-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       He said the biggest challenge in recording was objectivity; choosing which songs to use, as well as improving their studio recording technique. experiences" when listening to\n       <i>\n        Addicted Romantic\n       </i>\n       , which they hoped contained music people would relate to.\n       <sup class="reference" <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TheAge_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TheAge-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       while Guy Blackman drew comparisons to\n       <a </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Age2_6-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Age2-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <a
