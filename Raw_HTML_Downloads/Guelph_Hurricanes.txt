class="mw-headline" id="History">\n        History\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Guelph_Hurricanes&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Founded in 1960 as the Hespeler Shamrocks, the team became the Cambridge Shamrocks in 1979 due to a town amalgamation. In 1982, the Shamrocks were purchased by Joe Holody and moved to Guelph to become the farm team to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Guelph_Platers" title="Guelph Platers">\n        Guelph Platers\n       </a>\n       (OHL). The team kept the "Platers" moniker until 1996, despite their parent club moving and stranding them to become the\n       <a href="/wiki/Owen_Sound_Platers" title="Owen Sound Platers">\n        Owen Sound Platers\n       </a>\n       in 1989. The team became known as the Fire in 1996 for four seasons before rebranding once again as the Dominators until 2009.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The team first played in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Western_Ontario_Junior_C_Hockey_League" title="Western Ontario Junior C Hockey League">\n        Central Junior C Hockey League\n       </a>\n       , a league now known as the\n       <a href="/wiki/Western_Ontario_Junior_C_Hockey_League" title="Western Ontario Junior C Hockey League">\n        Western Junior C Hockey League\n       </a>\n       . In 1970, they joined the\n       <a href="/wiki/Mid-Ontario_Junior_B_Hockey_League" title="Mid-Ontario Junior B Hockey League">\n        Mid-Ontario Junior B Hockey League\n       </a>\n       , and in 1971 joined the new\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Central_Junior_B_Hockey_League" title="Central Junior B Hockey League">\n        Central Junior B Hockey League\n       </a>\n       , the precursor to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Ontario_Junior_Hockey_League" title="Ontario Junior Hockey League">\n        Ontario Junior Hockey League\n       </a>\n       . In 1974 they moved to the Waterloo-Wellington Junior B League, which, in 1977, became the\n       <a href="/wiki/Mid-Western_Junior_Hockey_League" title="Mid-Western Junior Hockey League">\n        Midwestern Junior Hockey League\n       </a>\n       and have been there ever since.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       During the mid to late 90s the Guelph Platers operated as the "farm team" for the\n       <a href="/wiki/Guelph_Storm" title="Guelph Storm">\n        Guelph Storm\n       </a>\n       . They would wear the same colour scheme as the original team (with the classic lightning bolt theme) and played out of Guelph Memorial Gardens. While the team generally did not fare well on the ice during most of this period, they had several players drafted into the OHL, receive walk-on tryouts to\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Major_Junior" title="Major Junior">\n        Major Junior\n       </a>\n       teams, and US college scholarships. Eventually the Platers would become the Guelph Fire. This lasted just four seasons before the team was purchased by a local pizza shop owner who rebranded the team as the Guelph Dominators. In 2009 the Dominators became the Guelph Hurricanes, who now call the Guelph\n       <a href="/wiki/Sleeman_Centre_(Guelph)" title="Sleeman Centre (Guelph)">\n        Sleeman Centre\n       </a>\n       home.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Season-by-season_record">\n        Season-by-season record\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span
