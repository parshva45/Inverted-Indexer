This may result in coastal upwelling.\n         </div>\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        Upwelling\n       </b>\n       is an\n       <a href="/wiki/Physical_oceanography" title="Physical oceanography">\n        oceanographic\n       </a>\n       phenomenon that involves\n       <a href="/wiki/Wind" title="Wind">\n        wind\n       </a>\n       -driven motion of dense, cooler, and usually\n       <a href="/wiki/Nutrient" title="Nutrient">\n        nutrient\n       </a>\n       -rich water towards the\n       <a href="/wiki/Ocean" title="Ocean">\n        ocean\n       </a>\n       surface, replacing the warmer, usually nutrient-depleted\n       <a href="/wiki/Surface_water" title="Surface water">\n        surface water\n       </a>\n       . The nutrient-rich upwelled water stimulates the growth and reproduction of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Primary_producer" title="Primary producer">\n        primary producers\n       </a>\n       such as\n       <a href="/wiki/Phytoplankton" title="Phytoplankton">\n        phytoplankton\n       </a>\n       . Due to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Biomass" title="Biomass">\n        biomass\n       </a>\n       of phytoplankton and presence of cool water in these regions, upwelling zones can be identified by cool\n       <a href="/wiki/Sea_surface_temperature" title="Sea surface temperature">\n        sea surface temperatures\n       </a>\n       (SST) and high concentrations of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chlorophyll-a" title="Chlorophyll-a">\n        chlorophyll-a\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Anderson_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Anderson-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Sarhan_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Sarhan-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The increased availability in upwelling regions results in high levels of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Primary_productivity" title="Primary productivity">\n        primary productivity\n       </a>\n       and thus\n       <a href="/wiki/Fishery" title="Fishery">\n        fishery\n       </a>\n       production. Approximately 25% of the total global\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marine_fish" title="Marine fish">\n        marine fish\n       </a>\n       catches come from five upwellings that occupy only 5% of the total ocean area.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jennings_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Jennings-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Upwellings that are driven by coastal\n       <a href="/wiki/Ocean_current" title="Ocean current">\n        currents\n       </a>\n       or diverging open ocean have the greatest impact on nutrient-enriched waters and global fishery yields.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jennings_3-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Jennings-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mann_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Mann-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc"        Mechanisms\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Upwelling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Mechanisms">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       The three main drivers that work together to cause upwelling are\n       <a href="/wiki/Wind" title="Wind">\n        wind\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coriolis_effect" title="Coriolis effect">\n        Coriolis effects\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Ekman_transport" title="Ekman transport">\n        Ekman transport\n       </a>\n       . They operate differently for different types of upwelling, but the general effects are the same.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bakun_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Bakun-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In the overall process of upwelling, winds blow across the sea surface at a particular direction, which causes a wind-water interaction. As a result of the wind, the water is transported a net of 90 degrees from the direction of the wind due to Coriolis forces and Ekman transport. Ekman transport causes the surface layer of water to move at about a 45 degree angle from the direction of the wind, and the friction between that layer and the layer beneath it causes the successive layers to move in the same direction. This results in a spiral of water movement down the water column. Then, it is the Coriolis forces that dictate which way the water will move; in the Northern hemisphere, the water is transported to the right of the direction of the wind. In the Southern Hemisphere, the water is transported to the left of the wind.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chelton_6-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Chelton-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       If this net movement of water is divergent, then upwelling of deep water occurs to replace the water that was lost.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Anderson_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Anderson-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bakun_5-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Bakun-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Types">\n        Types\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Upwelling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Types">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Upwelling_image1.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="337" data-file-width="514" height="144" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fd/Upwelling_image1.jpg/220px-Upwelling_image1.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fd/Upwelling_image1.jpg/330px-Upwelling_image1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fd/Upwelling_image1.jpg/440px-Upwelling_image1.jpg 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Upwelling_image1.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Areas of upwelling in red\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The major upwellings in the ocean are associated with the divergence of currents that bring deeper, colder, nutrient rich waters to the surface. There are at least five types of upwelling: coastal upwelling, large-scale wind-driven upwelling in the ocean interior, upwelling associated with eddies, topographically-associated upwelling, and broad-diffusive upwelling in the ocean interior.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Coastal">\n        Coastal\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Upwelling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Coastal">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Coastal upwelling is the best known type of upwelling, and the most closely related to human activities as it supports some of the most productive\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fisheries" title="Fisheries">\n        fisheries\n       </a>\n       in the world. Wind-driven currents are diverted to the right of the winds in the Northern Hemisphere and to the left in the Southern Hemisphere due to the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coriolis_effect" title="Coriolis effect">\n        Coriolis effect\n       </a>\n       . The result is a net movement of surface water at right angles to the direction of the wind, known as the\n       <a href="/wiki/Ekman_transport" title="Ekman transport">\n        Ekman transport\n       </a>\n       (See also\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ekman_Spiral" title="Ekman Spiral">\n        Ekman Spiral\n       </a>\n       ). When Ekman transport is occurring away from the coast, surface waters moving away are replaced by deeper, colder, and denser water.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mann_4-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Mann-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Normally, this upwelling process occurs at a rate of about 5\xe2\x80\x9310 meters per day, but the rate and proximity of upwelling to the coast can be changed due to the strength and distance of the wind.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Anderson_1-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Anderson-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Nelson_7-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Nelson-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Deep waters are rich in nutrients, including\n       <a href="/wiki/Nitrate" title="Nitrate">\n        nitrate\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Phosphate" title="Phosphate">\n        phosphate\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Silicic_acid" title="Silicic acid">\n        silicic acid\n       </a>\n       , themselves the result of\n       <a href="/wiki/Decomposition" title="Decomposition">\n        decomposition\n       </a>\n       of sinking\n       <a href="/wiki/Organic_matter" title="Organic matter">\n        organic matter\n       </a>\n       (dead/detrital plankton) from surface waters. When brought to the surface, these nutrients are utilized by\n       <a href="/wiki/Phytoplankton" title="Phytoplankton">\n        phytoplankton\n       </a>\n       , along with dissolved CO\n       <sub>\n        2\n       </sub>\n       (\n       <a href="/wiki/Carbon_dioxide" title="Carbon dioxide">\n        carbon dioxide\n       </a>\n       ) and light energy from the\n       <a href="/wiki/Sun" title="Sun">\n        sun\n       </a>\n       , to produce\n       <a href="/wiki/Organic_compound" title="Organic compound">\n        organic compounds\n       </a>\n       , through the process of\n       <a href="/wiki/Photosynthesis" title="Photosynthesis">\n        photosynthesis\n       </a>\n       . Upwelling regions therefore result in very high levels of primary production (the amount of carbon fixed by\n       <a href="/wiki/Phytoplankton" title="Phytoplankton">\n        phytoplankton\n       </a>\n       ) in comparison to other areas of the ocean. They account for about 50% of global marine productivity.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Blanchette_8-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Blanchette-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       High primary production propagates up the\n       <a href="/wiki/Food_chain" title="Food chain">\n        food chain\n       </a>\n       because\n       <a href="/wiki/Phytoplankton" title="Phytoplankton">\n        phytoplankton\n       </a>\n       are at the base of the oceanic food chain.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lalli_9-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Lalli-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The food chain follows the course of:\n      </p>\n      <ul>\n       <li>\n        Phytoplankton \xe2\x86\x92\n        <a href="/wiki/Zooplankton" title="Zooplankton">\n         Zooplankton\n        </a>\n        \xe2\x86\x92 Predatory zooplankton \xe2\x86\x92\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Filter_feeders" title="Filter feeders">\n         Filter feeders\n        </a>\n        \xe2\x86\x92 Predatory fish\n        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mann_4-2">\n         <a href="#cite_note-Mann-4">\n          [4]\n         </a>\n        </sup>\n        \xe2\x86\x92 Marine birds, marine mammals\n        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Brodeur_10-0">\n         <a href="#cite_note-Brodeur-10">\n          [10]\n         </a>\n        </sup>\n       </li>\n      </ul>\n      <p>\n       Worldwide, there are five major coastal currents associated with upwelling areas: the\n       <a href="/wiki/Canary_Current" title="Canary Current">\n        Canary Current\n       </a>\n       (off\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northwest_Africa" title="Northwest Africa">\n        Northwest Africa\n       </a>\n       ), the\n       <a href="/wiki/Benguela_Current" title="Benguela Current">\n        Benguela Current\n       </a>\n       (off\n       <a href="/wiki/Southern_Africa" title="Southern Africa">\n        southern Africa\n       </a>\n       ), the\n       <a href="/wiki/California_Current" title="California Current">\n        California Current\n       </a>\n       (off\n       <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">\n        California\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Oregon" title="Oregon">\n        Oregon\n       </a>\n       ), the\n       <a href="/wiki/Humboldt_Current" title="Humboldt Current">\n        Humboldt Current\n       </a>\n       (off\n       <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">\n        Peru\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">\n        Chile\n       </a>\n       ), and the\n       <a href="/wiki/Somali_Current" title="Somali Current">\n        Somali Current\n       </a>\n       (off\n       <a href="/wiki/Somalia" title="Somalia">\n        Somalia\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Oman" title="Oman">\n        Oman\n       </a>\n       ). All of these currents support major fisheries. The four major eastern boundary currents in which coastal upwelling primarily occurs are the Canary Current, Benguela Current, California Current, and Humboldt Current.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cury_11-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Cury-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The\n       <a href="/wiki/Benguela_Current" title="Benguela Current">\n        Benguela Current\n       </a>\n       is the eastern boundary of the\n       <a href="/wiki/South_Atlantic_Gyre" title="South Atlantic Gyre">\n        South Atlantic subtropical gyre\n       </a>\n       and can be divided into a northern and southern sub-system with upwelling occurring in both areas. The subsystems are divided by an area of permanent upwelling off of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Luderitz" title="Luderitz">\n        Luderitz\n       </a>\n       , which is the strongest upwelling zone in the world. The\n       <a href="/wiki/California_Current" title="California Current">\n        California Current System\n       </a>\n       (CCS) is an eastern boundary current of the North Pacific that is also characterized by a north and south split. The split in this system occurs at\n       <a href="/wiki/Point_Conception" title="Point Conception">\n        Point Conception\n       </a>\n       , California due to weak upwelling in the South and strong upwelling in the north. The\n       <a href="/wiki/Canary_Current" title="Canary Current">\n        Canary Current\n       </a>\n       is an eastern boundary current of the\n       <a href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_Gyre" title="North Atlantic Gyre">\n        North Atlantic Gyre\n       </a>\n       and is also separated due to the presence of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Canary_Islands" title="Canary Islands">\n        Canary Islands\n       </a>\n       . Finally, the\n       <a href="/wiki/Humboldt_Current" title="Humboldt Current">\n        Humboldt Current\n       </a>\n       or the Peru Current flows west along the coast of\n       <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">\n        South America\n       </a>\n       from\n       <a href="/wiki/Peru" title="Peru">\n        Peru\n       </a>\n       to\n       <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">\n        Chile\n       </a>\n       and extends up to 1,000 kilometers offshore.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Blanchette_8-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Blanchette-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       These four eastern boundary currents comprise the majority of coastal upwelling zones in the oceans.\n      </p>\n      <div style="clear:both;">\n      </div>\n      <h4>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Equatorial">\n        Equatorial\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span title="Equator">\n        equator\n       </a>\n       is associated with the\n       <a href="/wiki/Intertropical_Convergence_Zone" title="Intertropical Convergence Zone">\n        Intertropical Convergence Zone\n       </a>\n       (ITCZ) which actually moves, and consequently, is often located just north or south of the equator. Easterly (westward) trade winds blow from the Northeast and Southeast and converge along the equator blowing West to form the ITCZ. Although there are no Coriolis forces present along the equator, upwelling still occurs just north and south of the equator. This results in a divergence, with denser, nutrient-rich water being upwelled from below, and results in the remarkable fact that the equatorial region in the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific" title="Pacific">\n        Pacific\n       </a>\n       can be detected from space as a broad line of high\n       <a href="/wiki/Phytoplankton" title="Phytoplankton">\n        phytoplankton\n       </a>\n       concentration.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jennings_3-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Jennings-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div style="clear:both;">\n      </div>\n      <h4>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Southern_Ocean">\n        Southern Ocean\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Upwelling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Southern Ocean">\n style="width:302px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Antarctic_bottom_water.svg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="470" data-file-width="745" height="189" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/Antarctic_bottom_water.svg/450px-Antarctic_bottom_water.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/Antarctic_bottom_water.svg/600px-Antarctic_bottom_water.svg.png 2x" width="300"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Antarctic_bottom_water.svg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         <div class="center" style="width:auto; margin-left:auto; margin-right:auto;">\n          Upwelling in the\n          <a href="/wiki/Southern_Ocean" title="Southern Ocean">\n           Southern Ocean\n          </a>\n         </div>\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Large-scale upwelling is also found in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Southern_Ocean" title="Southern Ocean">\n        Southern Ocean\n       </a>\n       . Here, strong westerly (eastward) winds blow around\n       <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">\n        Antarctica\n       </a>\n       , driving a significant flow of water northwards. This is actually a type of coastal upwelling. Since there are no continents in a band of open latitudes between\n       <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">\n        South America\n       </a>\n       and the tip of the Antarctic Peninsula, some of this water is drawn up from great depths. In many numerical models and observational syntheses, the Southern Ocean upwelling represents the primary means by which deep dense water is brought to the surface. Shallower, wind-driven upwelling is also found in off the west coasts of North and South America, northwest and southwest Africa, and southwest and\n       <a href="/wiki/Great_South_Australian_Coastal_Upwelling_System" title="Great South Australian Coastal Upwelling System">\n        south Australia\n       </a>\n       , all associated with oceanic subtropical high pressure circulations (see coastal upwelling above).\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Some models of the ocean circulation suggest that broad-scale upwelling occurs in the tropics, as pressure driven flows converge water toward the low latitudes where it is diffusively warmed from above. The required diffusion coefficients, however, appear to be larger than are observed in the real ocean. Nonetheless, some diffusive upwelling does probably occur.\n      </p>\n      <div style="clear:both;">\n      </div>\n      <h4>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Other_sources">\n        Other sources\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Upwelling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Other sources">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h4>\n      <ul>\n       <li>\n        Local and intermittent upwellings may occur when offshore islands,\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ridges" title="Ridges">\n         ridges\n        </a>\n        , or\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seamounts" title="Seamounts">\n         seamounts\n        </a>\n        cause a deflection of deep currents, providing a nutrient rich area in otherwise low productivity ocean areas. Examples include upwellings around the\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Galapagos_Islands" title="Galapagos Islands">\n         Galapagos Islands\n        </a>\n        and the\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seychelles_Islands" title="Seychelles Islands">\n         Seychelles Islands\n        </a>\n        , which have major\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pelagic" title="Pelagic">\n         pelagic\n        </a>\n        fisheries.\n        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jennings_3-3">\n         <a href="#cite_note-Jennings-3">\n          [3]\n         </a>\n        </sup>\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Upwelling can also occur when a\n        <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n         tropical cyclone\n        </a>\n        transits an area, usually when moving at speeds of less than 5\xc2\xa0mph (8\xc2\xa0km/h). The churning of a cyclone eventually draws up cooler water from lower layers of the ocean. This causes the cyclone to weaken.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Artificial upwelling is produced by devices that use ocean wave energy or ocean thermal energy conversion to pump water to the surface. Ocean wind turbines are also known to produce upwellings.\n        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">\n         <a href="#cite_note-12">\n          [12]\n         </a>\n        </sup>\n        Ocean wave devices have been shown to produce plankton blooms.\n        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">\n         <a href="#cite_note-13">\n          [13]\n         </a>\n        </sup>\n       </li>\n      </ul>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Variations">\n        Variations\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Upwelling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Variations">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Sstanom_199711_krig.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="219" data-file-width="350" height="188" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f6/Sstanom_199711_krig.jpg/300px-Sstanom_199711_krig.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/f6/Sstanom_199711_krig.jpg 1.5x" width="300"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Sstanom_199711_krig.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         <div class="center" style="width:auto; margin-left:auto; margin-right:auto;">\n          Unusually strong winds from the east push warm (red) surface water towards Africa, allowing cold (blue) water to upwell along the Sumatran coast\n         </div>\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Upwelling intensity depends on wind strength and seasonal variability, as well as the vertical structure of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">\n        water\n       </a>\n       , variations in the bottom\n       <a href="/wiki/Bathymetry" title="Bathymetry">\n        bathymetry\n       </a>\n       , and instabilities in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Current_(fluid)" title="Current (fluid)">\n        currents\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In some areas, upwelling is a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seasonal" title="Seasonal">\n        seasonal\n       </a>\n       event leading to periodic bursts of productivity similar to spring\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Phytoplankton_bloom" title="Phytoplankton bloom">\n        blooms\n       </a>\n       in coastal waters. Wind-induced upwelling is generated by temperature differences between the warm, light air above the land and the cooler denser air over the sea. In\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Temperate" title="Temperate">\n        temperate\n       </a>\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latitudes" title="Latitudes">\n        latitudes\n       </a>\n       , the temperature contrast is greatly seasonably variable, creating periods of strong upwelling in the spring and summer, to weak or no upwelling in the winter. For example, off the coast of Oregon, there are four or five strong upwelling events separated by periods of little to no upwelling during the six-month season of upwelling. In contrast,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical" title="Tropical">\n        tropical\n       </a>\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Latitudes" title="Latitudes">\n        latitudes\n       </a>\n       have a more constant temperature contrast, creating constant upwelling throughout the year. The Peruvian upwelling, for instance, occurs throughout most of the year, resulting in one of the world\'s largest marine fisheries for\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sardines" title="Sardines">\n        sardines\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anchovies" title="Anchovies">\n        anchovies\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mann_4-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Mann-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In anomalous years when the\n       <a href="/wiki/Trade_winds" title="Trade winds">\n        trade winds\n       </a>\n       weaken or reverse, the water that is upwelled is much warmer and low in nutrients, resulting in a sharp reduction in the biomass and\n       <a href="/wiki/Phytoplankton" title="Phytoplankton">\n        phytoplankton\n       </a>\n       productivity. This event is known as the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/El_Nino-Southern_Oscillation" title="El Nino-Southern Oscillation">\n        El Nino-Southern Oscillation\n       </a>\n       (ENSO) event. The Peruvian upwelling system is particularly vulnerable to ENSO events, and can cause extreme interannual variability in productivity.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mann_4-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Mann-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Changes in bathymetry can affect the strength of an upwelling. For example, a submarine\n       <a href="/wiki/Ridge" title="Ridge">\n        ridge\n       </a>\n       that extends out from the coast will produce more favorable upwelling conditions than neighboring regions. Upwelling typically begins at such ridges and remains strongest at the ridge even after developing in other locations.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mann_4-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Mann-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="High_productivity">\n        High productivity\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Upwelling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: High productivity">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Since upwelling regions are important sources of marine productivity, and they attract hundreds of species throughout the trophic levels, the diversity of these systems has been a focal point for\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marine_research" title="Marine research">\n        marine research\n       </a>\n       . While studying the trophic levels and patterns typical of upwelling regions, researchers have discovered that upwelling systems exhibit a wasp-waist richness pattern. In this type of pattern, the high and low\n       <a href="/wiki/Trophic_level" title="Trophic level">\n        trophic levels\n       </a>\n       are well-represented by high species diversity. However, the intermediate trophic level is only represented by one or two species. This trophic layer, which consists of small,\n       <a href="/wiki/Pelagic_fish" title="Pelagic fish">\n        pelagic fish\n       </a>\n       usually makes up about only three to four percent of the species diversity of all fish species present. The lower trophic layers are very well-represented with about 500 species of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Copepods" title="Copepods">\n        copepods\n       </a>\n       , 2500 species of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gastropods" title="Gastropods">\n        gastropods\n       </a>\n       , and 2500 species of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crustaceans" title="Crustaceans">\n        crustaceans\n       </a>\n       on average. At the apex and near-apex trophic levels, there are usually about 100 species of marine mammals and about 50 species of marine birds. The vital intermediate trophic species however are small pelagic fish that usually feed on\n       <a href="/wiki/Phytoplankton" title="Phytoplankton">\n        phytoplankton\n       </a>\n       . In most upwelling systems, these species are either anchovies or sardines, and usually only one is present, although two or three species may be present occasionally. These fish are an important food source for predators, such as large pelagic fish, marine mammals, and marine birds. Although they are not at the base of the trophic pyramid, they are the vital species that connect the entire marine ecosystem and keep the productivity of upwelling zones so high\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cury_11-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Cury-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Threats_to_upwelling_ecosystems">\n        Threats to upwelling ecosystems\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Upwelling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Threats to upwelling ecosystems">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       A major threat to both this crucial intermediate\n       <a href="/wiki/Trophic_level" title="Trophic level">\n        trophic level\n       </a>\n       and the entire upwelling trophic\n       <a href="/wiki/Ecosystem" title="Ecosystem">\n        ecosystem\n       </a>\n       is the problem of\n       <a href="/wiki/Commercial_fishing" title="Commercial fishing">\n        commercial fishing\n       </a>\n       . Since upwelling regions are the most productive and species rich areas in the world, they attract a high number of commercial fishers and fisheries. On one hand, this is another benefit of the upwelling process as it serves as a viable source of food and income for so many people and nations besides marine animals. However, just as in any ecosystem, the consequences of over-fishing from a population could be detrimental to that population and the ecosystem as a whole. In upwelling ecosystems, every species present plays a vital role in the functioning of that ecosystem. If one species is significantly depleted, that will have an effect throughout the rest of the trophic levels. For example, if a popular prey species is targeted by fisheries, fishermen may collect hundreds of thousands of individuals of this species just by casting their nets into the upwelling waters. As these fish are depleted, the food source for those who preyed on these fish is depleted. Therefore, the predators of the targeted fish will begin to die off, and there will not be as many of them to feed the predators above them. This system continues throughout the entire\n       <a href="/wiki/Food_chain" title="Food chain">\n        food chain\n       </a>\n       , resulting in a possible collapse of the ecosystem. It is possible that the ecosystem may be restored over time, but not all species can recover from events such as these. Even if the species can adapt, there may be a delay in the reconstruction of this upwelling community.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cury_11-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Cury-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The possibility of such an ecosystem collapse is the very danger of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fisheries" title="Fisheries">\n        fisheries\n       </a>\n       in upwelling regions. Fisheries may target a variety of different species, and therefore they are a direct threat to many species in the ecosystem, however they pose the highest threat to the intermediate\n       <a href="/wiki/Pelagic_fish" title="Pelagic fish">\n        pelagic fish\n       </a>\n       . Since these fish form the crux of the entire trophic process of upwelling ecosystems, they are highly represented throughout the ecosystem (even if there is only one species present). Unfortunately, these fish tend to be the most popular targets of fisheries as about 64 percent of their entire catch consists of pelagic fish. Among those, the six main species that usually form the intermediate trophic layer represent over half of the catch.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cury_11-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Cury-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:El_Ni%C3%B1o_Conditions.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="361" data-file-width="503" height="215" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9b/El_Ni%C3%B1o_Conditions.jpg/300px-El_Ni%C3%B1o_Conditions.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9b/El_Ni%C3%B1o_Conditions.jpg/450px-El_Ni%C3%B1o_Conditions.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/9/9b/El_Ni%C3%B1o_Conditions.jpg 2x" width="300"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:El_Ni%C3%B1o_Conditions.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         During an\n         <a href="/wiki/El_Ni%C3%B1o" title="El Ni\xc3\xb1o">\n          El Ni\xc3\xb1o\n         </a>\n         , wind indirectly drives warm water to the South American coast, reducing the effects of cold upwelling\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Besides directly causing the collapse of the ecosystem due to their absence, this can create problems in the ecosystem through a variety of other methods as well. The animals higher in the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trophic_levels" title="Trophic levels">\n        trophic levels\n       </a>\n       may not completely starve to death and die off, but the decreased food supply could still hurt the populations. If animals do not get enough food, it will decrease their reproductive viability meaning that they will not breed as often or as successfully as usual. This can lead to a decreasing population, especially in species that do not breed often under normal circumstances or become reproductively mature late in life. Another problem is that the decrease in the population of a species due to fisheries can lead to a decrease in genetic diversity, resulting in a decrease in\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bio-diversity" title="Bio-diversity">\n        bio-diversity\n       </a>\n       of a species. If the species diversity is decreased significantly, this could cause problems for the species in an environment that is so variable and quick-changing; they may not be able to adapt, which could result in a collapse of the population or ecosystem.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cury_11-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Cury-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Another threat to the productivity and ecosystems of upwelling regions is\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/El_Ni%C3%B1o-Southern_Oscillation" title="El Ni\xc3\xb1o-Southern Oscillation">\n        El Ni\xc3\xb1o-Southern Oscillation\n       </a>\n       (ENSO) system, or more specifically El Ni\xc3\xb1o events. During the normal period and\n       <a href="/wiki/La_Ni%C3%B1a" title="La Ni\xc3\xb1a">\n        La Ni\xc3\xb1a\n       </a>\n       events, the easterly trade winds are still strong, which continues to drive the process of upwelling. However, during El Ni\xc3\xb1o events, trade winds are weaker, causing decreased upwelling in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">\n        equatorial\n       </a>\n       regions as the divergence of water north and south of the equator is not as strong or as prevalent. The coastal upwelling zones diminish as well since they are wind driven systems, and the wind is no longer a very strong driving force in these areas. As a result, global upwelling drastically decreases, causing a decrease in productivity as the waters are no longer receiving nutrient-rich water. Without these nutrients, the rest of the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trophic_pyramid" title="Trophic pyramid">\n        trophic pyramid\n       </a>\n       cannot be sustained, and the rich upwelling ecosystem will collapse.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Carpenter_14-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Carpenter-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div style="clear:both;">\n      </div>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="References">\n        References\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Upwelling&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: References">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="reflist columns references-column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 32em; -webkit-column-width: 32em; column-width: 32em; list-style-type: decimal;">\n       <ol class="references">\n        <li id="cite_note-Anderson-1">\n         <span class="mw-cite-backlink">\n          ^\n          <a href="#cite_ref-Anderson_1-0">\n           <sup>\n            <i>\n             <b>\n              a\n             </b>\n            </i>\n           </sup>\n          </a>\n          <a href="#cite_ref-Anderson_1-1">\n           <sup>\n            <i>\n             <b>\n              b\n             </b>\n            </i>\n           </sup>\n          </a>\n          <a href="#cite_ref-Anderson_1-2">\n           <sup>\n            <i>\n             <b>\n              c\n             </b>\n            </i>\n           </sup>\n          </a>\n         </span>\n         <span class="reference-text">\n          Anderson DM, Prell WL.
