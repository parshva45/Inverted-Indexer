<b>\n          <a href="/wiki/1979_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1979 Atlantic hurricane season">\n           1979 Atlantic hurricane season\n          </a>\n         </b>\n        </td>\n       </tr>\n       <tr style="display:none">\n        <td colspan="2">\n        </td>\n       </tr>\n      </table>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        Hurricane Frederic\n       </b>\n       was an intense and damaging\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        tropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       that carved a path of damage from the\n       <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">\n        Lesser Antilles\n       </a>\n       to\n       <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">\n        Quebec\n       </a>\n       , in particular devastating areas of the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Gulf_Coast" title="United States Gulf Coast">\n        United States Gulf Coast\n       </a>\n       . Though only five were killed directly, the\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">\n        US\n       </a>\n       $2.3\xc2\xa0billion in damage accrued by Frederic made it the\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_hurricane" title="Atlantic hurricane">\n        Atlantic basin\n       </a>\n       \'s\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_costliest_Atlantic_hurricanes" title="List of costliest Atlantic hurricanes">\n        costliest\n       </a>\n       tropical cyclone on record at the time. Prior to its final\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">\n        landfall\n       </a>\n       , the threat that Frederic imposed on areas of the U.S. Gulf Coast triggered a mass exodus from the region larger than any other evacuation in the past. While the storm primarily impacted the\n       <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">\n        U.S. states\n       </a>\n       of\n       <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">\n        Mississippi\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">\n        Alabama\n       </a>\n       , lesser effects were felt throughout the\n       <a href="/wiki/Greater_Antilles" title="Greater Antilles">\n        Greater\n       </a>\n       and Lesser Antilles, as well as inland North America.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Frederic developed from a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_depression" title="Tropical depression">\n        tropical depression\n       </a>\n       south of the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cape_Verde_Islands" title="Cape Verde Islands">\n        Cape Verde Islands\n       </a>\n       on August\xc2\xa028, 1979.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UTC_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-UTC-1">\n         [nb 1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Tracking at a steady clip westward, the primitive cyclone reached\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_storm" title="Tropical storm">\n        tropical storm\n       </a>\n       intensity the next day. Favorable conditions in the open Atlantic allowed for Frederic to reach hurricane intensity on September\xc2\xa01. However,\n       <a href="/wiki/Outflow_(meteorology)" title="Outflow (meteorology)">\n        outflow\n       </a>\n       from nearby\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_David" title="Hurricane David">\n        Hurricane David\n       </a>\n       began to inhibit further intensification and would continue to do so for roughly a week, weakening Frederic as it tracked across the Greater Antilles. The tropical cyclone nearly dissipated over\n       <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">\n        Cuba\n       </a>\n       before redeveloping on September\xc2\xa09 near the\n       <a href="/wiki/Isla_de_la_Juventud" title="Isla de la Juventud">\n        Isle of Youth\n       </a>\n       . From then on, Frederic moved northwestward, intensifying to its peak intensity in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">\n        Gulf of Mexico\n       </a>\n       with winds of 135\xc2\xa0mph (215\xc2\xa0km/h) on September\xc2\xa012, shortly before making landfall at\n       <a href="/wiki/Dauphin_Island,_Alabama" title="Dauphin Island, Alabama">\n        Dauphin Island, Alabama\n       </a>\n       just below the state line between Alabama and Mississippi. Over the United States, Frederic weakened for a final time before becoming\n       <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">\n        extratropical\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">\n        Pennsylvania\n       </a>\n       on September\xc2\xa014 and dissipating the next day.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Damage estimates vary from $6\xe2\x80\x939\xc2\xa0billion (2008\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">\n        USD\n       </a>\n       ), with variations due to inadequate reporting of private insurance claims as well as lack of hard data on uninsured damage.\n       <a href="/wiki/Federal_Emergency_Management_Agency" title="Federal Emergency Management Agency">\n        FEMA\n       </a>\n       , which had been established only three months before Frederic hit, was the focal point for nearly $250\xc2\xa0million in federal aid for recovery, $188\xc2\xa0million of which went to Alabama (1979 USD).\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="toc">\n       <div class="toctitle">\n        <h2>\n         Contents\n        </h2>\n       </div>\n </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The precursor to Hurricane Frederic emerged as a loosely defined\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">\n        tropical wave\n       </a>\n       off the west coast of Africa late on August\xc2\xa027. The following day, satellite images indicated that the tropical wave had become more defined, and was beginning to show signs of\n       <a href="/wiki/Cyclonic_rotation" title="Cyclonic rotation">\n        cyclonic rotation\n       </a>\n       . At 0600\n       <a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">\n        UTC\n       </a>\n       on August\xc2\xa029, the\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">\n        National Hurricane Center\n       </a>\n       <small>\n        (NHC)\n       </small>\n       classified the system as a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_depression" title="Tropical depression">\n        tropical depression\n       </a>\n       based on observational data from ships nearby in conjunction with satellite images.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-2">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Upon\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclogenesis" title="Tropical cyclogenesis">\n        developing\n       </a>\n       into a tropical cyclone, the depression moved at an unusually rapid pace westward, gradually curving towards the west-northwest and slowly intensifying in ideal conditions. At 1200\xc2\xa0UTC on August\xc2\xa030,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       the tropical depression was upgraded to\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_storm" title="Tropical storm">\n        tropical storm\n       </a>\n       status and thus\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_naming" title="Tropical cyclone naming">\n        named\n       </a>\n       <i>\n        Frederic\n       </i>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_2-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-2">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Intensification continued after the storm\'s upgrade, and early on September\xc2\xa01, Frederic developed an\n       <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">\n        eye\n       </a>\n       , prompting the NHC to further upgrade the tropical cyclone to hurricane status at 0600\xc2\xa0UTC that day.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_2-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-2">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Frederic only maintained hurricane intensity for roughly eighteen hours over the open waters of the Atlantic on September\xc2\xa01 before\n       <a href="/wiki/Outflow_(meteorology)" title="Outflow (meteorology)">\n        outflow\n       </a>\n       from nearby\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_David" title="Hurricane David">\n        Hurricane David\n       </a>\n       began to adversely affect the tropical cyclone.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_2-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-2">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At 0000\xc2\xa0UTC on September\xc2\xa02, Frederic weakened back to tropical storm strength while it was still well east of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">\n        Lesser Antilles\n       </a>\n       . This weakening phase continued as Frederic began to assume a more westerly track and slow in forward motion.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_2-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-2">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On September\xc2\xa04, Frederic tracked over the\n       <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Islands" title="Virgin Islands">\n        Virgin Islands\n       </a>\n       before making\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">\n        landfall\n       </a>\n       on\n       <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">\n        Puerto Rico\n       </a>\n       later that day with\n       <a href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_wind" title="Maximum sustained wind">\n        maximum sustained winds\n       </a>\n       of 50\xc2\xa0mph (80\xc2\xa0km/h).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Frederic\'s interaction with Puerto Rico greatly disrupted the low-level circulation contained within the tropical cyclone, enhancing the weakening effects of Hurricane David\'s outflow. After passing over the island, the tropical storm briefly took a southwest course before curving into\n       <a href="/wiki/Hispaniola" title="Hispaniola">\n        Hispaniola\n       </a>\n       on September\xc2\xa06, inducing additional disruption within Frederic. At 1800\xc2\xa0UTC that day, Frederic was downgraded to tropical depression status north of\n       <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">\n        Haiti\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_2-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-2">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Frederic remained a tropical depression for two days as it tracked into and then parallel to the southern coast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">\n        Cuba\n       </a>\n       beginning on September\xc2\xa07.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       During this time, Hurricane David had tracked far into the\n       <a href="/wiki/Northeastern_United_States" title="Northeastern United States">\n        Northeastern United States\n       </a>\n       , and as a result its inhibiting effects of intensification ceased.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_2-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-2">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At 0000\xc2\xa0UTC on September\xc2\xa09,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Frederic regained tropical storm intensity while located roughly 100\xc2\xa0mi (160\xc2\xa0km/h) east of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Isla_de_la_Juventud" title="Isla de la Juventud">\n        Isle of Youth\n       </a>\n       . The following day, the cyclone tracked over western Cuba as it gradually curved towards the northwest.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Favorable conditions, marked by very warm\n       <a href="/wiki/Sea_surface_temperature" title="Sea surface temperature">\n        sea surface temperatures\n       </a>\n       as high as 86\xc2\xbaF\xc2\xa0(30\xc2\xbaC) and the presence of a large\n       <a href="/wiki/Anticyclone" title="Anticyclone">\n        anticyclone\n       </a>\n       over the system, allowed Frederic to strengthen to hurricane status for a second time while just northwest of Cuba, despite proximity to land.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_2-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-2">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Frederic took a northwesterly course throughout its trek across the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">\n        Gulf of Mexico\n       </a>\n       in early September, intensifying in a highly conducive environment.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_2-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-2">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       By 1800\xc2\xa0UTC on September\xc2\xa010, the hurricane became stronger than it had ever been over the central Atlantic. At 0000\xc2\xa0UTC on September\xc2\xa012, Frederic attained\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_hurricane_wind_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson hurricane wind scale">\n        major hurricane\n       </a>\n       status over the eastern\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">\n        Gulf of Mexico\n       </a>\n       , and twelve hours later reached peak intensity with a minimum\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barometric_pressure" title="Barometric pressure">\n        barometric pressure\n       </a>\n       of 943\n       <a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">\n        mbar\n       </a>\n       (\n       <a href="/wiki/Pascal_(unit)" title="Pascal (unit)">\n        hPa\n       </a>\n       ; 27.85\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inches_of_mercury" title="Inches of mercury">\n        inHg\n       </a>\n       ) and sustained winds of 135\xc2\xa0mph (215\xc2\xa0km/h),\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-5">\n         [nb 2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       making the cyclone a Category\xc2\xa04 hurricane on the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_hurricane_wind_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson hurricane wind scale">\n        Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson hurricane wind scale\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Moving gradually faster in the Gulf of Mexico,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_2-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-2">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Frederic eventually made two landfalls\xc2\xa0\xe2\x80\x93 one on\n       <a href="/wiki/Dauphin_Island,_Alabama" title="Dauphin Island, Alabama">\n        Dauphin Island\n       </a>\n       and the other near the border between\n       <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">\n        Alabama\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">\n        Mississippi\n       </a>\n       \xe2\x80\x93 with a virtually unchanged intensity.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USLandfalls_6-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-USLandfalls-6">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Tracking rapidly northward, Frederic began to weaken due to land interaction, and was downgraded to tropical storm status while it was near\n       <a href="/wiki/Meridian,_Mississippi" title="Meridian, Mississippi">\n        Meridian, Mississippi\n       </a>\n       on September\xc2\xa013.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_2-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-2">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At around the same time, the cyclone began to curve northeastward,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       eventually merging with an\n       <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">\n        extratropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       in southwestern\n       <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">\n        Pennsylvania\n       </a>\n       by 1800\xc2\xa0UTC on September\xc2\xa014. These extratropical remnants proceeded to track through the\n       <a href="/wiki/Mid-Atlantic_states" title="Mid-Atlantic states">\n        Mid-Atlantic states\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/New_England" title="New England">\n        New England\n       </a>\n       before they were last officially documented by the NHC in\n       <a href="/wiki/New_Brunswick" title="New Brunswick">\n        New Brunswick\n       </a>\n       on September\xc2\xa015.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_2-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-2">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       However, the\n       <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Hurricane_Centre" title="Canadian Hurricane Centre">\n        Canadian Hurricane Centre\n       </a>\n       still considered the cyclone active up until the storm entered the\n       <a href="/wiki/Labrador_Sea" title="Labrador Sea">\n        Labrador Sea\n       </a>\n       on September\xc2\xa016.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Canada_7-0">\n        <a </div>\n      <p>\n       The first\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_watches_and_warnings" title="Tropical cyclone watches and warnings">\n        tropical cyclone watches storm by the time Frederic moved over the Virgin Islands, the National Hurricane Center issued a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_watch" title="Hurricane watch">\n        hurricane </a>\n       </sup>\n       As precautionary measures, the\n       <a href="/wiki/Luis_Mu%C3%B1oz_Mar%C3%ADn_International_Airport" title="Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Mar\xc3\xadn International Airport">\n        Luis Mu\xc3\xb1oz Mar\xc3\xadn International Airport\n       </a>\n       and schools closed for the duration of the storm.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DowngradedFrederic_10-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-DowngradedFrederic-10">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Hundreds of residents were ordered to evacuate from\n       <a href="/wiki/Toa_Baja,_Puerto_Rico" Puerto Rico">\n        Trujillo Alto\n       </a>\n       , a dike was relieved in order to mitigate potential flooding.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HeadsPR_14-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HeadsPR-14">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       After its track across Puerto Rico, gale warnings were issued for portions of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">\n        Dominican Republic\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">\n        Haiti\n       </a>\n       , as Frederic was classified as a tropical depression over the Greater Antilles.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prelim5_16-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Prelim5-16">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       However, small craft warnings were posted for some coastal regions off of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Miami,_Florida" title="Miami, Florida">\n        Miami, Florida\n       </a>\n       due to strong winds caused by the nearby tropical cyclone.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SFlaThreaten_17-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SFlaThreaten-17">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       After Frederic reattained hurricane status in the Gulf of Mexico on September\xc2\xa010, a gale warning were issued for the\n       <a href="/wiki/Dry_Tortugas" title="Dry Tortugas">\n        Dry Tortugas\n       </a>\n       ; this was the first NHC warning issued in association with the newly developed tropical cyclone.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prelim5_16-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Prelim5-16">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Not long after, a hurricane watch was issued for coastal areas extending from\n       <a Municipality managers were asked to place their respective emergency personnel on standby.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FL-Alerted_18-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-FL-Alerted-18">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Key_West,_Florida" title="Key West, Florida">\n        Key West, Florida\n       </a>\n       , city workers had shortened workdays while the storm moved close by.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OR-Again_19-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-OR-Again-19">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Up to 500,000 were evacuated from the U.S. Gulf Coast in anticipation of Frederic\'s arrival.\n       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">\n        [\n        <i>\n         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">\n          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2015)">\n           citation needed\n          </span>\n         </a>\n        </i>\n        ]\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div style="clear:both;">\n      </div>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">\n        Impact\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Frederic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Impact">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Lesser_Antilles_and_Puerto_Rico">\n        Lesser Antilles and Puerto Rico\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Frederic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Lesser Antilles and Puerto Rico">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Effects from Frederic were first felt on the outward facing Leeward Islands. In\n       <a href="/wiki/Antigua" title="Antigua">\n        Antigua\n       </a>\n       , the threat of widespread\n       <a href="/wiki/Power_outage" title="Power outage">\n        power outages\n       </a>\n       forced the insular government to shut down power.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GuadeloulpeSmall_20-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-GuadeloulpeSmall-20">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PoorCousin_12-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-PoorCousin-12">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A peak gust of 62\xc2\xa0mph (100\xc2\xa0km/h) was documented on the island as a result of the storm.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_2-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-2">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       These strong winds also unroofed some buildings.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DowngradedFrederic_10-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-DowngradedFrederic-10">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Strong winds and rain were felt in\n       <a href="/wiki/Guadeloupe" title="Guadeloupe">\n        Guadeloupe\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Barbuda" title="Barbuda">\n        Barbuda\n       </a>\n       , and a number of other islands, but no damaging effects from the passing tropical cyclone occurred in those locations.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GuadeloulpeSmall_20-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-GuadeloulpeSmall-20">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Power outages knocked out electricity to half of\n       <a href="/wiki/Tortola" title="Tortola">\n        Tortola\n       </a>\n       in the\n       <a href="/wiki/British_Virgin_Islands" title="British Virgin Islands">\n        British Virgin Islands\n       </a>\n       and downed telephone lines. Rainfall and downed trees blocked several roads.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-StormFrederic_21-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-StormFrederic-21">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Offshore, the\n       <a href="/wiki/Yacht" title="Yacht">\n        yacht\n       </a>\n       <i>\n        Princess\n       </i>\n       blew out into sea, prompting a coast guard rescue of the ship and its crew.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DowngradedFrederic_10-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-DowngradedFrederic-10">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       As a tropical storm,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Frederic dropped heavy rain across the U.S. Virgin Islands and Puerto Rico. Despite having weakened from its prior hurricane intensity, the storm still brought\n       <a href="/wiki/Gale" title="Gale">\n        gale\n       </a>\n       -force winds coupled with gusts as strong as 70\xc2\xa0mph (110\xc2\xa0km/h) to the area.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_2-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-2">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Three apartments in the same apartment complex on\n       <a href="/wiki/Saint_Thomas,_U.S._Virgin_Islands" title="Saint Thomas, U.S. Virgin Islands">\n        St. Thomas\n       </a>\n       were unroofed, displacing roughly 50\xc2\xa0families.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SD_22-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SD-22">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Electricity was deliberately cut during the late night hours to prevent the spread of power outages.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HeadsPR_14-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HeadsPR-14">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Maarten" title="St. Maarten">\n        St. Maarten\n       </a>\n       , these strong winds toppled a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Radio_antenna" title="Radio antenna">\n        radio antenna\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FredericSlows_9-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-FredericSlows-9">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The entirety of the U.S. Virgin Islands reported numerous felled trees and downed utility lines as a result of the strong winds. Wind damage on nearby Puerto Rico was of only minor extent and much less severe than in the Virgin Islands.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SD_22-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SD-22">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       However, torrential precipitation accounted for most of the damage, as the passage of Hurricane David less than a week earlier saturated soils, priming the area for floods induced by the passage of Frederic. Rainfall peaked at around 10\xc2\xa0in (250\xc2\xa0mm) in 12\xc2\xa0hours in Puerto Rico and 24.04\xc2\xa0in (611\xc2\xa0mm) in 30\xc2\xa0hours in\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Croix,_U.S._Virgin_Islands" title="St. Croix, U.S. Virgin Islands">\n        St. Croix\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_2-14">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-2">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCRain_23-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCRain-23">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Frederic_1979_Puerto_Rico_rainfall.gif">\n         <img alt="Contoured map showing rainfall totals in increments of two inches (50\xc2\xa0millimeters)." class="thumbimage" data-file-height="791" data-file-width="1421" height="122" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Frederic_1979_Puerto_Rico_rainfall.gif/220px-Frederic_1979_Puerto_Rico_rainfall.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Frederic_1979_Puerto_Rico_rainfall.gif/330px-Frederic_1979_Puerto_Rico_rainfall.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Frederic_1979_Puerto_Rico_rainfall.gif/440px-Frederic_1979_Puerto_Rico_rainfall.gif 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Frederic_1979_Puerto_Rico_rainfall.gif" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Rainfall totals in Puerto Rico and the U.S. Virgin Islands\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       All rivers in southeastern Puerto Rico swelled and produce significant flooding in adjacent floodplains. The\n       <a href="/wiki/Discharge_(hydrology)" title="Discharge (hydrology)">\n        discharge\n       </a>\n       of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Santiago_River_(Puerto_Rico)" title="Santiago River (Puerto Rico)">\n        Rio Santiago\n       </a>\n       near\n       <a href="/wiki/Naguabo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Naguabo, Puerto Rico">\n        Naguabo\n       </a>\n       reached\n       <a href="/wiki/Return_period" title="Return period">\n        100-year levels\n       </a>\n       . These floods partially inundated several cities, submerged crops, and damaged numerous roads. Major arterial roads to and from\n       <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">\n        Ponce\n       </a>\n       were blocked by floodwater and landslide debris. Some portions of\n       <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico_Highway_3" title="Puerto Rico Highway 3">\n        Puerto Rico Highway\xc2\xa03\n       </a>\n       were submerged by water from both the torrential rain and wave action. Damage from Frederic in Puerto Rico reached at least US$5\xc2\xa0million, though western Puerto Rico sustained minimal damage from the storm. In St. Thomas, four small homes were destroyed by flooding, and an additional fifty sustained at least partial damage. One\n       <a href="/wiki/Waterspout" title="Waterspout">\n        waterspout\n       </a>\n       was reported off the coast, but did no damage. Normally dry\n       <a href="/wiki/Gut_(coastal_geography)" title="Gut (coastal geography)">\n        coastal guts\n       </a>\n       in St. Croix filled with floodwater from Frederic, damaging homes and other buildings in five communities on St. Croix. Culverts, bridges, and heavy\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beach_erosion" title="Beach erosion">\n        beach erosion\n       </a>\n       resulted from rough surf off the coast of the island. The floods generated various sewage problems.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SD_22-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SD-22">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Flowing floodwaters washed away plentiful food crops in St. Maarten.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DowngradedFrederic_10-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-DowngradedFrederic-10">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Some homes were damaged and others destroyed on the adjacent\n       <a href="/wiki/Sint_Maarten" title="Sint Maarten">\n        Sint Maarten\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MoreRain_13-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MoreRain-13">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Off of the island, seven people were killed after their fishing boat sank during the storm.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_2-15">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-2">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Damage in Virgin Islands also reached at least US$5\xc2\xa0million, similar to Puerto Rico.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SD_22-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SD-22">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Hispaniola_and_Cuba">\n        Hispaniola and Cuba\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Frederic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Hispaniola and Cuba">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Heavy rains buffeted the islands of\n       <a href="/wiki/Hispaniola" title="Hispaniola">\n        Hispaniola\n       </a>\n       and Cuba for several days as Frederic, albeit disorganized,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SoaksHispaniola_24-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SoaksHispaniola-24">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       tracked over the\n       <a href="/wiki/Greater_Antilles" title="Greater Antilles">\n        Greater Antilles\n       </a>\n       . As with Puerto Rico and the Lesser Antilles, precipitation damage was exacerbated by the recent passage of Hurricane David. In\n       <a href="/wiki/Guant%C3%A1namo" title="Guant\xc3\xa1namo">\n        Guant\xc3\xa1namo\n       </a>\n       on Cuba\'s eastern Atlantic coast, at least 6.88\xc2\xa0in (175\xc2\xa0mm) of rainfall was reported. Frederic continued to produce strong winds even as a weak tropical depression south of Cuba, as sustained winds of 46\xc2\xa0mph (74\xc2\xa0km/h) were clocked in\n       <a href="/wiki/Santa_Cruz_del_Sur" title="Santa Cruz del Sur">\n        Santa Cruz del Sur\n       </a>\n       on September\xc2\xa07. However, damage from Frederic in Hispaniola and eastern Cuba remained minimal.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_2-16">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-2">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Frederic\'s landfall on western Cuba as a redeveloped tropical cyclone was much more significant than its first Cuban landfall. Sustained winds peaked at 52\xc2\xa0mph (84\xc2\xa0km/h) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Bah%C3%ADa_Honda,_Cuba" title="Bah\xc3\xada Honda, Cuba">\n        Bah\xc3\xada Honda, Cuba\n       </a>\n       on September\xc2\xa010.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_2-17">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-2">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Despite recent renovations at\n       <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Mart%C3%AD_International_Airport" title="Jos\xc3\xa9 Mart\xc3\xad International Airport">\n        Jos\xc3\xa9 Mart\xc3\xad International Airport\n       </a>\n       , the lack of a proper drainage system allowed rainfall to inundate the airport, stranding several heads of state that were scheduled to hold a\n       <a href="/wiki/Summit_(meeting)" title="Summit (meeting)">\n        summit\n       </a>\n       and disrupting air traffic to and from the airport for an entire week.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Grandiose_25-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Grandiose-25">\n         [23]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Heavy precipitation triggered numerous landslides, causing severe damage to infrastructure and over 250\xc2\xa0dwellings. Areas at risk for additional landslide activity were declared inhospitable, resulting in the evacuation of 1,200\xc2\xa0people.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Eval_26-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Eval-26">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Rainfall estimates in Cuba peaked at 5\xc2\xa0in (125\xc2\xa0mm).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FloridaBraces_27-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-FloridaBraces-27">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A lack of damage reports was evident from western Cuba following Frederic\'s landfall, though damage estimates were high.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_2-18">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-2">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n     <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Fredericbrpic.JPG" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         The Dauphin Island Bridge was destroyed by Hurricane Frederic, making it only above the normal level being observed. Five deaths were directly attributed to Frederic in the United States, four of which occurred inland: a person swept from a boat near\n       <a href="/wiki/Pensacola,_Florida" title="Pensacola, Florida">\n        Pensacola\n       </a>\n       was the only casualty along the coast.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_2-19">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-2">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In total, Frederic was responsible for $2.3\xc2\xa0billion (1979 USD; $7.5\xc2\xa0 billion 2015 USD) in damages. This made Frederic the costliest hurricane in the history of the United States at the time; the figure was not surpassed until\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hugo" title="Hurricane Hugo">\n        Hurricane Hugo\n       </a>\n       in 1989.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ushfrederic_28-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ushfrederic-28">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Frederic also dumped heavy rainfall across much of the eastern United States. Over a dozen tornadoes were also reported in Frederic\'s wake. However, these had minimal impact.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nhc3_29-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nhc3-29">\n         [27]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h4>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Florida">\n        Florida\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Frederic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Florida">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h4>\n      <p>\n       As Frederic was strengthening in the Gulf of Mexico, strong winds were reported in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Florida_Keys" title="Florida Keys">\n        Florida Keys\n       </a>\n       . A station on the Dry Tortugas recorded a 58\xc2\xa0mph (93\xc2\xa0km/h) wind gust on September\xc2\xa010; this was stronger than any other Florida wind gust measurement in association with Frederic. A station in Key West, Florida clocked at 43\xc2\xa0mph (69\xc2\xa0km/h) wind gust, coupled with sustained winds of 29\xc2\xa0mph (47\xc2\xa0km/h).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_2-20">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-2">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Tides in the Dry Tortugas peaked at 15\xc2\xa0ft (4.6\xc2\xa0m), roughly 12\xc2\xa0ft (6.7\xc2\xa0m) above normal.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FL-Alerted_18-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-FL-Alerted-18">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gainsadded_30-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Gainsadded-30">\n         [28]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Rainfall from the extremities of the hurricane reached\n       <a href="/wiki/South_Florida" title="South Florida">\n        South Florida\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KeysWatch_31-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-KeysWatch-31">\n         [29]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       with some rainfall totals exceeding 7\xc2\xa0in (180\xc2\xa0mm).\n       <sup class="reference" Alabama\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Frederic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Alabama">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h4>\n      <p>\n       Frederic made landfall at\n       <a href="/wiki/Dauphin_Island,_Alabama" title="Dauphin Island, Alabama">\n        Dauphin Island, Alabama\n       </a>\n       and continued across to a point on the Alabama/Mississippi state line near\n       <a href="/wiki/Bayou_La_Batre,_Alabama" title="Bayou La Batre, Alabama">\n        Bayou La Batre, Alabama\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32">\n        <a href="#cite_note-32">\n         [30]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Near-total property damage occurred along the Alabama coastline between\n       <a href="/wiki/Fort_Morgan,_Alabama" title="Fort Morgan, Alabama">\n        Fort Morgan\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gulf_Shores" title="Gulf Shores">\n        Gulf Shores\n       </a>\n       , the latter seeing 80% of its buildings completely destroyed. The causeway linking\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dauphin_Island" title="Dauphin Island">\n        Dauphin Island\n       </a>\n       to the mainland was swept away in many areas.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ushfrederic_28-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ushfrederic-28">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Alabama\'s second-largest city,\n       <a href="/wiki/Mobile,_Alabama" title="Mobile, Alabama">\n        Mobile, Alabama\n       </a>\n       suffered extensive damage as well. Wind damage was also severe, especially across southern Alabama. Hurricane-force gusts were felt as far inland as\n       <a href="/wiki/Choctaw_County,_Alabama" title="Choctaw County, Alabama">\n        Choctaw County\n       </a>\n       . Structural failure was widespread in the immediate landfall area with industrial, residential and governmental buildings as well as hospitals suffering heavy damage. Nearly 90% of the Mobile area lost electricity, and the historic City Hall experienced heavy roof damage. Many small beach houses were completely destroyed by high winds before the storm surge could add any effects.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ushfrederic_28-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ushfrederic-28">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nwsbirmingham_33-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nwsbirmingham-33">\n         [31]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Tree damage with broken limbs was extensive, leaving thousands of tall pine trees all tilted, leaning in the direction the wind had come. 11 Alabama counties were declared disaster areas.\n      </p>\n      <h4>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Mississippi">\n        Mississippi\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Frederic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Mississippi">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h4>\n      <p>\n       8 to 12\xc2\xa0inches of rain fell in\n       <a href="/wiki/Jackson_County,_Mississippi" title="Jackson County, Mississippi">\n        Jackson County, Mississippi\n       </a>\n       around Biloxi and Pascagoula. 16 Mississippi counties were declared disaster areas; the most in the United States. Hurricane force winds were felt as far northward as\n       <a href="/wiki/Meridian,_Mississippi" title="Meridian, Mississippi">\n        Meridian, Mississippi\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Frederic_in_Mississippi_34-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Frederic_in_Mississippi-34">\n         [32]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Canada">\n        Canada\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Frederic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Canada">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Frederic dropped heavy rain across Canada as an extratropical storm, peaking at 137\xc2\xa0mm (5.39\xc2\xa0in) near\n       <a href="/wiki/Ottawa" title="Ottawa">\n        Ottawa, Ontario\n       </a>\n       . Record precipitation was recorded at the\n       <a href="/wiki/J._S._Marshall_Radar_Observatory" title="J. S. Marshall Radar Observatory">\n        J. S. Marshall Radar Observatory\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/Sainte-Anne-de-Bellevue,_Quebec" title="Sainte-Anne-de-Bellevue, Quebec">\n        Sainte-Anne-de-Bellevue, Quebec\n       </a>\n       , which documented 77\xc2\xa0mm (3.03\xc2\xa0in) of rain on September\xc2\xa015. Rainfall spread as far northeast as the eastern coast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Labrador" title="Labrador">\n        Labrador\n       </a>\n       . Flooding occurred in eastern Ontario and portions of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Niagara_Peninsula" title="Niagara Peninsula">\n        Niagara Peninsula\n       </a>\n       , as noted in\n       <a href="/wiki/Cornwall,_Ontario" title="Cornwall, Ontario">\n        Cornwall\n       </a>\n       . In\n       <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">\n        Toronto\n       </a>\n       , the rains led to a seven-car\n       <a href="/wiki/Traffic_collision" title="Traffic collision">\n        traffic collision\n       </a>\n       and a separate car accident that injured two. Flooding also took place in southern\n       <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">\n        Quebec\n       </a>\n       , with floodwater submerging streets, basements, and underground parking lots in\n       <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">\n        Montreal\n       </a>\n       . Damage from southern Quebec reached\n       <a href="/wiki/Canadian_dollar" title="Canadian dollar">\n        C$\n       </a>\n       8.238\xc2\xa0million (US$7.095\xc2\xa0million).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Canada_7-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Canada-7">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">\n        Aftermath\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Frederic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Aftermath">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Fred1979aftmth.JPG">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="409" data-file-width="500" height="180" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Fred1979aftmth.JPG/220px-Fred1979aftmth.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Fred1979aftmth.JPG/330px-Fred1979aftmth.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/Fred1979aftmth.JPG/440px-Fred1979aftmth.JPG 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Fred1979aftmth.JPG" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Damage after Hurricane Frederic in Gulf Shores, Alabama.\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Eleven counties in Alabama, sixteen in Mississippi, and five in Florida were declared eligible for disaster aid.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In retrospect, Frederic has been credited with spurring redevelopment in Mobile and the surrounding Gulf Coast region. For example, in testimony before Congress in 1992, Robert Sheets (then the director of the\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">\n        National Hurricane Center\n       </a>\n       ), described the economic aftermath of Frederic:\n      </p>\n      <dl>\n       <dd>\n        "Prior to Hurricane Frederic, there was one\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Condominium_(housing)" title="Condominium (housing)">\n         condominium complex\n        </a>\n        on\n        <a href="/wiki/Gulf_Shores,_Alabama" title="Gulf Shores, Alabama">\n         Gulf Shores, Alabama\n        </a>\n        . Most of the homes were single, individual homes built behind the sand dunes. Today, where there used to be one condominium, there are now at least 104 complexes\xc2\xa0\xe2\x80\x93 not units, complexes\xc2\xa0\xe2\x80\x93 on Gulf Shores, Alabama."\n       </dd>\n      </dl>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Retirement">\n        Retirement\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Frederic&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Retirement">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       See also:\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricane names">\n        List of retired Atlantic hurricane names\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Because of extensive destruction, the name Frederic was retired, and will never again be used for an\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_tropical_cyclone" title="North Atlantic tropical cyclone">\n        Atlantic hurricane\n       </a>\n       . It was replaced with\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Fabian" title="Hurricane Fabian">\n        Fabian\n       </a>\n       in the\n       <a href="/wiki/1985_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1985 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1985 season\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Hurricanes\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_David" title="Hurricane David">\n        David\n       </a>\n       and Frederic were the first male hurricane names to be retired, as the practice of\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_naming#History" title="Tropical cyclone naming">\n        alternating male and female hurricane names\n       </a>\n       was introduced in the 1979 season.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">\n        See also\n
