        <a href="/wiki/Random_House" title="Random House">\n          Random House\n         </a>\n         )\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       <i>\n        <b>\n         Storm\n        </b>\n       </i>\n       is a novel written by\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/George_Rippey_Stewart" title="George Rippey Stewart">\n        George Rippey Stewart\n       </a>\n       and published in 1941. The book became a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Best-seller" title="Best-seller">\n        best-seller\n       </a>\n       and helped lead to the naming of\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        tropical cyclones\n       </a>\n       worldwide,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-islandnet_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-islandnet-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       even though the titular storm is\n       <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">\n        extratropical\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AOML_FAQ_J4_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-AOML_FAQ_J4-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The book is divided into twelve chapters: one chapter for each day of the storm\'s existence.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="toc">\n       <div class="toctitle">\n    </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Plot_summary">\n        Plot summary\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Storm_(novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot summary">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       In January 1935, a cyclone develops in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">\n        Pacific Ocean\n       </a>\n       near\n       <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">\n        Japan\n       </a>\n       , and becomes a significant storm as it moves toward\n       <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">\n        California\n       </a>\n       . The storm, named "Maria" by the (unnamed) Junior\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meteorologist" title="Meteorologist">\n        Meteorologist\n       </a>\n       at the\n       <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">\n        San Francisco\n       </a>\n       Weather Bureau Office, becomes a blizzard that threatens the\n       <a href="/wiki/Sierra_Nevada_(U.S.)" title="Sierra Nevada (U.S.)">\n        Sierra Nevada\n       </a>\n       range with snowfall amounts of 20\xc2\xa0feet (6.1\xc2\xa0m). The storm\'s beneficial effects include averting a\n       <a href="/wiki/Locust" title="Locust">\n        locust\n       </a>\n       plague and ending a drought. Its harmful effects include flooding a valley near Sacramento, endangering a plane, stalling a train, and leading to the deaths of 16 people. It spawns a new cyclone, which significantly affects\n       <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">\n        New York\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Legacy">\n        Legacy\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Storm_(novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Legacy">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       This book was the inspiration for the Lerner and Loewe song "\n       <a href="/wiki/They_Call_the_Wind_Maria" title="They Call the Wind Maria">\n        They Call the Wind Maria\n       </a>\n       ",\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-islandnet_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-islandnet-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       performed in the musical\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Paint_Your_Wagon_(musical)" title="Paint Your Wagon (musical)">\n         Paint Your Wagon\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       . It also prompted the\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Weather_Service" title="National Weather Service">\n        National Weather Service\n       </a>\n       to use personal names to designate storms.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <i>\n        Storm\n       </i>\n       was dramatized as\n       <i>\n        A Storm Called Maria\n       </i>\n       on the November 2, 1959 episode of ABC\'s\n       <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_anthology_television_series" title="Walt Disney anthology television series">\n        <i>\n         Walt Disney Presents\n        </i>\n       </a>\n       . Co-produced by Ken Nelson Productions, it blended newsreel footage of several different storms to represent the mega-storm in the novel and traced the storm from its origins in Japan to the coast of California. The cast included non-actors, among them the dam superintendent George Kritsky, the telephone lineman Walt Bowen, and the highway superintendent Leo Quinn.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       American singer, songwriter and producer\n       <a href="/wiki/Mariah_Carey" title="Mariah Carey">\n        Mariah Carey\n       </a>\n       was named after this storm.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Sequel">\n        Sequel\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Storm_(novel)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Sequel">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Stewart\'s novel\n       <i>\n        Fire\n       </i>\n       (1948) was a sequel to\n       <i>\n        Storm,\n       </i>\n       again featuring the life of the (former) Junior Meteorologist, who was now a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/World_War_Two" title="World War Two">\n        World War Two\n       </a>\n       veteran and had been promoted. Dealing with a California\n       <a href="/wiki/Wildfire" title="Wildfire">\n        wildfire\n       </a>\n       , it also used the backdrop of an environmental catastrophe to disclose the personal struggles and triumphs of individual human beings.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">\n        See also\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n
