studio album from Barbados-based pop group\n       <a href="/wiki/Cover_Drive" title="Cover Drive">\n        Cover Drive\n       </a>\n       , released on 7 May 2012 through\n       <a href="/wiki/Polydor_Records" title="Polydor Records">\n        Polydor Records\n       </a>\n       . The band enlisted a variety of producers such as Future Cut,\n       <a href="/wiki/Quiz_%26_Larossi" title="Quiz &amp; Larossi">\n        Quiz &amp; Larossi\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/J.R._Rotem" title="J.R. Rotem">\n        J.R. Rotem\n       </a>\n       , Aliby, Orange Factory and Steve Mac to work on the record. The album is rooted in\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/R%26B" title="R&amp;B">\n        R&amp;B\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pop_Music" title="Pop Music">\n        pop\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Reggae_fusion" title="Reggae fusion">\n        reggae fusion\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Hip_hop_music" title="Hip hop music">\n        hip hop\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Synthpop" title="Synthpop">\n        synthpop\n       </a>\n       genres.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       "Bajan Style" received generally positive reviews from music critics, who complimented Cover Drive\'s Barbados "summer"-feel input to British charts and described the band\'s songs as "catchy". Critics also applauded the group\'s lead singer Amanda Reifer for her easy ability to perform different song styles, and compared her to recording artist\n       <a href="/wiki/Rihanna" title="Rihanna">\n        Rihanna\n       </a>\n       , who, like the band, also originates from\n       <a href="/wiki/Barbados" title="Barbados">\n        Barbados\n       </a>\n       . Some compared the album to the work of\n       <a href="/wiki/No_Doubt" title="No Doubt">\n        No Doubt\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/The_Black_Eyed_Peas" title="The Black Eyed Peas">\n        The Black Eyed Peas\n       </a>\n       . The album charted at number 14 on the\n       <a href="/wiki/UK_Albums_Chart" title="UK Albums Chart">\n        UK Albums Chart\n       </a>\n       and on the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scottish_Albums_Chart" title="Scottish Albums Chart">\n        Scottish Albums Chart\n       </a>\n       at 28.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Four singles have been released from the album as of August 2012, three of which have charted within the top ten of the\n       <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">\n        UK Singles Chart\n       </a>\n       . "\n       <a href="/wiki/Lick_Ya_Down" title="Lick Ya Down">\n        Lick Ya Down\n       </a>\n       " was released as the lead single on 28 August 2011. After receiving heavy airplay on both the radio and music channels in the United Kingdom, the song peaked at number nine on the UK Singles Chart. The second single "\n       <a href="/wiki/Twilight_(Cover_Drive_song)" title="Twilight (Cover Drive song)">\n        Twilight\n       </a>\n       ", became the group\'s first single to chart in Ireland, and their first UK number one. "\n       <a href="/wiki/Sparks_(Cover_Drive_song)" title="Sparks (Cover Drive song)">\n        Sparks\n       </a>\n       ", the third single, charted at number four in the UK and 29 in Ireland. "\n       <a href="/wiki/Explode_(Cover_Drive_song)" title="Explode (Cover Drive song)">\n        Explode\n       </a>\n       " featuring vocals from British grime rapper\n       <a href="/wiki/Dappy" title="Dappy">\n        Dappy\n       </a>\n       served as the fourth single from the album and reached number 29 in the UK and 27 in Ireland.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n Whilst in the studio, they met American producer\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/J._R._Rotem" title="J. R. Rotem">\n        J. R. Rotem\n       </a>\n       who produced their debut single, "\n       <a href="/wiki/Lick_Ya_Down" title="Lick Ya Down">\n        Lick Ya Down\n       </a>\n       ", the band also worked with\n       <a href="/wiki/Quiz_%26_Larossi" title="Quiz &amp; Larossi">\n        Quiz &amp; Larossi\n       </a>\n       . They described their experience with her saying, "That was one of our first sessions and it was great to work with her. She\xe2\x80\x99s an amazing writer and vocalist. What an incredible voice. It was a feel-good song as we like to write feelgood music. The reason we didn\xe2\x80\x99t go with it was because it wasn\xe2\x80\x99t completely Cover Drive and we wanted the first album to really reflect us and represent us 100%. That song is amazing, but it wouldn\xe2\x80\x99t have worked on the album we\xe2\x80\x99ve made."\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The band described "Bajan Style" as "a beach party in Barbados" and "guaranteed sand in your shoes." Lead singer Amanda explained, "Our aim with the album was to have as much fun as we did in our basement. We definitely did and you can hear it in the songs. They\xe2\x80\x99re about being young, having fun and living in the Caribbean sun."\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Singles">\n        Singles\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bajan_Style&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Singles">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <ul>\n       <li>\n        "\n        <a href="/wiki/Lick_Ya_Down" title="Lick Ya Down">\n         Lick Ya Down\n        </a>\n        " was released on 28 August 2011 as the lead single from the album, and the band\'s debut single. After receiving heavy airplay on both radio and music channels in the United Kingdom, the song peaked at number nine on the\n        <a href="/wiki/UK_Singles_Chart" title="UK Singles Chart">\n         UK Singles Chart\n        </a>\n        and number three on the\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UK_R%26B_Chart" title="UK R&amp;B Chart">\n         UK R&amp;B Chart\n        </a>\n        .\n        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n         <a href="#cite_note-3">\n          [3]\n         </a>\n        </sup>\n        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n         <a href="#cite_note-4">\n          [4]\n         </a>\n        </sup>\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        "\n        <a href="/wiki/Twilight_(Cover_Drive_song)" title="Twilight (Cover Drive song)">\n         Twilight\n        </a>\n        " was released on 22 January 2012 as the second single. She continued saying, "Amanda has a pretty rare quality in the fact that she has the ability to flip from a ballad, such as their chart topper \'Twilight\', to a slightly sassier \'Rihanna style\' track, such as debut single \'Lick Ya Down\'. The tracks that stand out the most have to be title track \'Bajan Style\' - which definitely shows off the band\'s Barbados roots, and another slightly more upbeat track \'Headphones\', which emphasises the band\'s musical ability and raw talent. With lyrics such as \'Boy I want to put you on repeat, you will be the bass of my heartbeat...\', it\'s easy to compare Amanda to fellow Bajan beauty Rihanna - however, this band definitely have their own sound and the album has some sure fire, catchy hits!"\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Krystal Scanlon of\n       <a href="/wiki/Gigwise" title="Gigwise">\n        Gigwise\n       </a>\n       gave the album a positive review commenting, "Cover Drive\xe2\x80\x99s first three singles have already scored Barbadian band huge hit singles, so it was obvious the band have already got their career off to a good start." She continued saying, "The opening track \'Bajan Style Intro\' is just a perfect taster of what the album has to offer. With a hint of an early\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Black_Eyed_Peas" title="Black Eyed Peas">\n        Black Eyed Peas\n       </a>\n       tone to some of their songs, this album offers its listeners a variety of solid beats, catchy lyrics, and an overall good feeling. The background beat of \'Headphones\' holds a similar style of tune to\n       <a href="/wiki/No_Doubt" title="No Doubt">\n        No Doubt\n       </a>\n       \'s "\n       <a href="/wiki/Hey_Baby_(No_Doubt_song)" title="Hey Baby (No Doubt song)">\n        Hey Baby\n       </a>\n       " \xe2\x80\x93 so if you\xe2\x80\x99re a fan of the lovely\n       <a href="/wiki/Gwen_Stefani" title="Gwen Stefani">\n        Gwen Stefani\n       </a>\n       , this is definitely worth a try. \'Lick Ya Down\' rivals Rihanna for the radio-friendly sounds heard on her recent album, but that said, Cover Drive still have their own unique style, and this song is equally catchy, just like the rest of this album. With their front girl, Amanda being labeled the new Bajan Queen of the music scene, Rihanna is definitely going to have a little competition on her hands in the charts. \'Wrongside\' is another standout tune and has a vibe similar to\n       <a href="/wiki/Shaggy_(musician)" title="Shaggy (musician)">\n        Shaggy\n       </a>\n       \xe2\x80\x99s \'\n       <a href="/wiki/It_Wasn%27t_Me" title="It Wasn\'t Me">\n        It Wasn\'t Me\n       </a>\n       \' with the backing rhythm. While a lot of these songs do have a catchy beat, and could be considered similar, it\xe2\x80\x99s safe to say there is still a lot of variety amongst the songs \xe2\x80\x93 from calmer, slow songs to upbeat songs that make you want to dance. Cover Drive are definitely some way from their pop peak on this, their first album, but there\xe2\x80\x99s no doubt that this is going to set the bar high. It\xe2\x80\x99ll be interesting to see what song they release next because they certainly have a good set to choose from."\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Lewis Corner of\n       <a href="/wiki/Digital_Spy" title="Digital Spy">\n        Digital Spy\n       </a>\n       awarded the album three out of five stars, commenting, "From the off, it\'s clear the group have been influenced by other mixed quartets No Doubt and the Black Eyed Peas. As such, singer Amanda Reifer serves as the\n       <a href="/wiki/Fergie_(singer)" title="Fergie (singer)">\n        Fergie\n       </a>\n       /Gwen Stefani and takes the lead on the majority of the tracks. She can flip from butter-wouldn\'t-melt on mid-tempo love song \'Twilight\' to passionate and sassy on debut single \'Lick Ya Down\' in an instant - and she pulls it off with ease. However, it\'s \'Headphones\' that showcases the band\'s real ability as musicians. "Boy I want to put you on repeat/ You will be the bass of my heartbeat," Amanda swoons over bouncy beats and funk-driven guitar riffs, packed with more tropical flavour and sweetness than an ice-cold can of Lilt. \'Sparks\' proves they are more than capable of taking on the trickier ballads, producing an ear-snagging pop melody with a floating piano riff and radio-friendly vocals. Penultimate cut \'Hurricane\' injects a welcome burst of back-end energy after non-starters \'Wrongside\' and \'Can\'t Live In A World\' begin to pull things down. Like all good cricket matches, there may be quiet spells during the game, but it\'s only the final result that really matters. Luckily for Cover Drive, their very first innings contains some very memorable hits."\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Alex MacPherson of\n       <a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">\n        The Guardian\n       </a>\n       awarded the album three out of five stars, saying "The summer dancehall hit, once an annual staple of the British charts, has been on a hiatus in recent years \xe2\x80\x93 as Bajan four-piece Cover Drive have spotted.
