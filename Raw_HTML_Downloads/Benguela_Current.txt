</div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <b>\n        Benguela Current\n       </b>\n       <span class="nowrap">\n        <span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt">\n         <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">\n          /\n          <span style="border-bottom:1px dotted">\n           <span title="\'b\' in \'buy\'">\n            b\n           </span>\n           <span title="/\xc9\x9b/: \'e\' in \'dress\'">\n            \xc9\x9b\n           </span>\n           <span title="/\xc5\x8b/: \'ng\' in \'sing\'">\n            \xc5\x8b\n           </span>\n           <span title="/\xcb\x88/: primary stress follows">\n            \xcb\x88\n           </span>\n           <span title="/\xc9\xa1/: \'g\' in \'guy\'">\n            \xc9\xa1\n           </span>\n           <span title="\'w\' in \'wind\'">\n            w\n           </span>\n           <span title="/e\xc9\xaa/: \'a\' in \'face\'">\n            e\xc9\xaa\n           </span>\n           <span title="\'l\' in \'lie\'">\n            l\n           </span>\n           <span title="/\xc9\x99/: \'a\' in \'about\'">\n            \xc9\x99\n           </span>\n          </span>\n          /\n         </a>\n        </span>\n       </span>\n       is the broad, northward flowing\n       <a href="/wiki/Ocean_current" title="Ocean current">\n        ocean current\n       </a>\n       that forms the eastern portion of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">\n        South Atlantic\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Ocean_gyre" title="Ocean gyre">\n        Ocean gyre\n       </a>\n       . The current extends from roughly\n       <a href="/wiki/Cape_Point" title="Cape Point">\n        Cape Point\n       </a>\n       in the south, to the position of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Angola" title="Angola">\n        Angola\n       </a>\n       -\n       <a href="/wiki/Benguela" title="Benguela">\n        Benguela\n       </a>\n       front in the north, at around 16\xc2\xb0S. The current is driven by the prevailing south easterly trade winds. Inshore of the Benguela Current proper, the south easterly winds drive\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coastal_upwelling" title="Coastal upwelling">\n        coastal upwelling\n       </a>\n       , forming the Benguela Upwelling System. The cold, nutrient rich waters that upwell from around 200\xe2\x80\x93300 m depth in turn fuel high rates of phytoplankton growth, and sustain the productive Benguela ecosystem.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="toc">\n       <div    </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Benguela_and_Agulhas_Currents.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="5368" data-file-width="4920" height="273" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Benguela_and_Agulhas_Currents.jpg/250px-Benguela_and_Agulhas_Currents.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Benguela_and_Agulhas_Currents.jpg/375px-Benguela_and_Agulhas_Currents.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Benguela_and_Agulhas_Currents.jpg/500px-Benguela_and_Agulhas_Currents.jpg 2x" width="250"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Benguela_and_Agulhas_Currents.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         The courses of the warm Agulhas current (red) along the east coast of South Africa, and the cold Benguela current (blue) along the west coast, originating in the\n         <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">\n          Indian Ocean\n         </a>\n         and\n         <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">\n          Atlantic Ocean\n         </a>\n         respectively. Note that the Benguela current does not originate from Antarctic waters in the South Atlantic Ocean, but from upwelling of water from the cold depths of the Atlantic Ocean against the west coast of the continent. The two currents do not "meet" anywhere along the south coast of Africa.\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Source waters for the Benguela include cold upwelled waters from the depths of the Atlantic Ocean close inshore, joined further off-shore by nutrient poor water that has crossed the Southern Atlantic from South America as part of\n       <a href="/wiki/South_Atlantic_Gyre" title="South Atlantic Gyre">\n        South Atlantic Gyre\n       </a>\n       . Eddies from the warm South Indian Ocean\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Agulhas_current" title="Agulhas current">\n        Agulhas current\n       </a>\n       along South Africa\'s east coast do round the\n       <a href="/wiki/Cape_of_Good_Hope" title="Cape of Good Hope">\n        Cape of Good Hope\n       </a>\n       from time to time to join the Bengulela current. The Benguela current is 200 to 300\xc2\xa0km wide and widens further as it flows north and northwest. Its western, seaward edge is well-defined, with many temporary and seasonal eddies and meanders. There is however a well-defined thermal front between the waters associated with the Benguela Upwelling System and those of the eastward flowing Atlantic currents which are not deflected northward by the African continent. The icy Benguela and the warm, south-flowing\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Agulhas_current" title="Agulhas current">\n        Agulhas current\n       </a>\n       do not meet off the\n       <a href="/wiki/Cape_of_Good_Hope" title="Cape of Good Hope">\n        Cape of Good Hope\n       </a>\n       (see diagram on the right, above), but there is a body of water off the South African south coast, east and particularly west of\n       <a href="/wiki/Cape_Agulhas" title="Cape Agulhas">\n        Cape Agulhas\n       </a>\n       that consists of eddies from both currents, so that off-shore water temperatures along the south coast of Africa vary chaotically.\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Upwelling_image1.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="337" data-file-width="514" height="164" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fd/Upwelling_image1.jpg/250px-Upwelling_image1.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fd/Upwelling_image1.jpg/375px-Upwelling_image1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fd/Upwelling_image1.jpg/500px-Upwelling_image1.jpg 2x" width="250"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Upwelling_image1.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         The red areas show major upwelling areas. The Benguela Current is on the southwest coast of Africa.\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Upwelling_and_primary_production">\n        Upwelling and primary production\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Benguela_Current&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Upwelling and primary production">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Agulhas_chla.png">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1400" data-file-width="950" height="324" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Agulhas_chla.png/220px-Agulhas_chla.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Agulhas_chla.png/330px-Agulhas_chla.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/37/Agulhas_chla.png/440px-Agulhas_chla.png 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Agulhas_chla.png" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Mean chlorophyll-a concentration map of the oceans surrounding Southern Africa. Note the very high concentrations along the west coast, due to the upwelling of mineral rich water from the cold depths the South Atlantic Ocean, forming the Benguela Current.\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Northward winds along the coast result in\n       <a href="/wiki/Ekman_transport" title="Ekman transport">\n        Ekman transport\n       </a>\n       offshore and\n       <a href="/wiki/Upwelling" title="Upwelling">\n        upwelling\n       </a>\n       of nutrient rich deep water to the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Euphotic_zone" title="Euphotic zone">\n        euphotic zone\n       </a>\n       . The intensity of the upwelling event is determined by wind strength.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Variations in wind strength cause pulses of upwelling, which propagate to the south along the coast with speeds of 5 to 8\xc2\xa0m/s. The pulses are similar to a\n       <a href="/wiki/Kelvin_wave" title="Kelvin wave">\n        Kelvin wave\n       </a>\n       , except on a scale of 30 to 60\xc2\xa0km instead of 1000\xc2\xa0km, and can propagate around the cape depending on wind systems.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Pulses of upwelling induce biological production. In the Benguela system,\n       <a href="/wiki/Phytoplankton" title="Phytoplankton">\n        phytoplankton\n       </a>\n       growth requires a period of upwelling followed by a period of stratification and relatively calm waters. The phytoplankton bloom usually lags the upwelling event by 1 to 4 days and blooms for 4 to 10 days. In order for\n       <a href="/wiki/Zooplankton" title="Zooplankton">\n        zooplankton\n       </a>\n       to have a continuous food supply, the phytoplankton blooms must not occur too far apart. Pulses of upwelling in the Benguela system regularly have a duration of 10 days, an optimal period for biological production. It is estimated that the annual new production in the Benguela system is 4.7 \xc3\x97 10^13 gC/y, making the Benguela system 30 to 65 times more productive per unit area than the global ocean average.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       While upwelling promotes abundant primary and secondary production in the upper parts of the water column and near the coast, deeper waters with limited oxygen exchange create hypoxic areas called\n       <a href="/wiki/Oxygen_minimum_zone" title="Oxygen minimum zone">\n        oxygen minimum zones\n       </a>\n       at the coastal shelf and upper coastal slope. The Benguela oxygen minimum zone starts around a depth of 100 m and is a few hundred meters thick. Bacteria that use sulpher rather than oxygen reside in the oxygen minimum zone.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The most abundant fishes in the Benguela system are\n       <i>\n        Sardinops\n       </i>\n       and\n       <i>\n        Engraulis\n       </i>\n       .\n       <i>\n        Sardinops ocelata\n       </i>\n       (\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pilchard" title="Pilchard">\n        pilchard\n       </a>\n       ) was intensely fished beginning in the 1950s and peaking in 1968 with landings over 1.3 million tons. Since then, the\n       <i>\n        Sardinops\n       </i>\n       fishery has declined and the\n       <i>\n        Engraulis capensis\n       </i>\n       (\n       <a href="/wiki/Anchovy" title="Anchovy">\n        anchovy\n       </a>\n       ) fishery has taken over.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mann_6-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Mann-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span id="Benguela_Ni\xc3\xb1o">\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Benguela_Ni.C3.B1o">\n        Benguela Ni\xc3\xb1o\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Benguela_Current&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Benguela Ni\xc3\xb1o">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Similar to the Pacific\n       <a href="/wiki/El_Ni%C3%B1o" title="El Ni\xc3\xb1o">\n        El Ni\xc3\xb1o\n       </a>\n       , a thick slab of warm, nutrient poor water enters the northern part of the Benguela upwelling system off the\n       <a href="/wiki/Namibia" title="Namibia">\n        Namibia\n       </a>\n       coast about once per decade.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mann_6-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Mann-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       During the Benguela Ni\xc3\xb1o, warm, salty waters from the\n       <a href="/wiki/Angola_Current" title="Angola Current">\n        Angola Current\n       </a>\n       move southward, from 15\xc2\xb0S to as far as 25\xc2\xb0S. This slab of warm salty water extends to 150\xc2\xa0km offshore and to 50 m depth. Heavy rains, changes in fish abundance, and temporal proximity to the Pacific El Ni\xc3\xb1o have been observed; however, the causes and effects of the Benguela Ni\xc3\xb1o are not well understood.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mann_6-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Mann-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       One research team has shown that the Benguela Ni\xc3\xb1o is caused by winds in the west-central equatorial\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">\n        Atlantic Ocean\n       </a>\n       that propagate as subsurface sea temperature anomalies to the African coast.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A recent study has demonstrated the importance of local winds in the development of the Benguela Ni\xc3\xb1o off the coast of Namibia and Angola. This local process together with the remote signal from the equatorial regions form the basis of the formation mechanism in which both processes sometimes reinforce each other.\n       <sup
