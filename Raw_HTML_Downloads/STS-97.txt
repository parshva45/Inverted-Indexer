 </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=STS-97&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Mission highlights">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Shuttle_Endeavour_on_launchpad.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2670" data-file-width="1752" height="229" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Shuttle_Endeavour_on_launchpad.jpg/150px-Shuttle_Endeavour_on_launchpad.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Shuttle_Endeavour_on_launchpad.jpg/225px-Shuttle_Endeavour_on_launchpad.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Shuttle_Endeavour_on_launchpad.jpg/300px-Shuttle_Endeavour_on_launchpad.jpg 2x" width="150"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Shuttle_Endeavour_on_launchpad.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Endeavour" title="Space Shuttle Endeavour">\n          <i>\n           Endeavour\n          </i>\n         </a>\n         on Launch Pad 39-B before STS-97.\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       During the 11-day mission, the primary objective was completed, which was to deliver and connect the first set of U.S.-provided\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ISS_Solar_Arrays" title="ISS Solar Arrays">\n        solar arrays\n       </a>\n       to the\n       <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">\n        International Space Station\n       </a>\n       . The astronauts completed three\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extra-vehicular_activity" title="Extra-vehicular activity">\n        spacewalks\n       </a>\n       , during which they prepared a docking port for arrival of the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Destiny_Laboratory_Module" title="Destiny Laboratory Module">\n        Destiny Laboratory Module\n       </a>\n       , installed Floating Potential Probes to measure\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Electrical_potential" title="Electrical potential">\n        electrical potential\n       </a>\n       surrounding the station, installed a camera cable outside the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unity_Module" title="Unity Module">\n        Unity Module\n       </a>\n       , and transferred supplies, equipment and refuse between\n       <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Endeavour" title="Space Shuttle Endeavour">\n        <i>\n         Endeavour\n        </i>\n       </a>\n       and the station.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NASA_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NASA-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       On Flight Day 3, Commander\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brent_W._Jett,_Jr." title="Brent W. Jett, Jr.">\n        Brent Jett\n       </a>\n       linked\n       <i>\n        Endeavour\n       </i>\n       to the ISS while 230\n       <a href="/wiki/Mile" title="Mile">\n        statute miles\n       </a>\n       above northeast\n       <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">\n        Kazakhstan\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The successful checkout of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Extravehicular_Mobility_Unit" title="Extravehicular Mobility Unit">\n        Extravehicular Mobility Units\n       </a>\n       (EMUs), the\n       <a href="/wiki/Simplified_Aid_For_EVA_Rescue" title="Simplified Aid For EVA Rescue">\n        Simplified Aid For EVA Rescue\n       </a>\n       (SAFER) units, the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Remote_Manipulator_System" title="Remote Manipulator System">\n        Canadarm\n       </a>\n       (RMS), the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orbiter_Space_Vision_System" title="Orbiter Space Vision System">\n        Orbiter Space Vision System\n       </a>\n       (OSVS) and the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orbiter_Docking_System" title="Orbiter Docking System">\n        Orbiter Docking System\n       </a>\n       (ODS) were all completed nominally. Also, the ODS centerline camera was installed with no misalignment noted.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NASA_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NASA-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       From inside\n       <i>\n        Endeavour\n       </i>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Space_Agency" title="Canadian Space Agency">\n        Canadian\n       </a>\n       Mission Specialist\n       <a href="/wiki/Marc_Garneau" title="Marc Garneau">\n        Marc Garneau\n       </a>\n       used the\n       <a href="/wiki/Canadarm" title="Canadarm">\n        Canadarm\n       </a>\n       to remove the\n       <a href="/wiki/Integrated_Truss_Structure" title="Integrated Truss Structure">\n        P6 truss\n       </a>\n       from the payload bay, maneuvering it into an overnight park position to warm its components. Mission Specialists\n       <a href="/wiki/Joseph_R._Tanner" title="Joseph R. Tanner">\n        Joseph Tanner\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Carlos_I._Noriega" title="Carlos I. Noriega">\n        Carlos Noriega\n       </a>\n       moved through\n       <i>\n        Endeavour\'\n       </i>\n       s docking tunnel and opened the hatch to the ISS docking port to leave supplies and computer hardware on the doorstep of the Station. On flight day 4, the\n       <a href="/wiki/Expedition_1" title="Expedition 1">\n        Expedition 1\n       </a>\n       Commander\n       <a href="/wiki/William_Shepherd" title="William Shepherd">\n        William Shepherd\n       </a>\n       , Pilot\n       <a href="/wiki/Yuri_Gidzenko" title="Yuri Gidzenko">\n        Yuri Gidzenko\n       </a>\n       and Flight Engineer\n       <a href="/wiki/Sergei_Krikalev" title="Sergei Krikalev">\n        Sergei Krikalev\n       </a>\n       \xe2\x80\x93 entered the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unity_Module" title="Unity Module">\n        Unity Module\n       </a>\n       for the first time and retrieved the items left for them.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NASA_1-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NASA-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       At 09:36\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_American_Eastern_Time_Zone" title="North American Eastern Time Zone">\n        EST\n       </a>\n       on 8 December 2000 the crew paid the first visit to the Expedition 1 crew residing in the space station. Until then the shuttle and the station had kept one hatch closed to maintain respective atmospheric pressures, allowing the shuttle crew to conduct their spacewalks and mission goals. After a welcome ceremony and briefing, the eight spacefarers conducted structural tests of the station and its\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ISS_Solar_Arrays" title="ISS Solar Arrays">\n        solar arrays\n       </a>\n       , transferred equipment, supplies and refuse back and forth between the spacecraft, and checked out the television camera cable installed by Tanner and Noriega for the upcoming mission.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NASA_1-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NASA-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       On 9 December 2000 the two crews completed final transfers of supplies to the station and other items being returned to Earth. The\n       <i>\n        Endeavour\n       </i>\n       crew bade farewell to the Expedition 1 crew at 10:51 EST and closed the hatches between the spacecraft. After being docked together for 6 days, 23 hours and 13 minutes,\n       <i>\n        Endeavour\n       </i>\n       undocked from the station at 14:13 EST. Piloted by Michael Bloomfield, it then made an hour-long, tail-first circle of the station. The undocking took place 235\n       <a href="/wiki/Mile" title="Mile">\n        statute miles\n       </a>\n       above the border of\n       <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">\n        Kazakhstan\n       </a>\n       and China. The final separation burn took place near the northeast coast of South America.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NASA_1-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NASA-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       STS-97 was the 15th flight of\n       <i>\n        Endeavour\n       </i>\n       and the 101st Space Shuttle mission.\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:212px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:STS-97_ISS.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="4100" data-file-width="4078" height="211" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/STS-97_ISS.jpg/210px-STS-97_ISS.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/STS-97_ISS.jpg/315px-STS-97_ISS.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bd/STS-97_ISS.jpg/420px-STS-97_ISS.jpg 2x" width="210"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:STS-97_ISS.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Taken from\n         <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Endeavour" title="Space Shuttle Endeavour">\n          <i>\n           Endeavour\n          </i>\n         </a>\n         on 9 December 2000 shortly after undocking. The new\n         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ISS_Solar_Arrays" title="ISS Solar Arrays">\n          solar arrays\n         </a>\n         are visible at the top.\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Wake-up_calls">\n        Wake-up calls\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span
