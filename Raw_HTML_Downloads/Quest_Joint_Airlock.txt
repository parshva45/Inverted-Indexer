</div>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <i>\n        Quest\n       </i>\n       Airlock consists of two segments, the "Equipment lock" that stores spacesuits and equipment, and the "Crew Lock" from which astronauts can exit into space.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       It was derived from the Space Shuttle airlock, although it was significantly modified to waste less atmospheric gas when used. It was attached to the starboard\n       <a href="/wiki/Common_Berthing_Mechanism" title="Common Berthing Mechanism">\n        CBM\n       </a>\n       of the\n       <i>\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Unity_Module" title="Unity Module">\n         Unity\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       during\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-104" title="STS-104">\n        STS-104\n       </a>\n       . It has mountings for four high-pressure gas tanks, two containing\n       <a href="/wiki/Oxygen" title="Oxygen">\n        oxygen\n       </a>\n       and two containing\n       <a href="/wiki/Nitrogen" title="Nitrogen">\n        nitrogen\n       </a>\n       , which provides for atmospheric replenishment to the American side of the space station, most specifically for the gas lost after a hatch opening during a space walk.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <i>\n        Quest\n       </i>\n       was necessary because American suits will not fit through a Russian airlock hatch and have different components, fittings, and connections. The airlock is designed to contain equipment that can work with both types of spacesuits, however, it is currently\n       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">\n        [\n        <i>\n         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers">\n          <span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (October 2011)">\n           when?\n          </span>\n         </a>\n        </i>\n        ]\n       </sup>\n       only able to host American spacewalks because the equipment necessary to work with Russian space suits has not been launched yet, which required the\n       <a href="/wiki/Expedition_9" title="Expedition 9">\n        Expedition 9\n       </a>\n       crew to take a circuitous route to a worksite because of problems with the American space suits.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Camp-out_procedure">\n        Camp-out procedure\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Quest_Joint_Airlock&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Camp-out procedure">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       <i>\n        Quest\n       </i>\n       provides an environment where astronauts can "camp out" before a spacewalk in a reduced-nitrogen atmosphere to purge nitrogen from their bloodstream and avoid\n       <a href="/wiki/Decompression_sickness" title="Decompression sickness">\n        decompression sickness\n       </a>\n       in the low-pressure (5\xc2\xa0psi, 34\xc2\xa0kPa) pure-oxygen atmosphere of the spacesuit.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tanner_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tanner-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The previous method of preparing for spacewalks involved breathing pure oxygen for several hours prior to an EVA to purge the body of nitrogen. In April 2006,\n       <a href="/wiki/Expedition_12" title="Expedition 12">\n        Expedition 12\n       </a>\n       Commander\n       <a href="/wiki/William_S._McArthur" title="William S. McArthur">\n        Bill McArthur\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Expedition_13" title="Expedition 13">\n        Expedition 13\n       </a>\n       flight engineer\n       <a href="/wiki/Jeffrey_Williams_(astronaut)" title="Jeffrey Williams (astronaut)">\n        Jeffrey Williams\n       </a>\n       tested a new method of preparing for spacewalks by "camping out", or spending the night, in the\n       <i>\n        Quest\n       </i>\n       Airlock.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-airlock_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-airlock-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In the chamber, the pressure was reduced from the normal 14.7 to 10.2\xc2\xa0psi (101 to 70\xc2\xa0kPa).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tanner_2-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tanner-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Four hours into the Expedition 13 crew\'s sleep period, an error tone prompted mission controllers to cut short the activity, but the test was still deemed a success. American spacewalk activities thereafter have employed the "camp-out" pre-breathing technique.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tanner_2-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tanner-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-airlock_3-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-airlock-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup These tanks provide a replenishable source of gas to the atmosphere control and supply system and 900\xc2\xa0psi (6.2\xc2\xa0MPa) oxygen for recharging the space suits (EMUs).\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Recharging the high-pressure tanks was accomplished by the Space Shuttle fleet until its retirement. When an orbiter was docked to the station\'s Pressurized Mating Adapters (PMA-2 or PMA-3), oxygen was routed through pressure lines from the PMAs to the\n       <i>\n        Quest\n       </i>\n       Airlock.
