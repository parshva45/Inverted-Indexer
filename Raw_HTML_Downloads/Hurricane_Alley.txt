Many\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane" title="Hurricane">\n        hurricanes\n       </a>\n       form within this area. The\n       <a href="/wiki/Sea_surface_temperature" title="Sea surface temperature">\n        sea surface temperature\n       </a>\n       of the Atlantic in Hurricane Alley has grown slightly warmer over the past decades\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-alleyheatsup_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-alleyheatsup-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       . this trend would lead to an increase in\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        hurricane\n       </a>\n       activity.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-earthobs_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-earthobs-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       See Effects of Climate Change below.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n  </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="How_hurricanes_form">\n        How hurricanes form\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Alley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: How hurricanes form">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main article:\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclogenesis" title="Tropical cyclogenesis">\n        Tropical cyclogenesis\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Alley.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1920" data-file-width="2560" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/Hurricane_Alley.jpg/220px-Hurricane_Alley.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/Hurricane_Alley.jpg/330px-Hurricane_Alley.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f0/Hurricane_Alley.jpg/440px-Hurricane_Alley.jpg 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Alley.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         The warm waters of Hurricane Alley are depicted in orange\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Hurricanes form over\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical" title="Tropical">\n        tropical\n       </a>\n       waters in areas of high humidity, light winds, and warm sea surface temperatures. These areas which were described above are usually between the latitudes of 8\xc2\xb0 and 20\xc2\xb0 north.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The perfect temperature for a hurricane is approximately 26\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0C. This temperature has been set as a standard. If the water is colder the hurricane will most likely weaken, but if the waters are warmer rapid growth can occur.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The area between 10\xc2\xb0 and 20\xc2\xb0N create the most hurricanes in a given season because of the warmer temperatures. Hurricanes do not form outside this range because the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coriolis_effect" title="Coriolis effect">\n        Coriolis effect\n       </a>\n       is not strong enough to create the tight circulation needed and above this range the temperatures are too cool.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The waters are only at the necessary temperatures from July until Mid-October. In the Atlantic this is the height of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="Atlantic hurricane season">\n        season\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Since hurricanes rely on\n       <a href="/wiki/Sea_surface_temperature" title="Sea surface temperature">\n        sea surface temperature\n       </a>\n       , sometimes an active season in the beginning becomes quiet later. This is because the hurricanes are so strong that they churn the waters and bring colder waters up from the deep. This creates an area of the sea the size of the hurricane, which has cooler waters, which can be 5-10\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0C lower than before the hurricane. When a new hurricane moves over the cooler waters they have no fuel to continue to thrive, so they weaken or even die out.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Effects_of_climate_change">\n        Effects of climate change\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Alley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Effects of climate change">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       The relationship of "hurricane alley" to\n       <a href="/wiki/Climate_change" title="Climate change">\n        climate change\n       </a>\n       is unclear. Recent scientific evidence suggests that hurricane intensity may be increasing due to warmer tropical sea surface temperature, but the connection to Atlantic hurricane frequency is less conclusive.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       There is a debate as to whether climate change is causing more severe storms, but more research is needed into hurricane dynamics.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Historical_trends">\n        Historical trends\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Alley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Historical trends">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       According to an\n       <a href="/wiki/Azores_High" title="Azores High">\n        Azores High\n       </a>\n       hypothesis of geographer Kam-biu Liu, an anti-phase pattern is expected to exist between the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">\n        Gulf of Mexico\n       </a>\n       coast and the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlantic_Seaboard" title="Atlantic Seaboard">\n        North American Atlantic coast\n       </a>\n       . During the quiescent periods (3000\xe2\x80\x931400 BC, and 1000 AD to present), a more northeasterly position of the Azores High would result in more hurricanes being steered towards the Atlantic coast. During the hyperactive period (1400 BC to 1000 AD), more hurricanes were steered towards the Gulf coast as the Azores High was shifted to a more southwesterly position near the Caribbean.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Liu1999_8-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Liu1999-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LiuFearn2000_9-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-LiuFearn2000-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Such a displacement of the Azores High is consistent with paleoclimatic evidence that shows an abrupt onset of a drier climate in\n       <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">\n        Haiti\n       </a>\n       around 3200 years BP,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Higuera-Gundy1999_10-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Higuera-Gundy1999-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and a change towards more humid conditions in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Great_Plains" title="Great Plains">\n        Great Plains\n       </a>\n       during the late-\n       <a href="/wiki/Holocene" title="Holocene">\n        Holocene\n       </a>\n       as more moisture was pumped up the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mississippi_Valley" title="Mississippi Valley">\n        Mississippi Valley\n       </a>\n       through the Gulf coast. Preliminary data from the northern Atlantic coast seem to support the Azores High hypothesis. A 3,000-year proxy record from a coastal lake in\n       <a href="/wiki/Cape_Cod" title="Cape Cod">\n        Cape Cod\n       </a>\n       suggests that hurricane activity has increased significantly during the past 500\xe2\x80\x931,000 years, just as the Gulf coast was amid a quiescent period of the last millennium.\n       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">\n        [\n        <i>\n         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">\n          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources.
