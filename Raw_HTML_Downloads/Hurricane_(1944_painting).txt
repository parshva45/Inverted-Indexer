     <p>\n       <i>\n        <b>\n         Hurricane\n        </b>\n       </i>\n       is a 1944\n       <a href="/wiki/Oil_painting" title="Oil painting">\n        oil painting\n       </a>\n       by American artist\n       <a href="/wiki/John_Marin" title="John Marin">\n        John Marin\n       </a>\n       , located in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis_Museum_of_Art" title="Indianapolis Museum of Art">\n        Indianapolis Museum of Art\n       </a>\n       , which is in\n       <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis" title="Indianapolis">\n        Indianapolis\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Indiana" title="Indiana">\n        Indiana\n       </a>\n       . He used quick brushstrokes and thickly daubed paint to depict a turbulent ocean such as he experienced near his summer home in\n       <a href="/wiki/Maine" title="Maine">\n        Maine\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lee_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-lee-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="toc">\n       <div class="toctitle">\n        <h2>\n         Contents\n        </h2>\n       </div>\n       <ul>\n        <li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1">\n         <a class="toctext">\n           External links\n          </span>\n         </a>\n        </li>\n       </ul>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">\n        Description\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_(1944_painting)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Although Marin is best known for his\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Watercolor" title="Watercolor">\n        watercolors\n       </a>\n       , beginning in the 1930s he developed an interest in the more expressive brushwork and rugged quality of oil paint. Marin was influenced by\n       <a href="/wiki/Cubism" title="Cubism">\n        Cubism\n       </a>\n       , with its flattened spaces and angularity. He honed a style of frenetic brushwork to create this dynamic image of water, wind, and clouds. He was so successful in his recreation of nature that a sailor purportedly declared it the first time he had "really seen a sea painted as the sea really is."\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lee_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-lee-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Historical_information">\n        Historical information\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_(1944_painting)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Historical information">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       <i>\n        Hurricane\n       </i>\n       was painted the same year the\n       <a href="/wiki/1944_Great_Atlantic_hurricane" title="1944 Great Atlantic hurricane">\n        Great Atlantic hurricane\n       </a>\n       smashed into Maine.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-green_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-green-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Location_history">\n        Location history\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_(1944_painting)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Location history">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       <i>\n        Hurricane\n       </i>\n       was first displayed in 1944 in the famous\n       <a href="/wiki/Alfred_Stieglitz#The_Intimate_Gallery_and_An_American_Place_.281925.E2.80.931937.29" title="Alfred Stieglitz">\n        An American Place\n       </a>\n       gallery. It was borrowed from the IMA to be part of an 1987 retrospective of Marin\'s work.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In 2011,\n       <i>\n        Hurricane\n       </i>\n       was loaned out again for a traveling exhibit called "John Marin: Modernism at Midcentury," of which it was a highlight.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       This exhibition took it to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Portland_Museum_of_Art" title="Portland Museum of Art">\n        Portland Museum of Art\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/Portland,_Maine" title="Portland, Maine">\n        Portland, Maine\n       </a>\n       ; the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Amon_Carter_Museum" title="Amon Carter Museum">\n        Amon Carter Museum\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/Fort_Worth,_Texas" title="Fort Worth, Texas">\n        Fort Worth, Texas\n       </a>\n       , and the\n       <a href="/wiki/Addison_Gallery_of_American_Art" title="Addison Gallery of American Art">\n        Addison Gallery of American Art\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/Andover,_Massachusetts" title="Andover, Massachusetts">\n        Andover, Massachusetts\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisition">\n        Acquisition\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_(1944_painting)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Acquisition">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Mrs. Caroline Marmon Fesler acquired\n       <i>\n        Hurricane\n       </i>\n       the same year it was painted, from Marin\'s long-time friend and art dealer,\n       <a href="/wiki/Alfred_Stieglitz" title="Alfred Stieglitz">\n        Alfred Stieglitz\n       </a>\n       . He congratulated Mrs. Fesler on her purchase, writing "The\n       <i>\n        Hurricane\n       </i>\n       is certainly a masterpiece, Every one agrees with every one else as to that." It was donated to the IMA as part of her estate in 1961, and was given the accession number 61.42.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ima_6-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ima-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">\n        See also\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span
