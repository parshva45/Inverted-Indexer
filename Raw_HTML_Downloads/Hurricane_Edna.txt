</tr>\n       <tr style="display:none">\n        <td colspan="2">\n        </td>\n       </tr>\n      </table>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        Hurricane Edna\n       </b>\n       was a deadly and destructive\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Major_hurricane" title="Major hurricane">\n        major hurricane\n       </a>\n       that impacted the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_East_Coast" title="United States East Coast">\n        United States East Coast\n       </a>\n       in September of the\n       <a href="/wiki/1954_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1954 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1954 Atlantic hurricane season\n       </a>\n       . It was one of two hurricanes to strike Massachusetts in that year, the other being\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Carol" title="Hurricane Carol">\n        Hurricane Carol\n       </a>\n       . The fifth\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        tropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       and storm of the season, as well as the fourth hurricane and second\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale#Category_3" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">\n        major hurricane\n       </a>\n       , Edna developed from a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">\n        tropical wave\n       </a>\n       on September 2. Moving towards the north-northwest, Edna skirted the northern\n       <a href="/wiki/Leeward_Islands" title="Leeward Islands">\n        Leeward Islands\n       </a>\n       as a tropical depression before turning more towards the west. The depression attained tropical storm status to the east of\n       <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">\n        Puerto Rico\n       </a>\n       and strengthened further to reach hurricane status by September 7. The storm\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" title="Rapid deepening">\n        rapidly intensified\n       </a>\n       and reached its peak intensity of 125\xc2\xa0mph (205\xc2\xa0km/h) north of the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">\n        Bahamas\n       </a>\n       before weakening to Category 2 status near\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">\n        landfall\n       </a>\n       in Massachusetts on September 11. Edna transitioned into an\n       <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">\n        extratropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Canada" title="Atlantic Canada">\n        Atlantic Canada\n       </a>\n       before its remnants dissipated in the northern Atlantic.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Edna caused 20 fatalities throughout its lifetime as a tropical cyclone, as well as a moderate amount of damage. It first caused rainfall-induced flooding in Puerto Rico, and it later brushed the Bahamas. High waves affected the coastline of North Carolina. Edna resulted in the heaviest day of rainfall in New York City in 45\xc2\xa0years, while strong waves cut off\n       <a href="/wiki/Montauk,_New_York" title="Montauk, New York">\n        Montauk\n       </a>\n       from the remainder of\n       <a href="/wiki/Long_Island" title="Long Island">\n        Long Island\n       </a>\n       . There were six highway deaths in the state, and $1.5\xc2\xa0million in crop damage. There were widespread evacuations in southern\n       <a href="/wiki/New_England" title="New England">\n        New England\n       </a>\n       , after\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Carol" title="Hurricane Carol">\n        Hurricane Carol\n       </a>\n       struck the same area only 11\xc2\xa0days prior. Strong winds caused extensive power outages for 260,000\xc2\xa0people, including nearly all of\n       <a href="/wiki/Cape_Cod" title="Cape Cod">\n        Cape Cod\n       </a>\n       . Edna became the costliest hurricane in the history of Maine, where the hurricane caused flooding that washed out roads and rail lines. There were 21\xc2\xa0deaths in New England, eight of whom in Maine due to drownings. Later, high winds severely damaged crops in Atlantic Canada.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="toc">\n       <div class="toctitle">\n        <h2>\n         Contents\n    <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">\n          Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale\n         </a>\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Hurricane Edna originated in an easterly\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">\n        tropical wave\n       </a>\n       which modern research has estimated to have spawned a tropical depression east of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">\n        Caribbean Sea\n       </a>\n       on September 2, 1954.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Low_pressure_area" title="Low pressure area">\n        low pressure area\n       </a>\n       was not observed in real time until several days later, on September 5, while situated between\n       <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">\n        Puerto Rico\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/The_Bahamas" title="The Bahamas">\n        The Bahamas\n       </a>\n       . Near the disturbance, a ship reported heavy squalls and wind gusts to 70\xc2\xa0mph (110\xc2\xa0km/h). Additionally, the island of Puerto Rico experienced torrential rainfall in association with the storm.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EdnaMWR_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-EdnaMWR-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Operationally, the system was not recognized to have organized into a tropical cyclone until September 6;\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       it is listed in the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlantic_hurricane_database" title="Atlantic hurricane database">\n        Atlantic hurricane database\n       </a>\n       as achieving tropical storm intensity early on September 4. Edna proceeded west-northwestward, bypassing\n       <a href="/wiki/Hispaniola" title="Hispaniola">\n        Hispaniola\n       </a>\n       to the north, and by September 7 it had strengthened into a minimal hurricane on the present-day\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson Hurricane Scale">\n        Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson Hurricane Scale\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Initially a rather small storm in terms of physical size, the hurricane steadily intensified throughout the day.\n       <a href="/wiki/Reconnaissance" title="Reconnaissance">\n        Reconnaissance\n       </a>\n       aircraft flew into the storm frequently to take observations on the cyclone\'s structure.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EdnaMWR_2-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-EdnaMWR-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_1-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The center of circulation passed near\n       <a href="/wiki/San_Salvador_Island" title="San Salvador Island">\n        San Salvador Island\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_3-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and on September 8, Edna further strengthened into a Category 3 major hurricane while broadly curving northward.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EdnaMWR_2-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-EdnaMWR-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_1-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Reconnaissance flights indicated a maturing and evolving\n       <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">\n        eye\n       </a>\n       , approximately 20\xc2\xa0mi (32\xc2\xa0km) in diameter. The overall circulation increased in size as banding features became more well-defined.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EdnaMWR_2-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-EdnaMWR-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The storm reached its peak winds of 120\xc2\xa0mph (190\xc2\xa0km/h) and maintained them for over two days.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_1-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       However, reconnaissance data was at times inconsistent and inaccurate, leading to suspicious and unlikely jogs in the storm\'s track. The cause of these errors is attributed to misinterpretation of\n       <a href="/wiki/Radar" title="Radar">\n        radar\n       </a>\n       information.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EdnaMWR_2-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-EdnaMWR-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Midday on September 9, while tracking nearly due north, Edna\'s minimum central\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barometric_pressure" title="Barometric pressure">\n        barometric pressure\n       </a>\n       fell to 968\n       <a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">\n        mbar\n       </a>\n       (28.6\n       <a href="/wiki/Inch_of_mercury" title="Inch of mercury">\n        inHg\n       </a>\n       ), but is believed to have leveled off shortly thereafter. The radius of hurricane-force winds increased, and the storm may have become asymmetrical, with convection concentrated to the right of the center, although this was not confirmed due to a lack of data. An approaching weak\n       <a href="/wiki/Weather_front" title="Weather front">\n        frontal boundary\n       </a>\n       spread overcast conditions over the\n       <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_of_the_United_States" title="East Coast of the United States">\n        Eastern Seaboard\n       </a>\n       north of\n       <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">\n        North Carolina\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EdnaMWR_2-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-EdnaMWR-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       By September 10, Edna was located just south of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cape_Hatteras,_North_Carolina" title="Cape Hatteras, North Carolina">\n        Cape Hatteras\n       </a>\n       , passing east of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Outer_Banks" title="Outer Banks">\n        Outer Banks\n       </a>\n       early the next day.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_1-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       As the hurricane accelerated to the northeast, it began to deteriorate and weaken.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_1-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Closely following the recent track of\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Carol" title="Hurricane Carol">\n        Hurricane Carol\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_3-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Edna approached\n       <a href="/wiki/New_England" title="New England">\n        New England\n       </a>\n       , but diverged from Carol\'s track upon skirting the eastern coast instead of moving inland.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_1-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Weather reporting stations in the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mid-Atlantic_States" title="Mid-Atlantic States">\n        Mid-Atlantic States\n       </a>\n       reported rapid clearing as the storm gained latitude, while heavy rain and gusty winds enveloped New England.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EdnaMWR_2-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-EdnaMWR-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On September 11, Edna passed directly over\n       <a href="/wiki/Cape_Cod" title="Cape Cod">\n        Cape Cod\n       </a>\n       after weakening to Category 2 status, although\n       <a href="/wiki/Surface_weather_analysis" title="Surface weather analysis">\n        surface weather analysis\n       </a>\n       depicted a central pressure of 950\xc2\xa0mbar (28\xc2\xa0inHg). It subsequently tracked just east of\n       <a href="/wiki/Eastport,_Maine" title="Eastport, Maine">\n        Eastport, Maine\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_1-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The storm continued northeastward into\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Canada" title="Atlantic Canada">\n        Atlantic Canada\n       </a>\n       and transitioned into an\n       <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">\n        extratropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       , although the exact time of this transition is unknown. The storm\'s extratropical remnants reemerged into the northern Atlantic after crossing eastern Canada and were last noted on September\xc2\xa015.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EdnaMWR_2-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-EdnaMWR-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_3-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">\n        Preparations\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Edna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       In advance of the storm,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_warning" title="Hurricane warning">\n        hurricane warnings\n       </a>\n       were issued for parts of The Bahamas and\n       <a href="/wiki/South_Florida" title="South Florida">\n        South Florida\n       </a>\n       , as winds of up to hurricane force were expected.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Farther to the north, storm warnings were posted from\n       <a href="/wiki/Myrtle_Beach,_South_Carolina" title="Myrtle Beach, South Carolina">\n        Myrtle Beach, South Carolina\n       </a>\n       to Eastport, Maine. Along the coast of North Carolina and the\n       <a href="/wiki/Virginia_Capes" title="Virginia Capes">\n        Virginia Capes\n       </a>\n       , where gale-force winds and high tides were expected, residents were advised to take precautions. In\n       <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">\n        New York City\n       </a>\n       , it was to be considered "a miracle" if Edna did not strike the area directly, and the storm was predicted to be one of the most severe hurricanes in the history of the New York\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weather_Bureau" title="Weather Bureau">\n        Weather Bureau\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In the 24\xc2\xa0hours before the storm struck,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_York_Telephone" title="New York Telephone">\n        New York Telephone\n       </a>\n       received 361,392\xc2\xa0calls, which was the third highest volume the company ever recorded.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In the\n       <a href="/wiki/Montauk,_New_York" title="Montauk, New York">\n        Montauk\n       </a>\n       area, 500 residents evacuated their homes by early September 11, while all businesses closed in\n       <a href="/wiki/Westerly,_Rhode_Island" title="Westerly, Rhode Island">\n        Westerly, Rhode Island\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Businesses in\n       <a href="/wiki/Providence,_Rhode_Island" title="Providence, Rhode Island">\n        Providence\n       </a>\n       , which was flooded during Hurricane Carol, prepared sandbags to mitigate flooding.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-day_8-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-day-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Most beach homes had closed for the summer season, and hundreds of people who lived along the southern New England coast evacuated.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-day_8-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-day-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Other threatened areas were evacuated, including\n       <a href="/wiki/Milford,_Connecticut" title="Milford, Connecticut">\n        Milford, Connecticut\n       </a>\n       , where 3,000 families left their homes. Sirens alerted other residents to remain indoors and drivers to stay off the highways. Along the coast of Connecticut, bus and train service was halted.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A state of emergency was declared in\n       <a href="/wiki/New_London,_Connecticut" title="New London, Connecticut">\n        New London\n       </a>\n       , and mail service was canceled in some areas.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On September 10, the Navy ordered the evacuation of hundreds of warships and aircraft.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">\n        Coast Guard\n       </a>\n       planes evacuated from Rhode Island and Massachusetts. About 40\n       <a href="/wiki/American_Red_Cross" title="American Red Cross">\n        Red Cross\n       </a>\n       shelters, which were opened during Hurricane Carol 11\xc2\xa0days prior, were reopened to provide food. Officers at\n       <a href="/wiki/Fort_Devens" title="Fort Devens">\n        Fort Devens\n       </a>\n       sent 40\xc2\xa0trucks with cots, blankets, and generators to areas expected to be struck by the storm.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-day_8-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-day-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Advance warning was credited with a lower death toll in Edna than Carol.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap912_12-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ap912-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n    <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Edna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: West Indies">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       On September 7, a bulletin from\n       <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">\n        San Juan, Puerto Rico\n       </a>\n       reported extensive, flood-inducing rainfall along the western and southern coasts of the island. Over 10\xc2\xa0in (250\xc2\xa0mm) of precipitation fell within a two-day period.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EdnaMWR_2-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-EdnaMWR-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The easternmost islands of The Bahamas, including San Salvador, experienced gale-force winds and high seas.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_3-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">\n        United States\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Edna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: United States">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Edna\'s path near North Carolina was about 50\xc2\xa0mi (80\xc2\xa0km) east of Carol.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Winds blew at up to 70\xc2\xa0mph (110\xc2\xa0km/h) along the shore, accompanied by strong surf, although no fatalities or major property damage resulted from the storm.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">\n        <a href="#cite_note-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Tides at\n       <a href="/wiki/Norfolk,_Virginia" title="Norfolk, Virginia">\n        Norfolk, Virginia\n       </a>\n       were only slightly above normal during the storm\'s passage to the east, and winds were moderate in strength.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">\n        <a href="#cite_note-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Peripheral rainfall in Maryland and Delaware eased drought conditions.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-noaa_16-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-noaa-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In coastal\n       <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">\n        New Jersey\n       </a>\n       , northwesterly winds reached 65\xc2\xa0mph (105\xc2\xa0km/h), and\n       <a href="/wiki/Long_Branch,_New_Jersey" title="Long Branch, New Jersey">\n        Long Branch\n       </a>\n       received around 4\xc2\xa0in (100\xc2\xa0mm) of rainfall. Further north,\n       <a href="/wiki/Long_Island" title="Long Island">\n        Long Island\n       </a>\n       also suffered moderate to strong winds, blowing from the north at their peak. A\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Weather_Bureau" title="United States Weather Bureau">\n        Weather Bureau\n       </a>\n       station in\n       <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">\n        New York City\n       </a>\n       recorded 45\xc2\xa0mph (72\xc2\xa0km/h) winds midday on September 11.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EdnaReport_17-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-EdnaReport-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Rainfall reached 4.98\xc2\xa0in (126\xc2\xa0mm) in the city, becoming the wettest day in 45\xc2\xa0years.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sar_18-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-sar-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The storm cut off\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Montauk_Point" title="Montauk Point">\n        Montauk Point\n       </a>\n       on eastern Long Island at its height, prompting the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Coast_Guard" title="U.S. Coast Guard">\n        Coast Guard\n       </a>\n       to temporarily relocate 500 families.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">\n        <a href="#cite_note-19">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Rainfall in\n       <a href="/wiki/Suffolk_County,_New_York" title="Suffolk County, New York">\n        Suffolk County\n       </a>\n       amounted to 9\xc2\xa0in (230\xc2\xa0mm).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">\n        <a href="#cite_note-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Crop damage in New York was estimated at $1.5\xc2\xa0million,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-noaa_16-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-noaa-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and six people died in the city due to highway deaths.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap912_12-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ap912-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       When Edna struck New England, it was moving quickly to the northeast at 45\xc2\xa0mph (72\xc2\xa0km/h). It struck eastern Massachusetts about 100\xc2\xa0mi (160\xc2\xa0km) east of where Hurricane Carol struck only a week prior. Hurricane-force winds affected much of the coastline, with peak gusts of 120\xc2\xa0mph (190\xc2\xa0km/h) on\n       <a href="/wiki/Martha%27s_Vineyard" title="Martha\'s Vineyard">\n        Martha\'s Vineyard\n       </a>\n       offshore Massachusetts, and 110\xc2\xa0mph (180\xc2\xa0km/h) on\n       <a href="/wiki/Block_Island" title="Block Island">\n        Block Island\n       </a>\n       offshore Rhode Island. Along the coast, wind gusts peaked at 100\xc2\xa0mph (160\xc2\xa0km/h) at\n       <a href="/wiki/Hyannis,_Massachusetts" title="Hyannis, Massachusetts">\n        Hyannis, Massachusetts\n       </a>\n       . The high winds caused widespread power outages, including for nearly all of\n       <a href="/wiki/Cape_Cod" title="Cape Cod">\n        Cape Cod\n       </a>\n       . The\n       <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">\n        storm surge\n       </a>\n       reached 6\xc2\xa0ft (1.8\xc2\xa0m) along the Massachusetts coast, causing flooding and heavy boating damage. Further west, there was lesser coastal flooding, although heavy rainfall after previously wet conditions caused urban and stream flooding; rainfall peaked at around 11\xc2\xa0in (280\xc2\xa0mm). Damage in Connecticut and Rhode Island was mainly in areas already affected by Hurricane Carol. Several streets were washed out, and rivers rose above flood stage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_21-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nws-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Damage was heaviest in Maine, estimated at $25\xc2\xa0million,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-noaa_16-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-noaa-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       which made Edna the costliest hurricane in the state\'s history. Strong wind gusts, reaching 74\xc2\xa0mph (119\xc2\xa0km/h), and heavy rainfall, peaking at 7.49\xc2\xa0in (190\xc2\xa0mm), extended into the state;\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ednamaine_22-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ednamaine-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       these were considered the heaviest rains in the state in 58\xc2\xa0years.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sar_18-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-sar-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The\n       <a href="/wiki/Androscoggin_River" title="Androscoggin River">\n        Androscoggin\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Kennebec_River" title="Kennebec River">\n        Kennebec\n       </a>\n       rivers both reached above-normal levels, causing flooding and washing out roads.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ednamaine_22-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ednamaine-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Augusta,_Maine" title="Augusta, Maine">\n        Augusta\n       </a>\n       , the Kennebec River reached 20.5\xc2\xa0ft (6.2\xc2\xa0m), which was 5\xc2\xa0ft (1.5\xc2\xa0m) above the peak level after the spring snow melt.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-free_23-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-free-23">\n         [23]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Flooding entered basements, affected fields, and covered bridges. In\n       <a href="/wiki/Peru,_Maine" title="Peru, Maine">\n        West Peru\n       </a>\n       , the floods washed out a bridge that was under construction.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">\n        <a href="#cite_note-24">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In addition, the winds downed trees,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ednamaine_22-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ednamaine-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       which blocked widespread roads and caused power outages in 18% of the state.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap912_12-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ap912-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Washed out roads and rail lines cut off the state from the rest of New England.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sar_18-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-sar-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Lewiston,_Maine" title="Lewiston, Maine">\n        Lewiston\n       </a>\n       , a trapped car in 5\xc2\xa0ft (1.5\xc2\xa0m) deep waters required rescue by boat.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">\n        <a href="#cite_note-25">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Another stranded family was rescued after seven hours in\n       <a href="/wiki/Unity,_Maine" title="Unity, Maine">\n        Unity\n       </a>\n       , in which one child and a rescuer were killed. There were eight deaths in the state, most of whom related to cars being swept away by floods.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap912_12-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ap912-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       There were 21\xc2\xa0deaths in New England,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_21-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nws-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       including eight drownings in Maine.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ednamaine_22-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ednamaine-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Throughout the United States, damage was estimated at $42,815,000. Damage was less from Edna than from Carol, primarily due to its strongest winds not occurring at the time of highest tides, and its track farther to the east.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-noaa_16-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-noaa-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Throughout New England, 260,000\xc2\xa0people lost power, most of whom in Massachusetts.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26">\n        <a href="#cite_note-26">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Atlantic_Canada">\n        Atlantic Canada\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Edna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Atlantic Canada">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       While rapidly losing characteristics of a tropical cyclone, Edna traversed central\n       <a href="/wiki/New_Brunswick" title="New Brunswick">\n        New Brunswick\n       </a>\n       . In contrast with the smaller, more compact\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Juan" title="Hurricane Juan">\n        Hurricane Juan\n       </a>\n       , which struck Atlantic Canada in 2003, Edna was a much larger storm with strong winds extending hundreds of miles from the center. The tightest pressure gradient was focused on the east side of the storm, over mainland\n       <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">\n        Nova Scotia\n       </a>\n       . Edna brought down approximately 700 million board feet of timber, and although the amount of trees the storm destroyed was comparable to that of Juan, its effects were more widespread and not as locally severe.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EdnaJuan_27-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-EdnaJuan-27">\n         [27]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Sustained winds reached 160\xc2\xa0km/h (99\xc2\xa0mph) at\n       <a href="/wiki/Yarmouth,_Nova_Scotia" title="Yarmouth, Nova Scotia">\n        Yarmouth\n       </a>\n       and 95\xc2\xa0km/h (59\xc2\xa0mph) at\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/City_of_Halifax" title="City of Halifax">\n        Halifax\n       </a>\n       . The intense winds downed power and telephone lines and destroyed many barns; one such structural collapse killed a man, and livestock were lost throughout the region. Other damage to property included several fallen chimneys, a toppled church steeple in\n       <a href="/wiki/Pictou" title="Pictou">\n        Pictou\n       </a>\n       , and roof failures. In\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kentville,_Nova_Scotia" title="Kentville, Nova Scotia">\n        Kentville\n       </a>\n       , an apartment building undergoing construction was destroyed. Approximately $3 million\n       <a href="/wiki/Canadian_dollar" title="Canadian dollar">\n        CAD\n       </a>\n       in apple crops were decimated, while in Yarmouth, both live lobsters and lobster pots were lost. Debris blocked streets across the province, and at least 800\xc2\xa0m (0.50\xc2\xa0mi) of road was washed out. Damage in Nova Scotia totaled $6 million CAD: in the aftermath, Yarmouth and Kentville declared states of emergency.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CHC_28-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CHC-28">\n         [28]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In New Brunswick, the storm dropped 130\xc2\xa0mm (5.1\xc2\xa0in) of rain and produced winds of 120\xc2\xa0km/h (75\xc2\xa0mph). Wind damage was less significant than in Nova Scotia, although still evident in structures and utility lines. A theatre in\n       <a href="/wiki/McAdam,_New_Brunswick" title="McAdam, New Brunswick">\n        McAdam\n       </a>\n       was destroyed, and several people were injured across the area. The storm flooded streets in\n       <a href="/wiki/St._Stephen,_New_Brunswick" title="St. Stephen, New Brunswick">\n        St. Stephen\n       </a>\n       . About 400 salmon, valued at $10,000 CAD, were lost in\n       <a href="/wiki/Dalhousie,_New_Brunswick" title="Dalhousie, New Brunswick">\n        Dalhousie\n       </a>\n       . Total damage in the province is estimated at $1.78 million CAD. Elsewhere, power outages were reported in\n       <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">\n        Montreal\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CHC_28-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CHC-28">\n         [28]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">\n        Aftermath\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Edna&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Aftermath">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       In Maine,\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_counties_in_Maine" title="List of counties in Maine">\n        20 counties\n       </a>\n       were declared states of emergency.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ednamaine_22-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ednamaine-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       After the storm,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southern_New_England_Telephone" title="Southern New England Telephone">\n        Southern New England Telephone\n       </a>\n       sent a crew of 100\xc2\xa0workers in 50\xc2\xa0trucks to Maine to assist in restoring power.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29">\n        <a href="#cite_note-29">\n         [29]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Power was quickly restored, and in some places the outages were less severe than during Carol.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30">\n        <a href="#cite_note-30">\n         [30]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       For only the third time in its history, the\n       <a href="/wiki/Evening_Express_(Portland)" title="Evening Express (Portland)">\n        Portland Evening Express\n       </a>\n       was not delivered due to the storm.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap912_12-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ap912-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Most primary roads were re-opened by two days after the storm, although rural areas and rail lines took longer to repair.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fcd_31-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-fcd-31">\n         [31]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       There was a temporary travel ban for all but emergency vehicles in Maine due to washed out roads.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-free_23-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-free-23">\n         [23]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Affecting densely populated portions of the state, Edna struck the day before the\n       <a href="/wiki/Maine_gubernatorial_election,_1954" title="Maine gubernatorial election, 1954">\n        governor race\n       </a>\n       between\n       <a href="/wiki/Republican_Party_(United_States)" title="Republican Party (United States)">\n        Republican\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Burton_M._Cross" title="Burton M. Cross">\n        Burton M. Cross\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Democratic_Party_(United_States)" title="Democratic Party (United States)">\n        Democratic\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Edmund_Muskie" title="Edmund Muskie">\n        Edmund Muskie\n       </a>\n       . Before the election, politicians commented how a suppressed turnout as a result of the storm would benefit Muskie, in a state where no Democrat had won governorship since 1934.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32">\n        <a href="#cite_note-32">\n         [32]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Muskie ultimately won the election in a close race, which saw a lower turnout than\n       <a href="/wiki/Maine_gubernatorial_election,_1950" title="Maine gubernatorial election, 1950">\n        1950\n       </a>\n       , likely due to Edna suppressing the rural, Republican turnout.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33">\n        <a href="#cite_note-33">\n         [33]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       After a survey from the\n       <a href="/wiki/Federal_Civil_Defense_Administration" title="Federal Civil Defense Administration">\n        Federal Civil Defense Administration\n       </a>\n       described damage as "tremendous",\n       <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">\n        President\n       </a>\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dwight_Eisenhower" title="Dwight Eisenhower">\n        Dwight Eisenhower\n       </a>\n       declared portions of Maine as a disaster area, which allocated federal funding for relief. The president also authorized additional aid to Rhode Island and Massachusetts due to additional damage there from Edna.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fcd_31-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-fcd-31">\n         [31]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Due to the severity of the storm, the name\n       <i>\n        Edna\n       </i>\n       was\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricane names">\n        retired\n       </a>\n       after this usage, for ten years before it was reintroduced for the\n       <a href="/wiki/1968_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1968 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1968 season\n       </a>\n       . At the 1969 Interdepartmental Hurricane Conference, it was proposed that the names Edna, Carol, and Hazel be permanently retired because of their importance to the research community. Despite the proposal, the name Edna was added to the 1972 naming list, but was ultimately unused.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34">\n        <a href="#cite_note-34">\n         [34]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">\n        See also\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span
