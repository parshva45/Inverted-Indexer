href="#External_links_2">\n          <span class="tocnumber">\n           13\n          </span>\n          <span class="toctext">\n           External links\n          </span>\n         </a>\n        </li>\n       </ul>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">\n        Description\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Salyut_7&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Salyut 7 was the back-up vehicle for\n       <a href="/wiki/Salyut_6" title="Salyut 6">\n        Salyut 6\n       </a>\n       and very similar in equipment and capabilities. With delays to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Mir" title="Mir">\n        Mir\n       </a>\n       programme it was decided to launch the back-up vehicle as Salyut 7. In orbit the station suffered technical failures though it benefited from the improved payload capacity of the visiting Progress and Soyuz craft and the experience of its crews who improvised many solutions (such as a fuel line rupture in September 1983 requiring\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extra-vehicular_activity" title="Extra-vehicular activity">\n        EVAs\n       </a>\n       by the\n       <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_T-10" title="Soyuz T-10">\n        Soyuz T-10\n       </a>\n       to repair). It was aloft for eight years and ten months (a record not broken until Mir), during which time it was visited by 10 crews constituting six main expeditions and four secondary flights (including French and Indian cosmonauts). The station also saw two flights of\n       <a href="/wiki/Svetlana_Savitskaya" title="Svetlana Savitskaya">\n        Svetlana Savitskaya\n       </a>\n       making her the second woman in space since 1963 and the first to perform an EVA during which she conducted metal cutting and welding alongside her colleague\n       <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Dzhanibekov" title="Vladimir Dzhanibekov">\n        Vladimir Dzhanibekov\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-welding_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-welding-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Aside from the many experiments and observations made on Salyut 7, the station also tested the docking and use of large modules with an orbiting space station. The modules were called "Heavy Kosmos modules" though in reality were variants of the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TKS_spacecraft" title="TKS spacecraft">\n        TKS spacecraft\n       </a>\n       intended for the cancelled\n       <a href="/wiki/Almaz" title="Almaz">\n        Almaz\n       </a>\n       military space station. They helped engineers develop technology necessary to build\n       <a href="/wiki/Mir" title="Mir">\n        Mir\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Equipment">\n        Equipment\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Salyut_7&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Equipment">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Salyut_7_assembly_.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="234" data-file-width="334" height="154" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/5/5e/Salyut_7_assembly_.jpg/220px-Salyut_7_assembly_.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/5/5e/Salyut_7_assembly_.jpg/330px-Salyut_7_assembly_.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/5/5e/Salyut_7_assembly_.jpg 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Salyut_7_assembly_.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Salyut 7 during assembly.\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       It had two docking ports, one on either end of the station, to allow docking with the Progress unmanned resupply craft, and a wider front docking port to allow safer docking with a Heavy Kosmos module. It carried three\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Photovoltaic_module" title="Photovoltaic module">\n        solar panels\n       </a>\n       , two in lateral and one in dorsal longitudinal positions, but they now had the ability to mount secondary panels on their sides. Internally, the Salyut 7 carried electric stoves, a refrigerator, constant hot water and redesigned seats at the command console (more like\n       <a href="/wiki/Bicycle" title="Bicycle">\n        bicycle\n       </a>\n       seats). Two portholes were designed to allow\n       <a href="/wiki/Ultraviolet" title="Ultraviolet">\n        ultraviolet\n       </a>\n       light in, to help kill infections.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-portree1_1-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-portree1-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nssdc.gsfc.nasa.gov_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nssdc.gsfc.nasa.gov-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The medical, biological and exercise sections were improved, to allow long stays in the station. The BST-1M telescope used in Salyut 6 was replaced by an\n       <a href="/wiki/X-ray" title="X-ray">\n        X-ray\n       </a>\n       detection system.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nssdc.gsfc.nasa.gov_3-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nssdc.gsfc.nasa.gov-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Crews_and_missions">\n        Crews and missions\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Salyut_7&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Crews and missions">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Salyut7_with_docked_spacecraft.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="385" data-file-width="575" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/Salyut7_with_docked_spacecraft.jpg/220px-Salyut7_with_docked_spacecraft.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/Salyut7_with_docked_spacecraft.jpg/330px-Salyut7_with_docked_spacecraft.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/Salyut7_with_docked_spacecraft.jpg/440px-Salyut7_with_docked_spacecraft.jpg 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Salyut7_with_docked_spacecraft.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Salyut 7 with docked spacecraft\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Following up the use of\n       <a href="/wiki/Kosmos_1267" title="Kosmos 1267">\n        Kosmos 1267\n       </a>\n       on Salyut 6, the Soviets launched\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kosmos_1443" title="Kosmos 1443">\n        Kosmos 1443\n       </a>\n       on 2 March 1983, from a Proton SL-13. It docked with the station on 10 March, and was used by the crew of\n       <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_T-9" title="Soyuz T-9">\n        Soyuz T-9\n       </a>\n       . It jettisoned its recovery module on 23 August, and re-entered the atmosphere on 19 September.\n       <a href="/wiki/Kosmos_1686" title="Kosmos 1686">\n        Kosmos 1686\n       </a>\n       was launched on 27 September 1985, docking with the station on 2 October. It did not carry a recovery vehicle, and remained connected to the station for use by the crew of\n       <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_T-14" title="Soyuz T-14">\n        Soyuz T-14\n       </a>\n       . Ten Soyuz T crews operated in Salyut 7. Only two Interkosmos "guest cosmonauts" worked in Salyut 7. The\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soyuz_T-10-1" title="Soyuz T-10-1">\n        first attempt to launch Soyuz T-10\n       </a>\n       was aborted on the launch pad when a fire broke out at the base of the vehicle. The payload was ejected, and the crew was recovered safely.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Resident_crews">\n        Resident crews\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Salyut_7&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Resident crews">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Salyut 7 had six resident crews.\n      </p>\n      <ul>\n       <li>\n        The first crew,\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anatoli_Berezovoy" title="Anatoli Berezovoy">\n         Anatoli Berezovoy\n        </a>\n        and\n        <a href="/wiki/Valentin_Lebedev" title="Valentin Lebedev">\n         Valentin Lebedev\n        </a>\n        , arrived on 13 May 1982 on\n        <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_T-5" title="Soyuz T-5">\n         Soyuz T-5\n        </a>\n        and remained for 211 days until 10 December 1982.\n        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-portree1_1-10">\n         <a href="#cite_note-portree1-1">\n          [1]\n         </a>\n        </sup>\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        On 27 June 1983, the crew of\n        <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Lyakhov" title="Vladimir Lyakhov">\n         Vladimir Lyakhov\n        </a>\n        and\n        <a href="/wiki/Aleksandr_Pavlovich_Aleksandrov" title="Aleksandr Pavlovich Aleksandrov">\n         Alexander Alexandrov\n        </a>\n        arrived on\n        <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_T-9" title="Soyuz T-9">\n         Soyuz T-9\n        </a>\n        and remained for 150 days, until 23 November 1983.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        On 8 February 1984,\n        <a href="/wiki/Leonid_Kizim" title="Leonid Kizim">\n         Leonid Kizim\n        </a>\n        ,\n        <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Solovyov_(cosmonaut)" title="Vladimir Solovyov (cosmonaut)">\n         Vladimir Solovyov\n        </a>\n        and\n        <a href="/wiki/Oleg_Atkov" title="Oleg Atkov">\n         Oleg Atkov\n        </a>\n        began a 237-day stay, the longest on Salyut 7, which ended on 2 October 1984.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Dzhanibekov" title="Vladimir Dzhanibekov">\n         Vladimir Dzhanibekov\n        </a>\n        and\n        <a href="/wiki/Viktor_Savinykh" title="Viktor Savinykh">\n         Viktor Savinykh\n        </a>\n        (\n        <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_T-13" title="Soyuz T-13">\n         Soyuz T-13\n        </a>\n        ) arrived at the space station on 6 June 1985.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        On 17 September 1985,\n        <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_T-14" title="Soyuz T-14">\n         Soyuz T-14\n        </a>\n        docked with the station carrying\n        <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Vasyutin" title="Vladimir Vasyutin">\n         Vladimir Vasyutin\n        </a>\n        ,\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alexander_Alexandrovich_Volkov_(cosmonaut)" title="Alexander Alexandrovich Volkov (cosmonaut)">\n         Alexander Volkov\n        </a>\n        and\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Georgi_Grechko" title="Georgi Grechko">\n         Georgi Grechko\n        </a>\n        . Eight days later Dzhanibekov and Grechko left the station and returned to Earth after 103 days, while Savinyikh, Vasyutin and Volkov remained on Salyut 7 and returned to Earth on 21 November 1985 after 65 days.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        On 6 May 1986,\n        <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_T-15" title="Soyuz T-15">\n         Soyuz T-15\n        </a>\n        carrying\n        <a href="/wiki/Leonid_Kizim" title="Leonid Kizim">\n         Leonid Kizim\n        </a>\n        and\n        <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Solovyov_(cosmonaut)" title="Vladimir Solovyov (cosmonaut)">\n         Vladimir Solovyov\n        </a>\n        docked with the space station and undocked, after a 50-day stay, on 25 June 1986. The Soyuz had come from the\n        <a href="/wiki/Mir" title="Mir">\n         Mir\n        </a>\n        space station and returned to Mir on 26 June 1986 in a flight lasting 29 hours.\n       </li>\n      </ul>\n      <p>\n       There were also four visiting missions, crews which came to bring supplies and make shorter duration visits with the resident crews.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Technical_and_crew_problems">\n        Technical and crew problems\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Salyut_7&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Technical and crew problems">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       The station suffered from two major problems, the first of which required extensive repair work to be performed on a number of EVAs.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Leaks">\n        Leaks\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Salyut_7&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Leaks">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       On 9 September 1983, during the stay of\n       <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Lyakhov" title="Vladimir Lyakhov">\n        Vladimir Lyakhov\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Aleksandr_Pavlovich_Aleksandrov" title="Aleksandr Pavlovich Aleksandrov">\n        Alexander Alexandrov\n       </a>\n       , while reorienting the station to perform a radiowave transmission experiment, Lyakhov noticed the pressure of one fuel tank was almost zero. Following this, Alexandrov spotted a fuel leak looking through the aft porthole. Ground control decided to try to repair the damaged pipes, in what was to be the most complex repair attempted during EVA at the time. This was to be attempted by the next crew, the current one lacking the necessary training and tools. The damage was eventually repaired by\n       <a href="/wiki/Leonid_Kizim" title="Leonid Kizim">\n        Leonid Kizim\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Solovyov_(cosmonaut)" title="Vladimir Solovyov (cosmonaut)">\n        Vladimir Solovyov\n       </a>\n       , who after four EVAs managed to fix two leaks but needed a special tool to fix the third. The tool was delivered by\n       <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_T-12" title="Soyuz T-12">\n        Soyuz T-12\n       </a>\n       , and the leak was subsequently fixed.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rzim_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rzim-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Loss_of_power">\n        Loss of power\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Salyut_7&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Loss of power">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Salyut7.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="229" data-file-width="310" height="163" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/8/82/Salyut7.jpg/220px-Salyut7.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/8/82/Salyut7.jpg 1.5x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Salyut7.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         The drifting station as seen from the approaching\n         <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_T-13" title="Soyuz T-13">\n          Soyuz T-13\n         </a>\n         .\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       On 11 February 1985, contact with Salyut 7 was lost. The station began to drift, and all systems shut down. At this time the station was uninhabited, after the departure of Leonid Kizim, Vladimir Solovyov and\n       <a href="/wiki/Oleg_Atkov" title="Oleg Atkov">\n        Oleg Atkov\n       </a>\n       , and before the next crew arrived. It was once again decided to attempt to repair the station, which was performed by\n       <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Dzhanibekov" title="Vladimir Dzhanibekov">\n        Vladimir Dzhanibekov\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Viktor_Savinykh" title="Viktor Savinykh">\n        Viktor Savinykh\n       </a>\n       on the\n       <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_T-13" title="Soyuz T-13">\n        Soyuz T-13\n       </a>\n       mission, in what was in the words of author David S. F. Portree "one of the most impressive feats of in-space repairs in history".\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-portree1_1-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-portree1-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       All Soviet and Russian space stations were equipped with automatic rendezvous and docking systems, from the first space station Salyut 1 using the\n       <a href="/wiki/Igla_(spacecraft_docking_system)" title="Igla (spacecraft docking system)">\n        Igla\n       </a>\n       system, to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Russian_Orbital_Segment" title="Russian Orbital Segment">\n        Russian Orbital Segment\n       </a>\n       of the\n       <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">\n        International Space Station\n       </a>\n       using the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kurs_(docking_system)" title="Kurs (docking system)">\n        Kurs\n       </a>\n       system. Upon arrival, on 6 June 1985, the Soyuz crew found the station was not broadcasting radar or telemetry for rendezvous, and after arrival and external inspection of the tumbling station, the crew estimated proximity using handheld laser rangefinders.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Dzhanibekov piloted his ship to intercept the forward port of Salyut 7 and matched the station\'s rotation. After\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spacecraft_docking" title="Spacecraft docking">\n        hard docking\n       </a>\n       to the station and confirming the station\'s electrical system was dead, Dzhanibekov and Savinykh sampled the station atmosphere prior to opening the hatch. Attired in winter fur-lined clothing, they entered the station to conduct repairs. The fault was eventually found to be an electrical sensor that determined when the batteries needed charging.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Once the batteries were replaced, the station started charging them, and warmed up over the next few days.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rzim_4-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rzim-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Within a week sufficient systems were brought back online to allow unmanned\n       <a href="/wiki/Progress_(spacecraft)" title="Progress (spacecraft)">\n        Progress\n       </a>\n       cargo ships to dock with the station.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-portree1_1-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-portree1-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="End_of_life">\n        End of life\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Salyut_7&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: End of life">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Salyut 7 was last inhabited in 1986 by the crew of\n       <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_T-15" title="Soyuz T-15">\n        Soyuz T-15\n       </a>\n       , who ferried equipment from Salyut 7 to the new\n       <a href="/wiki/Mir" title="Mir">\n        Mir\n       </a>\n       space station. Between 19 and 22 August 1986, engines on\n       <a href="/wiki/Kosmos_1686" title="Kosmos 1686">\n        Kosmos 1686\n       </a>\n       boosted Salyut 7 to a record-high mean orbital altitude of 475\xc2\xa0km to forestall reentry until 1994. Retrieval at a future date by a\n       <a href="/wiki/Buran_programme" title="Buran programme">\n        Buran shuttle\n       </a>\n       was also planned.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       However, unexpectedly high solar activity in the late 1980s and early 1990s increased atmospheric drag on the station and sped its orbital decay. It finally underwent an uncontrolled reentry on 7 February 1991 over the town of\n       <a href="/wiki/Capit%C3%A1n_Berm%C3%BAdez" title="Capit\xc3\xa1n Berm\xc3\xbadez">\n        Capit\xc3\xa1n Berm\xc3\xbadez\n       </a>\n       in Argentina after it overshot its intended entry point, which would have placed its debris in uninhabited portions of the southern Pacific Ocean.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Expeditions_and_visiting_spacecraft">\n        Expeditions and visiting spacecraft\n       </span>\n PE\n        </i>\n        means Principal Expedition\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        <i>\n         EP\n        </i>\n        (\n        <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">\n         Russian\n        </a>\n        :\n        <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">\n         \xd0\xad\xd0\x9f, \xd0\xad\xd0\xba\xd1\x81\xd0\xbf\xd0\xb5\xd0\xb4\xd0\xb8\xd1\x86\xd0\xb8\xd1\x8f
