role="main">\n   <a id="top">\n   </a>\n   <div class="mw-body-content" id="siteNotice">\n    <!-- CentralNotice -->\n   </div>\n   <div class="mw-indicators mw-body-content">\n   </div>\n   <h1 class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">\n    Space Station Processing Facility\n   </h1>\n   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">\n    <div class="noprint" id="siteSub">\n     From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia\n    </div>\n    <div id="contentSub">\n    </div>\n    <div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">\n     Jump to:\n     <a href="#mw-head">\n      navigation\n     </a>\n     ,\n     <a href="#p-search">\n      search\n     </a>\n    </div>\n    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">\n     <div class="mw-parser-output">\n      <p>\n       The\n       <b>\n        Space Station Processing Facility\n       </b>\n       (SSPF) is a three-story, 42,500 m\n       <sup>\n        2\n       </sup>\n       (457,000 square foot) building located in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Kennedy_Space_Center" title="Kennedy Space Center">\n        Kennedy Space Center\n       </a>\n       industrial area, just east of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Operations_and_Checkout_Building" title="Operations and Checkout Building">\n        Operations and Checkout Building\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       It was built for the processing of\n       <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">\n        International Space Station\n       </a>\n       flight hardware. The SSPF includes two processing bays, an airlock, operational control rooms, laboratories, logistics areas, office space, and a cafeteria. The processing areas, airlock, and laboratories were designed to support non-hazardous Station and\n       <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle" title="Space Shuttle">\n        Space Shuttle\n       </a>\n       payloads in 100,000 class clean work areas.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Station_Integration_Testing">\n        Station Integration Testing\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Space_Station_Processing_Facility&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Station Integration Testing">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Regarding the launch of modules of the\n       <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">\n        International Space Station\n       </a>\n       (ISS), there had been philosophical differences for years between designers and payload processors whether to ship-and-shoot or perform integration testing prior to launch. The former involved building a station module and launching it without ever physically testing it with other modules. The integration testing was not originally in the ISS plan, but in 1995\n       <a href="/wiki/Johnson_Space_Center" title="Johnson Space Center">\n        Johnson Space Center\n       </a>\n       designers began to consider it and embedding KSC personnel at module factories. Multi-Element Integration Testing (MEIT) of ISS modules at KSC was officially in the books in 1997.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lipartito_Butler_History_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Lipartito_Butler_History-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       "Three MEIT and one Integration Systems Test (IST) tests were conducted for the ISS," taking about three years from planning to completion and closure:\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <ul>\n       <li>\n        MEIT1:\n        <a href="/wiki/Destiny_(ISS_module)" title="Destiny (ISS module)">\n         US Lab\n        </a>\n        ,\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Z1_truss" title="Z1 truss">\n         Z1 truss\n        </a>\n        ,\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/P5_Truss_Segment" title="P5 Truss Segment">\n         P6 truss\n        </a>\n        , and a\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Node_1" title="Node 1">\n         Node 1\n        </a>\n        emulator\n        <ul>\n         <li>\n          Planning began in 1997, Testing began January 1999\n         </li>\n        </ul>\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        MEIT2:\n        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=S0_truss&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="S0 truss (page does not exist)">\n         S0 truss\n        </a>\n        /Mobile Transporter/Mobile Base System,\n        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=S1_truss&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="S1 truss (page does not exist)">\n         S1 truss\n        </a>\n        ,\n        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=P1_truss&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="P1 truss (page does not exist)">\n         P1 truss\n        </a>\n        , P3 truss, P4 truss, and a US Lab emulator.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        MEIT3:\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_Experiment_Module" title="Japanese Experiment Module">\n         Japanese Experiment Module\n        </a>\n        ,\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Node_2" title="Node 2">\n         Node 2\n        </a>\n        , and the US Lab emulator\n        <ul>\n         <li>\n          Completed in 2007\n         </li>\n        </ul>\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Node2 IST: Node 2 and US Lab and Node 1 emulators, as part of the ISS Flight Emulator\n       </li>\n      </ul>\n      <p>\n       After the launch of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Destiny_(ISS_module)" title="Destiny (ISS module)">\n        U.S. Laboratory module\n       </a>\n       , an emulator was built for MEIT testing, since the lab controlled many other modules. Among the items checked were mechanical connections, the ability to flow power and fluids between modules, and the flight software.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Numerous issues were found from these on the ground tests, many of which couldn\'t have been fixed in orbit. Many of the builders accompanied their modules from around the world and worked at KSC for months to years during testing. Many of the modules were renamed after successfully launching.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Station_components_currently_in_the_SSPF">\n        Station components
