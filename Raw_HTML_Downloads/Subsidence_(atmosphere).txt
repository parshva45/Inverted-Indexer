As the air cools, it becomes denser and moves towards the ground, as warm air becomes less dense and moves upwards. Subsiding air, when cool, is subject to\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adiabatic" title="Adiabatic">\n        adiabatic\n       </a>\n       warming, which tends to cause the evaporation of any clouds that might be present.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Subsidence generally causes high\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barometric_pressure" title="Barometric pressure">\n        barometric pressure\n       </a>\n       as more air moves into the same space: the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polar_high" title="Polar high">\n        polar highs\n       </a>\n       are areas of almost constant subsidence, as are the\n       <a href="/wiki/Horse_latitudes" title="Horse latitudes">\n        horse latitudes\n       </a>\n       , and the areas of subsidence are the sources of much of the world\'s\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prevailing_wind" title="Prevailing wind">\n        prevailing wind\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Subsidence also causes many smaller-scale\n       <a href="/wiki/Weather" title="Weather">\n        weather\n       </a>\n       phenomena, such as morning\n       <a href="/wiki/Fog" title="Fog">\n        fog\n       </a>\n       . An extreme form of subsidence is a\n       <a href="/wiki/Downburst" title="Downburst">\n        downburst\n       </a>\n       , which can result in damage similar to that produced by a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tornado" title="Tornado">\n        tornado\n       </a>\n       .
