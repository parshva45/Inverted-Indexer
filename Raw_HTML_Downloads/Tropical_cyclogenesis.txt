of a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        tropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Atmosphere" title="Atmosphere">\n        atmosphere\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CYCDEF_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CYCDEF-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The mechanisms through which tropical cyclogenesis occurs are distinctly different from those through which mid-\n       <a href="/wiki/Latitude" title="Latitude">\n        latitude\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Cyclogenesis" title="Cyclogenesis">\n        cyclogenesis\n       </a>\n       occurs. Tropical cyclogenesis involves the development of a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Warm_core" title="Warm core">\n        warm-core\n       </a>\n       cyclone, due to significant\n       <a href="/wiki/Convection" title="Convection">\n        convection\n       </a>\n       in a favorable atmospheric environment.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-A7_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-A7-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       There are six main requirements for tropical cyclogenesis: sufficiently warm\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sea_surface_temperatures" title="Sea surface temperatures">\n        sea surface temperatures\n       </a>\n       (at least 26.5\xc2\xb0C [80\xc2\xb0F]), atmospheric instability, high\n       <a href="/wiki/Humidity" title="Humidity">\n        humidity\n       </a>\n       in the lower to middle levels of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Troposphere" title="Troposphere">\n        troposphere\n       </a>\n       , enough\n       <a href="/wiki/Coriolis_force" title="Coriolis force">\n        Coriolis force\n       </a>\n       to develop a low pressure center, a preexisting low level focus or disturbance, and low vertical\n       <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">\n        wind shear\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-A15_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-A15-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Tropical cyclones tend to develop during the summer, but have been noted in nearly every month\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_basins" title="Tropical cyclone basins">\n        in most basins\n       </a>\n       .\n       <a href="/wiki/Climate" title="Climate">\n        Climate\n       </a>\n       cycles such as\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ENSO" title="ENSO">\n        ENSO\n       </a>\n       and the\n       <a href="/wiki/Madden%E2%80%93Julian_oscillation" title="Madden\xe2\x80\x93Julian oscillation">\n        Madden\xe2\x80\x93Julian oscillation\n       </a>\n       modulate the timing and frequency of tropical cyclone development.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AOML_climt_var_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-AOML_climt_var-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UEA_MJO_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-UEA_MJO-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       There is a limit on tropical cyclone intensity which is strongly related to the water temperatures along its path.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RSMAS_6-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-RSMAS-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       An average of 86 tropical cyclones of tropical storm intensity form annually worldwide. Of those, 47 reach hurricane/typhoon strength, and 20 become intense tropical cyclones (at least Category\xc2\xa03 intensity on the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" <p>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Requirements_for_tropical_cyclone_formation">\n        Requirements for tropical cyclone formation\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_cyclogenesis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Requirements for tropical cyclone formation">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Depth26Cisotherm.gif">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="227" data-file-width="1008" height="56" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/e/eb/Depth26Cisotherm.gif/250px-Depth26Cisotherm.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/e/eb/Depth26Cisotherm.gif/375px-Depth26Cisotherm.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/e/eb/Depth26Cisotherm.gif/500px-Depth26Cisotherm.gif 2x" width="250"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Depth26Cisotherm.gif" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Depth of 26\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0C\n         <a href="/wiki/Contour_line" title="Contour line">\n          isotherm\n         </a>\n         on October 1, 2006\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       There are six main requirements for tropical cyclogenesis: sufficiently warm sea surface temperatures, atmospheric instability, high\n       <a href="/wiki/Humidity" title="Humidity">\n        humidity\n       </a>\n       in the lower to middle levels of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Troposphere" title="Troposphere">\n        troposphere\n       </a>\n       , enough\n       <a href="/wiki/Coriolis_force" title="Coriolis force">\n        Coriolis force\n       </a>\n       to sustain a low pressure center, a preexisting low level focus or disturbance, and low vertical\n       <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">\n        wind shear\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-A15_3-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-A15-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       While these conditions are necessary for tropical cyclone formation,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Necessary_but_not_sufficient" title="Necessary but not sufficient">\n        they do not guarantee\n       </a>\n       that a tropical cyclone will form.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-A15_3-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-A15-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span id="Warm_waters,_instability,_and_mid-level_moisture">\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Warm_waters.2C_instability.2C_and_mid-level_moisture">\n        Warm waters, instability, and mid-level moisture\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_cyclogenesis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Warm waters, instability, and mid-level moisture">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main article:\n       <a href="/wiki/Lapse_rate" title="Lapse rate">\n        Lapse rate\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Atlantic_hurricane_graphic.png">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="263" data-file-width="350" height="188" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0e/Atlantic_hurricane_graphic.png/250px-Atlantic_hurricane_graphic.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/0/0e/Atlantic_hurricane_graphic.png 1.5x" width="250"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Atlantic_hurricane_graphic.png" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Waves in the trade winds in the Atlantic Ocean\xe2\x80\x94areas of converging winds that move slowly along the same track as the prevailing wind\xe2\x80\x94create instabilities in the atmosphere that may lead to the formation of hurricanes.\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Normally, an ocean temperature of 26.5\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0C (79.7\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0F) spanning through at least a 50-metre depth is considered the minimum to maintain a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        tropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-A15_3-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-A15-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       These warm waters are needed to maintain the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Warm_core" title="Warm core">\n        warm core\n       </a>\n       that fuels tropical systems. This value is well above 16.1\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0C (60.9\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0F), the global average surface temperature of the oceans.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SSTMEAN_8-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SSTMEAN-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Tropical cyclones are known to form even when normal conditions are not met. For example, cooler air temperatures at a higher altitude (e.g., at the 500\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/HPa" title="HPa">\n        hPa\n       </a>\n       level, or 5.9\xc2\xa0km) can lead to tropical cyclogenesis at lower water temperatures, as a certain\n       <a href="/wiki/Lapse_rate" title="Lapse rate">\n        lapse rate\n       </a>\n       is required to force the atmosphere to be\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baroclinic_instability" title="Baroclinic instability">\n        unstable\n       </a>\n       enough for convection. In a moist atmosphere, this lapse rate is 6.5\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0C/km, while in an atmosphere with less than 100%\n       <a href="/wiki/Relative_humidity" title="Relative humidity">\n        relative humidity\n       </a>\n       , the required lapse rate is 9.8\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0C/km.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCS_EESC_9-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCS_EESC-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       At the 500\xc2\xa0hPa level, the air temperature averages \xe2\x88\x927\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0C (18\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0F) within the tropics, but air in the tropics is normally dry at this level, giving the air room to\n       <a href="/wiki/Wet-bulb_temperature" title="Wet-bulb temperature">\n        wet-bulb\n       </a>\n       , or cool as it moistens, to a more favorable temperature that can then support convection. A wetbulb temperature at 500\xc2\xa0hPa in a tropical atmosphere of \xe2\x88\x9213.2\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0C is required to initiate convection if the water temperature is 26.5\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0C, and this temperature requirement increases or decreases proportionally by 1\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0C in the sea surface temperature for each 1\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0C change at 500\xc2\xa0hpa. Under a cold cyclone, 500\xc2\xa0hPa temperatures can fall as low as \xe2\x88\x9230\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0C, which can initiate convection even in the driest atmospheres. This also explains why moisture in the mid-levels of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Troposphere" title="Troposphere">\n        troposphere\n       </a>\n       , roughly at the 500\xc2\xa0hPa level, is normally a requirement for development. However, when dry air is found at the same height, temperatures at 500\xc2\xa0hPa need to be even colder as dry atmospheres require a greater lapse rate for instability than moist atmospheres.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LANDSEACLI_11-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-LANDSEACLI-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At heights near the\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropopause" title="Tropopause">\n        tropopause\n       </a>\n       , the 30-year average temperature (as measured in the period encompassing 1961 through 1990) was \xe2\x88\x9277\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0C (\xe2\x88\x92105\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0F).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UAIRtropics_12-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-UAIRtropics-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A recent example of a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        tropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       that maintained itself over cooler waters was\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Epsilon" title="Hurricane Epsilon">\n        Epsilon\n       </a>\n       of the\n       <a href="/wiki/2005_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2005 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        2005 Atlantic hurricane season\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h4>\n       <span id="Role_of_Maximum_Potential_Intensity_(MPI)">\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Role_of_Maximum_Potential_Intensity_.28MPI.29">\n        Role of Maximum Potential Intensity (MPI)\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_cyclogenesis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Role of Maximum Potential Intensity (MPI)">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h4>\n      <p>\n       <a href="/wiki/Kerry_Emanuel" title="Kerry Emanuel">\n        Kerry Emanuel\n       </a>\n       created a\n       <a href="/wiki/Mathematical_model" title="Mathematical model">\n        mathematical model\n       </a>\n       around 1988 to compute the upper limit of tropical cyclone intensity based on sea surface temperature and atmospheric profiles from the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_prediction_model" title="Tropical cyclone prediction model">\n        latest global model runs\n       </a>\n       . Emanuel\'s model is called the\n       <i>\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maximum_potential_intensity" title="Maximum potential intensity">\n         maximum potential intensity\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       , or MPI. Maps created from this equation show regions where tropical storm and hurricane formation is possible, based upon the\n       <a href="/wiki/Thermodynamics" title="Thermodynamics">\n        thermodynamics\n       </a>\n       of the atmosphere at the time of the last model run. This does not take into account vertical\n       <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">\n        wind shear\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EMANUEL_14-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-EMANUEL-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_isabel_and_coriolis_force.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="6000" data-file-width="6000" height="250" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/03/Hurricane_isabel_and_coriolis_force.jpg/250px-Hurricane_isabel_and_coriolis_force.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/03/Hurricane_isabel_and_coriolis_force.jpg/375px-Hurricane_isabel_and_coriolis_force.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/03/Hurricane_isabel_and_coriolis_force.jpg/500px-Hurricane_isabel_and_coriolis_force.jpg 2x" width="250"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_isabel_and_coriolis_force.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Schematic representation of flow around a low-pressure area (in this case,\n         <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Isabel" title="Hurricane Isabel">\n          Hurricane Isabel\n         </a>\n         ) in the Northern hemisphere. The pressure gradient force is represented by blue arrows, the Coriolis acceleration (always perpendicular to the velocity) by red arrows\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Coriolis_force">\n        Coriolis force\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_cyclogenesis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Coriolis force">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main article:\n       <a href="/wiki/Coriolis_force" title="Coriolis force">\n        Coriolis force\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       A minimum distance of 500\xc2\xa0km (310\xc2\xa0mi) from the\n       <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">\n        equator\n       </a>\n       is normally needed for tropical cyclogenesis.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-A15_3-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-A15-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The\n       <a href="/wiki/Coriolis_force" title="Coriolis force">\n        Coriolis force\n       </a>\n       imparts rotation on the flow and arises as winds begin to flow in toward the lower pressure created by the pre-existing disturbance. In areas with a very small or non-existent Coriolis force (e.g. near the Equator), the only significant atmospheric forces in play are the\n       <i>\n        pressure gradient force\n       </i>\n       (the pressure difference that causes winds to blow from high to low pressure\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Pressure_Gradient_15-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Pressure_Gradient-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       ) and a smaller\n       <a href="/wiki/Friction" title="Friction">\n        friction\n       </a>\n       force; these two alone would not cause the large-scale rotation required for tropical cyclogenesis. The existence of a significant Coriolis force allows the developing vortex to achieve gradient wind balance.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gradient_Wind_16-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Gradient_Wind-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       This is a balance condition found in mature tropical cyclones that allows\n       <a href="/wiki/Latent_heat" title="Latent heat">\n        latent heat\n       </a>\n       to concentrate near the storm core; this results in the maintenance or intensification of the vortex if other development factors are neutral.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">\n        <a href="#cite_note-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Low_level_disturbance">\n        Low level disturbance\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_cyclogenesis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Low level disturbance">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Whether it be a depression in the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Intertropical_convergence_zone" title="Intertropical convergence zone">\n        intertropical covergence zone (ITCZ)\n       </a>\n       , a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">\n        tropical wave\n       </a>\n       , a broad\n       <a href="/wiki/Surface_weather_analysis" title="Surface weather analysis">\n        surface front\n       </a>\n       , or an\n       <a href="/wiki/Outflow_boundary" title="Outflow boundary">\n        outflow boundary\n       </a>\n       , a low level feature with sufficient\n       <a href="/wiki/Vorticity" title="Vorticity">\n        vorticity\n       </a>\n       and convergence is required to begin tropical cyclogenesis.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-A15_3-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-A15-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Even with perfect upper level conditions and the required atmospheric instability, the lack of a surface focus will prevent the development of organized convection and a surface low.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-A15_3-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-A15-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Tropical cyclones can form when smaller circulations within the\n       <a href="/wiki/Intertropical_Convergence_Zone" title="Intertropical Convergence Zone">\n        Intertropical Convergence Zone\n       </a>\n       merge.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">\n        <a href="#cite_note-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Weak_vertical_wind_shear">\n        Weak vertical wind shear\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_cyclogenesis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Weak vertical wind shear">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main article:\n       <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">\n        Wind shear\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Vertical wind shear of less than 10\n       <a href="/wiki/Metre_per_second" title="Metre per second">\n        m/s\n       </a>\n       (20\n       <a href="/wiki/Knot_(unit)" title="Knot (unit)">\n        kt\n       </a>\n       , 22\xc2\xa0mph) between the surface and the\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropopause" title="Tropopause">\n        tropopause\n       </a>\n       is favored for tropical cyclone development.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-A15_3-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-A15-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A weaker vertical shear makes the storm grow faster vertically into the air, which helps the storm develop and become stronger. If the vertical shear is too strong, the storm cannot rise to its full potential and its energy becomes spread out over too large of an area for the storm to strengthen.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">\n        <a href="#cite_note-19">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Strong wind shear can "blow" the tropical cyclone apart,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UIUC_hurricanes_20-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-UIUC_hurricanes-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       as it displaces the mid-level warm core from the surface circulation and dries out the mid-levels of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Troposphere" title="Troposphere">\n        troposphere\n       </a>\n       , halting development. In smaller systems, the development of a significant\n       <a href="/wiki/Mesoscale_convective_complex" title="Mesoscale convective complex">\n        mesoscale convective complex\n       </a>\n       in a sheared environment can send out a large enough outflow boundary to destroy the surface cyclone. Moderate wind shear can lead to the initial development of the convective complex and surface low similar to the mid-latitudes, but it must relax to allow tropical cyclogenesis to continue.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">\n        <a href="#cite_note-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h4>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Favorable_trough_interactions">\n        Favorable trough interactions\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_cyclogenesis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Favorable trough interactions">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h4>\n      <p>\n       Limited vertical wind shear can be positive for tropical cyclone formation. When an upper-level\n       <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">\n        trough\n       </a>\n       or upper-level low is roughly the same scale as the tropical disturbance, the system can be steered by the upper level system into an area with better\n       <a href="/wiki/Divergence" title="Divergence">\n        diffluence\n       </a>\n       aloft, which can cause further development. Weaker upper cyclones are better candidates for a favorable interaction. There is evidence that weakly sheared tropical cyclones initially develop more rapidly than non-sheared tropical cyclones, although this comes at the cost of a peak in intensity with much weaker wind speeds and higher\n       <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_pressure" title="Atmospheric pressure">\n        minimum pressure\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SHEARHELP_22-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SHEARHELP-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       This process is also known as\n       <i>\n        baroclinic initiation\n       </i>\n       of a tropical cyclone. Trailing upper cyclones and upper troughs can cause additional outflow channels and aid in the intensification process. It should be noted that developing tropical disturbances can help create or deepen upper troughs or upper lows in their wake due to the outflow jet emanating from the developing tropical disturbance/cyclone.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CLARK_23-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CLARK-23">\n         [23]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMSPAPER_24-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-AMSPAPER-24">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       There are cases where large, mid-latitude troughs can help with tropical cyclogenesis when an upper-level\n       <a href="/wiki/Jet_stream" title="Jet stream">\n        jet stream\n       </a>\n       passes to the northwest of the developing system, which will aid divergence aloft and inflow at the surface, spinning up the cyclone. This type of interaction is more often associated with disturbances already in the process of recurvature.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WISC_25-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-WISC-25">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Times_of_formation">\n        Times of formation\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_cyclogenesis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Times of formation">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:352px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:WorldwideTCpeaks.gif">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="221" data-file-width="354" height="219" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/22/WorldwideTCpeaks.gif/350px-WorldwideTCpeaks.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/22/WorldwideTCpeaks.gif 1.5x" width="350"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:WorldwideTCpeaks.gif" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Peaks of activity worldwide\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Worldwide, tropical cyclone activity peaks in late summer when water temperatures are warmest. Each basin, however, has its own seasonal patterns. On a worldwide scale, May is the least active month, while September is the most active.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AOML_FAQ_G1_26-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-AOML_FAQ_G1-26">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In the North\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">\n        Atlantic\n       </a>\n       , a distinct hurricane season occurs from June 1 through November 30, sharply peaking from late August through October.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AOML_FAQ_G1_26-1">\n        <a id="cite_ref-Sun_sentinel_27-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Sun_sentinel-27">\n         [27]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The Northeast Pacific has a broader period of activity, but in a similar time frame to the Atlantic.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AOML_FAQ_G1_26-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-AOML_FAQ_G1-26">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The Northwest Pacific sees tropical cyclones year-round, with a minimum in February and a peak in early September.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AOML_FAQ_G1_26-3">\n        <a  [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In the\n       <a href="/wiki/Southern_Hemisphere" title="Southern Hemisphere">\n        Southern Hemisphere\n       </a>\n       , class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_cyclogenesis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Middle latitudes">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Areas farther than 30\xc2\xa0degrees from the equator (except in the vicinity of a warm current) are not normally conducive to tropical cyclone formation or strengthening, and areas more than 40\xc2\xa0degrees from the equator are often very hostile to such development. The primary limiting factor is water temperatures, although higher shear at increasing latitudes is also a factor. These areas are sometimes frequented by cyclones moving poleward from tropical latitudes. On rare occasions, such as\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Alex_(2004)" title="Hurricane Alex (2004)">\n        Alex in 2004\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NHC_Alex_33-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NHC_Alex-33">\n         [33]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_Storm_Alberto_(1988)" title="Tropical Storm Alberto (1988)">\n        Alberto in 1988\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UnisysAlberto_34-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-UnisysAlberto-34">\n         [34]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and the\n       <a href="/wiki/1975_Pacific_Northwest_hurricane" title="1975 Pacific Northwest hurricane">\n        1975 Pacific Northwest hurricane\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Unisys12_35-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Unisys12-35">\n         [35]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       storms may form or strengthen in this region. Typically, tropical cyclones will undergo\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extratropical_transition" title="Extratropical transition">\n        extratropical transition\n       </a>\n       after\n       <a href="/wiki/Westerlies#Interaction_with_tropical_cyclones" title="Westerlies">\n        recurving\n       </a>\n       polewards, and typically become fully extratropical after reaching 45\xe2\x80\x9350\xcb\x9a of latitude. The majority of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclones" title="Extratropical cyclones">\n        extratropical cyclones\n       </a>\n       tend to restrengthen after completing the transition period.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36">\n        <a href="#cite_note-36">\n         [36]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Near_the_Equator">\n        Near the Equator\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main article:\n       <a Force\n       </a>\n       , a vital ingredient in tropical cyclone formation.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37">\n        <a href="#cite_note-37">\n         [37]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       However, recently a few tropical cyclones have been observed forming within five degrees of the equator.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RSMC_Nadi_Guidance_38-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-RSMC_Nadi_Guidance-38">\n         [38]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="South_Atlantic">\n        South Atlantic\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a   ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main article:\n       <a href="/wiki/South_Atlantic_tropical_cyclone" title="South Atlantic tropical cyclone">\n        South Atlantic tropical cyclone\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       A combination of\n       <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind </a>\n       </sup>\n       Over four tropical cyclones have been observed here\xc2\xa0such as\xe2\x80\x94 a weak tropical storm in 1991 off the coast of Africa near\n       <a href="/wiki/Angola" in\n       <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">\n        Brazil\n       </a>\n       in 2004 at\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" href="/wiki/Salvador,_Brazil" title="Salvador, Brazil">\n        Salvador, Brazil\n       </a>\n       . But in mid-2015, a rare\n       <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_cyclone" title="Subtropical cyclone">\n        subtropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       was identified in early May relatively close to\n       <a href="/wiki/Chile" title="Chile">\n        Chile\n       </a>\n       . This system was unofficially dubbed\n       <i>\n        Katie\n       </i>\n       by researchers.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44">\n        <a href="#cite_note-44">\n         [44]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <a href="/wiki/Vortex" title="Vortex">\n        Vortices\n       </a>\n       have been reported off the coast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Morocco" title="Morocco">\n        Morocco\n       </a>\n       in the past. However, it is debatable if they are truly tropical in character.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-UK_43-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-UK-43">\n         [43]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Tropical activity is also extremely rare in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Great_Lakes" title="Great Lakes">\n        Great Lakes\n       </a>\n       . However,\n       <a href="/wiki/1996_Lake_Huron_cyclone" title="1996 Lake Huron cyclone">\n        a storm system\n       </a>\n       that appeared similar to a subtropical or tropical cyclone formed in 1996 on\n       <a href="/wiki/Lake_Huron" title="Lake Huron">\n        Lake Huron\n       </a>\n       . It formed an\n       <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">\n        eye\n       </a>\n       -like structure in its center, and it may have briefly been a subtropical or tropical cyclone.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Huron_45-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Huron-45">\n         [45]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Influence_of_large-scale_climate_cycles">\n        Influence of large-scale climate cycles\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_cyclogenesis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Influence of large-scale climate cycles">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       See also:\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclones_and_climate_change" title="Tropical cyclones and climate change">\n        Tropical cyclones and climate change\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Influence_of_ENSO">\n        Influence of ENSO\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_cyclogenesis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Influence of ENSO">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Sstaanim.gif">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="234" data-file-width="486" height="120" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/Sstaanim.gif/250px-Sstaanim.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/Sstaanim.gif/375px-Sstaanim.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/c/cc/Sstaanim.gif 2x" width="250"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Sstaanim.gif" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Loop of\n         <a href="/wiki/Sea_surface_temperature" title="Sea surface temperature">\n          sea surface temperature\n         </a>\n         (SST) anomalies in the Tropical Pacific\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main article:\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/El_Ni%C3%B1o-Southern_Oscillation" title="El Ni\xc3\xb1o-Southern Oscillation">\n        El Ni\xc3\xb1o-Southern Oscillation\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       El Ni\xc3\xb1o (ENSO) shifts the region (warmer water, up and down welling at different locations, due to winds) in the Pacific and Atlantic where more storms form, resulting in nearly constant\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Accumulated_Cyclone_Energy" title="Accumulated Cyclone Energy">\n        Accumulated Cyclone Energy\n       </a>\n       (ACE) values in any one basin. The El Ni\xc3\xb1o event typically decreases hurricane formation in the Atlantic, and far western Pacific and Australian regions, but instead increases the odds in the central North and South Pacific and particular in the western North Pacific typhoon region.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IPCC2007_46-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-IPCC2007-46">\n         [46]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Tropical cyclones in the northeastern Pacific and north Atlantic basins are both generated in large part by\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">\n        tropical waves\n       </a>\n       from the same wave train.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_47-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-47">\n         [47]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In the Northwestern Pacific, El Ni\xc3\xb1o shifts the formation of tropical cyclones eastward. During El Ni\xc3\xb1o episodes, tropical cyclones tend to form in the eastern part of the basin, between\n       <a href="/wiki/150th_meridian_east" title="150th meridian east">\n        150\xc2\xb0E\n       </a>\n       and the\n       <a href="/wiki/International_Date_Line" title="International Date Line">\n        International Date Line\n       </a>\n       (IDL).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chan_1985_48-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Chan_1985-48">\n         [48]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Coupled with an increase in activity in the North-Central Pacific (IDL to\n       <a href="/wiki/140th_meridian_west" title="140th meridian west">\n        140\xc2\xb0W\n       </a>\n       ) and the South-Central Pacific (east of\n       <a href="/wiki/160th_meridian_east" title="160th meridian east">\n        160\xc2\xb0E\n       </a>\n       ), there is a net increase in tropical cyclone development near the International Date Line on both sides of the equator.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BOM_49-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-BOM-49">\n         [49]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       While there is no linear relationship between the strength of an El Ni\xc3\xb1o and tropical cyclone formation in the Northwestern Pacific, typhoons forming during El Ni\xc3\xb1o years tend to have a longer duration and higher intensities.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-50">\n        <a href="#cite_note-50">\n         [50]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Tropical cyclogenesis in the Northwestern Pacific is suppressed west of 150\xc2\xb0E in the year following an El Ni\xc3\xb1o event.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Chan_1985_48-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Chan_1985-48">\n         [48]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Influence_of_the_MJO">\n        Influence of the MJO\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_cyclogenesis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Influence of the MJO">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:MJO_5-day_running_mean_through_1_Oct_2006.png">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="700" data-file-width="700" height="250" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/MJO_5-day_running_mean_through_1_Oct_2006.png/250px-MJO_5-day_running_mean_through_1_Oct_2006.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/MJO_5-day_running_mean_through_1_Oct_2006.png/375px-MJO_5-day_running_mean_through_1_Oct_2006.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/MJO_5-day_running_mean_through_1_Oct_2006.png/500px-MJO_5-day_running_mean_through_1_Oct_2006.png 2x" width="250"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:MJO_5-day_running_mean_through_1_Oct_2006.png" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         5-day running mean of MJO. Note how it moves eastward with time.\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main article:\n       <a href="/wiki/Madden%E2%80%93Julian_oscillation" title="Madden\xe2\x80\x93Julian oscillation">\n        Madden\xe2\x80\x93Julian oscillation\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       In general, westerly wind increases associated with the Madden\xe2\x80\x93Julian oscillation lead to increased tropical cyclogenesis in all basins. As the oscillation propagates from west to east, it leads to an eastward march in tropical cyclogenesis with time during that hemisphere\'s summer season.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMS2_51-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-AMS2-51">\n         [51]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       There is an inverse relationship between tropical cyclone activity in the western Pacific basin and the north Atlantic basin, however. When one basin is active, the other is normally quiet, and vice versa. The main cause appears to be the phase of the Madden\xe2\x80\x93Julian oscillation, or MJO, which is normally in opposite modes between the two basins at any given time.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mad-Jul_52-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Mad-Jul-52">\n         [52]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Influence_of_equatorial_Rossby_waves">\n        Influence of equatorial Rossby waves\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_cyclogenesis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Influence of equatorial Rossby waves">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main article:\n       <a href="/wiki/Rossby_wave" title="Rossby wave">\n        Rossby wave\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Research has shown that trapped equatorial\n       <a href="/wiki/Rossby_wave" title="Rossby wave">\n        Rossby wave\n       </a>\n       packets can increase the likelihood of tropical cyclogenesis in the Pacific Ocean, as they increase the low-level\n       <a href="/wiki/Westerlies" title="Westerlies">\n        westerly winds\n       </a>\n       within that region, which then leads to greater low-level vorticity. The individual waves can move at approximately 1.8\n       <a href="/wiki/Metre_per_second" title="Metre per second">\n        m/s\n       </a>\n       (4\xc2\xa0mph) each, though the group tends to remain stationary.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AMS4_53-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-AMS4-53">\n         [53]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Seasonal_forecasts">\n        Seasonal forecasts\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_cyclogenesis&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Seasonal forecasts">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Since 1984,\n       <a href="/wiki/Colorado_State_University" title="Colorado State University">\n        Colorado State University\n       </a>\n       has been issuing seasonal tropical cyclone forecasts for the north Atlantic basin, with results that are better than climatology.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GRAY_54-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-GRAY-54">\n         [54]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The university has found several statistical relationships for this basin that appear to allow long range prediction of the number of tropical cyclones. Since then, numerous others have followed in the university\'s steps, with some organizations issuing seasonal forecasts for the northwest Pacific and the Australian region.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SEASONAL_55-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SEASONAL-55">\n         [55]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The predictors are related to regional oscillations in the global\n       <a href="/wiki/Climate" title="Climate">\n        climate\n       </a>\n       system: the\n       <a href="/wiki/Walker_circulation" title="Walker circulation">\n        Walker circulation\n       </a>\n       which is related to the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/El_Ni%C3%B1o-Southern_Oscillation" title="El Ni\xc3\xb1o-Southern Oscillation">\n        El Ni\xc3\xb1o-Southern Oscillation\n       </a>\n       ; the\n       <a href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_oscillation" title="North Atlantic oscillation">\n        North Atlantic oscillation\n       </a>\n       (NAO); the\n       <a href="/wiki/Arctic_oscillation" title="Arctic oscillation">\n        Arctic oscillation\n       </a>\n       (AO); and the Pacific North American pattern (PNA).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GRAY_54-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-GRAY-54">\n         [54]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">\n
