Connie formed on August\xc2\xa03 from a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">\n        tropical wave\n       </a>\n       in the eastern Atlantic Ocean. It moved quickly west-northwestward, strengthening into a well-developed hurricane by August\xc2\xa05. Initially, it posed a threat to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">\n        Lesser Antilles\n       </a>\n       , although it passed about 50\xc2\xa0mi (80\xc2\xa0km) north of the region. The outer rainbands produced hurricane-force wind gusts and intense precipitation, reaching 8.65\xc2\xa0in (220\xc2\xa0mm) in Puerto Rico. In the\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Virgin_Islands" title="United States Virgin Islands">\n        United States Virgin Islands\n       </a>\n       , three people died due to the hurricane, and a few homes were destroyed. In Puerto Rico, Connie destroyed 60\xc2\xa0homes and caused crop damage. After affecting Puerto Rico, Connie turned to the northwest, reaching peak winds of 140\xc2\xa0mph (220\xc2\xa0km/h). The hurricane weakened while slowing and turning to the north, and struck\n       <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">\n        North Carolina\n       </a>\n       on August\xc2\xa012 at Category 2 intensity, the first of three damaging tropical cyclones in the\n       <a href="/wiki/1955_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1955 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1955 Atlantic hurricane season\n       </a>\n       to hit the state.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Ahead of the storm, the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Weather_Bureau" title="United States Weather Bureau">\n        United States Weather Bureau\n       </a>\n       issued widespread\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">\n        hurricane warnings\n       </a>\n       , resulting in 14,000\xc2\xa0people evacuating southeastern North Carolina. Connie produced strong winds, high tides, and heavy rainfall as it moved ashore, causing heavy crop damage and 27\xc2\xa0deaths in the state. Connie tracked roughly along the eastern coast of Virginia before turning northwest, and it progressed inland until dissipating on August\xc2\xa015 near\n       <a href="/wiki/Sault_Ste._Marie,_Michigan" title="Sault Ste. Marie, Michigan">\n        Sault Ste. Marie, Michigan\n       </a>\n       . Four people were killed in Washington, D.C. due to a traffic accident. In\n       <a href="/wiki/Chesapeake_Bay" title="Chesapeake Bay">\n        Chesapeake Bay\n       </a>\n       , Connie capsized a boat, killing 14\xc2\xa0people and prompting a change in\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">\n        Coast Guard\n       </a>\n       regulation. There were six deaths each in Pennsylvania and New Jersey, and eleven deaths in New York, where record rainfall flooded homes and subways. At least 225,000\xc2\xa0people lost power during the storm. Damage in the United States totaled around $86\xc2\xa0million, although the rains from Connie contributed to flooding from Hurricane Diane that caused $700\xc2\xa0million in damage. The remnants of Connie destroyed a few houses and boats in Ontario and killed three people in Ontario.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="toc">\n       <div </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       A\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">\n        tropical wave\n       </a>\n       developed into a\n       <a On August\xc2\xa06, it passed about 60\xc2\xa0mi (97\xc2\xa0km) north of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">\n        Lesser Antilles\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">\n        Puerto Rico\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       By that time, Connie had attained peak winds of 140\xc2\xa0mph (230\xc2\xa0km/h), making it a Category\xc2\xa04 hurricane on the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_hurricane_wind_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson hurricane wind scale">\n        Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_2-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       For four days, Connie maintained its peak intensity as its track shifted from the west-northwest toward the northwest,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_2-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       passing to the northeast of the Bahamas as it rounded a large\n       <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">\n        ridge\n       </a>\n       . On August\xc2\xa07, its eye had decreased to a diameter of 7\xc2\xa0mi (11\xc2\xa0km). By August\xc2\xa010, the eye had lost its definition as Connie slowed its northwest track. It began a west-northwest drift due to slight\n       <a href="/wiki/Fujiwhara_effect" title="Fujiwhara effect">\n        Fujiwhara interaction\n       </a>\n       with developing\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Diane" title="Hurricane Diane">\n        Hurricane Diane\n       </a>\n       to its southeast, as well as a building ridge to its northeast. The hurricane turned to the north on August\xc2\xa011 and was steadily weakening, due to the combination of\n       <a href="/wiki/Upwelling" title="Upwelling">\n        upwelling\n       </a>\n       and entrainment of cool air. Connie turned toward the north-northeast on August\xc2\xa012, by which time it had weakened to a minimal hurricane.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_1-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Late on August\xc2\xa012, Hurricane Connie made\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">\n        landfall\n       </a>\n       near\n       <a href="/wiki/Fort_Macon_State_Park" title="Fort Macon State Park">\n        Fort Macon State Park\n       </a>\n       in North Carolina.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_1-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n 100\xc2\xa0mph (160\xc2\xa0km/h),\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_2-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       although the\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Research_Division" title="Hurricane Research Division">\n        Hurricane Research Division\n       </a>\n       initially suggested that the hurricane hit North Carolina with winds of at least 115\xc2\xa0mph (185\xc2\xa0km/h) but those reports were found to be too high in reanalysis.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hrd_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-hrd-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Connie briefly moved offshore before striking land again near\n       <a href="/wiki/Cape_Charles_(headland)" title="Cape Charles (headland)">\n        Cape Charles\n       </a>\n       along the\n       <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Shore_of_Virginia" title="Eastern Shore of Virginia">\n        Eastern Shore of Virginia\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-longshore_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-longshore-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       It progressed inland, weakening to a tropical storm over Virginia and moving northward through the\n       <a href="/wiki/Chesapeake_Bay" title="Chesapeake Bay">\n        Chesapeake Bay\n       </a>\n       . Connie turned to the northwest, passing through much of Pennsylvania before weakening to a tropical depression near the Pennsylvania/New York border. After crossing\n       <a href="/wiki/Lake_Ontario" title="Lake Ontario">\n        Lake Ontario\n       </a>\n       and southwestern Ontario, the system moved through\n       <a href="/wiki/Lake_Huron" title="Lake Huron">\n        Lake Huron\n       </a>\n       , dissipating on August 15 near\n       <a href="/wiki/Sault_Ste._Marie,_Michigan" title="Sault Ste. Marie, Michigan">\n        Sault Ste. The\n       <a href="/wiki/American_Red_Cross" title="American Red Cross">\n        American Red Cross\n       </a>\n       opened shelters and mobilized 41\xc2\xa0officials with experience in hurricanes. </sup>\n       and overall about 14,000\xc2\xa0people evacuated the coastline, some to the 79\xc2\xa0Red Cross shelters opened up.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ellen_11-0">\n        <a [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In Philadelphia, the\n       <a href="/wiki/Boy_Scouts_of_America" title="Boy Scouts of America">\n        Boy Scouts\n       </a>\n       evacuated 800\xc2\xa0scouts from\n       <a href="/wiki/Cradle_of_Liberty_Council" title="Cradle of Liberty Council">\n        Camp Delmont\n       </a>\n       due to the threat of the hurricane.\n       <sup Ten stations in the state, and sixteen in neighboring Maryland, reached the highest stage on record.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-geo_18-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-geo-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       As Connie progressed northward, it continued to drop significant amounts of precipitation. Totals of over 10\xc2\xa0in (250\xc2\xa0mm) were reported on both sides of the Chesapeake Bay, in Pennsylvania, and in southeastern New York.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rain_16-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rain-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Rainfall in\n       <a href="/wiki/Richmond,_Virginia" title="Richmond, Virginia">\n        Richmond, Virginia\n       </a>\n       totaled 8.79\xc2\xa0in (223\xc2\xa0mm) on August\xc2\xa012, breaking the day\'s precipitation record.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-free_19-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-free-19">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Record rainfall also occurred in Philadelphia and New York City.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap814_20-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ap814-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The highest precipitation related to Connie was 13.24\xc2\xa0in (336\xc2\xa0mm) at\n       <a href="/wiki/Fort_Schuyler" title="Fort Schuyler">\n        Fort Schuyler\n       </a>\n       in New York. Rainfall also extended as far west as Michigan and as far east as Maine.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rain_16-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rain-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Across the northeastern United States, high rainfall from the hurricane resulted in disastrous flooding, along with Diane which moved ashore four days after Connie; this was due to unusually moist air across the region, which resulted from above average water and air temperatures. Many areas were in drought conditions before the flooding.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aug_21-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-aug-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The combination of strong winds and high waves quickly wrecked a 125\xc2\xa0ft (38\xc2\xa0m)\n       <a href="/wiki/Schooner" title="Schooner">\n        schooner\n       </a>\n       in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Chesapeake_Bay" title="Chesapeake Bay">\n        Chesapeake Bay\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-longshore_4-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-longshore-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Capsizing near\n       <a href="/wiki/Fairhaven,_Anne_Arundel_County,_Maryland" title="Fairhaven, Anne Arundel County, Maryland">\n        Fairhaven, Maryland\n       </a>\n       , the boat, named the\n       <i>\n        Levin J. Marvel\n       </i>\n       , was 64\xc2\xa0years old, and was described as "unseaworthy" when it left from\n       <a href="/wiki/Annapolis,_Maryland" title="Annapolis, Maryland">\n        Annapolis, Maryland\n       </a>\n       . Of the 23\xc2\xa0passengers and four crew members, 14\xc2\xa0people drowned, making it "one of the worst maritime calamities in the history of Tidewater Maryland", as described by\n       <a href="/wiki/The_Baltimore_Sun" title="The Baltimore Sun">\n        The Baltimore Sun\n       </a>\n       . The other passengers were later rescued, some of whom after holding onto wreckage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-levin_22-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-levin-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In Virginia, flooding washed out a portion of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Richmond,_Fredericksburg_and_Potomac_Railroad" title="Richmond, Fredericksburg and Potomac Railroad">\n        Richmond, Fredericksburg and Potomac Railroad\n       </a>\n       near\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Lankford,_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Lankford, Virginia (page does not exist)">\n        Lankford\n       </a>\n       , and covered portions of\n       <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_1_in_Virginia" title="U.S. Route 1 in Virginia">\n        U.S. 1\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_301_in_Virginia" title="U.S. Route 301 in Virginia">\n        U.S. 301\n       </a>\n       . The storm downed trees and caused scattered power outages for at least 5,000\xc2\xa0people in the state.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-free_19-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-free-19">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Washington_D.C." title="Washington D.C.">\n        Washington D.C.\n       </a>\n       , a car accident caused by slick roads resulted in the death of four people after a car was sideswiped and knocked into a swollen creek where the occupants drowned.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVA1_23-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TVA1-23">\n         [23]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In Delaware, the rains caused\n       <a href="/wiki/Surface_runoff" title="Surface runoff">\n        flooding\n       </a>\n       that was described as "inconsequential" by the\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Geological_Survey" title="United States Geological Survey">\n        United States Geological Survey\n       </a>\n       , due to preceding drought conditions.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_24-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nws-24">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Rainfall reached 9\xc2\xa0in (230\xc2\xa0mm) in southeastern Pennsylvania, causing flooding that entered basements in low-lying areas and covered roads. Two people drowned after floods swept away their cars.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">\n        <a href="#cite_note-25">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       There were six deaths in the state, and also six deaths in neighboring New Jersey.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap814_20-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ap814-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Hurricane Connie brought the heaviest rain seen in New York in over 50 years during its passage, dropping 5.32\xc2\xa0in (135\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">\n        New York City\n       </a>\n       within a 20-hour span.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TVA1_23-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TVA1-23">\n         [23]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Large areas of the city, including subways and thousands of houses, were flooded, and about 100,000\xc2\xa0people were left without power.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyc_26-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nyc-26">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Between New York and New Jersey, about 225,000\xc2\xa0people lost power.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27">\n        <a href="#cite_note-27">\n         [27]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       High winds and tides from the storm caused the cancellation of ferry service,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nyc_26-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nyc-26">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and forced\n       <a href="/wiki/LaGuardia_Airport" title="LaGuardia Airport">\n        LaGuardia Airport\n       </a>\n       to temporarily shut down after it was flooded 1\xc2\xa0ft (0.30\xc2\xa0m) deep.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-longshore_4-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-longshore-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap814_20-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ap814-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The rains caused heavy damage across southeastern New York,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_24-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nws-24">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and 11\xc2\xa0people were killed across the state.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap814_20-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ap814-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In coastal Connecticut, the rainfall from Connie increased levels along streams, but there was little damage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-geo_18-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-geo-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Overall damage in the United States was estimated at about $86,065,000, mostly in North Carolina, Virginia, and Maryland.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28">\n        <a href="#cite_note-28">\n         [28]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Further inland, gusts from Connie reached 65\xc2\xa0mph (105\xc2\xa0km/h) along Lake Huron in Michigan, which caused high waves that damaged or sank many small boats. Damage in the state was estimated at $150,000.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29">\n        <a href="#cite_note-29">\n         [29]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Before Connie affected Canada, residents in\n       <a href="/wiki/Caledon,_Ontario" title="Caledon, Ontario">\n        Humber valley\n       </a>\n       prepared for potential evacuations, after Hurricane Hazel in the previous October produced deadly flooding.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30">\n        <a href="#cite_note-30">\n         [30]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       When the remnants of Connie entered Ontario as a tropical depression on August 14, it continued to produce winds of up to 46\xc2\xa0mph (75\xc2\xa0km/h), and the storm dropped 2.56\xc2\xa0in (65\xc2\xa0mm) of rainfall near the Great Lakes. In\n       <a href="/wiki/Burlington,_Ontario" title="Burlington, Ontario">\n        Burlington\n       </a>\n       , 27\xc2\xa0boats were destroyed, and one person drowned in\n       <a href="/wiki/Lake_Erie" title="Lake Erie">\n        Lake Erie\n       </a>\n       after his boat sank. Two other people drowned in the province. Connie destroyed six houses and damaged several others due to high waves. The storm also caused power outages and damage to the tobacco crop.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31">\n        <a href="#cite_note-31">\n         [31]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">\n        Aftermath\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Connie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Aftermath">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:322px;">\n        <div class="mediaContainer" style="width:320px">\n         <video class="kskin" controls="" data-durationhint="79.621224489796" data-mwprovider="wikimediacommons" data-mwtitle="Hurricane_Connie_1955.ogv" data-startoffset="0" id="mwe_player_0" poster="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/87/Hurricane_Connie_1955.ogv/320px--Hurricane_Connie_1955.ogv.jpg" preload="none" style="width:320px;height:240px">\n          <source data-bandwidth="304504" data-framerate="29.97002997003" data-height="240" data-shorttitle="WebM 240P" data-title="Small WebM (240P)" data-transcodekey="240p.webm" data-width="320" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/transcoded/8/87/Hurricane_Connie_1955.ogv/Hurricane_Connie_1955.ogv.240p.webm" type=\'video/webm; however, the floods were important in setting the conditions for later significant flooding across the northeastern United States.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-geo_18-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-geo-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Just five days after Connie struck North Carolina,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_1-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Hurricane Diane affected the same area, but instead of continuing to the northwest it turned to the northeast. Diane produced further rainfall in already wet areas from Connie.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-natural_disasters_32-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-natural_disasters-32">\n         [32]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Damage from Diane totaled at least $700\xc2\xa0million,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_1-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and six states were declared federal disaster areas from the combined hurricanes\' impact; this allowed federal assistance for the affected areas.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-natural_disasters_32-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-natural_disasters-32">\n         [32]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The loss of the\n       <i>\n        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Levin_J._Marvel&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Levin J. Marvel (page does not exist)">\n         Levin J. Marvel\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       during the hurricane prompted the\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Congress" title="United States Congress">\n        United States Congress\n       </a>\n       to pass a law in 1956, which allowed the\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">\n        Coast Guard\n       </a>\n       to inspect all vessels with more than six passengers; the previous law only allowed inspections for boats of more than 700\xc2\xa0tons, greatly higher than the 183\xc2\xa0tons that the\n       <i>\n        Marvel\n       </i>\n       weighed. A federal courthouse charged the inexperienced captain with negligence, giving him a one-year probation.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-levin_22-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-levin-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Due to its destructive impacts, the name Connie was retired, and will never again be used for an\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_tropical_cyclone" title="North Atlantic tropical cyclone">\n        Atlantic hurricane\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup
