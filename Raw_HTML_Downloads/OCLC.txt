  <b>\n        OCLC\n       </b>\n       , currently incorporated as\n       <b>\n        OCLC Online Computer Library Center, Incorporated\n       </b>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" Center\n       </b>\n       . </span>\n         </a>\n        </li>\n       </ul>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">\n        History\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=OCLC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       OCLC began in 1967, as the Ohio College Library Center, through a collaboration of Ohio university presidents, vice presidents, and library directors who wanted to create a cooperative, computerized network for Ohio libraries. The group first met on July 5, 1967 on the campus of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Ohio_State_University" title="The Ohio State University">\n        the Ohio State University\n       </a>\n       to sign the articles of incorporation for the nonprofit organization.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-In_the_beginning_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-In_the_beginning-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The group hired\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frederick_G._Kilgour" title="Frederick G. Kilgour">\n        Frederick G. Kilgour\n       </a>\n       , a former\n       <a href="/wiki/Yale_University" title="Yale University">\n        Yale University\n       </a>\n       medical school librarian, to design the shared cataloging system.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tech_6-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tech-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Kilgour wished to merge the latest information storage and retrieval system of the time, the computer, with the oldest, the library. The plan was to merge the catalogs of Ohio libraries electronically through a computer network and database to streamline operations, control costs, and increase efficiency in library management. The goal of this network and database was to bring libraries together to cooperatively keep track of the world\'s information to best serve researchers and scholars. The first library to do online cataloging through OCLC was the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alden_Library" title="Alden Library">\n        Alden Library\n       </a>\n       at\n       <a href="/wiki/Ohio_University" title="Ohio University">\n        Ohio University\n       </a>\n       on August 26, 1971. This was the first occurrence of online cataloging by any library worldwide.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-In_the_beginning_5-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-In_the_beginning-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Membership in OCLC is based on use of services and contribution of data. Between 1967 and 1977, OCLC membership was limited to institutions in Ohio, but in 1978, a new governance structure was established that allowed institutions from other states to join. In 2002, the governance structure was again modified to accommodate participation from outside the United States.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       As OCLC expanded services in the United States outside Ohio, it relied on establishing strategic partnerships with "networks," organizations that provided training, support and marketing services. By 2008, there were 15 independent United States regional service providers. OCLC networks played a key role in OCLC governance, with networks electing delegates to serve on OCLC Members Council. During 2008, OCLC commissioned two studies to look at distribution channels; at the same time, the OCLC Members Council approved governance changes that had been recommended by the Board of Trustees which severed the tie between the networks and governance. In early 2009, OCLC negotiated new contracts with the former networks and opened a centralized support center.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Services">\n        Services\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=OCLC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Services">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       OCLC provides\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bibliographic" title="Bibliographic">\n        bibliographic\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Abstract_(summary)" title="Abstract (summary)">\n        abstract\n       </a>\n       and full-text information to anyone.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       OCLC and its member libraries cooperatively produce and maintain\n       <a href="/wiki/WorldCat" title="WorldCat">\n        WorldCat\n       </a>\n       \xe2\x80\x94the OCLC Online Union Catalog, the largest\n       <a href="/wiki/Online_public_access_catalog" title="Online public access catalog">\n        online public access catalog\n       </a>\n       (OPAC) in the world. WorldCat has holding records from public and private libraries worldwide. The Open WorldCat program, launched in late 2003, exposed a subset of WorldCat records to Web users via popular Internet search, bibliographic, and bookselling sites;\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Open WorldCat later morphed into WorldCat.org. In October 2005, the OCLC technical staff began a\n       <a href="/wiki/Wiki" title="Wiki">\n        wiki\n       </a>\n       project, WikiD, allowing readers to add commentary and structured-field information associated with any WorldCat record.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       WikiD was later phased out.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The Online Computer Library Center acquired the trademark and copyrights associated with the\n       <a href="/wiki/Dewey_Decimal_Classification" title="Dewey Decimal Classification">\n        Dewey Decimal Classification\n       </a>\n       System when it bought Forest Press in 1988. A browser\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oclc.dewey_11-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-oclc.dewey-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       for books with their Dewey Decimal Classifications was available until July 2013; it was replaced by the Classify Service.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Until August 2009, when it was sold to Backstage Library Works, OCLC owned a preservation microfilm and digitization operation called the OCLC Preservation Service Center,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oclc-p_12-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-oclc-p-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       with its principal office in\n       <a href="/wiki/Bethlehem,_Pennsylvania" title="Bethlehem, Pennsylvania">\n        Bethlehem, Pennsylvania\n       </a>\n       , U.S.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The reference management service QuestionPoint\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oclc.questionpoint_13-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-oclc.questionpoint-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       provides libraries with tools to communicate with users. This around-the-clock reference service is provided by a cooperative of participating global libraries.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       OCLC has produced\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Catalog_card" title="Catalog card">\n        catalog cards\n       </a>\n       for members since 1971 with its shared online catalog; the company printed its last catalog cards on October 1, 2015.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">\n        <a href="#cite_note-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Software">\n        Software\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=OCLC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Software">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       OCLC commercially sells software, e.g., CONTENTdm for managing\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Digital_collection" title="Digital collection">\n        digital collections\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">\n        <a href="#cite_note-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Research">\n        Research\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=OCLC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Research">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       OCLC has been conducting research for the library community for more than 30 years. In accordance with its mission, OCLC makes its research outcomes known through various publications.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oclc.res_16-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-oclc.res-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       These publications, including journal articles, reports, newsletters, and presentations, are available through the organization\'s website.\n      </p>\n      <ul>\n       <li>\n        OCLC Publications \xe2\x80\x93 Research articles from various journals including Code4Lib Journal, OCLC Research,\n        <a href="/wiki/Reference_and_User_Services_Quarterly" title="Reference and User Services Quarterly">\n         Reference &amp; User Services Quarterly\n        </a>\n        ,\n        <a href="/wiki/College_%26_Research_Libraries_News" title="College &amp; Research Libraries News">\n         College &amp; Research Libraries News\n        </a>\n        , Art Libraries Journal, and\n        <a href="/wiki/National_Education_Association" title="National Education Association">\n         National Education Association\n        </a>\n        Newsletter. The most recent publications are displayed first, and all archived resources, starting in 1970, are also available.\n        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oclc.pubs_17-0">\n         <a href="#cite_note-oclc.pubs-17">\n          [17]\n         </a>\n        </sup>\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Membership Reports \xe2\x80\x93 A number of significant reports on topics ranging from virtual reference in libraries to perceptions about library funding.\n        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oclc.reports_18-0">\n         <a href="#cite_note-oclc.reports-18">\n          [18]\n         </a>\n        </sup>\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Newsletters \xe2\x80\x93 Current and archived newsletters for the library and archive community.\n        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oclc.newsletter_19-0">\n         <a href="#cite_note-oclc.newsletter-19">\n          [19]\n         </a>\n        </sup>\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Presentations \xe2\x80\x93 Presentations from both guest speakers and OCLC research from conferences, webcasts, and other events. The presentations are organized into five categories: Conference presentations, Dewey presentations, Distinguished Seminar Series, Guest presentations, and Research staff presentations.\n        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oclc.pres_20-0">\n         <a href="#cite_note-oclc.pres-20">\n          [20]\n         </a>\n        </sup>\n       </li>\n      </ul>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Advocacy">\n        Advocacy\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=OCLC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Advocacy">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Advocacy has been a part of OCLC\'s mission since its founding in 1967. OCLC staff members meet and work regularly with library leaders, information professionals, researchers, entrepreneurs, political leaders, trustees, students and patrons to advocate "advancing research, scholarship, education, community development, information access, and global cooperation."\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-De_Rosa_2009_719.E2.80.93726_21-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-De_Rosa_2009_719.E2.80.93726-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TheG2009_22-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TheG2009-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       WebJunction\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oclc.webjunction_23-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-oclc.webjunction-23">\n         [23]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       is a division of OCLC funded by a grant from the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bill_and_Melinda_Gates_Foundation" title="Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation">\n        Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation\n       </a>\n       , which provides training services to librarians.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       OCLC\'s advocacy campaign "Geek the Library," started in 2009, highlights the role of public libraries. The campaign, funded by a grant from the\n       <a href="/wiki/Bill_%26_Melinda_Gates_Foundation" title="Bill &amp; Melinda Gates Foundation">\n        Bill &amp; Melinda Gates Foundation\n       </a>\n       , uses a strategy based on the findings of the 2008 OCLC report, "From Awareness to Funding: A study of library support in America."\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-advocacy_24-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-advocacy-24">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Other past advocacy campaigns have focused on sharing the knowledge gained from library and information research. Such projects have included communities such as the\n       <a href="/wiki/Society_of_American_Archivists" title="Society of American Archivists">\n        Society of American Archivists\n       </a>\n       , the\n       <a href="/wiki/Open_Archives_Initiative" title="Open Archives Initiative">\n        Open Archives Initiative\n       </a>\n       , the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Institute_for_Museum_and_Library_Services" title="Institute for Museum and Library Services">\n        Institute for Museum and Library Services\n       </a>\n       , the\n       <a href="/wiki/International_Organization_for_Standardization" title="International Organization for Standardization">\n        International Organization for Standardization\n       </a>\n       , the\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Information_Standards_Organization" title="National Information Standards Organization">\n        National Information Standards Organization\n       </a>\n       , the\n       <a href="/wiki/World_Wide_Web_Consortium" title="World Wide Web Consortium">\n        World Wide Web Consortium\n       </a>\n       , the\n       <a href="/wiki/Internet_Engineering_Task_Force" title="Internet Engineering Task Force">\n        Internet Engineering Task Force\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Internet2" title="Internet2">\n        Internet2\n       </a>\n       . One of the most successful contributions to this effort was the\n       <a href="/wiki/Dublin_Core" title="Dublin Core">\n        Dublin Core\n       </a>\n       Metadata Initiative, "an open forum of libraries, archives, museums, technology organizations, and software companies who work together to develop interoperable online\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Metadata_standards" title="Metadata standards">\n        metadata standards\n       </a>\n       that support a broad range of purposes and business models."\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-De_Rosa_2009_719.E2.80.93726_21-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-De_Rosa_2009_719.E2.80.93726-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       OCLC partnered with search engine providers in 2003 to advocate for libraries and share information across the broadest possible Internet landscape. Google,\n       <a href="/wiki/Yahoo!" title="Yahoo!">\n        Yahoo!\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Ask.com" title="Ask.com">\n        Ask.com\n       </a>\n       have all collaborated with OCLC to make the\n       <a href="/wiki/WorldCat" title="WorldCat">\n        WorldCat\n       </a>\n       records searchable through those search engines.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-De_Rosa_2009_719.E2.80.93726_21-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-De_Rosa_2009_719.E2.80.93726-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Online_database:_WorldCat">\n        Online database: WorldCat\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=OCLC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Online database: WorldCat">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main article:\n       <a href="/wiki/WorldCat" title="WorldCat">\n        WorldCat\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       OCLC\'s WorldCat database is used by librarians for cataloging and research. Contributions to WorldCat are made via the Connexion computer program, which was introduced in 2001; its predecessor, OCLC Passport, was phased out in May 2005.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       WorldCat contains records in MAchine Readable Cataloging (\n       <a href="/wiki/MARC_standards" title="MARC standards">\n        MARC\n       </a>\n       ) format contributed by library catalogers worldwide who use OCLC as a cataloging tool, and these MARC format records can also be downloaded into other libraries\' local catalog systems. This allows libraries to find and download records for materials they are adding to their local catalog, without having to undergo the lengthy process of creating a new catalog entry from scratch for each new item.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       As of March 2015, the OCLC database contained over 336 million records with 2.2\xc2\xa0billion cataloged items, and is the world\'s largest bibliographic database covering 72,000 libraries.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">\n        <a href="#cite_note-25">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Connexion is available to professional librarians as a computer program or on the web at connexion.oclc.org.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       WorldCat is available to the public for searching via a subscription web-based service called FirstSearch,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-firstsearch_26-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-firstsearch-26">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       as well as through the publicly available WorldCat.org.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-worldcat_27-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-worldcat-27">\n         [27]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Identifiers_and_linked_data">\n        Identifiers and linked data\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=OCLC&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Identifiers and linked data">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       OCLC assigns a unique control number (referred to as an "OCN" for "OCLC Control Number") to each new bibliographic record in the WorldCat. Numbers are assigned serially, and as of mid-2013 over a billion OCNs had been created. In September 2013, the OCLC declared these numbers to be in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Public_domain" title="Public domain">\n        public domain\n       </a>\n       , removing a perceived barrier to widespread use of OCNs outside OCLC itself.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oclc.public_28-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-oclc.public-28">\n         [28]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The control numbers link WorldCat\'s records to local library system records by providing a common reference key for a record across libraries.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oclc.control_29-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-oclc.control-29">\n         [29]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       OCNs are particularly useful as\n       <a href="/wiki/Identifier" title="Identifier">\n        identifiers\n       </a>\n       for books and other bibliographic materials that do not have\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ISBN" title="ISBN">\n        ISBNs\n       </a>\n       (e.g., books published before 1970). OCNs are used as identifiers often in Wikipedia and\n       <a href="/wiki/Wikidata" title="Wikidata">\n        Wikidata\n       </a>\n       . In October 2013, it was reported that out of 29,673 instances of\n       <a href="/wiki/Infobox" title="Infobox">\n        Infobox\n       </a>\n       Book in Wikipedia, "there were 23,304 ISBNs and 15,226 OCNs"; and regarding Wikidata: "of around 14 million Wikidata items, 28,741 were books. 5403 Wikidata items have an ISBN associated with them, and 12,262 have OCNs."\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oclc.wild_30-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-oclc.wild-30">\n         [30]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       OCLC also runs the\n       <a href="/wiki/Virtual_International_Authority_File" title="Virtual International Authority File">\n        Virtual International Authority File\n       </a>\n       (VIAF), an international name authority file.
