<p>\n       <b>\n        Breaux Greer\n       </b>\n       (born October 19, 1976) is a retired American\n       <a href="/wiki/Track_and_field" title="Track and field">\n        track and field\n       </a>\n       athlete who competed in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Javelin_throw" title="Javelin throw">\n        javelin throw\n       </a>\n       . He is the current\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_records_in_track_and_field" title="United States records in track and field">\n        American record holder\n       </a>\n       in the event with a throw of 91.29\n       <a href="/wiki/Metre" title="Metre">\n        m\n       </a>\n       (299.5\n       <a href="/wiki/Foot_(unit)" title="Foot (unit)">\n        ft\n       </a>\n       ), achieved on June 21, 2007. This was also the best throw in the world for almost eight years until surpassed by\n       <a href="/wiki/Julius_Yego" title="Julius Yego">\n        Julius Yego\n       </a>\n       in 2015. He is an\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_javelin_throw_national_champions_(men)#United_States" title="List of javelin throw national champions (men)">\n        eight-time American Champion\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       His coach was Finnish javelin thrower\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Kari_Ihalainen&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Kari Ihalainen (page does not exist)">\n        Kari Ihalainen\n       </a>\n       .
