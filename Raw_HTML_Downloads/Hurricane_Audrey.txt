      </tr>\n       <tr style="display:none">\n        <td colspan="2">\n        </td>\n       </tr>\n      </table>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        Hurricane Audrey\n       </b>\n       in June\xc2\xa01957 was one of the deadliest\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        tropical cyclones\n       </a>\n       to ever strike the United States, claiming more than 400\xc2\xa0lives along its path. The first\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_naming" title="Tropical cyclone naming">\n        named storm\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_hurricane_wind_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson hurricane wind scale">\n        hurricane\n       </a>\n       of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="Atlantic hurricane season">\n        annual hurricane season\n       </a>\n       , it first formed on June\xc2\xa025, 1957, from a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">\n        tropical wave\n       </a>\n       which moved into the\n       <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Campeche" title="Bay of Campeche">\n        Bay of Campeche\n       </a>\n       . Situated within favorable conditions for\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclogenesis" title="Tropical cyclogenesis">\n        tropical development\n       </a>\n       , Audrey quickly strengthened, reaching hurricane status just a few hours after being classified as a tropical cyclone. Moving generally northwards, it continued to strengthen as it approached the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Gulf_Coast" title="United States Gulf Coast">\n        United States Gulf Coast\n       </a>\n       . On June\xc2\xa027, the hurricane reached\n       <a href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_wind" title="Maximum sustained wind">\n        peak sustained winds\n       </a>\n       of 125\xc2\xa0mph (205\xc2\xa0km/h), making it a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_scales" title="Tropical cyclone scales">\n        major hurricane\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-4">\n         [nb 1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At the time, Audrey had a minimum\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barometric_pressure" title="Barometric pressure">\n        barometric pressure\n       </a>\n       of 946\n       <a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">\n        mbar\n       </a>\n       (\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hectopascals" title="Hectopascals">\n        hPa\n       </a>\n       ; 27.91\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inches_of_mercury" title="Inches of mercury">\n        inHg\n       </a>\n       ). The hurricane made\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">\n        landfall\n       </a>\n       at the same intensity between the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mouth_(river)" title="Mouth (river)">\n        mouth\n       </a>\n       of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Sabine_River_(Texas%E2%80%93Louisiana)" title="Sabine River (Texas\xe2\x80\x93Louisiana)">\n        Sabine River\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Cameron,_Louisiana" title="Cameron, Louisiana">\n        Cameron, Louisiana\n       </a>\n       later that day, causing unprecedented destruction across the region. Once inland, Audrey rapidly weakened and turned\n       <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">\n        extratropical\n       </a>\n       over\n       <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">\n        Louisiana\n       </a>\n       on June\xc2\xa028, before fully dissipating on June\xc2\xa029.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Prior to making landfall, Audrey severely disrupted\n       <a href="/wiki/Offshore_drilling" title="Offshore drilling">\n        offshore drilling\n       </a>\n       operations in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">\n        Gulf of Mexico\n       </a>\n       . Damages from offshore oil facilities alone was estimated at $16\xc2\xa0million.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-5">\n         [nb 2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Audrey caused much of its destruction near the border between\n       <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">\n        Texas\n       </a>\n       and Louisiana upon its first and only landfall. The hurricane\'s strong winds resulted in widespread property and infrastructural damage.\n       <a href="/wiki/Power_outage" title="Power outage">\n        Power outages\n       </a>\n       also resulted from the strong winds. However, as typical with most landfalling tropical cyclones, most of the destruction at the coast was the result of the hurricane\'s strong\n       <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">\n        storm surge\n       </a>\n       , which was amplified by Audrey\'s rapid deepening just prior to landfall. The hurricane\'s storm surge was reported to have peaked as high as 12\xc2\xa0ft (3.7\xc2\xa0m), helping to inundate coastal areas. Damage from the surge alone extended 25\xc2\xa0mi (40\xc2\xa0km) inland. The rough seas killed nine people offshore after capsizing the boat they were in. Further inland in Louisiana, the storm spawned two\n       <a href="/wiki/Tornado" title="Tornado">\n        tornadoes\n       </a>\n       , causing additional damage. The hurricane also dropped heavy rainfall, peaking at 10.63\xc2\xa0in (270\xc2\xa0mm) near\n       <a href="/wiki/Basile,_Louisiana" title="Basile, Louisiana">\n        Basile, Louisiana\n       </a>\n       . In Louisiana and Texas, where Audrey first impacted, damages totaled $128\xc2\xa0million.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       After moving inland and transitioning into an extratropical cyclone, Audrey caused additional damage across the interior United States. The storm produced 23\xc2\xa0tornadoes across\n       <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">\n        Mississippi\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">\n        Alabama\n       </a>\n       , causing $600,000\xc2\xa0in losses and killing two people. As it moved towards the northeast, moisture associated with the extratropical remnants of Audrey intersected with a\n       <a href="/wiki/Weather_front" title="Weather front">\n        weather front\n       </a>\n       over the\n       <a href="/wiki/Midwestern_United_States" title="Midwestern United States">\n        Midwestern United States\n       </a>\n       , producing record rainfall that peaked at 10.20\xc2\xa0in (259.08\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Paris,_Illinois" title="Paris, Illinois">\n        Paris, Illinois\n       </a>\n       . The resultant flooding resulted in ten fatalities. Elsewhere in the United States, the storm brought strong winds, causing additional damage. Farther north in Canada, 15\xc2\xa0people were killed in\n       <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">\n        Ontario\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">\n        Quebec\n       </a>\n       . Strong winds and torrential rainfall disrupted transportation services. In Quebec, ten people were killed in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">\n        Montreal\n       </a>\n       area, making Audrey the deadliest hurricane to strike the Canadian province in recorded history. The storm was also considered the worst storm to strike Quebec in at least 20\xc2\xa0years. In the United States, Audrey killed at least 416\xc2\xa0people, the majority of whom were in\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cameron_Parish" title="Cameron Parish">\n        Cameron Parish\n       </a>\n       Louisiana, though the final death total may never be known. Damage totaled $147\xc2\xa0million in the country, at the time the fifth-costliest hurricane recorded in the US since 1900. The name Audrey was later\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricane names">\n        retired\n       </a>\n       from usage as an identifier for an\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_hurricane" title="Atlantic hurricane">\n        Atlantic hurricane\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" Meteorological history\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Audrey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Audrey_1957_track.png">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1704" data-file-width="2700" height="139" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Audrey_1957_track.png/220px-Audrey_1957_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Audrey_1957_track.png/330px-Audrey_1957_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Audrey_1957_track.png/440px-Audrey_1957_track.png 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Audrey_1957_track.png" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Map plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the\n         <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">\n          Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale\n         </a>\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Between June\xc2\xa020 and 25, 1957, an ill-defined\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">\n        tropical wave\n       </a>\n       moved across the\n       <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">\n        Caribbean Sea\n       </a>\n       , over the\n       <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula" title="Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula">\n        Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula\n       </a>\n       , and into the\n       <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Campeche" title="Bay of Campeche">\n        Bay of Campeche\n       </a>\n       . The system was difficult to trace until a report on June\xc2\xa024 from\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carmen,_Mexico" title="Carmen, Mexico">\n        Carmen, Mexico\n       </a>\n       , confirmed the presence of a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Low_pressure_area" title="Low pressure area">\n        low pressure area\n       </a>\n       . Later that evening, a shrimp boat in the Bay of Campeche reported sustained winds of 40\xc2\xa0to 45\xc2\xa0mph (65\xc2\xa0to 75\xc2\xa0km/h) and a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barometric_pressure" title="Barometric pressure">\n        barometric pressure\n       </a>\n       of 1008\n       <a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">\n        mbar\n       </a>\n       (\n       <a href="/wiki/Pascal_(unit)" title="Pascal (unit)">\n        hPa\n       </a>\n       ; 29.78\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inches_of_Mercury" title="Inches of Mercury">\n        inHg\n       </a>\n       ).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prelim_2-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Prelim-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       As the disturbance developed, a large\n       <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">\n        trough\n       </a>\n       extended from a low over the\n       <a href="/wiki/Hudson_Bay" title="Hudson Bay">\n        Hudson Bay\n       </a>\n       into the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">\n        Gulf of Mexico\n       </a>\n       . The "latitudinal superposition" of these systems resulted in the intensification of both.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Circulations_6-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Circulations-6">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Situated over an area of high\n       <a href="/wiki/Sea_surface_temperature" title="Sea surface temperature">\n        sea surface temperatures\n       </a>\n       (approximately 85\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0F (29\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0C)) and within a region of favorable upper-level divergence, the tropical disturbance\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" title="Rapid deepening">\n        rapidly deepened\n       </a>\n       overnight.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_7-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-7">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The system was declared a tropical depression early on June\xc2\xa025 as it became stationary over the southern Gulf of Mexico.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prelim_2-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Prelim-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       An aircraft reconnaissance mission into the storm on June\xc2\xa025 revealed that the system had already intensified into a hurricane, reporting winds of 100\xc2\xa0mph (155\xc2\xa0km/h).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_7-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-7">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At this time, Audrey was located approximately 380\xc2\xa0mi (610\xc2\xa0km) southeast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Brownsville,_Texas" title="Brownsville, Texas">\n        Brownsville, Texas\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prelim_2-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Prelim-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       After attaining hurricane status, Audrey began to move slowly northward in response troughing in the upper-levels of the atmosphere.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prelim_2-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Prelim-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Circulations_6-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Circulations-6">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Continued reconnaissance missions into the storm revealed a well-developed structure, indicating that the system had become increasingly powerful. Only one observation close to the storm\'s center was made from this point until its landfall; the tanker\n       <i>\n        Tillamook\n       </i>\n       encountered the hurricane\'s western\n       <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">\n        eyewall\n       </a>\n       between 0910\xc2\xa0and 1025\n       <a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">\n        UTC\n       </a>\n       on June\xc2\xa027. During this time, a pressure of 969\xc2\xa0mbar (hPa; 28.62\xc2\xa0inHg) was measured.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_7-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-7">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       According to the\n       <a href="/wiki/HURDAT" title="HURDAT">\n        Hurricane Database\n       </a>\n       , Audrey attained winds of 125 mph (205 km/h) shortly after passing this tanker, making it a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SSHS#Category_3" title="SSHS">\n        Category\xc2\xa03\n       </a>\n       on the modern-day\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_hurricane_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson hurricane scale">\n        Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson hurricane scale\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_8-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-8">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Around 1430\xc2\xa0UTC on June\xc2\xa027, the eye of Audrey made\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">\n        landfall\n       </a>\n       between the mouth of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Sabine_River_(Texas%E2%80%93Louisiana)" title="Sabine River (Texas\xe2\x80\x93Louisiana)">\n        Sabine River\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Cameron,_Louisiana" title="Cameron, Louisiana">\n        Cameron, Louisiana\n       </a>\n       . While winds were originally estimated to have been near 145 mph (235 km/h) at the time of landfall, later reanalysis indicated that Audrey maintained its 125 mph (205 km/h) while moving ashore.\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Audrey_1957_Radar_Animation.gif">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="450" data-file-width="450" height="220" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f6/Hurricane_Audrey_1957_Radar_Animation.gif/220px-Hurricane_Audrey_1957_Radar_Animation.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f6/Hurricane_Audrey_1957_Radar_Animation.gif/330px-Hurricane_Audrey_1957_Radar_Animation.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f6/Hurricane_Audrey_1957_Radar_Animation.gif/440px-Hurricane_Audrey_1957_Radar_Animation.gif 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Audrey_1957_Radar_Animation.gif" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Hourly radar image animation of Hurricane Audrey making landfall in Louisiana\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       After maintaining its eye 60\xc2\xa0mi (95\xc2\xa0km) inland,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-9">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Audrey dramatically weakened and transitioned into an\n       <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">\n        extratropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       as it turned northeastward over Louisiana, and reached\n       <a href="/wiki/Tennessee" title="Tennessee">\n        Tennessee\n       </a>\n       as a 995\xc2\xa0mb (hPa; 29.39\xc2\xa0inHg) low.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prelim_2-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Prelim-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At this point, the system interacted with a wave extending from a polar front near\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chicago,_Illinois" title="Chicago, Illinois">\n        Chicago, Illinois\n       </a>\n       and subsequently re-intensified.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Circulations_6-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Circulations-6">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Curving northward and later northwestward around another extratropical low, the system attained a pressure of 974\xc2\xa0mb (hPa; 28.76\xc2\xa0inHg) as it moved near\n       <a href="/wiki/Lake_Huron" title="Lake Huron">\n        Lake Huron\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prelim_2-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Prelim-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The rapid deepening of Audrey as an extratropical cyclone was stated to be similar to that of\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hazel" title="Hurricane Hazel">\n        Hurricane Hazel\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/1954_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1954 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1954\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Circulations_6-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Circulations-6">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       By this point, the system was again producing hurricane-force winds, with\n       <a href="/wiki/Jamestown,_New_York" title="Jamestown, New York">\n        Jamestown, New York\n       </a>\n       reporting gusts up to 100\xc2\xa0mph (155\xc2\xa0km/h). By June\xc2\xa029, the system became entangled with the other cyclone and was eventually absorbed into its circulation over southern\n       <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">\n        Quebec\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prelim_2-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Prelim-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">\n        Preparations\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Audrey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Shortly after Audrey was classified as a tropical cyclone, the United States Weather Bureau advised ships in the path of the storm to exercise caution and small craft to remain in port on June\xc2\xa026. In addition, the Weather Bureau requested that Texas and Louisiana issue\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_watch" title="Hurricane watch">\n        hurricane watches\n       </a>\n       for their coasts.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RareHurricane_10-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-RareHurricane-10">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       These requested watches were later succeeded by a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_warning" title="Hurricane warning">\n        hurricane warning\n       </a>\n       for the entire Louisiana coast later that day.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PerilLouisiana_11-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-PerilLouisiana-11">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       This warning was later extended westward to include areas of the Texas coast south to\n       <a href="/wiki/High_Island,_Texas" title="High Island, Texas">\n        High Island, Texas\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PostWire_12-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-PostWire-12">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Northwest storm warnings were issued for the upper Texas coast north of\n       <a href="/wiki/Galveston,_Texas" title="Galveston, Texas">\n        Galveston, Texas\n       </a>\n       , while southeast storm warnings were issued for coastal areas between the western border of\n       <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">\n        Mississippi\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Pensacola,_Florida" title="Pensacola, Florida">\n        Pensacola, Florida\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PerilLouisiana_11-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-PerilLouisiana-11">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       These warnings remained posted until Audrey made landfall. Small craft warnings were issued the next day for coastal areas between\n       <a href="/wiki/Brownsville,_Texas" title="Brownsville, Texas">\n        Brownsville, Texas\n       </a>\n       and Pensacola, Florida.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AimingPunch_13-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-AimingPunch-13">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       News outlets far removed from the actual catastrophe indicate residents in exposed low-lying areas were urged to evacuate by the Weather Bureau, due to the potential for high and damaging\n       <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">\n        storm surge\n       </a>\n       expected to be between five to eight feet.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HeadNevada_14-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HeadNevada-14">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In the Wednesday, June 26th 10:00 pm Weather Bureau update, the hurricane warnings were changed to evacuate the southwestern coastal area of Louisiana.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">\n        <a href="#cite_note-15">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">\n        <a href="#cite_note-16">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">\n        <a href="#cite_note-17">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Some people ignored this warning while others found out too late. While at sea, the hurricane produced waves up to 50 ft (15.2 meters) in height. On 27 June, peak winds of 145 mph (233 km/h) were attained as Hurricane Audrey made landfall near the Texas/Louisiana border.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">\n        <a href="#cite_note-18">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The hurricane pushed ashore a peak storm surge of 12 ft (3.7 m) that caused much destruction. Storm surges of at least 1.8 m (6 ft) were experienced from Galveston, Texas to Cocodrie, Louisiana. Most of the 600 or more lives lost in relation to the hurricane were casualties of the storm surge.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">\n        <a href="#cite_note-19">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Evacuation procedures in Texas began after landfall was made on June\xc2\xa027, starting with residents in the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bolivar_Peninsula" title="Bolivar Peninsula">\n        Bolivar Peninsula\n       </a>\n       area. Several power lines were redirected to\n       <a href="/wiki/Fort_Crockett" title="Fort Crockett">\n        Fort Davis\n       </a>\n       to act as an emergency supply in the event of a mass evacuation. In Louisiana, schools were set up as\n       <a href="/wiki/Emergency_shelter" title="Emergency shelter">\n        emergency shelters\n       </a>\n       . All residents on\n       <a href="/wiki/Grand_Isle,_Louisiana" title="Grand Isle, Louisiana">\n        Grand Isle\n       </a>\n       were urged to evacuate after the island was isolated from the mainland during\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Flossy_(1956)" title="Hurricane Flossy (1956)">\n        Hurricane Flossy\n       </a>\n       a year prior;\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AimingPunch_13-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-AimingPunch-13">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       3,400\xc2\xa0residents later evacuated from the island to cities in southern Louisiana. However, 600\xc2\xa0residents remained on the island during the storm.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PostWire_12-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-PostWire-12">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Civil defense groups in the state placed key personnel in the area on 24-hour duty.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AimingPunch_13-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-AimingPunch-13">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Impact_and_aftermath">\n        Impact and aftermath\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Audrey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Impact and aftermath">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Audrey_1957_rainfall.gif">\n         <img alt="Colored contoured map showing rainfall amounts as separate filled in contours. Reds and purples indicate higher rainfall totals, and yellower shades indicate lower rainfall totals. The path the storm took is indicated as a yellow line." class="thumbimage" data-file-height="908" data-file-width="761" height="262" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Audrey_1957_rainfall.gif/220px-Audrey_1957_rainfall.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Audrey_1957_rainfall.gif/330px-Audrey_1957_rainfall.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Audrey_1957_rainfall.gif/440px-Audrey_1957_rainfall.gif 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Audrey_1957_rainfall.gif" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Rainfall totals in the United States from Audrey and a Predecessor Rainfall Event (PRE)\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The name "Audrey" was soon retired and will never be used again to name a hurricane.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-retire_20-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-retire-20">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Because of this, it was the only use of the name Audrey for the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlantic" title="Atlantic">\n        Atlantic Basin\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">\n        <a href="#cite_note-21">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Hurricane Audrey left $147\xc2\xa0million (1957\xc2\xa0USD) in damage and at least 416\xc2\xa0fatalities in the US,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Deadliest_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Deadliest-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       most in eastern Texas and western Louisiana. Audrey is ranked as the eighth deadliest hurricane to hit the United States mainland since accurate record-keeping began in 1900.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_8-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-8">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       No future hurricane caused as many fatalities in the United States until\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Katrina" title="Hurricane Katrina">\n        Katrina\n       </a>\n       in 2005. A study conducted by the National Hurricane Research Project Report No. 23 Hurricane Audrey Storm Tide conducted by the U.S. Weather Bureau found the extensive collection of storm tide data available from Hurricane Audrey was not sufficient to permit a detailed reconstruction of the events as they occurred. It was strongly suggested that warning procedures could be more effective if information concerning the tide heights on the open coast were available at proper places inland. This information, in addition to the other information concerning hurricanes, would permit a better estimation of the ultimate storm tide peak. This information, properly related to other hurricane information, should also help the local officials responsible for public safety to determine the seriousness of the situation at an earlier hour, and might justify a forced evacuation, if necessary. Information on the current state of the tide, if promptly available at the right places, should lead to a significant reduction in the loss of life attributable to hurricanes.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">\n        <a href="#cite_note-22">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The afternoon and evening of June 26, 1957, saw the Cajun community of southwestern Louisiana were tracking a hurricane in the Gulf of Mexico that was predicted by the New Orleans Weather Bureau to make landfall listening intently to the radio and television reports and updates and any short wave radio transmissions that were available. A disturbance in the Bay of Campeche was classified as a tropical depression on the basis of weather reports from the Mexican coast and shrimpers in the Gulf. When a Navy reconnaissance flight investigated the storm the next morning, they found it was already a hurricane and it was named Audrey. The reports were bleak for the landfall area. As the landfall deadline grew nearer and with advisories indicating the storm would not be until late afternoon on June 27th residents were busy loading and packing precious belongings into cars that were filled with gas and pointed towards Lake Charles and points north so they would be ready to head out at daybreak.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">\n        <a href="#cite_note-23">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The 10 p.m. advisory was said to be 235 miles south of Lake Charles with forward speed of 10 mph with probable landfall late the following day. It was estimated the wind speed was 100 mph. There was plenty of time the next morning to leave their beloved community as radio and television announcers telling folk to rest easy for the night and leave the next morning. At 1 a.m. on June 27th, the Lake Charles Weather Bureau released an update bulletin announcing Audrey had increased intensity of winds to 150 mph and a forward speed of 15 mph and was 150 miles from landfall. The communities went to bed at 10:00 p.m. with twenty three hours to evacuate and unbeknownst to them, there was only ten hours left before landfall. No one knew of the change in velocity and speed as the broadcasts were off the air for the day. Residents of Cameron, Vermilion and Iberia Parishes were asleep and unaware of the danger approaching rapidly. A flight which left Corpus Christi Naval Station at 11:18 p.m. on June 26th never penetrated the storm. After making one radar fix the mission was aborted due to radar failure. The previous radar fix was made at 1:49 p.m. on the 26th. The information would not have been diseminated due to broadcasters being off air though. Waking up between 4:00 and 5:00 o\xe2\x80\x99clock with 6 feet of water coming into houses, flowing under houses was not uncommon the morning of June 27th. It was determined this rapid rise of water level was not a part of the storm surge, but was a tidal wave being pushed by the hurricane. About a thousand people sought refuge in the Cameron Parish Courthouse. Of the three stories the first two were inundated with storm water mixed with sewerage and debris pushed on shore. Those who did not make it to the safety of large safe structures hung on in upturned roots of oak trees sharing their space with water moccasins and nutria rats who were seeking safety from the storm.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">\n        <a href="#cite_note-24">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Sheriff O. B. Carter gave testimony in the Bartie v. United States that Von Lewin weathercaster for KPLC in Lake Charles, Louisiana, stated on the 10:00 p.m. June 26th broadcast \xe2\x80\x9cYou can rest well tonight, get a good night\'s sleep because the storm will not reach the shores, the land, before late tomorrow afternoon\xe2\x80\x9d.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">\n        <a href="#cite_note-25">\n         [23]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In a lawsuit, Bartie v. United States in which multiple families sued the U.S. Weather Bureau for not providing proper notification of the danger involved in Hurricane Audrey prior to landfall. The same ominous warning: "All persons in low exposed places should move to higher ground." This time the warning was coupled with, "winds are increasing and will reach gale force tonight and early Thursday" continued to create peace in the hearts of the Cameron Parish residents instead of giving better information that would have caused an urgency to vacate the Parish.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26">\n        <a href="#cite_note-26">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       There was a broadcast on both TV and Radio immediately after the regular 10:00 P.M. news on that night. The Supreme Court of the United States determined that the broadcasters comments were made on the Hurricane which were intended for Lake Charles residents primarily but which were heard and viewed in the Cameron and coastal areas as well. Since the Weather Bureau did not control the broadcast the expressions "there is no need for alarm tonight," and "you can rest well tonight" could not be attributed to the U.S. Weather Bureau.\n          <a href="#cite_note-Dead_2011-28">\n           [26]\n          </a>\n         </sup>\n        </th>\n       </tr>\n      </table>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Gulf_of_Mexico">\n        Gulf of Mexico\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Audrey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Gulf of Mexico">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       One mobile drilling rig sank, with four tenders suffering damage when pulled loose from their mooring and running aground. The damage from all offshore oil facilities totaled US$16\xc2\xa0million (1957\xc2\xa0dollars).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29">\n        <a href="#cite_note-29">\n         [27]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h4>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Texas">\n        Texas\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Audrey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Texas">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h4>\n      <p>\n       Although making landfall near the border between Texas and Louisiana, areas of eastern Texas saw relatively less damage associated with Audrey. In\n       <a href="/wiki/Port_Arthur,_Texas" title="Port Arthur, Texas">\n        Port Arthur, Texas\n       </a>\n       , winds gusted to 85\xc2\xa0mph (135\xc2\xa0km/h), while the barometric pressure fell to 966\xc2\xa0mbar (hPa; 28.52\xc2\xa0inHg). The strong winds blew down communication lines and uprooted trees. Storm surge heights exceeded 6\xc2\xa0ft (1.8\xc2\xa0m) in coastal areas north of Galveston. Farther south in\n       <a href="/wiki/Corpus_Christi,_Texas" title="Corpus Christi, Texas">\n        Corpus Christi, Texas\n       </a>\n       , storm tides peaked at 4\xc2\xa0ft (1.2\xc2\xa0m) above normal, washing out portions of\n       <a href="/wiki/Mustang_Island_State_Park" title="Mustang Island State Park">\n        Mustang Island Park\n       </a>\n       Road.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Texas_Hurricanes_30-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Texas_Hurricanes-30">\n         [28]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A 0.75\xc2\xa0mi (1.2\xc2\xa0km) section of\n       <a href="/wiki/Texas_State_Highway_87" title="Texas State Highway 87">\n        Texas State Highway 87\n       </a>\n       between High Island and Sabine Pass, Texas, was later closed after sections of the highway were washed out by high water.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AimingPunch_13-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-AimingPunch-13">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Further inland, rainfall peaked at 7.35\xc2\xa0in (187\xc2\xa0mm) at\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jefferson_County_Airport_(Texas)" title="Jefferson County Airport (Texas)">\n        Jefferson County Airport\n       </a>\n       , setting a daily rainfall record. Overall, Audrey caused $8\xc2\xa0million in damages and nine deaths in Texas.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Texas_Hurricanes_30-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Texas_Hurricanes-30">\n         [28]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Elsewhere_in_the_United_States">\n        Elsewhere in the United States\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Audrey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Elsewhere in the United States">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       While moving inland, Audrey spawned 23 tornadoes which killed two\xc2\xa0people and injured 14 others in\n       <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">\n        Mississippi\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">\n        Alabama\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31">\n        <a href="#cite_note-31">\n         [29]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       while causing $600,000 (1957\xc2\xa0USD) in damage. In the Midwest, the flow of moisture from Audrey intersected a weather front to its north, creating a large storm with associated rainfall of 5 inches (130\xc2\xa0mm) to 11 inches (280\xc2\xa0mm) extending from central Missouri east-northeast across central Illinois and central Indiana.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32">\n        <a href="#cite_note-32">\n         [30]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The 10.20 inches (259\xc2\xa0mm) of rain that fell in\n       <a href="/wiki/Paris,_Illinois" title="Paris, Illinois">\n        Paris, Illinois\n       </a>\n       , led to a monthly precipitation record for June of 17.65 inches (448\xc2\xa0mm) and its wettest year on record with a total of 61.59 inches (1,564\xc2\xa0mm).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33">\n        <a href="#cite_note-33">\n         [31]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       It also flooded the entire town.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34">\n        <a href="#cite_note-34">\n         [32]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The storm dropped huge amounts of rain that caused significant flooding, leaving 10 fatalities. In\n       <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">\n        Pennsylvania\n       </a>\n       , the storm produced 65\xc2\xa0mph (105\xc2\xa0km/h) sustained winds, while winds of 95\xe2\x80\x93100\xc2\xa0mph (153\xe2\x80\x93161\xc2\xa0km/h) were reported in New York. In Canada, winds up to 80\xc2\xa0mph (129\xc2\xa0km/h) were reported and there were 15 fatalities.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_7-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-7">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35">\n        <a href="#cite_note-35">\n         [33]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       As an extratropical cyclone, Audrey brought hurricane-force winds as far east as\n       <a href="/wiki/St._Albans_(city),_Vermont" title="St. Albans (city), Vermont">\n        St. Albans, Vermont\n       </a>\n       , where a gust of 80 miles per hour (130\xc2\xa0km/h) was measured. Throughout the state, countless trees and power lines were downed. In\n       <a href="/wiki/Maine" title="Maine">\n        Maine\n       </a>\n       , rough seas stirred up by the storm forced yachts to be grounded.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36">\n        <a href="#cite_note-36">\n         [34]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Canada">\n        Canada\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Audrey&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Canada">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       The remnants of Audrey entered Ontario with tropical storm force winds after crossing\n       <a href="/wiki/Lake_Ontario" title="Lake Ontario">\n        Lake Ontario\n       </a>\n       . Heavy rainfall in the province washed out roads and rail lines. Six people were trapped in\n       <a href="/wiki/Algonquin_Provincial_Park" title="Algonquin Provincial Park">\n        Algonquin Provincial Park\n       </a>\n       for four days due to dangerous river currents and downed trees blocking roads. One boy drowned and a firefighter died due to the storm, and three other people died in Ontario due to traffic accidents. In neighboring Quebec, the remnants of Audrey were considered the worst storm in about 20\xc2\xa0years, and over 100\xc2\xa0houses were damaged by floods. The\n       <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">\n        Montreal\n       </a>\n       district of\n       <a href="/wiki/Ahuntsic-Cartierville" title="Ahuntsic-Cartierville">\n        Saraguay\n       </a>\n       lost power for several days. Throughout Montreal, there were 10\xc2\xa0deaths, nine of which due to traffic accidents.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37">\n        <a href="#cite_note-37">\n         [35]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       This made Audrey the deadliest tropical cyclone in Quebec on record.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38">\n        <a href="#cite_note-38">\n         [36]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div style="clear:both;">\n      </div>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">\n        See also\n       </span>\n       <span
