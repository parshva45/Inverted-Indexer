</a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="toc">\n       <div class="toctitle">\n        <h2>\n         Contents\n        </h2>\n       </div>\n       <ul>\n <p>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">\n        Plot\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Neddy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       In the midst of a quiet afternoon, the wind starts to pick up, which leads\n       <a href="/wiki/Lisa_Simpson" title="Lisa Simpson">\n        Lisa\n       </a>\n       to find out that a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        hurricane\n       </a>\n       is on the way. The evening news confirms that "Hurricane Barbara" is close, resulting in panicked citizens storming the\n       <a href="/wiki/Kwik-E-Mart" title="Kwik-E-Mart">\n        Kwik-E-Mart\n       </a>\n       . The hurricane strikes, and after a few treacherous hours, the storm ends. The Simpsons cautiously leave their basement and learn that the home is untouched. Next door,\n       <a href="/wiki/Ned_Flanders" title="Ned Flanders">\n        Ned Flanders\n       </a>\n       emerges from a heap of rubble and sees that although his family is safe, his house has been destroyed. The Flanders family is forced to move into the Rescue Center in the church basement. Ned is further discouraged after learning that his business\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Leftorium" title="The Leftorium">\n        The Leftorium\n       </a>\n       was looted following the hurricane. Distraught with annoyance, Ned begins to believe that God is punishing him like\n       <a href="/wiki/Job_(biblical_figure)" title="Job (biblical figure)">\n        Job\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The following day,\n       <a href="/wiki/Marge_Simpson" title="Marge Simpson">\n        Marge\n       </a>\n       arrives at the church and takes the Flanders family back to their house, completely "rebuilt" by the people of Springfield. Overjoyed, Ned inspects the house, but becomes increasingly dismayed as he discovers its extremely shoddy construction. Immediately after the inspection, the house collapses. Ned is unable to contain his rage and finally snaps, lashing out at the residents. Ned drives to a mental hospital to seek\n       <a href="/wiki/Psychotherapy" title="Psychotherapy">\n        psychotherapy\n       </a>\n       . Ned is visited by his childhood psychiatrist, Dr. Foster, who tells Ned about his childhood as an out-of-control brat raised by\n       <a href="/wiki/Beatnik" title="Beatnik">\n        beatnik\n       </a>\n       parents. Ned\'s treatment involved eight months of continuous spanking. The treatment worked so well that it rendered him unable to express any anger, and Ned\'s repressed anger built up inside him until he erupted.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Dr. Foster enlists\n       <a href="/wiki/Homer_Simpson" title="Homer Simpson">\n        Homer\n       </a>\n       to help Ned learn how to appropriately express emotions. After several failed scripted insults, Homer manages to get Ned to open up about some of his repressed dislikes, finally admitting he hates his parents, after which he immediately feels better. Dr. Foster declares Flanders cured and he is immediately released. Outside the hospital, Ned is greeted by the townsfolk of Springfield. Ned promises that from now on, if anyone does something he does not like, they will hear about it, which Dr. Foster tells him is very healthy.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-officialsite_2-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-officialsite-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-book_3-0">\n        <a Young\n       </a>\n       , a writer for the\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Late_Show_with_David_Letterman" title="Late Show with David Letterman">\n         Late Show with David Letterman\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       , was brought in as a freelance writer to write the episode.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Weinstein_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Weinstein-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The writers wanted to explore what made Flanders tick and examine what made him act the way he does.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Weinstein_4-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Weinstein-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The original idea came from\n       <a href="/wiki/George_Meyer" title="George Meyer">\n        George Meyer\n       </a>\n       , who had also wanted an episode about Flanders\' faith being tested.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Meyer_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Meyer-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       One of the key story points came from his friend\n       <a href="/wiki/Jack_Handey" title="Jack Handey">\n        Jack Handey\n       </a>\n       , a writer for\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Saturday_Night_Live" title="Saturday Night Live">\n         Saturday Night Live\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       , who wanted to do a sketch about a down-on-his-luck shoemaker who is visited by elves who help him, but make very bad shoes. Likewise, it inspired the idea that the neighbors would rebuild Flanders\' house, but do a bad job and provoke an outburst.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Meyer_5-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Meyer-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image"    Bob Anderson\n       </a>\n       can be seen.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Anderson_6-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Anderson-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n title="The Critic">\n         The Critic\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       , who had previously appeared in "\n       <a href="/wiki/A_Star_Is_Burns" title="A Star Is Burns">\n        A Star Is Burns\n       </a>\n       ", can also be seen in the mental hospital repeatedly saying his\n       <a href="/wiki/Catchphrase" title="Catchphrase">\n        catchphrase\n       </a>\n       , "It stinks".\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Weinstein_4-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Weinstein-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The small door at the end of the "Hurricane Neddy" finished 18th in ratings for the week of December 23\xe2\x80\x9329, 1996, with a\n       <a href="/wiki/Nielsen_ratings" title="Nielsen ratings">\n        Nielsen rating\n       </a>\n       of 8.7, equivalent to approximately 8.4 million viewing households. It was the second-highest-rated show on the Fox network that week, following\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/The_X-Files" title="The X-Files">\n         The X-Files\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Marge\'s line, "Dear God, this is Marge Simpson.
