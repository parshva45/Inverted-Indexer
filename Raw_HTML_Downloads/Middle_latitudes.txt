    The\n       <b>\n        middle latitudes\n       </b>\n       (\n       <b>\n        mid-latitudes\n       </b>\n       , sometimes\n       <b>\n        midlatitudes\n       </b>\n       ) are between 23\xc2\xb026\'22" North and 66\xc2\xb033\'39" North, and between 23\xc2\xb026\'22" South and 66\xc2\xb033\'39" South\n       <a href="/wiki/Latitude" title="Latitude">\n        latitude\n       </a>\n       , or, the Earth\'s\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Temperate_zone" title="Temperate zone">\n        temperate zones\n       </a>\n       between the\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropics" title="Tropics">\n        tropics\n       </a>\n       and the\n       <a href="/wiki/Arctic" title="Arctic">\n        Arctic\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Antarctic" title="Antarctic">\n        Antarctic\n       </a>\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Polar_region" title="Polar region">\n        polar regions\n       </a>\n       .\n       <a href="/wiki/Weather_front" title="Weather front">\n        Weather fronts\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">\n        extratropical cyclones\n       </a>\n       are usually found in this area, as well as occasional\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclones" title="Tropical cyclones">\n        tropical cyclones\n       </a>\n       which have traveled from their areas of formation closer to the equator.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The prevailing winds in the middle latitudes are often very strong. These parts of the world also see a wide variety of fast-changing weather as cold\n       <a href="/wiki/Air_mass" title="Air mass">\n        air masses\n       </a>\n       from the poles and warm air masses from the tropics push up and down over them, sometimes alternating within hours of each other, especially in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Roaring_Forties" title="Roaring Forties">\n        roaring forties\n       </a>\n       (between 40 and 50 degrees latitude in both hemispheres).\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       There are four types of epic mid-latitude climates:\n       <a href="/wiki/Mediterranean_climate" title="Mediterranean climate">\n        mediterranean\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Humid_subtropical" title="Humid subtropical">\n        humid subtropical\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marine_west_coast" title="Marine west coast">\n        marine west coast\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Humid_continental" title="Humid continental">\n        humid continental\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span
