  </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <b>\n        Icelandic Low\n       </b>\n       is a semi-permanent centre of low\n       <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_pressure" title="Atmospheric pressure">\n        atmospheric pressure\n       </a>\n       found between\n       <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">\n        Iceland\n       </a>\n       and southern\n       <a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">\n        Greenland\n       </a>\n       and extending in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Northern_Hemisphere" title="Northern Hemisphere">\n        Northern Hemisphere\n       </a>\n       winter into the\n       <a href="/wiki/Barents_Sea" title="Barents Sea">\n        Barents Sea\n       </a>\n       . In summer it weakens and splits into two centres, one near\n       <a href="/wiki/Davis_Strait" title="Davis Strait">\n        Davis Strait\n       </a>\n       and the other west of Iceland. It is a principal centre of action in the atmosphere circulation of the Northern Hemisphere, associated with frequent\n       <a href="/wiki/Cyclone" title="Cyclone">\n        cyclone\n       </a>\n       activity. It forms one pole of the\n       <a href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_oscillation" title="North Atlantic oscillation">\n        North Atlantic oscillation\n       </a>\n       , the other being the\n       <a href="/wiki/Azores_High" title="Azores High">\n        Azores High\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">\n        See also\n
