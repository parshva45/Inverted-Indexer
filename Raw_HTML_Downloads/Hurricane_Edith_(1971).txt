    </tr>\n      </table>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        Hurricane Edith\n       </b>\n       was the strongest\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_tropical_cyclone" title="North Atlantic tropical cyclone">\n        hurricane\n       </a>\n       to form during the\n       <a href="/wiki/1971_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1971 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1971 Atlantic hurricane season\n       </a>\n       and the southernmost landfalling\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Category_5_hurricanes" title="List of Category 5 hurricanes">\n        Category 5 hurricane\n       </a>\n       on record in the Atlantic until surpassed by\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Felix" title="Hurricane Felix">\n        Hurricane Felix\n       </a>\n       of\n       <a href="/wiki/2007_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2007 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        2007\n       </a>\n       . Edith developed from a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">\n        tropical wave\n       </a>\n       on September 5 and quickly strengthened into a hurricane in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">\n        Caribbean Sea\n       </a>\n       . Edith\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" title="Rapid deepening">\n        rapidly intensified\n       </a>\n       on September 9 and made landfall on\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cape_Gracias_a_Dios" title="Cape Gracias a Dios">\n        Cape Gracias a Dios\n       </a>\n       as a\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_Category_5_Atlantic_hurricanes" title="List of Category 5 Atlantic hurricanes">\n        Category 5 hurricane\n       </a>\n       on the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">\n        Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale\n       </a>\n       . It quickly lost intensity over Central America and after briefly entering the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Honduras" title="Gulf of Honduras">\n        Gulf of Honduras\n       </a>\n       it crossed the\n       <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula" title="Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula">\n        Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula\n       </a>\n       in Mexico. After moving across the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">\n        Gulf of Mexico\n       </a>\n       a\n       <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">\n        trough\n       </a>\n       turned the storm to the northeast and Edith, after having restrengthened while accelerating towards the coast, made landfall on\n       <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">\n        Louisiana\n       </a>\n       with winds of 105\xc2\xa0mph (170\xc2\xa0km/h) on September 16. Edith steadily weakened over land and dissipated over\n       <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">\n        Georgia\n       </a>\n       on September 18.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The hurricane killed two people when it passed near\n       <a href="/wiki/Aruba" title="Aruba">\n        Aruba\n       </a>\n       . Striking northeastern Central America as a Category\xc2\xa05 hurricane, Edith destroyed hundreds of homes and killed at least 35\xc2\xa0people. In\n       <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">\n        Texas\n       </a>\n       high tides caused coastal flooding but little damage. Edith caused moderate to heavy damage in portions of Louisiana due to flooding and a tornado outbreak from the storm. One tornado, rated F3 on the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fujita_Scale" title="Fujita Scale">\n        Fujita Scale\n       </a>\n       , damaged several homes and injured multiple people in\n       <a href="/wiki/Baton_Rouge,_Louisiana" title="Baton Rouge, Louisiana">\n        Baton Rouge\n       </a>\n       . The tornado outbreak extended eastward into Florida, of which a few destroyed entire buildings. Damage in the United States totaled\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">\n        US$\n       </a>\n       25\xc2\xa0million (1971 dollars, $148\xc2\xa0million in 2016 dollars).\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="toc">\n       <div class="toctitle">\n        <h2>\n         Contents\n        </h2>\n       </div>\n       <ul>\n class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Edith_(1971)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Edith_1971_track.png">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="670" data-file-width="1024" height="144" </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       A\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">\n        tropical wave\n       </a>\n       moved off the coast of Africa near\n       <a href="/wiki/Dakar" title="Dakar">\n        Dakar\n       </a>\n       on August 31. It moved westward into the\n       <a href="/wiki/Intertropical_Convergence_Zone" title="Intertropical Convergence Zone">\n        Intertropical Convergence Zone\n       </a>\n       , and organized into a tropical disturbance on September 2 with a small, circular area of convection. The system moved to the west, and on September 3, the convection diminished after moving west of 40\xc2\xb0 W.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       By the next day, the tropical disturbance was barely discernible from the clouds of the Intertropical Convergence Zone.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr1_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr1-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The wave gradually became detached from the ITCZ, and based on a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hunters" title="Hurricane Hunters">\n        reconnaissance flight\n       </a>\n       that confirmed the existence of a low-level circulation, it is estimated the system developed into a tropical depression on September 5 while located 255\xc2\xa0miles (415\xc2\xa0km) east of\n       <a href="/wiki/Grenada" title="Grenada">\n        Grenada\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The depression moved westward quickly, and moved through the southern\n       <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">\n        Lesser Antilles\n       </a>\n       early on September 6. The southern portion of the circulation passed over northeastern\n       <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">\n        Venezuela\n       </a>\n       , though after entering the\n       <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">\n        Caribbean Sea\n       </a>\n       , another reconnaissance flight was unable to confirm the existence of a low-level circulation.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr1_2-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr1-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Shortly thereafter, while moving into an area of light\n       <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">\n        wind shear\n       </a>\n       , it was able to organize and strengthen further, and on September 7 the depression strengthened into Tropical Storm\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Edith" title="Edith">\n         Edith\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       near the island of\n       <a href="/wiki/Cura%C3%A7ao" title="Cura\xc3\xa7ao">\n        Cura\xc3\xa7ao\n       </a>\n       . While initially, a cold-core upper-level\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Low_pressure_area" title="Low pressure area">\n        low\n       </a>\n       persisted about 750\xc2\xa0miles (1200\xc2\xa0km) northwest of the storm, Edith moved west-northwestward due to the influence of a narrow and persistent\n       <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">\n        ridge of high pressure\n       </a>\n       , which extended from the southwestern Atlantic Ocean to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">\n        Gulf of Mexico\n       </a>\n       . As the storm continued into open waters of the Caribbean, the upper-level low gradually weakened and was replaced with an\n       <a href="/wiki/Anticyclone" title="Anticyclone">\n        anticyclone\n       </a>\n       . This allowed the storm to strengthen further, and on September 8, Edith became a hurricane in the south-central Caribbean Sea.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_1-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       On September 9, the storm\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" title="Rapid deepening">\n        rapidly intensified\n       </a>\n       , and within 24\xc2\xa0hours, Edith strengthened from a minimal hurricane to a powerful 160\xc2\xa0mph (260\xc2\xa0km/h)\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_Category_5_Atlantic_hurricanes" title="List of Category 5 Atlantic hurricanes">\n        Category 5 hurricane\n       </a>\n       just off the coast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">\n        Nicaragua\n       </a>\n       . The cause for the explosive deepening is unknown, though it is speculated that the transformation in the upper troposphere from an upper-level low to an anticyclone led to a release of\n       <a href="/wiki/Baroclinity" title="Baroclinity">\n        baroclinic\n       </a>\n       energy. Reconnaissance aircraft crews in the peak of the storm reported extreme turbulence, causing concern for the safety of the crews. At its peak intensity, the very well-defined\n       <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">\n        "pinhole" eye\n       </a>\n       was only 5\xc2\xa0miles (8\xc2\xa0km) in diameter. Late on September 9, Hurricane Edith made landfall on northeastern\n       <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">\n        Nicaragua\n       </a>\n       at\n       <a href="/wiki/Cabo_Gracias_a_Dios" title="Cabo Gracias a Dios">\n        Cabo Gracias a Dios\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_1-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Edith.JPG">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="719" data-file-width="899" height="176" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/Hurricane_Edith.JPG/220px-Hurricane_Edith.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/Hurricane_Edith.JPG/330px-Hurricane_Edith.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/Hurricane_Edith.JPG/440px-Hurricane_Edith.JPG 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Edith.JPG" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Radar image of Edith at peak intensity\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Hurricane Edith rapidly weakened over the mountainous terrain of northeastern Central America, and 18\xc2\xa0hours after it made landfall, it emerged into the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Honduras" title="Gulf of Honduras">\n        Gulf of Honduras\n       </a>\n       as an 80\xc2\xa0mph (130\xc2\xa0km/h)\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">\n        Category 1 hurricane\n       </a>\n       . It continued to weaken as it moved northwestward, and made landfall near\n       <a href="/wiki/Belize_City" title="Belize City">\n        Belize City\n       </a>\n       with\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone#Intensity_classifications" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        tropical storm winds\n       </a>\n       of 70\xc2\xa0mph (115\xc2\xa0km/h). Edith weakened further while crossing the\n       <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula" title="Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula">\n        Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula\n       </a>\n       , and emerged into the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">\n        Gulf of Mexico\n       </a>\n       near\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Campeche,_Campeche" title="Campeche, Campeche">\n        Campeche, Mexico\n       </a>\n       late on September 11 as a minimal tropical storm. Edith initially failed to re-intensify as it moved northwestward, despite low amounts of\n       <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">\n        wind shear\n       </a>\n       and warm waters.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_1-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       This was because an anticyclone over the Gulf was closely connected with\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Fern_(1971)" title="Hurricane Fern (1971)">\n        Hurricane Fern\n       </a>\n       , which developed and moved over the northwestern portion of the Gulf of Mexico. The anticyclone resulted in an easterly upper-level flow across Edith, creating\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclogenesis" title="Tropical cyclogenesis">\n        conditions not conducive for intensification\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr4_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr4-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       As Fern moved inland over\n       <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">\n        Texas\n       </a>\n       , the flow became more favorable around Edith, and 36\xc2\xa0hours after entering the Gulf of Mexico, the storm began to reintensify slightly.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_1-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Edith continued moving to the west-northwest, heading towards the coast of Mexico, but early on September 14, the storm stalled while located just off the coast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Tamaulipas" title="Tamaulipas">\n        Tamaulipas\n       </a>\n       . A mid-latitude\n       <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">\n        trough of low pressure\n       </a>\n       approached the storm, and caused Edith to turn to slowly drift towards the northeast. Located only miles from the Mexican coast, Edith again failed to strengthen until September 15, when it accelerated northeastward and regained hurricane status. The hurricane turned to the east-northeast as it approached the coast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">\n        Louisiana\n       </a>\n       , and made landfall on September 16 in a sparsely populated area 30\xc2\xa0miles (48\xc2\xa0km) east of\n       <a href="/wiki/Cameron,_Louisiana" title="Cameron, Louisiana">\n        Cameron\n       </a>\n       with winds of 105\xc2\xa0mph (170\xc2\xa0km/h), equivalent to a Category\xc2\xa02 hurricane in the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">\n        Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale\n       </a>\n       . Edith rapidly weakened over land, degenerating into a tropical storm over Louisiana, and into a tropical depression over\n       <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">\n        Mississippi\n       </a>\n       . It continued to the east-northeast, and dissipated over northwestern\n       <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">\n        Georgia\n       </a>\n       on September 18.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_1-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">\n        Preparations\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Edith_(1971)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Fourteen hours prior to Edith making landfall in Central America, the\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">\n        National Hurricane Center\n       </a>\n       warned citizens about the extreme danger of the approaching hurricane, and asked them to prepare for hurricane conditions. While the storm was located in the Gulf of Mexico, the National Hurricane Center issued a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches#Hurricane_Warning" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">\n        Hurricane Warning\n       </a>\n       from\n       <a href="/wiki/Cameron,_Louisiana" title="Cameron, Louisiana">\n        Cameron\n       </a>\n       to\n       <a href="/wiki/Morgan_City,_Louisiana" title="Morgan City, Louisiana">\n        Morgan City, Louisiana\n       </a>\n       eighteen hours before the hurricane made landfall. Edith later struck land in the middle portion of the warning area.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr6_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr6-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Belize" title="Belize">\n        Belize\n       </a>\n       , officials ordered the mandatory evacuation of low-lying areas, resulting in hundreds of residents leaving to the United States through the international airport. Officials sent police troops to maintain order and prevent looting.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-belizeupi_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-belizeupi-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">\n        Gulf of Mexico\n       </a>\n       , several oil facilities were closed or placed on automatic controls.\n       <a href="/wiki/Drilling_rig" title="Drilling rig">\n        Drilling rigs\n       </a>\n       as far east as the coastal waters off of\n       <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">\n        Mississippi\n       </a>\n       were prepared to evacuate in the event Edith moved further east than anticipated. Additionally, thousands evacuated coastal areas of\n       <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">\n        Louisiana\n       </a>\n       prior to the arrival of the hurricane. Several shelters opened in coastal cities, and many people\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_preparedness" title="Hurricane preparedness">\n        prepared for the hurricane\n       </a>\n       by purchasing emergency supplies. Officials closed schools throughout much of southern Louisiana.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-916ap_6-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-916ap-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">\n        Impact\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n   , and gusts reached 60\xc2\xa0mph (95\xc2\xa0km/h). Two fishermen were reported lost at sea and presumed dead as a result of Edith.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_1-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Edith produced strong winds across northeastern\n       <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">\n        Nicaragua\n       </a>\n       and eastern\n       <a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">\n        Honduras\n       </a>\n       , with\n       <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Lempira" title="Puerto Lempira">\n        Puerto Lempira\n       </a>\n       reporting an unofficial sustained wind of 140\xc2\xa0mph (225\xc2\xa0km/h). The meteorological service in\n       <a href="/wiki/British_Honduras" title="British Honduras">\n        British Honduras\n       </a>\n       stated there were 100 fatalities near Cape Gracias,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr5_7-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr5-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       though a later report indicated 35\xc2\xa0people died in Nicaragua. There, damage was estimated at over US$380,000 (1971 dollars, $2.25\xc2\xa0million in 2016 dollars).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dominica_8-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Dominica-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Three\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Air_Force" title="United States Air Force">\n        United States Air Force\n       </a>\n       aircraft delivered food, medical supplies, and fuel to the hurricane victims of Nicaragua.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cfnet_9-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-cfnet-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In Honduras, the hurricane produced 15\xc2\xa0foot (4.5\xc2\xa0m) tides and strong winds, while strong waves destroyed 40\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fishing_boat" title="Fishing boat">\n        fishing boats\n       </a>\n       as well.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-upi910_10-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-upi910-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       While the hurricane reportedly destroyed entire villages,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-upi916a_11-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-upi916a-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       no deaths occurred in Honduras.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_1-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Offshore islands in\n       <a href="/wiki/Belize" title="Belize">\n        Belize\n       </a>\n       reported winds of up to 60\xc2\xa0mph (95\xc2\xa0km/h).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_1-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Edith produced flooding in a few towns in the southern portion of the country, with some buildings damaged.    The storm dropped light to moderate amounts of rainfall peaking at 3.5\xc2\xa0inches (89\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sabine_Pass,_Texas" title="Sabine Pass, Texas">\n        Sabine Pass\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_1-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The passage of      .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_1-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The highest winds reported by a land station were 69\xc2\xa0mph (111\xc2\xa0km/h) at\n       <a href="/wiki/Cameron,_Louisiana" title="Cameron, Louisiana">\n        Cameron\n       </a>\n       , where a wind gust of 96\xc2\xa0mph (155\xc2\xa0km/h) was also reported. However, due to the lack of recording instruments near the hurricane\'s landing point, whether higher winds occurred there is not known, although likely.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr5_7-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr5-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Rainfall was moderate across Louisiana, including amounts of over 8\xc2\xa0inches (200\xc2\xa0mm) in the southwestern portion of the state.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_1-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A strong\n       <a href="/wiki/Rainband" title="Rainband">\n        rainband\n       </a>\n       well ahead of the hurricane,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_1-14">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       combined with the intrusion of dry air into the hurricane\'s circulation, produced 16 tornadoes from Louisiana to\n       <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">\n        Alabama\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-torn_13-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-torn-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       An\n       <a href="/wiki/Fujita_scale" title="Fujita scale">\n        F3 tornado\n       </a>\n       touched down in the eastern residential suburbs of\n       <a href="/wiki/Baton_Rouge,_Louisiana" title="Baton Rouge, Louisiana">\n        Baton Rouge\n       </a>\n       , causing heavy property damage totaling $2.5\xc2\xa0million (1971 dollars, $14.8\xc2\xa0million in 2016 dollars) along its intermittent 7\xc2\xa0mile (11\xc2\xa0km) path. The tornado also injured three people.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_1-15">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdclatorn_14-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ncdclatorn-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       An F2 tornado in\n       <a href="/wiki/Tangipahoa_Parish,_Louisiana" title="Tangipahoa Parish, Louisiana">\n        Tangipahoa Parish\n       </a>\n       caused $250,000 in damage (1971 dollars, $1.48\xc2\xa0million in 2016 dollars) along its 4\xc2\xa0mile (6\xc2\xa0km) path,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdcla2_15-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ncdcla2-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       while an F1 tornado in\n       <a href="/wiki/St._Martin_Parish,_Louisiana" title="St. Martin Parish, Louisiana">\n        St. Martin Parish\n       </a>\n       injured 6\xc2\xa0people on its 3\xc2\xa0mile (5\xc2\xa0km) path.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdclatorn3_16-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ncdclatorn3-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The hurricane caused extensive damage to the sugar cane crop in southwestern Louisiana.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr5_7-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr5-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       About a month after Edith struck the United States,\n       <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">\n        President\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Richard_Nixon" title="Richard Nixon">\n        Richard Nixon\n       </a>\n       declared portions of\n       <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">\n        Louisiana\n       </a>\n       as a disaster area, which allocated relief funds to aid the affected citizens.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-femala_17-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-femala-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">\n        Mississippi\n       </a>\n       , wind gusts peaked at 70\xc2\xa0mph (112\xc2\xa0km/h) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Hattiesburg,_Mississippi" title="Hattiesburg, Mississippi">\n        Hattiesburg\n       </a>\n       , with multiple locations reporting tropical-storm-force winds. Additionally, Edith produced moderate rainfall peaking at 6.15\xc2\xa0inches (156\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Liberty,_Mississippi" title="Liberty, Mississippi">\n        Liberty\n       </a>\n       . In\n       <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">\n        Alabama\n       </a>\n       , the storm caused light rains, moderate wind gusts, and a storm tide of 2.7\xc2\xa0feet (0.8\xc2\xa0m) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Mobile,_Alabama" title="Mobile, Alabama">\n        Mobile\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_1-16">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Edith spawned four tornadoes in Alabama, three of which were\n       <a href="/wiki/Fujita_scale" title="Fujita scale">\n        F2\n       </a>\n       tornadoes. Two touched down in\n       <a href="/wiki/Baldwin_County,_Alabama" title="Baldwin County, Alabama">\n        Baldwin County\n       </a>\n       ; one destroyed two homes and damaged several others, and the other destroyed two mobile homes, a few barns, and damaged ten houses. Two tornadoes also touched down in\n       <a href="/wiki/Washington_County,_Alabama" title="Washington County, Alabama">\n        Washington County\n       </a>\n       , one of which destroyed several small buildings and downed a few trees.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-alnws_18-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-alnws-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In Florida, Edith produced slightly above-normal tides and light rain. It spawned a tornado in\n       <a href="/wiki/Pensacola,_Florida" title="Pensacola, Florida">\n        Pensacola, Florida\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_1-17">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       injuring one person and inflicting $25,000 in damage (1971 dollars, $125,000 in 2006 dollars).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-flncdc_19-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-flncdc-19">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Damage throughout the United States totaled $25\xc2\xa0million (1971 dollars, $148\xc2\xa0million in 2016 dollars), primarily from crop damage in southwest Louisiana. No deaths were reported in the United States.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_1-18">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n
