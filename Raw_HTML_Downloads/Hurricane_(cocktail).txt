   <tr>\n        <th scope="row" style="width: 33%;">\n         Notes\n        </th>\n        <td>\n         +\n        </td>\n       </tr>\n      </table>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <b>\n        hurricane\n       </b>\n       is a sweet alcoholic drink made with\n       <a href="/wiki/Rum" title="Rum">\n        rum\n       </a>\n       , fruit juice, and\n       <a href="/wiki/Syrup" title="Syrup">\n        syrup\n       </a>\n       or\n       <a href="/wiki/Grenadine" title="Grenadine">\n        grenadine\n       </a>\n       . It is one of many popular drinks served in\n       <a href="/wiki/New_Orleans" title="New Orleans">\n        New Orleans\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The creation of this\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Passion_fruit" title="Passion fruit">\n        passion fruit\n       </a>\n       \xe2\x80\x93colored relative of a\n       <a href="/wiki/Daiquiri" title="Daiquiri">\n        daiquiri\n       </a>\n       is credited to New Orleans tavern owner\n       <a href="/wiki/Pat_O%27Brien%27s_Bar" title="Pat O\'Brien\'s Bar">\n        Pat O\'Brien\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Southern_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Southern-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The bar allegedly started as a\n       <a href="/wiki/Speakeasy" title="Speakeasy">\n        speakeasy\n       </a>\n       called Mr. O\'Brien\'s Club Tipperary and the password was "storm\'s brewin\'\n       <span style="padding-left:.2em;">\n        "\n       </span>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Southern_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Southern-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In the 1940s, he needed to create a new drink to help him get rid of all of the less-popular rum that local distributors forced him to buy before he could get a few cases of more popular liquors such as scotch and other whiskeys.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       He poured the concoction into\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_lamp" title="Hurricane lamp">\n        hurricane lamp\n       </a>\n       \xe2\x80\x93shaped glasses and gave it away to sailors.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The drink caught on, and it has been a mainstay in the\n       <a href="/wiki/French_Quarter" title="French Quarter">\n        French Quarter\n       </a>\n       ever since. It is more commonly served in a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Disposable" title="Disposable">\n        disposable\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Plastic_cup" title="Plastic cup">\n        plastic cup\n       </a>\n       , as New Orleans laws permit\n       <a href="/wiki/Drinking_in_public" title="Drinking in public">\n        drinking in public\n       </a>\n       and leaving a bar with a drink, but the city prohibits public drinking from glass containers.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The hurricane cocktail is made differently on the islands of the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">\n        Bahamas\n       </a>\n       . The drink is composed of various measures of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coffee_liqueur" title="Coffee liqueur">\n        coffee liqueur\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/151_rum" title="151 rum">\n        151 rum\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Irish_cream" title="Irish cream">\n        Irish cream\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Grand_Marnier" title="Grand Marnier">\n        Grand Marnier\n       </a>\n       . It is commonly found in the downtown bars of\n       <a href="/wiki/Nassau,_Bahamas" title="Nassau, Bahamas">\n        Nassau\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span
