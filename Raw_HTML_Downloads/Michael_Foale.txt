was born in\n       <a href="/wiki/Louth,_Lincolnshire" title="Louth, Lincolnshire">\n        Louth, Lincolnshire\n       </a>\n       to a British father, Colin, and an American mother, Mary. He was raised in\n       <a href="/wiki/Cambridge" title="Cambridge">\n        Cambridge\n       </a>\n       and educated at\n       <a href="/wiki/The_King%27s_School,_Canterbury" title="The King\'s School, Canterbury">\n        The King\'s School, Canterbury\n       </a>\n       . A member of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Air_Training_Corps" title="Air Training Corps">\n        Air Training Corps\n       </a>\n       , he studied at\n       <a href="/wiki/Queens%27_College,_Cambridge" title="Queens\' College, Cambridge">\n        Queens\' College\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/University_of_Cambridge" title="University of Cambridge">\n        Cambridge\n       </a>\n       , (with\n       <a href="/wiki/Stephen_Fry" title="Stephen Fry">\n        Stephen Fry\n       </a>\n       who mocked his ambition of going to space) receiving a\n       <a href="/wiki/British_undergraduate_degree_classification" title="British undergraduate degree classification">\n        first-class honours degree\n       </a>\n       in natural sciences in 1978 and a\n       <a href="/wiki/Doctorate" title="Doctorate">\n        doctorate\n       </a>\n       in laboratory astrophysics in 1982. When he left university he "owned two pairs of jeans, a donkey jacket, a bicycle and a pilot\'s licence; which shows I had my priorities absolutely right."\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Times1_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Times1-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       While a postgraduate student at Cambridge University, Foale participated in the organisation and execution of scientific scuba diving projects. Pursuing a career in the US Space Program, Foale moved to Houston, Texas, to work on Space Shuttle navigation problems for\n       <a href="/wiki/McDonnell_Douglas" title="McDonnell Douglas">\n        McDonnell Douglas\n       </a>\n       . In June 1983, Foale joined NASA Johnson Space Center in the payload operations area of the Mission Operations Directorate. In his capacity as payload officer in the Mission Control Center, he was responsible for payload operations on Space Shuttle missions STS-51G, 51-I, 61-B and 61-C.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Born in the United Kingdom, Foale applied and was turned down twice as an astronaut candidate. After the\n       <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Challenger_disaster" title="Space Shuttle Challenger disaster">\n        Space Shuttle Challenger disaster\n       </a>\n       in January 1986, Foale changed his application\n       <a href="/wiki/Essay" title="Essay">\n        essay\n       </a>\n       from writing about his dreams to focusing on the realities of leadership faced by NASA, and was selected in 1987.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Times1_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Times1-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Foale_ZeroG.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2008" data-file-width="3032" height="146" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c4/Foale_ZeroG.jpg/220px-Foale_ZeroG.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c4/Foale_ZeroG.jpg/330px-Foale_ZeroG.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c4/Foale_ZeroG.jpg/440px-Foale_ZeroG.jpg 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Foale_ZeroG.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Michael Foale (foreground) exercising on the\n         <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">\n          ISS\n         </a>\n         <br/>\n         (click to enlarge)\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       He flew on space shuttle missions\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-45" title="STS-45">\n        STS-45\n       </a>\n       (1992),\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-56" title="STS-56">\n        STS-56\n       </a>\n       (1993) and\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-63" title="STS-63">\n        STS-63\n       </a>\n       (1995). In the last mission he undertook a four-hour\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extra-vehicular_activity" title="Extra-vehicular activity">\n        EVA\n       </a>\n       . He was then selected for an extended mission aboard the Russian\n       <a href="/wiki/Mir" title="Mir">\n        Mir\n       </a>\n       space station.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Launched by\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-84" title="STS-84">\n        STS-84\n       </a>\n       and returned by\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-86" title="STS-86">\n        STS-86\n       </a>\n       , Foale spent four months on Mir in 1997 during the Mir 23 and Mir 24 missions. During Mir 23, the station\'s\n       <a href="/wiki/Spektr" title="Spektr">\n        Spektr\n       </a>\n       module was struck by a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Progress_spacecraft" title="Progress spacecraft">\n        Progress\n       </a>\n       resupply vessel.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Using knowledge from his physics degree Foale made calculations of how the stars were moving past his fixed-point\n       <a href="/wiki/Thumb" title="Thumb">\n        thumb\n       </a>\n       reference on a window, and was thus able to advise Russian ground control of how to stop the resulting roll.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Times1_1-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Times1-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The two Russian cosmonauts of Mir 23,\n       <a href="/wiki/Vasily_Tsibliyev" title="Vasily Tsibliyev">\n        Vasily Tsibliyev\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Aleksandr_Lazutkin" title="Aleksandr Lazutkin">\n        Aleksandr Lazutkin\n       </a>\n       , conducted an intravehicular activity (IVA) to inspect the collision damage from the interior of the space station. During Mir 24, Foale and Russian cosmonaut\n       <a href="/wiki/Anatoly_Solovyev" title="Anatoly Solovyev">\n        Anatoly Solovyev\n       </a>\n       conducted a six-hour EVA in the Russian Orlan spacesuit to inspect exterior damage to the Spektr module. For his efforts, he was awarded the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yuri_A._Gagarin_Gold_Medal" title="Yuri A. Gagarin Gold Medal">\n        Yuri A. Gagarin Gold Medal\n       </a>\n       by the\n       <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_A%C3%A9ronautique_Internationale" title="F\xc3\xa9d\xc3\xa9ration A\xc3\xa9ronautique Internationale">\n        F\xc3\xa9d\xc3\xa9ration A\xc3\xa9ronautique Internationale\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In 1999, Foale was a member of space shuttle mission\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-103" title="STS-103">\n        STS-103\n       </a>\n       , during which he conducted an eight-hour\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spacewalk" title="Spacewalk">\n        spacewalk\n       </a>\n       to replace components of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Hubble_Space_Telescope" title="Hubble Space Telescope">\n        Hubble Space Telescope\n       </a>\n       . In 2003, Foale was named commander of\n       <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">\n        International Space Station\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Expedition_8" title="Expedition 8">\n        Expedition 8\n       </a>\n       with cosmonaut\n       <a href="/wiki/Aleksandr_Kaleri" title="Aleksandr Kaleri">\n        Aleksandr Kaleri\n       </a>\n       . Their six-month tour of duty on the station ended on 29 April 2004. For about a year, Foale was Deputy Associate Administrator for Exploration Operations at NASA HQ,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Washington_D.C." title="Washington D.C.">\n        Washington D.C.\n       </a>\n       He then supported Soyuz and ISS operations and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spacesuit" title="Spacesuit">\n        spacesuit\n       </a>\n       development for NASA in Houston.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In August 2013 it was announced that he was leaving the agency after a 26-year career to work on developing an\n       <a href="/wiki/Electric_aircraft" title="Electric aircraft">\n        electric aircraft\n       </a>\n       to advance\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Green_technology" title="Green technology">\n        green aviation technology\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-BBC-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Since leaving NASA, Foale has become actively involved in the running of the International Space Schools Education Trust, especially their Mission Discovery and trips to\n       <a href="/wiki/Star_City,_Russia" title="Star City, Russia">\n        Star City, Russia\n       </a>\n       , giving unique experiences to students around the world. Mission Discovery is held each year at the Guy\'s Campus at King\'s College London.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Honours">\n        Honours\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Michael_Foale&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit <a href="/wiki/Honorary_degree" title="Honorary degree">\n        honorary degree\n       </a>\n       from the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/University_of_Lincolnshire_and_Humberside" href="#cite_note-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and an honorary\n       <a href="/wiki/Doctor_of_Science" title="Doctor of Science">\n        Doctor of Science\n       </a>\n       degree from the\n       <a href="/wiki/University_of_Kent" title="University of Kent">\n        University of Kent\n       </a>\n       in September 2000.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       He was named a Commander in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_British_Empire" title="Order of Honours\n       </a>\n       in December 2004.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       As of 2007, he has been patron to a pastoral house at the Folkestone Academy in Kent, which is also named after him.\n       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">\n        Personal life\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n He is fluent in\n       <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">\n        Russian\n       </a>\n       . Foale is qualified to fly fixed-wing aeroplanes up to fast jets, as well as helicopters. He has never owned a brand-new car, though he has always wanted one, and his hobbies include\n       <a href="/wiki/Windsurfing" title="Windsurfing">\n        windsurfing\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Macram%C3%A9" title="Macram\xc3\xa9">\n        macram\xc3\xa9\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Cross-country_skiing" title="Cross-country skiing">\n        cross-country skiing\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Foale was the driver of a car in 1980 in\n       <a href="/wiki/Yugoslavia" title="Yugoslavia">\n        Yugoslavia\n       </a>\n       , when a truck accidentally veered across the road and smashed into his car, killing his girlfriend and his brother.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Times1_1-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Times1-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       His father, Colin Foale, wrote a book in 1999 titled\n       <i>\n        Waystation to the Stars\n       </i>\n       about the astronaut\'s experiences on Mir.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In his spare time he has also worked with ISSET. This involvement is through him taking up the role of the resident astronaut during many of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Mission_Discovery" title="Mission Discovery">\n        Mission Discovery\n       </a>\n       programme operated by ISSET. At these programs, Foale shares his experiences of being in space with teenagers, while at the same time helping them learn new skills. He does this throughout the period of the time the programme runs.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n
