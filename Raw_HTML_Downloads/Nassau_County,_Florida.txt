</span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nassau_County,_Florida&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Nassau County was created in 1824 from Duval County. It was named for the\n       <a href="/wiki/Duchy_of_Nassau" title="Duchy of Nassau">\n        Duchy of Nassau\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">\n        Germany\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Law_and_government">\n        Law and government\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nassau_County,_Florida&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Law and government">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Nassau County is governed by the five-member Nassau County Board of County Commissioners, who are elected to four-year terms by the voters. The terms are staggered so that either three or two commissioners (alternately) are up for election every two years.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The main environmental and agricultural body is the\n       <a href="/wiki/Nassau_County_Soil_and_Water_Conservation_District" title="Nassau County Soil and Water Conservation District">\n        Nassau County Soil and Water Conservation District\n       </a>\n       , which works closely with other area agencies.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Local law enforcement is conducted by the\n       <a href="/wiki/Nassau_County_Sheriff%27s_Office_(Florida)" title="Nassau County Sheriff\'s Office (Florida)">\n        Nassau County Sheriff\'s Office,\n       </a>\n       under Sheriff Bill Leeper.\n       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">\n        [\n        <i>\n         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">\n          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (January 2017)">\n           citation needed\n          </span>\n         </a>\n        </i>\n        ]\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">\n        Geography\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nassau_County,_Florida&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Geography">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       According to the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Census_Bureau" title="U.S. Census Bureau">\n        U.S. Census Bureau\n       </a>\n       , the county has a total area of 726 square miles (1,880\xc2\xa0km\n       <sup>\n        2\n       </sup>\n       ), of which 649 square miles (1,680\xc2\xa0km\n       <sup>\n        2\n       </sup>\n       ) is land and 77 square miles (200\xc2\xa0km\n       <sup>\n        2\n       </sup>\n       ) (10.6%) is water.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR1_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-GR1-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The city of Fernandina Beach is located on\n       <a href="/wiki/Amelia_Island" title="Amelia Island">\n        Amelia Island\n       </a>\n       , the county\xe2\x80\x99s one inhabited island. Fernandina Beach municipality extends across the Intracoastal Waterway along A1A to Yulee.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Topography">\n        Topography\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nassau_County,_Florida&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Topography">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       There are 12 distinct topographical zones in Nassau County. Most of these zones run in narrow bands stretching from north to south, although this is less true as one approaches the Atlantic coast.\n      </p>\n      <ol>\n       <li>\n        Directly against the western border with\n        <a href="/wiki/Baker_County,_Florida" title="Baker County, Florida">\n         Baker County\n        </a>\n        , the topography ranges from fairly flat to slightly elevated. Drainage is poor and the soil is sandy.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Moving east, there are some areas (mostly in the northern and central county) of higher ground with much better drainage.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        East of these areas are some lower places, especially in the south, that are level and have extremely poor drainage.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Eastward again, there is a stretch that ranges from a few miles in the extreme northern areas to about 6-8 miles (13\xc2\xa0km) wide in the southern area, including\n        <a href="/wiki/Hilliard,_Florida" title="Hilliard, Florida">\n         Hilliard\n        </a>\n        and much of County Road 108 and State Road 301. This area again has very poor drainage, low-lying land, and fairly sandy soil.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        East of this are scattered areas of high, sandy land with spotty or poor drainage.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        East of this, there is an area including\n        <a href="/wiki/Callahan,_Florida" title="Callahan, Florida">\n         Callahan\n        </a>\n        with very sandy soil on top, and clay underneath. This section of the county is heavily permeated by small creeks and rivers, which bring with them low, poorly drained soils. This zone extends across the entire county from north to south at a fairly consistent width of about 3-4 miles (6.4\xc2\xa0km), except in the north, where it widens to nearly 6 miles (9.7\xc2\xa0km) across.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        East of this area is a large band of land with a consistent width of about 8 miles (13\xc2\xa0km). The land is low and level with extremely poor drainage, and it is permeated by small creeks and rivers. In the northern section, this is where some tributaries join the\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Marys_River_(Florida/Georgia)" title="St. Marys River (Florida/Georgia)">\n         St. Marys River\n        </a>\n        , while in the south a number of tributaries drain together into the Nassau River, which flows into the Nassau Sound and the Atlantic Ocean.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        East of this area is a section of land about 3 miles (4.8\xc2\xa0km) in width that has extremely sandy soils with bad drainage all around.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Further eastward is a large area, including\n        <a href="/wiki/Yulee,_Florida" title="Yulee, Florida">\n         Yulee\n        </a>\n        and O\'Neil, about 4 miles (6.4\xc2\xa0km) in width, with poor drainage and sandy soil at higher elevations, pockmarked by large reas of low lands with even worse drainage.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        To the south is an area of low-lying, organic soils which are essentially marshes and wetlands along the northern bank of the\n        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Nassau_River&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Nassau River (page does not exist)">\n         Nassau River\n        </a>\n        , continuing into the Nassau Sound.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Still eastward and somewhat to the north is a large area of marshes and organic soils which characterize area wetlands. There are many small islands in this area, and it is permeated by the Bells River and Jolly River, which empty into the Cumberland Sound to the north, just below\n        <a href="/wiki/Cumberland_Island" title="Cumberland Island">\n         Cumberland Island\n        </a>\n        .\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        <a href="/wiki/Amelia_Island" title="Amelia Island">\n         Amelia Island\n        </a>\n        , the easternmost section of the county, is characterized by poor drainage in the west and better drained, higher, sandier land as one travels eastward towards the beach. The northern area of the island features salty marshlands surrounding Egan\xe2\x80\x99s Creek, which runs directly beneath Atlantic Boulevard in Fernandina Beach.\n       </li>\n      </ol>\n Much of the land used for tree farming is owned by\n       <a href="/wiki/Rayonier" title="Rayonier">\n        Rayonier\n       </a>\n       , a major employer in the area, and the owner of a large pulp mill in Fernandina Beach. Historically, tree farming, trucking, and pulp production have characterized a large portion of the local economy. However, extensive growth is occurring in the outlying small towns in the rural western area, and the home construction market is still quite strong. Fernandina Beach has long been known for professional industries such as real estate, legal services, and medical care, and is also the closest city to two upscale resorts: the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ritz-Carlton" title="Ritz-Carlton">\n        Ritz-Carlton\n       </a>\n       Hotel and\n       <a href="/wiki/Amelia_Island_Plantation" title="Amelia Island Plantation">\n        Amelia Island Plantation\n       </a>\n       . In the central area of the county, a private tract of land known as the\n       <a href="/wiki/White_Oak_Conservation" title="White Oak Conservation">\n        White Oak Conservation\n       </a>\n       was once owned by a wealthy family who ran the area as a private zoo, complete with exotic animals such as giraffes and large albino cats. Libraries\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       The Nassau Coounty Public Library System mission statement includes the following goals: That it will provide a safe and comfortable physical space for use by county residents and visitors That it will provide high speed access to "the digital world" so that everyone can use the growing number of resources and services available through the internet That it will provide for children from birth to age 5 programs and services designed to ensure that they enter school ready to read, write and listen That it will provide library materials in a variety of formats to enhance patrons\' leisure time, and assistance to find what those patrons want, and the help they need to make appropriate choices among the options available to them That it will provide the resources they need to explore topics of personal interest so that they may continue to learn throughout their lives The Nassau County Public Library has 5 branches.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       A major renovation of the Fernandina Beach branch was completed in 2015 and a grand opening of the branch was held in April, 2016. The newly renovated library, located at 25 North 4th Street, doubled its size to approximately 16,000 square feet. Per the Nassau County Public Library\'s long range plan for 2013-2017, this renovation brought that building, originally opened in 1976, up to current Nassau County code. A resolution between the City of Fernandina Beach and Nassau County was also passed that details the ownership of the Library facility and Library operations. The Fernandina Beach branch also houses over one thousand titles that belong to the Amelia Island Genealogical Society.
