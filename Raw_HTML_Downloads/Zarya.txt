      Zarya and Unity rendezvous for the first time in 1998\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        Zarya\n       </b>\n       (\n       <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">\n        Russian\n       </a>\n       :\n       <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">\n        \xd0\x97\xd0\xb0\xd1\x80\xd1\x8f\xcc\x81\n       </span>\n       ; lit.\n       <i>\n        Red Sky Glow, Aurora,\n        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">\n         <a href="#cite_note-1">\n          [1]\n         </a>\n        </sup>\n       </i>\n       Sunrise), also known as the\n       <b>\n        <a href="/wiki/Functional_Cargo_Block" title="Functional Cargo Block">\n         Functional Cargo Block\n        </a>\n       </b>\n       or\n       <b>\n        FGB\n       </b>\n       (from the Russian "\n       <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">\n        \xd0\xa4\xd1\x83\xd0\xbd\xd0\xba\xd1\x86\xd0\xb8\xd0\xbe\xd0\xbd\xd0\xb0\xd0\xbb\xd1\x8c\xd0\xbd\xd0\xbe-\xd0\xb3\xd1\x80\xd1\x83\xd0\xb7\xd0\xbe\xd0\xb2\xd0\xbe\xd0\xb9 \xd0\xb1\xd0\xbb\xd0\xbe\xd0\xba\n       </span>\n       ",\n       <span lang="ru-Latn" xml:lang="ru-Latn">\n        <i>\n         Funktsionalno-gruzovoy blok\n        </i>\n       </span>\n       or\n       <i>\n        \xd0\xa4\xd0\x93\xd0\x91\n       </i>\n       ), was the first module of the\n       <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">\n        International Space Station\n       </a>\n       to be launched.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NASA_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NASA-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The FGB provided electrical power, storage, propulsion, and guidance to the ISS during the initial stage of assembly. With the launch and assembly in orbit of other modules with more specialized functionality, Zarya is now primarily used for storage, both inside the pressurized section and in the externally mounted fuel tanks. The Zarya is a descendant of the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TKS_spacecraft" title="TKS spacecraft">\n        TKS spacecraft\n       </a>\n       designed for the Russian\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salyut" title="Salyut">\n        Salyut\n       </a>\n       program. The name\n       <i>\n        Zarya\n       </i>\n       , which means sunrise,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NASA_2-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NASA-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       was given to the FGB because it signified the dawn of a new era of international cooperation in space. Although it was built by a Russian company, it is owned by the\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">\n        United States\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="toc">\n       <div class="toctitle">\n   </a>\n        </li>\n       </ul>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Construction">\n        Construction\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Zarya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Construction">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       The FGB design was originally intended as a module for the Russian\n       <a href="/wiki/Mir" title="Mir">\n        Mir\n       </a>\n       space station, but was not flown as of the end of the Mir program. A FGB cargo block was incorporated as an upper stage engine into the\n       <a href="/wiki/Polyus_(spacecraft)" title="Polyus (spacecraft)">\n        Polyus\n       </a>\n       spacecraft, flown (unsuccessfully) on the first\n       <a href="/wiki/Energia" title="Energia">\n        Energia\n       </a>\n       launch.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-JBIS-2002_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-JBIS-2002-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       With the end of the Mir program, the design was adapted to use for the International Space Station.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The Zarya module is capable of\n       <a href="/wiki/Orbital_station-keeping" title="Orbital station-keeping">\n        station keeping\n       </a>\n       and provides sizable battery power; it was suggested to have initially been built to both power and control the recoil from a further derivation of the then classified\n       <a href="/wiki/Strategic_Defense_Initiative#space_based_lasers" title="Strategic Defense Initiative">\n        Skif laser system\n       </a>\n       /Polyus satellite. Commentators in the West thought that the Zarya module was constructed cheaper and lifted to orbit faster than what should have been possible in the post-Soviet era, and that the FGB might have been largely constructed from\n       <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/mothball#Verb" title="wikt:mothball">\n        mothballed\n       </a>\n       hardware from the Skif laser program (which had been canceled after the failed 1987 Polyus launch).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The research and development of a similar design was paid for by Russia and the Soviet Union, the design of the module and all systems are Soviet/Russian. The United States funded Zarya through the U.S. prime contracts in the 1990s as the first module for ISS. Built from December 1994 to January 1998 in\n       <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">\n        Russia\n       </a>\n       at the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Khrunichev" title="Khrunichev">\n        Khrunichev State Research and Production Space Center\n       </a>\n       (KhSC) in Moscow,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NASA_2-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NASA-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       its control system was developed by the\n       <a href="/wiki/Khartron" title="Khartron">\n        Khartron Corp.\n       </a>\n       (\n       <a href="/wiki/Kharkiv" title="Kharkiv">\n        Kharkiv\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">\n        Ukraine\n       </a>\n       ). The module was included as part of NASA\'s plan for the International Space Station (ISS) instead of\n       <a href="/wiki/Lockheed_Martin" title="Lockheed Martin">\n        Lockheed\n       </a>\n       \'s "Bus-1" option because it was significantly cheaper (\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/US$" title="US$">\n        US$\n       </a>\n       220 million vs. $450 million). As part of the contract Khrunichev constructed much of an identical module (referred to as "FGB-2") for contingency purposes. FGB-2 has been proposed for a variety of projects; it is now slated to be used to construct the Russian Multipurpose Laboratory Module\n       <a href="/wiki/Nauka_(ISS_module)" title="Nauka (ISS module)">\n        Nauka\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Design">\n        Design\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Zarya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Design">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Zarya has a mass of 19,323 kilograms (42,600\xc2\xa0lb), is 12.56 meters (41.2\xc2\xa0ft) long and 4.11 meters (13.5\xc2\xa0ft) wide at its widest point.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The module has three\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spacecraft_docking" title="Spacecraft docking">\n        docking ports\n       </a>\n       : one axially on the front end at the docking sphere, one on the earth-facing side (\n       <a href="/wiki/Nadir" title="Nadir">\n        nadir\n       </a>\n       ) of the docking sphere and one axially on the aft end. Attached to the forward port is the\n       <a href="/wiki/Pressurized_Mating_Adapter" title="Pressurized Mating Adapter">\n        Pressurized Mating Adapter\n       </a>\n       <i>\n        PMA-1\n       </i>\n       , which in turn is connected to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Unity_(ISS_module)" title="Unity (ISS module)">\n        <i>\n         Unity\n        </i>\n        Module\n       </a>\n       \xe2\x80\x93 this is the connection between the\n       <a href="/wiki/Russian_Orbital_Segment" title="Russian Orbital Segment">\n        Russian Orbital Segment\n       </a>\n       (ROS) and the\n       <a href="/wiki/US_Orbital_Segment" title="US Orbital Segment">\n        US Orbital Segment\n       </a>\n       (USOS). Attached to the aft port is the\n       <a href="/wiki/Zvezda_(ISS_module)" title="Zvezda (ISS module)">\n        Zvezda Service Module\n       </a>\n       . The lower port (nadir) was initially used by visiting\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soyuz_spacecraft" title="Soyuz spacecraft">\n        Soyuz spacecraft\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Progress_spacecraft" title="Progress spacecraft">\n        Progress spacecraft\n       </a>\n       to dock to the ROS; The\n       <a href="/wiki/Rassvet_(ISS_module)" title="Rassvet (ISS module)">\n        <i>\n         Rassvet\n        </i>\n        module\n       </a>\n       is now docked semipermanently on the nadir port of Zarya, and visiting spacecraft use\n       <i>\n        Rassvet\'\n       </i>\n       s nadir docking port instead.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Zarya has two\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Photovoltaic_module" title="Photovoltaic module">\n        solar arrays\n       </a>\n       measuring 10.67 by 3.35 meters (35.0 by 11.0\xc2\xa0ft) and six\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nickel-cadmium_battery" title="Nickel-cadmium battery">\n        nickel-cadmium batteries\n       </a>\n       that can provide an average of 3 kilowatts of power \xe2\x80\x93 the solar arrays have been however retracted so the P1/S1 radiators of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Integrated_Truss_Structure" title="Integrated Truss Structure">\n        Integrated Truss Structure\n       </a>\n       could deploy. Zarya has 16 external fuel tanks that can hold over 5.4 metric tons of propellant. Zarya also has 24 large steering jets, 12 small steering jets, and two large engines that were used for reboost and major orbital changes; with the docking of Zvezda these are now permanently disabled. Since they are no longer needed for Zarya\'s engines, Zarya\'s propellant tanks are now used to store additional fuel for Zvezda.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Launch_and_flight">\n        Launch and flight\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Zarya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Launch and flight">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:STS-88_Sergei_Krikalev_and_Robert_Cabana_at_an_internal_hatch_in_the_Zarya_module.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1944" data-file-width="2972" height="196" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/STS-88_Sergei_Krikalev_and_Robert_Cabana_at_an_internal_hatch_in_the_Zarya_module.jpg/300px-STS-88_Sergei_Krikalev_and_Robert_Cabana_at_an_internal_hatch_in_the_Zarya_module.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/STS-88_Sergei_Krikalev_and_Robert_Cabana_at_an_internal_hatch_in_the_Zarya_module.jpg/450px-STS-88_Sergei_Krikalev_and_Robert_Cabana_at_an_internal_hatch_in_the_Zarya_module.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/STS-88_Sergei_Krikalev_and_Robert_Cabana_at_an_internal_hatch_in_the_Zarya_module.jpg/600px-STS-88_Sergei_Krikalev_and_Robert_Cabana_at_an_internal_hatch_in_the_Zarya_module.jpg 2x" width="300"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:STS-88_Sergei_Krikalev_and_Robert_Cabana_at_an_internal_hatch_in_the_Zarya_module.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Sergei K. After Zarya reached orbit,\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-88" title="STS-88">\n        STS-88\n       </a>\n       launched on December 4, 1998 to attach the Unity Module.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Although only designed to fly autonomously for six to eight months, Zarya was required to do so for almost two years due to delays to the Russian Service Module,\n       <a href="/wiki/Zvezda_(ISS_module)" title="Zvezda (ISS module)">\n        Zvezda\n       </a>\n       . Zvezda was finally launched on July 12, 2000, docking with Zarya on July 26 using the Russian\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kurs_(docking_system)" title="Kurs (docking system)">\n        Kurs docking system\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Zarya initially had problems with battery charging circuits, but these were resolved.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Zarya passed the 50,000-orbit mark at 15:17 UTC on August 14, 2007 during the\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-118" title="STS-118">\n        STS-118\n       </a>\n       mission to the International Space Station.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Dockings">\n        Dockings\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Zarya&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Dockings">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable plainlinks" role="note">\n       This list is\n       <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Lists#Incomplete_lists" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Lists">\n        incomplete\n       </a>\n       ;
