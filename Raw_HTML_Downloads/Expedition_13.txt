   Expedition 13\n       </b>\n       was the 13th expedition to the\n       <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">\n        International Space Station\n       </a>\n       (ISS), and launched at 02:30\n       <a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">\n        UTC\n       </a>\n       on 30 March 2006.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nasa_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nasa-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The expedition used the\n       <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-8" Astronaut\n       <a href="/wiki/Marcos_Pontes" title="Marcos Pontes">\n        Marcos Pontes\n       </a>\n       launched with Expedition 13 on the Soyuz TMA-8 spacecraft and became the first Brazilian in space. He returned with\n       <a href="/wiki/Expedition_12" title="Expedition 12">\n        Expedition 12\n       </a>\n       on\n       <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-7" title="Soyuz TMA-7">\n        Soyuz TMA-7\n       </a>\n       after a nine-day mission.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Reiter" title="Thomas Reiter">\n        Thomas Reiter\n       </a>\n       , from the\n       <a href="/wiki/European_Space_Agency" title="European Space Agency">\n        European Space Agency\n       </a>\n       , became part of the Expedition 13 crew in July 2006. class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_13&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit class="mw-headline" id="EVA_1">\n        EVA 1\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_13&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: EVA 1">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       The first spacewalk for Expedition 13, performed by\n       <a href="/wiki/Pavel_Vinogradov" title="Pavel Vinogradov">\n        Pavel Vinogradov\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Jeffrey_Williams_(astronaut)" title="Jeffrey Williams (astronaut)">\n        Jeffrey Williams\n       </a>\n       , began on 1 June, and ended on 2 June 2006.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       It was originally scheduled to include a\n       <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station#ISS_golf_event" title="International Space Station">\n        golf shot off the space station\n       </a>\n       but the event was postponed to\n       <a href="/wiki/Expedition_14" title="Expedition 14">\n        Expedition 14\n       </a>\n       , as NASA was still evaluating the risks.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-golf_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-golf-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The spacewalk ran behind schedule, requiring an extra 50 minutes to be added to the length in order to complete the camera replacement. The EVA began 1 June, at 23:48 UTC and ended 2 June at 06:19 UTC, lasting 6 hours and 31 minutes. Other tasks during the walk included repair of a vent for the station\'s oxygen-producing Elektron unit, and retrieval of experiment packages.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eva1_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-eva1-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="EVA_2">\n        EVA 2\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_13&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: EVA 2">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       The second spacewalk for Expedition 13 occurred on 3 August 2006. The spacewalk was performed by Jeffrey Williams and Thomas Reiter, it began at 14:04 UTC and ended at 19:58 UTC, for a duration of 5 hours and 54 minutes.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       During the spacewalk the astronauts installed the Floating Potential Measurement Unit (FPMU), installed two Materials on\n       <a href="/wiki/Materials_International_Space_Station_Experiment" title="Materials International Space Station Experiment">\n        Materials International Space Station Experiment\n       </a>\n       (MISSE) containers, installed a controller for a thermal radiator rotary joint, replaced a computer and installed a starboard jumper and spool positioning device (SPD), inspected a radiator beam valve module and installed another, installed a port jumper and SPD, tested an infrared camera, installed a light on the truss railway handcart, replaced a malfunctioning\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GPS" title="GPS">\n        GPS\n       </a>\n       antenna, installed a vacuum system valve, moved two articulated portable foot restraints, photographed a scratch on the airlock hatch, and retrieved a ball stack for inspection from PMA-1.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eva2_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-eva2-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="References">\n
