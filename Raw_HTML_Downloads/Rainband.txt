</div>\n      <p>\n       A\n       <b>\n        rainband\n       </b>\n       is a\n       <a href="/wiki/Cloud" title="Cloud">\n        cloud\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Precipitation_(meteorology)" title="Precipitation (meteorology)">\n        precipitation\n       </a>\n       structure associated with an area of rainfall which is significantly elongated. Rainbands can be\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Stratiform" title="Stratiform">\n        stratiform\n       </a>\n       or\n       <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_convection" title="Atmospheric convection">\n        convective\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and are generated by differences in temperature. When noted on\n       <a href="/wiki/Weather_radar" title="Weather radar">\n        weather radar\n       </a>\n       imagery, this precipitation elongation is referred to as banded structure.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Rainbands within tropical cyclones are curved in orientation. Tropical cyclone rainbands contain showers and thunderstorms that, together with the eyewall and the eye, constitute a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        hurricane or tropical storm\n       </a>\n       . The extent of rainbands around a tropical cyclone can help determine the cyclone\'s intensity.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Rainbands spawned near and ahead of\n       <a href="/wiki/Cold_front" title="Cold front">\n        cold fronts\n       </a>\n       can be\n       <a href="/wiki/Squall_line" title="Squall line">\n        squall lines\n       </a>\n       which are able to produce\n       <a href="/wiki/Tornado" title="Tornado">\n        tornadoes\n       </a>\n       . Rainbands associated with cold fronts can be warped by mountain barriers perpendicular to the front\'s orientation due to the formation of a low-level\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barrier_jet" title="Barrier jet">\n        barrier jet\n       </a>\n       . Bands of thunderstorms can form with\n       <a href="/wiki/Sea_breeze" title="Sea breeze">\n        sea breeze\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Land_breeze" title="Land breeze">\n        land breeze\n       </a>\n       boundaries, if enough moisture is present. If sea breeze rainbands become active enough just ahead of a cold front, they can mask the location of the cold front itself. Banding within the comma head precipitation pattern of an\n       <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">\n        extratropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       can yield significant amounts of\n       <a href="/wiki/Rain" title="Rain">\n        rain\n       </a>\n       or\n       <a href="/wiki/Snow" title="Snow">\n        snow\n       </a>\n       . Behind extratropical cyclones, rainbands can form downwind of relative warm bodies of water such as the\n       <a href="/wiki/Great_Lakes" title="Great Lakes">\n        Great Lakes\n       </a>\n       . If the atmosphere is cold enough, these rainbands can yield heavy snow.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="toc">\n       <div class="toctitle">\n        <h2>\n href="#cite_note-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Rainbands within tropical cyclones require ample moisture and a low level pool of cooler air.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Bands located 80 kilometres (50\xc2\xa0mi) to 150 kilometres (93\xc2\xa0mi) from a cyclone\'s and\n       <a href="/wiki/Squall" title="Squall">\n        squalls\n       </a>\n       of wind, as well as tornadoes,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">\n        <a href="#cite_note-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Some rainbands move closer to the center, forming a secondary, or outer,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eyewall" title="Eyewall">\n        eyewall\n       </a>\n       within intense hurricanes.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">\n        <a href="#cite_note-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Spiral rainbands are such a basic structure to a tropical cyclone that in most\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_basins" title="Tropical cyclone      is the primary method used to determine a tropical cyclone\'s\n       <a href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_wind" title="Maximum sustained wind">\n        maximum sustained winds\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ODT_17-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ODT-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Within this method, the extent of spiral banding and difference in temperature between the\n       <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">\n        eye\n       </a>\n       and eyewall is used to assign a maximum sustained wind and a central pressure.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">\n        <a href="#cite_note-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_pressure" pressure\n       </a>\n       derived from this technique are approximate.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Different programs have been studying these rainbands, including\n       <a class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Convective rainbands can form parallel to terrain on its\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Windward" title="Windward">\n        windward\n       </a>\n       side, due to\n       <a href="/wiki/Lee_wave" title="Lee wave">\n        lee waves\n       </a>\n       triggered by hills just upstream of the cloud\'s formation.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">\n        <a href="#cite_note-19">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Their spacing is normally 5 kilometres (3.1\xc2\xa0mi) to 10 kilometres (6.2\xc2\xa0mi) apart.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">\n        <a href="#cite_note-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       When bands of precipitation near frontal zones approach steep topography, a low-level barrier\n       <a href="/wiki/Jet_stream" title="Jet stream">\n        jet stream\n       </a>\n       forms parallel to and just prior to the mountain ridge, which slows down the frontal rainband just prior to the mountain barrier.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">\n        <a href="#cite_note-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       If enough moisture is present,\n       <a href="/wiki/Sea_breeze" title="Sea breeze">\n        sea breeze\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Land_breeze" title="Land breeze">\n        land breeze\n       </a>\n       fronts can form convective rainbands. Sea breeze front\n       <a href="/wiki/Thunderstorm" title="Thunderstorm">\n        thunderstorm\n       </a>\n       lines can become strong enough to mask the location of an approaching cold front by evening.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">\n        <a href="#cite_note-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The edge of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ocean_currents" title="Ocean currents">\n        ocean currents\n       </a>\n       can lead to the development of thunderstorm bands due to heat differential at this interface.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">\n        <a href="#cite_note-23">\n         [23]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Downwind of islands, bands of showers and thunderstorms can develop due to low level wind convergence downwind of the island edges. Offshore\n       <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">\n        California\n       </a>\n       , this has been noted in the wake of cold fronts.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">\n        <a href="#cite_note-24">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="References">\n        References\n
