At the summer\n       <a href="/wiki/Solstice" title="Solstice">\n        solstice\n       </a>\n       , the days are longest and the nights are shortest, with day-length decreasing as the season progresses after the solstice. The date of the beginning of summer varies according to\n       <a href="/wiki/Climate" title="Climate">\n        climate\n       </a>\n       , tradition and culture.    </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Summer_timing">\n        Summer timing\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Summer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Summer timing">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       From an astronomical view, the\n       <a href="/wiki/Equinox" title="Equinox">\n        equinoxes\n       </a>\n       and solstices would be the middle of the respective seasons,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       but sometimes astronomical summer is defined as starting at the solstice, the time of maximal\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Insolation" title="Insolation">\n        insolation\n       </a>\n       , or on the traditional date of June 21. A variable\n       <a href="/wiki/Seasonal_lag" title="Seasonal lag">\n        seasonal lag\n       </a>\n       means that the\n       <a href="/wiki/Meteorology" title="Meteorology">\n        meteorological\n       </a>\n       center of the season, which is based on average temperature patterns, occurs several weeks after the time of maximal insolation.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The meteorological convention is to define summer as comprising the months of June, July, and August in the northern hemisphere and the months of December, January, and February in the southern hemisphere.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Meteorological_Glossary_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Meteorological_Glossary-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Under meteorological definitions, all seasons are arbitrarily set to start at the beginning of a calendar month and end at the end of a month.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Meteorological_Glossary_4-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Meteorological_Glossary-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       This meteorological definition of summer also aligns with the commonly viewed notion of summer as the season with the longest (and warmest) days of the year, in which daylight predominates. The meteorological reckoning of seasons is used in\n       <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">\n        Australia\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">\n        Austria\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">\n        Denmark\n       </a>\n       , the former\n       <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">\n        Soviet Union\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">\n        Japan\n       </a>\n       . It is also used by many in the\n       <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">\n        United Kingdom\n       </a>\n       . In\n       <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">\n        Ireland\n       </a>\n       , the summer months according to the national meteorological service,\n       <a href="/wiki/Met_%C3%89ireann" title="Met \xc3\x89ireann">\n        Met \xc3\x89ireann\n       </a>\n       , are June, July and August. However, according to the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Irish_Calendar" title="Irish Calendar">\n        Irish Calendar\n       </a>\n       , summer begins on 1 May and ends on 1 August. School textbooks in Ireland follow the cultural norm of summer commencing on 1 May rather than the meteorological definition of 1 June.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Days continue to lengthen from equinox to solstice and summer days progressively shorten after the solstice, so meteorological summer encompasses the build-up to the longest day and a diminishing thereafter, with summer having many more hours of daylight than spring. Reckoning by hours of daylight alone, summer\n       <a href="/wiki/Solstice" title="Solstice">\n        solstice\n       </a>\n       marks the midpoint, not the beginning, of the seasons.\n       <a href="/wiki/Midsummer" title="Midsummer">\n        Midsummer\n       </a>\n       takes place over the shortest night of the year, which is the summer solstice, or on a nearby date that varies with tradition.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Where a\n       <a href="/wiki/Seasonal_lag" title="Seasonal lag">\n        seasonal lag\n       </a>\n       of half a season or more is common, reckoning based on astronomical markers is shifted half a season.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       By this method, in North America, summer is the period from the\n       <a href="/wiki/Summer_solstice" title="Summer solstice">\n        summer solstice\n       </a>\n       (usually 20 or 21 June in the Northern Hemisphere) to the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Autumn_equinox" title="Autumn equinox">\n        autumn equinox\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Reckoning by cultural festivals, the summer season in the\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">\n        United States\n       </a>\n       is commonly regarded as beginning on\n       <a href="/wiki/Memorial_Day" title="Memorial Day">\n        Memorial Day\n       </a>\n       weekend (the last weekend in May) and ending on\n       <a href="/wiki/Labor_Day" title="Labor Day">\n        Labor Day\n       </a>\n       weekend (the first weekend in September), more closely in line with the meteorological definition for the parts of the country that have four-season weather. The similar\n       <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">\n        Canadian\n       </a>\n       tradition starts summer on\n       <a href="/wiki/Victoria_Day" title="Victoria Day">\n        Victoria Day\n       </a>\n       one week prior (although summer conditions vary widely across Canada\'s expansive territory) and ends, as in the United States, on\n       <a href="/wiki/Labour_Day" title="Labour Day">\n        Labour Day\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Chinese_astronomy" title="Chinese astronomy">\n        Chinese astronomy\n       </a>\n       , summer starts on or around 5 May, with the\n       <i>\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jieqi" title="Jieqi">\n         ji\xc3\xa9q\xc3\xac\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       (\n       <a href="/wiki/Solar_term" title="Solar term">\n        solar term\n       </a>\n       ) known as\n       <a href="/wiki/Lixia" title="Lixia">\n        l\xc3\xacxi\xc3\xa0\n       </a>\n       (\xe7\xab\x8b\xe5\xa4\x8f), i.e. "establishment of summer", and it ends on or around 6 August.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In southern and southeast Asia, where the\n       <a href="/wiki/Monsoon" title="Monsoon">\n        monsoon\n       </a>\n       occurs, summer is more generally defined as lasting from March, April, May and June, the warmest time of the year, ending with the onset of the monsoon\n       <a href="/wiki/Rain" title="Rain">\n        rains\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">\n        [\n        <i>\n         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">\n          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2008)">\n           citation needed\n          </span>\n         </a>\n        </i>\n        ]\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Because the temperature lag is shorter in the oceanic temperate southern hemisphere,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       most countries in this region use the meteorological definition with summer starting on 1 December and ending on the last day of February.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span Summer is traditionally associated with hot or warm weather. In the Mediterranean regions, it is also associated with dry weather, while in other places (particularly in\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eastern_Asia" title="Eastern Asia">\n        Eastern Asia\n       </a>\n       because of the Monsoon) it is associated with rainy weather. The wet season is the main period of vegetation growth within the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Savanna_climate" title="Savanna climate">\n        savanna climate\n       </a>\n       regime.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Where the wet season is associated with a seasonal shift in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Prevailing_winds" title="Prevailing winds">\n        prevailing winds\n       </a>\n       , it is known as a monsoon.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">\n </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       In the northern Atlantic Ocean, a distinct tropical cyclone season occurs from 1 June to 30 November.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AOML_FAQ_G1_15-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-AOML_FAQ_G1-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The statistical peak of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="Atlantic hurricane season">\n        Atlantic hurricane season\n       </a>\n       is 10 September. The Northeast Pacific Ocean has a broader period of activity, but in a similar time frame to the Atlantic.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NH_Atl_climatology_16-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NH_Atl_climatology-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The Northwest Pacific sees tropical cyclones year-round, with a minimum in February and March and a peak in early September. In the North Indian basin, storms are most common from April to December, with peaks in May and November.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AOML_FAQ_G1_15-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-AOML_FAQ_G1-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In the\n       <a href="/wiki/Southern_Hemisphere" title="Southern Hemisphere">\n        Southern Hemisphere\n       </a>\n       , the tropical cyclone season runs from 1 November until the end of April with peaks in mid-February to early March.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AOML_FAQ_G1_15-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-AOML_FAQ_G1-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCOP_SEIO.26SPAC_17-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCOP_SEIO.26SPAC-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <a href="/wiki/Thunderstorm" title="Thunderstorm">\n        Thunderstorm\n       </a>\n       season in the United States and Canada runs in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Spring_(season)" title="Spring (season)">\n        spring\n       </a>\n       through summer. These storms can produce\n       <a href="/wiki/Hail" title="Hail">\n        hail\n       </a>\n       , strong winds and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tornadoes" title="Tornadoes">\n        tornadoes\n       </a>\n       , usually during the afternoon and evening.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Holidays">\n        Holidays\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Summer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Holidays">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="School_breaks">\n        School breaks\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Summer&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: School breaks">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Schools and universities typically have a summer break to take advantage of the warmer weather and longer days. In almost all countries, children are out of school during this time of year for\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Summer_break" title="Summer break">\n        summer break\n       </a>\n       , although dates vary. In the United States, public schools usually end in early June while colleges get out in early May, although some schools get out on the last or second last Thursday in May. In\n       <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">\n        England\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">\n        Wales\n       </a>\n       , school ends in mid-July and resumes again in early September; in\n       <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">\n        Scotland\n       </a>\n       , the summer holiday begins in late June and ends in mid- to late-August. Similarly, in\n       <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">\n        Canada\n       </a>\n       the summer holiday starts in late June and ends at the very start of September. In\n       <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">\n        Russia\n       </a>\n       the summer holiday begins at the end of May and ends on August 31. In the\n       <a href="/wiki/Southern_Hemisphere" title="Southern Hemisphere">\n        Southern Hemisphere\n       </a>\n       , school summer holiday dates include the major holidays of\n       <a href="/wiki/Christmas" title="Christmas">\n        Christmas\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/New_Year%27s_Day" title="New Year\'s Day">\n        New Year\'s Day\n       </a>\n       . School summer holidays in Australia, New Zealand and\n       <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">\n        South Africa\n       </a>\n       begin in early-December and end in early February, with the dates varying between states. In\n       <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">\n        India\n       </a>\n       , school ends in April and resumes in late June or early July. In\n       <a href="/wiki/Cameroon" title="Cameroon">\n        Cameroon\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Nigeria" title="Nigeria">\n        Nigeria\n       </a>\n       , schools usually go for summer vacation in mid-July and resume back in the later weeks of September or first week of October.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Public_holidays">\n        Public holidays\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       A wide range of public holidays fall during summer, including:\n      </p>\n      <ul>\n       <li>\n        <a href="/wiki/Memorial_Day" (Canada) through\n        <a href="/wiki/Labor_Day" title="Labor Day">\n         Labor Day\n        </a>\n        in Summer\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        <a href="/wiki/Bank_holiday" title="Bank holiday">\n         Bank holidays\n        </a>\n        in the United Kingdom and Ireland\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        <a href="/wiki/%C3%93lavs%C3%B8ka" title="\xc3\x93lavs\xc3\xb8ka">\n         \xc3\x93lavs\xc3\xb8ka\n        </a>\n        , national holiday in the\n        <a href="/wiki/Faroe_Islands" title="Faroe Islands">\n         Faroe Islands\n        </a>\n        on 29 July\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        <a href="/wiki/Carnival" title="Carnival">\n         Carnival\n        </a>\n        in Brazil, mid February to early March\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        <a href="/wiki/Independence_Day_(United_States)" title="Independence Day (United States)">\n         Independence Day\n        </a>\n        in the United States (4 July)\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        <a href="/wiki/Independence_Day_(Pakistan)" title="Independence Day (Pakistan)">\n         Independence Day (Pakistan)\n        </a>\n        in Pakistan (14 August)\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        <a href="/wiki/Independence_Day_(India)" title="Independence Day (India)">\n         Independence Day (India)\n        </a>\n        in India (15 August)\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        <a       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Myrtle-Beach-SC-0836.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1728" data-file-width="2592" height="147" class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Myrtle-Beach-SC-0836.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Hotels and tourists along the\n         <a Carolina">\n          Myrtle Beach, South Carolina\n         </a>\n         in the summer\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       People take advantage of the warmer temperatures by spending more time outdoors during the summer. Activities such as traveling to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Beach" title="Beach">\n        beach\n       </a>\n       and picnics occur during summer months. Sports such as\n       <a href="/wiki/Association_football" title="Association football">\n        association football\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Basketball" title="Basketball">\n        basketball\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/American_football" title="American football">\n        American football\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Volleyball" title="Volleyball">\n        volleyball\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Skateboarding" title="Skateboarding">\n        skateboarding\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">\n        baseball\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Softball" title="Softball">\n        softball\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Cricket" title="Cricket">\n        cricket\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Tennis" title="Tennis">\n        tennis\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Golf" title="Golf">\n        golf\n       </a>\n       are played. Water sports also occur. These include\n       <a href="/wiki/Water_skiing" title="Water skiing">\n        water skiing\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wake_boarding" title="Wake boarding">\n        wake boarding\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Human_swimming" title="Human swimming">\n        swimming\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Surfing" title="Surfing">\n        surfing\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Tubing_(recreation)" title="Tubing (recreation)">\n        tubing\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Water_polo" title="Water polo">\n        water polo\n       </a>\n       . The modern\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Olympics" title="Olympics">\n        Olympics\n       </a>\n       have been held during the summer months every four years since 1896. The\n       <a href="/wiki/2000_Summer_Olympics" title="2000 Summer Olympics">\n        2000 Summer Olympics\n       </a>\n       , in\n       <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">\n        Sydney\n       </a>\n       , however, were held during the Australian spring.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Summer is usually a low point in\n       <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">\n        television\n       </a>\n       viewing, and television schedules generally reflect this by not scheduling new episodes of their most popular shows between the end of May\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sweeps" title="Sweeps">\n        sweeps\n       </a>\n       and the beginning of the television season in September, instead scheduling low-cost\n       <a href="/wiki/Reality_television" title="Reality television">\n        reality television\n       </a>\n       shows and\n       <a href="/wiki/Burning_off" title="Burning off">\n        burning off\n       </a>\n       commitments to already-canceled series. There is an exception to this with children\'s television. Many television shows made for children and are popular with children are released during the summer months, especially on children\'s cable channels such as the\n       <a href="/wiki/Disney_Channel" title="Disney Channel">\n        Disney Channel\n       </a>\n       in the United States, as children are off school. Disney Channel, for example, ends its preschool programming earlier in the day for older school age children in the summer months while it reverts to the original scheduling as the school year begins. Conversely, the music and film industries generally experience higher returns during the summer than other times of the year and market their\n       <a href="/wiki/Summer_hit" title="Summer hit">\n        summer hits\n       </a>\n       accordingly. The summer season is also most popular for\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Animated_movies" title="Animated movies">\n        animated movies\n       </a>\n       to be released theatrically in\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Movie_theaters" title="Movie theaters">\n        movie theaters\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">\n        [\n        <i>\n         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">\n          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2012)">\n           citation needed\n          </span>\n         </a>\n        </i>\n        ]\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       With most school-age children and college students (except those attending\n       <a href="/wiki/Summer_school" title="Summer school">\n        summer school\n       </a>\n       ) on summer vacation during the summer months, especially in the United States, travel and vacationing traditionally peaks during the summer, with the volume of travel in a typical summer weekend rivaled only by\n       <a href="/wiki/Thanksgiving_(United_States)" title="Thanksgiving (United States)">\n        Thanksgiving\n       </a>\n       . Teenagers and college students often take\n       <a href="/wiki/Seasonal_industry" title="Seasonal industry">\n        summer jobs\n       </a>\n       in industries that cater to recreation. Business activity for the recreation, tourism, restaurant, and retail industries peak during the summer months as well as the holiday season.\n      </p>\n      <table
