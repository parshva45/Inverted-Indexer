<p>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Recording">\n        Recording\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_(Eric_Ben%C3%A9t_album)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Recording">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Recording sessions for the album took place at various recording locations, including the Hit Factory in New York, New York, Bowery Digital, Chalice Studios, Ocean Way Studio, The Studio, Warner Bros. Sound Stage, and Westlake Audio in Los Angeles, California, Chartmaker Studios, Drew\'s Groove Studios, Panic Room, and Soundstage Studio in Nashville, Tennessee, Jade Studios and Jupiter Studios in Franklin, Tennessee, and Eastman Scoring Stage in Burbank, California.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-notes_2-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-notes-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Production was handled by Ben\xc3\xa9t and several other record producers, including Walter Afanasieff, Michael Carney, Hod David, Dave Foster, George Nash, Jr., and Demont\xc3\xa9 Posey.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-credits_6-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-credits-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Composition">\n        Composition\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       <i>\n        Hurricane\n       </i>\n       incorporates musical elements of\n       <a href="/wiki/Gospel_music" title="Gospel music">\n        gospel\n       </a>\n       and contributions from\n       <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">\n        jazz\n       </a>\n       musicians\n       <a href="/wiki/Roy_Hargrove" title="Roy Hargrove">\n        Roy Hargrove\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Chris_Botti" title="Chris Botti">\n        Chris Botti\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Viktor_Krauss" title="Viktor Krauss">\n        Viktor Krauss\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Post_4-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Post-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A departure from the jazzy,\n       <a href="/wiki/Groove_(music)" title="Groove (music)">\n        groove\n       </a>\n       -based\n       <a href="/wiki/Urban_contemporary" title="Urban contemporary">\n        urban\n       </a>\n       sound of Ben\xc3\xa9t\'s previous work, the album features an\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adult_contemporary" title="Adult contemporary">\n        adult contemporary\n       </a>\n       radio format style, with sweeping strings, layered vocals, and nimble acoustic guitars.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ollison_7-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Ollison-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       According to Rashod D. Ollison of\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/The_Washington_Post" title="The Washington Post">\n         The Washington Post\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       , "\n       <i>\n        Hurricane\n       </i>\n       is lush in spots, the overall sound more in the adult contemporary vein. More\n       <a href="/wiki/Christopher_Cross" title="Christopher Cross">\n        Christopher Cross\n       </a>\n       , less\n       <a href="/wiki/R._Kelly" title="R. Kelly">\n        R. Kelly\n       </a>\n       ".\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ollison_7-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Ollison-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n
