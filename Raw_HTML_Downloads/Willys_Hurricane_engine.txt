      </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       The Hurricane was based on the earlier Go-Devil\n       <a href="/wiki/Flathead_engine" title="Flathead engine">\n        flathead The gross power and torque outputs fell to 72\xc2\xa0hp (54\xc2\xa0kW; 73\xc2\xa0PS) and 112\xc2\xa0lb\xc2\xb7ft (152\xc2\xa0N\xc2\xb7m) respectively when the engine had a 6.9:1 compression ratio. Bore and stroke dimensions were the same as the L-head engine at\n       <span class="nowrap">\n       </span>\n       <span class="frac nowrap">\n        3\n        <span class="visualhide">\n <span class="frac nowrap">\n        4\n        <span class="visualhide">\n        </span>\n        <sup>\n         3\n        </sup>\n        \xe2\x81\x84\n        <sub>\n         8\n        </sub>\n </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The F4-134 was introduced in 1950 in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Willys_Jeep_Truck" title="Willys Jeep Truck">\n        Jeep Truck\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Brown74_4-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Brown74-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Vehicles with this engine were given the model designation 4-73.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Allen215_6-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Allen215-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       This engine was not placed in a CJ until the introduction of the CJ-3B in 1953, which had a distinctive high hood to accommodate the much taller engine.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Brown80_2-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Brown80-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The engine remained in production until 1971, after\n       <a href="/wiki/American_Motors" title="American Motors">\n        American Motors Corporation\n       </a>\n       (AMC) purchased Kaiser Jeep.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Applications:\n      </p>\n      <ul>\n       <li>\n        1950\xe2\x80\x931961\n <a href="/wiki/Jeepster_Commando" title="Jeepster Commando">\n         Jeepster Commando\n        </a>\n       </li>\n      </ul>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="F161">\n     </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       The F6-161 Hurricane is an F-head version of the L6-161 Lightning flathead straight six. It was used in the Model 685 Station Wagon.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        BF-161\n       </b>\n       The BF-161 has a\n       <span class="nowrap">\n       </span>\n       <span class="frac nowrap">\n        3\n        <span class="visualhide">\n        </span>\n        <sup>\n         1\n        </sup>\n        \xe2\x81\x84\n        <sub>\n         8\n        </sub>\n       </span>\n       inch bore and a\n       <span class="nowrap">\n       </span>\n       <span class="frac nowrap">\n        3\n        <span class="visualhide">\n        </span>\n        <sup>\n         1\n        </sup>\n        \xe2\x81\x84\n        <sub>\n         2\n        </sub>\n       </span>\n       inch stroke, a one-barrel\n       <a href="/wiki/Carburetor" title="Carburetor">\n        carburetor\n       </a>\n       , and an output of 90\xc2\xa0hp (67\xc2\xa0kW; 91\xc2\xa0PS) at 4400\xc2\xa0rpm and 135\xc2\xa0lb\xc2\xb7ft (183\xc2\xa0N\xc2\xb7m) of torque at 2000\xc2\xa0rpm. Its 161.1\xc2\xa0cu\xc2\xa0in (2,640\xc2\xa0cc) displacement features a compression ratio of 7.6:1.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ruralwillys_8-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ruralwillys-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        2600\n       </b>\n       The 2600 was essentially the same BF-161 engine made in Brazil by Willys-Overland\'s subsidiary, but it had two one-barrel carburetors (simultaneously opened) and had an output of 130\xc2\xa0hp (97\xc2\xa0kW; 132\xc2\xa0PS) at 4400\xc2\xa0rpm and 140\xc2\xa0lb\xc2\xb7ft (190\xc2\xa0N\xc2\xb7m) of torque at 2000\xc2\xa0rpm. The compression ratio remained 7.6:1.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ruralwillys_8-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ruralwillys-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        3000\n       </b>\n       The 3-liter version is almost identical to the BF-161 engine with the stroke increased to 4 inches (100\xc2\xa0mm) giving it a displacement of 3,016\xc2\xa0cc (184.0\xc2\xa0cu\xc2\xa0in).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ruralwillys_8-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ruralwillys-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       With a 2-barrel carburetor it produced 140\xc2\xa0hp (104\xc2\xa0kW; 142\xc2\xa0PS) at 4400\xc2\xa0rpm and 161\xc2\xa0lb\xc2\xb7ft (218\xc2\xa0N\xc2\xb7m) of torque at 2000\xc2\xa0rpm. It also had a slightly higher compression ratio of 8:1.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ruralwillys_8-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ruralwillys-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       It initially used a different head with removable intake manifold. Later, after Ford acquired Willys-Overland do Brasil, they reverted the engine to its former head design with integral intake manifold, improved cooling between cylinders 5 and 6 and installed a side-mounted oil filter, instead of the front-mounted, hose connected arrangement used by Willys.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Applications:\n      </p>\n      <ul>\n       <li>\n   Rural Willys\n        </a>\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        1968\xe2\x80\x931971\n        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Willys_Itamaraty&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Willys    </sup>\n       from\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Continental_straight_six_engine" title="Continental straight six engine">\n        Continental\n       </a>\n       <sup        </sub>\n       </span>\n       inches and stroke of\n       <span class="nowrap">\n       </span>\n       <span class="frac nowrap">\n        4\n        <span class="visualhide">\n (3,707\xc2\xa0cc).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PSMar1954_12-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-PSMar1954-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Horsepower rating is 105\xc2\xa0hp (78\xc2\xa0kW; 106\xc2\xa0PS) at 3600\xc2\xa0rpm or 115\xc2\xa0hp (86\xc2\xa0kW; 117\xc2\xa0PS) at 3650\xc2\xa0rpm,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PSMar1954_12-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-PSMar1954-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       as well as a torque rating of 190\xc2\xa0lb\xc2\xb7ft (258\xc2\xa0N\xc2\xb7m) at 1400\xc2\xa0rpm or at 1800\xc2\xa0rpm,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PSMar1954_12-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-PSMar1954-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       depending on the year of production.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Mitsubishi_versions">\n        Mitsubishi versions\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Willys_Hurricane_engine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Mitsubishi versions">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Mitsubishi built a version of the Hurricane from 1954 as the JH4 (69 hp), mostly for use in their license-built version of the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mitsubishi_Jeep" title="Mitsubishi Jeep">\n        Jeep\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       They later developed a 61\xc2\xa0PS (45\xc2\xa0kW)\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Overhead-valve_engine" title="Overhead-valve engine">\n        overhead-valve\n       </a>\n       diesel version of the same, called\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_Mitsubishi_Fuso_engines#KE_series" title="List of Mitsubishi Fuso engines">\n        KE31\n       </a>\n       . This was also turned into a 3.3 liter six-cylinder version with the same internal dimensions, producing 85\xc2\xa0PS (63\xc2\xa0kW), which was named KE36. These diesel engines were used in the Jeep, but also in a number of light to medium-weight trucks and buses.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CamionFuso_14-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CamionFuso-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="References">\n        References\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Willys_Hurricane_engine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: References">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n       <div class="mw-references-wrap mw-references-columns">\n        <ol class="references">\n         <li id="cite_note-baeta-1">\n          <span class="mw-cite-backlink">\n           ^\n           <a href="#cite_ref-baeta_1-0">\n            <sup>\n             <i>\n              <b>\n               a\n              </b>\n             </i>\n            </sup>\n           </a>\n           <a href="#cite_ref-baeta_1-1">\n            <sup>\n             <i>\n              <b>\n               b\n              </b>\n             </i>\n            </sup>\n           </a>\n          </span>\n          <span class="reference-text">\n           <cite class="citation web">\n            <a class="external text" href="http://www.baeta.org/opie/engine.htm#R" rel="nofollow">\n             "Engine Specs - Jeep Engines - Hurricane F-Head 134 I4"\n            </a>\n            .
