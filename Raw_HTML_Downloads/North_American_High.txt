In summer it is replaced with an\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arctic_Low" title="Arctic Low">\n        Arctic Low\n       </a>\n       , or if it moves to continental land, a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_American_Low" title="North American Low">\n        North American Low\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       North American Highs move eastwards across the continent, often in the company of one or more\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Low-pressure_cell" title="Low-pressure cell">\n        low-pressure cells\n       </a>\n       or\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cyclones" title="Cyclones">\n        cyclones\n       </a>\n       . Its cold, dense air does not extend usually above 3\xc2\xa0km (1.9\xc2\xa0mi), lower than the\n       <a href="/wiki/Canadian_Rockies" title="Canadian Rockies">\n        Canadian Rockies\n       </a>\n       . Sometimes, in winter it breaks free and passes over the Rockies and brings a cold front into Southwestern United States and Mexico, freezing crops and bringing snow into Mexico\'s mountains as far south as\n       <a href="/wiki/Jalisco" title="Jalisco">\n        Jalisco\n       </a>\n       . The high\xe2\x80\x99s usual location east of the Rockies shelters it from the relatively warm\n       <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">\n        Pacific Ocean\n       </a>\n       and helps it maintain its strength. The average January sea level pressure at its centre is about 1,020\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Millibars" title="Millibars">\n        millibars\n       </a>\n       (30.12 inches of mercury). The Canadian high often moves southeastward until it eventually reaches the\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">\n        Atlantic Ocean\n       </a>\n       , where it merges with the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Azores_high" title="Azores high">\n        Azores high\n       </a>\n       . In the summer the Canadian high circulates cool, dry air to the United States east of the Rockies and parts of southern Canada.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The North American High is akin to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Siberian_High" title="Siberian High">\n        Siberian High\n       </a>\n       of\n       <a href="/wiki/Eurasia" title="Eurasia">\n        Eurasia\n       </a>\n       , but it is much smaller, and it has much less influence, merely affecting the weather of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Northern_Hemisphere" title="Northern Hemisphere">\n        Northern Hemisphere\n       </a>\n       . The\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sea-level_pressure" title="Sea-level pressure">\n        sea-level pressure\n       </a>\n       (\n       <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_pressure" title="Atmospheric pressure">\n        atmospheric pressure\n       </a>\n       ) rarely, if ever, exceeds 1055.0\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Millibars" title="Millibars">\n        millibars\n       </a>\n       (1055.0\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hectopascal" title="Hectopascal">\n        hectopascals\n       </a>\n       )(hPa)(\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SI" title="SI">\n        SI\n       </a>\n       ).\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Often, in the winter months, cool or cold dry air settles over the land in the vicinity of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Great_Basin" title="Great Basin">\n        Great Basin\n       </a>\n       where it builds into a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/High-pressure_cell" title="High-pressure cell">\n        high-pressure cell\n       </a>\n       or\n       <a href="/wiki/Anticyclone" title="Anticyclone">\n        anticyclone\n       </a>\n       that moves across the\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">\n        United States\n       </a>\n       with a\n       <a href="/wiki/Cold_front" title="Cold front">\n        cold front\n       </a>\n       on its leading edge. After reaching the\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">\n        Atlantic Ocean\n       </a>\n       , the moist environment brings on changes of the qualities of the air and the dissipation of the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/High-pressure_cell" title="High-pressure cell">\n        high-pressure cell\n       </a>\n       or\n       <a href="/wiki/Anticyclone" title="Anticyclone">\n        anticyclone\n       </a>\n       as the cold air warms and becomes humid. In Europe, a portion of the North American/Canadian high usually over\n       <a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">\n        Greenland\n       </a>\n       called the Greenland high which settles over Greenland affects northern European weather and may merge with the\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Scandinavian_High&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Scandinavian High (page does not exist)">\n        Scandinavian High\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="References">\n        References\n       </span>\n
