  </b>\n        </td>\n       </tr>\n       <tr style="display:none">\n        <td colspan="2">\n        </td>\n       </tr>\n      </table>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        Typhoon Nabi\n       </b>\n       (\n       <small>\n        pronounced\n       </small>\n       <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)">\n        <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Korean" title="Help:IPA/Korean">\n         [na.bi]\n        </a>\n       </span>\n       ), known in the Philippines as\n       <b>\n        Typhoon Jolina\n       </b>\n       , was a powerful\n       <a href="/wiki/Typhoon" title="Typhoon">\n        typhoon\n       </a>\n       that struck southwestern\n       <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">\n        Japan\n       </a>\n       in September 2005. The 14th\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_naming" title="Tropical cyclone naming">\n        named storm\n       </a>\n       of the\n       <a href="/wiki/2005_Pacific_typhoon_season" title="2005 Pacific typhoon season">\n        2005 Pacific typhoon season\n       </a>\n       , Nabi formed on August\xc2\xa029 to the east of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Northern_Mariana_Islands" title="Northern Mariana Islands">\n        Northern Mariana Islands\n       </a>\n       . It moved westward and passed about 55\xc2\xa0km (35\xc2\xa0mi) north of\n       <a href="/wiki/Saipan" title="Saipan">\n        Saipan\n       </a>\n       on August\xc2\xa031 as an intensifying typhoon. On the next day, the\n       <a href="/wiki/Joint_Typhoon_Warning_Center" title="Joint Typhoon Warning Center">\n        Joint Typhoon Warning Center\n       </a>\n       upgraded the storm to\n       <a href="/wiki/Typhoon#Intensity_classifications" title="Typhoon">\n        super typhoon\n       </a>\n       status, with winds equivalent to that of a Category\xc2\xa05 hurricane on the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">\n        Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale\n       </a>\n       . The\n       <a href="/wiki/Japan_Meteorological_Agency" title="Japan Meteorological Agency">\n        Japan Meteorological Agency\n       </a>\n       estimated peak ten-minute winds of 175\xc2\xa0km/h (110\xc2\xa0mph) on September\xc2\xa02. Nabi weakened while curving to the north, striking the Japanese island of\n       <a href="/wiki/Kyushu" title="Kyushu">\n        Kyushu\n       </a>\n       on September\xc2\xa06. After brushing\n       <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">\n        South Korea\n       </a>\n       , the storm turned to the northeast, passing over\n       <a href="/wiki/Hokkaido" title="Hokkaido">\n        Hokkaido\n       </a>\n       before becoming\n       <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">\n        extratropical\n       </a>\n       on September\xc2\xa08.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The typhoon first affected the Northern Marianas Islands, where it left\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">\n        US$\n       </a>\n       2.5\xc2\xa0million in damage,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-1">\n         [nb 1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       while damaging or destroying 114\xc2\xa0homes. The damage was enough to warrant a disaster declaration from the United States government. While passing near\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Okinawa" title="Okinawa">\n        Okinawa\n       </a>\n       , Nabi produced gusty winds and caused minor damage. Later, the western fringe of the storm caused several traffic accidents in\n       <a href="/wiki/Busan" title="Busan">\n        Busan\n       </a>\n       , South Korea, and throughout the country Nabi killed six people and caused US$115.4\xc2\xa0million in damage. About 250,000\xc2\xa0people evacuated along the Japanese island of Kyushu ahead of the storm, and there were disruptions to train, ferry, and airline services. In Kyushu, the storm left\n       <a href="/wiki/Japanese_yen" title="Japanese yen">\n        \xc2\xa5\n       </a>\n       4.08\xc2\xa0billion\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jpy_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-jpy-3">\n         [nb 2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       (US$36.9\xc2\xa0million) in crop damage after dropping 1,322\xc2\xa0mm (52.0\xc2\xa0in) of rain over three days. During the storm\'s passage, there were 61\xc2\xa0daily rainfall records broken by Nabi\'s precipitation. The rains caused flooding and landslides, forcing people to evacuate their homes and for businesses to close. Across Japan, Nabi killed 29\xc2\xa0people and caused \xc2\xa594.9\xc2\xa0billion (US$854\xc2\xa0million) in damage. Soldiers, local governments, and insurance companies helped residents recover from the storm damage. After affecting Japan, the typhoon affected the\n       <a href="/wiki/Kuril_Islands" title="Kuril Islands">\n        Kuril Islands\n       </a>\n       of Russia, where it dropped the equivalent of the monthly precipitation, while also causing road damage due to high waves. Overall, Nabi killed 35\xc2\xa0people, and its effects were significant enough for the name to be\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Pacific_typhoon_names" title="List of retired Pacific typhoon names">\n        retired\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="toc">\n       <div class="toctitle">\n        <h2>\n         Contents\n        </h2>\n <p>\n       On August\xc2\xa028, a large area of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Convection_(meteorology)" title="Convection (meteorology)">\n        convection\n       </a>\n       persisted about 1,035\xc2\xa0km (645\xc2\xa0mi) east of\n       <a href="/wiki/Guam" title="Guam">\n        Guam\n       </a>\n       . Located within an area of moderate\n       <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">\n        wind shear\n       </a>\n       , the system quickly organized while moving westward, its track influenced by a\n       <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">\n        ridge\n       </a>\n       to the north.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gp_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-gp-4">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At 00:00\n       <a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">\n        UTC\n       </a>\n       on August\xc2\xa029, a tropical depression formed from the system,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bt_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-bt-5">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       classified by the\n       <a href="/wiki/Joint_Typhoon_Warning_Center" title="Joint Typhoon Warning Center">\n        Joint Typhoon Warning Center\n       </a>\n       (JTWC)\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-7">\n         [nb 3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       as Tropical Depression 14W. In initial forecasts, the agency anticipated steady strengthening,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-8">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       due to warm\n       <a href="/wiki/Sea_surface_temperature" title="Sea surface temperature">\n        sea surface temperatures\n       </a>\n       in the area.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aug29_9-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-aug29-9">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At 12:00\xc2\xa0UTC on August\xc2\xa029, the\n       <a href="/wiki/Japan_Meteorological_Agency" title="Japan Meteorological Agency">\n        Japan Meteorological Agency\n       </a>\n       (JMA)\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-11">\n         [nb 4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       upgraded it to a tropical storm.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jma_10-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-jma-10">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       As such, the JMA\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_naming" title="Tropical cyclone naming">\n        named\n       </a>\n       the storm\n       <i>\n        Nabi\n       </i>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-12">\n         [nb 5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gp_4-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-gp-4">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       About 12\xc2\xa0hours later, the JMA upgraded Nabi further to a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_scales#Western_Pacific" title="Tropical cyclone scales">\n        severe tropical storm\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bt_5-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-bt-5">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       after the convection organized into spiral\n       <a href="/wiki/Rainband" title="Rainband">\n        rainbands\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aug29_9-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-aug29-9">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At 18:00\xc2\xa0UTC, Nabi intensified to typhoon status, reaching ten-minute\n       <a href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_wind" title="Maximum sustained wind">\n        sustained winds\n       </a>\n       of 120\xc2\xa0km/h (75\xc2\xa0mph).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bt_5-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-bt-5">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       On August\xc2\xa031, Nabi passed about 55\xc2\xa0km (35\xc2\xa0mi) north-northeast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Saipan" title="Saipan">\n        Saipan\n       </a>\n       in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Northern_Mariana_Islands" title="Northern Mariana Islands">\n        Northern Mariana Islands\n       </a>\n       during its closest approach.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-13">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The typhoon continued to intensify quickly as it moved to the west-northwest. On September\xc2\xa01, the JTWC upgraded the storm to a\n       <a href="/wiki/Typhoon#Intensity_classifications" title="Typhoon">\n        super typhoon\n       </a>\n       and later estimated peak one-minute winds of 260\xc2\xa0km/h (160\xc2\xa0mph); this is the equivalent of a Category\xc2\xa05 on the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">\n        Saffir-Simpson scale\n       </a>\n       . By contrast, the JMA estimated peak ten-minute winds of 175\xc2\xa0km/h (110\xc2\xa0mph) on September\xc2\xa02, with a\n       <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_pressure" title="Atmospheric pressure">\n        barometric pressure\n       </a>\n       of 925\xc2\xa0mbar (27.3\xc2\xa0inHg).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gp_4-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-gp-4">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bt_5-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-bt-5">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       While at peak intensity, the typhoon developed a large 95\xc2\xa0km (60\xc2\xa0mi) wide\n       <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">\n        eye\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">\n        <a href="#cite_note-14">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       For about 36\xc2\xa0hours, Nabi maintained its peak winds, during which it crossed into the area of responsibility of\n       <a href="/wiki/PAGASA" title="PAGASA">\n        PAGASA\n       </a>\n       ;\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gp_4-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-gp-4">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bt_5-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-bt-5">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       the Philippine-based agency gave it the local name "Jolina", although the storm remained away from the country.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gp_4-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-gp-4">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n slightly to a secondary peak of 215\xc2\xa0km/h (130\xc2\xa0mph).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bt_5-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-bt-5">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n     Kamchatka Peninsula\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">\n        <a href="#cite_note-16">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At   </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">\n        <a href="#cite_note-17">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       although the JMA continued tracking the storm.    </sup>\n       The remnants of Nabi weakened and later moved into southwestern\n       <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">\n        Alaska\n       </a>\n       on September\xc2\xa012.\n   </sup>\n       and forcing 162\xc2\xa0flights to be canceled.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-xin_37-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-xin-37">\n         [32]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Ferry service was also disrupted,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-xin_37-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-xin-37">\n         [32]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and prompted 138\xc2\xa0schools to close in the region.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-xin_37-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-xin-37">\n         [32]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Earlier, the storm spurred fears of a possible repeat of either typhoon\n       <a href="/wiki/Typhoon_Rusa" title="Typhoon Rusa">\n        Rusa\n       </a>\n       in 2002 or\n       <a href="/wiki/Typhoon_Maemi" title="Typhoon Maemi">\n        Maemi\n       </a>\n       in 2003, both of which were devastating storms in South Korea.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39">\n        <a href="#cite_note-39">\n         [34]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Officials in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Russian_Far_East" title="Russian Far East">\n        Russian Far East\n       </a>\n       issued a storm warning for\n       <a href="/wiki/Vladivostok" title="Vladivostok">\n        Vladivostok\n       </a>\n       , advising boats to remain at port.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40">\n        <a href="#cite_note-40">\n         [35]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">\n        Impact\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Typhoon_Nabi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Impact">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       While passing between Saipan and the volcanic island of\n       <a href="/wiki/Anatahan" title="Anatahan">\n        Anatahan\n       </a>\n       , Nabi brought tropical storm force winds to several islands in the Northern Mariana Islands.\n       <a href="/wiki/Saipan_International_Airport" title="Saipan International Airport">\n        Saipan International Airport\n       </a>\n       reported sustained winds of 95\xc2\xa0km/h (59\xc2\xa0mph), with gusts to 120\xc2\xa0km/h (75\xc2\xa0mph). Also on the island, Nabi produced 173\xc2\xa0mm (6.82\xc2\xa0in) of rainfall. The storm destroyed two houses and left 26\xc2\xa0others uninhabitable, while 77\xc2\xa0homes sustained minor damage, largely from flooding or roof damage. Nabi damaged 70\xe2\x80\x9380% of the crops on Saipan and also knocked down many trees, leaving behind 544\xc2\xa0tonnes (600\xc2\xa0tons) of debris. The entire island was left without power, some without water, after the storm. On Tinian to the south, Nabi damaged or destroyed nine homes, with heavy crop damage. On Rota, there was minor flooding and scattered power outages. Farther south, the outer reaches of the storm produced sustained winds of 69\xc2\xa0km/h (43\xc2\xa0mph) at\n       <a href="/wiki/Apra_Harbor" title="Apra Harbor">\n        Apra Harbor\n       </a>\n       on Guam, while gusts peaked at 101\xc2\xa0km/h (63\xc2\xa0mph) at\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mangilao,_Guam" title="Mangilao, Guam">\n        Mangilao\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdc_22-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ncdc-22">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Gusts reached 72\xc2\xa0km/h (45\xc2\xa0mph) at the international airport on Guam, the highest during 2005. The storm dropped 115\xc2\xa0mm (4.53\xc2\xa0in) of rainfall in 24\xc2\xa0hours on the island.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-soest_41-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-soest-41">\n         [36]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Flooding covered roads for several hours and entered classrooms at\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guam_Public_School_System#Middle_schools" title="Guam Public School System">\n        Untalan Middle School\n       </a>\n       , forcing hundreds of students to evacuate. Damage in the region was estimated US$2.5\xc2\xa0million.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdc_22-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ncdc-22">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       After Nabi exited the region, it produced high surf for several days on Guam and Saipan.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-soest_41-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-soest-41">\n         [36]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Later in its duration, Nabi brushed southeastern South Korea with rainbands.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gp_4-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-gp-4">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <a href="/wiki/Ulsan" title="Ulsan">\n        Ulsan\n       </a>\n       recorded a 24\xe2\x80\x91hour rainfall total of 319\xc2\xa0mm (12.6\xc2\xa0in),\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gp_4-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-gp-4">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       while Pohang recorded a record 24\xe2\x80\x91hour total of 540.5\xc2\xa0mm (21.28\xc2\xa0in).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-42">\n        <a href="#cite_note-42">\n         [37]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The highest total was 622.5\xc2\xa0mm (24.5\xc2\xa0in) of rainfall.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SKD_43-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SKD-43">\n         [38]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The periphery of the storm produced gusts of 121\xc2\xa0km/h (75\xc2\xa0mph) in the port city of\n       <a href="/wiki/Busan" title="Busan">\n        Busan\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kh97_38-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-kh97-38">\n         [33]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       strong enough to damage eight billboards and knock trees over.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gp_4-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-gp-4">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Heavy rains caused several traffic accidents and injuries in Busan,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kh97_38-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-kh97-38">\n         [33]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       while strong waves washed a cargo ship ashore in Pohang.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gp_4-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-gp-4">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Throughout South Korea, the storm </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In the\n       <a href="/wiki/Kuril_Islands" title="Kuril Islands">\n        Kuril Islands\n       </a>\n       of Russia, Nabi dropped about 75\xc2\xa0mm href="/wiki/Fujita_scale" title="Fujita scale">\n        F1\n       </a>\n       tornado in Miyazaki, which damaged several buildings.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64">\n        <a     <a href="#cite_note-65">\n         [60]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Throughout Japan, Nabi caused damage in 31 of the 47\n       <a [61]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       leaving over 270,000\xc2\xa0residences without power.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gp_4-14">\n        <a href="#cite_note-gp-4">\n 81\xc2\xa0ships along the coast.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dt_57-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-dt-57">\n         [52]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On the island of Kyushu, (US$106\xc2\xa0million), the fifth highest of any typhoon in the preceding 10\xc2\xa0years; about 20% of the total there was related to road damages.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69">\n        <a href="#cite_note-69">\n         [64]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In nearby\n       <a href="/wiki/Saga_Prefecture" title="Saga Prefecture">\n        Saga Prefecture\n       </a>\n       , crop damage totaled about \xc2\xa51.06\xc2\xa0billion (US$9.6\xc2\xa0million), mostly to rice but also to soybeans and various other vegetables.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70">\n        <a href="#cite_note-70">\n         [65]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Crop damage as a whole on Kyushu totaled \xc2\xa54.08\xc2\xa0billion (US$36.9\xc2\xa0million).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap99_60-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ap99-60">\n         [55]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In the capital city of Tokyo, heavy rainfall increased levels along several rivers, which inundated several houses.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Digital_Typhoon_71-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Digital_Typhoon-71">\n         [66]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Strong winds damaged \xc2\xa528.8\xc2\xa0million (US$259,000) in crop damage in\n       <a href="/wiki/Gifu_Prefecture" title="Gifu Prefecture">\n        Gifu Prefecture\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72">\n        <a href="#cite_note-72">\n         [67]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and \xc2\xa527.1\xc2\xa0million (US$244,000) in crop damage in\n       <a href="/wiki/Osaka" title="Osaka">\n        Osaka\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73">\n        <a href="#cite_note-73">\n         [68]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Yamaguchi_Prefecture" title="Yamaguchi Prefecture">\n        Yamaguchi Prefecture\n       </a>\n       on western\n       <a href="/wiki/Honshu" title="Honshu">\n        Honshu\n       </a>\n       , Nabi damaged a portion of the historical\n       <a href="/wiki/Kintai_Bridge" title="Kintai Bridge">\n        Kintai Bridge\n       </a>\n       , originally built in 1674.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74">\n        <a href="#cite_note-74">\n         [69]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Yamagata_Prefecture" title="Yamagata Prefecture">\n        Yamagata Prefecture\n       </a>\n       , the winds damaged a window in a school, injuring several boys from the debris.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75">\n        <a href="#cite_note-75">\n         [70]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       One person was seriously injured in\n       <a href="/wiki/Kitakata,_Fukushima" title="Kitakata, Fukushima">\n        Kitakata, Fukushima\n       </a>\n       after strong winds blew a worker from scaffolding of a building under construction.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76">\n        <a href="#cite_note-76">\n         [71]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Effects from Nabi spread as far north as Hokkaido, where heavy rainfall damaged roads and caused hundreds of schools to close.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77">\n        <a href="#cite_note-77">\n         [72]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Ashoro,_Hokkaido" title="Ashoro, Hokkaido">\n        Ashoro\n       </a>\n       , an overflown river flooded a hotel,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78">\n        <a href="#cite_note-78">\n         [73]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and a minor power outage occurred in\n       <a href="/wiki/Teshikaga,_Hokkaido" title="Teshikaga, Hokkaido">\n        Teshikaga\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79">\n        <a href="#cite_note-79">\n         [74]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Ahead of the storm, high waves and gusty winds led to one drowning when a woman was knocked off a ferry in\n       <a href="/wiki/Takamatsu,_Kagawa" title="Takamatsu, Kagawa">\n        Takamatsu, Kagawa\n       </a>\n       . A landslide in\n       <a href="/wiki/Miyazaki,_Miyazaki" title="Miyazaki, Miyazaki">\n        Miyazaki\n       </a>\n       destroyed five homes,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IOL1_80-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-IOL1-80">\n         [75]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       killing three people. A man who was listed as missing was found dead in a flooded rice field. In Tarumi, a landslide buried a home in mud, killing two people.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PDO1_67-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-PDO1-67">\n         [62]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Nabi caused a portion of the\n       <a href="/wiki/San%27y%C5%8D_Expressway" title="San\'y\xc5\x8d Expressway">\n        San\'y\xc5\x8d Expressway\n       </a>\n       to collapse in Yamaguchi Prefecture, killing three people.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Digital_Typhoon_71-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Digital_Typhoon-71">\n         [66]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Fukui_Prefecture" title="Fukui Prefecture">\n        Fukui Prefecture\n       </a>\n       , the winds knocked an elderly man off a bicycle, killing him.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-81">\n        <a href="#cite_note-81">\n         [76]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Overall, Nabi killed 29\xc2\xa0people in Japan and injured 179\xc2\xa0others, 45 of them severely. Damage was estimated at \xc2\xa594.9\xc2\xa0billion (US$854\xc2\xa0million).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dt_57-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-dt-57">\n         [52]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">\n        Aftermath\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Typhoon_Nabi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Aftermath">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       After the storm, members of\n       <a href="/wiki/Marine_Corps_Air_Station_Iwakuni" title="Marine Corps Air Station Iwakuni">\n        Marine Corps Air Station Iwakuni\n       </a>\n       provided $2,500 to the town of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iwakuni,_Yamaguchi" title="Iwakuni, Yamaguchi">\n        Iwakuni\n       </a>\n       toward cleanup and disaster relief.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-82">\n        <a href="#cite_note-82">\n         [77]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Soldiers also helped nearby residents and farmers to complete the rice harvest, after floods from the typhoon damaged harvesting machines.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-83">\n        <a href="#cite_note-83">\n         [78]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The local government of Iwakunda distributed disinfectant chemicals to flooded houses.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-84">\n        <a href="#cite_note-84">\n         [79]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Closed markets and decreased supplies caused the price of beef to reach record levels in the country.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-85">\n        <a href="#cite_note-85">\n         [80]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Following the storm, the General Insurance Association of Japan reported that insurance claims from the typhoon totaled \xc2\xa558.8\xc2\xa0billion (US$53\xc2\xa0million), the tenth-highest for any natural disaster in the country. Miyazaki Prefecture reported the highest claims with \xc2\xa512.6\xc2\xa0billion (US$11.4\xc2\xa0million). The total was split between \xc2\xa549\xc2\xa0billion (US$44\xc2\xa0million) in housing claims and \xc2\xa57.9\xc2\xa0billion (US$71\xc2\xa0million) in car claims.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jiji_86-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-jiji-86">\n         [81]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The Japanese government provided food, water, and rescue workers to the affected areas in the days after the storm, along with\n       <a href="/wiki/Japan_Post" title="Japan Post">\n        Japan Post\n       </a>\n       , the local post system; trucks were mobilized to affected towns, accompanied by a mobile bank and insurance agent.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-87">\n        <a href="#cite_note-87">\n         [82]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       On November\xc2\xa08, nearly two months after the dissipation of Typhoon Nabi,\n       <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">\n        President\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">\n        George W. Bush\n       </a>\n       declared a major disaster declaration for the Northern Mariana islands. The declaration allocated aid from the United States to help restore damaged buildings, pay for debris removal, and other emergency services. Federal funding was also made available on a cost-sharing basis for the islands to mitigate against future disasters.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-88">\n        <a href="#cite_note-88">\n         [83]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The government ultimately provided $1,046,074.03 to the commonwealth.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-89">\n        <a href="#cite_note-89">\n         [84]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Due to the damage of the storm in Japan, the Typhoon Committee of the\n       <a href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organization" title="World Meteorological Organization">\n        World Meteorological Organization\n       </a>\n       agreed to\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Pacific_typhoon_names" title="List of retired Pacific typhoon names">\n        retire\n       </a>\n       the name\n       <i>\n        Nabi\n       </i>\n       . The agency replaced it with the name\n       <i>\n        Doksuri\n       </i>\n       , effective January\xc2\xa01, 2007,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-90">\n        <a href="#cite_note-90">\n         [85]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and was first used during the\n       <a href="/wiki/2012_Pacific_typhoon_season" title="2012 Pacific typhoon season">\n        2012 Pacific typhoon season\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">\n        See also\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n
