   <p>\n       <b>\n        Gulfport\n       </b>\n       is the second largest city in\n       <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">\n        Mississippi\n       </a>\n       after the state capital,\n       <a href="/wiki/Jackson,_Mississippi" title="Jackson, Mississippi">\n        Jackson\n       </a>\n       . It is the larger of the two principal cities of the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gulfport-Biloxi,_Mississippi_Metropolitan_Statistical_Area" title="Gulfport-Biloxi, Mississippi Metropolitan Statistical Area">\n        Gulfport-Biloxi, Mississippi Metropolitan Statistical Area\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       which is included in the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gulfport-Biloxi-Pascagoula,_Mississippi_Combined_Statistical_Area" title="Gulfport-Biloxi-Pascagoula, Mississippi Combined Statistical Area">\n        Gulfport-Biloxi-Pascagoula, Mississippi Combined Statistical Area\n       </a>\n       . As of the\n       <a href="/wiki/2010_United_States_Census" title="2010 United States Census">\n        2010 census\n       </a>\n       , the city of Gulfport had a total population of 67,793. Gulfport is co-\n       <a href="/wiki/County_seat" title="County seat">\n        county seat\n       </a>\n       with\n       <a href="/wiki/Biloxi,_Mississippi" title="Biloxi, Mississippi">\n        Biloxi\n       </a>\n       of\n       <a href="/wiki/Harrison_County,_Mississippi" title="Harrison County, Mississippi">\n        Harrison County, Mississippi\n       </a>\n       .     </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       An early settlement near this location, known as\n       <a href="/wiki/Mississippi_City,_Mississippi" title="Mississippi City, Mississippi">\n        Mississippi City\n       </a>\n       , appeared on a map of Mississippi from 1855.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Mississippi City was the county seat of Harrison County from 1841 to 1902, but is now a suburb in east Gulfport.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GNIS_7-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-GNIS-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Gulfport was incorporated on July 28, 1898. Gulfport was founded by:\n       <a href="/wiki/William_H._Hardy" title="William H. Hardy">\n        William H. Hardy\n       </a>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       who was president of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_and_Ship_Island_Railroad" title="Gulf and Ship Island Railroad">\n        Gulf and Ship Island Railroad\n       </a>\n       (G&amp;SIRR) that connected inland lumber mills to the coast, and later on, joined by\n       <a href="/wiki/Joseph_T._Jones" title="Joseph T. Jones">\n        Joseph T. Jones\n       </a>\n       who later took over the G&amp;SIRR, dredged the harbor in Gulfport, and opened the shipping channel to the sea. In 1902, the harbor was completed and the Port of Gulfport became a working seaport that now accounts for millions of dollars in annual sales and tax revenue for the state of Mississippi.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In 1910, the U.S. Post Office and Customhouse was built. The Gulfport Post office was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1984.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In March 1916, Mayor\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=George_M._Foote_(Mississippi_politician)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="George M. Foote (Mississippi politician) (page does not exist)">\n        George M. Foote\n       </a>\n       announced that the\n       <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Carnegie" title="Andrew Carnegie">\n        Andrew Carnegie\n       </a>\n       Corporation was going to place a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carnegie_Library" title="Carnegie Library">\n        Carnegie Library\n       </a>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       in Gulfport.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       On August 17, 1969 Gulfport and the Mississippi Gulf Coast was hit by Hurricane Camille. By central pressure, Camille was the second strongest U.S. land falling hurricane in recorded history. The area of total destruction in Harrison County, Mississippi was 68 square miles (180 km2).[11] The total estimated cost of damage was $1.42 billion (1969 USD, $9 billion 2012 USD).[12] This made Camille the second-most expensive hurricane in the United States, up to that point (behind Hurricane Betsy).[13] The storm directly killed 143 people along Alabama, Mississippi, and Louisiana.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In December 1993, the City annexed 33 square miles (85\xc2\xa0km\n       <sup>\n        2\n       </sup>\n       ) north of Gulfport making it the second largest city in Mississippi.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Hurricane_Katrina">\n        Hurricane Katrina\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span      </a>\n          .\n         </div>\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">\n        Climate\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gulfport,_Mississippi&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Climate">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Gulfport has a\n       <a href="/wiki/Humid_subtropical_climate" title="Humid subtropical climate">\n        humid subtropical climate\n       </a>\n       , which is strongly moderated by the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">\n        Gulf of Mexico\n       </a>\n       . Winters are short and generally warm, cold spells do occur, but seldom last long. Snow flurries are rare in the city, with no notable accumulation occurring most years. Summers are generally long, hot and humid, though the city\'s proximity to the Gulf prevents extreme summer highs, as seen farther inland. With about 6.7 miles (10.7 kilometers) of white sand beaches along the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">\n        Gulf of Mexico\n       </a>\n       , Gulfport has become a tourism destination, due in large part to Mississippi\'s Coast Casinos. Gulfport has served as host to popular cultural events such as the "World\'s Largest Fishing Rodeo," "Cruisin\' the Coast" (a week of classic cars), and "Smokin\' the Sound" (speedboat races). Gulfport is a thriving residential community with a strong mercantile center. There are historic neighborhoods and home sites, as well as diverse shopping opportunities and several motels scattered throughout to accommodate golfing, gambling, and water-sport tourism.\n
