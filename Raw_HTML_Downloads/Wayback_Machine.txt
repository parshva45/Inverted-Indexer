   <a href="/w/index.php?title=Wayback_Machine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Website exclusion policy">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Historically, Wayback Machine respected the\n       <a href="/wiki/Robots_exclusion_standard" title="Robots exclusion standard">\n        robots exclusion standard\n       </a>\n       (robots.txt) in determining if a website would be crawled or not; or Website owners had the option to opt-out of Wayback Machine through the use of robots.txt. It applied robots.txt rules retroactively; if a site blocked the Internet Archive, any previously archived pages from the domain were immediately rendered unavailable as well. In addition the Internet Archive stated, "Sometimes a website owner will contact us directly and ask us to stop crawling or archiving a site. We comply with these requests."\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39">\n        <a href="#cite_note-39">\n         [39]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In addition, the website says: "The Internet Archive is not interested in preserving or offering access to Web sites or other Internet documents of persons who do not want their materials in the collection."\n       <sup <a href="/w/index.php?title=Wayback_Machine&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Oakland Archive Policy">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n
