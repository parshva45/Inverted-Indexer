class="mw-headline" id="Details">\n        Details\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Unity_(ISS_module)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Details">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:282px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Unity_module_interior.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2008" data-file-width="3032" height="185" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/34/Unity_module_interior.jpg/280px-Unity_module_interior.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/34/Unity_module_interior.jpg/420px-Unity_module_interior.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/34/Unity_module_interior.jpg/560px-Unity_module_interior.jpg 2x" width="280"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Unity_module_interior.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Interior of Node 1\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Essential space station resources such as fluids, environmental control and life support systems, electrical and data systems are routed through\n       <i>\n        Unity\n       </i>\n       to supply work and living areas of the station. More than 50,000 mechanical items, 216 lines to carry fluids and gases, and 121 internal and external electrical cables using six miles of wire were installed in the\n       <i>\n        Unity\n       </i>\n       node. It is also made of\n       <a href="/wiki/Aluminium" title="Aluminium">\n        aluminium\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Stainless_steel" title="Stainless steel">\n        stainless steel\n       </a>\n       . During the space station construction, a crew member placed 2 speed limit signs on the hatch (leading into the FGB) in 2003; noting the orbital velocity in mph and km/h.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Prior to its launch aboard\n       <i>\n        Endeavour\n       </i>\n       , conical\n       <a href="/wiki/Pressurized_Mating_Adapter" title="Pressurized Mating Adapter">\n        Pressurized Mating Adapters\n       </a>\n       (PMAs) were attached to the aft and forward berthing mechanisms of Unity.\n       <i>\n        Unity\n       </i>\n       and the two mating adapters together weighed about 25,600 pounds (11,600\xc2\xa0kg). The adapters allow the docking systems used by the Space Shuttle and by Russian modules to attach to the node\'s hatches and berthing mechanisms. PMA-1 now permanently attaches\n       <i>\n        Unity\n       </i>\n       to Zarya, while PMA-2 provided a Shuttle docking port. Attached to the exterior of PMA-1 are computers, or multiplexer-demultiplexers (MDMs), which provided early command and control of\n       <i>\n        Unity\n       </i>\n       .\n       <i>\n        Unity\n       </i>\n       also is outfitted with an early communications system that allows data, voice and low data rate video with\n       <a href="/wiki/Christopher_C._Kraft_Jr._Mission_Control_Center" title="Christopher C. Kraft Jr. Mission Control Center">\n        Mission Control Houston\n       </a>\n       , to supplement Russian communications systems during the early station assembly activities. PMA-3 was attached to Unity\'s nadir berthing mechanism by the crew of\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-92" title="STS-92">\n        STS-92\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Other_nodes">\n        Other nodes\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a       </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Node_3_-_Isolated_view.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="411" data-file-width="448" height="202" //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ea/Node_3_-_Isolated_view.jpg/440px-Node_3_-_Isolated_view.jpg 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Node_3_-_Isolated_view.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Node 3 (\n         <i>\n          Tranquility\n         </i>\n         ) in space\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The two remaining station connecting modules, or nodes, were built in\n       <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">\n        Italy\n       </a>\n       by\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alenia_Aerospazio" title="Alenia Aerospazio">\n        Alenia Aerospazio\n       </a>\n       , as part of an agreement between\n       <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">\n        NASA\n       </a>\n       and the\n       <a href="/wiki/European_Space_Agency" title="European Space Agency">\n        European Space Agency\n       </a>\n       (ESA).\n       <a href="/wiki/Harmony_(ISS_module)" title="Harmony (ISS module)">\n        Harmony\n       </a>\n       (formerly known as Node 2) and\n       <a href="/wiki/Tranquility_(ISS_module)" title="Tranquility (ISS module)">\n        Tranquility\n       </a>\n       (formerly known as Node 3) are slightly longer than Unity, measuring almost 6.4 meters (21 feet) long in total. In addition to their six berthing ports, each can hold eight\n       <a href="/wiki/International_Standard_Payload_Rack" title="International Standard Payload Rack">\n        International Standard Payload Racks\n       </a>\n       (ISPRs). Unity, in comparison, holds just four ISPRs. ESA built Nodes 2 and 3 as partial payment for the launch aboard the Shuttle of the\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Columbus_(ISS_module)" title="Columbus (ISS module)">\n         Columbus\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       laboratory module, and other ESA equipment.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Footnotes">\n        Footnotes\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Unity_(ISS_module)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Footnotes">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="reflist" style="list-style-type: decimal;">\n       <div class="mw-references-wrap">\n        <ol class="references">\n         <li id="cite_note-nssdc-1">\n          <span class="mw-cite-backlink">\n           <b>\n            <a href="#cite_ref-nssdc_1-0">\n             ^\n            </a>\n           </b>\n          </span>\n          <span class="reference-text">\n           <cite class="citation web">\n            <a class="external text" href="http://nssdc.gsfc.nasa.gov/nmc/spacecraftDisplay.do?id=1998-069F" rel="nofollow">\n             "NASA - NSSDC - Spacecraft - Details"\n            </a>\n            <span class="reference-accessdate">\n             .
