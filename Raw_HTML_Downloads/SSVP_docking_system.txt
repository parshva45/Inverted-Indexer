class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">\n     <div class="mw-parser-output">\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        Sistema Stykovki i Vnutrennego Perekhoda, SSVP\n       </b>\n       (\n       <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">\n        Russian\n       </a>\n       :\n       <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">\n        \xd0\xa1\xd0\xb8\xd1\x81\xd1\x82\xd0\xb5\xd0\xbc\xd0\xb0 \xd1\x81\xd1\x82\xd1\x8b\xd0\xba\xd0\xbe\xd0\xb2\xd0\xba\xd0\xb8 \xd0\xb8 \xd0\xb2\xd0\xbd\xd1\x83\xd1\x82\xd1\x80\xd0\xb5\xd0\xbd\xd0\xbd\xd0\xb5\xd0\xb3\xd0\xbe \xd0\xbf\xd0\xb5\xd1\x80\xd0\xb5\xd1\x85\xd0\xbe\xd0\xb4\xd0\xb0\n       </span>\n       ,\n       <i>\n        System for docking and internal transfer\n       </i>\n       ) is a\n       <a href="/wiki/Docking_and_berthing_of_spacecraft" title="Docking and berthing of spacecraft">\n        docking standard\n       </a>\n       used by\n       <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">\n        Soviet\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">\n        Russian\n       </a>\n       spacecraft,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dockingSystems_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-dockingSystems-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       sometimes called RDS for\n       <i>\n        Russian Docking System\n       </i>\n       . It has been used on all variants of\n       <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_(spacecraft)" title="Soyuz (spacecraft)">\n        Soyuz\n       </a>\n       other than the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soyuz_7K-L3" title="Soyuz 7K-L3">\n        Soyuz 7K-L3\n       </a>\n       and early flights of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_7K-OK" title="Soyuz 7K-OK">\n        Soyuz 7K-OK\n       </a>\n       , as well as\n       <a href="/wiki/Progress_(spacecraft)" title="Progress (spacecraft)">\n        Progress\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/TKS_(spacecraft)" title="TKS (spacecraft)">\n        TKS\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Automated_Transfer_Vehicle" title="Automated Transfer Vehicle">\n        ATV\n       </a>\n       , and on all Soviet and Russian\n       <a href="/wiki/Space_station" title="Space station">\n        space stations\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="toc">\n       <div </span>\n         </a>\n        </li>\n       </ul>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">\n        History\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=SSVP_docking_system&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       SSVP was initially conceived in 1967 by the TsKBEM design bureau for use on the then-planned OIS military space station. Though OIS never flew, in 1970 the design was selected for use on the\n       <a href="/wiki/Salyut_programme" title="Salyut programme">\n        Salyut\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Almaz" title="Almaz">\n        Almaz\n       </a>\n       space stations.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dockingSystems_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-dockingSystems-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       During its first attempted use on the\n       <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_10" title="Soyuz 10">\n        Soyuz 10\n       </a>\n       mission, docking was unsuccessful due to a faulty hatch, and a failure in the automatic docking system.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       This led to a number of redesigns to reduce damage from accidental loads.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dockingSystems_1-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-dockingSystems-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In the 1980s, SSVP was further upgraded to support docking of large modules, such as the ones that would be used to construct\n       <a href="/wiki/Mir" title="Mir">\n        Mir\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dockingSystems_1-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-dockingSystems-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       They were used to attach all pressurized modules of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Mir" title="Mir">\n        Mir\n       </a>\n       station, and for most spacecraft dockings (with the exception of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle" title="Space Shuttle">\n        Space Shuttle\n       </a>\n       flights, and\n       <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TM-16" title="Soyuz TM-16">\n        Soyuz TM-16\n       </a>\n       , which used the APAS-89 docking mechanisms located on\n       <a href="/wiki/Kristall" title="Kristall">\n        Kristall\n       </a>\n       and the\n       <a href="/wiki/Mir_Docking_Module" title="Mir Docking Module">\n        Mir Docking Module\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       )\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The modern version of SSVP is SSVP-G4000.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dockingSystems_1-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-dockingSystems-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_orbital_segment" title="Russian orbital segment">\n        Russian segment\n       </a>\n       of the\n       <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">\n        International Space Station\n       </a>\n       features four available passive SSVP-G4000 ports, located on\n       <a href="/wiki/Zvezda_(ISS_module)" title="Zvezda (ISS module)">\n        Zvezda\n       </a>\n       -aft,\n       <a href="/wiki/Rassvet_(ISS_module)" title="Rassvet (ISS module)">\n        Rassvet\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Poisk_(ISS_module)" title="Poisk (ISS module)">\n        Poisk\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Pirs_(ISS_module)" title="Pirs (ISS module)">\n        Pirs\n       </a>\n       . An additional port, on\n       <a href="/wiki/Zarya" title="Zarya">\n        Zarya\n       </a>\n       -nadir, was used to dock Rassvet. In addition to Russian spacecraft, the SSVP was also used on the\n       <a href="/wiki/European_Space_Agency" title="European Space Agency">\n        European\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Automated_Transfer_Vehicle" title="Automated Transfer Vehicle">\n        Automated Transfer Vehicle\n       </a>\n       , which docked to the aft port of Zvezda. These ports were provided by\n       <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">\n        Russia\n       </a>\n       in exchange for the Data Management System, supplied by ESA for use in Zvezda.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       An upgraded version, designed for reusability and to provide a wider tunnel, is planned for use on the next-generation\n       <a href="/wiki/Federation_(spacecraft)" title="Federation (spacecraft)">\n        Federation\n       </a>\n       spacecraft.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Design">\n        Design\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=SSVP_docking_system&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Design">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tmulti tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:342px;max-width:342px">\n        <div class="tsingle" style="float:left;margin:1px;width:160px;max-width:160px">\n         <div class="thumbimage" style="height:147px;overflow:hidden">\n          <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Russian_drogue.jpg">\n           <img alt="" data-file-height="328" data-file-width="351" height="148" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/Russian_drogue.jpg/158px-Russian_drogue.jpg" //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/Russian_drogue.jpg/316px-Russian_drogue.jpg 2x" width="158"/>\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         <div class="thumbcaption" style="clear:left;text-align:center">\n          SSVP Drogue\n         </div>\n        </div>\n        <div class="tsingle" style="float:left;margin:1px;width:178px;max-width:178px">\n         <div class="thumbimage" style="height:147px;overflow:hidden">\n          <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Russian_probe_extended.jpg">\n           <img alt="" data-file-height="632" data-file-width="754" style="clear:left;text-align:center">\n          SSVP Probe\n         </div>\n        </div>\n        <div style="clear:left">\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       SSVP consists of two components; an active probe, and a passive drogue. The probe enters the drogue, and its tip is grasped by soft-capture latches which are then retracted using electrically-driven motors to provide close alignment. Eight hard-capture locks then firmly attach the two spacecraft. After hard docking, the pressure between spacecraft is equalized using the Interface Leak Check System.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dockingSystems_1-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-dockingSystems-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-heritage_7-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-heritage-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The port contains a transfer tunnel, with an internal diameter of 800\xc2\xa0mm. The ring surrounding this tunnel also includes a number of connectors, providing for transfer of power, data, and fuel between two docked vehicles.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dockingSystems_1-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-dockingSystems-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div style="clear:both;">\n      </div>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Hybrid">\n        Hybrid\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=SSVP_docking_system&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Hybrid">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       A "hybrid" version, combining the design of SSVP and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/APAS-95" title="APAS-95">\n        APAS-95\n       </a>\n       , is also available, used for permanent docking of space station modules. This version uses the probe-and-drogue design of the standard SSVP, but with a hard-dock collar from APAS-95. The APAS-95 collar has twelve latches instead of the eight of standard SSVP. This variant is known as SSVP-M8000.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-heritage_7-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-heritage-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       These hybrid SSVP ports are used to permanently connect Pirs, Poisk and Zarya to Zvezda.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-heritage_7-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-heritage-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div style="clear:both;">\n      </div>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">\n
