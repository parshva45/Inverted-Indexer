Hurricanes\n       </b>\n       (formerly known as the\n       <b>\n        Wellington Hurricanes\n       </b>\n       ) are a\n       <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">\n        New composed of 18 teams from New Zealand, Argentina, Japan, Australia and South Africa. The team represents the\n       <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_Rugby_Football_Union" title="East Coast Rugby Football Union">\n        East Coast\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Hawke%27s_Bay_Rugby_Union" title="Hawke\'s Bay Rugby Union">\n        Hawke\'s Bay\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Horowhenua-Kapiti_Rugby_Football_Union" title="Horowhenua-Kapiti Rugby Football Union">\n        Horowhenua Kapiti\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Manawatu_Rugby_Union" title="Manawatu Rugby Union">\n        Manawatu\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Poverty_Bay_Rugby_Football_Union" title="Poverty Bay Rugby Football Union">\n        Poverty Bay\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a       </a>\n       unions, and currently plays at\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Westpac_Stadium" title="Westpac Stadium">\n        Westpac Stadium\n       </a>\n       , having previously played at the now-defunct\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Athletic_Park_(Wellington)" title="Athletic Park (Wellington)">\n        Athletic Park\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wotzon_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Wotzon-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VicAthletic_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-VicAthletic-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The Hurricanes were formed to represent the lower\n       <a href="/wiki/North_Island" title="North Island">\n        North Island\n       </a>\n       with the conception of the Super 12 competition in 1996, which featured teams from\n       <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">\n        New Zealand\n       </a>\n       , South Africa and Australia. The Hurricanes had a poor first season, but rebounded in 1997 with a third placing. The team did not reach the play-offs for another five years as the team struggled in the bottom four of the table. Since 2003 the Hurricanes have made the post-season play-offs seven times out of the last fourteen seasons; including the 2006 final, which they lost in horrendously foggy weather against the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crusaders_(rugby)" title="Crusaders (rugby)">\n        Crusaders\n       </a>\n       19\xe2\x80\x9312. After hosting, but failing to win the final in 2015, the 2016 season was the Hurricanes best season to date. They won the Super Rugby Final 20-3 against the\n       <a href="/wiki/Lions_(Super_Rugby)" title="Lions (Super Rugby)">\n        Lions\n       </a>\n       , after again finishing the regular season first and hosting the final.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="toc">\n       <div class="toctitle">\n        <h2>\n         Contents\n        </h2>\n       </div>\n <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricanes_(rugby_union)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <h3>\n       <span id="Early_years:_1996\xe2\x80\x9397">\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Early_years:_1996.E2.80.9397">\n        Early years: 1996\xe2\x80\x9397\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricanes_(rugby_union)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early years: 1996\xe2\x80\x9397">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       The Hurricanes were formed in 1996 as one of five New Zealand Super 12 teams, and were originally called the "Wellington Hurricanes". The team\'s first coach was former\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/All_Black" title="All Black">\n        All Black\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Frank_Oliver_(rugby_union)" title="Frank Oliver (rugby union)">\n        Frank Oliver\n       </a>\n       while\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mark_%22Bull%22_Allen" title=\'Mark "Bull" Allen\'>\n        Bull Allen\n       </a>\n       was named as captain. Their first match, played at\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Arena_Manawatu" title="Arena Manawatu">\n        Palmerston North Showgrounds\n       </a>\n       against the\n       <a href="/wiki/Blues_(Super_Rugby)" title="Blues (Super Rugby)">\n        Auckland Blues\n       </a>\n       , was the first ever Super Rugby match. They lost it 36\xe2\x80\x9328. The team performed below expectations in the inaugural year of the competition and finished ninth. In 1997 the team made the semi-finals, losing in\n       <a href="/wiki/Canberra" title="Canberra">\n        Canberra\n       </a>\n       to the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ACT_Brumbies" title="ACT Brumbies">\n        ACT Brumbies\n       </a>\n       . However the consistent form shown during this season would not be seen again for many years.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span id="Expect_the_unexpected:_1998\xe2\x80\x932002">\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Expect_the_unexpected:_1998.E2.80.932002">\n        Expect the unexpected: 1998\xe2\x80\x932002\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricanes_(rugby_union)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Expect the unexpected: 1998\xe2\x80\x932002">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Following their 1997 season, the Hurricanes failed to qualify for the semi-finals until 2003. Despite this, they were still known for the attacking nature of their backline that included the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/All_Blacks" title="All Blacks">\n        All Blacks\n       </a>\n       stars\n       <a href="/wiki/Tana_Umaga" title="Tana Umaga">\n        Tana Umaga\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Christian_Cullen" title="Christian Cullen">\n        Christian Cullen\n       </a>\n       . The team played with flair and could score at any moment, whatever their position on the field, giving rise to the teams catch cry of \'expect the unexpected\'. However the team struggled for consistent performances and at crunch time in matches, leading to patchy form and results.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       After the\n       <a href="/wiki/1999_Rugby_World_Cup" title="1999 Rugby World Cup">\n        1999 World Cup\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Jonah_Lomu" title="Jonah Lomu">\n        Jonah Lomu\n       </a>\n       \'s contract with the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/New_Zealand_Rugby_Union" title="New Zealand Rugby Union">\n        NZRU\n       </a>\n       expired he was linked to many clubs around the world, in\n       <a href="/wiki/Rugby_league" title="Rugby league">\n        rugby league\n       </a>\n       as well as union and also the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NFL" title="NFL">\n        NFL\n       </a>\n       \'s\n       <a href="/wiki/Dallas_Cowboys" title="Dallas Cowboys">\n        Dallas Cowboys\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lomu1_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-lomu1-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On 23 November 1999 it was announced that the winger had resigned from the NZRU and agreed terms with the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wellington_Rugby_Union" title="Wellington Rugby Union">\n        Wellington Rugby Union\n       </a>\n       , despite a reported a \xc2\xa31.1\xc2\xa0million offer by\n       <a href="/wiki/Bristol_Rugby" title="Bristol Rugby">\n        Bristol\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lomu1_3-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-lomu1-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lomu2_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-lomu2-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The move to the Wellington union meant he could be included in the protected group of players for the Hurricanes.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The Hurricanes also opened 2000 with a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Westpac_Stadium" title="Westpac Stadium">\n        new stadium\n       </a>\n       . The highlights of that year included the victory over eventual champions the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crusaders_(rugby)" title="Crusaders (rugby)">\n        Crusaders\n       </a>\n       , 41\xe2\x80\x9329, in front of a packed house. At the end of the season the \'Canes still had a mathematical chance of making the semis and only had to beat the\n       <a href="/wiki/Bulls_(rugby_union)" title="Bulls (rugby union)">\n        Bulls\n       </a>\n       to stay in contention. However, the Hurricanes played one of their worst games of the year, losing the match to one of the worst performing teams at that point in the competition\'s history and lost the possibility of qualifying for the semi-finals. The team finished eighth on the table.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Despite the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wellington_Lions" title="Wellington Lions">\n        Wellington Lions\n       </a>\n       (whom most of the Hurricanes squad were chosen from) winning the 2000\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Air_New_Zealand_Cup" title="Air New Zealand Cup">\n        NPC\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-npc2000_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-npc2000-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       the Hurricanes finished ninth in the final standings in 2001; one worse than the year before. Another ninth placing in 2002 resulted in\n       <a href="/wiki/Graham_Mourie" title="Graham Mourie">\n        Graham Mourie\n       </a>\n       , who had led the team since 2000, resigning.\n (rugby)">\n        Colin Cooper\n       </a>\n       , the then\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crusaders_(rugby)" title="Crusaders (rugby)">\n        Crusaders\n       </a>\n lure him away from the champions and made him their head coach for the 2003 season.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Cooper, along with newly appointed captain\n       <a href="/wiki/Tana_Umaga" title="Tana Umaga">\n        Tana Umaga\n       </a>\n       , helped to mould the inconsistent and ill-disciplined Hurricanes into one of the top teams in the competition.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hurrinew_8-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-hurrinew-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       2003 was the beginning of a new era for the Hurricanes as they reached the semi-finals for just the second time in their history on the back of a strong seven-game winning streak mid-season. Their success came partly with the break-out year for mid-fielder\n       <a href="/wiki/Ma%27a_Nonu" title="Ma\'a Nonu">\n        Ma\'a Nonu\n       </a>\n       , his strong performances and partnership with captain\n       <a href="/wiki/Tana_Umaga" title="Tana Umaga">\n        Tana Umaga\n       </a>\n       pushed out former\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/All_Black" title="All Black">\n        All Black\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Pita_Alatini" title="Pita Alatini">\n        Pita Alatini\n       </a>\n       and saw him score six tries en route to the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/All_Black" title="All Black">\n        All Black\n       </a>\n       squad. The team also benefited from the steady hand of\n       <a href="/wiki/David_Holwell" title="David Holwell">\n        David Holwell\n       </a>\n       at first five-eighth and an improving and mobile forward pack. <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Ownership_and_Finances">\n        Ownership and Finances\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricanes_(rugby_union)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Ownership and Finances">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       In 2012, it was announced that a new company, Hurricanes\' Investment Ltd Partnership, had purchased a license from the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NZRU" title="NZRU">\n        NZRU\n       </a>\n       to operate the club.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stuff-2012_17-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-stuff-2012-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Whilst the NZRU retains ownership of the team, as well as control of the contracts of the players and head coach, the licensee is responsible for overseeing day-to-day operations. Hurricanes\' Investment Ltd Partnership is a joint venture between the\n       <a href="/wiki/Wellington_Rugby_Football_Union" title="Wellington Rugby Football Union">\n        Wellington Rugby Football Union\n       </a>\n       owning 50 per cent of shares with the remaining 50 per cent held by a consortium of private investors, led by noted economist and author\n       <a href="/wiki/Gareth_Morgan_(economist)" title="Gareth Morgan (economist)">\n        Gareth Morgan\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stuff-2012_17-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-stuff-2012-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Development_team">\n        <span id="A">\n        </span>\n        Development team\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricanes_(rugby_union)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Development team">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       The Hurricanes have fielded a development team in competitions such as the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_Rugby_Cup" title="Pacific Rugby Cup">\n        Pacific Rugby Cup\n       </a>\n       and in matches against other representative teams for several seasons. Known as the Hurricanes Development XV, the squad is selected from the best emerging rugby talent in the Hurricanes catchment area and is composed of Hurricanes contracted players, wider training group members, under 20s, and selected club players.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dev-xv_18-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-dev-xv-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n
