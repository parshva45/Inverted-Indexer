 band\n       <a href="/wiki/Kyuss" title="Kyuss">\n        Kyuss\n       </a>\n       , released on July 11, 1995, nearly a year before their breakup. Drummer\n       <a href="/wiki/Alfredo_Hern%C3%A1ndez" title="Alfredo Hern\xc3\xa1ndez">\n        Alfredo Hern\xc3\xa1ndez\n       </a>\n       (\n       <a href="/wiki/Yawning_Man" title="Yawning Man">\n        Yawning Man\n       </a>\n       ) replaces\n       <a href="/wiki/Brant_Bjork" title="Brant Bjork">\n        Brant Bjork\n       </a>\n       , who left Kyuss in 1993. The album features a tighter and more straightforward sound, both in songwriting and production, than the band\'s preceding efforts.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Upon its release,\n       <i>\n        \xe2\x80\xa6And the Circus Leaves Town\n       </i>\n       was not as commercially or critically successful as the previous\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Blues_for_the_Red_Sun" title="Blues for the Red Sun">\n         Blues for the Red Sun\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       and\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Welcome_to_Sky_Valley" title="Welcome to Sky Valley">\n         Welcome to Sky Valley\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       . Critic Dean Brown attributes this partly to a lack of promotion and the band\'s breakup, but also notes that the album "deserves to be cherished as much as the two molten hot records that came right before it."\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A video was released for "One Inch Man", the album\'s only official single.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="toc">\n       <div class="toctitle">\n        <h2>\n         Contents\n        </h2>\n       </div>\n       <ul>\n
