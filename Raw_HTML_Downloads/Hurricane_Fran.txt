</tr>\n      </table>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        Hurricane Fran\n       </b>\n       caused extensive damage in the\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">\n        United States\n       </a>\n       in early September 1996. The sixth\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        named storm\n       </a>\n       , fifth hurricane, and fourth major hurricane of the\n       <a href="/wiki/1996_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1996 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1996 Atlantic hurricane season\n       </a>\n       , Fran developed from a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">\n        tropical wave\n       </a>\n       near\n       <a href="/wiki/Cape_Verde" title="Cape Verde">\n        Cape Verde\n       </a>\n       on August 23. Due to nearby\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Edouard_(1996)" title="Hurricane Edouard (1996)">\n        Hurricane Edouard\n       </a>\n       , the depression remained disorganized as it tracked westward, though it eventually intensified into Tropical Storm Fran on August 27. While heading west-northwestward, Fran steadily strengthened into a hurricane on August 29, but weakened back to a tropical storm on the following day. On August 31, Fran quickly re-intensified into a hurricane. By September 2, Fran began to parallel the islands of\n       <a href="/wiki/The_Bahamas" title="The Bahamas">\n        the Bahamas\n       </a>\n       and slowly curved north-northwestward. Fran peaked as a 120\xc2\xa0mph (195\xc2\xa0km/h)\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">\n        Category 3 hurricane\n       </a>\n       by early on September 5. Thereafter, Fran weakened slightly, before it made landfall near\n       <a href="/wiki/Cape_Fear_(headland)" title="Cape Fear (headland)">\n        Cape Fear\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">\n        North Carolina\n       </a>\n       early on September 6. The storm rapidly weakened inland and was only a tropical depression later that day. Eventually, Fran curved east-northeastward and transitioned into an extratropical cyclone over\n       <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">\n        Ontario\n       </a>\n       early on September 9.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In Florida, high tides capsized a boat with five people aboard, though all were rescued. No significant effects were reported in\n       <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">\n        Georgia\n       </a>\n       . The outerbands of Fran produced high winds and light to moderate rainfall in\n       <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina" title="South Carolina">\n        South Carolina\n       </a>\n       . As a result, numerous trees and powerlines were downed, which damaged cars, left over 63,000 people without electricity. Large waves in North Carolina caused significant coastal flooding in some cities. Overall, Fran was attributed to 27 fatalities and $3.2 billion (1996 USD) in damage. <p>\n       Hurricane Fran originated from a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">\n        tropical wave\n       </a>\n       that moved off the western coast of Africa, entering the Atlantic Ocean, on August\xc2\xa022, 1996. Not long after moving over water,\n       <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_convection" title="Atmospheric convection">\n        convective\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Rainband" title="Rainband">\n        banding features\n       </a>\n       formed around a developing\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Low_pressure_area" title="Low pressure area">\n        area of low pressure\n       </a>\n       . Ships in the vicinity of the system confirmed that a surface circulation had formed later that day. After further development, the\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">\n        National Hurricane Center\n       </a>\n       (NHC) initiated advisories on the system around 8:00\xc2\xa0am\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eastern_Daylight_Time" title="Eastern Daylight Time">\n        EDT\n       </a>\n       on August\xc2\xa023, designating it as Tropical Depression Six. At this time, the depression was situated to the southeast of the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cape_Verde_Islands" title="Cape Verde Islands">\n        Cape Verde Islands\n       </a>\n       . Over the following several days, little development took place as the system moved westward at 17\xc2\xa0mph (27\xc2\xa0km/h). The westward motion and lack of development were attributed to the low-level inflow from\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Edouard_(1996)" title="Hurricane Edouard (1996)">\n        Hurricane Edouard\n       </a>\n       located roughly 850\xc2\xa0mi (1,370\xc2\xa0km) west-northwest of the depression.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       By August\xc2\xa026, the depression had become significantly disorganized, prompting the NHC to issue their initial final advisory on the system.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The following day, satellite imagery depicted an improved circulation, leading to the re-issuance of advisories.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       However, post-storm analysis indicated that the system maintained tropical depression status during the 24\xe2\x80\x91hour span. During the afternoon of August\xc2\xa027, the depression intensified further, becoming a tropical storm and receiving the name Fran. At this time, Tropical Storm Fran was located about 1,000\xc2\xa0mi (1,600\xc2\xa0km) east of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">\n        Lesser Antilles\n       </a>\n       . Following a similar track as Edouard, the newly named storm maintained a west-northwest track while gaining strength.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_3-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Fran_peak.png">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="536" data-file-width="501" height="235" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Hurricane_Fran_peak.png/220px-Hurricane_Fran_peak.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Hurricane_Fran_peak.png/330px-Hurricane_Fran_peak.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Hurricane_Fran_peak.png/440px-Hurricane_Fran_peak.png 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Fran_peak.png" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Colorized infrared image of Fran near peak intensity east of the northern\n         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">\n          Bahamas\n         </a>\n         and\n         <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">\n          Florida\n         </a>\n         .\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Following the development of deep convection around Fran\'s center of circulation on August\xc2\xa029, the NHC upgraded it to a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SSHS#Category_1" title="SSHS">\n        Category\xc2\xa01\n       </a>\n       hurricane, the lowest ranking on the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson Hurricane Scale">\n        Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson Hurricane Scale\n       </a>\n       , with winds of 75\xc2\xa0mph (120\xc2\xa0km/h). However, the hurricane weakened to a tropical storm on August\xc2\xa030 as it became less organized, possibly due to an interaction with\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Edouard_(1996)" title="Hurricane Edouard (1996)">\n        Hurricane Edouard\n       </a>\n       to the north. During this time, the forward motion of the storm significantly decreased and it took a more northwestward track. However, this weakening was short-lived and Fran re-attained hurricane status the following day as Edouard moved towards the\n       <a href="/wiki/Mid-Atlantic_states" title="Mid-Atlantic states">\n        Mid-Atlantic coastline\n       </a>\n       . The storm also resumed its west-northwest movement as a\n       <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_cyclone" title="Subtropical cyclone">\n        subtropical\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">\n        ridge\n       </a>\n       to the north strengthened.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_3-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Fran.png">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="319" data-file-width="424" height="166" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/Hurricane_Fran.png/220px-Hurricane_Fran.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/Hurricane_Fran.png/330px-Hurricane_Fran.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/d/d7/Hurricane_Fran.png 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Fran.png" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Infrared image of Fran making landfall near Cape Fear, North Carolina on September\xc2\xa05\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Gradual intensification took place for the first several days of September, with Fran attaining winds of 90\xc2\xa0mph (150\xc2\xa0km/h) on September\xc2\xa03. By this point, the storm began to develop an\n       <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">\n        eye\n       </a>\n       and a more rapid phase of strengthening took place. Early the next day, Fran attained\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SSHS#Category_3" title="SSHS">\n        Category 3\n       </a>\n       intensity as its maximum sustained winds increased to 115\xc2\xa0mph (185\xc2\xa0km/h). A more northwesterly track also began to appear as it approached the Bahamas. Passing roughly 115\xc2\xa0mi (185\xc2\xa0km) east of the Bahamas, Fran attained its peak intensity on September\xc2\xa05 with winds of 120\xc2\xa0mi/h (195\xc2\xa0km/h) and a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barometric_pressure" title="Barometric pressure">\n        barometric pressure\n       </a>\n       of 946\xc2\xa0mbar (hPa; 27.94\xc2\xa0inHg). The eye of Fran was roughly 29\xc2\xa0mi (47\xc2\xa0km) in diameter at this time.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_3-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       A large hurricane, the storm\'s forward motion increased as it moved northwest towards\n       <a href="/wiki/The_Carolinas" title="The Carolinas">\n        The Carolinas\n       </a>\n       . Around 7:30\xc2\xa0 PM EDT on Thursday, September\xc2\xa05,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       the center of Hurricane Fran made\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">\n        landfall\n       </a>\n       near\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cape_Fear,_North_Carolina" title="Cape Fear, North Carolina">\n        Cape Fear, North Carolina\n       </a>\n       with sustained winds of 115\xc2\xa0mph (185\xc2\xa0km/h). Once overland, the storm began to rapidly weaken, degrading to a tropical storm within 12\xc2\xa0hours. As the weakening storm moved through Virginia, the NHC further downgraded it to a tropical depression. Continuing on a northwestern track, the remnants of Fran persisted as a tropical depression through September\xc2\xa08, at which time it transitioned into an\n       <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">\n        extratropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       over southern\n       <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">\n        Ontario\n       </a>\n       . After completing this transition, Fran turned northeastward and tracked near the Canadian-United States border before being absorbed by a\n       <a href="/wiki/Weather_front" title="Weather front">\n        frontal system\n       </a>\n       on September\xc2\xa010.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_3-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">\n        Preparations\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span the\n       <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">\n        Lesser Antilles\n       </a>\n       on August 29, a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_watch" title="Hurricane watch">\n        hurricane watch\n       </a>\n       was issued for the northernmost of the islands, between\n       <a href="/wiki/Antigua" title="Antigua">\n        Antigua\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Saint_Martin" title="Saint Martin">\n        Saint Martin\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-franserviceassesment_7-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-franserviceassesment-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       However, as the hurricane weakened and pulled away from the islands to the northwest, the watch was discontinued. At the same time, a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_watch" title="Hurricane watch">\n        hurricane watch\n       </a>\n       and a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_storm_warning" title="Tropical storm warning">\n        tropical storm warning\n       </a>\n       was declared for the central\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">\n        Bahamas\n       </a>\n       . On September 3, the hurricane watch was extended to include the northern Bahamas and a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_warning" title="Hurricane warning">\n        hurricane warning\n       </a>\n       was declared for the northwestern islands. All watches for the Bahamas were cancelled on September 4.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-franserviceassesment_7-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-franserviceassesment-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       No preparations were taken by the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Government_of_the_Bahamas" title="Government of the Bahamas">\n        government of the Bahamas\n       </a>\n       in anticipation of Fran.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bahamasdailycourier_8-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-bahamasdailycourier-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Florida">\n        Florida\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Fran&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Florida">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Early in its span, Hurricane Fran was forecast to heavily impact\n       <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">\n        Florida\n       </a>\n       , and multiple watches and warnings were issued for the state as Hurricane Fran neared the United States.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-franserviceassesment_7-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-franserviceassesment-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       As the hurricane progressively moved further to the north, watches and warnings imposed on Florida continued to move further north along the coast. On September 4, a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_storm_warning" title="Tropical storm warning">\n        tropical storm warning\n       </a>\n       was issued, but only from\n       <a href="/wiki/Brunswick,_Georgia" title="Brunswick, Georgia">\n        Brunswick, Georgia\n       </a>\n       to\n       <a href="/wiki/Flagler_Beach,_Florida" title="Flagler Beach, Florida">\n        Flagler Beach, Florida\n       </a>\n       , located in the northern parts of the state. All watches and warnings on the state were discontinued by September 5.\n       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">\n        [\n        <i>\n         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">\n          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Fran&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Georgia">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       The first hurricane watches and warnings for\n       <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">\n        Georgia\n       </a>\n       were first imposed on September 4 as Hurricane Fran became a major hurricane with a watch extending from Florida through Georgia and into\n       <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina" title="South Carolina">\n        South Carolina\n       </a>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-franserviceassesment_7-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-franserviceassesment-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The watch was upgraded to a warning later that day as Fran moved closer to the coast. While areas south of Brunswick, Georgia were only issued a tropical storm warning, areas north of the city were declared under a hurricane warning until September 5, when it was downgraded into a tropical storm warning. All watches and warnings on the Georgia coast were discontinued by the end of September 6.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       On September 4, while much of Georgia was under a hurricane watch, multiple\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Emergency_operation_center" title="Emergency operation center">\n        emergency operation centers\n       </a>\n       were activated. States, Fran produced strong winds and heavy rainfall, leading to widespread flash flooding and wind damage. The most severe damage took place in North Carolina where 14 people died, one of which was from a heart attack, and the storm left $2.4\xc2\xa0billion in losses. Throughout other states, 13 other people lost their lives and an additional $800\xc2\xa0million in damage was caused. Overall, Hurricane Fran was directly responsible for 22 fatalities and indirectly for five others as well as $3.2\xc2\xa0billion in damage. At the time, Fran was one of the ten costliest hurricanes to strike the United States; however, several other storms have since surpassed it.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Florida_and_South_Carolina">\n        Florida and South Carolina\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Fran&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Florida and South Carolina">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Prior to moving over\n       <a href="/wiki/The_Carolinas" title="The Carolinas">\n        The Carolinas\n       </a>\n       , large swells produced by Hurricane Fran impacted the Florida coastline. Along the beaches of\n       <a href="/wiki/Palm_Beach_County,_Florida" title="Palm Beach County, Florida">\n        Palm Beach County\n       </a>\n       , five people aboard an 18\xc2\xa0ft (5.5\xc2\xa0m) fishing boat were knocked into the water by the rough seas. However, the\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">\n        Coast Guard\n       </a>\n       rescued all five persons without incident.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       As Fran made landfall in North Carolina, the outer bands of the storm brought heavy rains and gusty winds to eastern South Carolina. Several areas reported winds in excess of 40\xc2\xa0mph (64\xc2\xa0km/h), leading to numerous downed trees and power lines. Some cars and homes were damaged after being struck by fallen trees.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Dillon_County,_South_Carolina" title="Dillon County, South Carolina">\n        Dillon County\n       </a>\n       , winds gusting up to 70\xc2\xa0mph (110\xc2\xa0km/h) caused significant damage to many homes. Debris was left in the wake of Fran across the county than during\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hugo" title="Hurricane Hugo">\n        Hurricane Hugo\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/1989_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1989 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1989\n       </a>\n       . One person was injured and damage to crops and infrastructure reached $6.5\xc2\xa0million.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">\n        <a href="#cite_note-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Marlboro_County,_South_Carolina" title="Marlboro County, South Carolina">\n        Marlboro County\n       </a>\n       , roughly 3,200 people were left without power and two sheriffs were injured after their car struck a fallen tree.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">\n        <a href="#cite_note-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The most severe damage in South Carolina took place in\n       <a href="/wiki/Horry_County,_South_Carolina" title="Horry County, South Carolina">\n        Horry County\n       </a>\n       where winds reached 77\xc2\xa0mph (124\xc2\xa0km/h). Numerous trees were felled by the winds, leaving roughly 60,000 residents without power. One person was killed after her car fell down an embankment. Extensive agricultural losses were sustained in the area, estimated at $19.8\xc2\xa0million. Structural damage was less severe, with losses estimated at $1\xc2\xa0million.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">\n        <a href="#cite_note-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A second car-related fatality during Fran took place in\n       <a href="/wiki/Williamsburg_County,_South_Carolina" title="Williamsburg County, South Carolina">\n        Williamsburg County\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">\n        <a href="#cite_note-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Combined economic losses in\n       <a href="/wiki/Berkeley_County,_South_Carolina" title="Berkeley County, South Carolina">\n        Berkeley\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Charleston_County,_South_Carolina" title="Charleston County, South Carolina">\n        Charleston\n       </a>\n       counties reached $20\xc2\xa0million.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">\n        <a href="#cite_note-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Throughout South Carolina, Fran was responsible for two fatalities, five injuries and roughly $48.5\xc2\xa0million in damage.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="North_Carolina">\n        North Carolina\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Fran&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: North Carolina">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:FEMA_-_1181_-_Photograph_by_FEMA_News_Photo_taken_on_09-06-1996_in_North_Carolina.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:FEMA_-_1181_-_Photograph_by_FEMA_News_Photo_taken_on_09-06-1996_in_North_Carolina.jpg" coastal damage from the\n       <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina" title="South Carolina">\n        South Carolina\n       </a>\n       border to\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Topsail_Island,_North_Carolina" title="Topsail Island, North Carolina">\n        Topsail Island, North Carolina\n       </a>\n       . Its 12-foot storm surge carried away a temporary\n       <a href="/wiki/North_Topsail_Beach,_North_Carolina" title="North Topsail Beach, North Carolina">\n        North Topsail Beach\n       </a>\n       police station and town hall, housed in a double-wide trailer since\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Bertha_(1996)" title="Hurricane Bertha (1996)">\n        Hurricane Bertha\n       </a>\n       \'s rampage across the same area in July. The Kure Beach Pier was destroyed during the storm as well. Extensive flooding struck the coast around\n       <a href="/wiki/Wrightsville_Beach,_North_Carolina" title="Wrightsville Beach, North Carolina">\n        Wrightsville Beach\n       </a>\n       , just up the coast from Cape Fear. In\n       <a href="/wiki/Jacksonville,_North_Carolina" title="Jacksonville, North Carolina">\n        Jacksonville, North Carolina\n       </a>\n       , three schools and several homes were damaged. The storm was most damaging to the barrier islands on the\n       <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">\n        North Carolina\n       </a>\n       coastline.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Inland, the storm caused damage on its way north from\n       <a href="/wiki/Wilmington,_North_Carolina" title="Wilmington, North Carolina">\n        Wilmington\n       </a>\n       to\n       <a href="/wiki/Raleigh,_North_Carolina" title="Raleigh, North Carolina">\n        Raleigh\n       </a>\n       . Unexpectedly, high wind damage extended along the I-40 corridor up through Raleigh and points north and as far west as\n       <a href="/wiki/Guilford_County,_North_Carolina" title="Guilford County, North Carolina">\n        Guilford County\n       </a>\n       , damaging historic buildings and trees throughout the Triangle including at\n       <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina_State_University" title="North Carolina State University">\n        North Carolina State University\n       </a>\n       and the\n       <a href="/wiki/University_of_North_Carolina_at_Chapel_Hill" title="University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill">\n        University of North Carolina\n       </a>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-daviepoplar_19-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-daviepoplar-19">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Classes were canceled for the day at UNC due to a state of emergency in Chapel Hill, and it was almost a week before the university\'s water supply was drinkable again.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Rain of up to 16 inches (406\xc2\xa0mm) deluged interior North Carolina, Virginia and\n       <a href="/wiki/West_Virginia" title="West Virginia">\n        West Virginia\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rain_20-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rain-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       bringing dangerous river flooding to much of the mid-Atlantic. Hurricane Fran\'s thrashing of North Carolina aggravated the state\'s problems caused by numerous weather disasters in 1996.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       At least six people were killed in the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carolinas" title="Carolinas">\n        Carolinas\n       </a>\n       ; most of them were from auto accidents and more died as a result of the shock from the damage of the storm. In\n       <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">\n        North Carolina\n       </a>\n       , 1.3 million people were left without power. In\n       <a href="/wiki/North_Topsail_Beach,_North_Carolina" title="North Topsail Beach, North Carolina">\n        North Topsail Beach\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Carteret_County,_North_Carolina" title="Carteret County, North Carolina">\n        Carteret County\n       </a>\n       , there was over $500\xc2\xa0million (1996\xc2\xa0USD) in damage and 90% of structures were damaged.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-assess_21-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-assess-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       One male teenager died from drowning caused by flooding of\n       <a href="/wiki/Crabtree_Creek_(Neuse_River)" title="Crabtree Creek (Neuse River)">\n        Crabtree Creek\n       </a>\n       at Old Lassiter Mill in Raleigh. Fran also destroyed the Basketball Gym on the Campus of St. Andrews College in Laurinburg, North Carolina. Hurricane Fran also destroyed 3 piers in Surf City.\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Fran_1996_rainfall.gif">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="896" data-file-width="856" height="230" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/48/Fran_1996_rainfall.gif/220px-Fran_1996_rainfall.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/48/Fran_1996_rainfall.gif/330px-Fran_1996_rainfall.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/48/Fran_1996_rainfall.gif/440px-Fran_1996_rainfall.gif 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Fran_1996_rainfall.gif" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Rainfall from Hurricane Fran in the United States\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Total damage in North Carolina amounted to roughly $2.4 billion.\n       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">\n        [\n        <i>\n         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">\n          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2013)">\n           citation needed\n          </span>\n         </a>\n        </i>\n        ]\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       This was the second hurricane to hit North Carolina that year. The first was Hurricane Bertha, which hit the state a few weeks prior.\n       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">\n        [\n        <i>\n         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">\n          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2013)">\n           citation needed\n          </span>\n         </a>\n        </i>\n        ]\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Virginia">\n        Virginia\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Fran&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Virginia">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       In Virginia, winds between 39 and 73\xc2\xa0mph (63 and 117\xc2\xa0km/h) lashed\n       <a href="/wiki/Chesapeake_Bay" title="Chesapeake Bay">\n        Chesapeake Bay\n       </a>\n       and increased water levels in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Potomac_River" title="Potomac River">\n        Potomac River\n       </a>\n       around the nation\'s capital, where it backed up into\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Georgetown,_Washington,_D.C." title="Georgetown, Washington, D.C.">\n        Georgetown\n       </a>\n       and Old Town\n       <a href="/wiki/Alexandria,_Virginia" title="Alexandria, Virginia">\n        Alexandria, Virginia\n       </a>\n       . There was severe damage to power lines that left 415,000 people without electricity, making it the largest storm related power outage in history until\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Isabel" title="Hurricane Isabel">\n        Hurricane Isabel\n       </a>\n       in 2003.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-assess_21-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-assess-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Along the\n       <a href="/wiki/Rappahannock_River" title="Rappahannock River">\n        Rappahannock River\n       </a>\n       , a storm surge of 5\xc2\xa0ft (1.5\xc2\xa0m) damaged or sank several small boats and damaged wharfs and bulkheads. This was the highest tide in the state since\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hazel" title="Hurricane Hazel">\n        Hurricane Hazel\n       </a>\n       of 1954.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-assess_21-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-assess-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Rain up to 16\xc2\xa0in (410\xc2\xa0mm) fell in the western part of\n       <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">\n        Virginia\n       </a>\n       , making Fran the fourth wettest known tropical cyclone to impact Virginia and causing major flash flooding. The floods shut down many of the primary and secondary roads and closed\n       <a href="/wiki/Shenandoah_National_Park" title="Shenandoah National Park">\n        Shenandoah National Park\n       </a>\n       . Fran destroyed about 300 homes, mostly from flooding, and 100 people had to be rescued.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <a href="/wiki/Page_County,_Virginia" title="Page County, Virginia">\n        Page County\n       </a>\n       , downslope of the 16" of rainfall from\n       <a href="/wiki/Big_Meadows" title="Big Meadows">\n        Big Meadows\n       </a>\n       , was the hardest hit locality in the state of Virginia with regards to damage. Three days after the storm had passed, "hundreds" of people were still stranded. Some 78 homes were destroyed and 417 were damaged, however there were no deaths. At one point on Friday every town in the county was isolated due to high water.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">\n        <a href="#cite_note-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In the county seat of\n       <a href="/wiki/Luray,_Virginia" title="Luray, Virginia">\n        Luray\n       </a>\n       , the Hawksbill Creek cut the town in half for much of the day, and the strong current forced a house off its foundation and placed in the endzone of\n       <a href="/wiki/Luray_High_School" title="Luray High School">\n        Luray High School\n       </a>\n       \'s football field. Water from the Hawksbill reached 2\xc2\xa0ft (0.61\xc2\xa0m) from the top of the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Field_goal_(football)" title="Field goal (football)">\n        field goal\n       </a>\n       upright\xe2\x80\x94 16\xc2\xa0ft (4.9\xc2\xa0m) of water covered the ground. Bulldog field was flooded for over a week after the storm, until finally the standing water was pumped across\n       <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_340" title="U.S. Route 340">\n        U.S. Route 340\n       </a>\n       back into the Hawksbill Creek. Also in downtown Luray, the large flood-driven waves of the creek demolished three buildings, including the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Adelphia_Cable" title="Adelphia Cable">\n        Adelphia Cable\n       </a>\n       building. The creek, typically less than a foot deep, overtook the downtown Main Street Bridge, which rises some 15\xc2\xa0ft (4.6\xc2\xa0m) above the creek bed.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">\n        <a href="#cite_note-23">\n         [23]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <a href="/wiki/Shenandoah_River" title="Shenandoah River">\n        Shenandoah River\n       </a>\n       crested some 20\xc2\xa0ft (6.1\xc2\xa0m) above flood stage. The South Fork of the Shenandoah River recorded its highest crests ever, 26.95\xc2\xa0ft (8.21\xc2\xa0m) in Luray and 37\xc2\xa0ft (11\xc2\xa0m) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Front_Royal,_Virginia" title="Front Royal, Virginia">\n        Front Royal, Virginia\n       </a>\n       , which was 22\xc2\xa0ft (6.7\xc2\xa0m) above the 15\xc2\xa0ft (4.6\xc2\xa0m) flood stage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">\n        <a href="#cite_note-24">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Rockingham_County,_Virginia" title="Rockingham County, Virginia">\n        Rockingham County, Virginia\n       </a>\n       , over 10,000 people were evacuated from their homes, however most were allowed to return to their homes after the water subsided.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">\n        <a href="#cite_note-25">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span align="center">\n         21\n        </td>\n        <td align="center">\n         33\n        </td>\n       </tr>\n      </table>\n      <p>\n       Up to 7\xc2\xa0inches (178\xc2\xa0mm) of rain fell,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rain_20-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rain-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       causing widespread flash flooding. Pendleton and Hardy County were the hardest hit as the floods swept away several bridges, damaged several water plants and caused a reported gas leak.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-assess_21-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-assess-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Maryland">\n        Maryland\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Fran&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Maryland">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Western\n       <a href="/wiki/Maryland" title="Maryland">\n        Maryland\n       </a>\n       was hard hit by Fran, mostly from flash flooding. About 650 homes were damaged and there was $100\xc2\xa0million (1996\xc2\xa0USD) in damage. This was the worst flooding event to hit Maryland since\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hazel" title="Hurricane Hazel">\n        Hurricane Hazel\n       </a>\n       and the\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=January_flood_of_1996&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="January flood of 1996 (page does not exist)">\n        January flood of 1996\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-assess_21-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-assess-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="District_of_Columbia">\n        District of Columbia\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Fran&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: District of Columbia">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       In the capital city, the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/District_of_Columbia" title="District of Columbia">\n        District of Columbia\n       </a>\n       , Fran produced gusty winds and moderate rainfall. Gusts in the area were recorded in excess of 40\xc2\xa0mph (64\xc2\xa0km/h) and rainfall peaked near 3.5\xc2\xa0in (89\xc2\xa0mm). Minor flash flooding was reported on many streets, hampering travel and leaving several roads were closed.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27">\n        <a href="#cite_note-27">\n         [27]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       These winds downed several trees in saturated soil.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28">\n        <a href="#cite_note-28">\n         [28]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A strong southerly wind and high tide led to a 5.1\xc2\xa0ft (1.6\xc2\xa0m) storm surge in Washington Harbor.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29">\n        <a href="#cite_note-29">\n         [29]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In addition to the storm surge, a river flood rivaling that of the January 1996 flood took place in the metropolitan area.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30">\n        <a href="#cite_note-30">\n         [30]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Throughout Washington D.C., Fran left roughly $20\xc2\xa0million in damage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-assess_21-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-assess-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Pennsylvania">\n        Pennsylvania\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Fran&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Pennsylvania">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       About 15\n       <a href="/wiki/Western_Pennsylvania" title="Western Pennsylvania">\n        Western Pennsylvania\n       </a>\n       counties were hit by flash flooding as rainfall up to 7\xc2\xa0inches (178\xc2\xa0mm) caused the\n       <a href="/wiki/Juniata_River" title="Juniata River">\n        Juniata River\n       </a>\n       to overrun its banks.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rain_20-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rain-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span id="Ohio,_Michigan,_New_England,_and_Canada">\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Ohio.2C_Michigan.2C_New_England.2C_and_Canada">\n        Ohio, Michigan, New England, and Canada\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Fran&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Ohio, Michigan, New England, and Canada">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       The remnants of Fran brought moderate to heavy rainfall to parts of eastern Ohio, especially along the coast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Lake_Erie" title="Lake Erie">\n        Lake Erie\n       </a>\n       , as the storm moved through the region.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rain_20-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rain-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A maximum of 6.47\xc2\xa0in (164\xc2\xa0mm) of rain fell near\n       <a href="/wiki/Elyria,_Ohio" title="Elyria, Ohio">\n        Elyria\n       </a>\n       in relation to Fran.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HPCMW_31-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HPCMW-31">\n         [31]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Sustained winds in the state were recorded around 30\xc2\xa0mph (48\xc2\xa0km/h) and gusts reached 60\xc2\xa0mph (97\xc2\xa0km/h). These winds downed numerous trees and power lines, some of which fell on cars and homes. Agricultural land and the region sustained significant damage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32">\n        <a href="#cite_note-32">\n         [32]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cleveland,_Ohio" title="Cleveland, Ohio">\n        Cleveland\n       </a>\n       , the city zoo sustained some flood damage and the monkey island was completely inundated.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33">\n        <a href="#cite_note-33">\n         [33]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Cuyahoga_County,_Ohio" title="Cuyahoga County, Ohio">\n        Cuyahoga County\n       </a>\n       at least 90 homes reported basement flooding.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34">\n        <a href="#cite_note-34">\n         [34]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Widespread street and basement flooding took place across\n       <a href="/wiki/Lorain_County,_Ohio" title="Lorain County, Ohio">\n        Lorain County\n       </a>\n       , with some areas reporting standing water several days after Fran\'s passage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35">\n        <a href="#cite_note-35">\n         [35]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Throughout Ohio, the remnants of Fran left roughly $40\xc2\xa0million in damage and no loss of life.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_3-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC_36-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NCDC-36">\n         [36]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Continuing northward, Fran moved into southern Canada on September\xc2\xa07; however, the outer bands of the storm brought some rainfall to extreme eastern Michigan. In\n       <a href="/wiki/Port_Huron,_Michigan" title="Port Huron, Michigan">\n        Port Huron\n       </a>\n       , a state maximum amount of 4.07\xc2\xa0in (103\xc2\xa0mm) of rain fell during the storm\'s passage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HPCMW_31-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HPCMW-31">\n         [31]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       No known flooding or damage took place throughout the state.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCDC_36-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NCDC-36">\n         [36]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Turning northeast and becoming extratropical, Fran brought scattered rainfall to parts of New England. A small area around\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boston,_Massachusetts" title="Boston, Massachusetts">\n        Boston, Massachusetts\n       </a>\n       received the heaviest rain in the region, peaking at 5.53\xc2\xa0in (140\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/East_Wareham,_Massachusetts" title="East Wareham, Massachusetts">\n        East Wareham\n       </a>\n       . Isolated areas of 1 to 2\xc2\xa0in (25 to 51\xc2\xa0mm) of rain fell across portions of Vermont, New Hampshire and Maine before Fran dissipated over eastern Canada on September\xc2\xa08.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-New_Eng_37-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-New_Eng-37">\n         [37]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In nearby southern Canada, the remnants of Fran had little impact in\n       <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">\n        Ontario\n       </a>\n       , other than causing rain delays in a minor t-ball tournament, producing light to moderate rainfall between 1.8 and 2.6\xc2\xa0in (45 and 65\xc2\xa0mm).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38">\n        <a href="#cite_note-38">\n         [38]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n
