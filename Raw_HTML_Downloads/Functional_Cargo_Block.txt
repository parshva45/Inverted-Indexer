design.\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <b>\n        Functional Cargo Block\n       </b>\n       or\n       <b>\n        FGB\n       </b>\n       (from the Russian\n       <i>\n        <span lang="ru" xml:lang="ru">\n         "\xd0\xa4\xd1\x83\xd0\xbd\xd0\xba\xd1\x86\xd0\xb8\xd0\xbe\xd0\xbd\xd0\xb0\xd0\xbb\xd1\x8c\xd0\xbd\xd0\xbe-\xd0\xb3\xd1\x80\xd1\x83\xd0\xb7\xd0\xbe\xd0\xb2\xd0\xbe\xd0\xb9 \xd0\xb1\xd0\xbb\xd0\xbe\xd0\xba", \xd0\xa4\xd0\x93\xd0\x91\n        </span>\n       </i>\n       ,\n       <i>\n        <span lang="ru-Latn" xml:lang="ru-Latn">\n         "Funktsionalno-gruzovoy blok", FGB\n        </span>\n       </i>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/GRAU" title="GRAU">\n        GRAU\n       </a>\n       index\n       <b>\n        11F77\n       </b>\n       ) was part of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">\n        Soviet\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/TKS_(spacecraft)" title="TKS (spacecraft)">\n        TKS spacecraft\n       </a>\n       . The TKS spacecraft was intended to be used as a resupply craft for\n       <a href="/wiki/Almaz" title="Almaz">\n        Almaz\n       </a>\n       space stations and saw some test flights in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Salyut_programme" title="Salyut programme">\n        Salyut space station program\n       </a>\n       . The TKS spacecraft was formed by mating a FGB with a\n       <a href="/wiki/VA_spacecraft" title="VA spacecraft">\n        VA spacecraft\n       </a>\n       , with both the VA and the FGB being capable of independent operation.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Following the development of the FGB for the TKS spacecraft, the FGB (without VA spacecraft) formed the basis for\n       <a href="/wiki/Space_station" title="Space station">\n        space station\n       </a>\n       modules in the Soviet and Russian space program \xe2\x80\x93 these space station modules are to some extent called Functional Cargo Block (FGB) as well, like the\n       <a href="/wiki/Zarya" title="Zarya">\n        Zarya\n       </a>\n       FGB module.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RSWtks_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-RSWtks-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EAtks_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-EAtks-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The FGB provides "functional" support in the form of electrical power, propulsion, guidance and docking; The support for "cargo" operations is supplied in the form of a pressurized habitable cargo storage section (accessible by the crew) and the externally mounted fuel tanks.\n      </p>\n       </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main articles:\n       <a href="/wiki/Almaz" title="Almaz">\n        Almaz\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/TKS_(spacecraft)" title="TKS (spacecraft)">\n        TKS (spacecraft)\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/VA_spacecraft" title="VA spacecraft">\n        VA spacecraft\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Salyut_programme" title="Salyut programme">\n        Salyut programme\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The Functional Cargo Block (FGB) was originally developed as support for the\n       <a href="/wiki/VA_spacecraft" title="VA spacecraft">\n        VA spacecraft\n       </a>\n       \xe2\x80\x93 with both together forming the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/TKS_spacecraft" title="TKS spacecraft">\n        TKS spacecraft\n       </a>\n       , intended for resupply flights to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Almaz" title="Almaz">\n        Almaz\n       </a>\n       space stations. For\n       <a href="/wiki/Salyut_programme" title="Salyut programme">\n        Salyut\n       </a>\n       space station modules, it was initially intended that the first such modules be derived from the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Progress_spacecraft" title="Progress spacecraft">\n        Progress spacecraft\n       </a>\n       (which in turn is a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Soyuz_spacecraft" title="Soyuz spacecraft">\n        Soyuz spacecraft\n       </a>\n       derivate), and not the FGB. As the Almaz program wound down, TKS test missions to Salyut stations were used to gather experience on docking larger modules to space stations. The TKS flights proved that the FGBs could form the basis of such a space station module, with the TKS being flight ready, and having a considerable larger capacity than the Progress \xe2\x80\x93 so the FGB and not the Progress became the basis of many Soviet and Russian space station modules. The Gamma observatory, the initially intended first Progress-based research module, would become after these and other changes the free-flying\n       <a href="/wiki/Gamma_(satellite)" title="Gamma (satellite)">\n        Gamma satellite\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mir_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Mir-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Design">\n        Design\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Functional_Cargo_Block&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Design">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       The basic layout of the exemplary\n       <a href="/wiki/Zarya" title="Zarya">\n        Zarya\n       </a>\n       FGB consists of two sections:\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ENGzar_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ENGzar-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <ul>\n       <li>\n        The Instrumentation and Cargo Compartment (ICC) with a pressurized volume of 64.5 cubic metres (2,280\xc2\xa0cu\xc2\xa0ft)\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        The Pressurized Adapter (PA) with a pressurized volume of 7.0 cubic metres (250\xc2\xa0cu\xc2\xa0ft)\n       </li>\n      </ul>\n      <p>\n       The PA is separated from the ICC by a spherical bulkhead containing an 800\xc2\xa0mm hatch.
