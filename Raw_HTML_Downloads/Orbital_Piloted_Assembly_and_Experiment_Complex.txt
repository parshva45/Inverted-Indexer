href="#cite_note-bbc-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       (\n       <b>\n        \xd0\x9e\xd0\x9f\xd0\xa1\xd0\xad\xd0\x9a\n       </b>\n       ,\n       <b>\n        OPSEK\n class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Around the predicted decommissioning of the International Space Station in the late 2020s, construct a successor station in low earth orbit.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Early proposals considered re-using some ISS modules to form the first parts of a new station, which would late be replaced by new modules.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-russianspaceweb_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-russianspaceweb-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On 17 elements of the orbital assembly and experimental piloted space complex by the end of the ISS life cycle."\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-russianspaceweb_4-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-russianspaceweb-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       . href="#cite_note-noISSmodules-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       According to the Russian manned spaceflight contractor\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RKK_Energia" title="RKK Energia">\n        RKK Energia\n       </a>\n       , the new station must be able to perform the following tasks:\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-interfax0818_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-interfax0818-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <ul>\n       <li>\n        Large spacecraft assembly\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Flight tests and launches\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Creating, servicing and completing inter-orbital tugs\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Providing medical and biological conditions required for the rehabilitation of interplanetary expedition crews after their return to Earth orbit.\n       </li>\n      </ul>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       OPSEK will follow the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salyut" title="Salyut">\n        Salyut\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Almaz" title="Almaz">\n        Almaz\n       </a>\n       series,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cosmos_557" title="Cosmos Science), also known as the Multipurpose Laboratory Module (MLM) or FGB-2, (\n       <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">\n        Russian\n       </a>\n       : \xd0\x9c\xd0\xbd\xd0\xbe\xd0\xb3\xd0\xbe\xd1\x84\xd1\x83\xd0\xbd\xd0\xba\xd1\x86\xd0\xb8\xd0\xbe\xd0\xbd\xd0\xb0\xd0\xbb\xd1\x8c\xd0\xbd\xd1\x8b\xd0\xb9 \xd0\xbb\xd0\xb0\xd0\xb1\xd0\xbe\xd1\x80\xd0\xb0\xd1\x82\xd0\xbe\xd1\x80\xd0\xbd\xd1\x8b\xd0\xb9 \xd0\xbc\xd0\xbe\xd0\xb4\xd1\x83\xd0\xbb\xd1\x8c, or \xd0\x9c\xd0\x9b\xd0\x9c), is the major Russian laboratory module. This module will be separated from the ISS before de-orbit with support modules to become the OPSEK space station. It contains an additional set of life support systems and orientation control. Nauka\'s mission has changed over time; during the mid-1990s it was intended as a backup for the first FGB, and later as a universal docking module (UDM). Its docking ports will be able to support automatic docking of both spacecraft, additional modules and fuel transfer. Prior to the arrival of the MLM, a Progress robotic spacecraft will dock with the ISS PIRS module, depart with that module, and both will be discarded. to form part of OPSEK\n       </li>\n      </ul>\n      <p>\n       <a href="/wiki/Uzlovoy_Module" title="Uzlovoy Module">\n        Node Module\n       </a>\n       (UM)/(NM) This 4-ton ball shaped module will support the docking of two scientific and power modules during the final stage of the station assembly and provide the Russian segment additional docking ports to receive Soyuz TMA (transportation modified anthropometric) and Progress M spacecraft. NM is to be incorporated into the ISS in 2016. It will be integrated with a special version of the Progress cargo ship and launched by a standard Soyuz rocket. The Progress would use its own propulsion and flight control system to deliver and dock the Node Module to the nadir (Earth-facing) docking port of the Nauka MLM/FGB-2 module. One port is equipped with an active hybrid docking port, which enables docking with the MLM module. The remaining five ports are passive hybrids, enabling docking of Soyuz and Progress vehicles, as well as heavier modules and future spacecraft with modified docking systems. More importantly, the node module was conceived to serve as the only permanent element of OPSEK. Equipped with six docking ports, the Node Module would serve as a single permanent core of the future station with all other modules coming and going as their life span and mission required.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">\n        <a href="#cite_note-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">\n        <a href="#cite_note-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Uzlovoy will be launched after Nauka MLM; launch dates will be 2017 or later\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">\n        <a href="#cite_note-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <ul>\n       <li>\n        <a href="/wiki/Science-Power_Module-1" title="Science-Power Module-1">\n         Science and Power Module 1\n        </a>\n        (NEM-1) -
