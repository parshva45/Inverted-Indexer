</td>\n       </tr>\n      </table>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <b>\n        Arabian Sea\n       </b>\n       is a region of the northern\n       <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">\n        Indian Ocean\n       </a>\n       bounded on the north by\n       <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">\n        Pakistan\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">\n        Iran\n       </a>\n       , on the west by northeastern\n       <a href="/wiki/Somalia" title="Somalia">\n        Somalia\n       </a>\n       and the\n       <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula" title="Arabian Peninsula">\n        Arabian Peninsula\n       </a>\n       , and on the east by\n       <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">\n        India\n       </a>\n       . Historically the sea has been known by other names including the\n       <a href="/wiki/Erythraean_Sea" title="Erythraean Sea">\n        Erythraean Sea\n       </a>\n       and the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Persian_Sea" title="Persian Sea">\n        Persian Sea\n       </a>\n       . Its total area is 3,862,000\xc2\xa0km\n       <sup>\n        2\n       </sup>\n       (1,491,000\xc2\xa0sq\xc2\xa0mi) and its maximum depth is 4,652 metres (15,262\xc2\xa0ft). The\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Aden" title="Gulf of Aden">\n        Gulf of Aden\n       </a>\n       is in the southwest, connecting the Arabian Sea to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Red_Sea" title="Red Sea">\n        Red Sea\n       </a>\n       through the strait of\n       <a href="/wiki/Bab-el-Mandeb" title="Bab-el-Mandeb">\n        Bab-el-Mandeb\n       </a>\n       , and the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Oman" title="Gulf of Oman">\n        Gulf of Oman\n       </a>\n       is in the northwest, connecting it to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Persian_Gulf" title="Persian Gulf">\n        Persian Gulf\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The Arabian Sea has been crossed by important marine trade routes since the third or second millennium BCE. Major seaports include\n       <a href="/wiki/Jawaharlal_Nehru_Port" title="Jawaharlal Nehru Port">\n        Jawaharlal Nehru Port\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/Mumbai" title="Mumbai">\n        Mumbai\n       </a>\n       , the\n       <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Karachi" title="Port of Karachi">\n        Port of Karachi\n       </a>\n       and the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gwadar_Port" title="Gwadar Port">\n        Gwadar Port\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">\n        Pakistan\n       </a>\n       and the Port of Salalah in\n       <a href="/wiki/Oman" title="Oman">\n        Oman\n       </a>\n       . Other important ports include in India,\n       <a href="/wiki/Cochin_Port" title="Cochin Port">\n        Cochin Port\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Kandla" title="Kandla">\n        Kandla Port\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Mormugao" title="Mormugao">\n        Mormugao\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/Goa" title="Goa">\n        Goa\n       </a>\n       . The largest islands in the Arabian Sea include\n       <a href="/wiki/Socotra" title="Socotra">\n        Socotra\n       </a>\n       (Yemen),\n       <a href="/wiki/Masirah_Island" title="Masirah Island">\n        Masirah Island\n       </a>\n       (Oman),\n       <a href="/wiki/Astola_Island" title="Astola Island">\n        Astola Island\n       </a>\n       (Pakistan) and\n       <a href="/wiki/Andrott" title="Andrott">\n        Andrott\n       </a>\n       (India).\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="toc">\n       <div class="toctitle">\n        <h2>\n The history of the port is intertwined with that of the city of Karachi. Several ancient ports have been attributed in the area including "Krokola", "Morontobara" (Woman\'s Harbour) (mentioned by\n       <a href="/wiki/Nearchus" title="Nearchus">\n        Nearchus\n       </a>\n       ),\n       <a href="/wiki/Barbarikon" title="Barbarikon">\n        Barbarikon\n       </a>\n       (the\n       <a href="/wiki/Periplus_of_the_Erythraean_Sea" title="Periplus of the Erythraean Sea">\n        Periplus of the Erythraean Sea\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Debal" title="Debal">\n        Debal\n       </a>\n       (a city invaded and captured by the Muslim general\n       <a href="/wiki/Muhammad_bin_Qasim" title="Muhammad bin Qasim">\n        Muhammad bin Qasim\n       </a>\n       in 712 AD). There is a reference to the early existence of the port of Karachi in the "\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Umdah" title="Umdah">\n        Umdah\n       </a>\n       ", by the Arab navigator\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sulaiman_al_Mahri" title="Sulaiman al Mahri">\n        Sulaiman al Mahri\n       </a>\n       (AD 1511), who mentions "\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ras_al_Karazi" title="Ras al Karazi">\n        Ras al Karazi\n       </a>\n       " and "\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ras_Karashi" title="Ras Karashi">\n        Ras Karashi\n       </a>\n       " while describing a route along the coast from\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pasni_City" title="Pasni City">\n        Pasni\n       </a>\n       to Ras Karashi.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <a href="/wiki/Karachi" title="Karachi">\n        Karachi\n       </a>\n       is also mentioned in the sixteenth century\n       <a href="/wiki/Turkish_language" title="Turkish language">\n        Turkish\n       </a>\n       treatise\n       <a href="/wiki/Mirat_ul_Memalik" title="Mirat ul Memalik">\n        Mirat ul Memalik\n       </a>\n       (Mirror of Countries, 1557) by the\n       <a href="/wiki/Ottoman_Empire" title="Ottoman Empire">\n        Ottoman\n       </a>\n       captain\n       <a href="/wiki/Seydi_Ali_Reis" title="Seydi Ali Reis">\n        Seydi Ali Reis\n       </a>\n       , which is a compilation of sailing directions from the\n       <a href="/wiki/Portugal" title="Portugal">\n        Portuguese\n       </a>\n       island of\n       <a href="/wiki/Diu_Island" title="Diu Island">\n        Diu\n       </a>\n       to\n       <a href="/wiki/Strait_of_Hormuz" title="Strait of Hormuz">\n        Hormuz\n       </a>\n       in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Persian_Gulf" title="Persian Gulf">\n        Persian Gulf\n       </a>\n       . It warns sailors about whirlpools and advises them to seek safety in "\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Kaurashi" title="Kaurashi">\n        Kaurashi\n       </a>\n       " harbour if they found themselves drifting dangerously.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The gate facing the sea was called "Kharadar" (salt gate), and the gate facing the\n       <a href="/wiki/Lyari_River" title="Lyari River">\n        Lyari River\n       </a>\n       was called "\n       <a href="/wiki/Mithadar" title="Mithadar">\n        Mithadar\n       </a>\n       " (sweet gate). The modern neighbourhoods around the location of the gates are called\n       <a href="/wiki/Mithadar" title="Mithadar">\n        Mithadar\n       </a>\n       and Kharadar. Surrounded by mangrove swamps to the east, the sea to the southwest, and the\n       <a href="/wiki/Lyari_River" title="Lyari River">\n        Lyari River\n       </a>\n       to the north, the town was well defended and engaged in a profitable trade with Muscat and Bahrain.\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Karachi_port_and_harbour_aerial.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="399" data-file-width="600" height="146" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/4/46/Karachi_port_and_harbour_aerial.jpg/220px-Karachi_port_and_harbour_aerial.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/4/46/Karachi_port_and_harbour_aerial.jpg/330px-Karachi_port_and_harbour_aerial.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/4/46/Karachi_port_and_harbour_aerial.jpg/440px-Karachi_port_and_harbour_aerial.jpg 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Karachi_port_and_harbour_aerial.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         The\n         <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Karachi" title="Port of Karachi">\n          Port of Karachi\n         </a>\n         in Pakistan\'s largest city, Karachi, with some of the city\'s residential areas visible. The port is one of the busiest in the Arabian Sea\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <a href="/wiki/Gwadar_Port" title="Gwadar Port">\n        Gwadar Port\n       </a>\n       is a warm-water, deep-sea port situated at\n       <a href="/wiki/Gwadar" title="Gwadar">\n        Gwadar\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/Balochistan,_Pakistan" title="Balochistan, Pakistan">\n        Balochistan, Pakistan\n       </a>\n       at the apex of the Arabian Sea and at the entrance of the Persian Gulf, about 460\xc2\xa0km west of\n       <a href="/wiki/Karachi" title="Karachi">\n        Karachi\n       </a>\n       and approximately 75\xc2\xa0km (47\xc2\xa0mi) east of Pakistan\'s border with\n       <a href="/wiki/Iran" title="Iran">\n        Iran\n       </a>\n       . The port is located on the eastern bay of a natural hammerhead-shaped peninsula jutting out into the Arabian Sea from the coastline.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Salalah" title="Port of Salalah">\n        Port of Salalah\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/Salalah" title="Salalah">\n        Salalah\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Oman" title="Oman">\n        Oman\n       </a>\n       is also a major port in the area. From a modest start in 1997, the Omani container transhipment port has achieved consistent growth. It is a key container transhipment hub on the Arabian Sea and is often used as the first port of call for vessels whose crew have just been released from the clutches of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Piracy_in_Somalia" title="Piracy in Somalia">\n        Somali pirates\n       </a>\n       following ransom payments for withheld vessels and crew. The port also plays host as a supply base for the visiting warships that provide protective escorts for merchant shipping in the sea lanes. From that dual role has emerged another, one as an intelligence network\xc2\xa0\xe2\x80\x94 both military and civilian\xc2\xa0\xe2\x80\x94 to exchange information on possible pirate sightings and near misses. Also, the International Task Force often uses the port as a base. There is a significant number of warships of all nations coming in and out of the port, which makes it a very safe bubble. The main industries here are\n       <a href="/wiki/Fishing" title="Fishing">\n        fishing\n       </a>\n       and traditional textile manufacturing. Formerly, traditional ship building was important. The rugged terrain of the island and surrounding rough coastline has led to the appearance of many wrecked\n       <a href="/wiki/Dhow" title="Dhow">\n        dhows\n       </a>\n       on the beaches of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Island" title="Island">\n        island\n       </a>\n       , most of them well preserved by the salt water and intense heat. The ocean bottom environment surrounding Masirah is hostile as the majority of the area is covered in either sand or hard rock. Despite the poor quality ocean bottom, the area is very productive with marine fisheries, and any hard objects (barrels, engines) are immediately colonized by local fauna.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n
