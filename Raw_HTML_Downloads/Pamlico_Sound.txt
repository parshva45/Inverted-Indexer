It is part of a large, interconnected network of lagoon estuaries that includes Albemarle Sound,\n       <a href="/wiki/Currituck_Sound" title="Currituck Sound">\n        Currituck Sound\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Croatan_Sound" title="Croatan Sound">\n        Croatan Sound\n       </a>\n       , Pamlico Sound,\n       <a href="/wiki/Bogue_Sound" title="Bogue Sound">\n        Bogue Sound\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Core_Sound" title="Core Sound">\n        Core Sound\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Roanoke_Sound" title="Roanoke Sound">\n        Roanoke Sound\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uri_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-uri-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-outer_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-outer-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Together, these sounds, known as the Albemarle-Pamlico sound system, comprise the second largest estuary in the United States, covering over 3,000 sq. mi. of open water.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-:1-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       (\n       <a href="/wiki/Chesapeake_Bay" title="Chesapeake Bay">\n        Chesapeake Bay\n       </a>\n       is the largest.) The Albemarle-Pamlico Sound is one of nineteen great waters recognized by the America\'s Great Waters Coalition.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-4">\n <p>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Hydrology">\n        Hydrology\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pamlico_Sound&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Hydrology">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Pamlico Sound is connected to the\n       <a href="/wiki/North" title="North">\n        north\n       </a>\n       with\n       <a href="/wiki/Albemarle_Sound" title="Albemarle Sound">\n        Albemarle Sound\n       </a>\n       through\n       <a href="/wiki/Channel_(geography)" title="Channel (geography)">\n        passages\n       </a>\n       provided by the\n       <a href="/wiki/Roanoke_Sound" title="Roanoke Sound">\n        Roanoke Sound\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Croatan_Sound" title="Croatan Sound">\n        Croatan Sound\n       </a>\n       .\n       <a href="/wiki/Core_Sound" title="Core Sound">\n        Core Sound\n       </a>\n       is located at the Pamlico\'s narrow southern end.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uri_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-uri-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-outer_2-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-outer-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       It is fed by the\n       <a href="/wiki/Neuse_River" title="Neuse River">\n        Neuse\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Pamlico_River" title="Pamlico River">\n        Pamlico\n       </a>\n       rivers (the latter of which is the\n       <a href="/wiki/Estuary" title="Estuary">\n        estuary\n       </a>\n       of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Tar_River" title="Tar River">\n        Tar River\n       </a>\n       ) from the west and by\n       <a href="/wiki/Oregon_Inlet" title="Oregon Inlet">\n        Oregon Inlet\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Hatteras_Inlet" title="Hatteras Inlet">\n        Hatteras Inlet\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Ocracoke_Inlet" title="Ocracoke Inlet">\n        Ocracoke Inlet\n       </a>\n       , which also provide passage to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">\n        Atlantic Ocean\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-:0-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The\n       <a href="/wiki/Salinity" title="Salinity">\n        salinity\n       </a>\n       of the sound averages 20\n       <a href="/wiki/Parts-per_notation" title="Parts-per notation">\n        ppt\n       </a>\n       , compared to an average coastal salinity of 35 ppt in the Atlantic and 3 ppt in the Currituck Sound, which is located north of the Albemarle Sound.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:2_6-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-:2-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The sound and its ocean inlets are noted for wide expanses of shallow water and occasional\n       <a href="/wiki/Shoal" title="Shoal">\n        shoaling\n       </a>\n       , making the area hazardous for larger vessels. While the deepest hole of the estuary (26\') can be found in the Pamlico Sound,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:3_7-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-:3-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       depths generally range from 5 to 6 feet.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In addition, the shallow waters are susceptible to wind and barometric pressure-driven tidal fluctuations. This effect is amplified on the\n       <a href="/wiki/Tributary" title="Tributary">\n        tributary\n       </a>\n       rivers, where water levels can change by as much as two feet in three hours when winds are aligned with the rivers\' axes and are blowing strongly.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uri_1-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-uri-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-outer_2-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-outer-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="History_and_current_use">\n        History and current use\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pamlico_Sound&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History and current use">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       In March 1524, Italian Explorer\n       <a href="/wiki/Giovanni_da_Verrazzano" title="Giovanni da Verrazzano">\n        Giovanni da Verrazzano\n       </a>\n       mistook the sound for the\n       <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">\n        Pacific Ocean\n       </a>\n       because of its wide expanse and separation from the Atlantic Ocean by the Outer Banks barrier islands.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_5-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-:0-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The sound was named for the Pamlico Native American tribe that lived along the sound\'s mainland banks and who were referred to as the Pamouik by the\n       <a href="/wiki/Walter_Raleigh" title="Walter Raleigh">\n        Raleigh\n       </a>\n       expeditions (circa 1584).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Three locations of Pamlico Sound in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Outer_Banks" title="Outer Banks">\n        Outer Banks\n       </a>\n       between\n       <a href="/wiki/Cape_Hatteras" title="Cape Hatteras">\n        Cape Hatteras\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cape_Fear,_N.C." title="Cape Fear, N.C.">\n        Cape Fear\n       </a>\n       were once under serious consideration by the\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Atomic_Energy_Commission" title="United States Atomic Energy Commission">\n        United States Atomic Energy Commission\n       </a>\n       as an\n       <a href="/wiki/Nuclear_weapons_testing" title="Nuclear weapons testing">\n        atomic bomb test\n       </a>\n       site during the late 1940s and early 1950s.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Portions of Pamlico Sound are used as a bombing and training range for\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Camp_Lejeune" title="Camp Lejeune">\n        Camp Lejeune\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In 1987, Congress declared the Albemarle-Pamlico Sound an "estuary of national significance."\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_3-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-:1-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       For vacationers to the Outer Banks, the Pamlico Sound is a "watersports playground" providing opportunities for fishing and crabbing, boating, kayaking, sailing, windsurfing, kiteboarding, parasailing, paddleboarding, and more.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:0_5-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-:0-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In 2012, the economic impact of tourism to the Albemarle-Pamlico Sound system exceeded $1.3 billion.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:1_3-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-:1-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The sound also supports local commercial fishing, crabbing, shrimping, clamming, and oystering. 90% of North Carolina\'s commercial fishing catches are attributed to the Pamlico Sound, generating almost $100 million per year.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Wildlife">\n        Wildlife\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Pamlico_Sound&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Wildlife">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Along the coastal areas are numerous\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Waterfowl" title="Waterfowl">\n        waterfowl\n       </a>\n       nesting sites, including\n       <a href="/wiki/Pea_Island_National_Wildlife_Refuge" title="Pea Island National Wildlife Refuge">\n        Pea Island National Wildlife Refuge\n       </a>\n       on the Outer Banks, and\n       <a href="/wiki/Swanquarter_National_Wildlife_Refuge" title="Swanquarter National Wildlife Refuge">\n        Swanquarter National Wildlife Refuge\n       </a>\n       on the mainland.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uri_1-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-uri-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-outer_2-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-outer-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <a href="#cite_note-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       with occasional visits by\n       <a href="/wiki/Pinniped" title="Pinniped">\n        seals\n       </a>\n       such as\n       <a href="/wiki/Harp_seal" title="Harp seal">\n        harp seal\n       </a>\n       in early January and February. Many other\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cetacean" title="Cetacean">\n        cetaceans\n       </a>\n       including rare species such as\n       <a href="/wiki/Fin_whale" title="Fin whale">\n        fin whales\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Cuvier%27s_beaked_whale" title="Cuvier\'s beaked whale">\n        Cuvier\'s beaked whales\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orca" title="Orca">\n        orcas\n       </a>\n       are present off Outer Banks and Cape Hatteras.\n       <a href="/wiki/Whale" title="Whale">\n        Whales\n       </a>\n       such as\n       <a href="/wiki/Gray_whale" title="Gray whale">\n        Atlantic gray\n       </a>\n       (now extirpated),\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">\n        <a href="#cite_note-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <a href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_right_whale" title="North Atlantic right whale">\n        North Atlantic right\n       </a>\n       (critically endangerend), and\n       <a href="/wiki/Humpback_whale" title="Humpback whale">\n        North Atlantic humpback\n       </a>\n       were historically common. Endangered species such as\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Leatherback_turtle" title="Leatherback turtle">\n        leatherback turtles\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">\n        <a href="#cite_note-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <a href="/wiki/Great_white_shark" title="Great white shark">\n        great white sharks\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Basking_shark" title="Basking shark">\n        basking sharks\n       </a>\n       are also known to visit the sound as well.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:2_6-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-:2-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The sound also sports a variety of fish populations including\n       <a href="/wiki/Red_drum" title="Red drum">\n        red drum\n       </a>\n       , speckled trout,\n       <a href="/wiki/Flounder" title="Flounder">\n        flounder\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Striped_bass" title="Striped bass">\n        striped bass\n       </a>\n       (known as rockfish by local populations),\n       <a href="/wiki/Sciaenidae" title="Sciaenidae">\n        croaker\n       </a>\n       , spot,\n       <a href="/wiki/Pompano" title="Pompano">\n        pompano\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/King_mackerel" title="King mackerel">\n        kingfish\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Bluefish" title="Bluefish">\n        bluefish\n       </a>\n       . clams\n       </a>\n       are healthy.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-:3_7-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-:3-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n
