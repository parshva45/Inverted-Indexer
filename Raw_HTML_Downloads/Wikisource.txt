</th>\n        <td class="category">\n         Online\n        </td>\n       </tr>\n      </table>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        Wikisource\n       </b>\n       is an online\n       <a href="/wiki/Digital_library" title="Digital library">\n        digital library\n       </a>\n       of\n       <a href="/wiki/Free_content" title="Free content">\n        free content\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Source_text" title="Source text">\n        textual sources\n       </a>\n       on a\n       <a href="/wiki/Wiki" title="Wiki">\n        wiki\n       </a>\n       , operated by the\n       <a href="/wiki/Wikimedia_Foundation" title="Wikimedia Foundation">\n        Wikimedia Foundation\n       </a>\n       . Wikisource is the name of the project as a whole and the name for each instance of that project (each instance usually representing a different language); multiple Wikisources make up the overall project of Wikisource. The project\'s aims are to host all forms of free text, in many languages, and translations. Originally conceived as an archive to store useful or important historical texts (its first text was the\n       <span class="plainlinks">\n        <a class="external text" href="//fr.wikisource.org/w/index.php?title=D\xc3\xa9claration_universelle_des_Droits_de_l\xe2\x80\x99Homme&amp;oldid=1338">\n         <i>\n          D\xc3\xa9claration universelle des Droits de l\'Homme\n         </i>\n        </a>\n       </span>\n       ), it has expanded to become a general-content library. The project officially began in November 24, 2003 under the name\n       <b>\n        Project Sourceberg\n       </b>\n       , a play on the famous\n       <a href="/wiki/Project_Gutenberg" title="Project Gutenberg">\n        Project Gutenberg\n       </a>\n       . The name Wikisource was adopted later that year and it received its own\n       <a href="/wiki/Domain_name" title="Domain name">\n        domain name\n       </a>\n       seven months later. The project has come under criticism for lack of reliability but it is also cited by organisations such as the\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Archives_and_Records_Administration" title="National Archives and Records Administration">\n        National Archives and Records Administration\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NationalArchivesMention_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NationalArchivesMention-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The project holds works that are either in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Public_domain" title="Public domain">\n        public domain\n       </a>\n       or\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Freely_licensed" title="Freely licensed">\n        freely licensed\n       </a>\n       ; professionally published works or historical source documents, not\n       <a href="/wiki/Vanity_press" title="Vanity press">\n        vanity products\n       </a>\n       ; and are verifiable. Verification was initially made offline, or by trusting the reliability of other digital libraries. Now works are supported by online scans via the ProofreadPage extension, which ensures the reliability and accuracy of the project\'s texts.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Some individual Wikisources, each representing a specific language, now only allow works backed up with scans. While the bulk of its collection are texts, Wikisource as a whole hosts other media, from comics to film to\n       <a href="/wiki/Audiobook" title="Audiobook">\n        audio books\n       </a>\n       . Some Wikisources allow user-generated annotations, subject to the specific policies of the Wikisource in question.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="toc">\n       <div         <span class="toctext">\n           External links\n          </span>\n         </a>\n        </li>\n       </ul>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">\n        History\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikisource&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Wikisource\'s early (2003\xe2\x80\x932005) history included several changes of name and location (\n       <a href="/wiki/URL" title="URL">\n        URL\n       </a>\n       ), and the move to language subdomains in 2005.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Early_history">\n        Early history\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikisource&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early history">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       The original concept for Wikisource was as storage for useful or important historical texts. These texts were intended to support\n       <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia" title="Wikipedia">\n        Wikipedia\n       </a>\n       articles, by providing primary evidence and original source texts, and as an archive in its own right. The collection was initially focused on important historical and cultural material, distinguishing it from other digital archives such as Project Gutenberg.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ayersmatthewsyates_2-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ayersmatthewsyates-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:137px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Wikisourcelogo.jpg">\n         <img alt="Composite photograph showing an iceberg both above and below the waterline." class="thumbimage" data-file-height="143" data-file-width="135" height="143" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/0/01/Wikisourcelogo.jpg" width="135"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Wikisourcelogo.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         The original Wikisource logo\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The project was originally called Project Sourceberg during its planning stages (a play on words for\n       <a href="/wiki/Project_Gutenberg" title="Project Gutenberg">\n        Project Gutenberg\n       </a>\n       ).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ayersmatthewsyates_2-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ayersmatthewsyates-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In 2001, there was a dispute on Wikipedia regarding the addition of primary source material, leading to\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Edit_war" title="Edit war">\n        edit wars\n       </a>\n       over their inclusion or deletion. Project Sourceberg was suggested as a solution to this. In describing the proposed project, user The Cunctator said, "It would be to Project Gutenberg what Wikipedia is to\n       <a href="/wiki/Nupedia" title="Nupedia">\n        Nupedia\n       </a>\n       ,"\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cunctator_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Cunctator-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       soon clarifying the statement with "we don\'t want to try to duplicate Project Gutenberg\'s efforts; rather, we want to complement them. Perhaps Project Sourceberg can mainly work as an interface for easily linking from Wikipedia to a Project Gutenberg file, and as an interface for people to easily submit new work to PG."\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Initial comments were sceptical, with\n       <a href="/wiki/Larry_Sanger" title="Larry Sanger">\n        Larry Sanger\n       </a>\n       questioning the need for the project, writing "The hard question, I guess, is why we are reinventing the wheel, when Project Gutenberg already exists? We\'d want to complement Project Gutenberg--how, exactly?",\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Jimmy_Wales" title="Jimmy Wales">\n        Jimmy Wales\n       </a>\n       adding "like Larry, I\'m interested that we think it over to see what we can add to Project Gutenberg. It seems unlikely that primary sources should in general be editable by anyone -- I mean, Shakespeare is Shakespeare, unlike our commentary on his work, which is whatever we want it to be."\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The project began its activity at ps.wikipedia.org. The contributors understood the "PS" subdomain to mean either "primary sources" or Project Sourceberg.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cunctator_4-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Cunctator-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       However, this resulted in Project Sourceberg occupying the subdomain of the\n       <a class="extiw" href="https://ps.wikipedia.org/wiki/" title="ps:">\n        Pashto Wikipedia\n       </a>\n       (the\n       <a href="/wiki/ISO_639-1" title="ISO 639-1">\n        ISO language code\n       </a>\n       of the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pashto_language" title="Pashto language">\n        Pashto language\n       </a>\n       is "ps").\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Project Sourceberg officially launched on November 24, 2003 when it received its own temporary URL, at sources.wikipedia.org, and all texts and discussions hosted on ps.wikipedia.org were moved to the temporary address. A vote on the project\'s name changed it to\n       <b>\n        Wikisource\n       </b>\n       on December 6, 2003. Despite the change in name, the project did not move to its permanent URL (at\n       <a class="external free" href="http://wikisource.org/">\n        http://wikisource.org/\n       </a>\n       ) until July 23, 2004.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div style="clear:both;">\n      </div>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Logo_and_slogan">\n        Logo and slogan\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikisource&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Logo and slogan">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Since Wikisource was initially called "Project Sourceberg", its first logo was a picture of an\n       <a href="/wiki/Iceberg" title="Iceberg">\n        iceberg\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ayersmatthewsyates_2-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ayersmatthewsyates-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Two votes conducted to choose a successor were inconclusive, and the original logo remained until 2006. Finally, for both legal and technical reasons \xe2\x80\x93 because the picture\'s license was inappropriate for a Wikimedia Foundation logo and because a photo cannot scale properly \xe2\x80\x93 a stylized vector iceberg inspired by the original picture was mandated to serve as the project\'s logo.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The first prominent use of Wikisource\'s slogan \xe2\x80\x94\n       <i>\n        The Free Library\n       </i>\n       \xe2\x80\x94 was at the project\'s\n       <a class="extiw" href="https://wikisource.org/wiki/Main_Page" title="oldwikisource:Main Page">\n        multilingual portal\n       </a>\n       , when it was redesigned based upon the Wikipedia portal on August 27, 2005, (historical version).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       As in the Wikipedia portal the Wikisource slogan appears around the logo in the project\'s ten largest languages.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Clicking on the portal\'s central images (the iceberg logo in the center and the "Wikisource" heading at the top of the page) links to a\n       <i>\n        <a class="extiw" href="https://wikisource.org/wiki/Wikisource" title="oldwikisource:Wikisource">\n         list of translations\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       for\n       <i>\n        Wikisource\n       </i>\n       and\n       <i>\n        The Free Library\n       </i>\n       in 60 languages.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Tools_built">\n        Tools built\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikisource&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit This displays pages of scanned works side-by-side with the text relating to that page, allowing the text to be proofread and its accuracy later verified independently by any other editor.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Springer2010_10-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Springer2010-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Once a book, or other text, has been scanned, the raw images can be modified with\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Image_processing" title="Image processing">\n        image processing\n       </a>\n       software to correct for page rotations and other problems. The retouched images can then be converted into a\n       <a href="/wiki/Portable_Document_Format" title="Portable Document Format">\n        PDF\n       </a>\n       or\n       <a href="/wiki/DjVu" title="DjVu">\n        DjVu\n       </a>\n       file and uploaded to either Wikisource or\n       <a href="/wiki/Wikimedia_Commons" title="Wikimedia Commons">\n        Wikimedia Commons\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Springer2010_10-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Springer2010-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       This system assists editors in ensuring the accuracy of texts on Wikisource. The original page scans of completed works remain available to any user so that errors may be corrected later and readers may check texts against the originals. ProofreadPage also allows greater participation, since access to a physical copy of the original work is not necessary to be able to contribute to the project once images have been uploaded. Thus it enhances the project\'s commitment to the Wikimedia principle that anyone can contribute.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       ThomasV built other tools as well: when the choice of whether publishing annotations or not was discussed, he made a gadget to offer the choice between texts alone or annotated texts. When the choice of modernizing or not the texts was discussed, he made another gadget to modernize the original text only when it was wished, so that it could be decided then that the texts themselves would be the original ones.\n       <br/>\n      </p>\n      <div class="mw-collapsible mw-collapsed" style="width: auto; background-color: #F3F9FF;">\n       <dl>\n        <dd>\n      </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Within two weeks of the project\'s official start at sources.wikipedia.org, over 1,000 pages had been created, with approximately 200 of these being designated as actual articles. On January 4, 2004, Wikisource welcomed its 100th registered user. In early July, 2004 the number of articles exceeded 2,400, and more than 500 users had registered. On April 30, 2005, there were 2667 registered users (including 18 administrators) and almost 19,000 articles. The project passed its 96,000th edit that same day.\n       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">\n        [\n        <i>\n         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">\n          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2011)">\n           citation needed\n          </span>\n         </a>\n        </i>\n        ]\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       On November 27, 2005, the English Wikisource passed 20,000 text-units in its third month of existence, already holding more texts than did the entire project in April (before the move to language subdomains). On February 14, 2008, the English Wikisource passed 100,000 text-units with\n       <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Six_Months_at_the_White_House/LXXIV" title="wikisource:Six Months at the White House/LXXIV">\n        Chapter LXXIV\n       </a>\n       of\n       <i>\n        <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Six_Months_at_the_White_House" title="wikisource:Six Months at the White House">\n         Six Months at the White House\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       , a memoir by painter\n       <a href="/wiki/Francis_Bicknell_Carpenter" title="Francis Bicknell Carpenter">\n        Francis Bicknell Carpenter\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In November, 2011, 250,000 text-units milestone was passed. But counting was difficult because what a text-unit is, could not be clearly defined.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       On May 10, 2006, the\n       <span class="plainlinks">\n        <a class="external text" href="//fr.wikisource.org/w/index.php?title=Portail:Philosophie&amp;oldid=91377">\n         first Wikisource Portal\n        </a>\n       </span>\n       was created.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Library_contents">\n        Library contents\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikisource&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Library contents">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Wikisource_Inclusion_Venn_Diagram.svg">\n         <img alt=\'A Venn diagram of the inclusion criteria for works to be added to Wikisource. The areas where just two overlap are shown in yellow (except the Sourced-Published overlap, which remains blank)\' class="thumbimage" data-file-height="625" data-file-width="660" height="208" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/Wikisource_Inclusion_Venn_Diagram.svg/220px-Wikisource_Inclusion_Venn_Diagram.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/Wikisource_Inclusion_Venn_Diagram.svg/330px-Wikisource_Inclusion_Venn_Diagram.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/Wikisource_Inclusion_Venn_Diagram.svg/440px-Wikisource_Inclusion_Venn_Diagram.svg.png 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Wikisource_Inclusion_Venn_Diagram.svg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Wikisource inclusion criteria expressed as a\n         <a href="/wiki/Venn_diagram" title="Venn diagram">\n          Venn diagram\n         </a>\n         . Green indicates the best possible case, where the work satisfies all three primary requirements. Yellow indicates acceptable but not ideal cases.\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Wikisource collects and stores in\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Digitizing" title="Digitizing">\n        digital format\n       </a>\n       previously published texts; including novels, non-fiction works, letters, speeches, constitutional and historical documents, laws and a range of other documents. All texts collected are either free of copyright or released under the\n       <a href="/wiki/Creative_Commons" title="Creative Commons">\n        Creative Commons Attribution/Share-Alike License\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ayersmatthewsyates_2-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ayersmatthewsyates-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Texts in all languages are welcome, as are translations. In addition to texts, Wikisource hosts material such as comics, films, recordings and spoken-word works.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ayersmatthewsyates_2-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ayersmatthewsyates-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       All texts held by Wikisource must have been previously published; the project does not host "\n       <a href="/wiki/Vanity_press" title="Vanity press">\n        vanity press\n       </a>\n       " books or documents produced by its contributors.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ayersmatthewsyates_2-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ayersmatthewsyates-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">\n        <a href="#cite_note-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">\n        <a href="#cite_note-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">\n        <a href="#cite_note-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Boot_17-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Boot-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       A scanned source is preferred on many Wikisources and required on some. Most Wikisources will, however, accept works transcribed from offline sources or acquired from\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_digital_library_projects" title="List of digital library projects">\n        other digital libraries\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ayersmatthewsyates_2-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ayersmatthewsyates-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The requirement for prior publication can also be waived in a small number of cases if the work is a source document of notable historical importance. The legal requirement for works to be licensed or free of copyright remains constant.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The only original pieces accepted by Wikisource are annotations and translations.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">\n        <a href="#cite_note-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Wikisource, and its sister project\n       <a href="/wiki/Wikibooks" title="Wikibooks">\n        Wikibooks\n       </a>\n       , has the capacity for\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Annotated_novel" title="Annotated novel">\n        annotated editions\n       </a>\n       of texts. On Wikisource, the annotations are supplementary to the original text, which remains the primary objective of the project. By contrast, on Wikibooks the annotations are primary, with the original text as only a reference or supplement, if present at all.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Boot_17-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Boot-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Annotated editions are more popular on the German Wikisource.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Boot_17-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Boot-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The project also accommodates translations of texts provided by its users. A significant translation on the English Wikisource is the\n       <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Wikisource:WikiProject_Wiki_Bible" title="wikisource:Wikisource:WikiProject Wiki Bible">\n        Wiki Bible\n       </a>\n       project, intended to create a new, "laissez-faire translation" of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Bible" title="The Bible">\n        The Bible\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-newsweek_19-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-newsweek-19">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Structure">\n        Structure\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikisource&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Structure">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Language_subdomains">\n        Language subdomains\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikisource&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Language subdomains">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       A separate\n       <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/he:%D7%A2%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%93_%D7%A8%D7%90%D7%A9%D7%99" title="s:he:\xd7\xa2\xd7\x9e\xd7\x95\xd7\x93 \xd7\xa8\xd7\x90\xd7\xa9\xd7\x99">\n        Hebrew version\n       </a>\n       of Wikisource (\n       <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/he:%D7%A2%D7%9E%D7%95%D7%93_%D7%A8%D7%90%D7%A9%D7%99" title="s:he:\xd7\xa2\xd7\x9e\xd7\x95\xd7\x93 \xd7\xa8\xd7\x90\xd7\xa9\xd7\x99">\n        he.wikisource.org\n       </a>\n       ) was created in August 2004. The need for a language-specific\n       <a href="/wiki/Hebrew_language" title="Hebrew language">\n        Hebrew\n       </a>\n       website derived from the difficulty of typing and editing Hebrew texts in a\n       <a href="/wiki/Bi-directional_text" title="Bi-directional text">\n        left-to-right\n       </a>\n       environment (Hebrew is written right-to-left). In the ensuing months, contributors in other languages including\n       <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">\n        German\n       </a>\n       requested their own wikis, but a December vote on the creation of separate language domains was inconclusive. Finally, a\n       <a class="extiw" href="https://wikisource.org/wiki/Wikisource:New_vote_on_language_subdomains" title="oldwikisource:Wikisource:New vote on language subdomains">\n        second vote\n       </a>\n       that ended May 12, 2005, supported the adoption of separate language subdomains at Wikisource by a large margin, allowing each language to host its texts on its own wiki.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       An initial wave of 14 languages was set up by\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brion_Vibber" title="Brion Vibber">\n        Brion Vibber\n       </a>\n       on August 23, 2005.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">\n        <a href="#cite_note-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The new languages did not include English, but the code en: was temporarily set to redirect to the main website (\n       <a class="extiw" href="https://wikisource.org/wiki/" title="oldwikisource:">\n        wikisource.org\n       </a>\n       ).\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       At this point the Wikisource community, through a mass project of manually sorting thousands of pages and categories by language, prepared for a second wave of page imports to local wikis. On September 11, 2005, the wikisource.org wiki was reconfigured to enable the\n       <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/" title="s:">\n        English version\n       </a>\n       , along with 8 other languages that were created early that morning and late the night before.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">\n        <a href="#cite_note-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Three more languages were created on March 29, 2006,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">\n        <a href="#cite_note-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and then another large wave of 14 language domains was created on June 2, 2006.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">\n        <a href="#cite_note-23">\n         [23]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Currently, there are individual subdomains for Wikisources in more than 60 languages,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">\n        <a href="#cite_note-24">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       besides the additional languages hosted at\n       <a class="extiw" href="https://wikisource.org/wiki/" title="oldwikisource:">\n        wikisource.org\n       </a>\n       , which serves as an incubator or a home for languages without their own subdomains (31 languages are currently\n       <a class="extiw" href="https://wikisource.org/wiki/Category:Main_Pages" title="oldwikisource:Category:Main Pages">\n        hosted locally\n       </a>\n       ).\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="wikisource.org">\n        wikisource.org\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Wikisource&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: wikisource.org">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       During the move to language subdomains, the community requested that the main\n       <a class="extiw" href="https://wikisource.org/wiki/" title="oldwikisource:">\n        wikisource.org\n       </a>\n       website remain a functioning wiki, in order to serve three purposes:\n      </p>\n      <ol>\n       <li>\n        <i>\n         To be a multilingual coordination site for the entire Wikisource project in all languages.\n        </i>\n        In practice, use of the website for multilingual coordination has not been heavy since the conversion to language domains. Nevertheless, there is some policy activity at the\n        <a class="extiw" href="https://wikisource.org/wiki/Wikisource:Scriptorium" title="oldwikisource:Wikisource:Scriptorium">\n         Scriptorium\n        </a>\n        , and multilingual updates for news and language milestones at pages such as\n        <a class="extiw" href="https://wikisource.org/wiki/Wikisource:2007" title="oldwikisource:Wikisource:2007">\n         Wikisource:2007\n        </a>\n        .\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        <i>\n         To be a home for texts in languages without their own subdomains, each with its own local main page for self-organization.\n        </i>\n        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">\n         <a href="#cite_note-25">\n          [25]\n         </a>\n        </sup>\n        As a language incubator, the wiki currently provides a home for over 30 languages that do not yet have their own language subdomains. Some of these are very active, and have built libraries with hundreds of texts (such as Esperanto and Volapuk), and one with thousands (Hindi).\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        <i>\n         To provide direct, ongoing support by a local wiki community for a dynamic multilingual portal at its Main Page, for users who go to\n         <a class="extiw" href="https://wikisource.org/wiki/" title="oldwikisource:">\n          http://wikisource.org\n         </a>\n         .\n        </i>\n        The current\n        <a class="extiw" href="https://wikisource.org/wiki/" title="oldwikisource:">\n         Main Page portal\n        </a>\n        was created on August 26, 2005, by\n        <a class="extiw" href="https://wikisource.org/wiki/User:ThomasV" title="oldwikisource:User:ThomasV">\n         ThomasV\n        </a>\n        , who based it upon the Wikipedia portal.\n       </li>\n      </ol>\n      <p>\n       The idea of a project-specific coordination wiki, first realized at Wikisource, also took hold in another Wikimedia project, namely at\n       <a href="/wiki/Wikiversity" title="Wikiversity">\n        Wikiversity\n       </a>\n       \'s\n       <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wikiversity.org/wiki/Beta" title="v:Beta">\n        Beta Wiki\n       </a>\n       . Like wikisource.org, it serves Wikiversity coordination in all languages, and as a language incubator. But unlike Wikisource, its\n       <a class="extiw" href="https://en.wikiversity.org/wiki/Beta:Main_Page" title="v:Beta:Main Page">\n        Main Page\n       </a>\n       does not serve as its multilingual portal\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26">\n        <a href="#cite_note-26">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       (which is not a wiki page).\n      </p>\n
