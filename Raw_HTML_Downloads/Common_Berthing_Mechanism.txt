CBM\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <b>\n        Common Berthing Mechanism\n       </b>\n       (CBM) is a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spacecraft_docking_and_berthing_mechanism" title="Spacecraft docking and berthing mechanism">\n        berthing mechanism\n       </a>\n       primarily used to connect pressurized elements within the\n       <a href="/wiki/US_Orbital_Segment" title="US Orbital Segment">\n        US Orbital Segment\n       </a>\n       of the\n       <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">\n        International Space Station\n       </a>\n       . It roughly corresponds to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Androgynous_Peripheral_Attach_System#APAS-95" title="Androgynous Peripheral Attach System">\n        docking ports\n       </a>\n       that are used to join such elements within the\n       <a href="/wiki/Russian_Orbital_Segment" title="Russian Orbital Segment">\n        Russian Orbital Segment\n       </a>\n       . It was developed by\n       <a href="/wiki/Boeing" title="Boeing">\n        Boeing\n       </a>\n       at the\n       <a href="/wiki/Marshall_Space_Flight_Center" title="Marshall Space Flight Center">\n        Marshall Space Flight Center\n       </a>\n       (MSFC) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Huntsville,_Alabama" title="Huntsville, Alabama">\n        Huntsville, Alabama\n       </a>\n       under contract NAS15-10000 from the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Aeronautics_and_Space_Administration" title="National Aeronautics and Space Administration">\n        National Aeronautics and Space Administration\n       </a>\n       (NASA).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cbm-tech_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-cbm-tech-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The CBM system is a combination of structural, mechanical and control components that perform the capture, closure, and passive structural connection between two pressurized modules. The total CBM system is composed of two distinct sets of equipment: the active and passive halves. The active half [ACBM] supports the mechanical and control elements which effect the actual capture and closure functions of the berthing operations. The passive half [PCBM] contains the inert elements required to complete the berthing and closure action. It also contains the seals effecting pressurized capability at the Module/Module interface.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The ACBM was developed in response to specification S683-29902 (\n       <a href="/wiki/Commercial_and_Government_Entity_code" title="Commercial and Government Entity code">\n        CAGE\n       </a>\n       3A768). The PCBM was developed under specification S683-28943 (CAGE 3A768). According to the specifications, the CBM provides a Module/Module clear passage diameter of 50"\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       , which is large enough to accommodate a standard hatch and allow\n       <a href="/wiki/International_Standard_Payload_Rack" title="International Standard Payload Rack">\n        International Standard Payload Racks\n       </a>\n       to be passed between modules. Using such racks, payloads can be pre-configured and transported to the station aboard a variety of logistics modules. Once the hatches have been opened and the Module/Module vestibule configured, electrical, data, and fluid lines can be manually connected by the crew in a "shirt-sleeve" environment.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In addition to Module/Module attachment locations, the CBM is also used as a structural/mechanical/utility interface (e.g., for the base of the Z1 truss) and for docking adapters at various locations.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The first modules in space using the CBM were\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Unity_(ISS_module)" title="Unity (ISS module)">\n         Unity\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       and the\n       <a href="/wiki/Pressurized_Mating_Adapter" title="Pressurized Mating Adapter">\n        Pressurized Mating Adapters\n       </a>\n       <i>\n        PMA-1\n       </i>\n       and\n       <i>\n        PMA-2\n       </i>\n       , with all three being launched attached together aboard\n       <i>\n        Endeavour\n       </i>\n       . The first berthing in space using the CBM was the connecting of the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Integrated_truss_structure#Z1_truss" title="Integrated truss structure">\n        Z1 truss\n       </a>\n       to\n       <i>\n        Unity\n       </i>\n       on the International Space Station.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cbm-tech_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-cbm-tech-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The CBM has been used as a berthing mechanism for the\n       <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_program" title="Space Shuttle program">\n        NSTS\n       </a>\n       -transported\n       <a href="/wiki/Multi-Purpose_Logistics_Module" title="Multi-Purpose Logistics Module">\n        Multi-Purpose Logistics Modules\n       </a>\n       and the unmanned Japanese\n       <a href="/wiki/H-II_Transfer_Vehicle" title="H-II Transfer Vehicle">\n        H-II Transfer Vehicle\n       </a>\n       (HTV),\n       <a href="/wiki/SpaceX_Dragon" title="SpaceX Dragon">\n        SpaceX Dragon\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Cygnus_(spacecraft)" title="Cygnus (spacecraft)">\n        Orbital Sciences\' Cygnus\n       </a>\n       spacecraft. These spacecraft feature a PCBM, and are berthed to one of the station\'s open ACBMs using the station\'s robot arm.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Each CBM compatible module that connects to previously launched modules requires a PCBM to connect to an ACBM.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cbm-tech_1-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-cbm-tech-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The three ISS connecting nodes feature four radial ACBM ports.\n       <i>\n        Unity\n       </i>\n       features two ACBM on the axial side, while\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Harmony_(ISS_module)" title="Harmony (ISS module)">\n         Harmony\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       and\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Tranquility_(ISS_module)" title="Tranquility (ISS module)">\n         Tranquility\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       feature a PCBM on one axial side and an ACBM on the other axial side.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-node2-design_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-node2-design-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-node3-design_6-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-node3-design-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Destiny_(ISS_module)" title="Destiny (ISS module)">\n         Destiny\n        </a>\n        \'\n       </i>\n       laboratory and the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Japanese_Pressurised_Module" title="Japanese Pressurised Module">\n        Japanese Pressurised Module\n       </a>\n       both feature one PCBM and one ACBM.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sts-98-presskit_7-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-sts-98-presskit-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pm-cbm_8-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-pm-cbm-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Gallery">\n        Gallery\n
