</tr>\n       <tr style="display:none">\n        <td colspan="2">\n        </td>\n       </tr>\n      </table>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        Hurricane Beulah\n       </b>\n       was the second tropical storm, second hurricane, and only major hurricane during the\n       <a href="/wiki/1967_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1967 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1967 Atlantic hurricane season\n       </a>\n       . It tracked through the Caribbean, struck the Yucat\xc3\xa1n peninsula of Mexico as a major hurricane, and moved west-northwest into the Gulf of Mexico, briefly gaining\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_scales" title="Tropical cyclone scales">\n        Category 5\n       </a>\n       intensity. It was the strongest hurricane during the 1967\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="Atlantic hurricane season">\n        Atlantic hurricane season\n       </a>\n       . The hurricane made landfall just north of the mouth of the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rio_Grande_River" title="Rio Grande River">\n        Rio Grande River\n       </a>\n       as a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SSHS#Category_3" title="SSHS">\n        Category\xc2\xa03\n       </a>\n       . It spawned 115\n       <a href="/wiki/Tornado" title="Tornado">\n        tornadoes\n       </a>\n       across\n       <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">\n        Texas\n       </a>\n       , which established a new record for the highest amount of tornadoes produced by a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        tropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       . Due to its slow movement over Texas, Beulah led to significant flooding. At the time, Beulah ranked as the second-costliest hurricane on record, having left roughly $1\xc2\xa0billion (1967\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">\n        USD\n       </a>\n       ) in damage. Only\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Betsy" title="Hurricane Betsy">\n        Hurricane Betsy\n       </a>\n       two years prior had caused such considerable losses. Throughout its path, at least 59 people were killed.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="toc">\n       <div class="toctitle">\n        <h2>\n         Contents\n <p>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Meteorological_history">\n        Meteorological history\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Beulah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">\n style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Beulah_1967_track.png">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" height="136" class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Beulah_1967_track.png" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Map plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the\n         <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">\n          Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale\n         </a>\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       On August\xc2\xa022, 1967, an\n       <a href="/wiki/Television_Infrared_Observation_Satellite" title="Television Infrared Observation Satellite">\n        ESSA-5\n       </a>\n       satellite image depicted an area of enhanced\n       <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_convection" title="Atmospheric convection">\n        convection\n       </a>\n       \xe2\x80\x94shower and thunderstorm activity\xe2\x80\x94east of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Western_High_Plateau" title="Western High Plateau">\n        Western High Plateau\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/Cameroon" title="Cameroon">\n        Cameroon\n       </a>\n       over central Africa. Reaching the western slopes of the mountains two days later, the\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">\n        tropical wave\n       </a>\n       became more coherent with clouds condensing along its axis. As it moved over west Africa,\n       <a href="/wiki/Cyclonic_rotation" title="Cyclonic rotation">\n        cyclonic rotation\n       </a>\n       became apparent about 2,000\xc2\xa0ft (610\xc2\xa0m) above the surface. A research paper published in 1969 refers to the disturbance as a depression as it neared the west coast of Africa;\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disturbancepaper_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-disturbancepaper-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       however, this significantly differs from the official\n       <a href="/wiki/HURDAT" title="HURDAT">\n        Atlantic hurricane database\n       </a>\n       which does not mention the system at that time,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       as a surface circulation likely did not exist. Regardless, the system emerged over the Atlantic Ocean around\n       <a href="/wiki/12th_parallel_north" title="12th parallel north">\n        12\xc2\xb0N\n       </a>\n       on August\xc2\xa028, as represented by\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barometric_pressure" title="Barometric pressure">\n        barometric pressure\n       </a>\n       falls in\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Dakar,_Senegal" title="Dakar, Senegal">\n        Dakar, Senegal\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disturbancepaper_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-disturbancepaper-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Once over water, the system interacted with the\n       <a href="/wiki/Intertropical_Convergence_Zone" title="Intertropical Convergence Zone">\n        Intertropical Convergence Zone\n       </a>\n       and continued westward along an undulating path with no further organization. It was not until a\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Navy" title="United States Navy">\n        United States Navy\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Weather_reconnaissance" title="Weather reconnaissance">\n        weather reconnaissance\n       </a>\n       plane flew into the disturbance on September\xc2\xa04, while it was located east of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">\n        Lesser Antilles\n       </a>\n       , that signs of\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclogenesis" title="Tropical cyclogenesis">\n        development\n       </a>\n       were apparent. Corresponding observations from ships in the region on September\xc2\xa05 confirmed the existence of a 1010\n       <a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">\n        mbar\n       </a>\n       (\n       <a href="/wiki/Pascal_(unit)" title="Pascal (unit)">\n        hPa\n       </a>\n       ; 28.23\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Inches_of_Mercury" title="Inches of Mercury">\n        inHg\n       </a>\n       )\n       <a href="/wiki/Low-pressure_area" title="Low-pressure area">\n        low-pressure area\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In light of this, the disturbance was classified as a tropical depression at 12:00\n       <a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">\n        UTC\n       </a>\n       that day, with its center situated roughly 175\xc2\xa0mi (285\xc2\xa0km) east-northeast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Barbados" title="Barbados">\n        Barbados\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_2-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       A slow-moving system, the depression steadily organized as it approached the Lesser Antilles. Observations from aircraft reconnaissance indicated that the system attained gale-force winds by 12:00\xc2\xa0UTC on September\xc2\xa07, resulting in its upgrade to a tropical storm. It was also\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_naming" title="Tropical cyclone naming">\n        assigned the name\n       </a>\n       <i>\n        Beulah\n       </i>\n       , making it the second named storm of the\n       <a href="/wiki/1967_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1967 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1967 season\n       </a>\n       . Shortly after being named, Beulah clipped the southern coast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Martinique" title="Martinique">\n        Martinique\n       </a>\n       and entered the eastern\n       <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">\n        Caribbean Sea\n       </a>\n       . Feeding off the warm waters of the Caribbean, the cyclone quickly strengthened and reached hurricane intensity by 18:00\xc2\xa0UTC on September\xc2\xa08.\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" title="Rapid deepening">\n        Rapid deepening\n       </a>\n       ensued thereafter with the storm\'s central pressure falling to 940\xc2\xa0mbar (hPa; 27.75\xc2\xa0inHg) the following day as it passed 100\xc2\xa0mi (160\xc2\xa0km) south of\n       <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">\n        Puerto Rico\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_3-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At this time winds were estimated to be at least 140\xc2\xa0mph (225\xc2\xa0km/h), ranking Beulah as a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SSHS#Category_4" title="SSHS">\n        Category\xc2\xa04\n       </a>\n       on the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_hurricane_wind_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson hurricane wind scale">\n        Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson hurricane scale\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_2-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Upon reaching this strength,\n       <a href="/wiki/Weather_radar" title="Weather radar">\n        weather radar\n       </a>\n       imagery from\n       <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">\n        San Juan, Puerto Rico\n       </a>\n       showed that Beulah featured a 15\xc2\xa0mi (24\xc2\xa0km) wide\n       <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">\n        eye\n       </a>\n       surrounded by an intense eyewall about 8\xc2\xa0mi (13\xc2\xa0km) thick.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Radar_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Radar-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n     During the evening of September\xc2\xa09 Beulah turned westward as weak\n       <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">\n        ridge\n       </a>\n       developed over the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">\n        Bahamas\n       </a>\n       , between it and the newly formed\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Doria_(1967)" title="Hurricane Doria (1967)">\n        Tropical Storm Doria\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_3-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The powerful storm weakened somewhat as an\n       <a href="/wiki/Eyewall_replacement_cycle" title="Eyewall replacement cycle">\n        eyewall replacement cycle\n       </a>\n       began to take shape. During this phase, the inner-eye of Beulah contracted to roughly 3\xe2\x80\x934\xc2\xa0mi (5\xe2\x80\x936\xc2\xa0km) in diameter while a second eyewall spanned an area 28\xc2\xa0mi (45\xc2\xa0km) across. The smaller eye soon dissipated and the larger one became the single, dominant feature by the morning of September\xc2\xa010. The completion of this marked the first time that an eyewall replacement cycle was observed in its entirety.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Radar_4-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Radar-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The aforementioned westward turn placed the\n       <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">\n        Dominican Republic\n       </a>\n       in the line of danger, an area still reeling from the devastating effects of\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Inez" title="Hurricane Inez">\n        Hurricane Inez\n       </a>\n       just\n       <a href="/wiki/1966_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1966 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        one year prior\n       </a>\n       . Fortunately, the storm unexpectedly collapsed as it approached the\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Barahona_Peninsula&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Barahona Peninsula (page does not exist)">\n        Barahona Peninsula\n       </a>\n       and struck the area as a greatly weakened, though still significant, hurricane with estimated winds of 90\xc2\xa0mph (150\xc2\xa0km/h) around 18:00\xc2\xa0UTC on September\xc2\xa011.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_3-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_2-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Skirting the southern coast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">\n        Haiti\n       </a>\n       \'s\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tiburon_Peninsula,_Haiti" title="Tiburon Peninsula, Haiti">\n        Tiburon Peninsula\n       </a>\n       , Beulah further degraded to a tropical storm by the morning of September\xc2\xa012. Unseasonably strong\n       <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">\n        wind shear\n       </a>\n       associated with the\n       <a href="/wiki/Jet_stream" title="Jet stream">\n        jet stream\n       </a>\n       , resulting from an upper-level\n       <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">\n        trough\n       </a>\n       to the north, and the cyclone\'s interaction with land were responsible for the dramatic degradation. By the time it had cleared Haiti, Beulah was no more than a minimal tropical storm with winds near 40\xc2\xa0mph (65\xc2\xa0km/h).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_3-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Its pressure had also risen by roughly 60\xc2\xa0mbar (hPa; 1.77\xc2\xa0inHg) to 1000\xc2\xa0mbar (hPa; 29.53\xc2\xa0inHg).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_2-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Originally seen as a threat to\n       <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">\n        Jamaica\n       </a>\n       , northeasterly flow induced a southerly component to the track and pushed the cyclone south of the island on September\xc2\xa013. The shear previously impeding organization abated on September\xc2\xa014 and a ridge re-established itself to Beulah\'s north, allowing the storm to resume a west-northwest to northwest track. The upper-level changes led to a favorable environment for intensification and Beulah regained hurricane strength by 12:00\xc2\xa0UTC on September\xc2\xa014 while located about 425\xc2\xa0mi (685\xc2\xa0km) south-southeast of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Havana,_Cuba" title="Havana, Cuba">\n        Havana, Cuba\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_3-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_2-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prelim_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Prelim-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_3-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Beulah quickly regained major hurricane status on September\xc2\xa015 with winds estimated at 115\xc2\xa0mph (185\xc2\xa0km/h).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_2-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The storm\'s pressure fell to 964\xc2\xa0mb (hPa; 28.47\xc2\xa0inHg) on September\xc2\xa016 before some weakening took place. Beulah ultimately made\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">\n        landfall\n       </a>\n       on\n       <a href="/wiki/Cozumel" title="Cozumel">\n        Cozumel\n       </a>\n       island with winds of at least 100\xc2\xa0mph (160\xc2\xa0km/h) later that day and struck the mainland\n       <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula" title="Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula">\n        Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula\n       </a>\n       hours later. Despite moving over land, little weakening took place by the time the hurricane emerged over the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">\n        Gulf of Mexico\n       </a>\n       about 24\xc2\xa0hours later.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_3-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The hurricane maintained its intensity throughout September\xc2\xa018 as it moved west-northwest to southern\n       <a href="/wiki/Tamaulipas" title="Tamaulipas">\n        Tamaulipas\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">\n        Mexico\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_2-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       However, on September\xc2\xa019, a pronounced phase of rapid intensification took place as Beulah turned northwest to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Rio_Grande_Valley" title="Rio Grande Valley">\n        Rio Grande Valley\n       </a>\n       region. Aircraft reconnaissance throughout the day found falling pressures and ultimately measured a value of 923\xc2\xa0mbar (hPa; 27.25\xc2\xa0inHg) around 18:00\xc2\xa0UTC. This was the second-lowest pressure ever recorded by aircraft at the time, behind a 920\xc2\xa0mbar (hPa; 27.17\xc2\xa0inHg) measurement in\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hattie" title="Hurricane Hattie">\n        Hurricane Hattie\n       </a>\n       of\n       <a href="/wiki/1961_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1961 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1961\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_3-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Beulah was estimated to have achieved its peak intensity shortly thereafter as a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SSHS#Category_5" title="SSHS">\n        Category\xc2\xa05 hurricane\n       </a>\n       with winds estimated at 160\xc2\xa0mph (260\xc2\xa0km/h).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_2-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Regarded as the third-largest hurricane on record at the time,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DRTX_6-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-DRTX-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Beulah moved along a slowing, erratic, and somewhat\n       <a href="/wiki/Cycloid" title="Cycloid">\n        cycloidal\n       </a>\n       path. No direct measurements exist at the core of the hurricane as it moved ashore; however, based on a minimum pressure of 951\xc2\xa0mbar (hPa; 28.09\xc2\xa0inHg) in Brownsville, the hurricane likely struck land at an intensity below that value.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_3-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A vessel anchored in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Port_of_Brownsville" title="Port of Brownsville">\n        Port of Brownsville\n       </a>\n       measured peak wind gusts of 136\xc2\xa0mph (219\xc2\xa0km/h), equivalent to a low-end\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SSHS#Category_4" title="SSHS">\n        Category\xc2\xa04 hurricane\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prelim_5-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Prelim-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DRTX_6-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-DRTX-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       According to the\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">\n        National Hurricane Center\n       </a>\n       , Beulah struck as a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SSHS#Category_3" title="SSHS">\n        Category\xc2\xa03\n       </a>\n       and had a pressure of 950\xc2\xa0mbar (hPa; 28.05\xc2\xa0inHg), though a conclusive estimate awaits re-evaluation as part of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_hurricane_reanalysis_project" title="Atlantic hurricane reanalysis project">\n        Atlantic hurricane reanalysis project\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Once over land, the hurricane slowly weakened as it remained relatively close to the coast. Winds dropped below hurricane-force on September\xc2\xa021, roughly 24\xc2\xa0hours after landfall. The system subsequently stalled near\n       <a href="/wiki/Alice,_Texas" title="Alice, Texas">\n        Alice, Texas\n       </a>\n       before turning to the southwest.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_3-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       After diminishing to a tropical depression late on September\xc2\xa022,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_2-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Beulah\'s circulation finally dissipated over the mountainous terrain of\n       <a href="/wiki/Nuevo_Le%C3%B3n" title="Nuevo Le\xc3\xb3n">\n        Nuevo Le\xc3\xb3n\n       </a>\n       , Mexico.\n       <sup class="reference" Warnings were soon raised for areas between\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barahona_(city)" title="Barahona (city)">\n        Barahona\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Cape_Enga%C3%B1o_(Dominican_Republic)" title="Cape Enga\xc3\xb1o (Dominican Republic)">\n        Cabo Enga\xc3\xb1o\n       </a>\n       in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">\n        Dominican Republic\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prelim_5-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Prelim-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Across the Dominican Republic, an estimated 200,000\xc2\xa0people evacuated from coastal areas.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_3-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">\n        United States\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Beulah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: United States">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Beginning on the afternoon of September 17, people were advised to remain off the beaches of Padre, Mustang, and St. Joseph Islands. Immediate evacuation of Port Aransas and Mustang, Padre, and St. Joseph Islands was advised on the morning of September 19. Most residents and others on the islands evacuated, including the personnel of Padre Island National Seashore. About 40 persons remained on the islands, including about 20 at Port Aransas. Immediate evacuation of Rockport and Live Oak and Lamar Peninsulas was advised in the evening of September 19. These areas and the towns of Ingleside and Aransas Pass were nearly completely evacuated. About 50 persons remained in Rockport. The evacuation of the University of Corpus Christi was advised on the morning of September 20, and Corpus Christi Beach and parts of Flour Bluff were also evacuated. During the storm there were 30,000\xc2\xa0people in shelters in Nueces and San Patricio Counties, including 6,000 in Corpus Christi.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">\n        Impact\n       </span>\n       <span section: Impact">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Eastern_Caribbean">\n        Eastern Caribbean\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Beulah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Eastern Caribbean">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">\n        Lesser Antilles\n       </a>\n       produced several days of heavy rain and set the stage for significant flooding.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_3-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On September\xc2\xa07 Beulah brought torrential rains to the already saturated region, with reports of more than 10\xc2\xa0in (250\xc2\xa0mm) received by the Weather Bureau. Hardest hit were the\n       <a href="/wiki/French_West_Indies" title="French West Indies">\n        French West Indies\n       </a>\n       , especially\n       <a href="/wiki/Martinique" title="Martinique">\n        Martinique\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lewiston1_9-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Lewiston1-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       During an 18\xe2\x80\x91hour span, 11.85\xc2\xa0in (301\xc2\xa0mm) fell on the island. Severe flooding claimed 15\xc2\xa0lives on Martinique and destroyed many homes.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_3-14">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lewiston2_10-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Lewiston2-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Parts of\n       <a href="/wiki/Fort-de-France" title="Fort-de-France">\n        Fort-de-France\n       </a>\n       were inundated by 3\xc2\xa0ft (0.91\xc2\xa0m) of water while the\n       <a href="/wiki/Martinique_Aim%C3%A9_C%C3%A9saire_International_Airport" title="Martinique Aim\xc3\xa9 C\xc3\xa9saire International Airport">\n        Martinique International Airport\n       </a>\n       saw 1\xc2\xa0ft (0.30\xc2\xa0m) of flooding. The banana crop sustained extensive losses.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lewiston1_9-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Lewiston1-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lewiston2_10-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Lewiston2-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Across the island nearly 1,000\xc2\xa0residents were left homeless, including 400 in Fort-de-France.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RDF1_11-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-RDF1-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Damage on the island amounted to $4.5\xc2\xa0million.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_3-15">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Supplies of fresh water were severely limited for two days following the storm.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RDF1_11-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-RDF1-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Heavy rains triggered flooding and landslides across\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Vincent_(island)" title="Saint Vincent (island)">\n        St. Vincent\n       </a>\n       , blocking numerous roads and causing some damage. Two children were killed after a boulder, dislodged by the rains, crashed into their home.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Damage on\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Lucia" title="St. Lucia">\n        St. Lucia\n       </a>\n       reached $3\xc2\xa0million, mainly stemming from the banana crop which was largely ruined.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_3-16">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The periphery of the hurricane brought rainfall primarily to southwestern\n       <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">\n        Puerto Rico\n       </a>\n       , where a maximum of 9.76 inches (248\xc2\xa0mm) fell at Maricao.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       One person lost their life on the island and damage was a minimal $150,000.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_3-17">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Owing to effective evacuations, only two people lost their lives in the Dominican Republic. The core of Beulah impacted areas devastated by Hurricane Inez a year prior, and left extensive damage in its wake.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_3-18">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Early reports indicated that more than 1,000\xc2\xa0people were left homeless across the Barahona Peninsula.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">\n        <a href="#cite_note-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <a href="/wiki/Flash_flood" title="Flash flood">\n        Flash flooding\n       </a>\n       affected the southern coasts of both the Dominican Republic and Haiti.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">\n        <a href="#cite_note-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Beulah had limited impact on Jamaica as it brushed the island as a weak tropical storm.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_3-19">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       According to newspapers, gale-force winds affected the nation though there were no reports of damage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">\n        <a href="#cite_note-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Mexico">\n        Mexico\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Beulah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Mexico">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Beulah_1967_rainfall.png">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="887" data-file-width="1161" height="168" Texas\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Striking\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cozumel_Island" title="Cozumel Island">\n        Cozumel Island\n       </a>\n       and the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yucatan_Peninsula" title="Yucatan Peninsula">\n        Yucatan Peninsula\n       </a>\n       on September\xc2\xa017 as Category\xc2\xa02 hurricane, Beulah caused considerable damage and killed 11 people across the region. Wind gusts up to 125\xc2\xa0mph (205\xc2\xa0km/h) severed communication lines, downed power lines and felled trees.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prelim_5-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Prelim-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The\n       <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Morelos_Lighthouse" title="Puerto Morelos Lighthouse">\n        Puerto Morelos lighthouse\n       </a>\n       on the\n       <a href="/wiki/Riviera_Maya" title="Riviera Maya">\n        Riviera Maya\n       </a>\n       was undermined by the storm; it was never torn down and the leaning tower remains a tourist attraction in the area.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">\n        <a href="#cite_note-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/M%C3%A9rida,_Yucat%C3%A1n" title="M\xc3\xa9rida, Yucat\xc3\xa1n">\n        M\xc3\xa9rida, Yucat\xc3\xa1n\n       </a>\n       , winds were recorded up to 75\xc2\xa0mph (120\xc2\xa0km/h). Under the force of the powerful winds, several structures collapsed across the Peninsula, resulting in six fatalities.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TB1_18-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TB1-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Nearly every buildings on Cozumel Island sustained damage, roughly half of which lost their roofs.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">\n        <a href="#cite_note-19">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Four people were also killed in\n       <a href="/wiki/Playa_del_Carmen" title="Playa del Carmen">\n        Playa del Carmen\n       </a>\n       . Along the coast, Beulah\'s\n       <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">\n        storm surge\n       </a>\n       flooded areas within 600\xc2\xa0yd (550\xc2\xa0m) of the coastline, washing out roads and leaving "graveyards of boats."\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TB1_18-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TB1-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Throughout the Yucatan Peninsula, an estimated 5,000 people were left homeless and at least 30,000 were affected by the storm.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">\n        <a href="#cite_note-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Throughout Mexico, Beulah killed 19 people.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">\n        <a href="#cite_note-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Economic losses across\n       <a href="/wiki/Tampico" title="Tampico">\n        Tampico\n       </a>\n       reached 500\xc2\xa0million\n       <a href="/wiki/Mexican_peso" title="Mexican peso">\n        pesos\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">\n        <a href="#cite_note-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="United_States_2">\n        United States\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Beulah&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: United States">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       In Texas upon landfall, an 18 feet (5.5\xc2\xa0m) to 20 feet (6.1\xc2\xa0m) storm surge inundated lower Padre Island. The force of the storm tide made 31 cuts completely through the barrier island.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DRTX_6-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-DRTX-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <a href="/wiki/Padre_Island" title="Padre Island">\n        Padre Island\n       </a>\n       suffered significant devastation, and the island\'s sensitive ecosystem was altered by the storm. The highest sustained wind was reported as 136 miles per hour (219\xc2\xa0km/h), recorded in the town of\n       <a href="/wiki/South_Padre_Island,_Texas" title="South Padre Island, Texas">\n        South Padre Island\n       </a>\n       , across the Laguna Madre from\n       <a href="/wiki/Port_Isabel,_Texas" title="Port Isabel, Texas">\n        Port Isabel\n       </a>\n       . Winds as high as 109 miles per hour (175\xc2\xa0km/h) were measured at the Brownsville National Weather Service office at landfall. Since the hurricane bent the\n       <a href="/wiki/Anemometer" title="Anemometer">\n        anemometer\n       </a>\n       30\xc2\xa0degrees from the vertical, it is possible the winds at Brownsville were underestimated.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IAH_23-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-IAH-23">\n         [23]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Gusts of over 100 miles per hour (160\xc2\xa0km/h) were recorded as far inland as the towns of\n       <a href="/wiki/McAllen,_Texas" title="McAllen, Texas">\n        McAllen\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Edinburg,_Texas" title="Edinburg, Texas">\n        Edinburg\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Mission,_Texas" title="Mission, Texas">\n        Mission\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Pharr,_Texas" title="Pharr, Texas">\n        Pharr\n       </a>\n       , some 50 miles (80\xc2\xa0km) from the gulf coast. Beulah spawned a record 115\n       <a href="/wiki/Tornado" title="Tornado">\n        tornadoes\n       </a>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">\n        <a href="#cite_note-24">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       which destroyed homes, commercial property, and inflicted serious damage on the region\'s agricultural industry. The tornado record from Beulah would survive until\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Ivan" title="Hurricane Ivan">\n        Hurricane Ivan\n       </a>\n       set a new record in 2004. The\n       <a href="/wiki/Rio_Grande_Valley" title="Rio Grande Valley">\n        Rio Grande Valley\n       </a>\n       \'s citrus industry, based on cultivation of the famous "Ruby Red"\n       <a href="/wiki/Grapefruit" title="Grapefruit">\n        grapefruit\n       </a>\n       , was particularly hard hit.\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Wea00713.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1188" data-file-width="1600" height="149" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Wea00713.jpg/200px-Wea00713.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Wea00713.jpg/300px-Wea00713.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Wea00713.jpg/400px-Wea00713.jpg 2x" width="200"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Wea00713.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Damage and Flooding in Brownsville, Texas from Hurricane Beulah.\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The lower Rio Grande Valley, the four county region that comprises deep south Texas, was inundated with torrential rains. Within a 36\xe2\x80\x91hour period it dropped over 27 inches (690\xc2\xa0mm) of rain near\n       <a href="/wiki/Beeville,_Texas" title="Beeville, Texas">\n        Beeville, Texas\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">\n        <a href="#cite_note-25">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Falfurrias received more rain from Beulah than it normally records during one year. Areas south of Laredo, San Antonio, and Matagorda were isolated for more than a week due to the resulting flood.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-DRTX_6-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-DRTX-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On September 28,\n       <a href="/wiki/Lyndon_B._Johnson" title="Lyndon B. Johnson">\n        President Lyndon Baines Johnson\n       </a>\n       declared twenty-four counties in southern Texas a disaster area.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26">\n        <a href="#cite_note-26">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Animal life in the region responded in various ways to survive. Ants survived the floods by congregating in spheres of living colonies and floated down streams to safety. Predaceous beetle larvae preyed on frogs and rodents. Crustaceans from the beaches migrated en masse to the protection of high ground.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27">\n        <a href="#cite_note-27">\n         [27]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Hurricane Beulah caused an estimated US$1.41\xc2\xa0billion (2010\xc2\xa0dollars) in damage. Sources report 15 total deaths from the storm.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28">\n        <a href="#cite_note-28">\n         [28]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n
