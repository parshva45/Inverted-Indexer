8 Commander and Science Officer Michael Foale conducts an inspection of the Microgravity Science Glovebox.\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <b>\n        Microgravity Science Glovebox\n       </b>\n       (MSG) is a\n       <a href="/wiki/Glovebox" title="Glovebox">\n        glovebox\n       </a>\n       aboard the\n       <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">\n        International Space Station\n       </a>\n       . It provides a safe contained environment for research with liquids, combustion and hazardous materials in the microgravity conditions of the ISS. Without the MSG, many types of hands-on investigations would be impossible or severely limited on board the Station. The\n       <b>\n        Microgravity Science Glovebox\n       </b>\n       (MSG) occupies a floor-to-ceiling rack inside the\n       <a href="/wiki/Destiny_(ISS_module)" title="Destiny (ISS module)">\n        Destiny module\n       </a>\n       of the\n       <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">\n        International Space Station\n       </a>\n       (ISS). It is more than twice as large as gloveboxes flown on the\n       <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle" title="Space Shuttle">\n        Space Shuttle\n       </a>\n       and can hold larger investigations that are about twice the size of an airline carry-on bag.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nasa.gov_-_MSG_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nasa.gov_-_MSG-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="toc">\n       <div class="toctitle">\n        <h2>\n         Contents\n        </h2>\n       </div>\n       <ul>\n class="toctext">\n           External links\n          </span>\n         </a>\n        </li>\n       </ul>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">\n        Description\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Microgravity_Science_Glovebox&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Description">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <b>\n        Core Facility\n       </b>\n       of MSG occupies the upper half of the overall rack and includes the large\n       <b>\n        work volume (WV)\n       </b>\n       , an\n       <a href="/wiki/Airlock" title="Airlock">\n        airlock\n       </a>\n       and electronics for control, housekeeping and investigation resources. The WV holds the experiment and related equipment. The work volume is approximately 3 feet (910\xc2\xa0mm) wide, 2 feet (610\xc2\xa0mm) high, and 1.5 feet (460\xc2\xa0mm) deep with a usable volume of about 255 litres. This area can be sealed and held at a negative pressure, isolating the crew and the Station from possible hazards associated with the investigations that are taking place inside.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       An airlock under the WV can be accessed to bring objects in safely while other activities are going on inside MSG. The MSG has 40\xc2\xa0cm diameter side ports (equipped with rugged gloves that are sealed to prevent leaks) for setting up and manipulating equipment in the WV. A cold-plate provides cooling for experiment hardware and the air is continuously circulated and filtered. Experiments are provided with 1\xc2\xa0kW of power and cooling.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Vacuum, venting, nitrogen gas input (that can keep the oxygen volume at 10 percent or less), power and data interfaces are also provided within MSG. A video system consists of a self-standing subsystem of four colour cameras, two monitors, two analogue recorders and two digital recorders integrated into an International\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sub-rack_Interface_Standard_(ISIS)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sub-rack Interface Standard (ISIS) (page does not exist)">\n        Sub-rack Interface Standard (ISIS)\n       </a>\n       drawer. The command and monitoring panel monitors the facility status and performance and provides for manual operation of MSG by the crew.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       MSG was delivered to ISS during\n       <a href="/wiki/Expedition_5" title="Expedition 5">\n        Expedition 5\n       </a>\n       , whereupon it was installed in the Destiny module. On March 21, 2008, during\n       <a href="/wiki/Expedition_16" title="Expedition 16">\n        Expedition 16\n       </a>\n       , MSG was relocated to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Columbus_(ISS_module)" title="Columbus (ISS module)">\n        Columbus module\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nasa.gov_-_MSG_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nasa.gov_-_MSG-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On October 21, 2010, during\n       <a href="/wiki/Expedition_25" title="Expedition 25">\n        Expedition 25\n       </a>\n       , MSG was transferred back to the Destiny module.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Description_Summary">\n        Description Summary\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Microgravity_Science_Glovebox&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Description Summary">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <ul>\n       <li>\n        The\n        <b>\n         Microgravity Science Glovebox\n        </b>\n        (MSG) makes it possible to do investigations in microgravity that are similar to those carried out in ground-based laboratories. Without containment, liquids and particles involved in experiments on board the Space Station would float about the cabin. This could cause damage to equipment or harm the crew.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Crewmembers access the work area through ports equipped with rugged, sealed gloves that can be removed when contaminants are not present. A video system and data downlinks allow for control of the enclosed experiments from the ground, if desired.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        In addition to doing complete, laboratory-like experiments, the MSG allows scientists to test small parts of larger investigations in a microgravity environment and to try out new equipment in microgravity.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        The MSG can support all key areas of microgravity research as well as other scientific fields. This makes it a useful laboratory resource for scientists in many different fields conducting a wide variety of investigations.\n        <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nasa.gov_-_MSG_1-2">\n         <a href="#cite_note-nasa.gov_-_MSG-1">\n          [1]\n         </a>\n        </sup>\n       </li>\n      </ul>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Operations">\n        Operations\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Microgravity_Science_Glovebox&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Operations">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       The MSG accommodates small and medium-sized investigations from any disciplines including biotechnology, combustion science, life sciences, fluid physics, fundamental physics and materials science. Many of these experiments use chemicals, burning or molten materials or other hazards that must be contained.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nasa.gov_-_MSG_1-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nasa.gov_-_MSG-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Results">\n        Results\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Microgravity_Science_Glovebox&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Results">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       The MSG on the ISS has been utilized for a large body of research. The MSG has operated on orbit for more than 3500 hours; used by various types of investigations, including material science, thermal management, protein crystal growth, life sciences, fire detection, combustion and technology demonstration. The versatility of the resources MSG provides makes it an ideal platform for microgravity research (Spivey 2006 - 2008).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nasa.gov_-_MSG_1-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nasa.gov_-_MSG-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">\n        See also\n       </span>\n       <span
