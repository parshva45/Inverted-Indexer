    </a>\n       , but was defeated along with his partner Rattler and taken into custody.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-1">\n         [1]\n <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_(comics)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3"   Hurricane\n       </b>\n       (\n       <b>\n        Albert Potter\n       </b>\n       ) was an enemy of\n       <a href="/wiki/Captain_Britain" title="Captain Britain">\n        Captain Britain\n       </a>\n       and a former\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meteorologist" title="Meteorologist">\n        meteorologist\n       </a>\n       . A weather experiment gave him strong weather and energy manipulating powers.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       He initially clashed with\n       <a href="/wiki/Captain_Britain" title="Captain Britain">\n        Captain Britain\n       </a>\n       , but was defeated and incarcerated in Darkmoor Prison.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       While jailed, Potter agreed to participate in medical experiments. Doctor Samuel Merrick used him as one of the first test subjects for his Gene Match Device (GMD), rewriting Potter\'s DNA to include genetic material from a soldier ant. The process was intended to enhance Potter\'s abilities, but seemingly killed him.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gd2_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-gd2-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Hurricane\'s body was stolen by the\n       <a href="/wiki/Mys-Tech" title="Mys-Tech">\n        Mys-Tech\n       </a>\n       organisation, who later revived and healed him, intending to use Potter as a catspaw in their conflict with Otomocorp and the S.T.O.R.M. anti-terrorist team known as the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gene_Dogs" title="Gene Dogs">\n        Gene Dogs\n       </a>\n       . Hurricane was sent to recover a Gene Scanner device from S.T.O.R.M. headquarters - the device had initially been stolen from Mys-Tech by Otomocorp\'s Q7 Strike Force, but had then been seized by S.T.O.R.M.\'s Gene Dogs.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Hurricane easily succeeded in this task (also capturing Doctor Merrick, who had created the Gene Dogs by using a later version of the GMD process he used on Hurricane) but was then betrayed by his new associates - Mys-Tech had booby-trapped the device and always intended that Otomocorp should seize it, so arranged this by leaking Hurricane\'s location to Otomocorp. Hurricane was then beaten and defeated by the Q7 team.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       However, Potter escaped and took control of Q7 after realising that the GMD process had not been a complete failure - it had successfully enhanced his energy control powers, and had also granted him "hive mind" abilities patterned on the soldier ant, allowing him to mentally control others.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gd3_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-gd3-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Despite this, in\n       <i>\n        Gene Dogs\n       </i>\n       #4 he was eventually defeated by the combined forces of the Q7 and Gene Dogs teams.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       He subsequently reformed and emigrated to the United States, but jobless, he nearly relapsed into a life of crime, before being convinced to stick to the straight and narrow after encountering the\n       <a href="/wiki/Fantastic_Four" title="Fantastic Four">\n        Fantastic Four\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Albert Potter is considered a "potential recruit" for the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Fifty_State_Initiative" title="Fifty State Initiative">\n        Initiative\n       </a>\n       program, according to\n       <i>\n        Civil War: Battle Damage Report\n       </i>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-civilwarbattledamage_7-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-civilwarbattledamage-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Dark_Riders">\n        Dark Riders\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_(comics)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" Hurricane\n       </b>\n       was a member of the second incarnation of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Dark_Riders_(comics)" title="Dark Riders (comics)">\n        Dark Riders\n       </a>\n       . The Riders are killing mutants with healing powers. The manage to kill\n       <a href="/wiki/Elixir_(comics)" title="Elixir (comics)">\n        Elixir\n       </a>\n       , but are ambushed on\n       <a href="/wiki/Genosha" title="Genosha">\n        Genosha\n       </a>\n       . It is assumed they died, since Magneto strapped them to a bonb and leveled the entire island.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Civil_War">\n        <i>\n         Civil War\n        </i>\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_(comics)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Civil War">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       A new Hurricane character was introduced in the\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Civil_War:_The_Initiative" title="Civil War: The Initiative">\n         Civil War: The Initiative\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       one-shot. Not much is known about this character, except that he has an inhuman resistance to damage and an unusual intellect which he used to design his own special "gear", which apparently grants him his powers. It is also mentioned that Hurricane had only been a super-hero for nine months and three days when the story began. As an unregistered superhuman, he was confronted by the\n       <a href="/wiki/Thunderbolts_(comics)" title="Thunderbolts (comics)">\n        Thunderbolts\n       </a>\n       . In turn, the Thunderbolts fought and practically crippled Hurricane. The Thunderbolts later apparently exposed Hurricane to the public, and what became of him afterwards is unknown.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The character is referred to as "Hurricane II" during the issue. OHOTMU writer Stuart Vandal said on a Comixfan forum that this Hurricane is not the same person as Albert Potter.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Other_versions">\n        Other versions\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_(comics)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Other versions">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Marvel_Zombies_5">\n        Marvel Zombies 5\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_(comics)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Marvel Zombies 5">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       An alternate universe Harry Kane runs a small saloon with the assistance of his daughter, Jacali. He is tormented by the loss of his wife in a trick-show; his powers only work against a threat and he had killed her because she bore no ill-will against him. Zombies invade his town. Despite his speed he dies in battle but not before transferring his powers to his daughter. She continues his legacy of heroism.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">\n        <a href="#cite_note-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Ultimate_Marvel">\n        Ultimate Marvel\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_(comics)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Ultimate Marvel">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       This\n       <b>\n        Hurricane\n       </b>\n       is a character in the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ultimate_Marvel_Universe" title="Ultimate Marvel Universe">\n        Ultimate Marvel Universe\n       </a>\n       , a woman with super-human speed (gained from surgery) from\n       <a href="/wiki/North_Korea" title="North Korea">\n        North Korea\n       </a>\n       . She is a member of\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=The_Liberators_(Marvel_Comics)&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="The Liberators (Marvel Comics) (page does not exist)">\n        The Liberators\n       </a>\n       , an international counterpart of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/The_Ultimates" title="The Ultimates">\n        The Ultimates\n       </a>\n       . The Liberators invade the USA, killing thousands. She is defeated when Quicksilver forced her to move at speeds beyond her limits, tearing her body apart.
