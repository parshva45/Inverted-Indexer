  <p>\n       <b>\n        Hurricane Debbie\n       </b>\n       was an intense and long-lived hurricane that formed during August 1969. The fifth\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        tropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       , fourth named storm, third hurricane and second major hurricane of the\n       <a href="/wiki/1969_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1969 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1969 Atlantic hurricane season\n       </a>\n       , Debbie formed on August 14 in the southern Atlantic Ocean and took a general northwesterly path until turning northward into the central Atlantic. The storm was characterized by numerous fluctuations in intensity, and it reached winds corresponding to Category 3 status on the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson Hurricane Scale">\n        Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson Hurricane Scale\n       </a>\n       on four separate occasions. The hurricane bypassed the island of Bermuda to the southeast on August 22, before ultimately brushing southeastern\n       <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_(island)" title="Newfoundland (island)">\n        Newfoundland\n       </a>\n       with strong winds. It dissipated over the cold waters east of\n       <a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">\n        Greenland\n       </a>\n       . Although Debbie had little effect on land, it was extensively researched and was subject to a\n       <a href="/wiki/Weather_modification" title="Weather modification">\n        weather modification\n       </a>\n       experiment by\n       <a href="/wiki/Project_Stormfury" title="Project Stormfury">\n        Project Stormfury\n       </a>\n       , in which it was\n       <a href="/wiki/Cloud_seeding" title="Cloud seeding">\n        seeded\n       </a>\n       with\n       <a href="/wiki/Silver_iodide" title="Silver iodide">\n        silver iodide\n       </a>\n       .\n <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       It attained\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale#Category_1" title="Saffir-Simpson It continued to mature, and at around 1200 UTC the next day, it achieved winds corresponding to Category 2 on the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">\n        Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale\n       </a>\n       . On August 18, Debbie further intensified to Category 3 status, making it a major hurricane.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hurdat_1-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-hurdat-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       However, the storm quickly downgraded, and by August 19 it was once again at minimal hurricane force. At roughly the same time, it turned more to the west, although it maintained a general northwesterly path.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hurdat_1-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-hurdat-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The abrupt weakening may have been the result of a\n       <a href="/wiki/Project_Stormfury" title="Project Stormfury">\n        seeding experiment\n       </a>\n       carried out on the storm in an attempt to deteriorate it.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       By later in the day, Debbie had begun to restrengthen. It resumed Category 3 intensity on August 20, despite a minor oscillation in magnitude during the day. At this point, the cyclone acquired peak winds of 120\xc2\xa0mph (195\xc2\xa0km/h); shortly thereafter, its lowest recorded barometric pressure fell to 951\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Millibars" title="Millibars">\n        millibars\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hurdat_1-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-hurdat-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The storm turned northward on August 21,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and eventually curved northeastward. Debbie weakened to Category 2 strength but, for the fourth time, restrengthened to major hurricane intensity.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hurdat_1-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-hurdat-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The hurricane then passed well to the southeast of Bermuda,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hurdat_1-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-hurdat-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       although it is believed that if not for the presence of nearby\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Camille" title="Hurricane Camille">\n        Hurricane Camille\n       </a>\n       which emerged into the Atlantic from the\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">\n        United States\n       </a>\n       on August 20, Debbie would have likely ended up further west, closer to the island.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_5-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       It maintained its severity through August 22 as it continued generally toward the northeast.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hurdat_1-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-hurdat-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Eye_of_hurricane_debbie_(1969).jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="460" data-file-width="700" height="145" class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Debbie began to lose its tropical characteristics as it accelerated towards the northeast,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc38_8-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-disc38-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and it weakened into a tropical storm early on August 25.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hurdat_1-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-hurdat-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       As it moved over increasingly cold waters, it dissipated east of\n       <a href="/wiki/Greenland" title="Greenland">\n        Greenland\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_5-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Impact_and_Project_Stormfury">\n        Impact and Project Stormfury\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Debbie_(1969)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Impact and Project Stormfury">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:DebbieAug2019691519zNavyRadar.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2225" data-file-width="1814" height="270"    <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:DebbieAug2019691519zNavyRadar.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         This radar image from a Navy reconnaissance aircraft of Hurricane Debbie was taken on August 20, 1969 around 1519 UTC.\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main article:\n       <a href="/wiki/Project_Stormfury" title="Project Stormfury">\n        Project Stormfury\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Debbie was subject to an experiment called\n       <a href="/wiki/Project_Stormfury" title="Project Stormfury">\n        Project Stormfury\n       </a>\n       , which attempted to weaken tropical cyclones by\n       <a href="/wiki/Cloud_seeding" title="Cloud seeding">\n        seeding\n       </a>\n       them with\n       <a href="/wiki/Silver_iodide" title="Silver iodide">\n        silver iodide\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_5-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The storm provided an excellent opportunity to test the underpinnings of Project Stormfury. In many ways it was the perfect storm for seeding: it did not threaten any land; it passed within range of seeding aircraft; and was intense with a distinct eye.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On August 18 and again on August 20, thirteen planes flew out to the storm to monitor and seed it. On the first day, windspeeds fell by 31%. On the second day, windspeeds fell by 18%.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_5-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Both changes were consistent with Stormfury\'s working hypothesis. The results were so encouraging that "a greatly expanded research program was planned."\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Among other conclusions was the need for frequent seeding at close to hourly intervals.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_5-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Debbie remained predominately at sea throughout its 3,000\xc2\xa0mi (4,800\xc2\xa0km) path, and as a result, it caused little damage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-MWR_5-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-MWR-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The storm had little or no impact on the island of Bermuda as it passed to the south.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Later, winds of 50 to 65\xc2\xa0mph (80 to 105\xc2\xa0km/h) were recorded over eastern Newfoundland.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc38_8-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-disc38-8">\n
