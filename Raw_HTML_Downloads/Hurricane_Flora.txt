history">\n        recorded history\n       </a>\n       , with a death total of at least 7,186. The seventh tropical storm and sixth hurricane of the\n       <a href="/wiki/1963_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1963 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1963 Atlantic hurricane season\n       </a>\n       , Flora developed from a disturbance in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Intertropical_Convergence_Zone" title="Intertropical Convergence Zone">\n        Intertropical Convergence Zone\n       </a>\n       on September 26 while located 755 miles (1,215\xc2\xa0km) southwest of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Cape_Verde" title="Cape Verde">\n        Cape Verde\n       </a>\n       islands. After remaining a weak depression for several days, it rapidly organized on September 29 to attain tropical storm status. Flora continued to quickly strengthen to reach\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">\n        Category 3\n       </a>\n       hurricane status before moving through the\n       <a href="/wiki/Windward_Islands" title="Windward Islands">\n        Windward Islands\n       </a>\n       and passing over\n       <a href="/wiki/Tobago" title="Tobago">\n        Tobago\n       </a>\n       , and it reached\n       <a href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_wind" title="Maximum sustained wind">\n        maximum sustained winds\n       </a>\n       of 145 miles per hour (233\xc2\xa0km/h) in the Caribbean.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The storm struck southwestern\n       <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">\n        Haiti\n       </a>\n       near peak intensity, turned to the west, and drifted over\n       <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">\n        Cuba\n       </a>\n       for four days before turning to the northeast. Flora passed over the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">\n        Bahamas\n       </a>\n       and accelerated northeastward, becoming an\n       <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">\n        extratropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       on October 12. Due to its slow movement across Cuba, Flora is the wettest known tropical cyclone for Cuba, Haiti, and the Dominican Republic.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">\n        <a </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       A disturbance in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Intertropical_Convergence_Zone" title="Intertropical Convergence Zone">\n        Intertropical Convergence Zone\n Upon forming, the depression had a poorly organized circulation with\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rainbands" title="Rainbands">\n        banding features\n       </a>\n       to its north and east. The depression moved west-northwestward, and the system failed to organize significantly by a day after developing. On September 27, the banding features dissipated, though the area of convection around the center increased. Satellite imagery was unavailable until September 30, during which there were not sufficient ship reports to indicate the presence of a low-level circulation. The depression continued west-northwestward, and it is estimated it intensified into Tropical Storm Flora on September 29 while located about 560 miles (900\xc2\xa0km) east-southeast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Trinidad" title="Trinidad">\n        Trinidad\n       </a>\n       , or about 350 miles (560\xc2\xa0km) north of\n       <a href="/wiki/Cayenne" title="Cayenne">\n        Cayenne\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/French_Guiana" title="French Guiana">\n        French Guiana\n       </a>\n       . Operationally, advisories were not initiated until a day later.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GORDON63_2-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-GORDON63-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Flora quickly intensified after it became a tropical storm, and early on September 30 it attained hurricane status. Later that day,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hunters" title="Hurricane Hunters">\n        Reconnaissance Aircraft\n       </a>\n       confirmed the existence of the hurricane, with the flight reporting a well-defined, circular 8-mile (13\xc2\xa0km) wide\n       <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">\n        eye\n       </a>\n       . One observer noted Flora to be the best organized tropical cyclone over the previous two years. After attaining major hurricane status, Flora passed directly over the island of\n       <a href="/wiki/Tobago" title="Tobago">\n        Tobago\n       </a>\n       late on the 30th with winds of 120\xc2\xa0mph (190\xc2\xa0km/h). The hurricane continued west-northwestward as it entered the Caribbean, and early on October 2 its winds reached 140 miles per hour (230\xc2\xa0km/h). Thirty hours later, Flora intensified slightly further and attained peak winds of 145\xc2\xa0mph (233\xc2\xa0km/h) while located about 105 miles (169\xc2\xa0km) south of the border of\n       <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">\n        Haiti\n       </a>\n       and the\n       <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">\n        Dominican Republic\n       </a>\n 320\xc2\xa0km/h).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr3_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr3-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The calm of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">\n        eye\n       </a>\n       lasted for up to 70\xc2\xa0minutes in one location.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mic2_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mic2-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       After quickly weakening to a 120\xc2\xa0mph (190\xc2\xa0km/h) hurricane, Flora turned more to the west in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Windward_Passage" title="Windward Passage">\n        Windward Passage\n       </a>\n       , and restrengthened slightly to strike southeastern\n       <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">\n        Cuba\n       </a>\n       about 30 miles (48\xc2\xa0km) east of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Guant%C3%A1namo_Bay_(Cuba)" title="Guant\xc3\xa1namo Bay (Cuba)">\n        Guantanamo Bay\n       </a>\n       with winds of 125\xc2\xa0mph (201\xc2\xa0km/h). A\n       <a href="/wiki/Anticyclone" title="Anticyclone">\n        high pressure system\n       </a>\n       to its north turned the motion of the hurricane to a westward drift, causing it to weaken quickly over land. Flora neared the north coast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">\n        Cuba\n       </a>\n       on October 4 before turning to the south. It executed a cyclonic loop and entered the coastal waters off\n       <a href="/wiki/Granma_Province" title="Granma Province">\n        Granma Province\n       </a>\n       . An anticyclone to the west of Flora turned the hurricane to the north, bringing it ashore near\n       <a href="/wiki/Santa_Cruz_del_Sur" title="Santa Cruz del Sur">\n        Santa Cruz del Sur\n       </a>\n       on October 7 with winds of around 90\xc2\xa0mph (140\xc2\xa0km/h). Flora initially turned southeastward over central Cuba, and late on October 8 a\n       <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">\n        short-wave trough\n       </a>\n       turned the hurricane northeastward, bringing it into the coastal waters of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Holgu%C3%ADn_Province" title="Holgu\xc3\xadn Province">\n        Holgu\xc3\xadn Province\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GORDON63_2-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-GORDON63-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Flora remained a hurricane while drifting over land due to abundant moisture and a favorable upper level environment.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr3_3-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr3-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       After passing over the southeastern\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">\n        Bahamas\n       </a>\n       early on October 9, Flora began to restrengthen, and on October 10 it again reached major hurricane status while located 290 miles (470\xc2\xa0km) south of\n       <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">\n        Bermuda\n       </a>\n       . The hurricane gradually weakened as it continued northeastward, and weakened to a Category\xc2\xa01 hurricane on October 11. Flora gradually lost its convection over the north Atlantic Ocean, and transitioned into an\n       <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">\n        extratropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       on October 12 while located 270 miles (430\xc2\xa0km) east-southeast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Cape_Race" title="Cape Race">\n        Cape Race\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_(island)" title="Newfoundland (island)">\n        Newfoundland\n       </a>\n       . The extratropical remnant dissipated the next day.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GORDON63_2-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-GORDON63-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">\n        Preparations\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Flora&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Oceanic_and_Atmospheric_Administration" title="National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration">\n        Weather Bureau\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">\n        San Juan, Puerto Rico\n       </a>\n       issued a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">\n        hurricane warning\n       </a>\n       for\n       <a href="/wiki/Trinidad" title="Trinidad">\n        Trinidad\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Tobago" title="Tobago">\n        Tobago\n       </a>\n       , and the\n       <a href="/wiki/Grenadines" title="Grenadines">\n        Grenadines\n       </a>\n       south of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Vincent_(island)" title="Saint Vincent (island)">\n        Saint Vincent\n       </a>\n       in the cyclone\'s first advisory on Hurricane Flora. Gale warnings were later issued for islands off northern\n       <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">\n        Venezuela\n       </a>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr3_3-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr3-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and from Saint Vincent northward to\n       <a href="/wiki/Martinique" title="Martinique">\n        Martinique\n       </a>\n       . Advisories on Flora emphasized the danger of the hurricane and advised preparations to be rushed to completion. The advisories also recommended small craft throughout the\n       <a href="/wiki/Windward_Islands" title="Windward Islands">\n        Windward Islands\n       </a>\n       to remain at harbor and for shipping in the path of the hurricane to advise extreme caution.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr4_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr4-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       People in low-lying areas and near beaches were advised to evacuate to higher grounds, as well.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr5_6-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr5-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Lead time was short, especially in Tobago which received news of the approaching hurricane just two hours before it struck.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eval2_7-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-eval2-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       On October 2, two days before it made landfall on southwestern\n       <a href="/wiki/Hispaniola" title="Hispaniola">\n        Hispaniola\n       </a>\n       , the San Juan Weather Bureau issued a gale warning from\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Barahona_(city)" title="Barahona (city)">\n        Barahona\n       </a>\n       in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">\n        Dominican Republic\n       </a>\n       to\n       <a href="/wiki/Sud-Est_(department)" title="Sud-Est (department)">\n        Sud-Est\n       </a>\n       , Haiti. Advisories recommended small craft in the southern portions of the countries to remain at port.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr9_8-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr9-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Later that day, the gale warning was upgraded to a hurricane warning in southwestern Haiti.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr10_9-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr10-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On the day of when Flora made landfall, advisories recommended all citizens on beaches and in low-lying areas west of\n       <a href="/wiki/Santo_Domingo" title="Santo Domingo">\n        Santo Domingo\n       </a>\n       to evacuate.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr14_10-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr14-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Carmelo Di Franco, the provisional Director of Civic Defense for the Dominican Republic, organized safety procedures and the dissemination of tropical cyclone bulletins from the San Juan Weather Bureau. Di Franco also organized for the transmission of hurricane emergency information to citizens, believed to reduce the loss of life.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mic2_4-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mic2-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On the afternoon before the hurricane struck, the head of the Haitian\n       <a href="/wiki/International_Red_Cross_and_Red_Crescent_Movement" title="International Red Cross and Red Crescent Movement">\n        Red Cross\n       </a>\n       prohibited radio broadcasts of tropical cyclone advisories for fear of panic among citizens. As a result, many thought the hurricane would miss the country.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mic5_11-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mic5-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Officials at the\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cuban_National_Observatory&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cuban National Observatory (page does not exist)">\n        Cuban National Observatory\n       </a>\n       issued radio bulletins on the hurricane, which included the position of Flora, its intensity, direction of motion, and necessary warnings. By the time the hurricane left the island, more than 40,000 had been evacuated to safer areas.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jose_12-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-jose-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The Weather Bureau predicted Flora to turn to the northwest after entering the\n       <a href="/wiki/Windward_Passage" title="Windward Passage">\n        Windward Passage\n       </a>\n       and affect the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">\n        Bahamas\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr19_13-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr19-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Forecasters advised those in The Bahamas to quickly complete preparations, though the eye of the hurricane did not pass over the archipelago until four days later. When Flora turned to the northeast out to sea, forecasters again advised Bahamian citizens to prepare for the hurricane, and on October 9, weather advisories advised the southeastern Bahamas to prepare for gale-force winds and strong tides.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LincolnStar1_14-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-LincolnStar1-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       One advisory considered there to be less than a 50% chance of the hurricane reaching southeastern\n       <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">\n        Florida\n       </a>\n       , though weather bulletins advised Florida citizens to monitor the hurricane. At its closest approach to Florida, the hurricane remained 330\xc2\xa0miles (530\xc2\xa0km) away, though gale warnings were issued from\n       <a href="/wiki/Stuart,_Florida" title="Stuart, Florida">\n        Stuart\n       </a>\n       to\n       <a href="/wiki/Key_West,_Florida" title="Key West, Florida">\n        Key West\n       </a>\n       due to the hurricane\'s large size.\n Throughout its path, Hurricane Flora resulted in more than 7,000 deaths and over $525\xc2\xa0million in damage (1963\xc2\xa0USD, $4.11\xc2\xa0billion 2017\xc2\xa0USD). It is estimated that, if a hurricane like Flora had struck in 1998, it would have caused over 12,000 casualties.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NHRCuba_18-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NHRCuba-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="East_Caribbean">\n        East Caribbean\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Flora&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: East Caribbean">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Hurricane Flora passed\n       <a href="/wiki/Barbados" title="Barbados">\n        Barbados\n       </a>\n       a few days after\n       <a href="/wiki/1963_Atlantic_hurricane_season#Hurricane_Edith" title="1963 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        Hurricane Edith\n       </a>\n       moved through the area. The two hurricanes resulted in about $65,000 in damage (1963\xc2\xa0USD), primarily to fishing interests.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gordon_19-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-gordon-19">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       About four hours before the\n       <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">\n        eye\n       </a>\n       of Hurricane Flora moved over\n       <a href="/wiki/Tobago" title="Tobago">\n        Tobago\n       </a>\n       , the large hurricane began producing heavy rainfall throughout the island. Two hours later, strong winds began affecting the island, and while moving across the island Flora produced winds of up to 100 miles per hour (160\xc2\xa0km/h).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GORDON63_2-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-GORDON63-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-draftd2_20-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-draftd2-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       While moving past Tobago, the hurricane produced rough surf and tides 5 to 7 feet (1.5 to 2.1\xc2\xa0m) above normal.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr4_5-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr4-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The hurricane sunk six ships between 4 and 9 tons in Scarborough harbour. One crew member drowned while attempting to save his vessel.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-john_21-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-john-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Heavy rainfall caused a large mudslide from Mount Dillon onto a road leading to\n       <a href="/wiki/Castara" title="Castara">\n        Castara\n       </a>\n       . This was considered the most well-known mudslide on the island.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tobnews2_22-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tobnews2-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The strong winds caused severe damage to\n       <a href="/wiki/Coconut" title="Coconut">\n        coconut\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Banana" title="Banana">\n        banana\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Theobroma_cacao" title="Theobroma cacao">\n        cocoa\n       </a>\n       plantations,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tobagodive_23-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tobagodive-23">\n         [23]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       with 50% of the coconut trees being destroyed and another 11% being severely damaged. 75% of forest trees fell, and most of the remaining were greatly damaged. The passage of Hurricane Flora destroyed 2,750 of Tobago\'s 7,500 houses, and damaged 3,500 others. The hurricane killed 18 on the island and resulted in $30\xc2\xa0million in crop and property damage (1963\xc2\xa0USD).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-trintob_24-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-trintob-24">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Winds on\n       <a href="/wiki/Trinidad" title="Trinidad">\n        Trinidad\n       </a>\n       reached 55\xc2\xa0mph (89\xc2\xa0km/h) with much higher gusts of over 70\xc2\xa0mph (110\xc2\xa0km/h). Heavy rainfall and strong winds in the northern mountainous region lowered the visibility to zero. Due to the mountain range on its northern coast, damage on the island was minimal, totaling to $100,000 (1963\xc2\xa0USD).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GORDON63_2-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-GORDON63-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Two people on the island died due to drowning.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-trintob_24-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-trintob-24">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       When Flora passed the island and the winds turned to the southwest, many small boats in the westward facing harbor were sunk.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GORDON63_2-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-GORDON63-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Near\n       <a href="/wiki/Chaguaramas,_Trinidad" title="Chaguaramas, Trinidad">\n        Chaguaramas\n       </a>\n       , nine boats were destroyed and eight were damaged.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-john_21-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-john-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Additionally, several large vessels sustained damage and resulted in them being intentionally sunk. Damage in\n       <a href="/wiki/Grenada" title="Grenada">\n        Grenada\n       </a>\n       was minor, around $25,000 (1963\xc2\xa0USD), though six people died due to drowning.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GORDON63_2-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-GORDON63-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Rough seas and higher than normal tides were reported along the south coast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">\n        Puerto Rico\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr10_9-1">\n        <a     <a href="#cite_note-GORDON63-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       resulting in many deaths. The path of Flora over the area was best determined by the trajectory of fallen trees across the peninsula. The strong winds left entire villages roofless, with many buildings entirely destroyed. The combination of rough waves and strong winds destroyed three entire communities. Many of those who died in southwestern Haiti suffered from intense burns from strong winds.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mic4_33-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mic4-33">\n         [33]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In most areas, crops were entirely destroyed.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GORDON63_2-16">\n        <a href="#cite_note-GORDON63-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The\n       <a href="/wiki/Coffee" title="Coffee">\n        coffee\n       </a>\n       crop was harvested prior to the arrival of the hurricane, though heavy rainfall and severe flooding ruined the crop for later years. Additionally, strong winds downed shading trees for the coffee crop on the southern peninsula. One official estimated it would require three years for the coffee crop to be replanted and regrown.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mic3_32-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mic3-32">\n         [32]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       About 3,500 people were confirmed dead, with several thousands missing at one report five months after the hurricane. Winds in Cuba reached 125 miles per hour (201\xc2\xa0km/h). Nearly all crops in southeastern Cuba were affected by strong winds and flooding. Many citizens were left stranded at the tops of their houses. Several entire houses were swept away by the flooding, and many roads and bridges were destroyed, resulting in major disruptions to communications.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jose_12-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-jose-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Isolated locations in Cuba received over 80 inches (2,000\xc2\xa0mm) of rain,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NHRCuba_18-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NHRCuba-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       with Santiago de Cuba recording 100.39 inches (2,550\xc2\xa0mm), which is the highest rainfall total measured on Cuba from any rainfall event on record.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CUBARAIN_34-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CUBARAIN-34">\n         [34]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Heavy rainfall fell across the island of Jamaica due to southwest flow into the mountains on Flora\'s southern periphery over several days. The maximum amount recorded was 60 inches (1,500\xc2\xa0mm) at Spring Hill persistent,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WPCMAX_31-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-WPCMAX-31">\n         [31]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       which led to numerous landslides across the eastern portion of the island.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-uwi_35-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-uwi-35">\n         [35]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Damage to the island totaled $11.9\xc2\xa0million (1963 dollars).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GORDON63_2-18">\n        <a href="#cite_note-GORDON63-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Rough seas from Flora affected the Bahamas and the southeastern Florida coast on October 5 as the hurricane stalled over Cuba, keeping small craft in port,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Newport1_36-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Newport1-36">\n         [36]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Oshkosh1_37-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Oshkosh1-37">\n         [37]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and hurricane-force winds affected\n       <a href="/wiki/Ragged_Island,_Bahamas" title="Ragged Island, Bahamas">\n        Ragged Island\n       </a>\n       later that day. Rainfall amounts peaked at 17.19 inches (437\xc2\xa0mm) at Duncan Town, in the Bahamas.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WPCMAX_31-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-WPCMAX-31">\n         [31]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Damage to the island\n       <a href="/wiki/Archipelago" title="Archipelago">\n        archipelago\n       </a>\n       reached $1.5\xc2\xa0million (1963 dollars).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GORDON63_2-19">\n        <a href="#cite_note-GORDON63-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span agricultural damage from Flora caused the crop plantations to be abandoned. As a result, the economy of the island changed towards tourism.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tobagodive_23-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tobagodive-23">\n         [23]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The destruction of the Tobagonian rainforests resulted in a continual decline of agriculture on the island due to animals previously in the forest eating the crops for food.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tobnew1_38-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tobnew1-38">\n         [38]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The passage of the hurricane reduced the height of the rainforest canopy above 800 feet (240\xc2\xa0m) by half. Twenty-five years passed before the canopy reached its previous height.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-trintobnew3_39-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-trintobnew3-39">\n         [39]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In the\n       <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">\n        Dominican Republic\n       </a>\n       , damage reports were largely unknown by a month after the hurricane passed the island, primarily in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_the_Dominican_Republic" title="Provinces of the Dominican Republic">\n        western provinces\n       </a>\n       . There, roads were still impassable, large areas remained without electricity, and helicopters could not land in remote areas due to mud, silt, and up to 3 feet (0.91\xc2\xa0m) of water in all landing fields. One official estimated several months would pass before survey teams could obtain information on loss of life and overall damages.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mic1_40-0">\n        <a
