March 1963) is a Spanish\n       <a href="/wiki/Astronaut" title="Astronaut">\n        astronaut\n       </a>\n       and a veteran of two space missions.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Duque earned a degree in Aeronautical Engineering from the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Universidad_Polit%C3%A9cnica_de_Madrid" title="Universidad Polit\xc3\xa9cnica de Madrid">\n        Universidad Polit\xc3\xa9cnica de Madrid\n       </a>\n       (UPM) in 1986. He worked for\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GMV,_S.A." title="GMV, S.A.">\n        GMV\n       </a>\n       and for the\n       <a href="/wiki/European_Space_Agency" title="European Space Agency">\n        European Space Agency\n       </a>\n       (ESA) for six years before being selected as an astronaut candidate in 1992. Duque underwent training in both Russia and the United States. His first spaceflight was as a mission specialist aboard\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Space_shuttle" title="Space shuttle">\n        space shuttle\n       </a>\n       mission\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-95" title="STS-95">\n        STS-95\n       </a>\n       , during which Duque supervised ESA experimental modules. In October 2003, Duque visited the\n       <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">\n        International Space Station\n       </a>\n       for several days during a crew changeover. The scientific program of this visit was called by ESA/Spain\n       <a class="external text" href="http://www.esa.int/Our_Activities/Human_Spaceflight/Cervantes_Mission" rel="nofollow">\n        Misi\xc3\xb3n Cervantes\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       He worked at the UPM, in the\n       <span lang="es" xml:lang="es">\n        Escuela T\xc3\xa9cnica Superior de Ingenieros Aeron\xc3\xa1uticos\n       </span>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       he used to work at\n       <a class="external text" href="http://www.deimos-imaging.com" rel="nofollow">\n        Deimos Imaging\n       </a>\n       . Currently he is back as an\n       <a href="/wiki/Astronaut" title="Astronaut">\n        astronaut\n       </a>\n       of ESA, and leads the Flight Operations Office near Munich.\n      </p>\n
