<tr>\n        <td colspan="2" style="text-align:center;padding:0px 2px">\n        </td>\n       </tr>\n       <tr>\n        <td colspan="2" style="text-align:center; border-top:#aaa 1px solid;">\n         Part of the\n         <b>\n          <a href="/wiki/1933_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1933 Atlantic hurricane season">\n           1933 Atlantic hurricane season\n          </a>\n         </b>\n        </td>\n       </tr>\n       <tr style="display:none">\n        <td colspan="2">\n        </td>\n       </tr>\n      </table>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <b>\n        1933 Chesapeake\xe2\x80\x93Potomac hurricane\n       </b>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wpc_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-wpc-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       was among the most damaging hurricanes in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Mid-Atlantic_states" title="Mid-Atlantic states">\n        Mid-Atlantic states\n       </a>\n       in the eastern United States. The sixth storm and third hurricane of the very active\n       <a href="/wiki/1933_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1933 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1933 Atlantic hurricane season\n       </a>\n       , it formed in the eastern\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">\n        Atlantic\n       </a>\n       , where it moved west-northwestward and eventually became a\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_Category_4_Atlantic_hurricanes" title="List of Category 4 Atlantic hurricanes">\n        Category 4\n       </a>\n       on the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_hurricane_wind_scale" title="Saffir-Simpson hurricane wind scale">\n        Saffir-Simpson hurricane wind scale\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-4">\n         [nb 1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A strong\n       <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">\n        ridge\n       </a>\n       over\n       <a href="/wiki/New_England" title="New England">\n        New England\n       </a>\n       allowed a continued northwest course, bringing the storm south of Bermuda and later toward the middle coast of the eastern United States. Advance warning allowed hundreds of people to evacuate ahead of the hurricane making\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">\n        landfall\n       </a>\n       . It did so in northeastern North Carolina on August\xc2\xa023 with winds of about 90\xc2\xa0mph (150\xc2\xa0km/h). Soon after, the\n       <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">\n        eye\n       </a>\n       crossed over\n       <a href="/wiki/Norfolk,_Virginia" title="Norfolk, Virginia">\n        Norfolk, Virginia\n       </a>\n       , the first time that happened since\n       <a href="/wiki/1821_Norfolk_and_Long_Island_hurricane" title="1821 Norfolk and Long Island hurricane">\n        1821\n       </a>\n       . The hurricane weakened into a tropical storm over northern Virginia shortly before passing near\n       <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">\n        Washington, D.C.\n       </a>\n       , becoming the worst tropical cyclone there since\n       <a href="/wiki/1896_Atlantic_hurricane_season#Hurricane_Four" title="1896 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1896\n       </a>\n       . Curving northward, the storm moved through Pennsylvania and New York before losing tropical characteristics on August\xc2\xa025. Now\n       <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">\n        extratropical\n       </a>\n       , the former hurricane moved across\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Canada" title="Atlantic Canada">\n        Atlantic Canada\n       </a>\n       , dissipating on August\xc2\xa028.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Across the eastern United States, the hurricane left widespread damage amounting to over $40\xc2\xa0million (equivalent to about $5.2\xc2\xa0billion in 2016) and causing 47\xc2\xa0deaths.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-5">\n         [nb 2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Although the storm struck North Carolina, damage in the state totaled only about $250,000, largely to crops and transport. Along the\n       <a href="/wiki/Chesapeake_Bay" title="Chesapeake Bay">\n        Chesapeake Bay\n       </a>\n       , the storm produced\n       <a href="/wiki/Return_period" title="Return period">\n        100-year flooding\n       </a>\n       from its\n       <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">\n        storm surge\n       </a>\n       , setting records that remained for over 80\xc2\xa0years. In Virginia, flooding covered downtown portions of Norfolk in the southeast and\n       <a href="/wiki/Alexandria,_Virginia" title="Alexandria, Virginia">\n        Alexandria\n       </a>\n       in the north. Damage in the state was estimated at $17.5\xc2\xa0million. Similarly heavy damage occurred in Maryland, including over $7\xc2\xa0million to crops. High waves along the coast eroded beaches and created a new inlet at\n       <a href="/wiki/Ocean_City,_Maryland" title="Ocean City, Maryland">\n        Ocean City\n       </a>\n       . The highest rainfall associated with the hurricane was 13.28\xc2\xa0in (337\xc2\xa0mm) at\n       <a href="/wiki/York,_Pennsylvania" title="York, Pennsylvania">\n        York, Pennsylvania\n       </a>\n       . In the state, the rains flooded several rivers which forced thousands to evacuate. In neighboring New Jersey, high waves wrecked boats and destroyed a fishing pier, while in New York, flooding caused traffic jams. In Atlantic Canada, heavy rainfall assisted firefighters in combating wildfires, and the associated winds caused isolated power outages.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc"    </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       On August\xc2\xa013, a tropical depression developed near the west coast of Africa, southeast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Cape_Verde" title="Cape Verde">\n        Cape Verde\n       </a>\n       , with an associated\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone#Physical_structure" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        closed circulation\n       </a>\n       . Based on observations from a nearby ship, it is estimated the depression quickly intensified into a tropical storm while moving generally to the west-northwest. For several days the storm continued this general trajectory, eventually shifting more to the northwest. Based on interpolation of reports, it is estimated the storm intensified into a hurricane on August\xc2\xa016 while halfway between Africa and the\n       <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">\n        Lesser Antilles\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-meta_6-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-meta-6">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Weather_Service" title="National Weather Service">\n        Weather Bureau\n       </a>\n       began tracking the system on August\xc2\xa018.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_7-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-7">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Later that day, a ship reported a\n       <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_pressure" title="Atmospheric pressure">\n        pressure\n       </a>\n       of 948\xc2\xa0mbar (28.0\xc2\xa0inHg), suggesting winds of 135\xc2\xa0mph (215\xc2\xa0km/h). This made the hurricane a\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_Category_4_Atlantic_hurricanes" title="List of Category 4 Atlantic hurricanes">\n        Category 4\n       </a>\n       on the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_hurricane_wind_scale" title="Saffir-Simpson hurricane wind scale">\n        Saffir-Simpson hurricane wind scale\n       </a>\n       . Another ship report on August\xc2\xa020 indicated a pressure of 940\xc2\xa0mbar (28\xc2\xa0inHg) while reporting hurricane winds, suggesting peak winds of 140\xc2\xa0mph (230\xc2\xa0km/h).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-meta_6-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-meta-6">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       After remaining near peak intensity for three days, the hurricane began steadily weakening while curving more to the west-northwest,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-meta_6-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-meta-6">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       a rare track for the region due to steering from a\n       <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">\n        ridge\n       </a>\n       over\n       <a href="/wiki/New_England" title="New England">\n        New England\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rick_8-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rick-8">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On August\xc2\xa021, the storm passed about 150\xc2\xa0mi (240\xc2\xa0km) southwest of\n       <a href="/wiki/Bermuda" title="Bermuda">\n        Bermuda\n       </a>\n       . At 1000\n       <a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">\n        UTC\n       </a>\n       on August\xc2\xa023, the hurricane made\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">\n        landfall\n       </a>\n       along the northern\n       <a href="/wiki/Outer_Banks" title="Outer Banks">\n        Outer Banks\n       </a>\n       of\n       <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">\n        North Carolina\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-meta_6-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-meta-6">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       near\n       <a href="/wiki/Nags_Head,_North_Carolina" title="Nags Head, North Carolina">\n        Nags Head\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wpc_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-wpc-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       with a pressure of 963\xc2\xa0mbar (28.4\xc2\xa0inHg). Based on the steady weakening, coastal observations, and a larger than normal size, landfall winds were estimated at 90\xc2\xa0mph (150\xc2\xa0km/h). A few hours later, the hurricane made another landfall on the North Carolina mainland after crossing the\n       <a href="/wiki/Albemarle_Sound" title="Albemarle Sound">\n        Albemarle Sound\n       </a>\n       . While the hurricane continued to the northwest, the\n       <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">\n        eye\n       </a>\n       briefly moved over\n       <a href="/wiki/Norfolk,_Virginia" title="Norfolk, Virginia">\n        Norfolk, Virginia\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-meta_6-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-meta-6">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       for the first time since the\n       <a href="/wiki/1821_Norfolk_and_Long_Island_hurricane" title="1821 Norfolk and Long Island hurricane">\n        1821 Norfolk and Long Island hurricane\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wpc_1-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-wpc-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Early on August\xc2\xa024, the hurricane weakened into a tropical storm while passing near\n       <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">\n        Washington, D.C.\n       </a>\n       Curving to the north, the storm crossed Pennsylvania and into New York, where it weakened further into a tropical depression. On August\xc2\xa025, the former hurricane turned to the east near the Canada\xe2\x80\x93US border, and after interacting with a\n       <a href="/wiki/Cold_front" title="Cold front">\n        cold front\n       </a>\n       , became\n       <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">\n        extratropical\n       </a>\n       . It emerged from Maine and briefly re-intensified, moving across southern\n       <a href="/wiki/Nova_Scotia" title="Nova Scotia">\n        Nova Scotia\n       </a>\n       with gale force winds. On August\xc2\xa028, the circulation dissipated to the south of\n       <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_(island)" title="Newfoundland (island)">\n        Newfoundland\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-meta_6-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-meta-6">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations_and_impact">\n        Preparations and impact\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=1933_Chesapeake%E2%80%93Potomac_hurricane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations and impact">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:NOAA33trk.JPG">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2002" data-file-width="1453" height="303" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5f/NOAA33trk.JPG/220px-NOAA33trk.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5f/NOAA33trk.JPG/330px-NOAA33trk.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5f/NOAA33trk.JPG/440px-NOAA33trk.JPG 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:NOAA33trk.JPG" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Track of the 1933 Hurricane across\n         <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">\n          Virginia\n         </a>\n         and\n         <a href="/wiki/Maryland" title="Maryland">\n          Maryland\n         </a>\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The hurricane first affected Bermuda when it passed to the south. Winds at\n       <a href="/wiki/St._George%27s,_Bermuda" title="St. George\'s, Bermuda">\n        St. George\'s\n       </a>\n       reached 64\xc2\xa0mph (103\xc2\xa0km/h).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_7-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-7">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       High waves along the coast prevented boats from docking.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-9">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Early on August\xc2\xa021, the Weather Bureau issued\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">\n        storm warnings\n       </a>\n       from\n       <a href="/wiki/Cape_Hatteras" title="Cape Hatteras">\n        Cape Hatteras\n       </a>\n       to\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Boston,_Massachusetts" title="Boston, Massachusetts">\n        Boston, Massachusetts\n       </a>\n       . As the hurricane approached land, the warnings were extended southward to\n       <a href="/wiki/Southport,_North_Carolina" title="Southport, North Carolina">\n        Southport, North Carolina\n       </a>\n       . In\n       <a href="/wiki/Norfolk,_Virginia" title="Norfolk, Virginia">\n        Norfolk, Virginia\n       </a>\n       , most ships either remained at port or sought shelter due to advance warning.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_7-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-7">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Residents were advised to evacuate in\n       <a href="/wiki/Ocean_View_(Norfolk)" title="Ocean View (Norfolk)">\n        Ocean View\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Virginia_Beach,_Virginia" title="Virginia Beach, Virginia">\n        Virginia Beach\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Willoughby_Spit" title="Willoughby Spit">\n        Willoughby Spit\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-erh_10-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-erh-10">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and about 350\xc2\xa0people left their houses in Virginia Beach.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lewis_11-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-lewis-11">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Along the coast, the\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">\n        United States Coast Guard\n       </a>\n       rescued more than 200\xc2\xa0people, many of whom due to capsized boats.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rick_8-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rick-8">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A 1993 article published in the\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Monthly_Weather_Review" title="Monthly Weather Review">\n         Monthly Weather Review\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       considered the storm to have been "one of the most severe storms that has ever visited the Middle Atlantic coast."\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_7-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-7">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Flooding affected a larger portion of the northeastern United States than any other hurricane in the 1900s after\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Agnes" title="Hurricane Agnes">\n        Hurricane Agnes\n       </a>\n       in 1972.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rick_8-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rick-8">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The\n       <a href="/wiki/Federal_Emergency_Management_Agency" title="Federal Emergency Management Agency">\n        Federal Emergency Management Agency\n       </a>\n       later used high water levels from the storm as a\n       <a href="/wiki/Return_period" title="Return period">\n        100-year event\n       </a>\n       for the\n       <a href="/wiki/Chesapeake_Bay" title="Chesapeake Bay">\n        Chesapeake Bay\n       </a>\n       region. The next storm to approach the storm surge levels was\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Isabel" title="Hurricane Isabel">\n        Isabel\n       </a>\n       in 2003, which was lower in most locations in Virginia,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-12">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       although higher in Washington, D.C. and Baltimore due to sea level rise.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-13">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       This hurricane caused damage from North Carolina through New Jersey, due to the combination of high winds and storm tides,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_7-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-7">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and left 47\xc2\xa0people dead overall.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rick_8-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rick-8">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="North_Carolina_and_Virginia">\n        North Carolina and Virginia\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=1933_Chesapeake%E2%80%93Potomac_hurricane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: North Carolina and Virginia">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       When the hurricane made landfall in the Outer Banks, it produced winds of 76\xc2\xa0mph (122\xc2\xa0km/h) at Cape Hatteras.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-meta_6-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-meta-6">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The winds caused crop damage as far inland as\n       <a href="/wiki/Granville_County,_North_Carolina" title="Granville County, North Carolina">\n        Granville County\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nc_14-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nc-14">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The four-masted\n       <a href="/wiki/Schooner" title="Schooner">\n        schooner\n       </a>\n       GA Kohler hailing from Baltimore and bound for Haiti was driven ashore at Hatteras by the high winds. Across the region, the storm downed power lines, washed out highways, wrecked boats, and destroyed houses, and overall damage in the state was around $250,000.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdc_15-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ncdc-15">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       As the hurricane moved into Virginia, winds were as strong as 82\xc2\xa0mph (131\xc2\xa0km/h) at\n       <a href="/wiki/Cape_Henry" title="Cape Henry">\n        Cape Henry\n       </a>\n       , while Norfolk reported winds of 70\xc2\xa0mph (113\xc2\xa0km/h).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-meta_6-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-meta-6">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The latter city reported a record high tide of 9.8\xc2\xa0ft (3.0\xc2\xa0m) above normal at\n       <a href="/wiki/Sewell%27s_Point" title="Sewell\'s Point">\n        Sewell\'s Point\n       </a>\n       , which flooded the downtown section with 5\xc2\xa0ft (1.5\xc2\xa0m) of water. Water levels were 5 to 8\xc2\xa0ft (1.5 to 2.4\xc2\xa0m) higher than any previous\n       <a href="/wiki/High_water_mark" title="High water mark">\n        high water mark\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/Newport_News,_Virginia" title="Newport News, Virginia">\n        Newport News\n       </a>\n       and most of\n       <a href="/wiki/Fort_Eustis" title="Fort Eustis">\n        Fort Eustis\n       </a>\n       was flooded. In\n       <a href="/wiki/Hampton,_Virginia" title="Hampton, Virginia">\n        Hampton\n       </a>\n       the storm surge flooded\n       <a href="/wiki/Langley_Air_Force_Base" title="Langley Air Force Base">\n        Langley Air Force Base\n       </a>\n       , swamped homes and businesses, wrecked boats, and destroyed fishing piers. Rainfall in\n       <a href="/wiki/Chesapeake,_Virginia" title="Chesapeake, Virginia">\n        Chesapeake\n       </a>\n       reached about 10\xc2\xa0in (250\xc2\xa0mm),\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wpc_1-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-wpc-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and reached about 7\xc2\xa0in (180\xc2\xa0mm) in Norfolk.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_7-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-7">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Flooding near Norfolk damaged crops,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wpc_1-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-wpc-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and after its downtown section was flooded, residents were forced to travel by boat. When the flood levels dropped, many fish were left behind in the streets.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rick_8-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rick-8">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       High water levels of around 4\xc2\xa0ft (1.2\xc2\xa0m) along the\n       <a href="/wiki/York_River_(Virginia)" title="York River (Virginia)">\n        York River\n       </a>\n       destroyed buildings at\n       <a href="/wiki/Gloucester_Point,_Virginia" title="Gloucester Point, Virginia">\n        Gloucester Point\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wpc_1-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-wpc-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In Virginia Beach, the storm knocked down about 600\xc2\xa0trees, many of them about 100\xc2\xa0years old, and over 79,000\xc2\xa0people lost telephone service.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tomacco_16-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tomacco-16">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Due to advance warning, there was minimal damage to shipping in the region.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_7-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-7">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       However, high waves damaged the steamer\n       <i>\n        Madison\n       </i>\n       with 90\xc2\xa0people on board and caused it to drift off Cape Charles, which necessitated rescue from the Coast Guard.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lewis_11-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-lewis-11">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Inland flooding occurred along the\n       <a href="/wiki/James_River" title="James River">\n        James River\n       </a>\n       as far west as\n       <a href="/wiki/Richmond,_Virginia" title="Richmond, Virginia">\n        Richmond\n       </a>\n       , where damage was limited to downed trees and broken windows. The pier of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Jamestown_Ferry" title="Jamestown Ferry">\n        Jamestown Ferry\n       </a>\n       was washed out in\n       <a href="/wiki/Surry,_Virginia" title="Surry, Virginia">\n        Surry\n       </a>\n       and a marina at\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jordan_Point" title="Jordan Point">\n        Jordan Point\n       </a>\n       near\n       <a href="/wiki/Hopewell,_Virginia" title="Hopewell, Virginia">\n        Hopewell\n       </a>\n       was wrecked after the river reached the highest level on record. A powerful storm surge moved up the Chesapeake Bay and flooded waterfront locations. At\n       <a href="/wiki/Colonial_Beach,_Virginia" title="Colonial Beach, Virginia">\n        Colonial Beach\n       </a>\n       along the\n       <a href="/wiki/Potomac_River" title="Potomac River">\n        Potomac River\n       </a>\n       , the surge flooded the town with 4\xc2\xa0ft (1.2\xc2\xa0m) of water and wrecked a local amusement park. The Potomac in\n       <a href="/wiki/Alexandria,_Virginia" title="Alexandria, Virginia">\n        Alexandria\n       </a>\n       was at its highest level since 1899, causing floods 8\xc2\xa0ft (2.4\xc2\xa0m) deep along\n       <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_1_in_Virginia" title="U.S. Route 1 in Virginia">\n        U.S. Route 1\n       </a>\n       , and flooding the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Old_Town_Alexandria" title="Old Town Alexandria">\n        Old Town section\n       </a>\n       . High winds in the city caused power outages, and flooding along the\n       <a href="/wiki/Cameron_Run" title="Cameron Run">\n        Cameron Run\n       </a>\n       washed out a bridge. The combination of rain and winds damaged crops in\n       <a href="/wiki/Fairfax_County,_Virginia" title="Fairfax County, Virginia">\n        Fairfax\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Loudoun_County,_Virginia" title="Loudoun County, Virginia">\n        Loudoun\n       </a>\n       counties, mainly to corn and peaches.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wpc_1-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-wpc-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Statewide, the storm caused $5.25\xc2\xa0million in crop damage, largely to corn and tobacco.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tomacco_16-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tomacco-16">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Damage in the state was estimated at $17.5\xc2\xa0million, and there were 15\xc2\xa0deaths.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdc_15-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ncdc-15">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       After the storm, about 350\xc2\xa0people helped clear debris from the streets of Norfolk.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eve_17-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-eve-17">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Maryland_north">\n        Maryland north\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=1933_Chesapeake%E2%80%93Potomac_hurricane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Maryland north">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Farther north, it is estimated the storm produced hurricane-force winds in portions of Maryland.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-meta_6-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-meta-6">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Along the coast, high waves eroded about 2\xc2\xa0sq\xc2\xa0mi (5.2\xc2\xa0km\n       <sup>\n        2\n       </sup>\n       ) of beaches in Maryland and another 1\xc2\xa0sq\xc2\xa0mi (2.6\xc2\xa0km\n       <sup>\n        2\n       </sup>\n       ) in Delaware.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_7-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-7">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       High waves created an inlet that turned\n       <a href="/wiki/Assateague_Island" title="Assateague Island">\n        Assateague\n       </a>\n       into an island. The storm damaged or destroyed several hotels in the region.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wpc_1-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-wpc-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Power, telephone, and telegraph outages occurred from\n       <a href="/wiki/Cape_Charles_(headland)" title="Cape Charles (headland)">\n        Cape Charles\n       </a>\n       through Delaware.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_7-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-7">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baltimore,_Maryland" title="Baltimore, Maryland">\n        Baltimore\n       </a>\n       , 7.62\xc2\xa0in (194\xc2\xa0mm) of precipitation fell in 24\xc2\xa0hours, setting a record and causing flooding.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdc_15-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ncdc-15">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The storm surge wrecked a steamboat pier and promenade at\n       <a href="/wiki/Chesapeake_Beach,_Maryland" title="Chesapeake Beach, Maryland">\n        Chesapeake Beach\n       </a>\n       , and damaged or destroyed 70\xc2\xa0houses in neighboring\n       <a href="/wiki/North_Beach,_Maryland" title="North Beach, Maryland">\n        North Beach\n       </a>\n       . In\n       <a href="/wiki/Cheverly,_Maryland" title="Cheverly, Maryland">\n        Cheverly\n       </a>\n       , flooding caused nine cars of a train to derail,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rick_8-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rick-8">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       killing four people.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eve_17-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-eve-17">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Statewide, the storm caused about $7\xc2\xa0million in crop damage, mostly to tobacco, tomato, and corn. The high tides caused $3\xc2\xa0million in damage to the fishing industry, having damaged or destroyed many boats and docks. There was about $90,000 worth of damage to the\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Naval_Academy" title="United States Naval Academy">\n        United States Naval Academy\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/Annapolis,_Maryland" title="Annapolis, Maryland">\n        Annapolis\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_7-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-7">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       after docks were damaged.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rick_8-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rick-8">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Throughout the state, the hurricane caused about $10\xc2\xa0million in property damage, with another $1.1\xc2\xa0million to federal properties, about $960,000 in transportation damage, and $364,000 in utility damage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdc_15-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ncdc-15">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Aug1933kocinrain.gif">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="529" data-file-width="686" height="170" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/Aug1933kocinrain.gif/220px-Aug1933kocinrain.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/Aug1933kocinrain.gif/330px-Aug1933kocinrain.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/Aug1933kocinrain.gif/440px-Aug1933kocinrain.gif 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Aug1933kocinrain.gif" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         1933 hurricane rainfall across the Northeast\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       In neighboring Delaware, road damage was about $150,000,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_7-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-7">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       after three bridges were wrecked along\n       <a href="/wiki/U.S._Route_113" title="U.S. Route 113">\n        DuPont Highway\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eve_17-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-eve-17">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Rainfall in the state reached 13.24\xc2\xa0in (336\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Bridgeville,_Delaware" title="Bridgeville, Delaware">\n        Bridgeville\n       </a>\n       . In Washington, D.C., the storm dropped 6.39\xc2\xa0in (162\xc2\xa0mm) of rainfall, at the time the highest on record for a single day total.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rick_8-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rick-8">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       High winds knocked down trees and destroyed the roofs of several houses. Along the\n       <a href="/wiki/Anacostia_River" title="Anacostia River">\n        Anacostia River\n       </a>\n       , flooding surpassed a seawall, and traffic was disrupted when the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Benning_Bridge" title="Benning Bridge">\n        Benning Bridge\n       </a>\n       was flooded with 2\xc2\xa0ft (0.61\xc2\xa0m) of water. The\n       <a href="/wiki/Washington-Hoover_Airport" title="Washington-Hoover Airport">\n        Washington-Hoover Airport\n       </a>\n       was also flooded.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wpc_1-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-wpc-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Damage in the nation\'s capital was the worst since a\n       <a href="/wiki/1896_Atlantic_hurricane_season#Hurricane_Four" title="1896 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        tropical storm in 1896\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lewis_11-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-lewis-11">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The highest rainfall associated with the hurricane was 13.28\xc2\xa0in (337\xc2\xa0mm) near\n       <a href="/wiki/York,_Pennsylvania" title="York, Pennsylvania">\n        York, Pennsylvania\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">\n        <a href="#cite_note-18">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On August\xc2\xa024, the minimum pressure in Philadelphia dropped to 996\xc2\xa0mbar (29.40\xc2\xa0inHg), which was the lowest on record in the month of August at the time. Wind gusts in the city reached 42\xc2\xa0mph (68\xc2\xa0km/h). In the surrounding area, winds and rainfall caused $1\xc2\xa0million in damage, mostly to crops and houses, and there were four deaths.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdc_15-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ncdc-15">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Rainfall caused the worst flooding in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Lehigh_Valley" title="Lehigh Valley">\n        Lehigh Valley\n       </a>\n       since 1902. In\n       <a href="/wiki/York_County,_Pennsylvania" title="York County, Pennsylvania">\n        York County\n       </a>\n       , floods destroyed 47\xc2\xa0bridges, while in\n       <a href="/wiki/York,_Pennsylvania" title="York, Pennsylvania">\n        York\n       </a>\n       proper, about 3,000\xc2\xa0people evacuated along the swollen\n       <a href="/wiki/Codorus_Creek" title="Codorus Creek">\n        Codorus Creek\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rick_8-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rick-8">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Hurricane-force winds potentially affected portions of Delaware and southern New Jersey. In\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_City,_New_Jersey" title="Atlantic City, New Jersey">\n        Atlantic City, New Jersey\n       </a>\n       , winds reached 76\xc2\xa0mph (122\xc2\xa0km/h) at a height of 171\xc2\xa0ft (52\xc2\xa0m), which is 67\xc2\xa0mph (107\xc2\xa0km/h) at sea-level.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-meta_6-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-meta-6">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At the time, this was the highest wind report for the station in the month of August.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdc_15-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ncdc-15">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Rainfall in Atlantic City totaled 8.12\xc2\xa0in (206\xc2\xa0mm), including 2.25\xc2\xa0in (57\xc2\xa0mm) that fell in an hour on August\xc2\xa020,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rick_8-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rick-8">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       which was the monthly average. Two people drowned along the Jersey Shore due to high waves,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">\n        <a href="#cite_note-19">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and the storm capsized nine boats.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap822_20-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ap822-20">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The waves destroyed a 300\xc2\xa0ft (91\xc2\xa0m) long fishing pier in\n       <a href="/wiki/Cape_May,_New_Jersey" title="Cape May, New Jersey">\n        Cape May\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lewis_11-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-lewis-11">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       High winds damaged the boardwalk while streets were flooded in Atlantic City, and there was about $3\xc2\xa0million in damage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdc_15-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ncdc-15">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The storm spawned a tornado in\n       <a href="/wiki/Wildwood,_New_Jersey" title="Wildwood, New Jersey">\n        Wildwood\n       </a>\n       . At\n       <a href="/wiki/Picatinny_Arsenal" title="Picatinny Arsenal">\n        Picatinny Arsenal\n       </a>\n       , residents and members of the military helped prevent a dam from breaking.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rick_8-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rick-8">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Sustained winds of around 35\xc2\xa0mph (55\xc2\xa0km/h) were observed across northern New York,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-meta_6-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-meta-6">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       while the top of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Empire_State_Building" title="Empire State Building">\n        Empire State Building\n       </a>\n       reported a gust of 90\xc2\xa0mph (140\xc2\xa0km/h).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eve_17-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-eve-17">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Heavy rainfall in the state increased water levels along streams, causing one person to drown at\n       <a href="/wiki/Mount_Tremper" title="Mount Tremper">\n        Mount Tremper\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdc_15-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ncdc-15">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       After a dam broke, 190\xc2\xa0people at\n       <a href="/wiki/Deerpark,_New_York" title="Deerpark, New York">\n        Godeffroy\n       </a>\n       were stranded until they were rescued by a fire crew.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">\n        <a href="#cite_note-21">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A power outage during the storm caused the\n       <a href="/wiki/Statue_of_Liberty" title="Statue of Liberty">\n        Statue of Liberty\n       </a>\n       torch to extinguish for the first time since 1929.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rick_8-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rick-8">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Streets and basements of New York City were flooded after the heavy rainfall.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap822_20-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ap822-20">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The combination of flooding and fallen trees caused heavy traffic jams.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-eve_17-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-eve-17">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The former hurricane passed near southern Quebec as a tropical depression, and later crossed southern Nova Scotia as an extratropical cyclone. Along\n       <a href="/wiki/Lake_Ontario" title="Lake Ontario">\n        Lake Ontario\n       </a>\n       , winds reached 51\xc2\xa0mph (81\xc2\xa0km/h), and in\n       <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">\n        Montreal\n       </a>\n       , winds reached 21\xc2\xa0mph (33\xc2\xa0km/h). After a dry summer, the storm\'s accompanying rainfall was beneficial, reaching 4.4\xc2\xa0in (112\xc2\xa0mm) in Fredericton, New Brunswick and 2\xc2\xa0in (50\xc2\xa0mm) in Halifax, Nova Scotia. The rains helped farmers in Ontario and assisted firefighters in New Brunswick and Nova Scotia. In Montreal, 2.4\xc2\xa0in (60\xc2\xa0mm) of rainfall flooded a tunnel. Gusty winds knocked down power lines in portions of Quebec, Nova Scotia, and Nova Scotia. Overall effects were minor, limited to some utility damage and delays for shipping.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">\n        <a href="#cite_note-22">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n
