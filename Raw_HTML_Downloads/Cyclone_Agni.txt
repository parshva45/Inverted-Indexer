Cyclonic Storm Agni\n       </b>\n       was a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        tropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       of the\n       <a href="/wiki/2004_North_Indian_Ocean_cyclone_season" title="2004 North Indian Ocean cyclone season">\n        2004 North Indian Ocean cyclone season\n       </a>\n       notable for its record proximity to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">\n        equator\n       </a>\n       . It was the second North Indian Ocean cyclone to receive a name, after Onil earlier in the year. Agni formed on November\xc2\xa028 well to the southwest of India in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Sea" title="Arabian Sea">\n        Arabian Sea\n       </a>\n       , and steadily intensified as it tracked northwestward. The\n       <a href="/wiki/Joint_Typhoon_Warning_Center" title="Joint Typhoon Warning Center">\n        Joint Typhoon Warning Center\n       </a>\n       (JTWC) estimated peak 1\xc2\xa0minute\n       <a href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_wind" title="Maximum sustained wind">\n        sustained winds\n       </a>\n       of 120\xc2\xa0km/h (75\xc2\xa0mph), while the\n       <a href="/wiki/India_Meteorological_Department" title="India Meteorological Department">\n        India Meteorological Department\n       </a>\n       (IMD) estimated peak 3\xc2\xa0minute sustained winds of 100\xc2\xa0km/h (65\xc2\xa0mph); the IMD is the official\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Regional_Specialized_Meteorological_Centre" title="Regional Specialized Meteorological Centre">\n        warning center\n       </a>\n       for the north\n       <a href="/wiki/Indian_Ocean" title="Indian Ocean">\n        Indian Ocean\n       </a>\n       . After peaking, it weakened due to\n       <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">\n        wind shear\n       </a>\n       , dry air, and cooler waters, and the JTWC issued its final advisory on December\xc2\xa03 as it approached the coast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Somalia" title="Somalia">\n        Somalia\n       </a>\n       . The remnants of Agni moved along the Somalian coastline until dissipating on December\xc2\xa05.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="toc">\n       <div class="toctitle">\n    </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Meteorological_history">\n        Meteorological history\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cyclone_Agni&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Agni_2004_track.png">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Agni_2004_track.png/220px-Agni_2004_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Agni_2004_track.png/330px-Agni_2004_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Agni_2004_track.png/440px-Agni_2004_track.png 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Agni_2004_track.png" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Map plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the\n         <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">\n          Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale\n         </a>\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       A tropical disturbance was observed on November\xc2\xa019 about 800\xc2\xa0km (500\xc2\xa0mi) southeast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Colombo" title="Colombo">\n        Colombo\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Sri_Lanka" title="Sri Lanka">\n        Sri Lanka\n       </a>\n       in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Bengal" title="Bay of Bengal">\n        Bay of Bengal\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mmm_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mmm-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The disturbance tracked westward, gradually organizing and prompting the JTWC to issue a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_Cyclone_Formation_Alert" title="Tropical Cyclone Formation Alert">\n        Tropical Cyclone Formation Alert\n       </a>\n       (TCFA) on November\xc2\xa022. After passing south of Sri Lanka, it became disorganized and was no longer considered likely to\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclogenesis" title="Tropical cyclogenesis">\n        develop\n       </a>\n       into a tropical cyclone.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gp_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-gp-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The\n       <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_circulation" title="Atmospheric circulation">\n        circulation\n       </a>\n       associated with the system continued westward, reorganizing on November\xc2\xa026 in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Sea" title="Arabian Sea">\n        Arabian Sea\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mmm_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mmm-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Despite being located unusually close to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">\n        equator\n       </a>\n       , the disturbance maintained\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Convection_(meteorology)" title="Convection (meteorology)">\n        convection\n       </a>\n       , or thunderstorms, which was becoming organized around the weak low-level circulation. With low\n       <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">\n        wind shear\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Deformation_(meteorology)" title="Deformation (meteorology)">\n        diffluence\n       </a>\n       aloft, the JTWC remarked the system had a fair chance of developing.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       While the system was organizing, the center crossed the equator to reach about 0.5\xc2\xb0\n       <a href="/wiki/Latitude" title="Latitude">\n        S\n       </a>\n       , thus becoming an\n       <a href="/wiki/Anticyclone" title="Anticyclone">\n        anticyclonic\n       </a>\n       circulation in the southern hemisphere. This was unusual, as the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coriolis_effect" title="Coriolis effect">\n        Coriolis effect\n       </a>\n       is nonexistent along the equator\xe2\x80\x94the Coriolis effect refers to planetary\n       <a href="/wiki/Vorticity" title="Vorticity">\n        vorticity\n       </a>\n       , which provides the spin in a cyclone.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mmm_1-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mmm-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wmo_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-wmo-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The JTWC later assessed the system as remaining in the northern hemisphere, reaching as far south as 0.7\xc2\xb0\xc2\xa0N, or about 80\xc2\xa0km (50\xc2\xa0mi) from the equator.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jtwc_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-jtwc-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       After the circulation organized further beneath the convection, the JTWC issued another TCFA at 0300\n       <a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">\n        UTC\n       </a>\n       on November\xc2\xa028.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Three hours later, the agency classified the system as Tropical Cyclone 05A about 1300\xc2\xa0km (800\xc2\xa0mi) southwest of the southern tip of India.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In a post-season analysis, the JTWC estimated the cyclone became a tropical storm six hours earlier.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jtwc_5-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-jtwc-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At around the same time, the IMD classified it as a "low pressure area... likely to become well marked."\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Upon being classified, the tropical cyclone was moving northwestward under the influence of a\n       <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">\n        ridge\n       </a>\n       over India. Its\n       <a href="/wiki/Outflow_(meteorology)" title="Outflow (meteorology)">\n        outflow\n       </a>\n       to the north and south became better defined, although its proximity to the equator limited its southerly outflow somewhat due to\n       <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">\n        wind shear\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Early on November\xc2\xa029, the IMD classified the system as a depression,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and throughout the day it rapidly intensified to become a cyclonic storm; a cyclonic storm is a tropical cyclone with at least 65\xc2\xa0km/h (40\xc2\xa0mph)\n       <a href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_wind" title="Maximum sustained wind">\n        winds\n       </a>\n       sustained for 3\xc2\xa0minutes.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Around that time, the JTWC assessed the cyclone as reaching peak winds of 120\xc2\xa0km/h (75\xc2\xa0mph), sustained for 1\xc2\xa0minute, along with gusts to 150\xc2\xa0km/h (90\xc2\xa0mph). Late on November\xc2\xa029, the cyclone weakened slightly, only to regain its peak intensity by November\xc2\xa030;\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jtwc_5-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-jtwc-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       at that time, it developed a\n       <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">\n        banding eye\n       </a>\n       , and was located about 1500\xc2\xa0km (900\xc2\xa0mi) southeast of the coast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Somalia" title="Somalia">\n        Somalia\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At 1200\xc2\xa0UTC on November\xc2\xa030, the IMD issued its first complete advisory on the cyclone, naming it Severe Cyclonic Storm Agni and estimating peak winds at 100\xc2\xa0km/h (65\xc2\xa0mph); the agency predicted the storm would continue northwestward and strengthen slightly before weakening.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-imd1_13-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-imd1-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At its peak, the cyclone had a\n       <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_pressure" title="Atmospheric pressure">\n        barometric pressure\n       </a>\n       of 985\n       <a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">\n        mbar\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bt_14-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-bt-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Tropical_Cyclone_Agni_05A_2004.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="6108" data-file-width="8144" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/44/Tropical_Cyclone_Agni_05A_2004.jpg/220px-Tropical_Cyclone_Agni_05A_2004.jpg"      <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Tropical_Cyclone_Agni_05A_2004.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Cyclone Agni near peak intensity\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       After reaching peak intensity, an increase in wind shear caused the eye to disappear,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">\n        <a href="#cite_note-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and the convection decreased significantly.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">\n        <a href="#cite_note-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On December\xc2\xa01, the IMD estimated Agni weakened to cyclonic storm status,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">\n        <a href="#cite_note-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       around which time the center became exposed from the convection.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">\n        <a href="#cite_note-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The cyclone continued its steady weakening continued due to the wind shear, the presence of dry air, and cooler\n       <a href="/wiki/Sea_surface_temperature" title="Sea surface temperature">\n        water temperatures\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">\n        <a href="#cite_note-19">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and by December\xc2\xa02, the IMD downgraded Agni to depression status, which was its final warning on the system.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">\n        <a href="#cite_note-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       As it approached the coast of Somalia, it turned westward due to the building of a ridge over\n       <a href="/wiki/Saudi_Arabia" title="Saudi Arabia">\n        Saudi Arabia\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">\n        <a href="#cite_note-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Despite unfavorable conditions, thunderstorms briefly reformed over the circulation, which allowed the storm to maintain its intensity.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">\n        <a href="#cite_note-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       By late on December\xc2\xa03, Agni weakened to below tropical storm status, and the JTWC issued its final warning while the storm was about 450\xc2\xa0km (275\xc2\xa0mi) south-southeast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Cape_Guardafui" title="Cape Guardafui">\n        Cape Guardafui\n       </a>\n       \xe2\x80\x94the easternmost point of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Horn_of_Africa" title="Horn of Africa">\n        Horn of Africa\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">\n        <a href="#cite_note-23">\n         [23]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The circulation became difficult to locate by early on December\xc2\xa04, by which time the system had little remaining convection.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">\n        <a href="#cite_note-24">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       It turned southwestward and later to the south, moving ashore on eastern Somalia before dissipating on December\xc2\xa05 near\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ceel_Huur" title="Ceel Huur">\n        Ceel Huur\n       </a>\n       over water.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jtwc_5-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-jtwc-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span id="Records,_naming,_and_impact">\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Records.2C_naming.2C_and_impact">\n        Records, naming, and impact\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Cyclone_Agni&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Records, naming, and impact">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       According to RSMC New Delhi, Cyclone Agni developed into a depression at 1.5\xc2\xb0\xc2\xa0N, which is the same latitude as where according to RSMC Tokyo,\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_Storm_Vamei" title="Tropical Storm Vamei">\n        Tropical Storm Vamei\n       </a>\n       formed during December 2001.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">\n        <a href="#cite_note-25">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IMD_BT_26-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-IMD_BT-26">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       However, according to RSMC La Reunion, the precursor low to Cyclone Agni moved into the Southern Hemisphere and became an anticyclone as it had kept its counter-clockwise circulation.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27">\n        <a href="#cite_note-27">\n         [27]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Agni was the second storm in the north Indian Ocean to receive a name, after the IMD began\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_naming" title="Tropical cyclone naming">\n        tropical cyclone naming\n       </a>\n       in the middle of 2004.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-naming_28-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-naming-28">\n         [28]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       As the cyclone was weak when it moved over Somalia, no casualties or significant damage was reported.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jtwc_5-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-jtwc-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div style="clear:both;">\n      </div>\n      <h2>\n       <span
