</a>\n    </div>\n    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">\n     <div class="mw-parser-output">\n      <p>\n       The\n       <b>\n        Satellite Catalog Number\n       </b>\n       (also known as\n       <b>\n        NORAD Catalog Number\n       </b>\n       ,\n       <b>\n        NASA catalog number\n       </b>\n       ,\n       <b>\n        USSPACECOM object number\n       </b>\n       or simply\n       <b>\n        Catalog number\n       </b>\n       and similar variants) is a sequential 5-digit number assigned by USSPACECOM (\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Space_Command" title="United States Space Command">\n        United States Space Command\n       </a>\n       ) to all Earth orbiting\n       <a href="/wiki/Satellite" title="Satellite">\n        satellites\n       </a>\n       in order of identification. Before USSPACECOM, the catalog was maintained by\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NORAD" title="NORAD">\n        NORAD\n       </a>\n       . The first catalogued object, catalog number 1957-001A, is the final stage of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Sputnik_(rocket)" title="Sputnik (rocket)">\n        rocket\n       </a>\n       which launched\n       <a href="/wiki/Sputnik_1" title="Sputnik 1">\n        Sputnik 1\n       </a>\n       and remained in orbit for almost two months. (Sputnik itself is 1957-001B and lasted in orbit an additional month.)\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       As of August 2017\n       <sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;">\n        <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Satellite_Catalog_Number&amp;action=edit">\n         [update]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       , the\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Space_Science_Data_Center" title="National Space Science Data Center">\n        National Space Science Data Center\n       </a>\n       Master Catalog listed more than 42,900 tracked objects including more than 7,800 satellites launched into orbit since 1957.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n
