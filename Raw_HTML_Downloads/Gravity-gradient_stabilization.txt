class="firstHeading" id="firstHeading" lang="en">\n    Gravity-gradient stabilization\n   </h1>\n   <div class="mw-body-content" id="bodyContent">\n    <div class="noprint" id="siteSub">\n     From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia\n    </div>\n    <div id="contentSub">\n    </div>\n    <div class="mw-jump" id="jump-to-nav">\n     Jump to:\n     <a href="#mw-head">\n      navigation\n     </a>\n     ,\n     <a href="#p-search">\n      search\n     </a>\n    </div>\n    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">\n     <div class="mw-parser-output">\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        Gravity-gradient stabilization\n       </b>\n       (a.k.a. "tidal stabilization") is a method of stabilizing\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Artificial_satellite" title="Artificial satellite">\n        artificial satellites\n       </a>\n       or\n       <a href="/wiki/Space_tether" title="Space tether">\n        space tethers\n       </a>\n       in a fixed orientation using only the orbited body\'s mass distribution and gravitational field. The main advantage over using active stabilization with\n       <a href="/wiki/Propellant" title="Propellant">\n        propellants\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Gyroscope" title="Gyroscope">\n        gyroscopes\n       </a>\n       or\n       <a href="/wiki/Reaction_wheel" title="Reaction wheel">\n        reaction wheels\n       </a>\n       is the low use of power and resources.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The idea is to use the\n       <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">\n        Earth\n       </a>\n       \'s\n       <a href="/wiki/Gravitational_field" title="Gravitational field">\n        gravitational field\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tidal_forces" title="Tidal forces">\n        tidal forces\n       </a>\n       to keep the spacecraft aligned in the desired orientation. The\n       <a href="/wiki/Gravity" title="Gravity">\n        gravity\n       </a>\n       of the Earth decreases according to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Inverse-square_law" title="Inverse-square law">\n        inverse-square law\n       </a>\n       , and by extending the long axis perpendicular to the orbit, the "lower" part of the orbiting structure will be more attracted to the Earth. The effect is that the satellite will tend to align its axis of minimum\n       <a href="/wiki/Moment_of_inertia" title="Moment of inertia">\n        moment of inertia\n       </a>\n       vertically.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The first experimental attempt to use the technique on a human spaceflight was performed on September 13, 1966, on the US\n       <a href="/wiki/Gemini_11" title="Gemini 11">\n        Gemini 11\n       </a>\n       mission, by attaching the Gemini spacecraft to its\n       <a href="/wiki/Agena_target_vehicle" title="Agena target vehicle">\n        Agena target vehicle\n       </a>\n       by a 100 feet (30\xc2\xa0m) tether. The attempt was a failure, as insufficient gradient was produced to keep the tether taut.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gatland_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Gatland-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The technique was first successfully used in a near-\n       <a href="/wiki/Geosynchronous_orbit" title="Geosynchronous orbit">\n        geosynchronous orbit\n       </a>\n       on the\n       <a href="/wiki/DODGE_(satellite)" title="DODGE (satellite)">\n        Department of Defense Gravity Experiment\n       </a>\n       (DODGE) satellite in July 1967.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Guenter_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Guenter-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       It was first used for\n       <a href="/wiki/Low_Earth_orbit" title="Low Earth orbit">\n        low Earth orbit\n       </a>\n       and tested unsuccessfully for\n       <a href="/wiki/Geosynchronous_orbit" title="Geosynchronous orbit">\n        geosynchronous orbit\n       </a>\n       in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Applications_Technology_Satellite" title="Applications Technology Satellite">\n        Applications Technology Satellites\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/ATS-2" title="ATS-2">\n        ATS-2\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/ATS-4" title="ATS-4">\n        ATS-4\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=ATS-5&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="ATS-5 (page does not exist)">\n        ATS-5\n       </a>\n       from 1966 until 1969.\n       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">\n        [\n        <i>\n         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">\n          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2012)">\n           citation needed\n          </span>\n         </a>\n        </i>\n        ]\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <a href="/wiki/Long_Duration_Exposure_Facility" title="Long Duration Exposure Facility">\n        Long Duration Exposure Facility\n       </a>\n       (LDEF) used this method for 3-axis stabilization; yaw about the vertical axis was stabilized.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" style="white-space:nowrap;">\n        :7\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       An example of gravity-gradient stabilization was attempted during NASA\'s TSS-1 mission in July, 1992. The project failed because of tether deployment problems.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In 1996 another mission, TSS-1R, was attempted but failed when the tether broke. Just prior to tether separation, the tension on the tether was about 65 N (14.6\xc2\xa0lbs).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TSS-1R_Failure_Investigation_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TSS-1R_Failure_Investigation-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">\n        See also\n
