   Science Magazine\n        </b>\n       </i>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       is the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Peer-reviewed" title="Peer-reviewed">\n        peer-reviewed\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Academic_journal" title="Academic journal">\n        academic journal\n       </a>\n       of the\n       <a href="/wiki/American_Association_for_the_Advancement_of_Science" title="American Association for the Advancement of Science">\n        American Association for the Advancement of Science\n       </a>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       (AAAS) and one of the world\'s top academic journals.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lemonick_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Lemonick-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       It was first published in 1880, is currently circulated weekly and has a print subscriber base of around 130,000. Because institutional subscriptions and online access serve a larger audience, its estimated readership is 570,400 people.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The major focus of the journal is publishing important original scientific research and research reviews, but\n       <i>\n        Science\n       </i>\n       also publishes science-related news, opinions on\n       <a href="/wiki/Science_policy" title="Science policy">\n        science policy\n       </a>\n       and other matters of interest to scientists and others who are concerned with the wide implications of science and technology. Unlike most\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scientific_journals" title="Scientific journals">\n        scientific journals\n       </a>\n       , which focus on a specific field,\n       <i>\n        Science\n       </i>\n       and its rival\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Nature_(journal)" title="Nature (journal)">\n         Nature\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       cover the full range of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_academic_disciplines#Natural_sciences" title="List of academic disciplines">\n        scientific disciplines\n       </a>\n       . According to the\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Journal_Citation_Reports" title="Journal Citation Reports">\n         Journal Citation Reports\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       ,\n       <i>\n        Science\n       </i>\n       <span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">\n        \'\n       </span>\n       s 2015\n       <a href="/wiki/Impact_factor" title="Impact factor">\n        impact factor\n       </a>\n       was 34.661.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WoS_6-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-WoS-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Although it is the journal of the AAAS, membership in the AAAS is not required to publish in\n       <i>\n        Science\n       </i>\n       . Papers are accepted from authors around the world. Competition to publish in\n       <i>\n        Science\n       </i>\n       is very intense, as an article published in such a highly cited journal can lead to attention and career advancement for the authors. Fewer than 7% of articles submitted are accepted for publication.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <i>\n        Science\n       </i>\n       is based in Washington, D.C., United States, with a second office in\n       <a href="/wiki/Cambridge" title="Cambridge">\n        Cambridge\n       </a>\n       , England.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="toc">\n       <div Entomologist\n       <a href="/wiki/Samuel_Hubbard_Scudder" title="Samuel Hubbard Scudder">\n        Samuel H. Scudder\n       </a>\n       resurrected the journal one year later and had some success while covering the meetings of prominent American scientific societies, including the AAAS.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       However, by 1894,\n       <i>\n        Science\n       </i>\n       was again in financial difficulty and was sold to psychologist\n       <a href="/wiki/James_McKeen_Cattell" title="James McKeen Cattell">\n        James McKeen Cattell\n       </a>\n       for $500.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In an agreement worked out by Cattell and AAAS secretary\n       <a href="/wiki/Leland_Ossian_Howard" title="Leland Ossian Howard">\n        Leland O. Howard\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <i>\n        Science\n       </i>\n       became the journal of the American Association for the Advancement of Science in 1900.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       During the early part of the 20th century important articles published in\n       <i>\n        Science\n       </i>\n       included papers on\n       <a href="/wiki/Drosophila_melanogaster" title="Drosophila melanogaster">\n        fruit fly\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Genetics" title="Genetics">\n        genetics\n       </a>\n       by\n       <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Hunt_Morgan" title="Thomas Hunt Morgan">\n        Thomas Hunt Morgan\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Gravitational_lens" title="Gravitational lens">\n        gravitational lensing\n       </a>\n       by\n       <a href="/wiki/Albert_Einstein" title="Albert Einstein">\n        Albert Einstein\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spiral_nebulae" title="Spiral nebulae">\n        spiral nebulae\n       </a>\n       by\n       <a href="/wiki/Edwin_Hubble" title="Edwin Hubble">\n        Edwin Hubble\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       After Cattell died in 1944, the ownership of the journal was transferred to the AAAS.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       After Cattell\'s death in 1944, the journal lacked a consistent editorial presence until Graham DuShane became editor in 1956. In 1958, under DuShane\'s leadership,\n       <i>\n        Science\n       </i>\n       absorbed\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/The_Scientific_Monthly" title="The Scientific Monthly">\n         The Scientific Monthly\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       , thus increasing the journal\'s circulation by over 60% from 38,000 to more than 61,000.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-maturing3_11-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-maturing3-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Physicist\n       <a href="/wiki/Philip_Abelson" title="Philip Abelson">\n        Philip Abelson\n       </a>\n       , a co-discoverer of\n       <a href="/wiki/Neptunium" title="Neptunium">\n        neptunium\n       </a>\n       , served as editor from 1962 to 1984. Under Abelson the efficiency of the review process was improved and the publication practices were brought up to date.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-maturing3_11-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-maturing3-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       During this time, papers on the\n       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_program" title="Apollo program">\n        Apollo program\n       </a>\n       missions and some of the earliest reports on AIDS were published.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-change3_12-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-change3-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Biochemist\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daniel_E._Koshland,_Jr." title="Daniel E. Koshland, Jr.">\n        Daniel E. Koshland, Jr.\n       </a>\n       served as editor from 1985 until 1995. From 1995 until 2000, neuroscientist\n       <a href="/wiki/Floyd_E._Bloom" title="Floyd E. Bloom">\n        Floyd E. Bloom\n       </a>\n       held that position.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-change3_12-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-change3-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Biologist\n       <a href="/wiki/Donald_Kennedy" title="Donald Kennedy">\n        Donald Kennedy\n       </a>\n       became the editor of\n       <i>\n        Science\n       </i>\n       in 2000. Biochemist\n       <a href="/wiki/Bruce_Alberts" title="Bruce Alberts">\n        Bruce Alberts\n       </a>\n       took his place in March 2008.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Geophysicist\n       <a href="/wiki/Marcia_McNutt" title="Marcia McNutt">\n        Marcia McNutt\n       </a>\n       became editor-in-chief in June 2013.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">\n        <a href="#cite_note-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       During her tenure the family of journals expanded to include\n       <i>\n        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Science_Robotics&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Science Robotics (page does not exist)">\n         Science Robotics\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       and\n       <i>\n        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Science_Immunology&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Science Immunology (page does not exist)">\n         Science Immunology\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">\n        <a href="#cite_note-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Open_access" title="Open access">\n        open access\n       </a>\n       publishing with\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Science_Advances" title="Science Advances">\n         Science Advances\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">\n        <a href="#cite_note-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_M._Berg" title="Jeremy M. Berg">\n        Jeremy M. Berg\n       </a>\n       became editor-in-chief on July 1, 2016.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">\n        <a href="#cite_note-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In February 2001, draft results of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Human_genome" title="Human genome">\n        human genome\n       </a>\n       were simultaneously published by\n       <i>\n        Nature\n       </i>\n       and\n       <i>\n        Science\n       </i>\n       with\n       <i>\n        Science\n       </i>\n       publishing the\n       <a href="/wiki/Celera_Corporation" title="Celera Corporation">\n        Celera Genomics\n       </a>\n       paper and\n       <i>\n        Nature\n       </i>\n       publishing the publicly funded\n       <a href="/wiki/Human_Genome_Project" title="Human Genome Project">\n        Human Genome Project\n       </a>\n       . In 2007\n       <i>\n        Science\n       </i>\n       (together with\n       <a href="/wiki/Nature_(journal)" title="Nature (journal)">\n        <i>\n         Nature\n        </i>\n       </a>\n       ) received the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Prince_of_Asturias_Awards" title="Prince of Asturias Awards">\n        Prince of Asturias Award\n       </a>\n       for Communications and Humanity.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">\n        <a href="#cite_note-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In 2015\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rush_D._Holt,_Jr." title="Rush D. Holt, Jr.">\n        Rush D. Holt, Jr.\n       </a>\n       , chief executive officer of the AAAS and executive publisher of\n       <i>\n        Science\n       </i>\n       , stated that the journal was becoming increasingly international: "[I]nternationally co-authored papers are now the norm\xe2\x80\x94they represent almost 60 percent of the papers. In 1992, it was slightly less than 20 percent."\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Science_and_Diplomacy._19-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Science_and_Diplomacy.-19">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Family_of_journals">\n        Family of journals\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Science_(journal)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Family of journals">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       The Science family of journals includes\n       <i>\n        Science\n       </i>\n       ,\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Science_Translational_Medicine" title="Science Translational Medicine">\n         Science Translational Medicine\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       ,\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Science_Signaling" title="Science Signaling">\n         Science Signaling\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       , and\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Science_Advances" title="Science Advances">\n         Science Advances\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       . In 2015, Holt announced another expansion:\n       <i>\n        Science Robotics\n       </i>\n       and\n       <i>\n        Science Immunology\n       </i>\n       would begin publication in mid-2016.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">\n        <a href="#cite_note-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Availability">\n        Availability\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Science_(journal)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Availability">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       The full journal is available online, through the main journal website, only to subscribers, AAAS members, and for delivery to\n       <a href="/wiki/IP_address" title="IP address">\n        IP addresses\n       </a>\n       at institutions that subscribe; students, K\xe2\x80\x9312 teachers, and some others can subscribe at a reduced fee. However, research articles published after 1997 are available free (with online registration) one year after they are published. Significant public-health related articles are also available for free, sometimes immediately after publication. AAAS members may also access the pre-1997 Science archives at the\n       <i>\n        Science\n       </i>\n       website, where it is called "Science Classic". Institutions can opt to add Science Classic to their subscriptions for an additional fee. Some older articles can also be accessed via\n       <a href="/wiki/JSTOR" title="JSTOR">\n        JSTOR\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/ProQuest" title="ProQuest">\n        ProQuest\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The journal also participates in initiatives that provide free or low-cost access to readers in developing countries, including\n       <a href="/wiki/HINARI" title="HINARI">\n        HINARI\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=OARE&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="OARE (page does not exist)">\n        OARE\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/AGORA" title="AGORA">\n        AGORA\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Scidev.net" title="Scidev.net">\n        Scidev.net\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Other features of the\n       <i>\n        Science\n       </i>\n       website include the free "ScienceNow" section with "up to the minute news from science",\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">\n        <a href="#cite_note-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and "ScienceCareers", which provides free career resources for scientists and engineers.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">\n        See also\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n
