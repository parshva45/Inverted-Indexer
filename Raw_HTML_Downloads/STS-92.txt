class="image" href="/wiki/File:STS-92_landing.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2400" data-file-width="3000" height="136" href="/wiki/File:ISSafterSTS92.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1920" data-file-width="2560" height="113" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/96/ISSafterSTS92.jpg/150px-ISSafterSTS92.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/96/ISSafterSTS92.jpg/225px-ISSafterSTS92.jpg 1.5x, <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:ISSafterSTS92.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Illustration of the ISS after STS-92.\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       STS-92 was an ISS assembly flight that brought the\n       <a href="/wiki/Integrated_Truss_Structure#Z1_truss" title="Integrated Truss Structure">\n        Z1 truss\n       </a>\n       , Control Moment Gyros,\n       <a href="/wiki/Pressurized_Mating_Adapter" title="Pressurized Mating Adapter">\n        Pressurized Mating Adapter\n       </a>\n       -3 (PMA-3) (mounted on a Spacelab pallet) and two DDCU (Heat pipes) to the space station.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The Z1 truss was the first exterior framework installed on the ISS and allowed the first U.S. solar arrays to be temporarily installed on\n       <i>\n        Unity\n       </i>\n       for early power during flight 4A. The Ku-band communication system supported early science capabilities and U.S. television on flight 6A. The CMGs (Control Moment Gyros) weigh about 27 kilograms (60\xc2\xa0lb) and provide non-propulsive (electrically powered) attitude control when activated on flight 5A, and PMA-3 provides shuttle docking port for solar array installation on flight 4A and Lab\n       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">\n        [\n        <i>\n         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify">\n          <span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (August 2012)">\n           clarification needed\n          </span>\n         </a>\n        </i>\n        ]\n       </sup>\n       installation on flight 5A.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The mission included seven days of docked operations with the space station, four EVAs, and two ingress opportunities.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Over the course of four scheduled spacewalks, two teams of space walkers and an experienced robot arm operator collaborated to install the Z1 (Z for zenith port) truss structure on top of the U.S. Unity connecting node on the growing station and to deliver the third Pressurized Mating Adapter (PMA 3) to the ISS for the future berthing of new station components and to accommodate shuttle dockings.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The Z1 truss was the first permanent lattice-work structure for the ISS, very much like a girder, setting the stage for the future addition of the station\'s major trusses or backbones. The Z1 fixture also served as the platform on which the huge U.S. solar arrays were mounted on the next shuttle assembly flight,\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-97" title="STS-97">\n        STS-97\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The Z1 contains four large gyroscopic devices, called\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Control_Moment_Gyroscope" title="Control Moment Gyroscope">\n        Control Moment Gyroscope\n       </a>\n       (CMGs), which are used to maneuver the space station into the proper orientation on orbit once they were activated following the installation of the U.S. laboratory.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       During the fourth spacewalk, astronauts Wisoff and L\xc3\xb3pez-Alegr\xc3\xada tested the\n       <a href="/wiki/Simplified_Aid_For_EVA_Rescue" title="Simplified Aid For EVA Rescue">\n        SAFER\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Jet_pack" title="Jet pack">\n        jet backpack\n       </a>\n       , flying up to 50 feet while remaining tethered to the spacecraft.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n
