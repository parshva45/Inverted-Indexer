  <p>\n       <b>\n        Hurricane Lenny\n       </b>\n       was the twelfth\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_storm" title="Tropical storm">\n        tropical storm\n       </a>\n       , eighth hurricane, and record-breaking fifth\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_Hurricane_Scale#Category_4" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson Hurricane Scale">\n        Category 4\xc2\xa0hurricane\n       </a>\n       in the\n       <a href="/wiki/1999_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1999 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1999 Atlantic hurricane season\n       </a>\n       . It is the second-strongest November\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_hurricane" title="Atlantic hurricane">\n        Atlantic hurricane\n       </a>\n       on record, behind the\n       <a href="/wiki/1932_Cuba_hurricane" title="1932 Cuba hurricane">\n        1932 Cuba hurricane\n       </a>\n       . Lenny formed on November\xc2\xa013 in the western\n       <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">\n        Caribbean Sea\n       </a>\n       and maintained an unprecedented west-to-east track for its entire duration. It attained hurricane status south of\n       <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">\n        Jamaica\n       </a>\n       on November 15 and passed south of\n       <a href="/wiki/Hispaniola" title="Hispaniola">\n        Hispaniola\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">\n        Puerto Rico\n       </a>\n       over the next few days. Lenny rapidly intensified over the northeastern Caribbean on November\xc2\xa017, attaining peak winds of 155\xc2\xa0mph (249\xc2\xa0km/h) about 21\xc2\xa0mi (34\xc2\xa0km) south of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Croix" title="Saint Croix">\n        Saint Croix\n       </a>\n       in the\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Virgin_Islands" title="United States Virgin Islands">\n        United States Virgin Islands\n       </a>\n       . It gradually weakened while moving through the\n       <a href="/wiki/Leeward_Islands" title="Leeward Islands">\n        Leeward Islands\n       </a>\n       , eventually dissipating on November\xc2\xa023 over the open Atlantic Ocean.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Before moving through the Lesser Antilles, Lenny produced rough surf that killed two people in northern\n       <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">\n        Colombia\n       </a>\n       . Strong winds and rainfall resulted in heavy crop damage in southeastern Puerto Rico. Despite the hurricane\'s passage near Saint Croix at peak intensity, damage on the small island was only described as "moderate", although there was widespread flooding and erosion. Damage in the United States territories totaled about $330\xc2\xa0million. The highest precipitation total was 34.12\xc2\xa0in (867\xc2\xa0mm) at the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gendarmerie" title="Gendarmerie">\n        police station\n       </a>\n       on the French side of\n       <a href="/wiki/Saint_Martin" title="Saint Martin">\n        Saint Martin\n       </a>\n       . On the island, the hurricane killed three people and destroyed more than 200\xc2\xa0properties. In nearby\n       <a href="/wiki/Antigua_and_Barbuda" title="Antigua and Barbuda">\n        Antigua and Barbuda\n       </a>\n       , the hurricane killed one person; torrential rainfall there contaminated the local water supply. Significant storm damage occurred as far south as\n       <a href="/wiki/Grenada" title="Grenada">\n        Grenada\n       </a>\n       , where high surf isolated towns from the capital city.\n       ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Hurricane Lenny began as a\n       <a href="/wiki/Low-pressure_area" title="Low-pressure area">\n        low-pressure area\n       </a>\n       that was first observed in the southwestern Caribbean Sea on November\xc2\xa08. It developed an area of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Convection_(meteorology)" title="Convection (meteorology)">\n        convection\n       </a>\n       but remained poorly defined for the next few days. Thunderstorms spread across the region, producing heavy rainfall in portions of Mexico and Central America. On November\xc2\xa013, the system became better organized; a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hunters" title="Hurricane Hunters">\n        Hurricane Hunters\n       </a>\n       flight later that day discovered a surface circulation and\n       <a href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_wind" title="Maximum sustained wind">\n        winds\n       </a>\n       of about 35\xc2\xa0mph (56\xc2\xa0km/h). The data indicated the\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclogenesis" title="Tropical cyclogenesis">\n        development\n       </a>\n       of Tropical Depression Sixteen at 1800\n       <a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">\n        UTC\n       </a>\n       , about 175\xc2\xa0mi (282\xc2\xa0km) south of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Cayman_Islands" title="Cayman Islands">\n        Cayman Islands\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The depression\'s convection was fairly disorganized, and the\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">\n        National Hurricane Center\n       </a>\n       (NHC) did not anticipate any strengthening for three days.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       For much of its existence, the\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        tropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       maintained a track from west to east across the Caribbean Sea, which was unprecedented in the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Atlantic_hurricane_seasons" title="List of Atlantic hurricane seasons">\n        Atlantic hurricane database\n       </a>\n       , earning it the nickname "Left-Hand Lenny". The path resulted from its movement along the southern end of a\n       <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">\n        trough\n       </a>\n       over the western Atlantic Ocean.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       After its formation, the depression gradually became better organized;\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       the NHC upgraded it to Tropical Storm Lenny on November\xc2\xa014,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       based on reports from the Hurricane Hunters. When it was first upgraded to a tropical storm, the cyclone already had winds of 65\xc2\xa0mph (105\xc2\xa0km/h) and a developing\n       <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">\n        eye feature\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At 0000\xc2\xa0UTC on November\xc2\xa015, Lenny attained hurricane status about 175\xc2\xa0mi (282\xc2\xa0km) southwest of\n       <a href="/wiki/Kingston,_Jamaica" title="Kingston, Jamaica">\n        Kingston, Jamaica\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The quick intensification was unexpected and occurred after a large area of convection blossomed over the center. At the same time, Lenny developed an\n       <a href="/wiki/Anticyclone" title="Anticyclone">\n        anticyclone\n       </a>\n       aloft, which provided favorable conditions for the hurricane\'s development.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       After moving east-southeastward during its initial development stages, the hurricane turned more to the east on November\xc2\xa015. The Hurricane Hunters reported winds of 100\xc2\xa0mph (160\xc2\xa0km/h), which indicated that Lenny had become a Category\xc2\xa02 on the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">\n        Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       However, the cloud pattern subsequently became less organized as the eye disappeared, and Lenny\'s winds weakened to 85\xc2\xa0mph (137\xc2\xa0km/h) while the hurricane passed south of Hispaniola.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The NHC noted the deterioration could have been due to a disruption of the storm\'s small inner core by "subtle environmental changes". After the sudden weakening,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       the Hurricane Hunters reported that the eye had reformed and the hurricane\'s winds had reached 100\xc2\xa0mph (160\xc2\xa0km/h). At the time, a\n       <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">\n        ridge\n       </a>\n       was expected to build to Lenny\'s east and turn the storm northeastward into\n       <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">\n        Puerto Rico\n       </a>\n       24\xc2\xa0hours later.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-d12_8-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-d12-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Beginning on November\xc2\xa016, Hurricane Lenny underwent a 24-hour period of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" title="Rapid deepening">\n        rapid deepening\n       </a>\n       , reaching major hurricane status about 165\xc2\xa0mi (266\xc2\xa0km) south of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Mona_Passage" title="Mona Passage">\n        Mona Passage\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       It developed well-defined\n       <a href="/wiki/Rainband" title="Rainband">\n        banding features\n       </a>\n       , good\n       <a href="/wiki/Outflow_(meteorology)" title="Outflow (meteorology)">\n        outflow\n       </a>\n       , and a circular eye that was visible from the radar in\n       <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">\n        San Juan, Puerto Rico\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The hurricane continued to become better organized, with an eye 29\xc2\xa0mi (47\xc2\xa0km) in diameter surrounded by a closed eyewall.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Around 1200\xc2\xa0UTC on November\xc2\xa017, Lenny intensified into a Category\xc2\xa04 hurricane while approaching the islands of the northeastern Caribbean. It was the fifth storm of such intensity in the year, setting the record for most Category\xc2\xa04 hurricanes in a season.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The hurricane then made its closest approach to Puerto Rico, passing about 75\xc2\xa0mi (121\xc2\xa0km) southeast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Maunabo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Maunabo, Puerto Rico">\n        Maunabo\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-srh_11-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-srh-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Shortly thereafter, Lenny attained peak winds of 155\xc2\xa0mph (249\xc2\xa0km/h) while passing 21\xc2\xa0mi (34\xc2\xa0km) south of the island of\n       <a href="/wiki/Saint_Croix,_U.S._Virgin_Islands" title="Saint Croix, U.S. Virgin Islands">\n        Saint Croix\n       </a>\n       in the\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Virgin_Islands" title="United States Virgin Islands">\n        United States Virgin Islands\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       This made it the second-strongest hurricane on record to form during the month of November.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Hurricane Hunters reported Lenny\'s peak winds in the southeastern portion of the hurricane; the group also reported a minimum\n       <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_pressure" title="Atmospheric pressure">\n        pressure\n       </a>\n       of 933\n       <a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">\n        mbar\n       </a>\n       , a drop of 34\xc2\xa0mbar in 24\xc2\xa0hours. In addition, a\n       <a href="/wiki/Dropsonde" title="Dropsonde">\n        dropsonde\n       </a>\n       recorded winds of 210\xc2\xa0mph (340\xc2\xa0km/h) while descending to the surface, the highest dropsonde wind speed recording in a hurricane at the time.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Around the time it peaked in intensity, Lenny\'s forward speed decreased in response to light\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone#Steering_winds" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        steering currents\n       </a>\n       between two ridges. Despite favorable conditions for strengthening, the hurricane weakened as it turned to an eastward drift, possibly due to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Upwelling" title="Upwelling">\n        upwelling\n       </a>\n       of cooler waters. Late on November\xc2\xa018, Lenny\'s eye moved over\n       <a href="/wiki/Saint_Martin" title="Saint Martin">\n        Saint Martin\n       </a>\n       with winds of 125\xc2\xa0mph (201\xc2\xa0km/h). After continued weakening, the hurricane struck\n       <a href="/wiki/Anguilla" title="Anguilla">\n        Anguilla\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Saint_Barth%C3%A9lemy" title="Saint Barth\xc3\xa9lemy">\n        Saint Barth\xc3\xa9lemy\n       </a>\n       the next day. It turned southeastward while still drifting, bringing heavy rainfall and strong winds to the islands across the northeastern Caribbean.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Late on November\xc2\xa019, Lenny weakened to tropical storm intensity after increased\n       <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">\n        wind shear\n       </a>\n       exposed the cyclone\'s center from the deepest convection.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Early on November\xc2\xa020, the storm made landfall on Anguilla,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       although by then the center had become difficult to locate.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">\n        <a href="#cite_note-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Later that day, the cyclone exited the Caribbean,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">\n        <a href="#cite_note-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       continuing its southeast track. On November\xc2\xa021, Lenny turned to the northeast and weakened to a tropical depression.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The deep convection was located at least 100\xc2\xa0mi (160\xc2\xa0km) east of the increasingly elongated center.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">\n        <a href="#cite_note-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Lenny turned to the east for the final time early on November\xc2\xa022, dissipating the next day about 690\xc2\xa0mi (1,110\xc2\xa0km) east of the Lesser Antilles.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n Hurricane Lenny south of Saint Croix\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Early in Lenny\'s duration, a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">\n        tropical storm warning\n       </a>\n       and hurricane watch were issued for Jamaica. Later, a hurricane watch was issued for the southern coast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Hispaniola" title="Hispaniola">\n        Hispaniola\n       </a>\n       , and a tropical storm warning was also issued for the Dominican Republic.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-14">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Haitian officials declared a state of alert in three southern provinces and allocated about $1\xc2\xa0million (1999\xc2\xa0USD) in hurricane funds.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp1116_17-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-afp1116-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Residents in flood-prone areas were advised to evacuate in southern Haiti and in the neighboring Dominican Republic.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">\n        <a href="#cite_note-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ppg_19-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ppg-19">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       A hurricane watch was issued for Puerto Rico and the\n       <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Islands" title="Virgin Islands">\n        Virgin Islands\n       </a>\n       late on November\xc2\xa015, which was upgraded to a hurricane warning six hours later.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-15">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       By that time, Lenny was projected to move over Puerto Rico.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-d12_8-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-d12-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       After Lenny made its closest approach to the island, the hurricane warning was downgraded to a tropical storm warning on November\xc2\xa017, which was discontinued the following day along with the advisories in the Virgin Islands.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-16">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In Puerto Rico, the media maintained continuous coverage on the hurricane based on statements and warnings from the San Juan\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Weather_Service" title="National Weather Service">\n        National Weather Service\n       </a>\n       office. Based on the coverage, the public was well informed of the hurricane\'s threat to the island. Before the storm and as a result of its impact, around 4,700\xc2\xa0people evacuated to 191\xc2\xa0shelters. This included 1,190\xc2\xa0residents in\n       <a href="/wiki/Ponce,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ponce, Puerto Rico">\n        Ponce\n       </a>\n       who evacuated to 27\xc2\xa0schools, as well as 584\xc2\xa0people in western Puerto Rico.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-is_20-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-is-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Officials closed all schools, banned the sale of alcohol, and ordered a freeze on the price of emergency supplies.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_article_21-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-BBC_article-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The\n       <a href="/wiki/Federal_Emergency_Management_Agency" title="Federal Emergency Management Agency">\n        Federal Emergency Management Agency\n       </a>\n       (FEMA) activated six medical assistance teams, three medical support teams, and two advance medical assessment units. The agency stored five days\' worth of food in schools in the U.S. Virgin Islands.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">\n        <a href="#cite_note-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Before the hurricane\'s arrival, U.S. Virgin Islands governor\n       <a href="/wiki/Charles_Wesley_Turnbull" title="Charles Wesley Turnbull">\n        Charles Wesley Turnbull\n       </a>\n       declared a state of emergency.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">\n        <a href="#cite_note-23">\n         [23]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In St. Croix, 309\xc2\xa0people rode out the storm in shelters.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-srh_11-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-srh-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Officials opened eight shelters in the\n       <a href="/wiki/British_Virgin_Islands" title="British Virgin Islands">\n        British Virgin Islands\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-is_20-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-is-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       There, airlines and hotels assisted in evacuating tourists from the area.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">\n        <a href="#cite_note-24">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Governments across the eastern Caribbean issued hurricane warnings as far south as\n       <a href="/wiki/Montserrat" title="Montserrat">\n        Montserrat\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-17">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In Anguilla, residents near the coast were advised to evacuate. Schools closed ahead of the storm, and the ferry between the island and Saint Martin was halted and moved to a safe location. In Saint Kitts and Nevis, the National Emergency Management Agency was activated on November\xc2\xa016. Officials there advised residents living near\n       <a href="/wiki/Ghat" title="Ghat">\n        ghauts\n       </a>\n       to evacuate, and one shelter was located in each district of the country. In addition, stores were open for longer hours to allow people to stock up on supplies.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">\n        <a href="#cite_note-25">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Most businesses and schools were closed in Antigua and Barbuda during the storm, while in Dominica, the airport was closed.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ifrc1118_26-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ifrc1118-26">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Further south, there was little warning for the hurricane in Grenada, and most people left their boats in the water.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27">\n        <a href="#cite_note-27">\n         [27]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n Across the eastern Caribbean, Hurricane Lenny damaged vital infrastructure, including roads and piers in the tourism-dependent islands. Most of the islands\' tourism areas were on western-facing beaches, many of which were unprepared for the high waves and winds produced by Lenny.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lennyspo_28-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-lennyspo-28">\n         [28]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Central_Caribbean">\n        Central Caribbean\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Lenny&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Central Caribbean">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Early in its existence, Lenny produced large waves and high tides along the\n       <a href="/wiki/Guajira_Peninsula" title="Guajira Peninsula">\n        Guajira Peninsula\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">\n        Colombia\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-18">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       sinking two boats and flooding 1,200\xc2\xa0houses. There were also reports of flooded businesses and damaged crops. In the country, strong winds on the storm\'s fringe killed a man by striking him with a beam. Although there were initial reports of nine people missing,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp1116_17-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-afp1116-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       only two were counted in the death toll for mainland Colombia.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-19">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Two sailors were killed offshore when their yacht was lost in the southern Caribbean Sea.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-20">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Along the\n       <a href="/wiki/ABC_islands_(Lesser_Antilles)" title="ABC islands (Lesser Antilles)">\n        ABC Islands\n       </a>\n       off the north coast of Venezuela, the hurricane produced 10-to-20\xc2\xa0ft (3-to-6\xc2\xa0m) waves along the southwest coastlines.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29">\n        <a href="#cite_note-29">\n         [29]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The waves caused heavy beach erosion and coastal damage to properties and boats.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-naa_30-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-naa-30">\n         [30]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In Jamaica, the hurricane dropped heavy rainfall but left little damage. Rains in the Dominican Republic caused flooding in the country\'s southwest portion.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ppg_19-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ppg-19">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Flooding around\n       <a href="/wiki/Les_Cayes" title="Les Cayes">\n        Les Cayes\n       </a>\n       in southwestern Haiti destroyed 60\xc2\xa0percent of the rice, corn, and banana plantations, while high waves wrecked several houses in\n       <a href="/wiki/Cavaellon" title="Cavaellon">\n        Cavaellon\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BBC_article_21-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-BBC_article-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Hurricane Lenny was originally forecast to strike Puerto Rico, although it remained south of the island.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-21">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Beginning on November\xc2\xa017, Lenny affected Puerto Rico with gusty winds and heavy rainfall. Rainfall in the days prior to Lenny\'s approach left areas susceptible to flooding, which caused many rivers in the northeastern portion of the island to overflow their banks following the storm. Such flooding forced towns to evacuate along the rivers, and also resulted in the closure of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Puerto_Rico_Highways" title="List of Puerto Rico Highways">\n        secondary and primary highways\n       </a>\n       . The heavy rains also caused mudslides and rockslides.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-srh_11-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-srh-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The peak rainfall on the island was 14.64\xc2\xa0in (372\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Jayuya,_Puerto_Rico" title="Jayuya, Puerto Rico">\n        Jayuya\n       </a>\n       in central Puerto Rico.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hpc_31-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-hpc-31">\n         [31]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Storm_tide" title="Storm tide">\n        Tides\n       </a>\n       in San Juan were about 1.8\xc2\xa0ft (0.55\xc2\xa0m) above normal. There, high seas washed a 546\xc2\xa0ft (166\xc2\xa0m) freighter ashore. Winds in the Puerto Rican mainland were not significant, gusting to 48\xc2\xa0mph (77\xc2\xa0km/h) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Ceiba,_Puerto_Rico" title="Ceiba, Puerto Rico">\n        Ceiba\n       </a>\n       . The storm left 22,000\xc2\xa0people without power and 103,000\xc2\xa0people without water.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-srh_11-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-srh-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Because of the heavy rainfall, about 200\xc2\xa0farmers in southeastern Puerto Rico sustained about $19\xc2\xa0million in crop damage (1999\xc2\xa0USD). In the affected region, the heavy rainfall destroyed 80\xc2\xa0percent of the vegetables and 50\xc2\xa0percent of the plantains. Damage throughout the island totaled $105\xc2\xa0million (1999\xc2\xa0US).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdc_32-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ncdc-32">\n         [32]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Virgin_Islands">\n        Virgin Islands\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Lenny&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Virgin Islands">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:FEMA_-_670_-_Photograph_by_FEMA_News_Photo_taken_on_11-23-1999_in_US_Virgin_Islands.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="225" data-file-width="300" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/FEMA_-_670_-_Photograph_by_FEMA_News_Photo_taken_on_11-23-1999_in_US_Virgin_Islands.jpg/220px-FEMA_-_670_-_Photograph_by_FEMA_News_Photo_taken_on_11-23-1999_in_US_Virgin_Islands.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/e/e6/FEMA_-_670_-_Photograph_by_FEMA_News_Photo_taken_on_11-23-1999_in_US_Virgin_Islands.jpg 1.5x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:FEMA_-_670_-_Photograph_by_FEMA_News_Photo_taken_on_11-23-1999_in_US_Virgin_Islands.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Property damage on the island of Saint Croix\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       After passing southeast of Puerto Rico, Hurricane Lenny struck St. Croix in the United States Virgin Islands, although its strongest winds remained southeast of the island. There, gusts reached 112\xc2\xa0mph (180\xc2\xa0km/h), while sustained winds officially peaked around 70\xc2\xa0mph (110\xc2\xa0km/h). Strong winds damaged the roofs of many houses in eastern St. Croix\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-22">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and knocked down trees and power lines. The winds left severe damage to vegetation after fruits and vegetables were blown away. Rainfall peaked at 10.47\xc2\xa0in (266\xc2\xa0mm),\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hpc_31-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-hpc-31">\n         [31]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       which caused widespread flooding of many properties in the island\'s western portion. In\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Frederiksted,_United_States_Virgin_Islands" title="Frederiksted, United States Virgin Islands">\n        Frederiksted\n       </a>\n       , the hurricane produced a\n       <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">\n        storm surge\n       </a>\n       of 15\xe2\x80\x9320\xc2\xa0ft (4.6\xe2\x80\x936.1\xc2\xa0m)\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-23">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       along with high waves that washed out roads and damaged coastal structures. There was also severe beach erosion in western St. Croix; high waves dumped 6.5\xc2\xa0ft (2.0\xc2\xa0m) of sand onto coastal roads about 100\xc2\xa0ft (30\xc2\xa0m) inland,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-srh_11-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-srh-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and also washed several boats ashore.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-24">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The National Weather Service described the damage as "moderate".\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-srh_11-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-srh-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Elsewhere in the U.S. Virgin Islands, Lenny produced a storm surge of about 1.8\xc2\xa0ft (0.55\xc2\xa0m) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Saint_Thomas,_U.S._Virgin_Islands" title="Saint Thomas, U.S. Virgin Islands">\n        St. Thomas\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-25">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Sustained winds on the island reached 53\xc2\xa0mph (85\xc2\xa0km/h) at the\n       <a href="/wiki/Cyril_E._King_Airport" title="Cyril E. King Airport">\n        Cyril E. King Airport\n       </a>\n       , with gusts to 70\xc2\xa0mph (110\xc2\xa0km/h). On nearby\n       <a href="/wiki/Saint_John,_U.S._Virgin_Islands" title="Saint John, U.S. Virgin Islands">\n        St. John\n       </a>\n       , wind gusts reached 92\xc2\xa0mph (148\xc2\xa0km/h). Rains were not as heavy as on St. Croix; the maximum amounts were 4.34\xc2\xa0in (110\xc2\xa0mm) on St. Thomas and 2.95\xc2\xa0in (75\xc2\xa0mm) on St. John. Both islands reported beach erosion along their southern coastlines.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-srh_11-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-srh-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Damage on St. Thomas was minimal, limited to minor flooding and mudslides. The\n       <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Islands_National_Park" title="Virgin Islands National Park">\n        Virgin Islands National Park\n       </a>\n       in St. John reported over $1.6\xc2\xa0million in damage (1999\xc2\xa0USD).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdc_32-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ncdc-32">\n         [32]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In Puerto Rico and the U.S. Virgin Islands, the hurricane left about $330\xc2\xa0million in damage (1999\xc2\xa0USD), but caused no deaths.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-26">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Gorda" title="Virgin Gorda">\n        Virgin Gorda\n       </a>\n       in the nearby British Virgin Islands, the hurricane produced sustained winds of 55\xc2\xa0mph (89\xc2\xa0km/h), with gusts to 85\xc2\xa0mph (137\xc2\xa0km/h). Rainfall amounted to around 4\xc2\xa0in (100\xc2\xa0mm)\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-27">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and caused a mudslide near Coxheath. High waves eroded a portion of\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Sir_Francis_Drake_Highway&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Sir Francis Drake Highway (page does not exist)">\n        Sir Francis Drake Highway\n       </a>\n       , and the high winds destroyed the roof of an apartment.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-is_20-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-is-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Property damage in the British Virgin Islands totaled $5.6\xc2\xa0million (1999\xc2\xa0USD); however, the damage combined with the loss of tourism and productivity yielded a loss of $22\xc2\xa0million to the islands\' economy, or 3.1\xc2\xa0percent of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gross_domestic_product" title="Gross domestic product">\n        gross domestic product\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33">\n        <a href="#cite_note-33">\n         [33]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Lesser_Antilles">\n        Lesser Antilles\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;">\n        <a </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The eye of Lenny moved over Anguilla, an island located east of the British Virgin Islands.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-28">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Localized flooding was reported,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rw1118_34-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rw1118-34">\n         [34]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       including in the capital,\n       <a href="/wiki/The_Valley,_Anguilla" title="The Valley, Anguilla">\n        The Valley\n       </a>\n       , where waters reportedly reached a depth of 14\xc2\xa0ft (4.3\xc2\xa0m).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35">\n        <a href="#cite_note-35">\n         [35]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The hurricane struck only a month after\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Jose_(1999)" title="Hurricane Jose (1999)">\n        Hurricane Jose\n       </a>\n       had affected the region, causing significant beach erosion along Anguilla\'s coastline.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36">\n        <a href="#cite_note-36">\n         [36]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Damage from Lenny amounted to $65.8\xc2\xa0million.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37">\n        <a href="#cite_note-37">\n         [37]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Saint Barth\xc3\xa9lemy and the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SSS_Islands" title="SSS Islands">\n        SSS Islands\n       </a>\n       , which include\n       <a href="/wiki/Saba" title="Saba">\n        Saba\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Sint_Eustatius" title="Sint Eustatius">\n        Sint Eustatius\n       </a>\n       , and Saint Martin, were in the path of Hurricane Lenny on November\xc2\xa018 through 19.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-29">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On Saint Barth\xc3\xa9lemy, the hurricane produced record-breaking precipitation of around 15\xc2\xa0in (380\xc2\xa0mm). Waves reached 16\xc2\xa0ft (4.9\xc2\xa0m) on the island\'s western portion.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-30">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On Saba, there was an unofficial wind gust of 167\xc2\xa0mph (269\xc2\xa0km/h) before the instrument blew away. The island sustained damage to several buildings, including airport facilities.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-31">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The highest precipitation related to the storm occurred at the police station on the French side of Saint Martin, where a total of 34.12\xc2\xa0in (867\xc2\xa0mm) was recorded.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hpc_31-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-hpc-31">\n         [31]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       This included a record 24-hour total of 18.98\xc2\xa0in (482\xc2\xa0mm). On the Dutch side of the island, rainfall peaked at 27.56\xc2\xa0in (700\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Philipsburg,_Sint_Maarten" title="Philipsburg, Sint Maarten">\n        Philipsburg\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-32">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The rains resulted in mudslides and flooding and were the primary form of impact on the island. For 36\xc2\xa0hours, Saint Martin experienced tropical storm conditions, and there were three times when the winds surpassed hurricane force. Sustained winds on the island peaked at 84\xc2\xa0mph (135\xc2\xa0km/h) at the\n       <a href="/wiki/Princess_Juliana_International_Airport" title="Princess Juliana International Airport">\n        Princess Juliana International Airport\n       </a>\n       ;\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-naa_30-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-naa-30">\n         [30]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       these were the highest sustained winds observed on land.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-33">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The airport also reported a wind gust of 104\xc2\xa0mph (167\xc2\xa0km/h).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-naa_30-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-naa-30">\n         [30]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Due to the hurricane\'s unusual track from the west, it produced unparalleled waves of 10\xe2\x80\x9316\xc2\xa0ft (3.0\xe2\x80\x934.9\xc2\xa0m) along the western coast of St. Martin,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-naa_30-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-naa-30">\n         [30]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       which damaged or destroyed many boats.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-34">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       During its passage, Lenny left widespread damage to the infrastructure, including to the airport, harbor, resorts, power utilities, schools, and hospitals.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ontw_38-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ontw-38">\n         [38]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       As a result, the three SSS Islands sustained power and telephone outages.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-r1119_39-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-r1119-39">\n         [39]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       There was widespread destruction of the roofs of houses across the island,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ontw_38-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ontw-38">\n         [38]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and over 200\xc2\xa0houses were destroyed.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ifrc1122_40-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ifrc1122-40">\n         [40]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Damage was estimated at $69\xc2\xa0million,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ontw_38-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ontw-38">\n         [38]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and there were three deaths on the Dutch side of St. Martin.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-naa_30-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-naa-30">\n         [30]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Two of these deaths were from flying debris, and the other was due to a collapsed roadway.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-35">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       While passing over Antigua, Hurricane Lenny dropped 18.32\xc2\xa0in (465\xc2\xa0mm) of rain at the\n       <a href="/wiki/V._C._Bird_International_Airport" title="V. C. Bird International Airport">\n        V. C. Bird International Airport\n       </a>\n       , while locations in the southern portion recorded over 25\xc2\xa0in (640\xc2\xa0mm).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-36">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The rainfall caused severe flooding in Antigua, resulting in landslides in the northwestern and southern portion of the island.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lennyspo_28-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-lennyspo-28">\n         [28]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Flooding washed out major roadways, including one bridge.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rw1130_41-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rw1130-41">\n         [41]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Along the coast, the storm caused severe beach erosion. About 65\xc2\xa0percent of Barbuda experienced flooding due to the rainfall and the island\'s flat topography. The flooding contaminated the water storage facilities and all private wells.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lennyspo_28-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-lennyspo-28">\n         [28]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       About 95\xc2\xa0percent of the crops in Barbuda were destroyed.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-garypadgett_42-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-garypadgett-42">\n         [42]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Damage in the country of\n       <a href="/wiki/Antigua_and_Barbuda" title="Antigua and Barbuda">\n        Antigua and Barbuda\n       </a>\n       totaled $51.3\xc2\xa0million,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lennyspo_28-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-lennyspo-28">\n         [28]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and there was one death.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usaid_43-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-usaid-43">\n         [43]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n Several businesses were flooded, and some beach erosion was reported.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rw1118_34-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rw1118-34">\n         [34]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The hurricane destroyed 46\xc2\xa0homes and damaged 332\xc2\xa0others to varying degree.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usaid_43-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-usaid-43">\n         [43]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Home damage forced four families to evacuate.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rw1118_34-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rw1118-34">\n         [34]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Heavy rains caused mudslides on Saint Kitts,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-37">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and heavy damage occurred in\n       <a href="/wiki/Old_Road_Town" title="Old Road Town">\n        Old Road Town\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pan_50-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-pan-50">\n         [50]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Damage in the country amounted to $41.4\xc2\xa0million.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rw1130_41-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rw1130-41">\n         [41]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In Montserrat, damage was reported along its western coastline.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rw1118_34-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rw1118-34">\n         [34]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       After high waves capsized a boat, a crew of three required rescue.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51">\n        <a href="#cite_note-51">\n         [51]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <a href="/wiki/Guadeloupe" title="Guadeloupe">\n        Guadeloupe\n       </a>\n       received record precipitation amounts in some areas, generally ranging from 6 to 12 inches (150 to 300\xc2\xa0mm). On\n       <a href="/wiki/Grande-Terre" title="Grande-Terre">\n        Grande-Terre\n       </a>\n       , the eastern island of Guadeloupe, the hurricane produced a\n       <a href="/wiki/Significant_wave_height" title="Significant wave height">\n        significant wave height\n       </a>\n       of 9.8\xc2\xa0ft (3.0\xc2\xa0m), with estimates as high as 13\xc2\xa0ft (4.0\xc2\xa0m). The extent of damage was from waves in the western portion of the island and from unrelated flooding inland. There were five deaths in Guadeloupe.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-38">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In Dominica, high waves damaged the island\'s western coastal highway, leaving the most heavily traveled road temporarily closed.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lennyspo_28-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-lennyspo-28">\n         [28]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Road closures cut off links between towns on the island.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rw1118_34-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rw1118-34">\n         [34]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The hurricane destroyed at least 50\xc2\xa0homes,\n       <sup class="reference" damage, and across the nation the hurricane\'s impact was worse than that from\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Luis" title="Hurricane Luis">\n        Hurricane Luis\n       </a>\n       four years prior.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ifrc1119_52-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ifrc1119-52">\n         [52]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Damage on the island totaled $21.5\xc2\xa0million.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lennyspo_28-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-lennyspo-28">\n         [28]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Rainfall of around 3\xc2\xa0in (76\xc2\xa0mm) reached as far south as\n       <a href="/wiki/Martinique" title="Martinique">\n        Martinique\n       </a>\n       , where one person was killed.\n       <sup left homeless in the country.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ifrc1122_40-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ifrc1122-40">\n         [40]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n <a href="#cite_note-lennyspo-28">\n         [28]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The waves covered the entire Grand Anse Beach in\n       <a href="/wiki/Saint_George_Parish,_Grenada" title="Saint </sup>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Saint_John_Parish,_Grenada" title="Saint John Parish, Grenada">\n        Saint John Parish\n       </a>\n       , the storm knocked out the water and power </a>\n       </sup>\n       The small island\n       <a href="/wiki/Carriacou" title="Carriacou">\n        Carriacou\n       </a>\n       , located north of Grenada, sustained damage to the road to its primary airport.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lennyspo_28-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-lennyspo-28">\n         [28]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At least  </sup>\n       In the country, storm surge caused damage to boats and coastal structures, while beach erosion was reported in Tobago.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53">\n        <a The Saint Lucian government provided housing to 70\xc2\xa0families. In Grenada, workers repaired the road system to allow fuel transportation across the island and began to reclaim land near its airport to mitigate erosion.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lennyspo_28-16">\n        <a href="#cite_note-lennyspo-28">\n         [28]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Regions in Antigua and Grenada were declared disaster areas.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usaid_43-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-usaid-43">\n         [43]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rw1120_58-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rw1120-58">\n         [58]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Across the eastern Caribbean, local Red Cross offices provided food and shelter to affected citizens.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ifrc1119_52-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ifrc1119-52">\n         [52]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       High damage to tourist areas caused a decrease in cruise lines.
