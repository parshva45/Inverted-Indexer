</a>\n    </div>\n    <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" id="mw-content-text" lang="en">\n     <div class="mw-parser-output">\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        Accumulated cyclone energy\n       </b>\n       (\n       <b>\n        ACE\n       </b>\n       ) is a measure used by the\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Oceanic_and_Atmospheric_Administration" title="National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration">\n        National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration\n       </a>\n       (NOAA) to express the activity of individual\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclones" title="Tropical cyclones">\n        tropical cyclones\n       </a>\n       and entire tropical cyclone seasons, particularly the North\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlantic" title="Atlantic">\n        Atlantic\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="Atlantic hurricane season">\n        hurricane season\n       </a>\n       . It uses an approximation of the wind energy used by a tropical system over its lifetime and is calculated every six hours. The ACE of a season is the sum of the ACEs for each storm and takes into account the number, strength, and duration of all the\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        tropical storms\n       </a>\n       in the season. The highest ACE calculated for a single storm is 82, for\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Ioke" title="Hurricane Ioke">\n        Hurricane/Typhoon Ioke\n       </a>\n       in 2006.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="toc">\n       <div class="toctext">\n           External links\n          </span>\n         </a>\n        </li>\n       </ul>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Calculation">\n        Calculation\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Accumulated_cyclone_energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Calculation">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       The ACE of a season is calculated by summing the squares of the estimated maximum sustained velocity of every active tropical storm (wind speed 35 knots [65 km/h, 40 mph] or higher), at six-hour intervals. Since the calculation is sensitive to the starting point of the six-hour intervals, the convention is to use 00:00, 06:00, 12:00, and 18:00\n       <a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">\n        UTC\n       </a>\n       . If any storms of a season happen to cross years, the storm\'s ACE counts for the previous year.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The numbers are usually divided by 10,000 to make them more manageable. One unit of ACE equals\n       <span class="nowrap">\n        10\n        <sup>\n         4\n        </sup>\n        <a href="/wiki/Knot_(unit)" title="Knot (unit)">\n         kn\n        </a>\n        <sup>\n         2\n        </sup>\n        ,\n       </span>\n       and for use as an index the unit is assumed. Thus:\n      </p>\n      <dl>\n       <dd>\n        <span class="mwe-math-element">\n         <span class="mwe-math-mathml-inline mwe-math-mathml-a11y" style="display: none;">\n          <math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML">\n           <semantics>\n            <mrow class="MJX-TeXAtom-ORD">\n             <mstyle displaystyle="true" scriptlevel="0">\n              <mrow class="MJX-TeXAtom-ORD">\n               <mtext>\n                ACE\n               </mtext>\n              </mrow>\n              <mo>\n               =\n              </mo>\n              <msup>\n               <mn>\n                10\n               </mn>\n               <mrow class="MJX-TeXAtom-ORD">\n                <mo>\n                 \xe2\x88\x92\n                 <!-- \xe2\x88\x92 -->\n                </mo>\n                <mn>\n                 4\n                </mn>\n               </mrow>\n              </msup>\n              <mo>\n               \xe2\x88\x91\n               <!-- \xe2\x88\x91 -->\n              </mo>\n              <msubsup>\n               <mi>\n                v\n               </mi>\n               <mrow class="MJX-TeXAtom-ORD">\n                <mo form="prefix" movablelimits="true">\n                 max\n                </mo>\n               </mrow>\n               <mrow class="MJX-TeXAtom-ORD">\n                <mn>\n                 2\n                </mn>\n               </mrow>\n              </msubsup>\n             </mstyle>\n            </mrow>\n            <annotation encoding="application/x-tex">\n             {\\displaystyle {\\text{ACE}}=10^{-4}\\sum v_{\\max }^{2}}\n            </annotation>\n           </semantics>\n          </math>\n         </span>\n         <img alt="{\\text{ACE}}=10^{{-4}}\\sum v_{\\max }^{2}" aria-hidden="true" class="mwe-math-fallback-image-inline" src="https://wikimedia.org/api/rest_v1/media/math/render/svg/ea1e9ac3d034e4e6d102ebcc5fe328051b810409" style="vertical-align: -1.338ex; width:21.429ex; height:3.843ex;"/>\n        </span>\n       </dd>\n      </dl>\n      <p>\n       where\n       <i>\n        v\n       </i>\n       <sub>\n        max\n       </sub>\n       is estimated sustained wind speed in\n       <a href="/wiki/Knot_(unit)" title="Knot (unit)">\n        knots\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <a href="/wiki/Kinetic_energy" title="Kinetic energy">\n        Kinetic energy\n       </a>\n       is proportional to the square of velocity, and by adding together the energy per some interval of time, the accumulated energy is found. As the duration of a storm increases, more values are summed and the ACE also increases such that longer-duration storms may accumulate a larger ACE than more-powerful storms of lesser duration. Although ACE is a value proportional to the energy of the system, it is not a direct calculation of energy (the mass of the moved air and therefore the size of the storm would show up in a real energy calculation).\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       A related quantity is hurricane destruction potential (HDP), which is ACE but only calculated for the time where the system is a hurricane.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BellEtAl2000_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-BellEtAl2000-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Competing_measurement_metrics">\n        Competing measurement metrics\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Accumulated_cyclone_energy&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Competing measurement metrics">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       ACE focuses upon the most intense winds concentrated in a storm\'s center. Whereas, the actual destructive power of a storm depends upon the total area impacted with damaging winds, waves, and storm surge. The metric Integrated Kinetic Energy (IKE), developed in 2007, more accurately reflects the destructive potential. It examines the kinetic energy that forces a storm surge and creates high waves as well as the horizontal wind loads over the full storm. This metric yields a time specific value for a storm and doesn\'t account for the destruction caused by a storm over its entire life. Therefore, in 2013 the researchers at the University of Florida created a metric called Track Integrated Kinetic Energy (TIKE). TIKE expands the concept by accumulating IKE over the lifespan of an individual tropical cyclone and a total value for all named tropical cyclones in the season.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Atlantic_basin_ACE">\n        Atlantic basin categorize the hurricane season into 3 groups by its activity.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-background_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-background-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Measured over the period 1951\xe2\x80\x932000 for the Atlantic basin, the\n       <a href="/wiki/Median" title="Median">\n        median\n       </a>\n       annual index was 87.5 and the\n       <a href="/wiki/Arithmetic_mean" title="Arithmetic mean">\n        mean\n       </a>\n       annual index was 93.2. The NOAA categorization system divides seasons into:\n      </p>\n      <ul>\n       <li>\n        <i>\n         Above-normal season\n        </i>\n        : An ACE value above 111 (120% of the 1981\xe2\x80\x932010 median), provided at least two of the following three parameters are also exceeded: number of tropical storms: 12, hurricanes: 6, and major hurricanes: 2.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        <i>\n         Near-normal season\n        </i>\n        : neither above-normal nor below normal\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        <i>\n         Below-normal season\n        </i>\n        : An ACE value below 66 (71.4% of the 1981\xe2\x80\x932010 median), or none of the following three parameters are exceeded: number of tropical storms: 9, hurricanes: 4, and major hurricanes: 1.\n       </li>\n      </ul>\n      <p>\n       According to the NOAA categorization system for the Atlantic, the most recent above-normal season is the\n       <a href="/wiki/2017_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2017 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        2017 season\n       </a>\n       , the most recent near-normal season is the\n       <a href="/wiki/2014_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2014 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        2014 season\n       </a>\n       , and the most recent below normal season is the\n       <a href="/wiki/2015_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2015 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        2015 season\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <h4>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Hyperactivity">\n        Hyperactivity\n       </span>\n       <span
