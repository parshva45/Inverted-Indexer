<tr>\n        <td colspan="2" style="text-align:center;padding:0px 2px">\n        </td>\n       </tr>\n       <tr>\n        <td colspan="2" style="text-align:center; border-top:#aaa 1px solid;">\n         Part of the\n         <b>\n          <a href="/wiki/2000_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2000 Atlantic hurricane season">\n           2000 Atlantic hurricane season\n          </a>\n         </b>\n        </td>\n       </tr>\n       <tr style="display:none">\n        <td colspan="2">\n        </td>\n       </tr>\n      </table>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        Hurricane Keith\n       </b>\n       was an\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_hurricane" title="Atlantic hurricane">\n        Atlantic hurricane\n       </a>\n       in October 2000 that caused extensive damage in\n       <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">\n        Central America\n       </a>\n       , especially in\n       <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">\n        Mexico\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Belize" title="Belize">\n        Belize\n       </a>\n       . It was the fifteenth\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        tropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       , eleventh named storm, and seventh hurricane of the\n       <a href="/wiki/2000_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2000 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        that year\'s Atlantic hurricane season\n       </a>\n       . Keith developed as a tropical depression from a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">\n        tropical wave\n       </a>\n       in the western\n       <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">\n        Caribbean Sea\n       </a>\n       on September\xc2\xa028. The depression gradually strengthened, and became Tropical Storm Keith on the following day. As the storm tracked westward, it continued to intensify and was upgraded to a hurricane on September\xc2\xa030. Shortly thereafter, Keith began to\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" title="Rapid deepening">\n        rapidly deepen\n       </a>\n       , and peaked as a Category\xc2\xa04 hurricane less than 24\xc2\xa0hours later. Keith then began to meander erratically offshore of Belize, which significantly weakened the storm due to land interaction. By late on October\xc2\xa02, Keith made landfall in\n       <a href="/wiki/Ambergris_Caye" title="Ambergris Caye">\n        Ambergris Caye\n       </a>\n       , Belize as a minimal hurricane. It quickly weakened to a tropical storm, before another landfall occurred near\n       <a href="/wiki/Belize_City" title="Belize City">\n        Belize City\n       </a>\n       early on the following day. While moving inland over the\n       <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula" title="Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula">\n        Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula\n       </a>\n       , Keith weakened further, and was downgraded to a tropical depression before emerging into the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">\n        Gulf of Mexico\n       </a>\n       on October\xc2\xa04. Once in the Gulf of Mexico, Keith began to re-strengthen and was upgraded to a tropical storm later that day, and a hurricane on the following day. By late on October\xc2\xa05, Keith made its third and final landfall near\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tampico,_Tamaulipas" title="Tampico, Tamaulipas">\n        Tampico, Tamaulipas\n       </a>\n       , Mexico as a moderately strong Category 1 hurricane. The storm quickly weakened inland and dissipated as a tropical cyclone by 24\xc2\xa0hours after landfall.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Keith brought heavy rainfall to several countries in Central America, which resulted in extensive flooding, especially in Belize and Mexico. In\n       <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">\n        Guatemala\n       </a>\n       , the storm flooded 10 towns, and caused one fatality. Similarly, one fatality also occurred in\n       <a href="/wiki/El_Salvador" title="El Salvador">\n        El Salvador\n       </a>\n       , and at least 300\xc2\xa0people were affected by flooding in that country. Thirteen communities in\n       <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">\n        Nicaragua\n       </a>\n       were completely isolated after Keith made roads impassable. Twelve deaths were reported in Nicaragua, all of which were flood-related. Five people were presumed dead in\n       <a href="/wiki/Honduras" title="Honduras">\n        Honduras\n       </a>\n       after an aircraft disappeared near\n       <a href="/wiki/Roat%C3%A1n" title="Roat\xc3\xa1n">\n        Roat\xc3\xa1n\n       </a>\n       ; one other fatality occurred due to flooding. The storm brought torrential rainfall to Belize, with many areas reporting at least 10 inches (250\xc2\xa0mm) of rain, while highest reported amount of precipitation was greater than 30 inches (760\xc2\xa0mm). In Belize, a village reported that only 12\xc2\xa0houses remained, while elsewhere in the country, at least 60 homes were destroyed or damaged; several houses in\n       <a href="/wiki/Belize_City" title="Belize City">\n        Belize City\n       </a>\n       suffered minor roof damage. At least 19\xc2\xa0people were killed in Belize and damages totaled to $280\xc2\xa0million (2000\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">\n        USD\n       </a>\n       ). Heavy rainfall also occurred in Mexico, especially in the states of\n       <a href="/wiki/Nuevo_Le%C3%B3n" title="Nuevo Le\xc3\xb3n">\n        Nuevo Le\xc3\xb3n\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Tamaulipas" title="Tamaulipas">\n        Tamaulipas\n       </a>\n       . Flooding from Keith caused several mudslides and a few rivers reached historic levels. Throughout Mexico, at least 460\xc2\xa0houses were damaged or destroyed and other losses in infrastructure occurred. In addition, one person drowned due to heavy rainfall. Damages incurred in associated with Keith in Mexico totaled to approximately $365.9\xc2\xa0million (2000\xc2\xa0MXN, $38.7\xc2\xa0million 2000\xc2\xa0USD). Overall, Keith was responsible for 68 deaths and $319 million (2000 USD) in damage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-assessed_damage_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-assessed_damage-1">\n It moved westward across the Atlantic Ocean without\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclogenesis" title="Tropical cyclogenesis">\n        development\n       </a>\n       due to the presence of upper-level\n       <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">\n        wind shear\n       </a>\n       . After moving across the\n       <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">\n        Caribbean Sea\n       </a>\n       , a disturbance along the wave started to become better organized on September\xc2\xa027.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The next day, a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone#physical_structure" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        closed circulation\n       </a>\n       and winds of 37\xc2\xa0mph (60\xc2\xa0km/h); upon receiving the data, the\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">\n        National Hurricane Center\n       </a>\n       (NHC) initiated advisories on Tropical Depression Fifteen at 2100\n       <a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">\n        UTC\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc1_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-disc1-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The NHC later estimated the depression had formed three hours earlier, about 70\xc2\xa0mi (110\xc2\xa0km) north-northeast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Cabo_Gracias_a_Dios" title="Cabo Gracias a Dios">\n        Cabo Gracias a Dios\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Upon forming, the depression had good\n       <a href="/wiki/Outflow_(meteorology)" title="Outflow (meteorology)">\n        outflow\n       </a>\n       , although the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Convection_(meteorology)" title="Convection (meteorology)">\n        convection\n       </a>\n       was not as organized.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc1_3-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-disc1-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       With an\n       <a href="/wiki/Anticyclone" title="Anticyclone">\n        anticyclone\n       </a>\n       over the system, the depression was expected to gradually intensify while moving slowly to the west-northwest, a motion caused by weak\n       <a href="/wiki/Prevailing_winds" title="Prevailing winds">\n        steering winds\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Late on September\xc2\xa028, the NHC upgraded the depression to tropical storm status and\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_naming" title="Tropical cyclone naming">\n        named\n       </a>\n       it Keith,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       after another Hurricane Hunters mission reported flight-level winds of 61\xc2\xa0mph (98\xc2\xa0km/h).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc5_5-0">\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" title="Rapid deepening">\n        rapid deepening\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       fueled by warm waters, low wind shear, and an organizing cloud pattern.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc5_5-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-disc5-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       There initially was uncertainty in the storm\'s future movement, due to a\n       <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">\n        ridge\n       </a>\n       in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">\n        Gulf of Mexico\n       </a>\n       .\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_prediction_model" title="Tropical cyclone prediction model">\n        Tropical cyclone prediction models\n       </a>\n       differed on their assessments; three models anticipated a turn to the northeast toward Florida due to a trough, while others predicted a continued slow motion to the west-northwest, eventually reaching the\n       <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Campeche" title="Bay of Campeche">\n        Bay of Campeche\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       After continued strengthening, Keith became a hurricane on September\xc2\xa030, just 18\xc2\xa0hours after becoming a tropical storm,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and making it the seventh such storm of that intensity of the season.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       That day, an\n       <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">\n        eye\n       </a>\n       began developing as the structure became much better organized.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In a 13\xe2\x80\x91hour period beginning at 1808\xc2\xa0UTC on September\xc2\xa030, Keith underwent\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening#Explosive_intensification" title="Rapid deepening">\n        explosive deepening\n       </a>\n       , as its\n       <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_pressure" title="Atmospheric pressure">\n        barometric pressure\n       </a>\n       dropped at a rate of nearly 3\xc2\xa0mbar (0.089\xc2\xa0inHg) per hour.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The NHC noted that Keith experienced conditions "ideal for strengthening", with the exception of the approaching land interaction with the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_peninsula" title="Yucat\xc3\xa1n peninsula">\n        Yucat\xc3\xa1n peninsula\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The hurricane slowed until stalling offshore eastern\n       <a href="/wiki/Belize" title="Belize">\n        Belize\n       </a>\n       , caused by the ridge to the north and the precursor to\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_Storm_Leslie_(2000)" title="Tropical Storm Leslie (2000)">\n        Tropical Storm Leslie\n       </a>\n       forming over Cuba.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       By early on October\xc2\xa01, Keith had developed a well-defined eye about 20\xc2\xa0mi (32\xc2\xa0km) in diameter, with what the NHC described as a "spectacular appearance."\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Later that day, Hurricane Hunters estimated a minimum pressure of 939\xc2\xa0mbar (27.7\xc2\xa0inHg) and deployed a\n       <a href="/wiki/Dropsonde" title="Dropsonde">\n        dropsonde\n       </a>\n       that observed peak winds of 176\xc2\xa0mph (283\xc2\xa0km/h). Based on the data, the NHC estimated that Keith attained peak\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_winds" title="Maximum sustained winds">\n        winds\n       </a>\n       of 140\xc2\xa0mph (220\xc2\xa0km/h) at 0700\xc2\xa0UTC on October\xc2\xa01, while just offshore eastern Belize. This made it a\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_Category_4_Atlantic_hurricanes" title="List of Category 4 Atlantic hurricanes">\n        Category 4\n       </a>\n       on the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">\n        Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Shortly after Keith reached peak intensity, the eye had moved over portions of Belize, causing it to lose definition. Outflow became restricted, and the developing disturbance to the northeast increased wind shear. As a result, Keith began to weaken steadily on October\xc2\xa01.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       While remaining nearly stationary, Keith made a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">\n        landfall\n       </a>\n       on\n       <a href="/wiki/Ambergris_Cay" title="Ambergris Cay">\n        Ambergris Cay\n       </a>\n       late on October\xc2\xa02 as a minimal hurricane. Shortly thereafter, Keith weakened into a tropical storm,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and its convection became limited to the southeastern quadrant.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n and within nine hours weakened into a tropical depression.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The building ridge to the north caused the system to accelerate more to the west-northwest across the Yucat\xc3\xa1n peninsula.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Early on October\xc2\xa04, Keith emerged into the\n       <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Campeche" title="Bay of Campeche">\n        Bay of Campeche\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       where favorable conditions allowed convection to quickly redevelop.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">\n        <a href="#cite_note-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Later that day, it re-intensified into a tropical storm,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       with improved outflow and convective organization.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">\n        <a href="#cite_note-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On October\xc2\xa05, the Hurricane Hunters reported a circular eye had reformed along with winds supporting an upgrade to hurricane status.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">\n        <a href="#cite_note-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Keith intensified further that day, until it made its final landfall 23\xc2\xa0mi (37\xc2\xa0km) north of\n       <a href="/wiki/Tampico" title="Tampico">\n        Tampico\n       </a>\n       with winds of 90\xc2\xa0mph (150\xc2\xa0km/h).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       It rapidly weakened over the high terrain of northeastern Mexico,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">\n        <a href="#cite_note-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       dissipating late on October\xc2\xa06.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">\n        Preparations\n       </span>\n       <span    [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In Quintana Roo, 5,000\xc2\xa0people from\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chetumal,_Mexico" title="Chetumal, Mexico">\n  <p>\n       Keith\'s slow and erratic movement caused it to produce strong winds and heavy rainfall in Central America. Belize bore the brunt of the storm, with about $280\xc2\xa0million in damage and 19\xc2\xa0confirmed fatalities, primarily related to flooding. Several other countries reported significant but less severe flooding, including El Salvador, Guatemala, Honduras, Mexico, and Nicaragua. Overall, Keith was responsible for 40\xc2\xa0deaths,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-20">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-allenpress_30-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-allenpress-30">\n         [30]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       as well as approximately $319\xc2\xa0million in damage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-assessed_damage_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-assessed_damage-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CENAPRED_32-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CENAPRED-32">\n         [32]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Belize">\n the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bay_of_Chetumal" title="Bay of Chetumal">\n        Bay of Chetumal\n       </a>\n       . There were reports of people walking onto the temporarily dry bay floor, despite the potential for the waters to return in the event of shifting winds. At\n       <a href="/wiki/Caye_Caulker" title="Caye Caulker">\n        Caye Caulker\n       </a>\n       , the hurricane produced a 4\xc2\xa0ft (1.2\xc2\xa0m)\n       <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">\n        storm surge\n       </a>\n       from the west. Winds were unofficially estimated to have reached 125\xc2\xa0mph (201\xc2\xa0km/h) in the offshore islands of Belize. Wind gusts on the mainland reached 61\xc2\xa0mph (98\xc2\xa0km/h) at\n       <a href="/wiki/Philip_S._W._Goldson_International_Airport" title="Philip S. W. Goldson International Airport">\n        Philip S. W. Goldson International Airport\n       </a>\n       . Due to its slow motion, Keith dropped heavy rainfall, amounting to over 10\xc2\xa0in (250\xc2\xa0mm) in many parts of the country. The highest recorded precipitation total was 32.67 inches (830\xc2\xa0mm) at the international airport in Belize City.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-21">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The strong winds destroyed 130\xc2\xa0houses in Belize.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp103_22-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-afp103-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Two hotel roofs were wrecked, and the winds knocked down trees and power lines. Widespread areas of Belize lost power and telephone service, including Belize City. There, the heavy rainfall flooded streets,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dr102_26-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-dr102-26">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       reaching 3\xc2\xa0ft (0.91\xc2\xa0m) deep.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sa_33-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-sa-33">\n         [33]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Twenty homes were damaged to some degree in the capital, while many more suffered minor roof damage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ifrcdocs_20-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ifrcdocs-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The rains flooded the first floor of the primary hospital in the city,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34">\n        <a href="#cite_note-34">\n         [34]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       causing severe damage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocha6_35-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ocha6-35">\n         [35]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Nationwide, 11\xc2\xa0health facilities were damaged.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-paho_36-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-paho-36">\n         [36]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Elsewhere in the country, the rains increased levels along rivers,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocha4_18-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ocha4-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       including the\n       <a href="/wiki/Belize_River" title="Belize River">\n        Belize River\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Hondo_River_(Belize)" title="Hondo River (Belize)">\n        Rio Hondo\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/New_River_(Belize)" title="New River (Belize)">\n        New River\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ifrc1_37-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ifrc1-37">\n         [37]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The Belize River rose for several days after the storm,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fact3_38-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-fact3-38">\n         [38]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       reaching record levels of 21\xc2\xa0ft (6.6\xc2\xa0m) in width,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ifrc1_37-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ifrc1-37">\n         [37]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       which isolated 15\xc2\xa0villages.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-arc_39-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-arc-39">\n         [39]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Flooding covered\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Northern_Highway_(Belize)" title="Northern Highway (Belize)">\n        Northern Highway\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/George_Price_Highway" title="George Price Highway">\n        Western Highway\n       </a>\n       as well as the primary bridge from Belmopan to the rest of\n       <a href="/wiki/Cayo_District" title="Cayo District">\n        Cayo District\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocha5_40-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ocha5-40">\n         [40]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Residual floodwaters persisted due to the low-lying land of the country and poor drainage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocha1011_41-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ocha1011-41">\n         [41]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Floods contaminated water supply in the northern four districts,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-paho1010_42-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-paho1010-42">\n         [42]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       causing an\n       <a href="/wiki/Escherichia_coli" title="Escherichia coli">\n        E. coli\n       </a>\n       outbreak and food poisoning.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-paho1012_43-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-paho1012-43">\n         [43]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In Belize, Corozal, and Orange Walk districts, about 30% of the houses were severely damaged by flooding.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-paho1010_42-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-paho1010-42">\n         [42]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n id="cite_ref-paho1010_42-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-paho1010-42">\n         [42]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Throughout the country, approximately 3,279\xc2\xa0homes were either damaged or destroyed.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rw-kriner_47-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rw-kriner-47">\n         [47]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The damage total from Keith (23.61\xc2\xa0m), slightly under the 1976 record. Overall, the storm produced $117.8\xc2\xa0million in damage (2000 MXN, $12.4\xc2\xa0million 2000\xc2\xa0USD) in the state.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CENAPRED_32-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CENAPRED-32">\n         [32]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Nuevo_Le%C3%B3n" title="Nuevo Le\xc3\xb3n">\n        Nuevo Le\xc3\xb3n\n       </a>\n       , the remnants of Keith interacted with a cold front to produce heavy rainfall,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rain_49-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rain-49">\n         [49]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       reaching 14.43\xc2\xa0in (367\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Sabinas_Hidalgo,_Nuevo_Le%C3%B3n" title="Sabinas Hidalgo, Nuevo Le\xc3\xb3n">\n        Sabinas\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-27">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The average precipitation in the state was 5 inches (130\xc2\xa0mm), and the deluge caused causing mudslides in several cities, including San Pedro, Guadalupe and Escobedo. High rainfall also caused\n       <a href="/wiki/Flash_flood" title="Flash flood">\n        flash flood\n       </a>\n       in mountainous areas near\n       <a href="/wiki/Monterrey" title="Monterrey">\n        Monterrey\n       </a>\n       , forming swift river currents that rose up to 9,000\xc2\xa0ft\xc2\xb3/s (250\xc2\xa0m\xc2\xb3/s), and dragged 130\xc2\xa0people into local rivers; however, all were rescued. The rivers also dragged 30 vehicles, all of which were moderate to total losses. The storm runoff caused the El Cuchillo dam to receive 105,000\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Acre_foot" title="Acre foot">\n        acre feet\n       </a>\n       (130,000,000\xc2\xa0m\n       <sup>\n        3\n       </sup>\n       ) of water daily, and the La Boca dam to receive 810\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Acre_foot" title="Acre foot">\n        acre feet\n       </a>\n       (1,000,000\xc2\xa0m\n       <sup>\n        3\n       </sup>\n       ) per hour. About 13,000\xc2\xa0residents lost power. About 5,000\xc2\xa0people were forced evacuated from several municipalities in the state, including 2,465 from\n       <a href="/wiki/Monterrey_(municipality)" title="Monterrey (municipality)">\n        Monterrey\n       </a>\n       and 1,900 from rural areas, at a cost of $400,000 (2000\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/MXN" title="MXN">\n        MXN\n       </a>\n       ).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CENAPRED_32-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CENAPRED-32">\n         [32]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CENAPRED_32-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CENAPRED-32">\n         [32]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The total damages in the state rose to $115.6\xc2\xa0million (2000\n       <a href="/wiki/Mexican_peso" title="Mexican peso">\n        MXN\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">\n        US$\n       </a>\n       12.2\xc2\xa0million in year 2000), with the damage to urban infrastructure being the most expensive portion of the damage, with $44.2\xc2\xa0million (2000 MXN, US$4.6\xc2\xa0million in 2000) used to repair damage in eight municipalities; in particular,\n       <a href="/wiki/San_Pedro_Garza_Garc%C3%ADa" title="San Pedro Garza Garc\xc3\xada">\n        San Pedro Garza Garc\xc3\xada\n       </a>\n       received approximately three quarters of the infrastructure damage, with $30.8\xc2\xa0million (2000 MXN, $3.3\xc2\xa0million 2000\xc2\xa0USD). Additionally, about 460\xc2\xa0homes were damaged or destroyed, and 300\xc2\xa0families had to be relocated to safer areas.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CENAPRED_32-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CENAPRED-32">\n         [32]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Keith2000Olivia1a.png">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="619" data-file-width="890" height="153" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Keith2000Olivia1a.png/220px-Keith2000Olivia1a.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Keith2000Olivia1a.png/330px-Keith2000Olivia1a.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Keith2000Olivia1a.png/440px-Keith2000Olivia1a.png 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Keith2000Olivia1a.png" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Hurricane Keith making its final landfall in Mexico with\n         <a href="/wiki/2000_Pacific_hurricane_season#Tropical_Storm_Olivia" title="2000 Pacific hurricane season">\n          Tropical Storm Olivia\n         </a>\n         in the Pacific on October 5\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       In other states, damage was lighter, but still significant. In\n       <a href="/wiki/Sonora" title="Sonora">\n        Sonora\n       </a>\n       , total damages rose to $64.4\xc2\xa0million (2000 MXN, $6.8\xc2\xa0million 2000\xc2\xa0USD); in\n       <a href="/wiki/Chiapas" title="Chiapas">\n        Chiapas\n       </a>\n       , $25.6\xc2\xa0million (2000 MXN, $2.7\xc2\xa0million 2000\xc2\xa0USD) of property damage were caused by the storm. This brings up the total damage in Mexico to $365.9\xc2\xa0million (2000 MXN, $38.7\xc2\xa0million 2000\xc2\xa0USD).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CENAPRED_32-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CENAPRED-32">\n         [32]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Tabasco" title="Tabasco">\n        Tabasco\n       </a>\n       , flooding damaged 7,896\xc2\xa0houses.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-crs_51-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-crs-51">\n         [51]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Approximately 24,000\xc2\xa0residents in the state were impacted by flooding. At the capital city of\n       <a href="/wiki/Villahermosa" title="Villahermosa">\n        Villahermosa\n       </a>\n       , large rivers in the area overflowed, flooding some streets. However, dikes and sandbags along the banks of the rivers prevented further flooding.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-El_Diario_52-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-El_Diario-52">\n         [52]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Overall, about 46,000\xc2\xa0people had to evacuate Mexico due to the storm and its flooding.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocha7_29-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ocha7-29">\n         [29]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       There was one fatality when a man in Tampico was electrocuted by a downed power line.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-reut105_28-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-reut105-28">\n         [28]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Xicot%C3%A9ncatl,_Tamaulipas" title="Xicot\xc3\xa9ncatl, Tamaulipas">\n        Xicot\xc3\xa9ncatl, Tamaulipas\n       </a>\n       , a swollen river killed a family of six when their house was flooded.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53">\n        <a href="#cite_note-53">\n         [53]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Overall, Keith killed 23\xc2\xa0people in Mexico \xe2\x80\x93 13 in Veracruz, 4 in\n       <a href="/wiki/Puebla" title="Puebla">\n        Puebla\n       </a>\n       , and 6 in Tamaulipas. There were six indirect deaths when a plane crashed while attempting to land in\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reynosa,_Tamaulipas" title="Reynosa, Tamaulipas">\n        Reynosa, Tamaulipas\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-afp1010_31-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-afp1010-31">\n         [31]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Elsewhere">\n        Elsewhere\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Keith&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Elsewhere">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span amounts were very light.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ocha3_54-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ocha3-54">\n         [54]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       While Keith was organizing, the storm drew moisture from the eastern Pacific Ocean across Central America, producing heavy rainfall.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-55">\n        <a href="#cite_note-55">\n         [55]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In Guatemala, the rains caused flooding in ten towns and inundated approximately 500\xc2\xa0farms in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Melchor_de_Mencos" title="Melchor de Mencos">\n        Melchor de Mencos\n       </a>\n       municipality with about 4 inches (100\xc2\xa0mm) of water.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-28">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Additionally, one fatality was reported.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bbc_56-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-bbc-56">\n         [56]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Governor of\n       <a href="/wiki/Pet%C3%A9n_Department" title="Pet\xc3\xa9n Department">\n        Pet\xc3\xa9n Department\n       </a>\n       Ad\xc3\xa1n Regalado remarked that, "many communities are cut off by flooded rivers".\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-El_Diario_52-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-El_Diario-52">\n         [52]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In El Salvador, a 20-year-old man drowned in a river,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnn_57-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-cnn-57">\n         [57]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and 200\xc2\xa0families had to evacuate after several houses were damaged or destroyed.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-act102_58-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-act102-58">\n         [58]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Rainfall in Honduras forced over 200\xc2\xa0families to evacuate their houses. One person died when a wall collapsed,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59">\n        <a href="#cite_note-59">\n         [59]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and there were five other fatalities an aircraft disappeared near\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Roatan_Island" title="Roatan Island">\n        Roatan Island\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-29">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A bridge along the\n       <a href="/wiki/Pan-American_Highway" title="Pan-American Highway">\n        Pan-American Highway\n       </a>\n       between Honduras and Nicaragua was destroyed, which had been rebuilt following\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Mitch" title="Hurricane Mitch">\n        Hurricane Mitch\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-act102_58-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-act102-58">\n         [58]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Additionally, a government agency reported that as many as 80,000\xc2\xa0people in southern Honduras were left isolated.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-El_Diario_52-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-El_Diario-52">\n         [52]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In Nicaragua, floods from the storm forced 3,962\xc2\xa0people to evacuate their houses to 57\xc2\xa0shelters,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nic105_60-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nic105-60">\n         [60]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       many of which were schools.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usaid104_61-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-usaid104-61">\n         [61]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Floods affected 11 of the 17\n       <a href="/wiki/Departments_of_Nicaragua" title="Departments of Nicaragua">\n        Departments of Nicaragua\n       </a>\n       , which began receding on October\xc2\xa05 as Keith exited the region. The floods caused several landslides and covered roads, which isolated communities.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gon105_62-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-gon105-62">\n         [62]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Keith damaged 436\xc2\xa0houses in the country and destroyed another 160.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fact3_38-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-fact3-38">\n         [38]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       There were 12\xc2\xa0fatalities due to the storm in Nicaragua,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_2-30">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       one of whom a boy who was swept away by a fast moving flooded river northeast of Managua,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnn_57-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-cnn-57">\n         [57]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       while another death occurred after a man drowned in\n       <a href="/wiki/Lake_Managua" title="Lake Managua">\n        Lake Managua\n       </a>\n       . A man in the western portions of the country died after stepping on a power lines, which had fallen due to strong winds. Fifty communities were isolated, with at least thirteen of them due to impassable roads.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-paho_36-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-paho-36">\n         [36]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Several neighborhoods in\n       <a href="/wiki/Corinto,_Nicaragua" title="Corinto, Nicaragua">\n        Corinto\n       </a>\n       flooded, forcing the Civil Defense to evacuate 9\xc2\xa0families. One house collapsed in\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Los_%C3%81ngeles,_Regi%C3%B3n_Aut%C3%B3noma_del_Atl%C3%A1ntico_Sur" title="Los \xc3\x81ngeles, Regi\xc3\xb3n Aut\xc3\xb3noma del Atl\xc3\xa1ntico Sur">\n        Los \xc3\x81ngeles\n       </a>\n       , though its ten occupant escape without injury. After rainfall lashed In\n       <a href="/wiki/Villanueva,_Chinandega" title="Villanueva, Chinandega">\n        Villanueva, Chinandega\n       </a>\n       , for six days, the resultant flooding forced 300\xc2\xa0families evacuated, while there was significant losses the corn, beans and plantains crops. An additional 20\xc2\xa0families left their homes due to flooding in\n       <a href="/wiki/Poneloya,_Nicaragua" title="Poneloya, Nicaragua">\n        Poneloya\n       </a>\n       . About 15\xc2\xa0families in the port city of\n       <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Sandino" title="Puerto Sandino">\n        Puerto Sandino\n       </a>\n       were evacuated after the El Contrabando river overflowed. Another 18\xc2\xa0homes were flooded in the region of Salinas Grandes. About 52\xc2\xa0fled Troilo after at least 11\xc2\xa0homes flooded.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63">\n        <a href="#cite_note-63">\n         [63]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">\n        Aftermath\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n id="cite_ref-Belize_66-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Belize-66">\n         [66]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       By that time, boat service was re-instated with the most affected areas to pick
