</table>\n      <p>\n       <i>\n        <b>\n         Caf\xc3\xa9 Racers\n        </b>\n       </i>\n       is the eighth studio album by\n       <a href="/wiki/Kim_Carnes" title="Kim Carnes">\n        Kim Carnes\n       </a>\n       , released in 1983 (see\n       <a href="/wiki/1983_in_music" title="1983 in music">\n        1983 in music\n       </a>\n       ).\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The album spawned three chart singles in the United States, "\n       <a href="/wiki/Invisible_Hands_(song)" title="Invisible Hands (song)">\n        Invisible Hands\n       </a>\n       ", "\n       <a href="/wiki/You_Make_My_Heart_Beat_Faster_(And_That%27s_All_That_Matters)" title="You Make My Heart Beat Faster (And That\'s All That Matters)">\n        You Make My Heart Beat Faster (And That\'s All That Matters)\n       </a>\n       ", and "I Pretend" which charted on various\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Billboard_(magazine)" title="Billboard (magazine)">\n         Billboard\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       charts. "The Universal Song" was also released as a single in West Germany, Austria, Switzerland, the Netherlands and Scandinavia.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The song "I\'ll Be Here Where the Heart Is" was included on the chart-topping, six times Platinum\n       <a href="/wiki/Flashdance_(soundtrack)" title="Flashdance (soundtrack)">\n        <i>\n         Flashdance\n        </i>\n        soundtrack\n       </a>\n       which received a\n       <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">\n        Grammy Award\n       </a>\n       for Best Album Of Original Score Written for a Motion Picture at 26th Annual Grammy Awards. It was also released as a single in Germany, Netherlands, Spain and France.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Kim\'s album, meanwhile, was a commercial failure, running out of steam at #97 on the\n
