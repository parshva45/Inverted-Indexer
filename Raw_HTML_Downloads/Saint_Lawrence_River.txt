It traverses the\n       <a href="/wiki/Provinces_and_territories_of_Canada" title="Provinces and territories of Canada">\n        Canadian provinces\n       </a>\n       of\n       <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">\n        Quebec\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">\n        Ontario\n       </a>\n       , and is part of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Canada%E2%80%93United_States_border" title="Canada\xe2\x80\x93United States border">\n        international boundary\n       </a>\n       between Ontario, Canada, and the\n       <a href="/wiki/U.S._state" title="U.S. state">\n        U.S. state\n       </a>\n       of\n       <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">\n        New York\n       </a>\n       . This river also provides the basis of the commercial\n       <a href="/wiki/Saint_Lawrence_Seaway" title="Saint Lawrence Seaway">\n        Saint Lawrence Seaway\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n id="Geography">\n        Geography\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Lawrence_River&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geography">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       The Saint Lawrence River begins at the outflow of\n       <a href="/wiki/Lake_Ontario" title="Lake Ontario">\n        Lake Ontario\n       </a>\n       and flows through\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gananoque,_Ontario" title="Gananoque, Ontario">\n        Gananoque\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Brockville" title="Brockville">\n        Brockville\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Morristown,_New_York" title="Morristown, New York">\n        Morristown\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Ogdensburg,_New_York" title="Ogdensburg, New York">\n        Ogdensburg\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Massena_(village),_New_York" title="Massena (village), New York">\n        Massena\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Cornwall,_Ontario" title="Cornwall, Ontario">\n        Cornwall\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">\n        Montreal\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Trois-Rivi%C3%A8res" title="Trois-Rivi\xc3\xa8res">\n        Trois-Rivi\xc3\xa8res\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Quebec_City" title="Quebec City">\n        Quebec City\n       </a>\n       before draining into the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Saint_Lawrence" title="Gulf of Saint Lawrence">\n        Gulf of Saint Lawrence\n       </a>\n       , the largest\n       <a href="/wiki/Estuary" title="Estuary">\n        estuary\n       </a>\n       in the world.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HuffPo_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HuffPo-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The estuary begins at the eastern tip of\n       <a href="/wiki/%C3%8Ele_d%27Orl%C3%A9ans" title="\xc3\x8ele d\'Orl\xc3\xa9ans">\n        \xc3\x8ele d\'Orl\xc3\xa9ans\n       </a>\n       , just downstream from Quebec City.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BenkeCushing2005_2-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-BenkeCushing2005-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The river becomes tidal around Quebec City.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Dawson2007_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Dawson2007-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The Saint Lawrence River runs 3,058\n       <a href="/wiki/Kilometre" title="Kilometre">\n        kilometres\n       </a>\n       (1,900\n       <a href="/wiki/Mile" title="Mile">\n        mi\n       </a>\n       ) from the farthest headwater to the mouth and 1,197\xc2\xa0km (743.8\xc2\xa0mi) from the outflow of Lake Ontario. These numbers include the estuary; without the estuary the length from Lake Ontario is ca. 500 km (ca. 300 mi). The farthest headwater is the\n       <a href="/wiki/North_River_(Minnesota)" title="North River (Minnesota)">\n        North River\n       </a>\n       in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Mesabi_Range" title="Mesabi Range">\n        Mesabi Range\n       </a>\n       at\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hibbing" title="Hibbing">\n        Hibbing\n       </a>\n       , Minnesota. Its drainage area, which includes the Great Lakes, the world\'s largest system of freshwater lakes, is 1,344,200 square kilometres (518,998.5\xc2\xa0sq\xc2\xa0mi), of which 839,200\xc2\xa0km\n       <sup>\n        2\n       </sup>\n       (324,016.9\xc2\xa0sq\xc2\xa0mi) is in Canada and 505,000\xc2\xa0km\n       <sup>\n        2\n       </sup>\n       (194,981.6\xc2\xa0sq\xc2\xa0mi) is in the United States. The basin covers parts of\n       <a href="/wiki/Ontario" title="Ontario">\n        Ontario\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Quebec" title="Quebec">\n        Quebec\n       </a>\n       in Canada, parts of\n       <a href="/wiki/Illinois" title="Illinois">\n        Illinois\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Indiana" title="Indiana">\n        Indiana\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Minnesota" title="Minnesota">\n        Minnesota\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/New_York_(state)" title="New York (state)">\n        New York\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Ohio" title="Ohio">\n        Ohio\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">\n        Pennsylvania\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Vermont" title="Vermont">\n        Vermont\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Wisconsin" title="Wisconsin">\n        Wisconsin\n       </a>\n       , and nearly the entirety of the state of\n       <a href="/wiki/Michigan" title="Michigan">\n        Michigan\n       </a>\n       in the United States. The average discharge below the\n       <a href="/wiki/Saguenay_River" title="Saguenay River">\n        Saguenay River\n       </a>\n       is 16,800 cubic metres per second (590,000\xc2\xa0cu\xc2\xa0ft/s). At Quebec City, it is 12,101\xc2\xa0m\n       <sup>\n        3\n       </sup>\n       /s (427,300\xc2\xa0cu\xc2\xa0ft/s). The average discharge at the river\'s source, the outflow of Lake Ontario, is 7,410\xc2\xa0m\n       <sup>\n        3\n       </sup>\n       /s (262,000\xc2\xa0cu\xc2\xa0ft/s).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BenkeCushing2005_2-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-BenkeCushing2005-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The Saint Lawrence River includes\n       <a href="/wiki/Lake_Saint-Louis" title="Lake Saint-Louis">\n        Lake Saint-Louis\n       </a>\n       south of\n       <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">\n        Montreal\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Lake_Saint_Francis_(Canada)" title="Lake Saint Francis (Canada)">\n        Lake Saint Francis\n       </a>\n       at\n       <a href="/wiki/Salaberry-de-Valleyfield" title="Salaberry-de-Valleyfield">\n        Salaberry-de-Valleyfield\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lac_Saint-Pierre" title="Lac Saint-Pierre">\n        Lac Saint-Pierre\n       </a>\n       east of Montreal. It encompasses four\n       <a href="/wiki/Archipelago" title="Archipelago">\n        archipelagoes\n       </a>\n       : the\n       <a href="/wiki/Thousand_Islands" title="Thousand Islands">\n        Thousand Islands\n       </a>\n       chain near\n       <a href="/wiki/Alexandria_Bay,_New_York" title="Alexandria Bay, New York">\n        Alexandria Bay, New York\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Kingston,_Ontario" title="Kingston, Ontario">\n        Kingston, Ontario\n       </a>\n       ; the\n       <a href="/wiki/Hochelaga_Archipelago" title="Hochelaga Archipelago">\n        Hochelaga Archipelago\n       </a>\n       , including the\n       <a href="/wiki/Island_of_Montreal" title="Island of Montreal">\n        Island of Montreal\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/%C3%8Ele_J%C3%A9sus" title="\xc3\x8ele J\xc3\xa9sus">\n        \xc3\x8ele J\xc3\xa9sus\n       </a>\n       (\n       <a href="/wiki/Laval,_Quebec" title="Laval, Quebec">\n        Laval\n       </a>\n       ); the Lake St.\xc2\xa0Pierre Archipelago (classified biosphere world reserve by the UNESCO in 2000)\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and the smaller\n       <a href="/wiki/Mingan_Archipelago" title="Mingan Archipelago">\n        Mingan Archipelago\n       </a>\n       . Other islands include \xc3\x8ele d\'Orl\xc3\xa9ans near Quebec City and\n       <a href="/wiki/Anticosti_Island" title="Anticosti Island">\n        Anticosti Island\n       </a>\n       north of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gasp%C3%A9_Peninsula" title="Gasp\xc3\xa9 Peninsula">\n        Gasp\xc3\xa9\n       </a>\n       . It is the second longest river in Canada.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <a href="/wiki/Lake_Champlain" title="Lake Champlain">\n        Lake Champlain\n       </a>\n       and the\n       <a href="/wiki/Ottawa_River" title="Ottawa River">\n        Ottawa\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Richelieu_River" title="Richelieu River">\n        Richelieu\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Saint-Maurice_River" title="Saint-Maurice River">\n        Saint-Maurice\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint-Francois_River" title="Saint-Francois River">\n        Saint-Fran\xc3\xa7ois\n       </a>\n       and Saguenay rivers drain into the Saint Lawrence.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The Saint Lawrence River is in a seismically active zone where\n       <a href="/wiki/Fault_(geology)" title="Fault (geology)">\n        fault\n       </a>\n       reactivation is believed to occur along late\n       <a href="/wiki/Proterozoic" title="Proterozoic">\n        Proterozoic\n       </a>\n       to early\n       <a href="/wiki/Paleozoic" title="Paleozoic">\n        Paleozoic\n       </a>\n       normal faults related to the opening of\n       <a href="/wiki/Iapetus_Ocean" title="Iapetus Ocean">\n        Iapetus Ocean\n       </a>\n       . The faults in the area are\n       <a href="/wiki/Rift" title="Rift">\n        rift\n       </a>\n       related and are called the\n       <a href="/wiki/Saint_Lawrence_rift_system" title="Saint Lawrence rift system">\n        Saint Lawrence rift system\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       According to the United States Geological Survey, the Saint Lawrence Valley is a\n       <a href="/wiki/Physiographic_regions_of_the_world" title="Physiographic regions of the world">\n        physiographic province\n       </a>\n       of the larger\n       <a href="/wiki/Appalachian_Mountains" title="Appalachian Mountains">\n        Appalachian\n       </a>\n       division, containing the\n       <a href="/wiki/Champlain_Valley" title="Champlain Valley">\n        Champlain\n       </a>\n       and Northern physiographic section.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USGS-Water_7-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-USGS-Water-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       However, in Canada, where most of the valley is, it is instead considered part of a distinct\n       <a href="/wiki/Saint_Lawrence_Lowlands" title="Saint Lawrence Lowlands">\n        Saint Lawrence Lowlands\n       </a>\n       physiographic division, and not part of the Appalachian division at all.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">\n        History\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Saint_Lawrence_River&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Basques_Newfoundland.gif">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="522" data-file-width="665" height="173" class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Basques_Newfoundland.gif" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Basque settlements and sites dating from the 16th and 17th centuries\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Though the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Norse_colonization_of_the_Americas" title="Norse colonization of the Americas">\n        Norse\n       </a>\n       explored the Gulf of Saint Lawrence in the 11th century and were followed by fifteenth and early sixteenth century European mariners, such as\n       <a href="/wiki/John_Cabot" title="John Cabot">\n        John Cabot\n       </a>\n       , the brothers\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gaspar_Corte_Real" title="Gaspar Corte Real">\n        Gaspar\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Miguel_Corte-Real" title="Miguel Corte-Real">\n        Miguel Corte-Real\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Alonso_Sanchez" title="Alonso Sanchez">\n        Alonso Sanchez\n       </a>\n       , the first European explorer known to have sailed up the Saint Lawrence River itself was\n       <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Cartier" title="Jacques Cartier">\n        Jacques Cartier\n       </a>\n       . At that time, the land along the river was inhabited by the\n       <a href="/wiki/St._Lawrence_Iroquoians" title="St. Lawrence Iroquoians">\n        St. Lawrence Iroquoians\n       </a>\n       ; at the time of Cartier\'s second voyage in 1535. Because Cartier arrived in the estuary on\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lawrence_of_Rome" title="Lawrence of Rome">\n        Saint Lawrence\n       </a>\n       \'s feast day, he named it the\n       <i>\n        Gulf of Saint Lawrence\n       </i>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The Saint Lawrence River is partly within the U.S. and as such is that country\'s sixth oldest surviving European place-name.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The earliest regular Europeans in the area were the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/History_of_the_Basque_people#Modern_Period" title="History of the Basque people">\n        Basques\n       </a>\n       , who came to the St Lawrence Gulf and River in pursuit of whales from the early 16th century. The\n       <a href="/wiki/History_of_Basque_whaling" title="History of Basque whaling">\n        Basque whalers\n       </a>\n       and fishermen traded with indigenous Americans and set up settlements, leaving vestiges all over the coast of eastern Canada and deep into the Saint Lawrence River. Basque commercial and fishing activity reached its peak before the\n       <i>\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Armada_Invencible" title="Armada Invencible">\n         Armada Invencible\n        </a>\n        \'\n       </i>\n       s disaster (1588), when the Spanish Basque whaling fleet was confiscated by King\n       <a href="/wiki/Philip_II_of_Spain" title="Philip II of Spain">\n        Philip II of Spain\n       </a>\n       and largely destroyed. Initially, the whaling galleons from\n       <a href="/wiki/Labourd" title="Labourd">\n        Labourd\n       </a>\n       were not affected by the Spanish defeat.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Until the early 17th century, the French used the name\n       <i>\n        Rivi\xc3\xa8re du Canada\n       </i>\n       to designate the Saint Lawrence upstream to Montreal and the Ottawa River after Montreal. The Saint Lawrence River served as the main route for European exploration of the North American interior, first pioneered by French explorer\n       <a href="/wiki/Samuel_de_Champlain" title="Samuel de Champlain">\n        Samuel de Champlain\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Control of the river was crucial to\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Great_Britain_in_the_Seven_Years_War" title="Great Britain in the Seven Years War">\n        British strategy to capture New France\n       </a>\n       in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Seven_Years%27_War" title="Seven Years\' War">\n        Seven Years\' War\n       </a>\n       . Having\n       <a href="/wiki/Siege_of_Louisbourg_(1758)" title="Siege of Louisbourg (1758)">\n        captured Louisbourg\n       </a>\n       in 1758, the British sailed up to Quebec the following year thanks to charts drawn up by\n       <a href="/wiki/James_Cook" title="James Cook">\n        James Cook\n       </a>\n       . British troops were ferried via the Saint Lawrence to attack the city from the west, which they successfully did at the\n       <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_Plains_of_Abraham" title="Battle of the Plains of Abraham">\n        Battle of the Plains of Abraham\n       </a>\n       . The river was used again by the British\n       <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Neuville" title="Battle of Neuville">\n        to defeat\n       </a>\n       the French siege of Quebec under the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chevalier_de_L%C3%A9vis" title="Chevalier de L\xc3\xa9vis">\n        Chevalier de L\xc3\xa9vis\n       </a>\n       in 1760.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Because of the virtually impassable\n       <a href="/wiki/Lachine_Rapids" title="Lachine Rapids">\n        Lachine Rapids\n       </a>\n       , the Saint Lawrence was once continuously navigable only as far as Montreal. Opened in 1825, the\n       <a href="/wiki/Lachine_Canal" title="Lachine Canal">\n        Lachine Canal\n       </a>\n       was the first to allow ships to pass the rapids. An extensive system of canals and locks, known as the\n       <a href="/wiki/Saint_Lawrence_Seaway" title="Saint Lawrence Seaway">\n        Saint Lawrence Seaway\n       </a>\n       , was officially opened on 26 June 1959 by\n       <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II" title="Elizabeth II">\n        Elizabeth II\n       </a>\n       (representing Canada) and President\n       <a href="/wiki/Dwight_D._Eisenhower" title="Dwight D. Eisenhower">\n        Dwight D. Eisenhower\n       </a>\n       (representing the United States). The Seaway now permits ocean-going vessels to pass all the way to\n       <a href="/wiki/Lake_Superior" title="Lake Superior">\n        Lake Superior\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       During the\n       <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">\n        Second World War\n       </a>\n       , the\n       <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_the_St._Lawrence" title="Battle of the St. Lawrence">\n        Battle of the St. Lawrence\n       </a>\n       involved submarine and anti-submarine actions throughout the lower Saint Lawrence River and the entire\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Saint_Lawrence" title="Gulf of Saint Lawrence">\n        Gulf of Saint Lawrence\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Strait_of_Belle_Isle" title="Strait of Belle Isle">\n        Strait of Belle Isle\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Cabot_Strait" title="Cabot Strait">\n        Cabot Strait\n       </a>\n       from May to October 1942, September 1943, and again in October and November 1944. During this time, German\n       <a href="/wiki/U-boat" title="U-boat">\n        U-boats\n       </a>\n       sank several merchant marine ships and three Canadian warships.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In the late 1970s, the river was the subject of a successful ecological campaign (called "Save the River"), originally responding to planned development by the\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Corps_of_Engineers" title="United States Army Corps of Engineers">\n        United States Army Corps of Engineers\n       </a>\n       .
