The alarm was caused by a cooling pump that had failed and caused a\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Remote_Power_Controller&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Remote Power Controller (page does not exist)">\n        Remote Power Controller\n       </a>\n       to trip and cut power to some of the International Space Station (ISS). Astronauts Tracy Caldwell Dyson and Doug Wheelock performed some steps to assist ground controllers in re-powering some of the station components such as two main power buses and one\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Control_Moment_Gyroscope" title="Control Moment Gyroscope">\n        Control Moment Gyroscope\n       </a>\n       . After the steps had been completed\n       <a href="/wiki/Flight_controller#Capsule_Communicator" title="Flight controller">\n        Capcomm\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/James_M._Kelly_(astronaut)" title="James M. Kelly (astronaut)">\n        James Kelly\n       </a>\n       told the crew they could go back to bed as all the work required by the crew on the ISS was complete. A short time later, another alarm sounded and awoke the crew, when the ground attempted to restart the pump module.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-alarm_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-alarm-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Docking_ring">\n        Docking ring\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_24&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Docking ring">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       A failure in the docking ring on the Mini-Research Module 2 (MRM2)\n       <a href="/wiki/Poisk_(ISS_module)" title="Poisk (ISS module)">\n        Poisk\n       </a>\n       , caused a delay in the planned landing of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-18" title="Soyuz TMA-18">\n        Soyuz TMA-18\n       </a>\n       spacecraft. Soyuz TMA-18 was originally planned to undock and land on 24 September 2010, but instead undocked less than 24 hours later on 25 September 2010. The failure is believed to be due to a faulty indication from a micro-switch on the hatch between the Soyuz and MRM2. A drive gear, which is related to the docking mechanism was also found to have two broken teeth, and is believed to be related to the problem as well.\n       <sup class="reference"
