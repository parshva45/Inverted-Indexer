In the original ISS plans,\n       <i>\n        Nauka\n       </i>\n       was to use the location of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Rassvet_(ISS_module)" title="Rassvet (ISS module)">\n        Docking and Stowage Module\n       </a>\n       . Later, the DSM was moved to\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Zarya" title="Zarya">\n         Zarya\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       <span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">\n        \'\n       </span>\n       s nadir port and named\n       <a href="/wiki/Rassvet_(ISS_module)" title="Rassvet (ISS module)">\n        <i>\n         Rassvet\n        </i>\n       </a>\n       . During 2013, the launch was rescheduled to April 2014 and then to June 2014; NASA documents at that time indicated that the module would not fly before November 2015.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rsw_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rsw-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dec13_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-dec13-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ria_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ria-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In April 2014 it    <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nauka_(ISS_module)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Original plans for Nauka">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       In the 1990s, plans for the Russian segment of the ISS included several research modules that were intended to be adjunct to\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Zarya" title="Zarya">\n         Zarya\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       and\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Zvezda_(ISS_module)" title="Zvezda (ISS module)">\n         Zvezda\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       . In these earlier plans, the now-cancelled complex of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Universal_Docking_Module" title="Universal Docking Module">\n        Universal Docking Module\n       </a>\n       (UDM) and the two newly developed\n       <a href="/wiki/Russian_Research_Module" title="Russian Research Module">\n        Russian Research Modules\n       </a>\n       were to be attached\n       <a href="/wiki/Zvezda_(ISS_module)" title="Zvezda (ISS module)">\n        <i>\n         Zvezda\n        </i>\n        <span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">\n         \'\n        </span>\n        s\n       </a>\n       nadir port \xe2\x80\x93 the UDM was to be based on the\n       <a href="/wiki/Functional_Cargo_Block" title="Functional Cargo Block">\n        Functional Cargo Block\n       </a>\n       FGB-2. The FGB-2 was originally built as a backup for the original launch of the\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Zarya" title="Zarya">\n         Zarya\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       (FGB) module \xe2\x80\x93 FGB-2\'s construction had been halted at 70-percent completeness in the late 1990s.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       However, the plans changed in the early 2000s. In August 2004, it was decided to build the\n       <i>\n        Nauka\n       </i>\n       ISS module based on the FGB-2.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1997_memos_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-1997_memos-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       During that time there was an alternate, rejected proposal for\n       <i>\n        Nauka\n       </i>\n       from\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RKK_Energia" title="RKK Energia">\n        RKK Energia\n       </a>\n       , based on the cancelled Commercial Enterprise Module (entertainment and studio module), which was to be jointly funded by\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RKK_Energia" title="RKK Energia">\n        RKK Energia\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/SPACEHAB" title="SPACEHAB">\n        SPACEHAB\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Zak_6-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Zak-6">\n         [6]\n </a>\n       (ESA) agreed with the Russians that the\n       <a href="/wiki/European_Robotic_Arm" title="European Robotic Arm">\n        European Robotic Arm\n       </a>\n       would be launched together with\n       <i>\n        Nauka\n       </i>\n       , mated on its surface for a later deployment in space. A spare elbow joint for the European Robotic Arm was already launched together with\n       <a href="/wiki/Rassvet_(ISS_module)" title="Rassvet (ISS module)">\n        <i>\n         Rassvet\n        </i>\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In 2004, the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_Federal_Space_Agency" title="Russian Federal Space Agency">\n        Russian Federal Space Agency\n       </a>\n       stated that\n       <i>\n        Nauka\n       </i>\n       should be ready for launch in 2007 on a Russian\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Proton_rocket" title="Proton rocket">\n        Proton rocket\n       </a>\n       . However, the\n       <i>\n        Nauka\n       </i>\n       project was delayed further, first to 2008 and later to 2009. A November 2006 ESA bulletin mentioned that the RSA was negotiating with the ISS partners to push back the prospective launch date to the end of 2008. In October 2011, it was reported that\n       <i>\n        Nauka\n       </i>\n       was expected to be launched at the end of 2013.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rus_7-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Rus-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In May 2012, it was reported that the launch date had been pushed back to 2014.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The next planned launch date was April 2014, according to Vitaly Lopota, president of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/RSC_Energia" title="RSC Energia">\n        RSC Energia\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dec13_2-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-dec13-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       On October 25, 2013, Parabolic Arc reported that Nauka was flawed and had failed acceptance testing at RKK Energia.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ria_3-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ria-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Found problems in the propulsion system included a leaking fueling valve that needed to be replaced and contamination, which would require a lengthy cleaning.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rsw_1-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rsw-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The module was to be returned to Khrunichev for repairs which would take another 12 to 18 months.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ria_3-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ria-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On November 27, 2013 it was reported that the Russian Space Agency had informed NASA that the launch had been postponed until at least 2015.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In April 2014 it emerged that the target date for launch was postponed to February 2017: a new propulsion system needed to be manufactured, as the installed had exceeded its warranty.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rsw_1-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rsw-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Usage">\n        Usage\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       <i>\n        Nauka\n       </i>\n       will initially be used for experiments, docking and cargo. It will also serve as a crew work and rest area.\n       <i>\n        Nauka\n       </i>\n       will be equipped with full guidance and navigation control including engines and an\n       <a href="/wiki/Attitude_control" title="Attitude control">\n        attitude control\n       </a>\n       system that can be used as a backup by the ISS. It will be docked onto the\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Zvezda_(ISS_module)" title="Zvezda (ISS module)">\n         Zvezda\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       module\'s nadir (Earth facing) docking port. Outfitting equipment launched in 2010 with the\n       <a href="/wiki/Rassvet_(ISS_module)" title="Rassvet (ISS module)">\n        <i>\n         Rassvet\n        </i>\n       </a>\n       (Mini-Research Module 1) on\n       <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">\n        NASA\n       </a>\n       \'s\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-132" title="STS-132">\n        STS-132\n       </a>\n       will also be used for\n       <i>\n        Nauka\n       </i>\n       , including the spare elbow joint for the European Robotic Arm, internal hardware and an experimental airlock to be positioned on one of the side-facing ports at the bottom of the module. The new module will contain crew quarters with life support equipment including atmospheric processing, galley and toilet.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Rus_7-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Rus-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Primary_research_module">\n        Primary research module\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Nauka_(ISS_module)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Primary research module">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       <i>\n        Nauka\n       </i>\n       will be Russia\'s primary ISS research module. For some time, NASA\'s official plans included a second research module around the same size as\n       <i>\n        Nauka\n       </i>\n       , listed to be "under review", but it was eventually cancelled, leaving\n       <i>\n        Nauka\n       </i>\n       to be the only\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_research_module" title="Russian research module">\n        Russian research module\n       </a>\n       besides\n       <i>\n        Rassvet\n       </i>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Poisk_(ISS_module)" title="Poisk (ISS module)">\n        <i>\n         Poisk\n        </i>\n       </a>\n       (Mini-Research Module 2).\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Specifications">\n        Specifications\n       </span>\n       <span
