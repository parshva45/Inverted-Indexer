</div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The mission was to have conducted\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Space_walk" title="Space walk">\n        space walks\n       </a>\n       on several occasions, using both NASA and Russian\n       <a href="/wiki/Space_suit" title="Space suit">\n        space suits\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       On 28 July 2005 at 11:18 UTC, during mission\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-114" title="STS-114">\n        STS-114\n       </a>\n       , the\n       <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Discovery" title="Space Shuttle Discovery">\n        Space Shuttle Discovery\n       </a>\n       , docked to the Station, and delivered a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Control_Moment_Gyroscope" title="Control Moment Gyroscope">\n        Control Moment Gyroscope\n       </a>\n       to replace one failed unit and the\n       <a href="/wiki/External_stowage_platform" title="External stowage platform">\n        External stowage platform\n       </a>\n       2 as part of the approximately 4.100\xc2\xa0kg cargo carried in Discovery\'s payload bay and inside the\n       <a href="/wiki/Multi-Purpose_Logistics_Module" title="Multi-Purpose Logistics Module">\n        Multi-Purpose Logistics Module\n       </a>\n       Raffaello. On 6 August 2005 the Orbiter undocked from the ISS taking the MPLM back.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       During the Expedition 11 mission, Russian Commander Sergei Krikalev exceeded the record for total time in space (formerly held by\n       <a href="/wiki/Sergei_Avdeyev" title="Sergei Avdeyev">\n        Sergei Avdeyev\n       </a>\n       with 747.593 days). Krikalev at launch had spent 624.387 days in space. He passed the record on the 123rd day of the mission, on 16 August 2005. His cumulative time in space was 803 days and 9 hours and 39 minutes upon landing.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       On 7 September 2005 the unpiloted\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Progress_spacecraft" title="Progress spacecraft">\n        Progress spacecraft\n       </a>\n       53 (P18) undocked from the station and was deorbited, to make way for the arrival of Progress 54 (P19) which docked in September 2005 and transferred around 2300\xc2\xa0kg of cargo, (fuel, water, and dry cargo including oxygen generators) to the station.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       On 3 October 2005\n       <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-7" title="Soyuz TMA-7">\n        Soyuz TMA-7\n       </a>\n       docked bringing the\n       <a href="/wiki/Expedition_12" title="Expedition 12">\n        Expedition 12\n       </a>\n       crew.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Reiter" title="Thomas Reiter">\n        Thomas Reiter\n       </a>\n       (\n       <a href="/wiki/European_Space_Agency" title="European Space Agency">\n        ESA\n       </a>\n       ) was scheduled to join the mission in October 2005 on the supply mission\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-121" title="STS-121">\n        STS-121\n       </a>\n       to the ISS, but due to that mission\'s delay until 2006 he became a crew member of\n       <a href="/wiki/Expedition_13" title="Expedition 13">\n        Expedition 13\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Spacewalks">\n        Spacewalks\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_11&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Spacewalks">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       On 18 August 2005 19:02 UTC (3:02\xc2\xa0p.m. EDT) the crew started a 4-hour, 58-minute spacewalk. They removed and brought inside the station a Russian Biorisk experiment container housing\n       <a href="/wiki/Bacteria" title="Bacteria">\n        bacteria\n       </a>\n       from the outside of Pirs; an MPAC (a micrometeoroid and orbital debris collector) and SEED (a materials exposure array) panel from the\n       <a href="/wiki/Zvezda_(ISS_module)" title="Zvezda (ISS module)">\n        Zvezda Service Module\n       </a>\n       ; and the Matroska experiment, (\n       <a href="/wiki/Ionizing_radiation" title="Ionizing radiation">\n        radiation\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Dosimeter" title="Dosimeter">\n        dosimeters\n       </a>\n       in human-tissue-equivalent material). They installed a television camera on Zvezda, and checked a Korma contamination-exposure experiment tablet, and removed and replaced a materials exposure experiment container.\n      </p>\n
