  <p>\n       <b>\n        Hurricane Ione\n       </b>\n       <span class="nowrap">\n        <span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt">\n         <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">\n          /\n          <span style="border-bottom:1px dotted">\n           <span title="/a\xc9\xaa/: \'i\' in \'tide\'">\n            a\xc9\xaa\n           </span>\n           <span title="/\xcb\x88/: primary stress follows">\n            \xcb\x88\n           </span>\n           <span title="/o\xca\x8a/: \'o\' in \'code\'">\n            o\xca\x8a\n           </span>\n           <span title="\'n\' in \'nigh\'">\n            n\n           </span>\n          </span>\n          /\n         </a>\n        </span>\n       </span>\n       was a strong,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Category_4_hurricane" title="Category 4 hurricane">\n        Category\xc2\xa04 hurricane\n       </a>\n       that affected\n       <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">\n        North Carolina\n       </a>\n       in September 1955, bringing high winds and significant rainfall.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rainfall_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rainfall-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       It came on the heels of Hurricanes\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Connie" title="Hurricane Connie">\n        Connie\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Diane" title="Hurricane Diane">\n        Diane\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and compounded problems already caused by the two earlier hurricanes. Spawned by a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">\n        tropical wave\n       </a>\n       which left the\n       <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">\n        African\n       </a>\n       coast on September 6, the system became a tropical depression in the tropical North Atlantic, before turning northwest and developing into a hurricane. After turning back to the west east of the Bahamas, Ione turned northwest and northward, moving across eastern North Carolina before moving east-northeastward out to sea. Ione caused $600\xc2\xa0million (2005\xc2\xa0USD) in damage, much of it to crops across North Carolina. As a result of Ione\'s impacts seven people lost their lives.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n <p>\n       A tropical wave moved through\n       <a href="/wiki/Cape_Verde" title="Cape Verde">\n        Cape Verde\n       </a>\n       on September 6 and on September 11 Ione developed into a tropical depression.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rainfall_2-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rainfall-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Ione remained weak for the next few days, and then began to steadily intensify as it moved north of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">\n        Lesser Antilles\n       </a>\n       , reaching hurricane strength on September 15.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rainfall_2-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rainfall-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Conditions were favorable for additional development, and Ione peaked with winds of 140 miles per hour (230\xc2\xa0km/h) on September 18 while north of the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bahamas" title="Bahamas">\n        Bahamas\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_3-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Drier and cooler air gradually became entrained in Ione\'s circulation, and the storm weakened into a Category 2 hurricane at the time of its\n       <a href="/wiki/Wilmington,_North_Carolina" title="Wilmington, North Carolina">\n        Wilmington, North Carolina\n       </a>\n       , landfall on September 19, which made Ione the third hurricane to hit the state in six weeks and fourth in 11\xc2\xa0months.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rainfall_2-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rainfall-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Ione was the first tropical cyclone to be observed on the Cape Hatteras radar during landfall and was one of the first observed to make small-scale oscillations within its track.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-smallscale_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-smallscale-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The storm weakened to a tropical storm over land but restrengthened to a Category\xc2\xa02 hurricane over the northwestern Atlantic.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_1-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Ione continued northeastward and became an\n       <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">\n        extratropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       on September 21. The extratropical storm crossed over\n       <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_(island)" title="Newfoundland (island)">\n        Newfoundland\n       </a>\n       and was last seen on September 24 moving across the North Atlantic.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-advisories_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-advisories-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">\n        Preparations\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Ione&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Hurricane warnings were in effect from the Virginia Capes to Morehead, North Carolina and a Hurricane alert was issued northward to\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_City,_New_Jersey" title="Atlantic City, New Jersey">\n        Atlantic City, New Jersey\n       </a>\n       . Dump trucks loaded with dirt were ready to make earthen dikes around the Riegel paper mill, due to the severe damage caused to it by hurricanes earlier that year which caused the 1955 Delaware River flood.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nj.com_6-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nj.com-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Recent flooding, from\n       <a       </a>\n       kept residents along its banks alert should the storm strike, business owners were also ready to move their merchandise if the need arose.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nj.com_6-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nj.com-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">\n        New York City\n       </a>\n       officials prepared for the worst, with police officers filling gas tanks of their patrol cars and extra patrol men being called to duty in advance of the hurricane.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Time_7-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Time-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Flights were canceled in New York, firemen stood at the ready in\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Staten_Island,_New_York" title="Staten Island, New York">\n        Staten Island\n       </a>\n       , and the\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">\n        Coast Guard\n       </a>\n       warned shipping companies of the impending storm.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Time_7-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Time-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A boxing match between\n       <a href="/wiki/Rocky_Marciano" title="Rocky Marciano">\n        Rocky Marciano\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Archie_Moore" title="Archie Moore">\n        Archie Moore\n       </a>\n       was delayed, which allowed the promoters to sell an additional $150,000 (1955) in tickets, which is $1.34\xc2\xa0million in present-day figures.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Time_7-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Time-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">\n        Impact\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Ione&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Impact">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Ione1955filledrainblk.GIF">\n         <img alt="A rainfall graphic showing the eastern seaboard of the eastern seaboard of the United States. The Diamond Shoals lightship was reported to have broken loose during the hurricane. Tides did not fully recede at Swan Quarter until 3 a.m. on September 20, with winds finally dying down after 7 a.m. the same day.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_8-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-history-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Cherry Point recorded a maximum sustained wind of 75 miles per hour (121\xc2\xa0km/h), with gusts to 107 miles per hour (172\xc2\xa0km/h). Prolonged easterly winds forced the tide to increase to levels 3 feet (0.91\xc2\xa0m) to 10 feet (3.0\xc2\xa0m) above normal, which resulted in one of the largest inundations in North Carolina history.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The storm surge from Ione was recorded at 3.6 feet (1.1\xc2\xa0m) at Morehead City, North Carolina, while a storm tide of 5.3 feet (1.6\xc2\xa0m) was measured at\n       <a href="/wiki/Wrightsville_Beach,_North_Carolina" title="Wrightsville Beach, North Carolina">\n        Wrightsville Beach, North Carolina\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-surge_10-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-surge-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       It was reported that the storm lasted for nearly two days in North Carolina.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history_8-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-history-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       HMTS\n       <i>\n        Monarch\n       </i>\n       , which had departed from Newfoundland, was laying telephone cable across the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlantic" title="Atlantic">\n        Atlantic\n       </a>\n       ocean when Ione crossed its path.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-monarch_11-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-monarch-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The ship weathered the storm, and managed to reach Scotland to complete its mission by September 26.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-monarch_11-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-monarch-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Ione caused $600\xc2\xa0million (2005\xc2\xa0USD) in damage, much of it to crops across North Carolina.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_3-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The rest came from flooding in an area already devastated by Hurricanes\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Connie" title="Hurricane Connie">\n        Connie\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Diane" title="Hurricane Diane">\n        Diane\n       </a>\n       six weeks earlier.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hurricanes_12-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-hurricanes-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Along the Trent River near\n       <a href="/wiki/Trent_Woods,_North_Carolina" title="Trent Woods, North Carolina">\n        Trent\n       </a>\n       and the New River near Gum Branch, North Carolina, the river stage reached heights not seen again until\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Floyd" title="Hurricane Floyd">\n        Hurricane Floyd\n       </a>\n       of the\n       <a href="/wiki/1999_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1999 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1999 Atlantic hurricane season\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USGS_13-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-USGS-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Ione caused seven deaths in North Carolina, a low number attributed to well-executed warnings and effectiveness of relief groups such as the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Red_Cross" title="Red Cross">\n        Red Cross\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_3-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hurricanes_12-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-hurricanes-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Ione made landfall in Newfoundland, Canada during the evening of September 21 as a tropical storm and as a result cause wind and rain damage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-canada_14-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-canada-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       That day the storm became extratropical, yet still had negative effects on Canada.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-advisories_5-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-advisories-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference"   </a>\n       a boy broke his leg after being blown into a car and thousands of dollars in damage, as well as broken polls, uprooted trees, interrupted telephone service, damaged chimneys and power outages were reported in\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._John%27s,_Canada" title="St. John\'s, Canada">\n        St. John\'s, Canada\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup torn for its foundation.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-canada_14-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-canada-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Three flights were cancelled, schools were closed and the ferry service between\n       <a href="/wiki/Bell_Island_(Newfoundland_and_Labrador)" title="Bell Island (Newfoundland and Labrador)">\n        Bell Island\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Portugal_Cove_%E2%80%93_St._Philip%27s" title="Portugal Cove \xe2\x80\x93 St. Philip\'s">\n        Portugal Cove\n       </a>\n       was disrupted.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-canada_14-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-canada-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On September 23 there were reports of boats that had been lost and in\n       <a href="/wiki/Harbour_Grace" title="Harbour Grace">\n        Harbour Grace\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Harbour_Main" title="Harbour Main">\n        Harbour Main\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-canada_14-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-canada-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       There was also a report of a garage being flipped with a car still in it.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-canada_14-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-canada-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       There were also reports of a street and two mines being flooded in\n       <a href="/wiki/Corner_Brook" title="Corner Brook">\n        Corner Brook\n       </a>\n       and in\n       <a href="/wiki/Swift_Current,_Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="Swift Current, Newfoundland and Labrador">\n        Swift Current\n       </a>\n       and damage to the power company.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-canada_14-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-canada-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On September 27 it was reported that buoys were
