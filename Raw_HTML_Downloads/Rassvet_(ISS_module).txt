This planned Russian element was intended to provide facilities for stowage and an additional docking port, and would have been launched to the station on a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Proton_(rocket)" title="Proton (rocket)">\n        Proton\n       </a>\n       launch vehicle. The DSM would have been mounted to\n       <i>\n        Zarya\n       </i>\n       \'s nadir (Earth-facing) docking port. It would have been similar in size and shape to the\n       <i>\n        Zarya\n       </i>\n       module.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The DSM was cancelled due to Russian budgetary constraints for some time, but its design was eventually modified into the Docking and Cargo Module (\n       <i>\n        Rassvet\n       </i>\n       ) that was to be connected to the same\n       <i>\n        Zarya\n       </i>\n       location to provide stowage space and a docking port. During the cancellation period, it was proposed that a Multi Purpose Module (MPM) called\n       <i>\n        Enterprise\n       </i>\n       should be docked to\n       <i>\n        Zarya\n       </i>\n       , and later the Multipurpose Laboratory Module (MLM) was proposed to be located there as well, but the\n       <i>\n        Enterprise\n       </i>\n       module has since been cancelled and the MLM will be docked to\n       <i>\n        Zvezda\'s\n       </i>\n       nadir port instead.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Purpose">\n        Purpose\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Rassvet_(ISS_module)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Purpose">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       <i>\n        Rassvet\n       </i>\n       was designed as a solution to two problems facing the ISS partners:\n      </p>\n      <ol>\n       <li>\n        NASA was under contract to carry the MLM outfitting equipment into space.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        The overlapping missions of the\n        <a href="/wiki/Progress_(spacecraft)" title="Progress (spacecraft)">\n         Progress\n        </a>\n        ,\n        <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_programme" title="Soyuz programme">\n         Soyuz\n        </a>\n        , and\n        <a href="/wiki/Automated_Transfer_Vehicle" title="Automated Transfer Vehicle">\n         ATV\n        </a>\n        spacecraft highlighted the need to have four Russian docking ports available on the ISS. The cancellation of both Russian Research Modules meant that the ISS would be left with just three such docking ports after the installation of the\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Permanent_Multipurpose_Module" title="Permanent Multipurpose Module">\n         Permanent Multipurpose Module\n        </a>\n        in 2011, which made the nadir port of\n        <i>\n         Zarya\n        </i>\n        unusable.\n       </li>\n      </ol>\n      <p>\n       <i>\n        Rassvet\n       </i>\n       solved both of these issues. NASA did not need to add another payload flight to accommodate the MLM outfitting equipment, as it could attach the hardware to the exterior of MRM-1. The ISS now had 4 docking ports available on the Russian segment: the aft port of\n       <i>\n        Zvezda\n       </i>\n       , the port of\n       <i>\n        Pirs\n       </i>\n       , later MLM (on the nadir port of\n       <i>\n        Zvezda\n       </i>\n       ), the port of MRM-2 (on the zenith port of\n       <i>\n        Zvezda\n       </i>\n       ), and the port on MRM-1 (on the nadir port of\n       <i>\n        Zarya\n       </i>\n       ). Russia\'s cancellation of the Research Module thus came to be of less consequence for the ISS program as a whole.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Design_and_construction">\n        Design and construction\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Rassvet_(ISS_module)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Design and construction">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:STS132_MRM1_Media_Event3.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2000" data-file-width="3000" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/STS132_MRM1_Media_Event3.jpg/220px-STS132_MRM1_Media_Event3.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/STS132_MRM1_Media_Event3.jpg/330px-STS132_MRM1_Media_Event3.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/STS132_MRM1_Media_Event3.jpg/440px-STS132_MRM1_Media_Event3.jpg 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:STS132_MRM1_Media_Event3.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         The Experiment Airlock on the\n         <i>\n          Rassvet\n         </i>\n         module.\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The module was designed and built by\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/S.P._Korolev_Rocket_and_Space_Corporation_Energia" title="S.P. Korolev Rocket and Space Corporation Energia">\n        S.P. Korolev RSC Energia\n       </a>\n       , from the already-made pressurized hull of the mock-up for dynamic tests of the canceled\n       <a href="/wiki/Science_Power_Platform" title="Science Power Platform">\n        Science Power Platform\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       On December 17, 2009, an\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Antonov_An-124" title="Antonov An-124">\n        Antonov An-124\n       </a>\n       carrying the\n       <i>\n        Rassvet\n       </i>\n       Module and ground process equipment arrived at the\n       <a href="/wiki/Kennedy_Space_Center" title="Kennedy Space Center">\n        Kennedy Space Center\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">\n        Florida\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Upon unloading, the equipment was delivered to a prelaunch processing facility run by the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Astrotech" title="Astrotech">\n        Astrotech\n       </a>\n       . Energia specialists and technicians continued their work on the processing of the\n       <i>\n        Rassvet\n       </i>\n       module at the facility, completing stand-alone electrical tests and leak tests of the module and the airlock. They also prepared the airlock and the radiative heat exchanger for installation onto Rassvet. The module was moved to NASA\'s Space Station Processing Facility on April 2, 2010. After completing the final touches, it was placed into the shuttle payload transporter on April 5, 2010. The payload canister containing the\n       <i>\n        Rassvet\n       </i>\n       Module arrived at\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Launch_Pad_39-A" title="Launch Pad 39-A">\n        Launch Pad 39A\n       </a>\n       on April 15, 2010.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Engineers at Launch Pad 39A preparing\n       <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Atlantis" title="Space Shuttle Atlantis">\n        Space Shuttle\n        <i>\n         Atlantis\n        </i>\n       </a>\n       had noticed paint peeling from the MRM-1 module. Although the problem was declared to have no impact on the operation of\n       <i>\n        Rassvet\n       </i>\n       , it posed a potential threat of releasing debris on orbit.\n       <sup
