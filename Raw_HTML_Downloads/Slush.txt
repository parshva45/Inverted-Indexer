This often mixes with dirt and other materials, resulting in a gray or muddy brown color. Often, solid ice or snow will block the drainage of fluid water from slushy areas, so slush often goes through multiple freeze/thaw cycles before completely disappearing. In areas where\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Road_salt" title="Road salt">\n        road salt\n       </a>\n       is used to clear\n       <a href="/wiki/Road" title="Road">\n        roadways\n       </a>\n       , slush forms at lower temperatures than it would ordinarily, and only in salted areas; this can produce a number of different consistencies over the same geographical area.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Slushfall or slushing is the action of a wet snow falling from the sky.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Hazards">\n        Hazards\n </a>\n       . <a href="/wiki/Collision#Traffic" title="Collision">\n        other accidents\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Slush refreezing in overnight\n       <a href="/wiki/Frost"
