style="display:none">\n        <td colspan="2">\n        </td>\n       </tr>\n      </table>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        Hurricane Hilda\n       </b>\n       was the most intense\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        tropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       of the\n       <a href="/wiki/1964_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1964 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1964 Atlantic hurricane season\n       </a>\n       and ravaged areas of the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Gulf_Coast" title="United States Gulf Coast">\n        United States Gulf Coast\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       particularly\n       <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">\n        Louisiana\n       </a>\n       . In addition to causing impacts inland, the hurricane greatly disrupted offshore oil production, and at its time was the costliest tropical cyclone for Louisiana\'s offshore oil production. Due in part to flights made by the\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Research_Laboratory" title="National Hurricane Research Laboratory">\n        National Hurricane Research Laboratory\n       </a>\n       , Hilda became one of the most well-documented storms meteorologically in the Atlantic.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HildaReport1_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HildaReport1-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Lasting for seven days as a tropical cyclone, Hilda caused US$126\xc2\xa0million in damage and 38\xc2\xa0deaths.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [nb 1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Hilda developed over the southern shores of\n       <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">\n        Cuba\n       </a>\n       on September\xc2\xa028 as a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_depression" title="Tropical depression">\n        tropical depression\n       </a>\n       , tracking westward in an area of favorable conditions and reaching\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_storm" title="Tropical storm">\n        tropical storm\n       </a>\n       intensity the next day. Once situated in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">\n        Gulf of Mexico\n       </a>\n       , Hilda strengthened into a hurricane and began a slow trawl northward,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" title="Rapid deepening">\n        rapidly intensifying\n       </a>\n       to its peak intensity with\n       <a href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_wind" title="Maximum sustained wind">\n        winds\n       </a>\n       of 150\xc2\xa0mph (240\xc2\xa0km/h) on October\xc2\xa01, making it a\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_Category_4_Atlantic_hurricanes" title="List of Category 4 Atlantic hurricanes">\n        Category\xc2\xa04 hurricane\n       </a>\n       equivalent. Slight weakening occurred as Hilda made\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">\n        landfall\n       </a>\n       on the southern Louisiana coast on October\xc2\xa03. After reaching land, the hurricane took a sharp turn eastward and rapidly weakened as a result of land interaction and the presence of cool, dry air. The weakened remnants of Hilda merged with a\n       <a href="/wiki/Cold_front" title="Cold front">\n        cold front\n       </a>\n       a day after landfall and dissipated on October\xc2\xa05.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Originating near Cuba, the cyclone intensified while moving through the Gulf of Mexico, and became a Category\xc2\xa04 hurricane in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">\n        Gulf of Mexico\n       </a>\n       before striking\n       <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">\n        Louisiana\n       </a>\n       in early October. In combination with a\n       <a href="/wiki/Weather_front" title="Weather front">\n        frontal zone\n       </a>\n       located across the Southeast\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">\n        United States\n       </a>\n       , the hurricane spread heavy rains through the South through the Carolinas into the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mid-Atlantic_States" title="Mid-Atlantic States">\n        Mid-Atlantic States\n       </a>\n       . Hilda led to significant damage to oil platforms in the Gulf of Mexico, as well as $126\xc2\xa0million (1964\xc2\xa0USD) in damage, and 38 deaths.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div       </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Gulf_of_Mexico">\n        Gulf of Mexico\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Hilda&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Gulf of Mexico">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <a href="/wiki/Motor_ship" title="Motor ship">\n        motor ship\n       </a>\n       <i>\n        Cornelia B III\n       </i>\n       , carrying a truck on deck, encountered Hilda\'s rough surf and winds as it traversed the Gulf of Mexico, resulting in the truck being washed overboard.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HeadingCoast_22-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HeadingCoast-22">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       As Hilda tracked northward through the Gulf of Mexico, the storm threatened an estimated $350\xc2\xa0million worth of offshore oil drilling facilities. Hilda would become the most destructive tropical cyclone to Louisiana\'s offshore oil industry at the time of its impact, accruing losses over $100\xc2\xa0million. Strong winds and heavy surf destroyed 13\xc2\xa0oil platforms and damaged 5\xc2\xa0beyond repair.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HistLAOffshore_23-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HistLAOffshore-23">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       All but one of the lost oil rigs were built to withstand a "\n       <a href="/wiki/Return_period" title="Return period">\n        25-year storm\n       </a>\n       ", with the other constructed to withstand a "100-year storm".\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SafetyandOil_24-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SafetyandOil-24">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <a href="/wiki/Oil_spill" title="Oil spill">\n        Oil spills\n       </a>\n       resulting from the impacted rigs released 11,869\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oil_barrel" title="Oil barrel">\n        barrels\n       </a>\n       of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Crude_oil" title="Crude oil">\n        crude oil\n       </a>\n       into the gulf.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OffshoreOilHS_25-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-OffshoreOilHS-25">\n         [23]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Despite widespread evacuation of offshore drilling platforms, the 14\xc2\xa0crewmembers of the\n       <i>\n        Ocean Driller\n       </i>\n       managed to ride out the storm, at times withstanding winds as high as 120\xc2\xa0mph (190\xc2\xa0km/h).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PluckyMen_26-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-PluckyMen-26">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="United_States_Gulf_Coast">\n        United States Gulf Coast\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Hilda&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: United States Gulf Coast">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <h4>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Louisiana">\n        Louisiana\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Hilda&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Louisiana">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h4>\n      <p>\n       The majority of deaths associated with Hilda in Louisiana were a result of\n       <a href="/wiki/History_of_tropical_cyclone-spawned_tornadoes" title="History of tropical cyclone-spawned tornadoes">\n        tornadoes\n       </a>\n       spawned by the hurricane in its outer peripheral\n       <a href="/wiki/Rainband" title="Rainband">\n        rainbands\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Squall" title="Squall">\n        squall lines\n       </a>\n       , which began tracking across the region on October\xc2\xa03.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prelim5_16-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Prelim5-16">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Eight tornadoes occurred in Louisiana due to the hurricane, of which two were\n       <a href="/wiki/Waterspout" title="Waterspout">\n        waterspouts\n       </a>\n       . Despite tracking for only 1\xe2\x80\x931.5\xc2\xa0mi (1.6\xe2\x80\x932.4\xc2\xa0km) near\n       <a href="/wiki/Larose,_Louisiana" title="Larose, Louisiana">\n        Larose, Louisiana\n       </a>\n       , a tornado killed 22\xc2\xa0people and injured 165\xc2\xa0others, destroying 35\xc2\xa0homes in the process. Automobiles in the path of the twister were also damaged. Debris picked up by the tornado was found 16\xc2\xa0mi (26\xc2\xa0km) away in\n       <a href="/wiki/Grand_Coteau,_Louisiana" title="Grand Coteau, Louisiana">\n        Grand Coteau, Louisiana\n       </a>\n       . No other tornado in the state resulted in deaths, though multiple twisters in the\n       <a href="/wiki/New_Orleans_metropolitan_area" title="New Orleans metropolitan area">\n        New Orleans metropolitan area\n       </a>\n       caused extensive damage to several automobiles and buildings and injured five.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-StormData_27-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-StormData-27">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The same tornadoes also knocked down power lines, knocking out\n       <a href="/wiki/Power_outage" title="Power outage">\n        power\n       </a>\n       in localized areas.\n       <a href="/wiki/KLEB" title="KLEB">\n        KLEB\n       </a>\n       was knocked off the air as a result of these outages.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HildaChurns_17-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HildaChurns-17">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Data from the hardest hit areas along the central Louisiana coast remains sparse due to the small population density of the area.\n       <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">\n        Storm surge\n       </a>\n       unofficially peaked at 10\xc2\xa0ft (3.0\xc2\xa0m) at the\n       <a href="/wiki/Point_Au_Fer_Reef_Light" title="Point Au Fer Reef Light">\n        Point Au Fer Reef Light\n       </a>\n       . At the mouth of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Mississippi_River" title="Mississippi River">\n        Mississippi River\n       </a>\n       , tides reached as high as 6\xc2\xa0ft (1.8\xc2\xa0m) above\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mean_sea_level" title="Mean sea level">\n        mean sea level\n       </a>\n       . The strongest wind report was from\n       <a href="/wiki/Franklin,_Louisiana" title="Franklin, Louisiana">\n        Franklin, Louisiana\n       </a>\n       , where a station clocked sustained winds of 120\xc2\xa0mph (190\xc2\xa0km/h). Near the coast in\n       <a href="/wiki/Erath,_Louisiana" title="Erath, Louisiana">\n        Erath\n       </a>\n       , a 125\xc2\xa0ft (38\xc2\xa0m) high\n       <a href="/wiki/Water_tower" title="Water tower">\n        water tower\n       </a>\n       succumbed to Hilda\'s strong winds and collapsed onto an adjacent town hall where civil defense personnel were operating.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prelim5_16-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Prelim5-16">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The incident resulted in the deaths of eight and six injuries.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-StormData_27-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-StormData-27">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Although there were no stations to report the intensity of winds in Erath, winds 6\xc2\xa0mi (9.7\xc2\xa0km) away in\n       <a href="/wiki/Abbeville,_Louisiana" title="Abbeville, Louisiana">\n        Abbeville\n       </a>\n       reached 110\xc2\xa0mph (175\xc2\xa0km/h).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prelim5_16-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Prelim5-16">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Hilda\'s sharp curve eastward after landfall and the advection of cold air from the north resulted in gusty winds over the\n       <a href="/wiki/New_Orleans" title="New Orleans">\n        New Orleans\n       </a>\n       area, causing waves to break and spill over the seawall protecting the city from\n       <a href="/wiki/Lake_Pontchartrain" title="Lake Pontchartrain">\n        Lake Pontchartrain\n       </a>\n       . The resulting waterfront flooding damaged fishing camps and other shoreline property.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prelim5_16-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Prelim5-16">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prelim6_28-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Prelim6-28">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Elsewhere in southeastern Louisiana, strong winds stripped pecans and other nuts off of trees.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prelim6_28-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Prelim6-28">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Despite Hilda\'s intensity at landfall,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_1-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       much of the damage attributed to the hurricane was a result of heavy rains associated with the storm and its interaction with a nearby frontal boundary.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prelim6_28-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Prelim6-28">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCRAIN_29-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCRAIN-29">\n         [27]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Rainfall caused by the storm peaked at 17.71\xc2\xa0in (450\xc2\xa0mm) northwest of\n       <a href="/wiki/Jeanerette,_Louisiana" title="Jeanerette, Louisiana">\n        Jeanerette\n       </a>\n       . The strong winds and rain caused extensive crop damage; 98%\xc2\xa0of Louisiana\'s sugar crop sustained some measure of damage. Although most of the state\'s cotton crop was harvested prior to Hilda\'s landfall, the heavy rains flattened unpicked cotton in southern Louisiana, resulting in US$10\xc2\xa0million in damage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prelim6_28-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Prelim6-28">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Overall, the effects of Hilda in Louisiana destroyed or heavily damaged nearly 2,600\xc2\xa0residences and inflicted minor damage on an additional 19,000. The\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Red_Cross" title="United States Red Cross">\n        United States Red Cross\n       </a>\n       estimated that about 5,000\xc2\xa0people were injured and that of those 357\xc2\xa0were hospitalized.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-StormData_27-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-StormData-27">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n minimal, with gusts peaking at 45\xc2\xa0mph (72\xc2\xa0km/h) near\n       <a href="/wiki/High_Island,_Texas" title="High Island, Texas">\n        High Island, Texas\n       </a>\n       and areas of the state receiving trace amounts of precipitation at most.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReportAustin_21-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ReportAustin-21">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReportAustinB_33-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ReportAustinB-33">\n         [31]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Damage was limited to the portions of the coast which was impacted by minor storm surge peaking at 3.9\xc2\xa0ft (1.2\xc2\xa0m) above\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mean_sea_level" title="Mean sea level">\n        mean sea level\n       </a>\n       at\n       <a href="/wiki/Freeport,_Texas" title="Freeport, Texas">\n        Freeport\n       </a>\n       . Some piers and arterial beach roads adjacent to the coast were damaged. Portions of\n       <a href="/wiki/Texas_State_Highway_87" title="Texas State Highway 87">\n        Texas State Highway 87\n       </a>\n       on the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bolivar_Peninsula" title="Bolivar Peninsula">\n        Bolivar Peninsula\n       </a>\n       were inundated by floodwater. Minor property damage occurred, and no persons were injured in Texas.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReportAustin_21-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ReportAustin-21">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Tracking across the southern areas of\n       <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">\n        Mississippi\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prelim7_34-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Prelim7-34">\n         [32]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Hilda and later its remnants brought torrential rainfall to central and southern portions of the state.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-StormData_27-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-StormData-27">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/McComb-Pike_County_Airport" title="McComb-Pike County Airport">\n        McComb-Pike County Airport\n       </a>\n       received 12.57\xc2\xa0in (319\xc2\xa0mm) of rain, the highest precipitation total statewide.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RainGC_30-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-RainGC-30">\n         [28]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The heavy rains resulted in record rainfall totals for the month of October in several locations and\n       <a href="/wiki/Flash_flood" title="Flash flood">\n        flash floods\n       </a>\n       . However, the majority of damage in the state was a result of strong northerly winds, which caused power outages and was destructive to the state\'s pecan and corn crops, which had been nearing harvest. Cotton crops that were harvested prior to the passage of Hilda were largely damaged. Power outages knocked out 460\xc2\xa0telephone circuits and over 30,000\xc2\xa0individual telephones. Strong winds also uprooted and felled trees and their limbs in addition to as well as a 200\xc2\xa0ft (61\xc2\xa0m) tall\n       <a href="/wiki/Radio_masts_and_towers" title="Radio masts and towers">\n        radio mast\n       </a>\n       near\n       <a href="/wiki/Moss_Point,_Mississippi" title="Moss Point, Mississippi">\n        Moss Point, Mississippi\n       </a>\n       . As a result of Hilda, 20\xc2\xa0people were injured and damage exceeded $50,000. Three of those injuries were a result of a tornado which impacted areas of\n       <a href="/wiki/Pearl_River_County,_Mississippi" title="Pearl River County, Mississippi">\n        Pearl River County\n       </a>\n       , destroying a home, two barns, and a\n       <a href="/wiki/Pumping_station" title="Pumping station">\n        pumping station\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-StormData_27-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-StormData-27">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Across Mississippi, over 14,000\xc2\xa0homes were damaged, of which 3,000\xc2\xa0were either heavily damaged or completely destroyed.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Prelim7_34-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Prelim7-34">\n         [32]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Interacting with a cold front as it traversed\n       <a href="/wiki/Alabama" title="Alabama">\n        Alabama\n       </a>\n       , the remnants of Hilda caused gusts as strong as 80\xc2\xa0mph (130\xc2\xa0km/h) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Mobile_County,_Alabama" title="Mobile County, Alabama">\n        Mobile\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Baldwin_County,_Alabama" title="Baldwin County, Alabama">\n        Baldwin counties\n       </a>\n       , downing telecommunication lines, uprooting trees, and shattering windows. Piers lining\n       <a href="/wiki/Mobile_Bay" title="Mobile Bay">\n        Mobile Bay\n       </a>\n       were either destroyed or damaged. Five people were injured in those two counties. Strong winds in\n       <a href="/wiki/Russell_County,_Alabama" title="Russell County, Alabama">\n        Russell\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Lee_County,_Alabama" title="Lee County, Alabama">\n        Lee counties\n       </a>\n       damaged or destroyed several buildings, injuring four. In addition to straight-line winds, three tornadoes occurred across six counties in Alabama, with one injuring three people after damaging five homes, a hospital, and a\n       <a href="/wiki/Physical_plant" title="Physical plant">\n        plant\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-StormData_27-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-StormData-27">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Although rainfall peaked well inland at 8.91\xc2\xa0in (226\xc2\xa0mm),\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RainGC_30-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-RainGC-30">\n         [28]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       damage as a result of rain was limited to the coast, where the combination of high tides and rain resulted in minor coastal inundation. As the remnants of Hilda moved across the\n       <a href="/wiki/Florida_Panhandle" title="Florida Panhandle">\n        Florida Panhandle\n       </a>\n       on October\xc2\xa04\xe2\x80\x935, rough surf was produced, with high tides causing minor tidal inundation along the Florida coast.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-StormData_27-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-StormData-27">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       One person drowned in the high surf produced by the storm off of\n       <a href="/wiki/Pensacola,_Florida" title="Pensacola, Florida">\n        Pensacola, Florida\n       </a>\n       on October\xc2\xa04. Squalls in western and northern Florida produced heavy rain,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-StormData_27-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-StormData-27">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       with a maximum occurring in\n       <a href="/wiki/Wewahitchka,_Florida" title="Wewahitchka, Florida">\n        Wewahitchka\n       </a>\n       , which received 12.42\xc2\xa0in (315\xc2\xa0mm).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RainFL_35-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-RainFL-35">\n         [33]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The precipitation produced localized flooding in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Tallahassee,_Florida" title="Tallahassee, Florida">\n        Tallahassee\n       </a>\n       area. The squalls also produced strong winds, at times gusting to 60\xc2\xa0mph (97\xc2\xa0km/h) near\n       <a href="/wiki/Pensacola,_Florida" title="Pensacola, Florida">\n        Pensacola\n       </a>\n       . A station at\n       <a href="/wiki/Naval_Air_Station_Pensacola" title="Naval Air Station Pensacola">\n        Naval Air Station Pensacola\n       </a>\n       clocked a 59\xc2\xa0mph (95\xc2\xa0km/h) gust. These winds caused minor damage limited to uprooted trees and broken limbs.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-StormData_27-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-StormData-27">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="United_States_East_Coast">\n        United States East Coast\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Hilda&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: United States East Coast">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Rainfall from the remnants of Hilda extended as far northeast as\n       <a href="/wiki/Delaware" title="Delaware">\n        Delaware\n       </a>\n       as a result of the remnants interacting with a frontal boundary that extended across the United States East Coast.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCRAIN_29-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCRAIN-29">\n         [27]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Extremely torrential rains fell over\n       <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">\n        Georgia\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-StormData_27-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-StormData-27">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       peaking at 12.73\xc2\xa0in (323\xc2\xa0mm) south of\n       <a href="/wiki/Waynesboro,_Georgia" title="Waynesboro, Georgia">\n        Waynesboro\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RainSE_31-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-RainSE-31">\n         [29]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The largest precipitation totals occurred in the state\'s northeastern extremities, where widespread totals of 8\xc2\xa0in (200\xc2\xa0mm) or higher were reported.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReportAthensB_36-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ReportAthensB-36">\n         [34]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The rains resulted in flash flooding, causing extensive damage to roads and other infrastructure across Georgia.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReportAthensA_37-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ReportAthensA-37">\n         [35]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The excess floodwater caused streams to overflow their banks. At least 30\xc2\xa0bridges were washed away by these swollen streams, and several county roads were washed out.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-StormData_27-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-StormData-27">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The most considerable damage was reported in northeastern Georgia, coincident with the high rainfall totals there. Damage in\n       <a href="/wiki/Rabun_County,_Georgia" title="Rabun County, Georgia">\n        Rabun County\n       </a>\n       alone exceeded US$100,000.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReportAthensA_37-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ReportAthensA-37">\n         [35]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In addition to infrastructure damage, thousands of acres of farmland were inundated.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-StormData_27-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-StormData-27">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Torrential rainfall continued northeastwards into the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carolinas" title="Carolinas">\n        Carolinas\n       </a>\n       , with statewide rainfall peaking at 11.66\xc2\xa0in (296\xc2\xa0mm) and 12.02\xc2\xa0in (305\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">\n        North\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina" title="South Carolina">\n        South Carolina\n       </a>\n       , respectively.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-RainSE_31-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-RainSE-31">\n         [29]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The rains in North Carolina exacerbated preexisting flood conditions which had previously caused widespread agricultural and infrastructural damage as well as soil erosion.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-StormData_27-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-StormData-27">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Though not directly a result of Hilda\'s remnants, they were likely influenced by the storm\'s interaction with a frontal boundary. Already overflowing rivers and streams rose even further, reaching near-record levels.\n       <a href="/wiki/Raleigh,_North_Carolina" title="Raleigh, North Carolina">\n        Raleigh\n       </a>\n       experienced severe flooding, and roughly 4,000\xc2\xa0people were forced to evacuate their homes due to dangerous floodwaters.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReportRaleighA_38-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ReportRaleighA-38">\n         [36]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Two cars in mountainous regions were swept into inundated areas during the floods, resulting in one death.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReportRaleighB_39-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ReportRaleighB-39">\n         [37]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Several tornadoes in six counties were also reported, injuring two.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-StormData_27-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-StormData-27">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Extensive wind damage was reported in\n       <a href="/wiki/Wayne_County,_North_Carolina" title="Wayne County, North Carolina">\n        Wayne County\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReportRaleighB_39-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ReportRaleighB-39">\n         [37]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Heavy rains in South Carolina from October\xc2\xa04\xe2\x80\x936 resulted in localized flash flooding, particularly near streams.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ReportRaleighA_38-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ReportRaleighA-38">\n         [36]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In mountainous regions, runoff from precipitation caused the\n       <a href="/wiki/Keowee_River" title="Keowee River">\n        Keowee\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Saluda_River" title="Saluda River">\n        Saluda\n       </a>\n       , and tributaries of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Broad_River_(Carolinas)" title="Broad River (Carolinas)">\n        Broad River\n       </a>\n       to swell, inundated and causing substantial damage to adjacent agricultural land and roads, in addition to breaching several small dams. The flooding triggered landslides, killing one person. Counties in the coastal plains of South Carolina also experienced farmland, road, and residential damage due to the heavy rains.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-StormData_27-14">\n        <a href="#cite_note-StormData-27">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Retirement">\n        Retirement\n       </span>\n
