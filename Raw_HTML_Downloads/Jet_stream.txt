</a>\n           </li>\n          </ul>\n         </div>\n        </td>\n       </tr>\n      </table>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        Jet streams\n       </b>\n       are fast flowing, narrow, meandering\n       <a href="/wiki/Thermal_wind" title="Thermal wind">\n        air currents\n       </a>\n       in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Atmosphere_of_Earth" title="Atmosphere of Earth">\n        atmospheres\n       </a>\n       of some planets, including\n       <a href="/wiki/Earth" title="Earth">\n        Earth\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On Earth, the main jet streams are located near the altitude of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropopause" title="Tropopause">\n        tropopause\n       </a>\n       and are westerly winds (flowing west to east). Their paths typically have a\n       <a href="/wiki/Meander" title="Meander">\n        meandering\n       </a>\n       shape. Jet streams may start, stop, split into two or more parts, combine into one stream, or flow in various directions including opposite to the direction of the remainder of the jet.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The strongest jet streams are the\n       <b>\n        polar jets\n       </b>\n       , at 9\xe2\x80\x9312\xc2\xa0km (30,000\xe2\x80\x9339,000\xc2\xa0ft) above sea level, and the higher altitude and somewhat weaker\n       <b>\n        subtropical jets\n       </b>\n       at 10\xe2\x80\x9316\xc2\xa0km (33,000\xe2\x80\x9352,000\xc2\xa0ft). The\n       <a href="/wiki/Northern_Hemisphere" title="Northern Hemisphere">\n        Northern Hemisphere\n       </a>\n       and the\n       <a href="/wiki/Southern_Hemisphere" title="Southern Hemisphere">\n        Southern Hemisphere\n       </a>\n       each have a polar jet and a subtropical jet. The northern hemisphere polar jet flows over the middle to northern latitudes of\n       <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">\n        North America\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">\n        Europe\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">\n        Asia\n       </a>\n       and their intervening\n       <a href="/wiki/Ocean" title="Ocean">\n        oceans\n       </a>\n       , while the southern hemisphere polar jet mostly circles\n       <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">\n        Antarctica\n       </a>\n       all year round.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Jet streams are the product of two factors: the atmospheric heating by\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Solar_radiation" title="Solar radiation">\n        solar radiation\n       </a>\n       that produces the large scale\n       <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_circulation" title="Atmospheric circulation">\n        Polar, Ferrel, and Hadley\n       </a>\n       circulation cells, and the action of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Coriolis_force" title="Coriolis force">\n        Coriolis force\n       </a>\n       acting on those moving masses. The Coriolis force is caused by the planet\'s\n       <a href="/wiki/Planet#Rotation" title="Planet">\n        rotation\n       </a>\n       on its axis. On other planets,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Internal_heat" title="Internal heat">\n        internal heat\n       </a>\n       rather than solar heating drives their jet streams. The Polar jet stream forms near the interface of the Polar and Ferrel circulation cells; while the subtropical jet forms near the boundary of the Ferrel and Hadley circulation cells.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Other jet streams also exist. During the Northern Hemisphere summer, easterly jets can form in tropical regions, typically where dry air encounters more humid air at high altitudes. Low-level jets also are typical of various regions such as the central United States.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Meteorologists use the location of some of the jet streams as an aid in\n       <a href="/wiki/Weather_forecasting" title="Weather forecasting">\n        weather forecasting\n       </a>\n       . The main commercial relevance of the jet streams is in air travel, as flight time can be dramatically affected by either flying with the flow or against, which results in significant fuel and time cost savings for airlines. Often, the airlines work to fly \'with\' the jet stream for this reason. Dynamic\n       <a href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_Tracks" title="North Atlantic Tracks">\n        North Atlantic Tracks\n       </a>\n       are one example of how airlines and\n       <a href="/wiki/Air_traffic_control" title="Air traffic control">\n        air traffic control\n       </a>\n       work together to accommodate the jet stream and winds aloft that results in the maximum benefit for airlines and other users.\n       <a href="/wiki/Clear-air_turbulence" title="Clear-air turbulence">\n        Clear-air turbulence\n       </a>\n       , a potential hazard to aircraft passenger safety, is often found in a jet stream\'s vicinity, but it does not create a substantial alteration on flight times. These are a narrow belt.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="toc">\n       <div class="toctitle">\n        <h2>\n         Contents\n        </h2>\n       </div>\n </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       After the\n       <a href="/wiki/1883_eruption_of_Krakatoa" title="1883 eruption of Krakatoa">\n        1883 eruption of the Krakatoa volcano\n       </a>\n       , weather watchers tracked and mapped the effects on the sky over several years. They labelled the phenomenon the "equatorial smoke stream".\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In the 1920s, a Japanese meteorologist,\n       <a href="/wiki/Wasaburo_Oishi" title="Wasaburo Oishi">\n        Wasaburo Oishi\n       </a>\n       , detected the jet stream from a site near\n       <a href="/wiki/Mount_Fuji" title="Mount Fuji">\n        Mount Fuji\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       He tracked\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pilot_balloon" title="Pilot balloon">\n        pilot balloons\n       </a>\n       , also known as pibals (balloons used to determine upper level winds),\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       as they rose into the atmosphere. Oishi\'s work largely went unnoticed outside Japan because it was published in\n       <a href="/wiki/Esperanto" title="Esperanto">\n        Esperanto\n       </a>\n       . American pilot\n       <a href="/wiki/Wiley_Post" title="Wiley Post">\n        Wiley Post\n       </a>\n       , the first man to fly around the world solo in 1933, is often given some credit for discovery of jet streams. Post invented a pressurized suit that let him fly above 6,200 metres (20,300\xc2\xa0ft). In the year before his death, Post made several attempts at a high-altitude transcontinental flight, and noticed that at times his ground speed greatly exceeded his air speed.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       German meteorologist\n       <a href="/wiki/Heinrich_Seilkopf" title="Heinrich Seilkopf">\n        Heinrich Seilkopf\n       </a>\n       is credited with coining a special term,\n       <i>\n        Strahlstr\xc3\xb6mung\n       </i>\n       (literally "\n       <a href="/wiki/Jet_(fluid)" title="Jet (fluid)">\n        jet\n       </a>\n       current"), for the phenomenon in 1939.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       (Modern German usage is\n       <i>\n        "Strahlstrom"\n       </i>\n       .\n       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">\n        [\n        <i>\n         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">\n          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2013)">\n           citation needed\n          </span>\n         </a>\n        </i>\n        ]\n       </sup>\n       ) Many sources credit real understanding of the nature of jet streams to regular and repeated flight-path traversals during\n       <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">\n        World War II\n       </a>\n       . Flyers consistently noticed westerly tailwinds in excess of 100\xc2\xa0mph (160\xc2\xa0km/h) in flights, for example, from the US to the UK.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Similarly in 1944 a team of American meteorologists in Guam, including\n       <a href="/wiki/Reid_Bryson" title="Reid Bryson">\n        Reid Bryson\n       </a>\n       , had enough observations to forecast very high west winds that would slow bombers going to Japan.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Description">\n        Description\n       </span>\n  </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Polar jet streams are typically located near the 250\n       <a href="/wiki/Pascal_(unit)" title="Pascal (unit)">\n        hPa\n       </a>\n       (about 1/4 atmosphere) pressure level, or 7 to 12 kilometres (4.3 to 7.5\xc2\xa0mi) above\n       <a href="/wiki/Sea_level" title="Sea level">\n        sea level\n       </a>\n       , while the weaker subtropical jet streams are much higher, between 10 and 16 kilometres (6.2 and 9.9\xc2\xa0mi). Jet streams wander laterally dramatically, and have large changes in their altitude. The jet streams form near breaks in the tropopause, at the transitions between the\n       <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_circulation" title="Atmospheric circulation">\n        Polar, Ferrel and Hadley circulation cells\n       </a>\n       , and whose circulation, with the Coriolis force acting on those masses, drives the jet streams. The Polar jets, at lower altitude, and often intruding into mid-latitudes, strongly affects weather and aviation.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-COOK_13-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-COOK-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">\n        <a href="#cite_note-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The polar jet stream is most commonly found between latitudes 30\xc2\xb0 and 60\xc2\xb0 (closer to 60\xc2\xb0), while the subtropical jet streams are located close to latitude 30\xc2\xb0. The northern Polar jet stream is said to "follow the sun" as it slowly migrates northward as that hemisphere warms, and southward again as it cools.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">\n        <a href="#cite_note-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">\n        <a href="#cite_note-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The width of a jet stream is typically a few hundred kilometres or miles and its vertical thickness often less than five kilometres (3.1 miles).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">\n        <a href="#cite_note-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:402px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Jetstream_-_Rossby_Waves_-_N_hemisphere.svg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="253" data-file-width="1042" height="97" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/72/Jetstream_-_Rossby_Waves_-_N_hemisphere.svg/400px-Jetstream_-_Rossby_Waves_-_N_hemisphere.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/72/Jetstream_-_Rossby_Waves_-_N_hemisphere.svg/600px-Jetstream_-_Rossby_Waves_-_N_hemisphere.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/72/Jetstream_-_Rossby_Waves_-_N_hemisphere.svg/800px-Jetstream_-_Rossby_Waves_-_N_hemisphere.svg.png 2x" width="400"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Jetstream_-_Rossby_Waves_-_N_hemisphere.svg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Meanders (Rossby Waves) of the Northern Hemisphere\'s polar jet stream developing (a), (b); then finally detaching a "drop" of cold air (c). Orange: warmer masses of air; pink: jet stream.\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Jet streams are typically continuous over long distances, but discontinuities are common.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GOMdef_18-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-GOMdef-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The path of the jet typically has a meandering shape, and these meanders themselves propagate eastward, at lower speeds than that of the actual wind within the flow. Each large meander, or wave, within the jet stream is known as a\n       <a href="/wiki/Rossby_wave" title="Rossby wave">\n        Rossby wave\n       </a>\n       (planetary wave). Rossby waves are caused by changes in the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coriolis_effect" title="Coriolis effect">\n        Coriolis effect\n       </a>\n       with latitude.\n       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">\n        [\n        <i>\n         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">\n          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2016)">\n           citation needed\n          </span>\n         </a>\n        </i>\n        ]\n       </sup>\n       <a href="/wiki/Shortwave_(meteorology)" title="Shortwave (meteorology)">\n        Shortwave troughs\n       </a>\n       , are smaller scale waves superimposed on the Rossby waves, with a scale of 1,000\xc2\xa0to 4,000 kilometres (620\xe2\x80\x932,490\xc2\xa0mi) long,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">\n        <a href="#cite_note-19">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       that move along through the flow pattern around large scale, or longwave, "ridges" and "troughs" within Rossby waves.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">\n        <a href="#cite_note-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Jet streams can split into two when it encounters an upper-level low, that diverts a portion of the jet stream under its base, while the remainder of the jet moves by to its north.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The wind speeds are greatest where temperature differences (\n       <a href="/wiki/Gradient" title="Gradient">\n        gradient\n       </a>\n       ) between air masses are greatest, and often exceed 92\xc2\xa0km/h (50\xc2\xa0kn; 57\xc2\xa0mph),\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GOMdef_18-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-GOMdef-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       to over 398\xc2\xa0km/h (215\xc2\xa0kn; 247\xc2\xa0mph) have been measured.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ROBROY_21-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ROBROY-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The jet stream moves from West to East bringing changes of weather.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">\n        <a href="#cite_note-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Meteorologists now understand that the path of jet streams affects\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cyclonic" title="Cyclonic">\n        cyclonic\n       </a>\n       storm systems at lower levels in the atmosphere, and so knowledge of their course has become an important part of weather forecasting. For example, in 2007 and 2012, Britain experienced severe flooding as a result of the polar jet staying south for the summer.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">\n        <a href="#cite_note-23">\n         [23]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">\n        <a href="#cite_note-24">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">\n        <a href="#cite_note-25">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The polar and subtropical jets merge at some locations and times, while at other times they are well separated.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Cause">\n        Cause\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jet_stream&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Cause">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Earth_Global_Circulation.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="477" data-file-width="556" height="214" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/Earth_Global_Circulation.jpg/250px-Earth_Global_Circulation.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/Earth_Global_Circulation.jpg/375px-Earth_Global_Circulation.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6d/Earth_Global_Circulation.jpg/500px-Earth_Global_Circulation.jpg 2x" width="250"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Earth_Global_Circulation.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Highly idealised depiction of the global circulation. The upper-level jets tend to flow latitudinally along the cell boundaries.\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       See also:\n       <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">\n        Extratropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Thermal_wind" title="Thermal wind">\n        Thermal wind\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       In general, winds are strongest immediately under the\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropopause" title="Tropopause">\n        tropopause\n       </a>\n       (except locally, during\n       <a href="/wiki/Tornado" title="Tornado">\n        tornadoes\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        tropical cyclones\n       </a>\n       or other anomalous situations). If two air masses of different temperatures or densities meet, the resulting pressure difference caused by the density difference (which ultimately causes wind) is highest within the transition zone. The wind does not flow directly from the hot to the cold area, but is deflected by the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Coriolis_effect" title="Coriolis effect">\n        Coriolis effect\n       </a>\n       and flows along the boundary of the two air masses.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stimac_26-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Stimac-26">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       All these facts are consequences of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Thermal_wind" title="Thermal wind">\n        thermal wind\n       </a>\n       relation. The balance of forces acting on an atmospheric air parcel in the vertical direction is primarily between the gravitational force acting on the mass of the parcel and the buoyancy force, or the difference in pressure between the top and bottom surfaces of the parcel. Any imbalance between these forces results in the acceleration of the parcel in the imbalance direction: upward if the buoyant force exceeds the weight, and downward if the weight exceeds the buoyancy force. The balance in the vertical direction is referred to as\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hydrostatic" title="Hydrostatic">\n        hydrostatic\n       </a>\n       . Beyond the tropics, the dominant forces act in the horizontal direction, and the primary struggle is between the Coriolis force and the pressure gradient force. Balance between these two forces is referred to as\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Geostrophic" title="Geostrophic">\n        geostrophic\n       </a>\n       . Given both hydrostatic and geostrophic balance, one can derive the thermal wind relation: the vertical gradient of the horizontal wind is proportional to the horizontal temperature gradient. If two air masses, one cold and dense to the North and the other hot and less dense to the South, are separated by a vertical boundary and that boundary should be removed, the difference in densities will result in the cold air mass slipping under the hotter and less dense air mass. The Coriolis effect will then cause poleward-moving mass to deviate to the East, while equatorward-moving mass will deviate toward the west. The general trend in the atmosphere is for temperatures to decrease in the poleward direction. As a result, winds develop an eastward component and that component grows with altitude. Therefore, the strong eastward moving jet streams are in part a simple consequence of the fact that the Equator is warmer than the North and South poles.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Stimac_26-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Stimac-26">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Polar_jet_stream">\n        Polar jet stream\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jet_stream&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Polar jet stream">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       The thermal wind relation does not explain why the winds are organized into tight jets, rather than distributed more broadly over the hemisphere. One factor that contributes to the creation of a concentrated polar jet is the undercutting of sub-tropical air masses by the more dense polar air masses at the polar front. This causes surface low pressure and higher pressure at altitude. At high altitudes, lack of friction allows air to respond freely to the steep pressure gradient with low pressure at high altitude over the pole. This results in the formation of planetary wind circulations that experience a strong Coriolis deflection and thus can be considered \'quasi-geostrophic\'. The polar front jet stream is closely linked to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Frontogenesis" title="Frontogenesis">\n        frontogenesis\n       </a>\n       process in midlatitudes, as the acceleration/deceleration of the air flow induces areas of low/high pressure respectively, which link to the formation of cyclones and anticyclones along the polar front in a relatively narrow region.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GOMdef_18-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-GOMdef-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Subtropical_jet">\n        Subtropical jet\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jet_stream&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Subtropical jet">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       A second factor which contributes to a concentrated jet, that is more applicable to the subtropical jet, which forms at the poleward limit of the tropical\n       <a href="/wiki/Hadley_cell" title="Hadley cell">\n        Hadley cell\n       </a>\n       and to first order this circulation is symmetric with respect to longitude. Tropical air rises to the tropopause, and moves poleward before sinking; this is the Hadley cell circulation. As it does so it tends to conserve angular momentum, since friction with the ground is significant. Air masses that begin moving poleward are deflected eastward by the\n       <a href="/wiki/Coriolis_force" title="Coriolis force">\n        Coriolis force\n       </a>\n       (true for either hemisphere), which for poleward moving air implies an increased eastward component of the winds\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27">\n        <a href="#cite_note-27">\n         [27]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       (note that leftward deflection in the southern hemisphere).\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Other_planets">\n        Other planets\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jet_stream&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Other planets">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       <a href="/wiki/Jupiter" title="Jupiter">\n        Jupiter\n       </a>\n       \'s atmosphere has multiple jet streams, caused by the convection cells that form the familiar banded color structure; on Jupiter, these convection cells are driven by internal heating.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ROBROY_21-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ROBROY-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The factors that control the number of jet streams in a planetary atmosphere is an active area of research in dynamical meteorology. In models, as one increases the planetary radius, holding all other parameters fixed, the number of jet streams decreases.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" Commercial use of the jet stream began on 18 November 1952, when\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pan_Am" title="Pan Am">\n        Pan Am\n       </a>\n       flew from Tokyo to Honolulu at an altitude of 7,600 metres (24,900\xc2\xa0ft). It cut the trip time by over one-third, from 18 to 11.5 hours.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29">\n        <a href="#cite_note-29">\n         [29]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Not only does it cut time off the flight, it also nets fuel savings for the airline industry.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30">\n        <a href="#cite_note-30">\n         [30]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Within North America, the time needed to fly east across the\n       <a href="/wiki/Continent" title="Continent">\n        continent\n       </a>\n       can be decreased by about 30\n       <a href="/wiki/Minute" title="Minute">\n        minutes\n       </a>\n       if an\n       <a href="/wiki/Airplane" title="Airplane">\n        airplane\n       </a>\n       can fly with the jet stream, or increased by more than that amount if it must fly west against it.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Associated with jet streams is a phenomenon known as\n       <a href="/wiki/Clear-air_turbulence" title="Clear-air turbulence">\n        clear-air turbulence\n       </a>\n       (CAT), caused by vertical and horizontal\n       <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">\n        wind shear\n       </a>\n       caused by jet streams.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31">\n        <a href="#cite_note-31">\n         [31]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The CAT is strongest on the cold\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Air" title="Air">\n        air\n       </a>\n       side of the jet,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32">\n        <a href="#cite_note-32">\n         [32]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       next to and just under the axis of the jet.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33">\n        <a href="#cite_note-33">\n         [33]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Clear-air turbulence can cause aircraft to plunge and so present a passenger safety hazard that has caused fatal accidents, such as the death of one passenger on\n       <a href="/wiki/United_Airlines_Flight_826" title="United Airlines Flight 826">\n        United Airlines Flight 826\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34">\n        <a href="#cite_note-34">\n         [34]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CNN_UAF826_35-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CNN_UAF826-35">\n         [35]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Possible_future_power_generation">\n        Possible future power generation\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jet_stream&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Possible future power generation">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       See also:\n       <a href="/wiki/High-altitude_wind_power" title="High-altitude wind power">\n        High-altitude wind power\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Scientists are investigating ways to harness the wind energy within the jet stream. According to one estimate, of the potential wind energy in the jet stream, only 1 percent would be needed to meet the world\'s current energy needs. The required technology would reportedly take 10\xe2\x80\x9320 years to develop.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36">\n        <a href="#cite_note-36">\n         [36]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       There are two major but divergent scientific articles about jet stream power. Archer &amp; Caldeira\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37">\n        <a href="#cite_note-37">\n         [37]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       claim that the Earth\'s jet streams could generate a total power of 1700\n       <a href="/wiki/Watt#Terawatt" title="Watt">\n        terawatts (TW)\n       </a>\n       and that the climatic impact of harnessing this amount would be negligible. However, Miller, Gans, &amp; Kleidon\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38">\n        <a href="#cite_note-38">\n         [38]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       claim that the jet streams could generate a total power of only 7.5 TW and that the climatic impact would be catastrophic.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Unpowered_aerial_attack">\n        Unpowered aerial attack\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jet_stream&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Unpowered aerial attack">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Near the end of\n       <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">\n        World War II\n       </a>\n       the Japanese\n       <a href="/wiki/Fire_balloon" title="Fire balloon">\n        fire balloon\n       </a>\n       was designed as a cheap weapon intended to make use of the jet stream over the\n       <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">\n        Pacific Ocean\n       </a>\n       to reach the west coast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">\n        Canada\n       </a>\n       and the\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">\n        United States\n       </a>\n       . href="#cite_note-Caio-41">\n         [41]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h4>\n       <span id="El_Ni\xc3\xb1o">\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="El_Ni.C3.B1o">\n        El Ni\xc3\xb1o\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a   </span>\n       </span>\n      </h4>\n      <p>\n       During\n       <a href="/wiki/El_Ni%C3%B1o" title="El Ni\xc3\xb1o">\n        El Ni\xc3\xb1o\n       </a>\n       events, increased     </sup>\n       During the Ni\xc3\xb1o portion of ENSO, increased precipitation falls along the Gulf coast and Southeast due to a stronger than normal, and more southerly, polar jet stream.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-43">\n        <a href="#cite_note-43">\n         [43]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Snowfall is greater than average across the southern Rockies and Sierra Nevada mountain range, and is well below normal across the Upper Midwest and Great Lakes states.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cpc.ncep.noaa.gov_44-0">\n        <a Gulf coast experiences below normal temperatures during the winter season.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45">\n        <a href="#cite_note-45">\n         [45]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46">\n        <a href="#cite_note-46">\n         [46]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The subtropical jet stream across the deep\n       <a    </a>\n       within the Atlantic tropics below what is normal, and increases tropical cyclone activity across the eastern Pacific.\n       <sup class="reference" class="reference" id="cite_ref-Caio_41-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Caio-41">\n         [41]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h4>\n       <span id="La_Ni\xc3\xb1a">\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="La_Ni.C3.B1a">\n        La Ni\xc3\xb1a\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n    <span class="mw-headline" id="Dust_Bowl">\n        Dust Bowl\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jet_stream&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Dust Bowl">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Evidence suggests the jet stream was at least partly responsible for the widespread drought conditions during the 1930s\n       <a Normally, the jet stream flows east over the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">\n        Gulf of Mexico\n       </a>\n       and turns northward pulling up moisture and dumping\n       <a href="/wiki/Rain" title="Rain">\n        rain\n       </a>\n       onto the\n       <a href="/wiki/Great_Plains" title="Great Plains">\n        Great Plains\n       </a>\n       . During the Dust Bowl, the jet stream weakened and changed course traveling farther south than normal. This starved the Great Plains and other areas of the Midwest of rainfall, causing extraordinary drought conditions.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52">\n        <a href="#cite_note-52">\n         [52]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Longer-term_climatic_changes">\n        Longer-term climatic changes\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jet_stream&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Longer-term climatic changes">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Climate scientists have hypothesized that the jet stream will gradually weaken as a result of\n       <a href="/wiki/Global_warming" title="Global warming">\n        global warming\n       </a>\n       . Trends such as\n       <a href="/wiki/Arctic_sea_ice_decline" title="Arctic sea ice decline">\n        Arctic sea ice decline\n       </a>\n       , reduced snow cover,\n       <a href="/wiki/Evapotranspiration" title="Evapotranspiration">\n        evapotranspiration\n       </a>\n       patterns, and other weather anomalies are expected to make the Arctic heat up faster than other parts of the globe. This in turn reduces the temperature gradient that drives jet stream winds, causing the jet stream to become weaker and more variable in its course.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Time_53-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Time-53">\n         [53]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54">\n        <a href="#cite_note-54">\n         [54]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CSMonitor_55-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CSMonitor-55">\n         [55]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56">\n        <a href="#cite_note-56">\n         [56]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Screen_2013_57-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Screen_2013-57">\n         [57]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Francis_2012_58-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Francis_2012-58">\n         [58]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Petoukhov_2010_59-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Petoukhov_2010-59">\n         [59]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Since 2007, and particularly in 2012 and early 2013, the jet stream has been at an abnormally low latitude across the UK, lying closer to the\n       <a href="/wiki/English_Channel" title="English Channel">\n        English Channel\n       </a>\n       , around 50\xc2\xb0N rather than its more usual north of Scotland latitude of around 60\xc2\xb0N.\n       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">\n        [\n        <i>\n         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Verifiability" title="Wikipedia:Verifiability">\n          <span title="The material near this tag failed verification of its source citation(s). polar-night jet stream forms only during the winter months when the nights are much longer, hence\n       <a href="/wiki/Polar_night" title="Polar night">\n        polar nights\n       </a>\n       , in their respective hemispheres at around 60\xc2\xb0 latitude. The polar night jet moves at a greater height of about 80,000 feet (24,000\xc2\xa0m) than it does during the summer.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61">\n        <a href="#cite_note-61">\n         [61]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       During these dark months the air high over the poles becomes much colder than the air over the equator. This difference in temperature gives rise to extreme air pressure differences in the stratosphere, which, when combined with the Coriolis effect, create the polar night jets, that race eastward at an altitude of about 30 miles (48\xc2\xa0km).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62">\n        <a href="#cite_note-62">\n         [62]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The\n       <a href="/wiki/Polar_vortex" title="Polar vortex">\n        polar vortex\n       </a>\n       is circled by the polar night jet. The mountain barrier increases the strength of the low level wind by 45 percent.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64">\n        <a href="#cite_note-64">\n         [64]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In the North American\n       <a href="/wiki/Great_Plains" title="Great Plains">\n        Great Plains\n       </a>\n       a southerly low-level jet helps fuel overnight thunderstorm activity during the warm season, normally in the form of\n       <a href="/wiki/Mesoscale_convective_system" title="Mesoscale convective system">\n        mesoscale convective systems\n       </a>\n       which form during the overnight hours.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-65">\n        <a href="#cite_note-65">\n         [65]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A similar phenomenon develops across Australia, which pulls moisture poleward from the\n       <a href="/wiki/Coral_Sea" title="Coral Sea">\n        Coral Sea\n       </a>\n       towards cut-off lows which form mainly across southwestern portions of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Continent" title="Continent">\n        continent\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66">\n        <a href="#cite_note-66">\n         [66]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Valley_exit_jet">\n        Valley exit jet\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jet_stream&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Valley exit jet">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       A\n       <a href="/wiki/Valley_exit_jet" title="Valley exit jet">\n        valley exit jet\n       </a>\n       is a strong, down-valley, elevated air current that emerges above the intersection of the valley and its adjacent plain. These winds frequently reach a maximum of 20\xc2\xa0m/s (45\xc2\xa0mph or 72\xc2\xa0km/h) at a height of 40\xe2\x80\x93200 m above the ground. Surface winds below the jet may sway vegetation, but are significantly weaker.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       They are likely to be found in valley regions that exhibit diurnal mountain wind systems, such as those of the dry mountain ranges of the US. Deep valleys that terminate abruptly at a plain are more impacted by these factors than are those that gradually become shallower as downvalley distance increases.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67">\n        <a href="#cite_note-67">\n         [67]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Africa">\n        Africa\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jet_stream&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" <p>\n       The mid-level\n       <a href="/wiki/African_easterly_jet" title="African easterly jet">\n        African easterly jet\n       </a>\n       occurs during the Northern Hemisphere summer between 10\xc2\xb0N and 20\xc2\xb0N above West Africa, and the nocturnal poleward low-level jet occurs in the\n       <a class="new"
