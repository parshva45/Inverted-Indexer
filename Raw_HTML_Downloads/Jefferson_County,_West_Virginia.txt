id="History">\n        History\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jefferson_County,_West_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Formation">\n        Formation\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jefferson_County,_West_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Formation">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Jefferson County was formed from\n       <a href="/wiki/Berkeley_County,_West_Virginia" title="Berkeley County, West Virginia">\n        Berkeley County\n       </a>\n       in 1801 because the citizens of southeastern Berkeley county felt they had to travel too far to the county seat of Martinsburg.\n       <a href="/wiki/Charles_Washington" title="Charles Washington">\n        Charles Washington\n       </a>\n       , the founder of Charles Town and brother to George Washington petitioned for a new county to be formed. It was named for\n       <a href="/wiki/Thomas_Jefferson" title="Thomas Jefferson">\n        Thomas Jefferson\n       </a>\n       , author of the\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Declaration_of_Independence" title="United States Declaration of Independence">\n        Declaration of Independence\n       </a>\n       and third\n       <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_United_States" title="President of the United States">\n        President of the United States\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Virginia previously had a Jefferson County, which is now\n       <a href="/wiki/Jefferson_County,_Kentucky" title="Jefferson County, Kentucky">\n        part of Kentucky\n       </a>\n       . Accordingly, in the State records of Virginia, there will be listings for Jefferson County from 1780-1792 and Jefferson County from 1801-1863, neither of which is still in Virginia.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="John_Brown_Rebellion">\n        John Brown Rebellion\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jefferson_County,_West_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: John Brown Rebellion">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:John_brown_interior_engine_house.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="827" data-file-width="1024" height="178" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/John_brown_interior_engine_house.jpg/330px-John_brown_interior_engine_house.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/John_brown_interior_engine_house.jpg/440px-John_brown_interior_engine_house.jpg 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:John_brown_interior_engine_house.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Coverage of John Brown\'s raid in Frank Leslie\'s illustrated newspaper, v. 8, no. 205 (1859 Nov. 5), p. 359\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The county\'s\n       <a href="/wiki/Courthouse" title="Courthouse">\n        courthouse\n       </a>\n       was the site of the trial for the\n       <a href="/wiki/Abolitionism_in_the_United_States" title="Abolitionism in the United States">\n        abolitionist\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/John_Brown_(abolitionist)" title="John Brown (abolitionist)">\n        John Brown\n       </a>\n       after his October 1859 raid on the federal\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Armory_(military)" title="Armory (military)">\n        armory\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/Harpers_Ferry,_West_Virginia" title="Harpers Ferry, West Virginia">\n        Harpers Ferry\n       </a>\n       . Some 90 U.S. Marines serving under then Army Colonel\n       <a href="/wiki/Robert_E._Lee" title="Robert E. Lee">\n        Robert E. Lee\n       </a>\n       and Lieutenants\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/J.E.B._Stuart" title="J.E.B. Stuart">\n        J.E.B. Stuart\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Israel_Green&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Israel Green (page does not exist)">\n        Israel Green\n       </a>\n       put down the rebellion.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Brown was sentenced to death for murder, treason against the Commonwealth of Virginia, and conspiring with slaves to rebel. On 2 December 1859 John Brown was taken from the Charles Town jail a short distance to an open field and hanged. Among those attending the Brown execution was a contingent of 1500 cadets from\n       <a href="/wiki/Virginia_Military_Institute" title="Virginia Military Institute">\n        Virginia Military Institute\n       </a>\n       sent by the\n       <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Virginia" title="Governor of Virginia">\n        Governor of Virginia\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Henry_A._Wise" title="Henry A. Wise">\n        Henry A. Wise\n       </a>\n       under the supervision of Major\n       <a href="/wiki/William_Gilham" title="William Gilham">\n        William Gilham\n       </a>\n       and Major\n       <a href="/wiki/Stonewall_Jackson" title="Stonewall Jackson">\n        Thomas J. Jackson\n       </a>\n       . In the ranks of a Richmond militia company stood John Wilkes Booth.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Civil_War">\n        Civil War\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jefferson_County,_West_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Civil War">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       The county was a frequent site of conflict during the civil war, as Union and Confederate lines moved back and forth along the Shenandoah Valley. Some towns in the county changed hands between the Union and Confederacy over a dozen times, including Charles Town, and especially Harpers Ferry.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Jefferson County is the only part of modern-day\n       <a href="/wiki/West_Virginia" title="West Virginia">\n        West Virginia\n       </a>\n       not exempted from the effects of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Emancipation_Proclamation" title="Emancipation Proclamation">\n        Emancipation Proclamation\n       </a>\n       (as\n       <a href="/wiki/Berkeley_County,_West_Virginia" title="Berkeley County, West Virginia">\n        Berkeley County\n       </a>\n       and the 48 counties designated as\n       <a href="/wiki/West_Virginia" title="West Virginia">\n        West Virginia\n       </a>\n       had been). Slaves in the county were legally free as of January 1, 1863.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <a href="/wiki/Jefferson_County_Courthouse_(Charles_Town,_West_Virginia)" title="Jefferson County Courthouse (Charles Town, West Virginia)">\n        Jefferson County Courthouse\n       </a>\n       is the only courthouse in America to have held two treason trials: the trial of\n       <a href="/wiki/John_Brown_(abolitionist)" title="John Brown (abolitionist)">\n        John Brown\n       </a>\n       in 1859 and a trial arising from the\n       <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Blair_Mountain" title="Battle of Blair Mountain">\n        Battle of Blair Mountain\n       </a>\n       labor rebellion.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nrhpinv2_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nrhpinv2-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Joining_West_Virginia">\n        Joining West Virginia\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jefferson_County,_West_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Joining West Virginia">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:MD-VA-WV_tripoint_in_Potomac_River.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3216" data-file-width="4288" height="165" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/MD-VA-WV_tripoint_in_Potomac_River.jpg/330px-MD-VA-WV_tripoint_in_Potomac_River.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/67/MD-VA-WV_tripoint_in_Potomac_River.jpg/440px-MD-VA-WV_tripoint_in_Potomac_River.jpg 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:MD-VA-WV_tripoint_in_Potomac_River.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         <a href="/wiki/Tripoint" title="Tripoint">\n          Tripoint\n         </a>\n         of Virginia, West Virginia, and Maryland in Potomac River east of Harper\'s Ferry and the lowest point in West Virginia.\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Both Berkeley and Jefferson counties had voted for secession in the vote taken on May 23, 1861. However, these counties lying on the\n       <a href="/wiki/Potomac_River" title="Potomac River">\n        Potomac River\n       </a>\n       in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Shenandoah_Valley" title="Shenandoah Valley">\n        Shenandoah Valley\n       </a>\n       , with the consent of the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reorganized_Government_of_Virginia" title="Reorganized Government of Virginia">\n        Reorganized Government of Virginia\n       </a>\n       voted in favor of annexation to West Virginia in 1863 in a dubious election supervised by the occupying\n       <a href="/wiki/Union_Army" title="Union Army">\n        Union Army\n       </a>\n       . Virginia tried to nullify this after the\n       <a href="/wiki/American_Civil_War" title="American Civil War">\n        American Civil War\n       </a>\n       , but the counties remained part of West Virginia.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The question of the constitutionality of the formation of the new state was brought before the\n       <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_the_United_States" title="Supreme Court of the United States">\n        Supreme Court of the United States\n       </a>\n       in the following manner:\n       <a href="/wiki/Berkeley_County,_West_Virginia" title="Berkeley County, West Virginia">\n        Berkeley\n       </a>\n       and Jefferson County, West Virginia, counties lying on the Potomac east of the mountains, in 1863, with the consent of the Reorganized Government of Virginia, had supposedly voted in favor of annexation to West Virginia. However, many voters were absent in the Confederate Army when the vote was taken and they refused to accept the transfer upon their return. The\n       <a href="/wiki/Virginia_General_Assembly" title="Virginia General Assembly">\n        Virginia General Assembly\n       </a>\n       repealed the Act of Secession and in 1866 brought suit against West Virginia, asking the Supreme Court to declare the counties still part of Virginia. Congress, on March 10, 1866, passed a joint resolution recognizing the transfer. In 1871, the U.S. Supreme Court decided\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Virginia_v._West_Virginia" title="Virginia v. West Virginia">\n         Virginia v. West Virginia\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       upholding the "secession" of West Virginia, including Berkeley and Jefferson counties, from Virginia.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In 2011, West Virginia state delegate\n       <a href="/wiki/Larry_Kump" title="Larry Kump">\n        Larry Kump\n       </a>\n       sponsored legislation to allow Morgan, Berkeley, and Jefferson counties to rejoin Virginia by popular vote.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n census\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jefferson_County,_West_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: 2000 census">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       As of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Census" title="Census">\n        census\n       </a>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-GR8_16-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-GR8-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       of 2000, there were 42,190 people, 16,165 households, and 11,315 families residing in the county. The\n       <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">\n        population density\n       </a>\n       was 201 people per square mile (78/km\xc2\xb2). There were 17,623 housing units at an average density of 84 per square\xc2\xa0mile (32/km\xc2\xb2). The racial makeup of the county was 91.02%\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/White_American" title="White American">\n        White\n       </a>\n       , 6.09%\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/African_American" title="African American">\n        Black or African American\n       </a>\n       , 0.60%\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Asian_American" title="Asian American">\n        Asian\n       </a>\n       , 0.28%\n       <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">\n        Native American\n       </a>\n       , 0.04%\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pacific_Islander_American" title="Pacific Islander American">\n        Pacific Islander\n       </a>\n       , 0.60% from\n       <a href="/wiki/Race_and_ethnicity_in_the_United_States_Census" title="Race and ethnicity in the United States Census">\n        other races\n       </a>\n       , and 1.37% from two or more races. 1.74% of the population were\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hispanics_in_the_United_States" title="Hispanics in the United States">\n        Hispanics or Latinos\n       </a>\n       of any race.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       There were 16,165 households out of which 31.90% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 55.90% were\n       <a href="/wiki/Marriage" title="Marriage">\n        married couples\n       </a>\n       living together, 10.00% had a female householder with no husband present, and 30.00% were non-families. 23.20% of all households were made up of individuals and 8.50% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.54 and the average family size was 2.99.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In the county, the population was spread out with 23.90% under the age of 18, 10.00% from 18 to 24, 29.90% from 25 to 44, 25.10% from 45 to 64, and 11.20% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 37 years. For every 100 females there were 97.90 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 95.40 males.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The median income for a household in the county was $44,374, and the median income for a family was $51,351.
