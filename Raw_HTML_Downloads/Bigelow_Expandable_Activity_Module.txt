Space Station\n       </a>\n       (ISS) from 2016 to at least 2020. </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nasa2000conf_7-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nasa2000conf-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" </sup>\n       In 2006 and 2007, Bigelow launched two demonstration modules to Earth orbit,\n       <a href="/wiki/Genesis_I" title="Genesis I">\n        Genesis I\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a     <a href="/wiki/Nautilus-X" title="Nautilus-X">\n        Nautilus-X\n       </a>\n       multi-mission exploration concept vehicle.\n       <sup class="reference" module could be built and made flight-ready 24 months after a build contract was secured.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sdc20110126_15-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-sdc20110126-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       On December 20, 2012, NASA awarded Bigelow Aerospace a\n       <span style="white-space: nowrap">\n        <a </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NASAannouncement_17-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NASAannouncement-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <a module to the ISS.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sn20130612_18-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-sn20130612-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       During a press event  <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:BEAM_module_expansion_series.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Progression of expansion of BEAM\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       In early 2015, BEAM was scheduled for deployment on the next available ISS transport vehicle,\n       <a href="/wiki/SpaceX_CRS-8" title="SpaceX CRS-8">\n        SpaceX CRS-8\n       </a>\n       , which was scheduled for launch in September 2015. Due to a rocket failure during the SpaceX CRS-7 launch in June 2015, the delivery of BEAM was delayed.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nasasf20150907_20-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nasasf20150907-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-launchlog_21-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-launchlog-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The successful launch of SpaceX CRS-8 took place on April 8, 2016,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nasasf20160408_22-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nasasf20160408-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and the Dragon cargo vehicle was berthed to the nadir port of\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Harmony_(ISS_module)" title="Harmony (ISS module)">\n         Harmony\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       node on April 10.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-unitoday20160411_23-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-unitoday20160411-23">\n         [23]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On April 16, British astronaut\n       <a href="/wiki/Tim_Peake" title="Tim Peake">\n        Tim Peake\n       </a>\n       extracted BEAM from Dragon\'s trunk using Canadarm2, and installed it on the aft port of\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Tranquility_(ISS_module)" title="Tranquility (ISS module)">\n         Tranquility\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       node.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-sfnow20160416_24-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-sfnow20160416-24">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The first attempt at module inflation took place on May 26, 2016, and was suspended after higher-than-expected air pressure inside BEAM was detected with minimal expansion of the module.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-space20160527_25-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-space20160527-25">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The attempt was called off after two hours.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-verge20160527_26-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-verge20160527-26">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The failure to expand and unfold is thought to have been the result of the unanticipated 10-month delay in module inflation, which may have caused the fabric layers to stick together.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-space20160527_25-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-space20160527-25">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The module was expanded on May 28 over the course of seven hours, with air being injected 25 times for a total of 2 minutes 27 seconds.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nasa20160528_27-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nasa20160528-27">\n         [27]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Its length was extended 170\xc2\xa0cm (67\xc2\xa0in) from its stowed configuration, 2.5\xc2\xa0cm (1\xc2\xa0in) less than expected.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spacepol20160528_28-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-spacepol20160528-28">\n         [28]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       After expansion was complete, air tanks aboard BEAM were opened to equalize air pressure in the module with that of the ISS.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spacenews20160528_29-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-spacenews20160528-29">\n         [29]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The module will be monitored for two years.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spacepol20160528_28-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-spacepol20160528-28">\n         [28]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spacenews20160528_29-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-spacenews20160528-29">\n         [29]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:ISS-47_Jeff_Williams_works_inside_the_BEAM.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="506" data-file-width="900" height="124" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/28/ISS-47_Jeff_Williams_works_inside_the_BEAM.jpg/330px-ISS-47_Jeff_Williams_works_inside_the_BEAM.jpg 1.5x, class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:ISS-47_Jeff_Williams_works_inside_the_BEAM.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Jeff Williams inside BEAM\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       On June 6, 2016, astronaut\n       <a href="/wiki/Jeffrey_Williams_(astronaut)" title="Jeffrey Williams (astronaut)">\n        Jeff Williams\n       </a>\n       and cosmonaut\n       <a href="/wiki/Oleg_Skripochka" title="Oleg Skripochka">\n        Oleg Skripochka\n       </a>\n       opened the hatch to BEAM and entered to collect an air sample, download expansion data from sensors, and install monitoring equipment. The hatch to BEAM was re-sealed on June 8 after three days of tests.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nasa20160606_30-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nasa20160606-30">\n         [30]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nasa20160608_31-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nasa20160608-31">\n         [31]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A second round of tests took place on September 29 of that same year when astronaut\n       <a href="/wiki/Kathleen_Rubins" title="Kathleen Rubins">\n        Kathleen Rubins\n       </a>\n       entered the module to install temporary monitoring equipment.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nasa20160929_32-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nasa20160929-32">\n         [32]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       NASA noted in May 2017 that, after spending one year in space, the BEAM instrumentation had recorded "a few probable micrometeoroid debris impacts" but that the module\'s protective layers had resisted any penetration. Early results from monitors inside the module have shown that\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Galactic_cosmic_radiation" title="Galactic cosmic radiation">\n        galactic cosmic radiation\n       </a>\n       levels are comparable to those in the rest of the space station. Further testing will try to characterize whether the inflatable structure is any more resilient to radiation than traditional metal modules.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nasa20170526_33-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nasa20170526-33">\n         [33]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-arstech20170528_34-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-arstech20170528-34">\n         [34]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In October 2017, it was announced that the module would stay attached to the ISS for another three years, with options for two further one-year extensions. The module will be used to store up to 130 cargo transfer bags in an effort to free up additional space aboard the station.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-arstech20171003_35-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-arstech20171003-35">\n         [35]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Objectives">\n        Objectives\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bigelow_Expandable_Activity_Module&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Objectives">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       The BEAM is an experimental program in an effort to test and validate expandable habitat technology.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-characteristics_36-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-characteristics-36">\n         [36]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       If BEAM performs favorably, it could lead to development of expandable habitation structures for future crews traveling in deep space.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nasa-beammain_37-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nasa-beammain-37">\n         [37]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The two-year demonstration period will:\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-characteristics_36-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-characteristics-36">\n         [36]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bigelow-beam_38-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-bigelow-beam-38">\n         [38]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <ul>\n       <li>\n        Demonstrate launch and deployment of a commercial inflatable module. Implement folding and packaging techniques for inflatable shell. Implement a venting system for inflatable shell during ascent to ISS.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Determine\n        <a href="/wiki/Radiation_protection#Spacecraft_and_radiation_protection" title="Radiation protection">\n         radiation protection\n        </a>\n        capability of inflatable structures.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Demonstrate design performance of commercial inflatable structure like thermal, structural, mechanical durability, long term leak performance, etc.\n       </li>\n       <li>\n        Demonstrate safe deployment and operation of an inflatable structure in a flight mission.\n       </li>\n      </ul>\n      <p>\n       At the end of BEAM\'s mission, the plan was to remove it from the ISS and burn up during reentry.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ns20130116_39-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ns20130116-39">\n         [39]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On January 18, 2017, however, Bigelow and NASA announced they were discussing the possibility of extending the on-orbit life of BEAM and using it for other purposes.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spacenews20170119_40-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-spacenews20170119-40">\n         [40]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Characteristics">\n        Characteristics\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bigelow_Expandable_Activity_Module&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Characteristics">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:ISS-47_BEAM_installation_(1).jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="3280" data-file-width="4928" height="146" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/ISS-47_BEAM_installation_%281%29.jpg/220px-ISS-47_BEAM_installation_%281%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/ISS-47_BEAM_installation_%281%29.jpg/330px-ISS-47_BEAM_installation_%281%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/ISS-47_BEAM_installation_%281%29.jpg/440px-ISS-47_BEAM_installation_%281%29.jpg 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:ISS-47_BEAM_installation_(1).jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         BEAM in the process of being moved to the rear port of\n         <i>\n          Tranquility\n         </i>\n         in April 2016\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       BEAM is composed of two metal bulkheads, an aluminum structure, and multiple layers of soft fabric with spacing between layers, protecting an internal restraint and bladder system;\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FAQ_NASA_41-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-FAQ_NASA-41">\n         [41]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       it has neither windows nor internal power.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-airspace201509_42-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-airspace201509-42">\n         [42]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The module was expanded about a month after being attached to the space station. It was inflated from its packed dimensions of 2.16\xc2\xa0m (7.1\xc2\xa0ft) long and 2.36\xc2\xa0m (7.7\xc2\xa0ft) in diameter to its pressurized dimensions of 4.01\xc2\xa0m (13.2\xc2\xa0ft) long and 3.23\xc2\xa0m (10.6\xc2\xa0ft) in diameter.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-verge20160405_4-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-verge20160405-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The module has a mass of 1,413.0\xc2\xa0kg (3,115.1\xc2\xa0lb),\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nasa-overview_3-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nasa-overview-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and its interior pressure is 101.4\n       <a href="/wiki/Pascal_(unit)" title="Pascal (unit)">\n        kPa\n       </a>\n       (14.7\n       <a href="/wiki/Pounds_per_square_inch" title="Pounds per square inch">\n        psi\n       </a>\n       ; 1.0\n       <a href="/wiki/Atmosphere_(unit)" title="Atmosphere (unit)">\n        atm\n       </a>\n       ), the same as inside of the ISS.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-engadget20160325_43-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-engadget20160325-43">\n         [43]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       BEAM\'s internal dimensions provide 16\xc2\xa0m\n       <sup>\n        3\n       </sup>\n       (565\xc2\xa0cu\xc2\xa0ft) of volume where a crew member will enter the module three to four times per year to collect sensor data, perform microbial surface sampling, conduct periodic change-out of the radiation area monitors, and inspect the general condition of the module.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lvrj20130116_44-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-lvrj20130116-44">\n         [44]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FAQ_NASA_41-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-FAQ_NASA-41">\n         [41]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The hatch to the module will otherwise remain closed.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wapo20130116_45-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-wapo20130116-45">\n         [45]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Its interior is described as being "a large closet with padded white walls", with various equipment and sensors attached to two central supports.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap20130117_46-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ap20130117-46">\n         [46]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Radiation_shielding">\n        Radiation shielding\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bigelow_Expandable_Activity_Module&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Radiation shielding">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       The flexible Kevlar-like materials of construction are proprietary.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-US_7204460_B2_47-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-US_7204460_B2-47">\n         [47]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Lyle2015_48-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Lyle2015-48">\n         [48]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The multiple layers of flexible fabric and closed-cell\n       <a href="/wiki/Vinyl_polymer" title="Vinyl polymer">\n        vinyl polymer foam\n       </a>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Seedhouse2014-26_49-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Seedhouse2014-26-49">\n         [49]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       in the BEAM structural shell are expected to provide impact protection (see\n       <a href="/wiki/Whipple_shield" title="Whipple shield">\n        Whipple shield\n       </a>\n       ) as well as\n       <a href="/wiki/Radiation_protection#Spacecraft_and_radiation_protection" title="Radiation protection">\n        radiation protection\n       </a>\n       , but model calculations need to be validated by actual measurements.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FAQ_NASA_41-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-FAQ_NASA-41">\n         [41]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In a 2002 NASA study, it was suggested that materials that have high hydrogen contents, such as\n       <a href="/wiki/Polyethylene" title="Polyethylene">\n        polyethylene\n       </a>\n       , can reduce primary and secondary radiation to a greater extent than metals, such as aluminum.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nasa-radiation_50-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nasa-radiation-50">\n         [50]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Vinyl polymer may also be used in laboratories and other applications for radiation shield garments.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-newsci20021115_51-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-newsci20021115-51">\n         [51]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="BCSS_airlock">\n        BCSS airlock\n       </span>\n mentioned a concept to build a second BEAM module for use as an\n       <a href="/wiki/Airlock" title="Airlock">\n        airlock\n       </a>\n       on its planned\n       <a href="/wiki/Bigelow_Commercial_Space_Station" title="Bigelow Commercial Space Station">\n        Bigelow Commercial Space Station\n       </a>\n       .
