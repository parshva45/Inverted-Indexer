</tr>\n      </table>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        Khrunichev State Research and Production Space Center\n       </b>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       (\n       <i>\n        \xd0\x93\xd0\x9a\xd0\x9d\xd0\x9f\xd0\xa6 \xd0\xb8\xd0\xbc. \xd0\x9c. \xd0\x92. \xd0\xa5\xd1\x80\xd1\x83\xcc\x81\xd0\xbd\xd0\xb8\xd1\x87\xd0\xb5\xd0\xb2\xd0\xb0\n       </i>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">\n        Russian\n       </a>\n       ) is a\n       <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">\n        Moscow\n       </a>\n       -based producer of spacecraft and space-launch systems, including the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Proton_(rocket)" title="Proton (rocket)">\n        Proton\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Rokot" title="Rokot">\n        Rokot\n       </a>\n       rockets.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The company\'s history dates back to 1916, when an automobile factory was established outside Moscow. It soon switched production to airplanes and during\n       <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">\n        World War II\n       </a>\n       produced\n       <a href="/wiki/Ilyushin_Il-4" title="Ilyushin Il-4">\n        Ilyushin Il-4\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Tupolev_Tu-2" title="Tupolev Tu-2">\n        Tupolev Tu-2\n       </a>\n       bombers. A design bureau, OKB-23, was added to the company in 1951. In 1959, the company started developing\n       <a href="/wiki/Intercontinental_ballistic_missile" title="Intercontinental ballistic missile">\n        intercontinental ballistic missiles\n       </a>\n       , and later\n       <a href="/wiki/Spacecraft" title="Spacecraft">\n        spacecraft\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Space_launch_vehicle" title="Space launch vehicle">\n        space launch vehicles\n       </a>\n       . The company designed and produced all Soviet space stations, including\n       <a href="/wiki/Mir" title="Mir">\n        Mir\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       OKB-23, renamed to\n       <i>\n        Salyut Design Bureau\n       </i>\n       , became an independent company in 1988. In 1993, the Khrunichev Plant and the Salyut Design Bureau were joined again to form\n       <i>\n        Khrunichev State Research and Production Space Center\n       </i>\n       . In the 1990s, the company entered the\n       <a href="/wiki/International_Launch_Services" title="International Launch Services">\n        International Launch Services\n       </a>\n       joint-venture to market launches on its\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Proton_(rocket)" title="Proton (rocket)">\n        Proton\n       </a>\n       rocket. Khrunichev subsequently became a successful launch service provider on the international space launch market.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The company currently has an over 30% market share of the global space launch market, and its revenue from commercial space launches in 2009 was $584 million.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nesterov_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nesterov-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Current number of employees - about 35,000. It is named after\n       <a href="/wiki/Mikhail_Khrunichev" title="Mikhail Khrunichev">\n        Mikhail Khrunichev\n       </a>\n       , a Soviet minister.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Khrunichev\'s history dates back to April 1916, when an automobile factory called\n       <i>\n        Second "Russo-Balt" automobile plant\n       </i>\n       was established outside\n       <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">\n        Moscow\n       </a>\n       . The first cars of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Russo-Balt" title="Russo-Balt">\n        Russo-Balt\n       </a>\n       brand were produced there in 1922. The next year, the factory switched to producing\n       <a href="/wiki/Junkers_A_35" title="Junkers A 35">\n        Ju-20\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Junkers_T.21" title="Junkers T.21">\n        Ju-21\n       </a>\n       aircraft for the German company\n       <a href="/wiki/Junkers" title="Junkers">\n        Junkers\n       </a>\n       . The development of a\n       <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">\n        Russian\n       </a>\n       aircraft industry started soon after. During\n       <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">\n        World War II\n       </a>\n       , the plant produced Russian-designed\n       <a href="/wiki/Ilyushin_Il-4" title="Ilyushin Il-4">\n        Ilyushin Il-4\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Tupolev_Tu-2" title="Tupolev Tu-2">\n        Tu-2\n       </a>\n       long-range bombers. This factory later became the\n       <i>\n        Khrunichev Machine Building Plant\n       </i>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history1916_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-history1916-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nti_6-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nti-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Soviet_era">\n        Soviet era\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Khrunichev_State_Research_and_Production_Space_Center&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Soviet era">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main article:\n       <a href="/wiki/Myasishchev" title="Myasishchev">\n        Myasishchev\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       In 1951 the\n       <b>\n        Experimental Design Bureau No. 23\n       </b>\n       (\n       <b>\n        OKB-23\n       </b>\n       ) headed by V. Myasishchev was created and added to Khrunichev. In the 1950s, it designed the\n       <a href="/wiki/Myasishchev_M-4" title="Myasishchev M-4">\n        Myasishchev M-4\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Myasishchev_M-50" title="Myasishchev M-50">\n        Myasishchev M-50\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Myasishchev_M-52" title="Myasishchev M-52">\n        Myasishchev M-52\n       </a>\n       planes, among others. The Khrunichev plant was responsible for manufacturing OKB-23 designs.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history1916_5-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-history1916-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nti_6-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nti-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In 1959, the company\'s focus switched from aircraft to rocket technology, in accordange with a government decision. In the 1960s, Khrunichev and OKB-23 were subordinated to\n       <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Chelomey" title="Vladimir Chelomey">\n        Vladimir Chelomey\n       </a>\n       \'s\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/OKB-52" title="OKB-52">\n        OKB-52\n       </a>\n       (later renamed to TsKBM, today\n       <a href="/wiki/NPO_Mashinostroyeniya" title="NPO Mashinostroyeniya">\n        NPO Mashinostroyeniya\n       </a>\n       ).\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Under the OKB-52 leadership, OKB-23 started to design\n       <a href="/wiki/Intercontinental_ballistic_missile" title="Intercontinental ballistic missile">\n        intercontinental ballistic missiles\n       </a>\n       . Its first designs were the\n       <a href="/wiki/UR-200" title="UR-200">\n        UR-200\n       </a>\n       , which never flew, followed by the successful\n       <a href="/wiki/UR-100" title="UR-100">\n        UR-100\n       </a>\n       family of missiles. In 1962, the design process of the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UR-500" title="UR-500">\n        UR-500\n       </a>\n       super-heavy\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/ICBM" title="ICBM">\n        ICBM\n       </a>\n       was started. This later evolved into the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Proton_(rocket)" title="Proton (rocket)">\n        Proton\n       </a>\n       space launch vehicle in 1964. The first Proton rocket was launched on 16 July 1965.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nti_6-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nti-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history1961_7-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-history1961-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In 1966 OKB-52 was renamed the Central Design Bureau of Machine-Building (TsKBM), and OKB-23 became known as the\n       <b>\n        Fili Branch of TsKBM\n       </b>\n       . It was split off from TsKBM in the late 1970s, and renamed the\n       <b>\n        Salyut Design Bureau\n       </b>\n       , or\n       <b>\n        KB Salyut\n       </b>\n       for short. In 1981\xe2\x80\x941988, KB Salyut was part of the large\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NPO_Energiya" title="NPO Energiya">\n        NPO Energiya\n       </a>\n       company, and become an independent design bureau in 1988. The partnership with Khrunichev Machine-Building plant continued through all this time.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nti_6-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nti-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       KB Salyut and Khrunichev were responsible for designing and producing all Soviet space stations, including\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Salyut_program" title="Salyut program">\n        Salyut\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Almaz" title="Almaz">\n        Almaz\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Mir" title="Mir">\n        Mir\n       </a>\n       , as well as every heavy-weight module used for these stations.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-history1960b_8-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-history1960b-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Post-1991_adjustments">\n        Post-1991 adjustments\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Khrunichev_State_Research_and_Production_Space_Center&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Post-1991 adjustments">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:P42345701.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1445" data-file-width="2000" height="159" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/44/P42345701.jpg/220px-P42345701.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/44/P42345701.jpg/330px-P42345701.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/44/P42345701.jpg/440px-P42345701.jpg 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:P42345701.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Entrance to Khrunichev State Research and Production Space Center in\n         <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">\n          Moscow\n         </a>\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <a href="/wiki/Dissolution_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Dissolution of the Soviet Union">\n        dissolution of the Soviet Union\n       </a>\n       resulted in great difficulties for the Russian space industry. Over the years 1989\xe2\x80\x941999, the country\'s space budget dropped by 88%\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-harvey_companies_9-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-harvey_companies-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and established production cooperation chains disintegrated. Both Khrunichev and the Salyut Design Bureau, which were now separate companies, attempted to remedy the situation by seeking earning possibilities abroad. Salyut managed to win a contract for producing the\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=12KRB&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="12KRB (page does not exist)">\n        12KRB\n       </a>\n       booster for the Indian\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/GSLV" title="GSLV">\n        GSLV\n       </a>\n       rocket and entered an agreement with\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Daimler_Benz_Aerospace" title="Daimler Benz Aerospace">\n        Daimler Benz Aerospace\n       </a>\n       to develop a recoverable capsule used for experiments in the German-Japanese Express project.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kirillov_10-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-kirillov-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       At this time, the Proton launch vehicle proved as the most profitable product for both companies. The Khrunichev plant signed a $156 million contract to launch 21\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Iridium_(satellite)" title="Iridium (satellite)">\n        Iridium\n       </a>\n       satellites on three\n       <a href="/wiki/Proton-K" title="Proton-K">\n        Proton-K\n       </a>\n       rockets in 1997-1998. Salyut managed to sign only one contract for launching the\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Inmarsat-3_F&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Inmarsat-3 F (page does not exist)">\n        Inmarsat-3 F\n       </a>\n       satellite with Proton-K at the low price of $36 million. Both companies attempted to enter partnerships with foreign companies to market Proton launches.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kirillov_10-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-kirillov-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The situation where two companies, the design bureau and the factory, competed with each other to sell the same product proved problematic. To solve this, on 7 June 1993, President of Russia\n       <a href="/wiki/Boris_Yeltsin" title="Boris Yeltsin">\n        Boris Yeltsin\n       </a>\n       issued a decree to merge Khrunichev and Salyut, forming the company\n       <i>\n        Khrunichev State Research and Production Space Center\n       </i>\n       . The new company\'s status was unique in that it was not subordinate to any ministries or to the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Russian_Space_Agency" title="Russian Space Agency">\n        Russian Space Agency\n       </a>\n       . Vladimir Kirillov, writing for the\n       <i>\n        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Eksport_Vooruzheniy&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Eksport Vooruzheniy (page does not exist)">\n         Eksport Vooruzheniy\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       journal, speculates that this was because Yeltsin\'s daughter\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tatyana_Dyachenko" title="Tatyana Dyachenko">\n        Tatyana Dyachenko\n       </a>\n       , who had worked at Salyut and continued to work for Khrunichev until 1994, wished to secure a high status for her employer.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kirillov_10-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-kirillov-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Khrunichev_State_Research_and_Production_Space_Center&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Success on the international launch market">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:112px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:ILS_logo.svg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="68" data-file-width="52" height="144" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/1/11/ILS_logo.svg/110px-ILS_logo.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/1/11/ILS_logo.svg/165px-ILS_logo.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/1/11/ILS_logo.svg/220px-ILS_logo.svg.png 2x" width="110"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:ILS_logo.svg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         International Launch Service logo\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       On 15 April 1993 Khrunichev had created the Lockheed-Khrunichev-Energia joint venture with the American company\n       <a href="/wiki/Lockheed_Corporation" title="Lockheed Corporation">\n        Lockheed\n       </a>\n       , and in 1995, due to the merger of Lockheed and\n       <a Launch Services">\n        International Launch Services\n       </a>\n       (ILS). The joint venture marketed launches on both the Proton and the American\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlas_(rocket)" title="Atlas (rocket)">\n        Atlas\n       </a>\n       rockets. The\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">\n        United States\n       </a>\n       had given permission for the appearance of Proton on the international launch market, but introduced a quota to protect the launch market from "Russian dumping." Despite this, the Proton, built by Khrunichev, was successful and by the end of 2000 had earned launch contracts worth over $1.5 billion.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kirillov_10-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-kirillov-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The income from commercial launch contracts and investments from Lockheed enabled Khrunichev to conduct a serious upgrade of its facilities. This included an upgrade of the company\'s launch facilities in\n       <a href="/wiki/Baikonur_Cosmodrome" title="Baikonur Cosmodrome">\n        Baikonur\n       </a>\n       , for which several hundred million dollars were invested. The commercial earnings also allowed the company to develop new launch vehicles, boosters and spacecraft on its own without government support.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kirillov_10-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-kirillov-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Since its creation, International Launch Services has signed contracts for more than 100 launches valued at more than $8 billion.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ils_about_11-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ils_about-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In 1998, Khrunichev was made subordinate to the Russian Space Agency, then called Rosaviakosmos. Due to this, in 1996-1998, Khrunichev started the development of its own upper stage, called the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Briz-KM" title="Briz-KM">\n        Briz-KM\n       </a>\n       . Kirov\n        </a>\n        (UKVZ)\n       </li>\n      </ul>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Production">\n        Production\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Khrunichev_State_Research_and_Production_Space_Center&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Production">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Proton_Zvezda_crop.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2134" data-file-width="1466" height="247" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/14/Proton_Zvezda_crop.jpg/170px-Proton_Zvezda_crop.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/14/Proton_Zvezda_crop.jpg/255px-Proton_Zvezda_crop.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/14/Proton_Zvezda_crop.jpg/340px-Proton_Zvezda_crop.jpg 2x" width="170"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Proton_Zvezda_crop.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Proton_rocket" title="Proton rocket">\n          Proton rocket\n         </a>\n         manufactured by Khrunichev\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Khrunichev\'s main product is the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Proton_(rocket)" title="Proton (rocket)">\n        Proton\n       </a>\n       rocket, which has been launched more than 350 times since its creation in 1968. Latest version of the venerable rocket, which has been subject to several incremental upgrades, is\n       <a href="/wiki/Proton-M" title="Proton-M">\n        Proton-M\n       </a>\n       Enhanced; it has a lifting capability of 6,150kg to\n       <a href="/wiki/Geostationary_transfer_orbit" title="Geostationary transfer orbit">\n        geostationary transfer orbit\n       </a>\n       . For use as Proton\'s upper stage, Khrunichev produces\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Briz-M" title="Briz-M">\n        Briz-M\n       </a>\n       , which first flew in 2000.\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rockot" title="Rockot">\n        Rockot\n       </a>\n       , a converted ballistic missile, is offered for launching smaller payloads. For international marketing of Rockot launches, Khrunichev has created the joint-venture\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Eurockot" title="Eurockot">\n        Eurockot\n       </a>\n       GmbH. Another version of the Briz-M upper stage,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Briz-KM" title="Briz-KM">\n        Briz-KM\n       </a>\n       , is used as Rockot\'s third stage.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In 2009, the company manufactured a total of 25 launch vehicles, upper stage boosters and spacecraft, while in 2005 the figure was 11. In 2010, Khrunichev aims to raise the figure to 30.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nesterov_4-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nesterov-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       A record 15 rockets were launched by the company in 2009. In 2008, the company was responsible for lofting every third kilogram of the world\'s combined satellite payload.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nesterov_4-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nesterov-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Revenue">\n        Revenue\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Khrunichev_State_Research_and_Production_Space_Center&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Revenue">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Khrunichev\'s revenue from commercial launches (not including launches for the Ministry of Defense) in 2009 was $584 million\xe2\x80\x94growing considerably from the $198 million figure in 2004. Since 1994, Proton has earned $4.3 billion for the Russian space industry as a whole, and in 2011 this figure is expected to raise to $6 billion.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nesterov_4-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nesterov-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In 2006-2009, investments in modernization and retooling amounted to\n       <a href="/wiki/Russian_ruble" title="Russian ruble">\n        RUB\n       </a>\n       2.6 billion.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Angara_rocket_family">\n        Angara rocket family\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Khrunichev_State_Research_and_Production_Space_Center&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Angara rocket family">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Since 1995, Khrunichev has been developing the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Angara_rocket_family" title="Angara rocket family">\n        Angara rocket family\n       </a>\n       . The main purpose of Angara is to ensure Russia\'s independent access to space, as the rockets can be launched from the\n       <a href="/wiki/Plesetsk_Cosmodrome" title="Plesetsk Cosmodrome">\n        Plesetsk Cosmodrome\n       </a>\n       in northern Russia as well as the upcoming\n       <a href="/wiki/Vostochny_Cosmodrome" title="Vostochny Cosmodrome">\n        Vostochny Cosmodrome\n       </a>\n       , instead of having to rely on\n       <a href="/wiki/Baikonur_Cosmodrome" title="Baikonur Cosmodrome">\n        Baikonur Cosmodrome\n       </a>\n       , which is located on the territory of Kazakhstan. Angara also does not use the toxic fuel that is used by Proton. The Angara programme has been hit by several delays. State funding started to reach appropriate levels only in 2005. In 2009, it was estimated that Angara had 95-97% completion rate over all major program indicators. The main stumbling block is the construction of the new launch pad in Plesetsk. Due to unrealistic deflator indices set by the Ministry of Defense, Khrunichev has not received enough money to order all necessary equipment for finishing the pad, pushing Angara\'s expected first flight to 2013. When completed, the Angara rockets are intended to replace several existing launchers, including Proton.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nesterov_4-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nesterov-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="List_of_rockets_and_missiles">\n        List of rockets and
