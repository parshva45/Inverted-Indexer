Antarctica\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYT-20130206_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NYT-20130206-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On 17 href="/wiki/Cleanroom" title="Cleanroom">\n        spacecraft assembly clean rooms\n       </a>\n       . It\'s not currently known if such resistant microbes could have withstood\n       <a href="/wiki/Interplanetary_spaceflight" title="Interplanetary spaceflight">\n        space travel\n       </a>\n       and are present on the\n       <a href="/wiki/Curiosity_(rover)" title="Curiosity (rover)">\n        <i>\n         Curiosity\n        </i>\n        rover\n       </a>\n       now on the planet Mars.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NAT-20140519_23-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NAT-20140519-23">\n         [23]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       On 20 August 2014, scientists confirmed the existence of\n       <a href="/wiki/Microorganism" title="Microorganism">\n        microorganisms\n       </a>\n       living half a mile below the ice of\n       <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">\n        Antarctica\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NAT-20140820_24-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NAT-20140820-24">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-FRB-20140820_25-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-FRB-20140820-25">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Examples">\n        Examples\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Extremophile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Examples">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       New sub-types of -philes are identified frequently and the sub-category list for extremophiles is always growing. For example, microbial life lives in the liquid\n       <a href="/wiki/Asphalt" title="Asphalt">\n        asphalt\n       </a>\n       lake,\n       <a href="/wiki/Pitch_Lake" title="Pitch Lake">\n        Pitch Lake\n       </a>\n       . Research indicates that extremophiles inhabit the asphalt lake in populations ranging between 10\n       <sup>\n        6\n       </sup>\n       to 10\n       <sup>\n        7\n       </sup>\n       cells/gram.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26">\n        <a href="#cite_note-26">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27">\n        <a href="#cite_note-27">\n         [27]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Likewise, until recently\n       <a href="/wiki/Boron" title="Boron">\n        boron\n       </a>\n       tolerance was unknown but a strong borophile was discovered in bacteria. With the recent isolation of\n       <i>\n        Bacillus boroniphilus\n       </i>\n       , borophiles came into discussion.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28">\n        <a href="#cite_note-28">\n         [28]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Studying these borophiles may help illuminate the mechanisms of both boron toxicity and boron deficiency.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Industrial_uses">\n        Industrial uses\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Extremophile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Industrial uses">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       The thermoalkaliphilic\n       <a href="/wiki/Catalase" title="Catalase">\n        catalase\n       </a>\n       , which initiates the breakdown of hydrogen peroxide into oxygen and water, was isolated from an organism,\n       <i>\n        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Thermus_brockianus&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Thermus brockianus (page does not exist)">\n         Thermus brockianus\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       , found in\n       <a href="/wiki/Yellowstone_National_Park" title="Yellowstone National Park">\n        Yellowstone National Park\n       </a>\n       by\n       <a href="/wiki/Idaho_National_Laboratory" title="Idaho National Laboratory">\n        Idaho National Laboratory\n       </a>\n       researchers. The catalase operates over a temperature range from 30\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0C to over 94\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0C and a pH range from 6\xe2\x80\x9310. This catalase is extremely stable compared to other catalases at high temperatures and pH. In a comparative study, the\n       <i>\n        T. brockianus\n       </i>\n       catalase exhibited a half life of 15 days at 80\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0C and pH 10 while a catalase derived from\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Aspergillus_niger" title="Aspergillus niger">\n         Aspergillus niger\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       had a half life of 15 seconds under the same conditions. The catalase will have applications for removal of hydrogen peroxide in industrial processes such as pulp and paper bleaching, textile bleaching, food pasteurization, and surface decontamination of food packaging.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29">\n        <a href="#cite_note-29">\n         [29]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       DNA modifying enzymes such as\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Taq_polymerase" title="Taq polymerase">\n         Taq\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       DNA polymerase and some\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Bacillus" title="Bacillus">\n         Bacillus\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       enzymes used in clinical diagnostics and starch liquefaction are produced commercially by several biotechnology companies.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AnitoriRP_30-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-AnitoriRP-30">\n         [30]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="DNA_transfer">\n        DNA transfer\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Extremophile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: DNA transfer">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Over 65 prokaryotic species are known to be naturally competent for genetic transformation, the ability to transfer DNA from one cell to another cell followed by integration of the donor DNA into the recipient cell\xe2\x80\x99s chromosome.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31">\n        <a href="#cite_note-31">\n         [31]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Several extremophiles are able to carry out species-specific DNA transfer, as described below. However, it is not yet clear how common such a capability is among extremophiles.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The bacterium\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Deinococcus_radiodurans" title="Deinococcus radiodurans">\n         Deinococcus radiodurans\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       is one of the most radioresistant organisms known. This bacterium can also survive cold, dehydration, vacuum and acid and is thus known as a polyextremophile.\n       <i>\n        D. radiodurans\n       </i>\n       is competent to perform\n       <a href="/wiki/Transformation_(genetics)" title="Transformation (genetics)">\n        genetic transformation\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32">\n        <a href="#cite_note-32">\n         [32]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Recipient cells are able to repair DNA damage in donor transforming DNA that had been UV irradiated as efficiently as they repair cellular DNA when the cells themselves are irradiated. The extreme thermophilic bacterium\n       <i>\n        Thermus thermophilus\n       </i>\n       and other related\n       <i>\n        Thermus\n       </i>\n       species are also capable of genetic transformation.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-33">\n        <a href="#cite_note-33">\n         [33]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <i>\n        <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Halobacterium_volcanii&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Halobacterium volcanii (page does not exist)">\n         Halobacterium volcanii\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       , an extreme halophilic (\n       <a href="/wiki/Salinity" title="Salinity">\n        saline\n       </a>\n       tolerant) archaeon, is capable of natural genetic transformation. Cytoplasmic bridges are formed between cells that appear to be used for DNA transfer from one cell to another in either direction.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34">\n        <a href="#cite_note-34">\n         [34]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Sulfolobus_solfataricus" title="Sulfolobus solfataricus">\n         Sulfolobus solfataricus\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       and\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Sulfolobus_acidocaldarius" title="Sulfolobus acidocaldarius">\n         Sulfolobus acidocaldarius\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       are hyperthermophilic archaea. Exposure of these organisms to the DNA damaging agents UV irradiation, bleomycin or mitomycin C induces species-specific cellular aggregation.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Frols2008_35-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Frols2008-35">\n         [35]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ajon2011_36-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Ajon2011-36">\n         [36]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       UV-induced cellular aggregation of\n       <i>\n        S. acidocaldarius\n       </i>\n       mediates chromosomal marker exchange with high frequency.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ajon2011_36-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Ajon2011-36">\n         [36]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Recombination rates exceed those of uninduced cultures by up to three orders of magnitude. Frols et al.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Frols2008_35-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Frols2008-35">\n         [35]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and Ajon et al.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Ajon2011_36-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Ajon2011-36">\n         [36]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       hypothesized that cellular aggregation enhances species-specific DNA transfer between\n       <i>\n        Sulfolobus\n       </i>\n       cells in order to repair damaged DNA by means of homologous recombination. Van Wolferen et al.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37">\n        <a href="#cite_note-37">\n         [37]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       noted that this DNA exchange process may be crucial under DNA damaging conditions such as high temperatures. It has also been suggested that DNA transfer in\n       <i>\n        Sulfolobus\n       </i>\n       may be an early form of sexual interaction similar to the more well-studied bacterial transformation systems that involve species-specific DNA transfer leading to homologous recombinational repair of DNA damage\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38">\n        <a href="#cite_note-38">\n         [38]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       (and see\n       <a href="/wiki/Transformation_(genetics)" title="Transformation (genetics)">\n        Transformation (genetics)\n       </a>\n       ).\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Extracellular membrane vesicles (MVs) might be involved in DNA transfer between different hyperthermophilic archaeal species.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gaudin_viral_39-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Gaudin_viral-39">\n         [39]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       It has been shown that both plasmids\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-40">\n        <a href="#cite_note-40">\n         [40]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and viral genomes\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gaudin_viral_39-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Gaudin_viral-39">\n         [39]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       can be transferred via MVs. Notably, a horizontal plasmid transfer has been documented between hyperthermophilic\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Thermococcus" title="Thermococcus">\n         Thermococcus\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       and\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Methanocaldococcus" title="Methanocaldococcus">\n         Methanocaldococcus\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       species, respectively belonging to the orders\n       <i>\n        Thermococcales\n       </i>\n       and\n       <i>\n        Methanococcales\n       </i>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41">\n        <a href="#cite_note-41">\n         [41]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">\n        See also\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Extremophile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: See also">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="div-col columns column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 30em;
