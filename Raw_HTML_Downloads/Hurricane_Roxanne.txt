</tr>\n      </table>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        Hurricane Roxanne\n       </b>\n       was a rare and erratic\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        tropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       that caused extensive flooding in\n       <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">\n        Mexico\n       </a>\n       due to its unusual movement. The seventeenth storm, tenth hurricane, and the fifth and final major hurricane of the very active\n       <a href="/wiki/1995_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1995 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1995 Atlantic hurricane season\n       </a>\n       , Roxanne developed in the southwestern Caribbean Sea from an area of low pressure on October 7. The depression curved northward, causing it to avoid landfall in Central America. By October 9, the depression intensified enough to be upgraded to Tropical Storm Roxanne. On the following day, Roxanne turned west-northward, where it promptly intensified into a hurricane. As Roxanne headed generally westward, it began to rapidly deepen, and reached Category 3 intensity less than 24 hours after becoming a hurricane. Shortly thereafter, Roxanne made landfall near\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cozumel,_Mexico" title="Cozumel, Mexico">\n        Cozumel, Mexico\n       </a>\n       at its peak intensity, which caused severe damage.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Roxanne rapidly Intensify while traversing the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yucatan_Peninsula" title="Yucatan Peninsula">\n        Yucatan Peninsula\n       </a>\n       , and it emerged into the\n       <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Campeche" title="Bay of Campeche">\n        Bay of Campeche\n       </a>\n       on October 12, the storm was a Category 3 hurricane. Further weakening occurred, and Roxanne was downgraded to a tropical storm later that day. Roxanne tracked northwestward and eventually re-intensified into a hurricane on October 14. Thereafter, Roxanne began to meander erratically in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">\n        Gulf of Mexico\n       </a>\n       ; the storm turned abruptly southeastward, and remained nearly stationary offshore of the Yucatan Peninsula. Following day, Roxanne curved back northwestward and weakened back to a tropical storm on October 17. Roxanne completed a cyclonic loop across the Gulf of Mexico on October 18. Further weakening occurred, and Roxanne was downgraded to a tropical depression on October 19. A cold front in the Gulf of Mexico turned Roxanne abruptly southward, and the storm dissipated just offshore of\n       <a href="/wiki/Veracruz" title="Veracruz">\n        Veracruz\n       </a>\n       on October 21.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Roxanne was the first October hurricane that formed and reached Category 3 intensity on the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">\n        Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale\n       </a>\n       (SSHS) in the western Caribbean Sea since\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hattie" title="Hurricane Hattie">\n        Hurricane Hattie\n       </a>\n       in October 1961.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Due to its slow and erratic movement, Roxanne dropped heavy rainfall in many areas of southern Mexico, and some areas reported over 25 inches (640\xc2\xa0mm) of precipitation. Heavy rainfall, in turn, led to extensive flooding, which destroyed crop, washed out roads, and damaged at least 40,000 homes. In addition, significant coastal flooding also occurred, as storm surge for nearly a week caused water to travel inland for hundreds of yards. High winds also occurred over the Yucatan Peninsula, with one station reporting hurricane-force winds on October 11. Overall, it is estimated that Roxanne caused $1.5 billion (1995 USD) in damage, although not all damage could be distinguished from\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Opal" title="Hurricane Opal">\n        Hurricane Opal\n       </a>\n       . In addition, 29 fatalities were reported.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="toc">\n       <div class="toctitle">\n        <h2>\n         Contents\n        </h2>\n title="Honduras">\n        Honduras\n       </a>\n       . Although its development was complex, it is rather common in the western Caribbean.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On October 6, data indicated that a broad low, a tropical wave, was well established near the Cayman Islands and Honduras.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Satellite images indicated that the system was organizing and strengthening.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       All of these factors formed a tropical depression in the western Caribbean at 1800 UTC on October 7 near Nicaragua.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At 0000\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UTC" title="UTC">\n        UTC\n       </a>\n       on October 9, the depression became Tropical Storm Roxanne.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Roxanne now posed a threat to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Cayman_Islands" title="Cayman Islands">\n        Cayman Islands\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">\n        Cuba\n       </a>\n       . However, the trough that was steering Roxanne to the north moved on and was replaced with a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/High_pressure_system" title="High pressure system">\n        high pressure system\n       </a>\n       . This system forced Roxanne west. As the storm moved towards the\n       <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula" title="Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula">\n        Yucat\xc3\xa1n\n       </a>\n       , it intensified greatly.\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_roxanne_1995.gif">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="916" data-file-width="1160" height="174" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/94/Hurricane_roxanne_1995.gif/220px-Hurricane_roxanne_1995.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/94/Hurricane_roxanne_1995.gif/330px-Hurricane_roxanne_1995.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/94/Hurricane_roxanne_1995.gif/440px-Hurricane_roxanne_1995.gif 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_roxanne_1995.gif" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Roxanne making landfall on the\n         <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula" title="Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula">\n          Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula\n         </a>\n         .\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       On October 10, Roxanne strengthened into a hurricane at 0600 UTC and had a well-developed eye.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       By 1200 UTC on October 10, the storm continued to strengthen and its pressure had fallen to 972\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Millibar" title="Millibar">\n        mb\n       </a>\n       . Shortly after Roxanne reached hurricane strength, it rapidly intensified to Category\xc2\xa03 strength; this was the first time that had happened in the western\n       <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">\n        Caribbean Sea\n       </a>\n       since the\n       <a href="/wiki/1961_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1961 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1961 season\n       </a>\n       when\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hattie" title="Hurricane Hattie">\n        Hurricane Hattie\n       </a>\n       took a very similar turn and intensification cycle.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Roxanne reached its peak intensity with winds at 115\xc2\xa0mph and pressure of 956 mb.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Roxanne made landfall near peak intensity just north of Tulum, a small town near\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cozumel,_Mexico" title="Cozumel, Mexico">\n        Cozumel, Mexico\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Roxanne did not drop hurricane strength over land, despite the hurricane being inland for almost a full day and a half.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Roxanne emerged over water in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Campeche" title="Bay of Campeche">\n        Bay of Campeche\n       </a>\n       as a minimal hurricane. Roxanne weakened to a tropical storm again shortly after exiting the coast, and turned northwest. As soon as the circulation was mostly over water, Roxanne became a hurricane again. Since steering currents over the southern Gulf were weak, Roxanne took an erratic and unusual path. Hurricane Roxanne trudged southeast, threatening the Yucat\xc3\xa1n again before turning northwest again. Roxanne steadily weakened, turned south, and finally dissipated near the southern end of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Campeche" title="Bay of Campeche">\n        Bay of Campeche\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">\n        Preparations\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Roxanne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Throughout Roxanne\'s existence, it continuously threatened land, prompting numerous watches and warnings across Mexico and nearby areas. Upon becoming a tropical storm on October\xc2\xa09, the government of the Cayman Islands declared a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">\n        tropical storm warning\n       </a>\n       for all of the islands. Several hours later, the government of Cuba issued a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">\n        hurricane watch\n       </a>\n       and tropical storm warning for\n       <a href="/wiki/Pinar_del_R%C3%ADo_Province" title="Pinar del R\xc3\xado Province">\n        Pinar del R\xc3\xado\n       </a>\n       and the\n       <a href="/wiki/Isla_de_la_Juventud" title="Isla de la Juventud">\n        Isle of Youth\n       </a>\n       . At the same time, the government of Mexico raised hurricane watches for coastal areas between\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chetumal,_Quintana_Roo" title="Chetumal, Quintana Roo">\n        Chetumal, Quintana Roo\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Cabo_Catoche" title="Cabo Catoche">\n        Cabo Catoche\n       </a>\n       . Early on October\xc2\xa010, a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">\n        hurricane warning\n       </a>\n       was declared for areas west of Cabo Catoche to\n       <a href="/wiki/Progreso,_Yucat%C3%A1n" title="Progreso, Yucat\xc3\xa1n">\n        Progreso, Yucat\xc3\xa1n\n       </a>\n       . By the afternoon, the hurricane watch was extended past Progreso to\n       <a href="/wiki/Ciudad_del_Carmen" title="Ciudad del Carmen">\n        Ciudad del Carmen\n       </a>\n       . A new tropical storm warning was issued by the government of Belize that evening for areas north of\n       <a href="/wiki/Belize_City" title="Belize City">\n        Belize City\n       </a>\n       ; however, this warning was discontinued early on October\xc2\xa011 as the center of Roxanne was not expected to pass near the area.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In the\n       <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula" title="Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula">\n        Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula\n       </a>\n       , thousands of residents and tourists were urged to evacuate from coastal areas. In the city of\n       <a href="/wiki/Canc%C3%BAn" title="Canc\xc3\xban">\n        Canc\xc3\xban\n       </a>\n       , roughly 12,000 tourists and 3,900 residents relocated to safer areas; however, some refused to leave and decided to wait out the storm in hotels. On\n       <a href="/wiki/Cozumel" title="Cozumel">\n        Cozumel\n       </a>\n       , most hotel guests decided to ride out the storm instead of evacuate. There, most homeowners and businesses stored all lose items indoors and boarded up windows.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       An emergency alert was issued to hundreds of fishermen off the Yucat\xc3\xa1n coastline to move to port immediately.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In the state of Campeche, 150 shelters were opened and able to accommodate roughly 15,000 people.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       However, more than 20,000 people sought refuge from the storm throughout the state.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">\n        Impact\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Roxanne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Impact">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Roxanne_1995_rainfall.gif">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="806" data-file-width="1116" height="159" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Roxanne_1995_rainfall.gif/220px-Roxanne_1995_rainfall.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Roxanne_1995_rainfall.gif/330px-Roxanne_1995_rainfall.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Roxanne_1995_rainfall.gif/440px-Roxanne_1995_rainfall.gif 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Roxanne_1995_rainfall.gif" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Roxanne Rainfall across Mexico\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Roxanne caused 346 deaths, with six of them coming from the sinking of the pipelay derrick barge\n       <i>\n        DLB 269\n       </i>\n       with 245 people on board.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       There was massive damage in Mexico across numerous Mexican states. Over forty thousand homes were damaged in the States of Campeche, Quintana Roo, Tabasco Veracruz and Yucat\xc3\xa1n.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Crops were destroyed, cattle drowned, and roads were either washed out or blocked by mudslides. The road between the City of Carmen and Campeche was completely destroyed.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       There are unconfirmed reports that many hotel lobbies in Cancun and Cozumel were damaged from pounding waves.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Rainfall and storm surge combined with overflowing rivers caused the worst flooding in Campeche since 1927.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-14">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Rainfall in some areas was over 20\xc2\xa0inches, with the highest rainfall reported as 26.61\xc2\xa0inches/676\xc2\xa0mm at Silvituc/Champoton.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rainfall_7-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rainfall-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The storm surge also pounded the Mexican coastline for days and the Gulf waters surged inland hundreds of yards.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Roxanne caused the state owned\n       <a href="/wiki/Pemex" title="Pemex">\n        Pemex\n       </a>\n       to shut down 90% of its drilling in the Gulf of Mexico during its pass as well as halt all shipments from three oil terminals in the southeastern region of the gulf.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Oil_8-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Oil-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       As a result, Mexico lost millions of dollars.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Oil_8-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Oil-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Oil production dropped from 2.838 million barrels a day in September to 1.976 million barrels a day in October or 30.4%.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OilProd_9-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-OilProd-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       (A rough guess using a price of the Maya Crude averaging about $13.77/b,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-OilPrice_10-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-OilPrice-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       30 days in September, and 862,000 barrels a day of lost output yields $356 million, which in 2011 dollars equals about $530 million.) Oil production didn\'t completely recover in November, so there were some additional losses, but did completely return in December.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Throughout the state of Tabasco, nearly half of the highways sustained significant damage. Governor Roberto Madrazo Pintado estimated that it would cost roughly $60\xc2\xa0million to repair them.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Roxanne struck an area that had been directly struck by\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Opal" title="Hurricane Opal">\n        Hurricane Opal\n       </a>\n       just a few weeks earlier and all damage could not be sorted out from Opal and Roxanne. Combined damage was estimated in the neighborhood of $1.5\xc2\xa0billion.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_1-15">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Retirement">\n        Retirement\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Roxanne&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Retirement">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       See also:\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricane names">\n        List of retired Atlantic hurricane names\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Because of the major damage in\n       <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">\n        Mexico\n       </a>\n       , the name\n       <b>\n        Roxanne\n       </b>\n       was retired in the spring of 1996, and will never again be used for an\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_tropical_cyclone" title="North Atlantic tropical cyclone">\n        Atlantic hurricane\n       </a>\n       . It was replaced with\n       <b>\n        Rebekah\n       </b>\n       for the\n       <a href="/wiki/2001_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2001 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        2001 season\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">\n        See also\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span
