mission\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Launch_and_docking">\n        Launch and docking\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_14&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Launch and docking">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       The first crew of Expedition 14 launched, alongside spaceflight participant\n       <a href="/wiki/Anousheh_Ansari" title="Anousheh Ansari">\n        Anousheh Ansari\n       </a>\n       , in\n       <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-9" title="Soyuz TMA-9">\n        Soyuz TMA-9\n       </a>\n       , from Baikonur Cosmodrome in Kazakhstan aboard a Soyuz Rocket at 04:10\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UTC" title="UTC">\n        UTC\n       </a>\n       on 18 September 2006. Cosmonaut Tyurin piloted the capsule in for a perfect docking two days later at 05:21\xc2\xa0UTC on 20 September, with the hatch to the ISS opening at 08:30\xc2\xa0UTC. The members of Expedition 14 (along with Ansari) were greeted warmly by\n       <a href="/wiki/Expedition_13" title="Expedition 13">\n        Expedition 13\n       </a>\n       crew Pavel Vinogradov &amp; Jeff Williams, and also by the third member of their own expedition, ESA astronaut Thomas Reiter, who officially switched from a member of\n       <a href="/wiki/Expedition_13" title="Expedition 13">\n        Expedition 13\n       </a>\n       to membership of Expedition 14 when his soyuz seat-liner was switched.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Atlantis_reentry">\n        <i>\n         Atlantis\n        </i>\n        reentry\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_14&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Atlantis reentry">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       The day after docking, with the expedition crew working joint operations with\n       <a href="/wiki/Expedition_13" title="Expedition 13">\n        Expedition 13\n       </a>\n       , the ISS was positioned in such a way that the station\'s inhabitants were able to observe the re-entry of\n       <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Atlantis" title="Space Shuttle Atlantis">\n        Space Shuttle\n        <i>\n         Atlantis\n        </i>\n       </a>\n       at the end of\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-115" title="STS-115">\n        STS-115\n       </a>\n       . As they watched\n       <i>\n        Atlantis\n       </i>\n       create a glowing contrail during its plunge into the atmosphere, L\xc3\xb3pez-Alegr\xc3\xada and Williams provided commentary of the re-entry to the Mission Control Center in Houston. The station was a few hundred miles ahead of the shuttle at the time.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="EVA_1">\n        EVA 1\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_14&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: EVA 1">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       The expedition\'s first\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spacewalk" title="Spacewalk">\n        spacewalk\n       </a>\n       took place on 22 November 2006, starting at 19:17\n       <a href="/wiki/Eastern_Time_Zone" title="Eastern Time Zone">\n        EST\n       </a>\n       (23:17\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/UTC" title="UTC">\n        UTC\n       </a>\n       ), having been delayed from its scheduled 18:00 EST (20:00 UTC) start time due to a cooling issue in Tyurin\'s suit. Tyurin got out of the suit and straightened a suspect hose which apparently had become kinked.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       During the spacewalk, Tyurin\n       <a href="/wiki/Golf_ball#Aerodynamics" title="Golf ball">\n        hit a golf ball\n       </a>\n       from the exterior of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Pirs_(ISS_module)" title="Pirs (ISS module)">\n        Pirs\n       </a>\n       airlock.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       This "experiment" was sponsored by a Toronto-based commercial company,\n       <i>\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Element_21_(golf_company)" title="Element 21 (golf company)">\n         Element 21\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       , which manufactures golf clubs made of\n       <a href="/wiki/Scandium" title="Scandium">\n        scandium\n       </a>\n       . The ball weighs just 3\xc2\xa0grams, compared with 48\xc2\xa0grams for a standard golf ball. At that weight, it was unlikely to damage any station components which might accidentally have been hit. There were three balls allowing two options for repeating the shot if required, but only one shot was actually taken. Taken with a one-handed grip by Tyurin (with L\xc3\xb3pez-Alegr\xc3\xada stabilising Tyurin by holding his legs), the shot was a substantial slice, with the ball flying off to the right of the station instead of the rear. In 2006, there were plans for the video from the shot to be used in a TV commercial. The progress of the ball, which contains tracking equipment, could be followed on\n       <a class="external text" href="http://www.issgolf.com/" rel="nofollow">\n        E21\'s Track the Ball in Space\n       </a>\n       web site which was set up in 2006. Although, the site simply calculates an assumed distance of the ball based on a constant speed and does not perform any real tracking of the ball. The stunt was carried out around 35 years after\n       <a href="/wiki/Alan_Shepard" title="Alan Shepard">\n        Alan Shepard\n       </a>\n       hit two golf balls on the moon during\n       <a href="/wiki/Apollo_14" title="Apollo 14">\n        Apollo 14\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Michael_E._Lopez-Alegria_and_Mikhail_Tyurin_in_the_International_Space_Station_Pirs_Docking_Compartment.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2008" data-file-width="3032" height="166" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Michael_E._Lopez-Alegria_and_Mikhail_Tyurin_in_the_International_Space_Station_Pirs_Docking_Compartment.jpg/250px-Michael_E._Lopez-Alegria_and_Mikhail_Tyurin_in_the_International_Space_Station_Pirs_Docking_Compartment.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Michael_E._Lopez-Alegria_and_Mikhail_Tyurin_in_the_International_Space_Station_Pirs_Docking_Compartment.jpg/375px-Michael_E._Lopez-Alegria_and_Mikhail_Tyurin_in_the_International_Space_Station_Pirs_Docking_Compartment.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/18/Michael_E._Lopez-Alegria_and_Mikhail_Tyurin_in_the_International_Space_Station_Pirs_Docking_Compartment.jpg/500px-Michael_E._Lopez-Alegria_and_Mikhail_Tyurin_in_the_International_Space_Station_Pirs_Docking_Compartment.jpg 2x" width="250"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Michael_E._Lopez-Alegria_and_Mikhail_Tyurin_in_the_International_Space_Station_Pirs_Docking_Compartment.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Commander\n         <a href="/wiki/Michael_L%C3%B3pez-Alegr%C3%ADa" title="Michael L\xc3\xb3pez-Alegr\xc3\xada">\n          Michael E. L\xc3\xb3pez-Alegr\xc3\xada\n         </a>\n         (left) and cosmonaut\n         <a href="/wiki/Mikhail_Tyurin" title="Mikhail Tyurin">\n          Mikhail Tyurin\n         </a>\n         conduct pre-spacewalk operations in the\n         <a href="/wiki/Pirs_(ISS_module)" title="Pirs (ISS module)">\n          Pirs\n         </a>\n         airlock (February 2007)\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Inspection of a Kurs antenna on the\n       <a href="/wiki/Progress_(spacecraft)" title="Progress (spacecraft)">\n        Progress\n       </a>\n       23 unpiloted cargo carrier that docked at the aft end of the station\'s\n       <a href="/wiki/Zvezda_(ISS_module)" title="Zvezda (ISS module)">\n        Zvezda Service Module\n       </a>\n       on 26 October 2006 was the next task. Final latching of the spacecraft to the station during the docking procedure was delayed by more than three hours because Mission Control Moscow was not sure the antenna was completely retracted. Tyurin and L\xc3\xb3pez-Alegr\xc3\xada moved to the rear of Zvezda and photographed the antenna. It was still fully extended, so Tyurin used a screwdriver to release a latch and tried to retract the antenna. Russian flight controllers also tried to retract it by activating a motor. Neither succeeded, and the task was abandoned.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Next they relocated a WAL antenna, which was later used to guide the unpiloted European cargo carrier, the\n       <a href="/wiki/Automated_Transfer_Vehicle" title="Automated Transfer Vehicle">\n        Automated Transfer Vehicle\n       </a>\n       , when it docked with the station. The first ATV,\n       <a href="/wiki/Jules_Verne_ATV" title="Jules Verne ATV">\n        Jules Verne\n       </a>\n       , eventually docked with the ISS on 3 April 2008. In its previous position the antenna interfered with a cover for a Zvezda booster engine.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Then the two installed a BTN neutron experiment, which characterises charged and neutral particles in low Earth orbit. Atop Zvezda, its readings during solar bursts continue to be of special interest to scientists as of 2010. Two thermal covers from the BTN were jettisoned before the spacewalkers returned to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Pirs_(ISS_module)" title="Pirs (ISS module)">\n        Pirs\n       </a>\n       airlock at 00:55 EST (04:55 UTC) on the morning of 23 November, bringing the 5-hour, 38-minute EVA to a close .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       A final scheduled task, an inspection of bolts on one of two Strela hand-operated cranes on the docking compartment, was postponed to a future EVA.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Conclusion">\n        Conclusion\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_14&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Conclusion">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       This mission was the longest expedition to the ISS thus far. Also, the Soyuz capsule was the oldest one ever used. L\xc3\xb3pez-Alegr\xc3\xada, already the U.S. recordholder for spacewalks, now also holds the record for longest spaceflight by a NASA astronaut.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Spacewalks">\n        Spacewalks\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_14&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Spacewalks">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       There were five spacewalks conducted during Expedition 14, totaling 33 hours and 42 minutes.\n       <a href="/wiki/Michael_L%C3%B3pez-Alegr%C3%ADa" title="Michael L\xc3\xb3pez-Alegr\xc3\xada">\n        Michael L\xc3\xb3pez-Alegr\xc3\xada\n       </a>\n       participated in all five space walks, while\n       <a href="/wiki/Sunita_Williams" title="Sunita Williams">\n        Sunita Williams\n       </a>\n       took part in three, and\n       <a href="/wiki/Mikhail_Tyurin" title="Mikhail Tyurin">\n        Mikhail Tyurin\n       </a>\n       participated in 2. L\xc3\xb3pez-Alegr\xc3\xada\'s five spacewalks set a record for EVA\'s during an ISS mission. This was matched by\n       <a href="/wiki/Peggy_Whitson" title="Peggy Whitson">\n        Peggy Whitson\n       </a>\n       during\n       <a href="/wiki/Expedition_16" title="Expedition 16">\n        Expedition 16\n       </a>\n       , who also performed five spacewalks.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Note: \xe2\x80\xa1 indicates a spacewalk conducted from the\n       <a
