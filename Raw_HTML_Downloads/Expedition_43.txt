</div>\n        </td>\n       </tr>\n      </table>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        Expedition 43\n       </b>\n       was the 43rd expedition to the\n       <a href="/wiki/International_Space_Station" title="International Space Station">\n        International Space Station\n       </a>\n       . It commenced on 11 March 2015 with the undocking of\n       <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-14M" title="Soyuz TMA-14M">\n        Soyuz TMA-14M\n       </a>\n       , returning the crew of\n       <a href="/wiki/Expedition_42" title="Expedition 42">\n        Expedition 42\n       </a>\n       to Earth and ended with the departure of\n       <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-15M" title="Soyuz TMA-15M">\n        Soyuz TMA-15M\n       </a>\n       on 11 June 2015.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The Expedition 43 spend an extra four weeks due to a delay caused by a re-supply spacecraft issue, and the extra time was called a "bonus month" by the crew.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On June 8, 2015 ISS adjusted its orbit to allow a bigger distance to a piece of orbital space debris.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       This expedition also used the\n       <a href="/wiki/ISSpresso" title="ISSpresso">\n        ISSpresso\n       </a>\n       machine and tested a special cup designed to be drunk from in microgravity by using capillary flow.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       This was a further development of a zero gravity cup invented by Canadian astronaut\n       <a href="/wiki/Donald_Pettit" title="Donald Pettit">\n        Donald Pettit\n       </a>\n       and tested on ISS in 2008.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The new zero g coffee cup idea was further developed by a Fluid physicist at Portland State University among others.\n
