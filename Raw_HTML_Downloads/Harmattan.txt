class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sahara_Desert" title="Sahara Desert">\n        Sahara Desert\n       </a>\n       over West Africa into the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Guinea" title="Gulf of Guinea">\n        Gulf of Guinea\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BHarmattan_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-BHarmattan-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The name comes from or is related to\n       <a href="/wiki/Ga_language" title="Ga language">\n        Ga\n       </a>\n       <i>\n        haramata\n       </i>\n       .\n       <sup <a href="#cite_note-dove_press-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       in most places, but can be hot\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       in some places, depending on circumstances.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The Harmattan blows during the dry season, which occurs during the lowest-sun months, when the\n       <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_ridge" ambient temperatures\n       </a>\n       of the day and night. (86\xc2\xa0\xc2\xb0F), while the\n       <a href="/wiki/Relative_humidity" title="Relative humidity">\n        relative humidity\n       </a>\n       drops under 10%.\n      </p>\n      <div
