</div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       A\n       <b>\n        flash flood\n       </b>\n       is a rapid\n       <a href="/wiki/Flood" title="Flood">\n        flooding\n       </a>\n       of\n       <a href="/wiki/Geomorphology" title="Geomorphology">\n        geomorphic\n       </a>\n       low-lying areas: washes,\n       <a href="/wiki/River" title="River">\n        rivers\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Dry_lake" title="Dry lake">\n        dry lakes\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Depression_(geology)" title="Depression (geology)">\n        basins\n       </a>\n       . It may be caused by heavy\n       <a href="/wiki/Rain" title="Rain">\n        rain\n       </a>\n       associated with a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Severe_thunderstorm" title="Severe thunderstorm">\n        severe thunderstorm\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane" title="Hurricane">\n        hurricane\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_storm" title="Tropical storm">\n        tropical storm\n       </a>\n       , or\n       <a href="/wiki/Meltwater" title="Meltwater">\n        meltwater\n       </a>\n       from ice or snow flowing over ice sheets or snowfields. Flash floods may occur after the collapse of a natural\n       <a href="/wiki/Ice_dam" title="Ice dam">\n        ice\n       </a>\n       or\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Debris_dam" title="Debris dam">\n        debris dam\n       </a>\n       , or a human structure such as a man-made\n       <a href="/wiki/Dam" title="Dam">\n        dam\n       </a>\n       , as occurred before the\n       <a href="/wiki/Johnstown_Flood" title="Johnstown Flood">\n        Johnstown Flood\n       </a>\n       of 1889. Flash floods are distinguished from regular floods by a timescale of less than six hours.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The water that is temporarily available is often used by plants with rapid germination and short growth cycles and by specially adapted animal life.\n       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">\n        [\n        <i>\n         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">\n          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. <p>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Causes">\n        Causes\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Flash_flood&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Causes">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Flash floods can occur under several types of conditions. Flash flooding occurs when it rains rapidly on saturated soil or dry soil that has poor absorption ability. The runoff collects in gullies and streams and, as they join to form larger volumes, often forms a fast flowing front of water and debris.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Flash floods most often occur in normally dry areas that have recently received precipitation, but they may be seen anywhere downstream from the source of the precipitation, even many miles from the source. In areas on or near\n       <a href="/wiki/Volcano" title="Volcano">\n        volcanoes\n       </a>\n       , flash floods have also occurred after eruptions, when\n       <a href="/wiki/Glacier" title="Glacier">\n        glaciers\n       </a>\n       have been melted by the intense heat. Flash floods are known to occur in the highest mountain ranges of the\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">\n        United States\n       </a>\n       and are also common in the arid plains of the Southwestern United States. Flash flooding can also be caused by extensive rainfall released by\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        hurricanes\n       </a>\n       and other\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_storm" title="Tropical storm">\n        tropical storms\n       </a>\n       , as well as the sudden thawing effect of\n       <a href="/wiki/Ice_dam" title="Ice dam">\n        ice dams\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-srh.noaa.gov_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-srh.noaa.gov-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Human activities can also cause flash floods to occur. When\n       <a href="/wiki/Dam_failure" title="Dam failure">\n        dams fail\n       </a>\n       , a large quantity of water can be released and destroy everything in its path.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-srh.noaa.gov_3-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-srh.noaa.gov-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" href="/w/index.php?title=Flash_flood&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Hazards">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Canandaigua_flood_July_23%2C_2017_barn_flooded_4.jpg/440px-Canandaigua_flood_July_23%2C_2017_barn_flooded_4.jpg 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Canandaigua_flood_July_23,_2017_barn_flooded_4.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         A flash flood greatly inundates a small ditch, flooding barns and ripping out newly installed drain pipes.\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The United States\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Weather_Service" title="National Weather Service">\n        National Weather Service\n       </a>\n       gives the advice "Turn Around, Don\'t Drown" for flash floods; that is, it recommends that people get out of the area of a flash flood, rather than trying to cross it. Many people tend to underestimate the dangers of flash floods. What makes flash floods most dangerous is their sudden nature and fast-moving water. A vehicle provides little to no protection against being swept away; it may make people overconfident and less likely to avoid the flash flood. More than half of the fatalities attributed to flash floods are people swept away in vehicles when trying to cross flooded intersections.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       As little as 2 feet (0.61\xc2\xa0m) of water is enough to carry away most SUV-sized vehicles.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The U.S. National Weather Service reported in 2005 that, using a national 30-year average, more people die yearly in floods, 127 on average, than by\n       <a href="/wiki/Lightning" title="Lightning">\n        lightning\n       </a>\n       (73),\n       <a href="/wiki/Tornado" title="Tornado">\n        tornadoes\n       </a>\n       (65), or\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane" title="Hurricane">\n        hurricanes\n       </a>\n       (16).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Desert" title="Desert">\n        deserts\n       </a>\n       , flash floods can be particularly deadly for several reasons. First, storms in arid regions are infrequent, but they can deliver an enormous amount of water in a very short time. Second, these rains often fall on poorly absorbent and often clay-like soil, which greatly increase the amount of\n       <a href="/wiki/Surface_runoff" title="Surface runoff">\n        runoff\n       </a>\n       that rivers and other water channels have to handle. These regions tend not to have the infrastructure that wetter regions have to divert water from structures and roads, such as storm drains, culverts, and\n       <a href="/wiki/Retention_basin" title="Retention basin">\n        retention basins\n       </a>\n       , either because of sparse population, poverty, or because residents believe the risk of flash floods is not high enough to justify the expense. In fact, in some areas, desert roads frequently cross dry river and creek beds without bridges. From the driver\'s perspective, there may be clear weather, when a river unexpectedly forms ahead of or around the vehicle in a matter of seconds.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Finally, the lack of regular rain to clear water channels may cause flash floods in deserts to be headed by large amounts of debris, such as rocks, branches, and logs.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Deep\n       <a href="/wiki/Slot_canyon" title="Slot canyon">\n        slot canyons\n       </a>\n       can be especially dangerous to hikers as they may be flooded by a storm that occurs on a\n       <a href="/wiki/Mesa" title="Mesa">\n        mesa\n       </a>\n       miles away, sweeps through the canyon, and makes it difficult to climb up and out of the way to avoid the flood.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Significant_examples">\n        Significant examples\n       </span>\n
