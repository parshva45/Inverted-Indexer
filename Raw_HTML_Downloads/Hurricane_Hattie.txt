    </td>\n       </tr>\n       <tr>\n        <td colspan="2" style="text-align:center;padding:0px 2px">\n        </td>\n       </tr>\n       <tr>\n        <td colspan="2" style="text-align:center;    Hurricane Hattie\n       </b>\n       was the strongest and deadliest\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        tropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       of the\n       <a href="/wiki/1961_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1961 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1961 Atlantic hurricane season\n       </a>\n       , reaching a peak intensity equivalent to that of a\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_Category_5_Atlantic_hurricanes" title="List of Category 5 Atlantic hurricanes">\n        Category\xc2\xa05\xc2\xa0hurricane\n       </a>\n       . The ninth tropical storm and seventh hurricane and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Major_hurricane" title="Major hurricane">\n        major hurricane\n       </a>\n       of the season, Hattie originated from an area of\n       <a href="/wiki/Low-pressure_area" title="Low-pressure area">\n        low pressure\n       </a>\n       that strengthened into a tropical storm over the southwestern Caribbean Sea on October\xc2\xa027. Moving generally northward, the storm quickly became a hurricane and later major hurricane the following day. Hattie then turned westward west of\n       <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">\n        Jamaica\n       </a>\n       and strengthened into a Category\xc2\xa05 hurricane, with\n       <a href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_wind" title="Maximum sustained wind">\n        maximum sustained winds\n       </a>\n       of 160\xc2\xa0mph (260\xc2\xa0km/h). It weakened to Category\xc2\xa04 before making\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">\n        landfall\n       </a>\n       south of\n       <a href="/wiki/Belize_City" title="Belize City">\n        Belize City\n       </a>\n       on October\xc2\xa031. The storm turned southwestward and weakened rapidly over the mountainous terrain of Central America, dissipating on November\xc2\xa01.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Hattie first affected the southwestern Caribbean, where it produced hurricane-force winds and caused one death on\n       <a href="/wiki/San_Andr%C3%A9s_(island)" title="San Andr\xc3\xa9s (island)">\n        San Andres Island\n       </a>\n       . It was initially forecast to continue north and strike\n       <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">\n        Cuba\n       </a>\n       , prompting evacuations on the island. While turning west, Hattie dropped heavy rainfall of up to 11.5\xc2\xa0in (290\xc2\xa0mm) on\n       <a href="/wiki/Grand_Cayman" title="Grand Cayman">\n        Grand Cayman\n       </a>\n       . The country of\n       <a href="/wiki/Belize" title="Belize">\n        Belize\n       </a>\n       , at the time known as\n       <a href="/wiki/British_Honduras" title="British Honduras">\n        British Honduras\n       </a>\n       , sustained the worst damage from the hurricane.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [nb 1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The former capital, Belize City, was buffeted by strong winds and flooded by a powerful\n       <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">\n        storm surge\n       </a>\n       . The territory governor estimated that 70%\xc2\xa0of the buildings in the city had been damaged, leaving more than 10,000\xc2\xa0people homeless. The destruction was so severe that it prompted the government to relocate inland to a new city,\n       <a href="/wiki/Belmopan" title="Belmopan">\n        Belmopan\n       </a>\n       . Overall, Hattie caused about $60\xc2\xa0million in losses\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [nb 2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and 307\xc2\xa0deaths in the territory. Although damage was heavier in Hattie than a\n       <a href="/wiki/1931_Belize_hurricane" title="1931 Belize hurricane">\n        hurricane in 1931\n       </a>\n       that killed 2,000\xc2\xa0people, the death toll from Hattie was less due to advance warnings. Elsewhere in Central America, Hattie killed 11\xc2\xa0people in Guatemala and one in Honduras.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="toc">\n       <div class="toctitle">\n class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Hattie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hattie_1961_track.png">\n         <img alt="The path of a tropical cyclone, as represented by colored dots, denoting the storm\'s intensity and position at six hour intervals. Starting slightly right of center, the track moves up, before turning left and then to the bottom-left corner of the map." class="thumbimage" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dd/Hattie_1961_track.png/220px-Hattie_1961_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dd/Hattie_1961_track.png/330px-Hattie_1961_track.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dd/Hattie_1961_track.png/440px-Hattie_1961_track.png 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hattie_1961_track.png" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Map plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the\n         <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">\n          Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale\n         </a>\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       For several days toward the end of October 1961, a\n       <a href="/wiki/Low-pressure_area" title="Low-pressure area">\n        low-pressure area\n       </a>\n       persisted in the western\n       <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">\n        Caribbean Sea\n       </a>\n       , north of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Panama_Canal_Zone" title="Panama Canal Zone">\n        Panama Canal Zone\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-4">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On October\xc2\xa025, an upper-level\n       <a href="/wiki/Anticyclone" title="Anticyclone">\n        anticyclone\n       </a>\n       moved over the low; the next day, a\n       <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">\n        trough\n       </a>\n       over the western Gulf of Mexico provided favorable\n       <a href="/wiki/Outflow_(meteorology)" title="Outflow (meteorology)">\n        outflow\n       </a>\n       for the disturbance. At 0000\n       <a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">\n        UTC\n       </a>\n       on October\xc2\xa027, a ship nearby reported southerly winds of 46\xc2\xa0mph (74\xc2\xa0km/h). Later that day, the airport on\n       <a href="/wiki/San_Andr%C3%A9s_(island)" title="San Andr\xc3\xa9s (island)">\n        San Andres Island\n       </a>\n       reported easterly winds of 60\xc2\xa0mph (95\xc2\xa0km/h). The two observations confirmed the presence of a closed wind circulation, centered about 70\xc2\xa0miles (110\xc2\xa0km) southeast of San Andres, or 155\xc2\xa0mi (250\xc2\xa0km) east of the Nicaraguan coast; as a result, the\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Weather_Service" title="National Weather Service">\n        Miami Weather Bureau\n       </a>\n       began issuing advisories on the newly formed Tropical Storm Hattie.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-5">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       After being classified, Hattie moved steadily northward, passing very near or over San Andres Island. A station on the island recorded a pressure of 991\xc2\xa0mbar (29.3\xc2\xa0inHg) and sustained winds of 80\xc2\xa0mph (130\xc2\xa0km/h), which indicated that Hattie had reached hurricane status.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_5-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-5">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Late on October\xc2\xa028, a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hunters" title="Hurricane Hunters">\n        Hurricane Hunters\n       </a>\n       flight encountered a much stronger hurricane, with winds of 125\xc2\xa0mph (200\xc2\xa0km/h) in a small area near the center. At the time, gale-force winds extended outward 140\xc2\xa0mi (225\xc2\xa0km) to the northeast and 70\xc2\xa0miles (115\xc2\xa0km) to the southwest.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_4-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-4">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Early on October\xc2\xa029, a trough extending from Nicaragua to Florida was expected to allow Hattie to continue northward, based on climatology for similar hurricanes.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_5-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-5">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Later that day, Hattie was forecast to be an imminent threat to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Cayman_Islands" title="Cayman Islands">\n        Cayman Islands\n       </a>\n       and western Cuba.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_4-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-4">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Around that time, a strengthening\n       <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">\n        ridge\n       </a>\n       to the north turned the hurricane northwestward, which spared the\n       <a href="/wiki/Greater_Antilles" title="Greater Antilles">\n        Greater Antilles\n       </a>\n       but increased the threat to\n       <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">\n        Central America\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_5-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-5">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       With the strengthening ridge to its north, Hattie began restrengthening after retaining the same intensity for about 24\xc2\xa0hours.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_5-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-5">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hurdat_6-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-hurdat-6">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Initially, forecasters at the Miami Weather Bureau predicted the storm to turn northward again. Late on October\xc2\xa029, the center of the hurricane passed about 90\xc2\xa0miles (145\xc2\xa0km) southwest of\n       <a href="/wiki/Grand_Cayman" title="Grand Cayman">\n        Grand Cayman\n       </a>\n       , at which time the interaction between Hattie and the ridge to its north produced squally winds of around 30\xc2\xa0mph (50\xc2\xa0km/h) across Florida. Early on October\xc2\xa030, the Hurricane Hunters confirmed the increase in intensity, reporting winds of 140\xc2\xa0mph (225\xc2\xa0km/h).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_4-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-4">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The storm\'s\n       <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_pressure" title="Atmospheric pressure">\n        minimum central pressure\n       </a>\n       continued to drop throughout the day, reaching 924\xc2\xa0mbar (27.3\xc2\xa0inHg) by 1300\xc2\xa0UTC; a lower pressure of 920\xc2\xa0mbar (27\xc2\xa0inHg) was computed at 1700\xc2\xa0UTC that day, based on a flight-level reading from the Hurricane Hunters.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_5-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-5">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Hattie later curved toward the west-southwest, passing between the Cayman Islands and the\n       <a href="/wiki/Swan_Islands,_Honduras" title="Swan Islands, Honduras">\n        Swan Islands\n       </a>\n       . Late on October\xc2\xa030, Hattie attained peak winds of 160\xc2\xa0mph (260\xc2\xa0km/h) about 190\xc2\xa0mi (310\xc2\xa0km) east of the border of Mexico and\n       <a href="/wiki/British_Honduras" title="British Honduras">\n        British Honduras\n       </a>\n       . This made Hattie the equivalence of a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Atlantic_Category_5_hurricanes" title="List of Atlantic Category 5 hurricanes">\n        Category\xc2\xa05\n       </a>\n       hurricane on the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">\n        Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale\n       </a>\n       , making it the latest hurricane on record to reach the status until a\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_hurricane_reanalysis_project" title="Atlantic hurricane reanalysis project">\n        reanalysis\n       </a>\n       of the\n       <a href="/wiki/1932_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1932 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1932\xc2\xa0season\n       </a>\n       revealed that\n       <a href="/wiki/1932_Cuba_hurricane" title="1932 Cuba hurricane">\n        Hurricane Fourteen\n       </a>\n       had a similar intensity on November 5, six days after Hattie.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hurdat_6-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-hurdat-6">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Additionally, Hattie was the strongest October hurricane in the northwest Caribbean until\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Mitch" title="Hurricane Mitch">\n        Hurricane Mitch\n       </a>\n       in 1998.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-7">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Hattie maintained much of its intensity as it continued toward the coast of British Honduras. After moving through several small islands offshore, the hurricane made\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">\n        landfall\n       </a>\n       a short distance south of\n       <a href="/wiki/Belize_City" title="Belize City">\n        Belize City\n       </a>\n       on October\xc2\xa031, with an\n       <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">\n        eyewall\n       </a>\n       of about 25\xc2\xa0miles (40\xc2\xa0km) in diameter.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_5-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-5">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Based on a post-season analysis, it was determined that Hattie had weakened to winds of 140\xc2\xa0mph (225\xc2\xa0km/h) before moving ashore.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hurdat_6-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-hurdat-6">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The hurricane deteriorated rapidly over land, dissipating on November\xc2\xa01 as it moved into the mountains of\n       <a href="/wiki/Guatemala" title="Guatemala">\n        Guatemala\n       </a>\n       . During its dissipation,\n       <a href="/wiki/1961_Pacific_hurricane_season#Tropical_Storm_Simone" title="1961 Pacific hurricane season">\n        Tropical Storm Simone\n       </a>\n       was developing off the Pacific coast of Guatemala.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_5-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-5">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hurdat_6-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-hurdat-6">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       There was speculation that Hattie contributed to the development of Simone, and later\n       <a href="/wiki/1961_Atlantic_hurricane_season#Tropical_Storm_Inga" title="1961 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        Tropical Storm Inga\n       </a>\n       after the remnants of Simone merged with nearby disturbed weather.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-e15_8-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-e15-8">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">\n        Preparations\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Hattie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Upon initiating advisories on Hattie, the Miami Weather Bureau noted the potential for heavy rainfall and\n       <a href="/wiki/Flash_flood" title="Flash flood">\n        flash flooding\n       </a>\n       in the southwestern Caribbean. The advisories recommended for small ships to remain at harbor across the region.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_4-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-4">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Initially, the hurricane was predicted to move near or through the Cayman Islands, Jamaica, and Cuba.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_5-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-5">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       As a result, Cuban officials advised residents in low-lying areas to evacuate.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-9">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Hurricane Hattie first posed a threat to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula" title="Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula">\n        Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula\n       </a>\n       and British Honduras on October\xc2\xa030 when it turned toward the area.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_4-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-4">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Officials at the Miami Weather Bureau warned of the potential for high tides, strong winds, and torrential rainfall. The warnings allowed for extensive evacuations in high-risk areas.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_4-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-4">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Most people in the capital, Belize City, were evacuated or moved to shelters,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_5-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-5">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and a school was operated as a refuge.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-even_10-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-even-10">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A hospital in the city was evacuated,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-upi112_11-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-upi112-11">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and over 75% of the population of\n       <a href="/wiki/Dangriga" title="Dangriga">\n        Stann Creek\n       </a>\n       fled to safer locations.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_5-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-5">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       After Hattie made landfall, officials in Mexico ordered the closure of ports along the\n       <a href="/wiki/Isthmus_of_Tehuantepec" title="Isthmus of Tehuantepec">\n        Isthmus of Tehuantepec\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-upi112_11-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-upi112-11">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">\n        Impact\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Hattie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Impact">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Atoll_research_bulletin_(1969)_(20345898205).jpg">\n         <img alt="Black-and-white map of then-British Honduras showing hurricane damage." class="thumbimage" data-file-height="2158" data-file-width="3104" height="153" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/Atoll_research_bulletin_%281969%29_%2820345898205%29.jpg/220px-Atoll_research_bulletin_%281969%29_%2820345898205%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/Atoll_research_bulletin_%281969%29_%2820345898205%29.jpg/330px-Atoll_research_bulletin_%281969%29_%2820345898205%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/Atoll_research_bulletin_%281969%29_%2820345898205%29.jpg/440px-Atoll_research_bulletin_%281969%29_%2820345898205%29.jpg 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Atoll_research_bulletin_(1969)_(20345898205).jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Map of British Honduras showing hurricane damage\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Despite predictions for heavy rainfall in the southwestern Caribbean, the hurricane\'s movement was more northerly than expected, resulting in less precipitation along the Central American coast than anticipated.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_4-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-4">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In its early developmental stages, Hattie struck\n       <a href="/wiki/San_Andr%C3%A9s_(island)" title="San Andr\xc3\xa9s (island)">\n        San Andr\xc3\xa9s Island\n       </a>\n       , located offshore eastern\n       <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">\n        Nicaragua\n       </a>\n       , with maximum sustained winds of 80\xc2\xa0mph (130\xc2\xa0km/h) and gusts of 104\xc2\xa0mph (167\xc2\xa0km/h). As the hurricane neared the island, the airport was closed due to tropical-storm-force winds.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_5-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-5">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Rough seas and winds damaged private property and two hotels. Many palm tree plantations were devastated. The schooner\n       <i>\n        Admirar\n       </i>\n       , anchored in one of the island\'s bays, capsized during the storm.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PlanEmergencia_12-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-PlanEmergencia-12">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Overall, Hattie resulted in one death, fifteen injuries, and $300,000 in damage (1961\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">\n        USD\n       </a>\n       ) in San Andr\xc3\xa9s. The hurricane was the fourth on record to strike the island, and of the four was the only to approach from the south.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_5-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-5">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In the northwestern Caribbean, Hattie passed close to Grand Cayman with heavy rainfall. At least 11.5\xc2\xa0inches (292\xc2\xa0mm) of rain were reported on the island, including 7.8\xc2\xa0inches (198\xc2\xa0mm) in six hours.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_4-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-4">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Winds on Grand Cayman were below hurricane force, and only minor damage occurred due to the rain.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_4-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-4">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The interaction between Hattie and the ridge of high pressure to its north produced sustained winds of 20\xc2\xa0mph (35\xc2\xa0km/h) across most of Florida, with a gust of 72\xc2\xa0mph (116\xc2\xa0km/h) reported at\n       <a href="/wiki/Hillsboro_Inlet_Light" title="Hillsboro Inlet Light">\n        Hillsboro Inlet Light\n       </a>\n       ; the winds caused some\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beach_erosion" title="Beach erosion">\n        beach erosion\n       </a>\n       in the state.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_4-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-4">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The U.S. Weather Bureau issued a small craft warning for the west and east Florida coastlines, as well as northward to\n       <a href="/wiki/Brunswick,_Georgia" title="Brunswick, Georgia">\n        Brunswick, Georgia\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-13">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Later, Hattie impacted various countries in Central America with flash floods, causing 11\xc2\xa0deaths in Guatemala and one fatality in Honduras.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_5-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-5">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The\n       <a href="/wiki/Swan_Islands,_Honduras" title="Swan Islands, Honduras">\n        Swan Islands\n       </a>\n       reported wind gusts just below hurricane force, resulting in minor damage and one injury.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_4-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-4">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="British_Honduras">\n        British Honduras\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Hattie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: British Honduras">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Hurricane Hattie moved ashore in British Honduras with a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Storm_tide" title="Storm tide">\n        storm tide\n       </a>\n       of up to 14\xc2\xa0feet (4.3\xc2\xa0m) near Belize City,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_5-14">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-5">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       a city of 31,000\xc2\xa0people located at sea-level; its only defenses against the storm tide were a small seawall and a strip of swamp lands.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-re1031_14-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-re1031-14">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The capital experienced high waves and a 10\xc2\xa0ft (3\xc2\xa0m) storm tide along its waterfront that reached the third story of some buildings. A trained observer estimated winds of over 150\xc2\xa0mph (240\xc2\xa0km/h), and winds in the territory were unofficially estimated as strong as 200\xc2\xa0mph (325\xc2\xa0km/h).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_5-15">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-5">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       When Hattie affected the area, most buildings in Belize City were wooden, and most of this type were destroyed.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-upi112_11-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-upi112-11">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Offshore, the hurricane heavily damaged 80% of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Belize_Barrier_Reef" title="Belize Barrier Reef">\n        Belize Barrier Reef\n       </a>\n       , although the reef recovered after the storm.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-WorldAtlas_15-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-WorldAtlas-15">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       High winds caused a power outage,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-re1031_14-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-re1031-14">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       downed trees across the region, and destroyed the roofs of many buildings.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lee_16-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-lee-16">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/List_of_Governors_of_British_Honduras" title="List of Governors of British Honduras">\n        Governor Colin Thornley\n       </a>\n       estimated that over 70%\xc2\xa0of the buildings in the territory were damaged, and more than 10,000\xc2\xa0people were left homeless.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-upi112_11-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-upi112-11">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Some shelters set up before the storm were destroyed in the hurricane.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_4-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-4">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The hurricane destroyed the wall at an\n       <a href="/wiki/Psychiatric_hospital" title="Psychiatric hospital">\n        insane asylum\n       </a>\n       , which allowed the residents to escape.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lee_16-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-lee-16">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       High waves damaged a prison,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gadsden_17-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-gadsden-17">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       prompting officials to institute a "daily parole" program for the inmates.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lee_16-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-lee-16">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Hattie also flooded the\n       <a href="/wiki/Government_House,_Belize" title="Government House, Belize">\n        Government House\n       </a>\n       , washing away all records.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gadsden_17-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-gadsden-17">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       All of Belize City was coated in a layer of mud and debris,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-lee_16-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-lee-16">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and majority of the city was destroyed or severely damaged, as was nearby\n       <a href="/wiki/Stann_Creek_District" title="Stann Creek District">\n        Stann Creek\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_5-16">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-5">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The hurricane left significant crop damage across the region, including $2\xc2\xa0million in citrus fruits and similar losses to timber, cocoa, and bananas.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_5-17">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-5">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The year\'s production of sugar cane was also heavily damaged. About 70%\xc2\xa0of the territory\'s\n       <a href="/wiki/Mahogany" title="Mahogany">\n        mahogany\n       </a>\n       trees were downed, as were most citrus and grapefruit trees. The hurricane damaged several factories and oil rigs in the region.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap115_18-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ap115-18">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Damage throughout the territory totaled $60\xc2\xa0million (1961\xc2\xa0USD),\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_5-18">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-5">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and a total of 307\xc2\xa0deaths were reported;\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-b45_19-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-b45-19">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       more than 100\xc2\xa0of the fatalities were in Belize City,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_4-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-4">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       including 36 who evacuated to a British administration building that was later destroyed in the storm.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap115b_20-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ap115b-20">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The government of British Honduras considered Hurricane Hattie more damaging than a\n       <a href="/wiki/1931_Belize_hurricane" title="1931 Belize hurricane">\n        hurricane in 1931\n       </a>\n       that killed 2,000\xc2\xa0people; the lower death toll of Hattie was due to advance warning.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mwr_5-19">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mwr-5">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">\n        Aftermath\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Hattie&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Aftermath">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:British_Honduras_1962_Hurricane_Hattie_stamp.jpg">\n         <img alt="A rectangular postage stamp, denoting that the stamp is of the British Honduras and costs ten cents. <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:British_Honduras_1962_Hurricane_Hattie_stamp.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       After Hattie struck, officials in Belize City declared\n       <a href="/wiki/Martial_law" title="Martial law">\n        martial law\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-upi112_11-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-upi112-11">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A manager of\n       <a href="/wiki/United_Press_International" title="United Press International">\n        United Press International\n       </a>\n       described Belize City as "nothing but a huge pile of matchsticks,"\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-upi112_11-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-upi112-11">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and many roads were either flooded for days or covered with mud.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-calg_21-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-calg-21">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Doctors provided\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Typhoid" title="Typhoid">\n        typhoid\n       </a>\n       vaccinations to 12,000\xc2\xa0residents in two days to prevent the spread of the disease. Due to the high death toll, officials ordered mass cremations to stop additional disease from spreading.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-upi116_22-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-upi116-22">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At the city\'s police station, workers provided fresh water and rice to storm victims.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-upi112_11-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-upi112-11">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Many residents throughout British Honduras donated supplies to the storm victims, such that an airlines manager described it as "taxing... manpower and facilities." One airline allowed donations to be flown to Belize City at no cost.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap115_18-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ap115-18">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The city\'s three newspapers were unable to operate due to lack of power after the storm.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap115_18-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ap115-18">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       By November\xc2\xa05, Belize City\'s post office reopened on a limited basis, and all business initially remained closed. About 4,000\xc2\xa0homeless residents from\n       <a href="/wiki/Stann_Creek_District" title="Stann Creek District">\n        Stann Creek\n       </a>\n       were moved by boat to the northern portion of the territory.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap115b_20-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ap115b-20">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Many homeless people from the Belize City area set up a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tent_city" title="Tent city">\n        tent city\n       </a>\n       on\n       <a href="/wiki/Bushland" title="Bushland">\n        bushland\n       </a>\n       about 16\xc2\xa0mi (26\xc2\xa0km) inland, which was initially intended to be temporary.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap115b_20-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ap115b-20">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hattieville_23-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-hattieville-23">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In December 1961,\n       <a href="/wiki/Barracks" title="Barracks">\n        barracks\n       </a>\n       were erected near a Red Cross Hospital to house the homeless in the camp. The site was named\n       <a href="/wiki/Hattieville" title="Hattieville">\n        Hattieville\n       </a>\n       and became a proper city, with utilities installed in the subsequent decade.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hattieville_23-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-hattieville-23">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       About 200\xc2\xa0British soldiers arrived from Jamaica to quell looting and maintain order.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ap115b_20-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ap115b-20">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-upi116_22-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-upi116-22">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At least 20\xc2\xa0people were arrested in the day after Hattie struck.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gadsden_17-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-gadsden-17">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The British government sent flights of aid to the territory containing food, clothing, and medical supplies.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-upi112_11-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-upi112-11">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The\n       <a href="/wiki/House_of_Commons_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="House of Commons of the United Kingdom">\n        House of Commons\n       </a>\n       quickly passed a bill to provide\n       <a href="/wiki/Pound_sterling" title="Pound sterling">\n        \xc2\xa3\n       </a>\n       10,000\xc2\xa0in aid. The\n       <a href="/wiki/Save_the_Children" title="Save the Children">\n        Save the Children\n       </a>\n       fund sent \xc2\xa31,000\xc2\xa0to British Honduras,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-glascow_24-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-glascow-24">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and the Mexican government sent three flights with food and medicine to the territory.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-upi112_11-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-upi112-11">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Two American\n       <a href="/wiki/Destroyer" title="Destroyer">\n        destroyers\n       </a>\n       arrived in the country by November\xc2\xa02, reporting the need for assistance.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-upi112_11-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-upi112-11">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The\n       <a href="/wiki/USS_Antietam_(CV-36)" title="USS Antietam (CV-36)">\n        USS Antietam\n       </a>\n       remained at port for weeks after the storm with six medical officers and six\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Marines" title="United States Marines">\n        Marine\n       </a>\n       helicopters. Four other ships sailed to the territory to provide 458,000\xc2\xa0lb (208,000\xc2\xa0kg) of food.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-upi116_22-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-upi116-22">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25">\n        <a href="#cite_note-25">\n         [23]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The United States government allocated about $300,000\xc2\xa0in assistance through the\n       <a href="/wiki/International_Development_Association" title="International Development Association">\n        International Development Association\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-glascow_24-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-glascow-24">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The Canadian government provided\n       <a href="/wiki/Canadian_dollar" title="Canadian dollar">\n        C$\n       </a>\n       75,000\xc2\xa0worth of aid, including food, blankets, and medical supplies.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26">\n        <a href="#cite_note-26">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       By Hattie\'s one year anniversary, private and public workers repaired and rebuilt buildings affected by the storm. New hotels were constructed, and many stores were reopened.\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_Prime_Ministers_of_Belize" title="List of Prime Ministers of Belize">\n        Prime Minister\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/George_Cadle_Price" title="George Cadle Price">\n        George Cadle Price\n       </a>\n       successfully appealed for assistance from the British government, which ultimately provided \xc2\xa320\xc2\xa0million\xc2\xa0in loans.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27">\n        <a href="#cite_note-27">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In the days after the storm, the government announced plans to relocate the capital of British Honduras farther inland on higher ground. Work on the new capital,\n       <a href="/wiki/Belmopan" title="Belmopan">\n        Belmopan\n       </a>\n       , was completed in 1970.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-even_10-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-even-10">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28">\n        <a href="#cite_note-28">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On the 44th anniversary of the hurricane in 2005, the government of Belize unveiled a monument in Belize City to recognize the victims of the hurricane.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29">\n        <a href="#cite_note-29">\n         [27]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Due to the destruction and loss of life attributed to the hurricane, the name Hattie was\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricane names">\n        retired\n       </a>\n       by the\n       <a href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organization" title="World Meteorological Organization">\n        World Meteorological Organization\n       </a>\n       and will never again be used for an Atlantic hurricane.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30">\n        <a href="#cite_note-30">\n         [28]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline"
