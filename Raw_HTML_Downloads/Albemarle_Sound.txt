Orbital photo courtesy of NASA.\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        Albemarle Sound\n       </b>\n       (\n       <span class="nowrap">\n        <span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt">\n         <a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">\n          /\n          <span style="border-bottom:1px dotted">\n           <span title="/\xcb\x88/: primary stress follows">\n            \xcb\x88\n           </span>\n           <span title="/\xc3\xa6/: \'a\' in \'bad\'">\n            \xc3\xa6\n           </span>\n           <span title="\'l\' in \'lie\'">\n            l\n           </span>\n           <span title="\'b\' in \'buy\'">\n            b\n           </span>\n           <span title="/\xc9\x99/: \'a\' in \'about\'">\n            \xc9\x99\n           </span>\n           <span title="/\xcb\x8c/: secondary stress follows">\n            \xcb\x8c\n           </span>\n           <span title="\'m\' in \'my\'">\n            m\n           </span>\n           <span title="/\xc9\x91\xcb\x90/: \'a\' in \'father\'">\n            \xc9\x91\xcb\x90\n           </span>\n           <span title="\'r\' in \'rye\'">\n            r\n           </span>\n           <span title="\'l\' in \'lie\'">\n            l\n           </span>\n          </span>\n          /\n         </a>\n        </span>\n       </span>\n       ) is a large\n       <a href="/wiki/Estuary" title="Estuary">\n        estuary\n       </a>\n       on the coast of\n       <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">\n        North Carolina\n       </a>\n       in the United States located at the confluence of a group of rivers, including the\n       <a href="/wiki/Chowan_River" title="Chowan River">\n        Chowan\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Roanoke_River" title="Roanoke River">\n        Roanoke\n       </a>\n       . It is separated from the\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">\n        Atlantic Ocean\n       </a>\n       by the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Currituck_Banks" title="Currituck Banks">\n        Currituck Banks\n       </a>\n       , barrier peninsula upon which the town of\n       <a href="/wiki/Kitty_Hawk,_North_Carolina" title="Kitty Hawk, North Carolina">\n        Kitty Hawk\n       </a>\n       is located, at the eastern edge of the sound, and part of the greater\n       <a href="/wiki/Outer_Banks" title="Outer Banks">\n        Outer Banks\n       </a>\n       region.\n       <a href="/wiki/Roanoke_Island" title="Roanoke Island">\n        Roanoke Island\n       </a>\n       is situated at the southeastern corner of the sound, where it connects to\n       <a href="/wiki/Pamlico_Sound" title="Pamlico Sound">\n        Pamlico Sound\n       </a>\n       . Much of the water in the Albemarle Sound is brackish or fresh, as opposed to the saltwater of the ocean, as a result of river water pouring into the sound.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Some small portions of the Albemarle have been given their own "sound" names to distinguish these bodies of water from other parts of the large estuary. The\n       <a href="/wiki/Croatan_Sound" title="Croatan Sound">\n        Croatan Sound\n       </a>\n       , for instance, lies between mainland\n       <a href="/wiki/Dare_County,_North_Carolina" title="Dare County, North Carolina">\n        Dare County\n       </a>\n       and Roanoke Island. The eastern shore of the island to the Outer Banks is commonly referred to as the\n       <a href="/wiki/Roanoke_Sound" title="Roanoke Sound">\n        Roanoke Sound\n       </a>\n       (which is also a historical name for the entire body of water now known as Albemarle Sound\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       ). The long stretch of water from near the\n       <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">\n        Virginia\n       </a>\n       state line south to around the\n       <a href="/wiki/Currituck_County,_North_Carolina" title="Currituck County, North Carolina">\n        Currituck County\n       </a>\n       southern boundary is known as the\n       <a href="/wiki/Currituck_Sound" title="Currituck Sound">\n        Currituck Sound\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <a href="/wiki/Sound_(geography)" title="Sound (geography)">\n        sound\n       </a>\n       forms part of the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlantic_Intracoastal_Waterway" title="Atlantic Intracoastal Waterway">\n        Atlantic Intracoastal Waterway\n       </a>\n       . Its coast saw the first permanent English settlements in what became North Carolina, the\n       <a href="/wiki/Albemarle_Settlements" title="Albemarle Settlements">\n        Albemarle Settlements\n       </a>\n       . Many inland\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tidewater_(geographic_term)" title="Tidewater (geographic term)">\n        Tidewater\n       </a>\n       communities along the Albemarle today are part of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Inner_Banks" title="Inner Banks">\n        Inner Banks\n       </a>\n       region of the state.\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:NS_train_crosses_Albermarle_Sound_1918.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="485" data-file-width="714" height="204" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/NS_train_crosses_Albermarle_Sound_1918.jpg/300px-NS_train_crosses_Albermarle_Sound_1918.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/NS_train_crosses_Albermarle_Sound_1918.jpg/450px-NS_train_crosses_Albermarle_Sound_1918.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/76/NS_train_crosses_Albermarle_Sound_1918.jpg/600px-NS_train_crosses_Albermarle_Sound_1918.jpg 2x" width="300"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:NS_train_crosses_Albermarle_Sound_1918.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Norfolk_Southern_Railroad_(1883-1891,_1910-1942)" title="Norfolk Southern Railroad (1883-1891, 1910-1942)">\n          Norfolk Southern Railroad\n         </a>\n         passenger train crosses the Albemarle Sound\n         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trestle" title="Trestle">\n          trestle\n         </a>\n         in 1918. <p>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">\n        History\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Albemarle_Sound&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Before Europeans came to the Albemarle Sound, the\n       <a href="/wiki/Algonquian_peoples" title="Algonquian peoples">\n        Algonquian\n       </a>\n       Indians lived in the region. They traveled the sound in\n       <a href="/wiki/Dugout_canoe" title="Dugout canoe">\n        dugout canoes\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Trapped_fish" title="Trapped fish">\n        trapped fish\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In 1586 the first European explorers sailed up the fifty-five-mile (89\xc2\xa0km) length of the Albemarle Sound. Half a century later, the first European settlers came south from\n       <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">\n        Virginia\n       </a>\n       , establishing agricultural and trading colonies along the shores of the Sound. The Albemarle Sound soon became a very important thoroughfare, with small trading ships called coasters carrying cargo to and from other colonies, and larger merchant ships bringing spices, silks, and sugars from the\n       <a href="/wiki/West_Indies" title="West Indies">\n        West Indies\n       </a>\n       in exchange for products such as\n       <a href="/wiki/Tobacco" title="Tobacco">\n        tobacco\n       </a>\n       (a major export of the southern colonies),\n       <a href="/wiki/Herring" title="Herring">\n        herring\n       </a>\n       , and lumber.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In 1663, Albemarle Sound was made part of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Province_of_Carolina" title="Province of Carolina">\n        Province of Carolina\n       </a>\n       by\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/King_Charles_II_of_England" title="King Charles II of England">\n        King Charles II of England\n       </a>\n       and given to eight\n       <a href="/wiki/Lords_Proprietor" title="Lords Proprietor">\n        Lords Proprietors\n       </a>\n       . One of these royal beneficiaries was\n       <a href="/wiki/George_Monck,_1st_Duke_of_Albemarle" title="George Monck, 1st Duke of Albemarle">\n        George Monck, 1st Duke of Albemarle\n       </a>\n       , for whom the sound is named.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Ferries" title="Ferries">\n        Ferries\n       </a>\n       were a common method of transportation through the swamps surrounding the Albemarle Sound throughout the history of the region. One ferry that linked the towns of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Edenton" title="Edenton">\n        Edenton\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Mackeys,_North_Carolina" title="Mackeys, North Carolina">\n        Mackeys, North Carolina\n       </a>\n       , continued in service from 1734 to 1938, when a bridge was built across the Sound. Another longer bridge of more than 3 miles (4.8\xc2\xa0km) in length was built in 1990.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Fishing was a major industry in the Albemarle Sound. In late spring, plantation farmers would fish for\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Shad" title="Shad">\n        shad\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Striped_bass" title="Striped bass">\n        striped bass\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Herring" title="Herring">\n        herring\n       </a>\n       . Nets used by these fishermen were sometimes enormous, with some more than a mile (1600 m) long, and were frequently staffed 24 hours a day. Herring was cut and salted for export to Europe, while shad was packed in ice and shipped up the\n       <a href="/wiki/Chowan_River" title="Chowan River">\n        Chowan River\n       </a>\n       to be sold in northern colonies. Regional striped bass tournaments attracted sport fishermen to the area, and it was considered by many to be the greatest striped bass fishery in the world.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Pollution and development in recent years has depleted the fisheries of the Albemarle Sound by seventy percent.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">\n        See also\n
