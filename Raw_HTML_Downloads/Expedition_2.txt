They returned to Earth on mission\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-105" title="STS-105">\n        STS-105\n       </a>\n       , 22 August 2001 after having spent 163 days aboard the station and 167 days in space. Only Voss performed a spacewalk on STS-101, along with\n       <a href="/wiki/Jeffrey_Williams_(astronaut)" title="Jeffrey Williams (astronaut)">\n        Jeffrey Williams\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       During this expedition, research facilities launched to the Space Station included a Human Research Facility, two EXPRESS (Expedite the Processing of Experiments to the Space Station) Racks, one of which contains the Active Rack Isolation System and the Payload Equipment Restraint System. They also prepared the Destiny laboratory to enable upcoming experiments to be conducted.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SFexp2_2-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SFexp2-2">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       A major focus was on gaining a better understanding of how to protect crew members from radiation while working and living in space. Radiation exposure in high doses over long periods of time can damage human cells and cause cancer or injury to the central nervous system.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Exp2Science_7-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Exp2Science-7">\n         [NASA 5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Launch_and_docking">\n        Launch and docking\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Launch and docking">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main article:\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-102" title="STS-102">\n        STS-102\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The three-member Expedition 2 crew successfully launched on 8 March 2001 on Space Shuttle\n       <i>\n        Discovery\n       </i>\n       during mission\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-102" title="STS-102">\n        STS-102\n       </a>\n       . They docked with the International Space Station on 10 March, but the Expedition 2 increment didn\'t begin until the previous crew undocked from the station on 18 March.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="STS-100">\n        STS-100\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: STS-100">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main article:\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-100" title="STS-100">\n        STS-100\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The first visitors to the station during Expedition 2 was the crew of\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-100" title="STS-100">\n        STS-100\n       </a>\n       , when they docked Space Shuttle\n       <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Endeavour" title="Space Shuttle Endeavour">\n        <i>\n         Endeavour\n        </i>\n       </a>\n       with the ISS on 21 April 2001. They spent eight days docked to the station. The primary objective of this mission was to deliver and install the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Canadarm2" title="Canadarm2">\n        Canadarm2\n       </a>\n       on the ISS, which is a robotic arm similar to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Canadarm" title="Canadarm">\n        Canadarm\n       </a>\n       which is used on some Space Shuttle flights.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-STS100_8-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-STS100-8">\n         [NASA 6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A later mission in 2002,\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-111" title="STS-111">\n        STS-111\n       </a>\n       , would deliver a movable base platform which would allow the Canadarm2 to have a larger range.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="floatright">\n       <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Soyuz_TM-32_patch.png" title="EP-1 insignia">\n        <img alt="EP-1 insignia" This 8 day mission is sometimes referred to as\n       <i>\n        ISS EP-1\n       </i>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EA-EP1_9-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-EA-EP1-9">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <i>\n        ISS-2S\n       </i>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SR-2S_10-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SR-2S-10">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <i>\n        Soyuz 2 Taxi Flight\n       </i>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Soyuz2_11-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Soyuz2-11">\n         [NASA 7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       or simply by its launching spacecraft\n       <i>\n        Soyuz TM-32\n       </i>\n       . The Commander of this visiting mission was\n       <a href="/wiki/Kazakhs" title="Kazakhs">\n        Kazakh\n       </a>\n       cosmonaut\n       <a href="/wiki/Talgat_Musabayev" title="Talgat Musabayev">\n        Talgat Musabayev\n       </a>\n       , who had previously been on two long-duration missions aboard the space station\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Mir" title="Mir">\n         Mir\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       in the 1990s. The other crew member of ISS EP-1 was\n       <a href="/wiki/Yuri_Baturin" title="Yuri Baturin">\n        Yuri Baturin\n       </a>\n       , who had one other spaceflight,\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mir_EP-4&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mir EP-4 (page does not exist)">\n        Mir EP-4\n       </a>\n       , which was a visiting mission to\n       <i>\n        Mir\n       </i>\n       launched with the spacecraft\n       <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TM-28" title="Soyuz TM-28">\n        Soyuz TM-28\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-EA-EP4_12-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-EA-EP4-12">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Baturin\'s first mission occurred during the long-duration mission\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Mir_EO-25&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Mir EO-25 (page does not exist)">\n        Mir EO-25\n       </a>\n       , so he and Musabayev had already been in space together prior to ISS EP-1; in fact both were landed with the spacecraft\n       <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TM-27" title="Soyuz TM-27">\n        Soyuz TM-27\n       </a>\n       in August 1998.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="STS-104">\n        STS-104\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: STS-104">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main article:\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-104" title="STS-104">\n        STS-104\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       In July, Space Shuttle\n       <a href="/wiki/Space_Shuttle_Atlantis" title="Space Shuttle Atlantis">\n        <i>\n         Atlantis\n        </i>\n       </a>\n       docked with the station for an eight-day visit as a part of\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-104" title="STS-104">\n        STS-104\n       </a>\n       . The main objective of this mission was to install the\n       <a href="/wiki/Quest_Joint_Airlock" title="Quest Joint Airlock">\n        Quest Joint Airlock\n       </a>\n       onto the station.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The STS-104 crew performed 3\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extra-vehicular_activity" title="Extra-vehicular activity">\n        spacewalks\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <dl>\n       <dt>\n        First spacewalk; Joint Airlock Installation\n       </dt>\n      </dl>\n      <p>\n       The first spacewalk occurred on 15 July, and focused on installation of the Quest Joint Airlock. The spacewalkers helped as Susan Helms, using the station\'s robotic arm, lifted the new station airlock from Atlantis\' payload bay and moved it to the station\'s Unity module. During much of the 5 hour, 59 minute spacewalk, Jim Reilly worked from a foot platform attached to the end of the shuttle\'s robotic arm, operated by Janet Kavandi. After the spacewalk, crew members inside the Station attached connections to the airlock to prevent thermal damage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-STS104a_13-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-STS104a-13">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-STS104b_14-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-STS104b-14">\n         [NASA 8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-STS104f_15-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-STS104f-15">\n         [NASA 9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <dl>\n       <dt>\n        Second spacewalk\n       </dt>\n      </dl>\n      <p>\n       The second spacewalk which happened on 18 July, lasted 6 hours, 29 minutes. The internal hatches between the shuttle and station were closed at the end of Flight Day 6 so Atlantis\' cabin pressure could be lowered in preparation for the second spacewalk. The major objective was to attach and connect an oxygen and a nitrogen tank. Susan Helms operated the station arm to lift the tanks from the shuttle\'s payload bay and maneuver them to the new airlock. At the airlock, Mike Gernhardt and Jim Reilly latched the tanks in place and connected cables and hoses. The spacewalkers were able to get ahead of schedule and also install another oxygen tank, leaving only one tank to be connected on the third spacewalk.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-STS104a_13-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-STS104a-13">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-STS104f_15-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-STS104f-15">\n         [NASA 9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-STS104c_16-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-STS104c-16">\n         [NASA 10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <dl>\n       <dt>\n        Third spacewalk\n       </dt>\n      </dl>\n      <p>\n       The third spacewalk, which occurred on 21 July, was the first spacewalk based out of the new Quest Joint Airlock, and lasted 4 hours, 2 minutes. Primary objective was to install the final nitrogen tank outside the airlock.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-STS104f_15-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-STS104f-15">\n         [NASA 9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-quest1_17-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-quest1-17">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-STS104d_18-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-STS104d-18">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       This spacewalk tested a new protocol developed by former commercial diver Mike Gernhardt: essentially exercising while breathing oxygen to purge nitrogen from the spacewalkers\' bodies.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">\n        <a href="#cite_note-19">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Undocking_and_landing">\n        Undocking and landing\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Undocking and landing">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main article:\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-105" title="STS-105">\n        STS-105\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       In August, Space Shuttle\n       <i>\n        Discovery\n       </i>\n       returned to the station during the mission\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-105" title="STS-105">\n        STS-105\n       </a>\n       , which carried the three Expedition 2 crew members back down to Earth. They undocked from the station on 20 August 2001, marking the end of the Expedition 2 increment.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Mission_patch">\n        Mission patch\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Expedition_2&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Mission patch">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       The International Space Station Expedition Two patch depicts the Space Station as it appeared during the time the second crew was on board. The United States and Russian flags show the nationalities of the crew indicating the joint nature of the program. When asked about the stars in the background, a crew spokesperson said they "...represent the thousands of space workers throughout the ISS partnership who have contributed to the successful construction of our International Space Station."\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PatchBlurb_20-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-PatchBlurb-20">\n         [NASA 11]\n        </a>\n
