NRHP\n        </th>\n        <td style="border: 0;">\n         August 29, 1977\n        </td>\n       </tr>\n      </table>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <b>\n        Hurricane Canal\n       </b>\n       is a hand-excavated irrigation canal that diverted water from the\n       <a href="/wiki/Virgin_River" title="Virgin River">\n        Virgin River\n       </a>\n       to the vicinity of\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane,_Utah" title="Hurricane, Utah">\n        Hurricane, Utah\n       </a>\n       . The 7.5-mile (12.1\xc2\xa0km) canal was built starting in 1891 and completed in 1904. The work included a diversion dam on the Virgin River, the 12-foot (3.7\xc2\xa0m) wide canal grade with an 8-foot (2.4\xc2\xa0m) wide by 4-foot (1.2\xc2\xa0m) deep channel, and a series of flumes and trestles. Work was done by local men, mainly between November and May. The work was paid for by the canal\'s shareholders, whose lands on the Hurricane Bench were to be irrigated by the canal. Allotments were limited to 20 acres (8.1\xc2\xa0ha) for shareholder. The 2,000 acres (810\xc2\xa0ha) of new agricultural lands allowed the settlement of Hurricane after 1906.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nrhpinv1_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nrhpinv1-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The work and the shareholder system were organized by James Jepson and John Steele. The superintendent of construction was J.T. Willis. Work was hampered by the failure of the first two diversion dams at the Narrows section of the Virgin River. The final dam was a timber crib and boulder structure, later augmented with concrete. From the dam the canal runs westerly 3.5 miles (5.6\xc2\xa0km) along the wall of the Virgin River Canyon, then south for three miles along the Hurricane Cliffs to the Hurricane Bench. It passes through twelve tunnels and six flumes. Wood flumes have been replaced with metal, and ten cisterns for drinking water have been abandoned.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nrhpinv1_2-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nrhpinv1-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The Hurricane Canal was listed on the\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">\n        National Register of Historic Places\n       </a>\n       on August 26, 1977.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nris_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nris-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The canal no longer carries water. A trail now runs along the dry canal.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hurricanecity1_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-hurricanecity1-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Eleven of the twelve tunnels remain accessible to hikers.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-blm1_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-blm1-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n
