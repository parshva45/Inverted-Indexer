         </a>\n         </span>\n        </td>\n       </tr>\n      </table>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        Indonesian Agency for Meteorological, Climatological and Geophysics\n       </b>\n       (\n       <i>\n        <b>\n         Badan Meteorologi, Klimatologi, dan Geofisika\n        </b>\n       </i>\n       or simply\n       <b>\n        BMKG\n       </b>\n       ) is an\n       <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">\n        Indonesian\n       </a>\n       non-departmental government agency for\n       <a href="/wiki/Meteorology" title="Meteorology">\n        meteorology\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Climatology" title="Climatology">\n        climatology\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Geophysics" title="Geophysics">\n        geophysics\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="toc">\n       <div class="toctitle">\n        <h2>\n         Contents\n        </h2>\n       </div>\n       <ul>\n        <li class="toclevel-1 tocsection-1">\n         <a href="#History">\n          <span class="tocnumber">\n           1\n          </span>\n          <span class="toctext">\n class="toclevel-1 tocsection-3">\n         <a href="#References">\n          <span class="tocnumber">\n           3\n          </span>\n          <span class="toctext">\n           References\n   <span class="toctext">\n           External links\n          </span>\n         </a>\n        </li>\n       </ul>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">\n        History\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Indonesian_Agency_for_Meteorology,_Climatology_and_Geophysics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Its history began on 1841 with individual observation conducted by Dr. Onnen, the head of hospital in\n       <a href="/wiki/Bogor" title="Bogor">\n        Bogor\n       </a>\n       , and was established as a formal government institution on 1866 by the\n       <a href="/wiki/Dutch_East_Indies" title="Dutch East Indies">\n        Dutch East Indies\n       </a>\n       government by the name of\n       <i>\n        Magnetisch en Meteorologisch Observatorium\n       </i>\n       . The agency name changed several times and its current name was given on 6 September 2008.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Tropical_Cyclone_Warning_Centre">\n        Tropical Cyclone Warning Centre\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Indonesian_Agency_for_Meteorology,_Climatology_and_Geophysics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Tropical Cyclone Warning Centre">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Since 1986 the BMKG, has run a Tropical Cyclone Warning Centre (TCWC), within their headquarters in Jakarta.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jak_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Jak-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Over the next 12 seasons, the TCWC named and issued international warnings for the area from the Equator to 10\xc2\xb0S between 90\xc2\xb0E and 125\xc2\xb0E. In 1998, the\n       <a href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organization" title="World Meteorological Organization">\n        World Meteorological Organization\n       </a>\n       \xe2\x80\x99s RA V Tropical Cyclone Committee recommended that\n       <a href="/wiki/Bureau_of_Meteorology" title="Bureau of Meteorology">\n        TCWC Perth\n       </a>\n       , in Australia take over warning responsibility on an interim basis until the BMKG\'s staff had the training to run the TCWC.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jak_2-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Jak-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       TCWC Perth then took over the warning and naming responsibilities until the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2007-08_Australian_region_cyclone_season" title="2007-08 Australian region cyclone season">\n        2007-08 season\n       </a>\n       when they handed it back to TCWC Jakarta.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jak_2-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Jak-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The first depression to be named by TCWC Jakarta came later that year when Cyclone Durga became a Tropical Cyclone within their area of responsibility.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Jak_2-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Jak-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       During the next two seasons TCWC Jakarta, monitored several tropical cyclones in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Typhoon" title="Typhoon">\n        North Western Pacific Ocean\n       </a>\n       and the Australian region. At the start of the 2010-11 season, TCWC Jakarta\'s area of responsibility was then extended out to include the region from the Equator to 10\xc2\xb0S between 125\xc2\xb0E and 141\xc2\xb0E.\n
