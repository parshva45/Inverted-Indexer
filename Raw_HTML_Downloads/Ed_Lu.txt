<a href="/wiki/Pi_Kappa_Phi" title="Pi Kappa Phi">\n        Pi Kappa Phi\n       </a>\n       fraternity at Cornell.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Lu became a specialist in\n       <a missions\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-84" title="STS-84">\n        STS-84\n       </a>\n       in 1997 and\n       <a href="/wiki/STS-106" title="STS-106">\n        STS-106\n       </a>\n       in 2000, in which he carried out a six-hour spacewalk to perform construction work on the International Space Station. Having been flight engineer on\n       <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-2" title="Soyuz TMA-2">\n        Soyuz TMA-2\n       </a>\n       , Lu spent six months in space in 2003 as part of\n       <a href="/wiki/Expedition_7" title="Expedition 7">\n        ISS Expedition 7\n       </a>\n       , with cosmonaut\n       <a href="/wiki/Yuri_Malenchenko" title="Yuri Malenchenko">\n        Yuri Malenchenko\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NASA-bio_Ed_Lu_1-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NASA-bio_Ed_Lu-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In July 2003, Lu and Malenchenko answered questions from students participating in Japan\'s\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/NASDA" title="NASDA">\n        NASDA\n       </a>\n       special educational event, where Ed Lu performed\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Happy_Birthday_to_You" title="Happy Birthday to You">\n         Happy Birthday\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       on an electronic piano for a student\'s birthday in the live broadcast from space.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       He had demonstrated the difficulty of playing the piano instrument in space during a live in-orbit interview with CBS News and NASA TV video feed.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Also on this ISS mission on 15 October 2003, Lu communicated with the crewman of China\'s first manned mission into space,\n       <a href="/wiki/Yang_Liwei" title="Yang Liwei">\n        Yang Liwei\n       </a>\n       , flying aboard\n       <a href="/wiki/Shenzhou_5" title="Shenzhou 5">\n        Shenzhou 5\n       </a>\n       . Lu congratulated the Chinese for the achievement and said in\n       <i>\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Putonghua" title="Putonghua">\n         Putonghua\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       :\n       <i>\n        "Welcome to space. Have a safe journey"\n       </i>\n       . Malenchenko added:\n       <i>\n        "I love to have somebody else in space instead of (just) me and Ed"\n       </i>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       While still employed at NASA, Lu co-founded the\n       <a href="/wiki/B612_Foundation" title="B612 Foundation">\n        B612 Foundation\n       </a>\n       along with former astronaut\n       <a href="/wiki/Rusty_Schweickart" title="Rusty Schweickart">\n        Rusty Schweickart\n       </a>\n       and scientists Clark Chapman and\n       <a href="/wiki/Piet_Hut" title="Piet Hut">\n        Piet Hut\n       </a>\n       . It has conducted two lines of related research to help detect asteroids that could one day strike the Earth, and find the technological means for asteroid deflection. The foundation\'s current goal is to design and build a privately financed asteroid-finding space telescope, Sentinel, to be launched in 2017\xe2\x80\x932018. The Sentinel\'s infrared telescope, once parked in an orbit similar to that of Venus, will help identify asteroids and other near-Earth objects (NEOs) that pose a risk of collision with Earth.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NASA-bio_Ed_Lu_1-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NASA-bio_Ed_Lu-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Magic_trick_in_space">\n        Magic trick in space\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ed_Lu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Magic trick in space">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       While on the ISS, Lu teamed up with magician\n       <a href="/wiki/James_Randi" title="James Randi">\n        James Randi\n       </a>\n       (founder of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/JREF" title="JREF">\n        JREF\n       </a>\n       ) to perform a card trick in outer space. Randi asked Lu to (without looking) select a card from the middle of a brand new freshly shuffled deck of cards, turn it around and reinsert the card into the deck the opposite direction. Then place the deck of cards back into its box then take them out again and fan the cards to the camera. This same procedure was being done by Randi back on Earth at the JREF headquarters in front of witnesses from the Miami Herald. Both Randi and Lu successfully selected the same card, the seven of diamonds. The remaining cards were jettisoned for weight reasons before returning to Earth, but Lu smuggled the card home. The two cards complete with autographs are framed for public view at the JREF offices in Fort Lauderdale, FL. Randi asks Lu "What are the odds that we would both choose the same card?" Lu answers "Knowing you and your tricky ways, the odds were one in one, because it\'s a trick!"\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="After_NASA">\n        After NASA\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ed_Lu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: After NASA">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       On August 10, 2007, Lu announced he was retiring from NASA to work at\n       <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">\n        Google\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NASA-bio_Ed_Lu_1-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NASA-bio_Ed_Lu-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NASA-Press_Release:_07-176_2-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NASA-Press_Release:_07-176-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In June 2010, Lu left Google and worked out of the Sunfire Offices.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In September 2011, Lu joined\n       <a href="/wiki/Liquid_Robotics" title="Liquid Robotics">\n        Liquid Robotics\n       </a>\n       as Chief of Innovative Applications, where his work includes outreach to promote new applications for ocean science,\n       <sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">\n        [\n        <i>\n         <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed">\n          <span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (October 2011)">\n           citation needed\n          </span>\n         </a>\n        </i>\n        ]\n       </sup>\n       and in 2012, he joined Hover Inc. as its Chief Technology Officer.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       On June 28, 2012, Lu, with Apollo 9 Astronaut\n       <a href="/wiki/Rusty_Schweickart" title="Rusty Schweickart">\n        Rusty Schweickart\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/G._Scott_Hubbard" title="G. Scott Hubbard">\n        G. Scott Hubbard\n       </a>\n       , Astronautics professor at\n       <a href="/wiki/Stanford_University" title="Stanford University">\n        Stanford University\n       </a>\n       announced plans to build and operate the first\n       <a href="/wiki/Private_spaceflight" title="Private spaceflight">\n        privately\n       </a>\n       -funded deep space mission called\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sentinel_(space_telescope)" title="Sentinel (space telescope)">\n        Sentinel\n       </a>\n       . Their non-profit\n       <a href="/wiki/B612_Foundation" title="B612 Foundation">\n        B612 Foundation\n       </a>\n       will launch an\n       <a href="/wiki/Infrared_astronomy" title="Infrared astronomy">\n        infrared\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Space_telescope" title="Space telescope">\n        space telescope\n       </a>\n       in orbit around the Sun, where from a distance as great as 270,000,000 kilometers (170,000,000\xc2\xa0mi) from Earth, where it would detect and track asteroids and other\n       <a href="/wiki/Near-Earth_object" title="Near-Earth object">\n        near-Earth objects\n       </a>\n       posing threats to the planet.\n      </p>\n      <ul class="gallery mw-gallery-traditional">\n       <li class="gallerybox" style="width:
