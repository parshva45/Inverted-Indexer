href="#cite_note-Mattimore-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Radiation resistance is considered to be an incidental consequence of the organism\xe2\x80\x99s evolutionary adaptation to dehydration, a common physiological stress in nature.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Mattimore_2-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Mattimore-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The chromosomal DNA from desiccated\n       <i>\n        D. radiodurans\n       </i>\n       revealed increased DNA double-strand breaks.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Zahradka_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Zahradka-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       DNA double-strand breaks are repaired principally by a RecA-dependent recombination process that requires the presence of two genome copies.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Zahradka_3-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Zahradka-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       By this process\n       <i>\n        D. radiodurans\n       </i>\n       can survive thousands of double-strand breaks per cell.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Zahradka_3-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Zahradka-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Mycobacterium_smegmatis" title="Mycobacterium smegmatis">\n         Mycobacterium smegmatis\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       <a href="/wiki/Mutant" title="Mutant">\n        mutant\n       </a>\n       strains that are deficient in the ability to repair double-strand breaks by the non-homologous enjoining (NHEJ) pathway are more sensitive to prolonged desiccation during stationary phase than\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wild-type" title="Wild-type">\n        wild-type\n       </a>\n       strains.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid17360246_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-pmid17360246-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       NHEJ appears to be the preferred pathway for repairing double-strand breaks caused by desiccation during stationary phase. NHEJ can repair double-strand breaks even when only one chromosome is present in a cell.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Upon exposure to extreme dryness,\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Bacillus_subtilis" title="Bacillus subtilis">\n         Bacillus subtilis\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       <a href="/wiki/Endospore" title="Endospore">\n        endospores\n       </a>\n       acquire DNA-double strand breaks and DNA-protein crosslinks.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pmid7708386_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-pmid7708386-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Broadcasting">\n        Broadcasting\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Desiccation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Broadcasting">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Broadcast_engineering" title="Broadcast engineering">\n        broadcast engineering\n       </a>\n       , a desiccator may be used to\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pressurize" title="Pressurize">\n        pressurize\n       </a>\n       the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Feedline" title="Feedline">\n        feedline\n       </a>\n       of a high-power transmitter. Because it carries a large amount of energy from the\n       <a href="/wiki/Transmitter" title="Transmitter">\n        transmitter\n       </a>\n       to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Antenna_(radio)" title="Antenna (radio)">\n        antenna\n       </a>\n       , the feedline must have low\n       <a href="/wiki/Dielectric" title="Dielectric">\n        dielectric\n       </a>\n       losses. Because it must also be lightweight so as not to overload the\n       <a href="/wiki/Radio_masts_and_towers" title="Radio masts and towers">\n        radio tower\n       </a>\n       , air is often used as the dielectric. Since\n       <a href="/wiki/Moisture" title="Moisture">\n        moisture\n       </a>\n       can\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Condense" title="Condense">\n        condense\n       </a>\n       in these lines, desiccated air or\n       <a href="/wiki/Nitrogen" title="Nitrogen">\n        nitrogen\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Gas" title="Gas">\n        gas\n       </a>\n       is pumped in. This\n       <a href="/wiki/Pressure" title="Pressure">\n        pressure\n       </a>\n       also keeps\n       <a href="/wiki/Water" title="Water">\n        water\n       </a>\n       or other\n       <a href="/wiki/Moisture" title="Moisture">\n        dampness\n       </a>\n       from coming in the line at any point along its length.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline"
