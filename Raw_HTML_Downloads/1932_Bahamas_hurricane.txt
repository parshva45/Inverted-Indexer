</tr>\n      </table>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <b>\n        1932 Bahamas hurricane\n       </b>\n       , also known as the\n       <b>\n        Great Abaco hurricane of 1932\n       </b>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Neely_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Neely-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       was a large and powerful\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_Category_5_Atlantic_hurricanes" title="List of Category 5 Atlantic hurricanes">\n        Category 5\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone#Hurricane_or_typhoon" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        hurricane\n       </a>\n       that struck\n       <a href="/wiki/The_Bahamas" title="The Bahamas">\n        the Bahamas\n       </a>\n       at peak intensity. The fourth tropical storm and third hurricane in the 1932 Atlantic hurricane season, it was also one of two Category 5 hurricanes in the Atlantic Ocean that year, the other being the\n       <a href="/wiki/1932_Cuba_hurricane" title="1932 Cuba hurricane">\n        1932 Cuba hurricane\n       </a>\n       . The 1932 Bahamas hurricane originated north of the Virgin Islands, became a strong hurricane, and passed over the northern Bahamas before recurving. The storm never made landfall on the\n       <a href="/wiki/Contiguous_United_States" title="Contiguous United States">\n        continental United States\n       </a>\n       , but its effects were felt in the northeast part of the country and in the Bahamas, especially on the\n       <a href="/wiki/Abaco_Islands" title="Abaco Islands">\n        Abaco Islands\n       </a>\n       , where damage was very great.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AOML1932StormFour_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-AOML1932StormFour-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       As of 2016, it is one of only three Category 5 Atlantic hurricanes to make\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">\n        landfall\n       </a>\n       in the Bahamas at that intensity, the others having occurred in\n       <a href="/wiki/1933_Cuba%E2%80%93Brownsville_hurricane" title="1933 Cuba\xe2\x80\x93Brownsville hurricane">\n        1933\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Andrew" title="Hurricane Andrew">\n        1992\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="toc">\n       <div class="toctitle">\n        <h2>\n </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Meteorological_history">\n        Meteorological history\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=1932_Bahamas_hurricane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:1932_Bahamas_hurricane_track.png">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="1236" data-file-width="2000" height="136"  <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:1932_Bahamas_hurricane_track.png" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Map plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the\n         <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">\n          Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale\n         </a>\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The system was first detected just to the north of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Islands" title="Virgin Islands">\n        Virgin Islands\n       </a>\n       as a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_scales#Atlantic_and_East_Pacific" title="Tropical cyclone scales">\n        tropical depression\n       </a>\n       late on August\xc2\xa030.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The storm moved generally west-northwest, passing to the north of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Greater_Antilles" title="Greater Antilles">\n        Greater Antilles\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Grand_Turk_Island" title="Grand Turk Island">\n        Grand Turk\n       </a>\n       on the night of September 2\xe2\x80\x933. It reached\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale#Category_1" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">\n        minimal hurricane\n       </a>\n       intensity as it passed near the\n       <a href="/wiki/Turks_and_Caicos_Islands" title="Turks and Caicos Islands">\n        Turks and Caicos Islands\n       </a>\n       , and began a period of rapid strengthening shortly thereafter.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AOML1932StormFour_2-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-AOML1932StormFour-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       It became a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale#Category_3" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">\n        major hurricane\n       </a>\n       early on September 4 and reached winds of 140 miles per hour (230\xc2\xa0km/h), equivalent to those of a\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_Category_4_Atlantic_hurricanes" title="List of Category 4 Atlantic hurricanes">\n        Category 4\n       </a>\n       hurricane, as early as the afternoon. As late as the evening of September 4, however, no winds of hurricane force were reported to the\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Weather_Service" title="National Weather Service">\n        United States Weather Bureau\n       </a>\n       , the highest wind being 60\xc2\xa0mph (97\xc2\xa0km/h) from a ship 285\xc2\xa0mi (458.66\xc2\xa0km) east of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Miami,_Florida" title="Miami, Florida">\n        Miami, Florida\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Palm1_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Palm1-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The storm passed just to the east of the main islands and\n       <a href="/wiki/Nassau,_Bahamas" title="Nassau, Bahamas">\n        Nassau\n       </a>\n       while continuing to strengthen.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A gradual turn to the northwest and north began soon, and late on September\xc2\xa05 the storm peaked at Category\xc2\xa05 status with estimated\n       <a href="/wiki/Maximum_sustained_wind" title="Maximum sustained wind">\n        maximum sustained winds\n       </a>\n       of around 160\xc2\xa0mph (260\xc2\xa0km/h) at this time. Maintaining strength, the storm passed over\n       <a href="/wiki/Abaco_Islands" title="Abaco Islands">\n        Great Abaco\n       </a>\n       on September\xc2\xa05 and gradually began to curve northeast away from the mainland United States. It continued northeast while weakening in intensity, delivering\n       <a href="/wiki/Swell_(ocean)" title="Swell (ocean)">\n        sea swells\n       </a>\n       to the northeastern United States and winds of 56\xc2\xa0mph (90\xc2\xa0km/h) to\n       <a href="/wiki/Nantucket,_Massachusetts" title="Nantucket, Massachusetts">\n        Nantucket\n       </a>\n       as the storm bypassed\n       <a href="/wiki/New_England" title="New England">\n        New England\n       </a>\n       . The storm became\n       <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">\n        extratropical\n       </a>\n       on September\xc2\xa09, crossed south of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Avalon_Peninsula" title="Avalon Peninsula">\n        Avalon Peninsula\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_and_Labrador" title="Newfoundland and Labrador">\n        Newfoundland and Labrador\n       </a>\n       , on September 11, and eventually passed near the\n       <a href="/wiki/Sn%C3%A6fellsnes" title="Sn\xc3\xa6fellsnes">\n        Sn\xc3\xa6fellsnes\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">\n        Iceland\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jan_Mayen_Island" title="Jan Mayen Island">\n        Jan Mayen Island\n       </a>\n       . It dissipated on September\xc2\xa017.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_3-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div style="clear:both;">\n      </div>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">\n        Preparations\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=1932_Bahamas_hurricane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Initially, storm warnings in the United States were placed at 15\n       <a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">\n        UTC\n       </a>\n       on September 5 from\n       <a href="/wiki/Daytona_Beach,_Florida" title="Daytona Beach, Florida">\n        Daytona Beach, Florida\n       </a>\n       , to\n       <a href="/wiki/Punta_Gorda,_Florida" title="Punta Gorda, Florida">\n        Punta Gorda, Florida\n       </a>\n       . Due to the warnings, residents in the affected areas began boarding up windows and completing other preparations.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PalmBeach_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-PalmBeach-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       As the cyclone later appeared to miss South Florida, the first warnings were cancelled and new storm warnings issued between Daytona Beach and\n       <a href="/wiki/Wilmington,_North_Carolina" title="Wilmington, North Carolina">\n        Wilmington, North Carolina\n       </a>\n       . By early September 7, warnings were extended up the\n       <a href="/wiki/East_Coast_of_the_United_States" title="East Coast of the United States">\n        Eastern Seaboard\n       </a>\n       to\n       <a href="/wiki/Eastport,_Maine" title="Eastport, Maine">\n        Eastport, Maine\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AOML1932StormFour_2-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-AOML1932StormFour-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div style="clear:both;">\n      </div>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">\n        Impact\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=1932_Bahamas_hurricane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Impact">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       16 people were reported killed, along with an additional 300 injured.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AOML1932StormFour_2-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-AOML1932StormFour-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       This entire toll occurred in the Bahamas, notably on and around Abaco Island; damage estimates in dollars, however, were not released.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AOML1932StormFour_2-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-AOML1932StormFour-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Despite the large size\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Metadata_6-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Metadata-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and great intensity of the hurricane, ample warnings prevented loss of life and commerce at sea.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Oceans_7-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Oceans-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div style="clear:both;">\n      </div>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Bahamas">\n        Bahamas\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=1932_Bahamas_hurricane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Bahamas">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       As the storm passed north of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cat_Island_(Bahamas)" title="Cat Island (Bahamas)">\n        Cat Island\n       </a>\n       , it caused a pressure of 29.47\xc2\xa0inHg (998.0\xc2\xa0mb) and a wind of 20\xc2\xa0mph (32\xc2\xa0km/h) from the north.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Cat_8-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Cat-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Although the cyclone passed within 65\xc2\xa0mi (105\xc2\xa0km) of the Bahamian capital\n       <a href="/wiki/Nassau,_Bahamas" title="Nassau, Bahamas">\n        Nassau\n       </a>\n       , the storm only caused winds up to 50\xc2\xa0mph (80\xc2\xa0km/h) and no significant damage there.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Safe_9-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Safe-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The storm was very destructive on Abaco Island, where the reported barometric pressure was below 27.50\xc2\xa0inHg (931.3\xc2\xa0mb). At\n       <a href="/wiki/Hope_Town" title="Hope Town">\n        Hope Town\n       </a>\n       , strong winds shifting from northeast to southeast destroyed 83 homes and severely damaged 63. Food supplies ran low and salt contaminated the drinking supply. All public buildings along with the radio station were destroyed. The lowest pressure at Hope Town was 27.20\xc2\xa0inHg (921.1\xc2\xa0mb) around 15 UTC on September 5. At nearby\n       <a href="/wiki/Marsh_Harbour" title="Marsh Harbour">\n        Marsh Harbour\n       </a>\n       , northeast winds shifted to southwest at 20 UTC and calm conditions occurred for 15 minutes as the eye passed. The barometer dropped as low as 27.60\xc2\xa0inHg (934.6\xc2\xa0mb). 12 homes were destroyed and most of the remainder severely damaged. On\n       <a href="/wiki/Green_Turtle_Cay" title="Green Turtle Cay">\n        Green Turtle Cay\n       </a>\n       , near Abaco Island, north and northwest winds coincided with the worst conditions; a large\n       <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">\n        storm surge\n       </a>\n       inundated the island, all buildings and the graveyard were destroyed, and at least six people died with 25 injuries reported. At nearby\n       <a href="/wiki/Coopers_Town" title="Coopers Town">\n        Coopers Town\n       </a>\n       , just six homes survived the storm. Only four homes were still standing on\n       <a href="/wiki/Great_Guana_Cay" title="Great Guana Cay">\n        Great Guana Cay\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hurt_10-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Hurt-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On Green Turtle Cay, two large brick churches with stone walls 3\xc2\xa0ft (0.91\xc2\xa0m) thick were destroyed by the storm and winds were estimated by one resident to have exceeded 200\xc2\xa0mph (320\xc2\xa0km/h); some of the stone blocks from the churches were reportedly carried 0.50\xc2\xa0mi (0.80\xc2\xa0km). Newspaper reports and photos helped to establish estimated prevailing winds on Green Turtle Cay that possibly exceeded 150\xc2\xa0mph (240\xc2\xa0km/h) during the hurricane.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AOML1932StormFour_2-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-AOML1932StormFour-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       After the storm passed Abaco on September 6 and 7, several vessels caught in the storm recorded winds of\n       <a href="/wiki/Beaufort_scale" title="Beaufort scale">\n        Force 12\n       </a>\n       (\n       <a href="/wiki/Beaufort_scale" title="Beaufort scale">\n        Beaufort Wind Scale\n       </a>\n       ) and low barometric pressures; the\n       <a href="/wiki/Steamship" title="Steamship">\n        steamship\n       </a>\n       <i>\n        Yankee Arrow\n       </i>\n       recorded a pressure of 27.65\xc2\xa0inHg (936.3\xc2\xa0mb) on September 7, while the nearby steamship\n       <i>\n        Deer Lodge\n       </i>\n       reported a lower pressure of 27.58\xc2\xa0inHg (934.0\xc2\xa0mb).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AOML1932StormFour_2-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-AOML1932StormFour-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div style="clear:both;">\n      </div>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="United_States">\n        United States\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=1932_Bahamas_hurricane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: United States">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Although storm warnings were posted for the Florida and eastern United States coastline, the storm\'s recurvature prevented a landfall, leaving the main effects as heavy coastal swells and high winds. Gale-force winds affected the coastline, however.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Metadata_6-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Metadata-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       As the cyclone passed closest to New England, Nantucket recorded the highest winds at 56\xc2\xa0mph (90\xc2\xa0km/h) as the storm remained offshore, although it still contained hurricane-force winds.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AOML1932StormFour_2-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-AOML1932StormFour-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Winds peaked at 44\xc2\xa0mph (71\xc2\xa0km/h) at\n       <a href="/wiki/Cape_Hatteras" title="Cape Hatteras">\n        Cape Hatteras\n       </a>\n       . Strong gales on the evening of September 7 caused the\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Cape_Lookout_Lightship&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Cape Lookout Lightship (page does not exist)">\n        Cape Lookout Lightship\n       </a>\n       to be blown loose from her anchorage. Off the coast at the same time, the crew of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Munson_Line" title="Munson Line">\n        Munson\n       </a>\n       Steamer\n       <i>\n        Munloyal\n       </i>\n       , then believed to be 350\xc2\xa0mi (560\xc2\xa0km) southeast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Frying_Pan_Shoals" title="Frying Pan Shoals">\n        Frying Pan Shoals\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">\n        North Carolina\n       </a>\n       , reported that her position was unknown and her rudder blown away.\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">\n        United States Coast Guard\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Cutter_(boat)" title="Cutter (boat)">\n        cutters\n       </a>\n       from\n       <a href="/wiki/Fort_Lauderdale,_Florida" title="Fort Lauderdale, Florida">\n        Fort Lauderdale, Florida\n       </a>\n       , were dispatched to her assistance.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Munloyal_11-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Munloyal-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Large trees and signs fell down in\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Brooklyn,_New_York" title="Brooklyn, New York">\n        Brooklyn, New York\n       </a>\n       , due to high winds. Cold temperatures followed in the wake of the hurricane.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Northern_12-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Northern-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div style="clear:both;">\n      </div>\n      <h3>\n       <span id="Newfoundland,_Iceland,_and_Jan_Mayen_Island">\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Newfoundland.2C_Iceland.2C_and_Jan_Mayen_Island">\n        Newfoundland, Iceland, and Jan Mayen Island\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=1932_Bahamas_hurricane&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Newfoundland, Iceland, and Jan Mayen Island">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Although the storm was extratropical by the time it bypassed\n       <a href="/wiki/Newfoundland_(island)" title="Newfoundland (island)">\n        Newfoundland\n       </a>\n       and was near\n       <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">\n        Iceland\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jan_Mayen_Island" title="Jan Mayen Island">\n        Jan Mayen Island\n       </a>\n       , it was still intense: observed barometric pressures were at or below 29\xc2\xa0inHg (982.1\xc2\xa0mb).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AOML1932StormFour_2-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-AOML1932StormFour-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div style="clear:both;">\n      </div>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline"
