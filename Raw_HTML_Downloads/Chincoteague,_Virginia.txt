<p>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Geography">\n        Geography\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n class="external text" href="//tools.wmflabs.org/geohack/geohack.php?pagename=Chincoteague,_Virginia&amp;params=37_56_5_N_75_22_4_W_type:city">\n         <span class="geo-default">\n          <span \xef\xbb\xbf\n         </span>\n         <span class="geo-nondefault">\n          <span class="geo-dec" title="Maps, aerial photos, and other data for this location">\n           37.93472\xc2\xb0N class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Chincoteague,_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main article:\n       <a href="/wiki/History_of_Chincoteague,_Virginia" title="History of Chincoteague, Virginia">\n        History of Chincoteague, Virginia\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The year of 1650 marks the first land grant issued on Chincoteague Island, for 1,500 acres (6.1\xc2\xa0km\n       <sup>\n        2\n       </sup>\n       ). Daniel Jenifer became the first English landowner.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NG_6-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NG-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In 1671, settlers came to the island, and by 1672 there were large farms by Bishops, Bowdens, Jesters and Tarrs. By 1838, there were 36 houses on the island, and in 1861 the island voted 132-2 not to secede from the\n       <a href="/wiki/Union_(American_Civil_War)" title="Union (American Civil War)">\n        Union\n       </a>\n       and against slavery. The town saw minor action in the war via the\n       <a href="/wiki/Battle_of_Cockle_Creek" title="Battle of Cockle Creek">\n        Battle of Cockle Creek\n       </a>\n       fought in the bay in 1861.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The Native American name for the island is\n       <i>\n        Gingoteague\n       </i>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NG_6-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NG-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and the name of the town "Chincoteague" first appeared in the\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/United_States_Board_on_Geographic_Names#Publications" title="United States Board on Geographic Names">\n         Decisions of the United States Board on Geographical Names\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       in 1943.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In 1962, a major\n       <a href="/wiki/Nor%27easter" title="Nor\'easter">\n        nor\'easter\n       </a>\n       winter storm, the\n       <a href="/wiki/Ash_Wednesday_Storm_of_1962" title="Ash Wednesday Storm of 1962">\n        Ash Wednesday Storm\n       </a>\n       , struck the coast. The town was completely underwater, and went for days without electricity. The storm destroyed almost all structures on Assateague Island, where development was just beginning. Following this, most of the island was preserved from development as\n       <a href="/wiki/Assateague_Island_National_Seashore" title="Assateague Island National Seashore">\n        Assateague Island National Seashore\n       </a>\n       in 1965.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Assateague_Lighthouse" title="Assateague Lighthouse">\n        Assateague Lighthouse\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Captain_Timothy_Hill_House" title="Captain Timothy Hill House">\n        Captain Timothy Hill House\n       </a>\n       are listed on the\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Register_of_Historic_Places" title="National Register of Historic Places">\n        National Register of Historic Places\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nris_8-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nris-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nris_8-1">\n        <a </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Chincoteague_Ponies">\n        Chincoteague Ponies\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Chincoteague,_Virginia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Chincoteague Ponies">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main article:\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chincoteague_pony" title="Chincoteague pony">\n        Chincoteague pony\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Legend has it that the\n       <a href="/wiki/Feral_horse" title="Feral horse">\n        feral ponies\n       </a>\n       on Assateague are descendants of survivors of a Spanish\n       <a href="/wiki/Galleon" title="Galleon">\n        galleon\n       </a>\n       that sank on its way to\n       <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">\n        Spain\n       </a>\n       during a storm in 1750 off the east coast, but the likelihood is that they are actually descended from domesticated stock, brought to the island by Eastern Shore farmers in the 17th century to avoid fencing requirements and taxation.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In the\n       <a href="/wiki/Pony_Penning" title="Pony Penning">\n        Pony Penning\n       </a>\n       , which has been held annually since 1925, horses swim across the shallow water between the islands. If any animal is too small or weak to make the swim, it is placed on a barge and ferried over. All the horses are herded into large pens after running through the middle of town and down Main Street. Pony Penning takes place on the last consecutive Wednesday and Thursday in July. The actual swim occurs on Wednesday, the\n       <a href="/wiki/Chincoteague_Fire_Department" title="Chincoteague Fire Department">\n        Chincoteague Volunteer Fire Company\n       </a>\n       auctions that year\'s foals on Thursday, and on Friday the remaining ponies swim back to Assateague.\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner"
