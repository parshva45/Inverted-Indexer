The expedition marked the first time a six-member crew inhabited the station. Because each\n       <a href="/wiki/Soyuz-TMA" title="Soyuz-TMA">\n        Soyuz-TMA\n       </a>\n       spacecraft could hold only three people, two separate launches were necessary:\n       <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-14" title="Soyuz TMA-14">\n        Soyuz TMA-14\n       </a>\n       launched on 26 March 2009, and\n       <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-15" title="Soyuz TMA-15">\n        Soyuz TMA-15\n       </a>\n       followed on 27 May 2009.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-launch_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-launch-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Soyuz TMA-15 launched from\n       <a href="/wiki/Baikonur_Cosmodrome" title="Baikonur Cosmodrome">\n        Baikonur Cosmodrome\n       </a>\n       at 10:34 UTC on 27 May 2009.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-launch_1-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-launch-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The vehicle docked with the station on 29 May 2009, officially changing the Soyuz TMA-14 crew from\n       <a href="/wiki/Expedition_19" title="Expedition 19">\n        Expedition 19\n       </a>\n       to Expedition 20.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dock_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-dock-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <a href="/wiki/Gennady_Padalka" title="Gennady Padalka">\n        Gennady Padalka\n       </a>\n       was the first commander of a six-member station crew, and the first commander of two consecutive expeditions (Expedition 19 and 20).\n       <a href="/wiki/Nicole_Stott" title="Nicole Stott">\n        Nicole Stott\n       </a>\n       was the final expedition astronaut to be launched on the shuttle.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       During the expedition, Koichi Wakata performed a special experiment wherein he did not change his underpants for one month, in order to test a specially-designed underwear without washing or changing; he reportedly did not develop body odor due to the effects of the special garment.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The station would not be permanently occupied by six crew members all year. For example, when the Expedition 20 crew (\n       <a href="/wiki/Roman_Romanenko" title="Roman Romanenko">\n        Roman Romanenko\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Frank_De_Winne" title="Frank De Winne">\n        Frank De Winne\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Robert_Thirsk" title="Robert Thirsk">\n        Bob Thirsk\n       </a>\n       ) returned to Earth in November 2009, for a period of about two weeks only two crew members (\n       <a href="/wiki/Jeffrey_Williams_(astronaut)" title="Jeffrey Williams (astronaut)">\n        Jeff Williams\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Maksim_Surayev" title="Maksim Surayev">\n        Max Surayev\n       </a>\n       ) were aboard. This increased to five in early December, when\n       <a href="/wiki/Oleg_Kotov" title="Oleg Kotov">\n        Oleg Kotov\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Timothy_Creamer" title="Timothy Creamer">\n        Timothy Creamer\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Soichi_Noguchi" title="Soichi Noguchi">\n        Soichi Noguchi\n       </a>\n       arrived on\n       <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-17" title="Soyuz TMA-17">\n        Soyuz TMA-17\n       </a>\n       . It decreased to three when Williams and Surayev departed in March 2010, and finally returned to six in April 2010 with the arrival of\n       <a href="/wiki/Soyuz_TMA-18" title="Soyuz TMA-18">\n
