The system formed in the southern Caribbean Sea and affected several countries in South America before crossing\n       <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">\n        Nicaragua\n       </a>\n       and entering the\n       <a href="/wiki/1996_Pacific_hurricane_season" title="1996 Pacific hurricane season">\n        eastern Pacific\n       </a>\n       where it was renamed\n       <i>\n        Douglas\n       </i>\n       . elongated area of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Low_pressure_area" title="Low pressure area">\n        low pressure\n       </a>\n       that emerged into the Atlantic from the west coast of Africa on July\xc2\xa017. For several days, the wave moved westward without any organization, although an\n       <a href="/wiki/Anticyclone" title="Anticyclone">\n        anticyclone\n       </a>\n       aloft provided conditions favorable for\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclogenesis" title="Tropical cyclogenesis">\n        development\n       </a>\n       . Based on surface and satellite data, it is estimated the system developed into Tropical Depression Three at 1800\n       <a href="/wiki/Coordinated_Universal_Time" title="Coordinated Universal Time">\n        UTC\n       </a>\n       on July\xc2\xa024 near\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Isla_Margarita" title="Isla Margarita">\n        Isla Margarita\n       </a>\n       , off the north coast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Venezuela" title="Venezuela">\n        Venezuela\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CTCR_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CTCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Operationally, the\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">\n        National Hurricane Center\n       </a>\n       (NHC) did not consider it as a tropical depression until 18\xc2\xa0hours later.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CTCR_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CTCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-unisys_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-unisys-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       With an unusually strong\n       <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">\n        high pressure area\n       </a>\n       located over\n       <a href="/wiki/The_Bahamas" title="The Bahamas">\n        The Bahamas\n       </a>\n       , the tropical depression moved westward through the southern Caribbean near the northern coast of South America. Around 1200\xc2\xa0UTC on July\xc2\xa025, it struck the island of\n       <a href="/wiki/Cura%C3%A7ao" title="Cura\xc3\xa7ao">\n        Cura\xc3\xa7ao\n       </a>\n       , which reported sustained winds of 45\xc2\xa0mph (75\xc2\xa0km/h). The observation indicated the depression attained tropical storm status,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CTCR_1-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CTCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       although operationally the depression wasn\'t upgraded until the next day, at which point the NHC named the storm Cesar.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-unisys_2-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-unisys-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       After crossing Cura\xc3\xa7ao, the storm moved near or over\n       <a href="/wiki/Guajira_Peninsula" title="Guajira Peninsula">\n        Guajira Peninsula\n       </a>\n       in extreme northern\n       <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">\n        Colombia\n       </a>\n       . Its proximity to South America prevented significant strengthening, until late on July\xc2\xa026 when the storm reached the open waters of the southwest Caribbean Sea.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CTCR_1-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CTCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Cesar_28_july_1996_1217Z.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="880" data-file-width="880" height="220" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/04/Hurricane_Cesar_28_july_1996_1217Z.jpg/220px-Hurricane_Cesar_28_july_1996_1217Z.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/04/Hurricane_Cesar_28_july_1996_1217Z.jpg/330px-Hurricane_Cesar_28_july_1996_1217Z.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/04/Hurricane_Cesar_28_july_1996_1217Z.jpg/440px-Hurricane_Cesar_28_july_1996_1217Z.jpg 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Cesar_28_july_1996_1217Z.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Cesar making landfall in\n         <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">\n          Nicaragua\n         </a>\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       On July\xc2\xa027, Cesar attained hurricane status about halfway between Nicaragua and Colombia. Later that day, the hurricane passed over\n       <a href="/wiki/San_Andr%C3%A9s_(island)" title="San Andr\xc3\xa9s (island)">\n        San Andr\xc3\xa9s\n       </a>\n       island, which is part of a small Colombian archipelago off the east coast of Nicaragua. As Cesar approached Central America, a 17\xc2\xa0mile (19\xc2\xa0km)\n       <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">\n        eye\n       </a>\n       formed, surrounded by deep convection in the form of an eyewall. At about 0400\xc2\xa0UTC on July\xc2\xa028, Hurricane Cesar made\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">\n        landfall\n       </a>\n       just north of\n       <a href="/wiki/Bluefields" title="Bluefields">\n        Bluefields\n       </a>\n       , Nicaragua with winds of 75\xc2\xa0mph (140\xc2\xa0km/h). It moved quickly west-northward through the country, weakening to tropical storm status and emerging into the eastern Pacific Ocean by July\xc2\xa029.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CTCR_1-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CTCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       This made Cesar the most recent tropical cyclone to traverse from the Atlantic to east Pacific basin until Otto achieved the same feat in 2016.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NOAA_1_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NOAA_1-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In addition, following the dissipation of Cesar\xe2\x80\x93Douglas there was a policy change which determined that future storms would retain their original name upon crossing into another basin.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Bay_News_9_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Bay_News_9-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Upon reaching the Pacific, the system was\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_naming" title="Tropical cyclone naming">\n        renamed\n       </a>\n       Tropical Depression Seven-E,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tdpa_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tdpa-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       but in a post-analysis it was determined the cyclone maintained tropical storm status while crossing Central America.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CTCR_1-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CTCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Once its status as a tropical storm was confirmed, it was renamed Tropical Storm Douglas. At the time, the agreement through the\n       <a href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organization" title="World Meteorological Organization">\n        World Meteorological Organization\n       </a>\n       was for storms to be renamed if they cross from the Atlantic to the Pacific.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dtcr_6-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-dtcr-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       As the storm moved westward, it quickly intensified, with an eye-like feature developing by 0900\xc2\xa0UTC on July\xc2\xa029.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Shortly thereafter, Douglas attained hurricane status about 115\xc2\xa0mi (185\xc2\xa0km) southwest of the Guatemala/Mexico border.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dtcr_6-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-dtcr-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Around that time,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_prediction_model" title="Tropical cyclone prediction model">\n        tropical cyclone prediction models\n       </a>\n       anticipated two scenarios for the future of Douglas; one was a northwest track to make landfall near\n       <a href="/wiki/Acapulco" title="Acapulco">\n        Acapulco\n       </a>\n       , and the other was a continued west-northwest track while remaining offshore.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Hurricane Douglas ultimately took the latter track.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dtcr_6-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-dtcr-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       By late on July\xc2\xa029, the eye of the hurricane was well-defined on Mexican radar, and with favorable upper-level\n       <a href="/wiki/Outflow_(meteorology)" title="Outflow (meteorology)">\n        outflow\n       </a>\n       , warm\n       <a href="/wiki/Sea_surface_temperature" title="Sea surface temperature">\n        sea surface temperatures\n       </a>\n       , and a climatologically favorable region for intense hurricanes, the NHC forecast Douglas to strengthen to winds of 115\xc2\xa0mph (185\xc2\xa0km/h).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The next day, its structure became atypical of a strengthening hurricane, and the eye was briefly not seen on satellite imagery.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       On July\xc2\xa031, Douglas became much better organized as it turned more west-northwestward,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and it attained\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_scales" title="Tropical cyclone scales">\n        major hurricane status\n       </a>\n       , or a Category\xc2\xa03 on the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_hurricane_wind_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson hurricane wind scale">\n        Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale\n       </a>\n       , about 205\xc2\xa0miles (330\xc2\xa0km) southwest of\n       <a href="/wiki/Manzanillo,_Colima" title="Manzanillo, Colima">\n        Manzanillo\n       </a>\n       . By early on August\xc2\xa01, Douglas reached peak winds of 130\xc2\xa0mph (215\xc2\xa0km/h), a Category\xc2\xa04 on the Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale. Later that day, the hurricane attained its lowest pressure of 946\n       <a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">\n        mbar\n       </a>\n       about 275\xc2\xa0miles (440\xc2\xa0km) south of the southern tip of the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Baja_California_peninsula" title="Baja California peninsula">\n        Baja California peninsula\n       </a>\n       . Douglas maintained peak intensity for 36\xc2\xa0hours,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dtcr_6-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-dtcr-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       until August\xc2\xa02 when the eye became less-organized as the overall convection began to weaken.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Weakening continued due to cooler waters as Douglas turned to the west, and on August\xc2\xa03 the hurricane deteriorated to tropical storm status.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dtcr_6-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-dtcr-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       As a tropical storm, there was minimal deep convection, although the center remained very well-defined.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On August\xc2\xa05, Douglas weakened to tropical depression status, and by the next day could no longer be classified as a tropical cyclone. The remnant circulation continued westward for several days.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dtcr_6-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-dtcr-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">\n        Preparations\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Cesar%E2%80%93Douglas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Prior to Cesar\'s arrival in Venezuela, a tropical storm warning was issued for areas west of\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=La_Vela_de_Coro&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="La Vela de Coro (page does not exist)">\n        La Vela de Coro\n       </a>\n       to the border with Colombia; the warning was discontinued later that day.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CTCR_1-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CTCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The government of Colombia issued a tropical storm warning on July\xc2\xa025 from the border with Venezuela to\n       <a href="/wiki/Barranquilla" title="Barranquilla">\n        Barranquilla\n       </a>\n       as well as the islands of\n       <a href="/wiki/Aruba" title="Aruba">\n        Aruba\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Cura%C3%A7ao" title="Cura\xc3\xa7ao">\n        Cura\xc3\xa7ao\n       </a>\n       . These warnings were discontinued later that day after the storm\'s passage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CTCR_1-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CTCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       As Cesar approached Central America, hurricane warnings were posted in Nicaragua 31 hours before landfall, leaving ample time to prepare for the hurricane. With\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Joan%E2%80%93Miriam" title="Hurricane Joan\xe2\x80\x93Miriam">\n        Hurricane Joan\n       </a>\n       occurring only 8 years prior, 10,724 people were evacuated before and during the hurricane to take refuge at special camps.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Red_Alert_14-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Red_Alert-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       On July\xc2\xa029, shortly after Cesar emerged into the Pacific Ocean and was reclassified Tropical Depression Seven-E, the government of Mexico issued a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">\n        tropical storm watch\n       </a>\n       from\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Puerto_Madero,_Chiapas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Puerto Madero, Chiapas (page does not exist)">\n        Puerto Madero\n       </a>\n       to\n       <a href="/wiki/Acapulco" title="Acapulco">\n        Acapulco\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tdpa_5-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tdpa-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       About 12\xc2\xa0hours later, after the depression intensified into Tropical Storm Douglas, the Mexican government canceled the watch and issued a new tropical storm warning from\n       <a href="/wiki/Salina_Cruz" title="Salina Cruz">\n        Salina Cruz\n       </a>\n       to Acapulco; this was due to the large extent of tropical storm force winds associated with Douglas and its proximity to the south coast of Mexico. Another tropical storm watch was briefly issued on July\xc2\xa030 from Acapulco to\n       <a href="/wiki/Manzanillo,_Colima" title="Manzanillo, Colima">\n        Manzanillo\n       </a>\n       .\n along its path through the southern\n       <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">\n        Caribbean Sea\n       </a>\n       and Central America. Damage was moderate to extreme due to mudslides and flooding, and at least 113 people were killed.\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Lesser_Antilles_and_South_America">\n        Lesser Antilles and South America\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Cesar%E2%80%93Douglas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Lesser Antilles and South America">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       The precursor tropical wave to Cesar produced rains and gusty winds through a large portion of the Lesser Antilles.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CTCR_1-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CTCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In Venezuela, heavy rains from the storm triggered flooding and landslides that killed at least five people.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-VenezuelaDeaths_22-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-VenezuelaDeaths-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In the capital city of\n       <a href="/wiki/Caracas" title="Caracas">\n        Caracas\n       </a>\n       , 45 people were left homeless as a result of the storm.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-23">\n        <a href="#cite_note-23">\n         [23]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Although the storm passed directly over the region, the\n       <a href="/wiki/ABC_islands_(Lesser_Antilles)" title="ABC islands (Lesser Antilles)">\n        ABC islands\n       </a>\n       off the coast of Colombia and Venezuela received little rainfall, peaking at 0.15\xc2\xa0in (3.8\xc2\xa0mm) on Cura\xc3\xa7ao. Peak gusts were also measured at 60\xc2\xa0mph (95\xc2\xa0km/h) on the island.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CTCR_1-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CTCR-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The winds caused minor damage to roofs and trees across all three islands, as well as rough surf that drowned one person in Cura\xc3\xa7ao.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Curacao_18-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Curacao-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       As a tropical storm, Cesar struck the northern coastline of\n       <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">\n        Colombia\n       </a>\n       , bringing heavy rains and gusty winds. At least three people were killed in storm related incidents,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Colombia1_15-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Colombia1-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       two of which occurred when an avalanche buried a house in\n       <a href="/wiki/Pueblo_Bello" title="Pueblo Bello">\n        Pueblo Bello\n       </a>\n       in the northern part of the country.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">\n        <a href="#cite_note-24">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Cesar brought torrential rains to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Archipelago_of_San_Andr%C3%A9s,_Providencia_and_Santa_Catalina" title="Archipelago of San Andr\xc3\xa9s, Providencia and Santa Catalina">\n        Archipelago of San Andr\xc3\xa9s, Providencia and Santa Catalina\n       </a>\n       offshore eastern\n       <a href="/wiki/Nicaragua" title="Nicaragua">\n        Nicaragua\n       </a>\n       . Eleven people were killed across the archipelago, including eight children who died in a landslide.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Colombia2_16-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Colombia2-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Across the islands, 60 homes lost their roofs and numerous trees were felled due to high winds. The local governor stated that losses from Cesar reached 800\xc2\xa0million\n       <a href="/wiki/Colombian_peso" title="Colombian peso">\n        COP\n       </a>\n       ($440,00\xc2\xa0USD).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Colombia3_17-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Colombia3-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Nicaragua">\n        Nicaragua\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Cesar%E2%80%93Douglas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Nicaragua">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Torrential rainfall was the immediate effect of Cesar, peaking at 10.7\xc2\xa0inches (271\xc2\xa0mm) at\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bluefields,_Nicaragua" title="Bluefields, Nicaragua">\n        Bluefields, Nicaragua\n       </a>\n       with many other locations reporting over 6\xc2\xa0inches (150\xc2\xa0mm). The intense precipitation led to widespread mudslides and overflown rivers across the mountainous country. The most affected region was\n       <a href="/wiki/Lake_Managua" title="Lake Managua">\n        Lake Managua\n       </a>\n       where the water level was approaching dangerous levels.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Emergency_25-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Emergency-25">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The storm wrought extensive damage throughout the country, leaving roughly $50.5\xc2\xa0million in damage behind. Large portions of the country\'s crops were affected, resulting in a food shortage following the hurricane. According to Nicaraguan officials, more than 2,500 homes, 39 bridges and 40\xc2\xa0km (25\xc2\xa0mi) of road were destroyed by Cesar. In all, the storm killed 42 people and left an estimated 100,000 homeless.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26">\n        <a href="#cite_note-26">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Costa_Rica">\n        Costa Rica\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Cesar%E2%80%93Douglas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Costa Rica">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Like Nicaragua,\n       <a href="/wiki/Costa_Rica" title="Costa Rica">\n        Costa Rica\n       </a>\n       received heavy rainfall from Cesar, leading to mudslides and widespread flooding. River flooding damaged 51 houses and washed away 213 more; 72 bridges were also destroyed. The road network was significantly damaged.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28dead_27-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-28dead-27">\n         [27]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Costa Rica requested international aid subsequent to the storm. Across the country, at least 39 people were killed and damage amounted to $151\xc2\xa0million. Additionally, 29 people were listed as missing.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ECLAC_19-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ECLAC-19">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="El_Salvador">\n        El Salvador\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Cesar%E2%80%93Douglas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: El Salvador">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       As Cesar continued westward, it produced heavy flooding and mudslides in western\n       <a href="/wiki/El_Salvador" title="El Salvador">\n        El Salvador\n       </a>\n       , killing 9 in the community of\n       <a href="/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Cecilio_del_Valle" title="Jos\xc3\xa9 Cecilio del Valle">\n        Jos\xc3\xa9 Cecilio del Valle\n       </a>\n       . Four others drowned in other parts of the country.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28dead_27-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-28dead-27">\n         [27]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Mexico">\n        Mexico\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Cesar%E2%80%93Douglas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Mexico">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Hurricane Douglas brought up to 6\xc2\xa0inches (150\xc2\xa0mm) of rain on the south coast of Mexico and resulted in a 4\xc2\xa0foot (1.2\xc2\xa0m) storm surge, but no deaths were reported.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-35dead_28-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-35dead-28">\n         [28]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">\n        Aftermath\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Cesar%E2%80%93Douglas&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Aftermath">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       See also:\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricane names">\n        List of retired Atlantic hurricane names\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The name\n       <b>\n        Cesar\n       </b>\n       was retired in spring of 1997, and will not be used again in the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlantic_basin" title="Atlantic basin">\n        Atlantic basin\n       </a>\n       . It was replaced with Cristobal in the\n       <a href="/wiki/2002_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2002 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        2002 season\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Because it caused no major effects on land, the name Douglas was not retired. It was reused in the\n       <a href="/wiki/2002_Pacific_hurricane_season" title="2002 Pacific hurricane season">\n        2002\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/2008_Pacific_hurricane_season" title="2008 Pacific hurricane season">\n        2008\n       </a>\n       , and the\n       <a href="/wiki/2014_Pacific_hurricane_season" title="2014 Pacific hurricane season">\n        2014\n       </a>\n       seasons.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="See_also">\n        See also\n       </span>\n       <span
