A fairly long-lived July\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_tropical_cyclone" title="North Atlantic tropical cyclone">\n        Atlantic hurricane\n       </a>\n       , Claudette began as a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">\n        tropical wave\n       </a>\n       in the eastern Caribbean. It moved quickly westward, brushing past the\n       <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula" title="Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula">\n        Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula\n       </a>\n       before moving northwestward through the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">\n        Gulf of Mexico\n       </a>\n       . Claudette remained a tropical storm until just before making landfall in\n       <a href="/wiki/Port_O%27Connor,_Texas" title="Port O\'Connor, Texas">\n        Port O\'Connor, Texas\n       </a>\n       , when it quickly strengthened to a strong Category\xc2\xa01 hurricane on the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">\n        Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale\n       </a>\n       . Forecasting of its path and intensity was uncertain throughout its lifetime, resulting in widespread and often unnecessary preparations along its path.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Claudette was the first hurricane to make landfall in July in the United States since\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Danny_(1997)" title="Hurricane Danny (1997)">\n        Hurricane Danny\n       </a>\n       in the\n       <a href="/wiki/1997_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1997 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1997 season\n       </a>\n       . The hurricane caused one death and moderate damage in\n       <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">\n        Texas\n       </a>\n       , mostly from strong winds, as well as extensive beach erosion. Because of the damage, President\n       <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">\n        George W. Bush\n       </a>\n       declared portions of South Texas a Federal Disaster Area, allowing the affected citizens to apply for aid. Claudette also caused significant rainfall and minor damage in the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mexican_state" title="Mexican state">\n        Mexican state\n       </a>\n       of\n       <a href="/wiki/Quintana_Roo" title="Quintana Roo">\n        Quintana Roo\n       </a>\n       as a tropical storm, as well as minor damage on\n       <a href="/wiki/Saint_Lucia" title="Saint Lucia">\n        Saint Lucia\n       </a>\n       before developing into a tropical cyclone.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="toc">\n       <div class="toctitle">\n  </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       A\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">\n        tropical wave\n       </a>\n       moved off the coast of Africa on July 1. It steadily organized as it moved westward, and resembled a tropical depression by July 7. However, surface reports and\n       <a href="/wiki/Reconnaissance_aircraft" title="Reconnaissance aircraft">\n        reconnaissance aircraft\n       </a>\n       indicated the system did not have tropical characteristics, and remained simply a low-pressure system. The wave produced tropical storm-force winds as it moved through the\n       <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">\n        Lesser Antilles\n       </a>\n       on July 8, but did not acquire tropical characteristics until it reached the eastern Caribbean. Because it had tropical storm-force winds, the system was upgraded immediately to Tropical Storm Claudette after it developed a low-level circulation later on July 8.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       By early on July 9, while south of\n       <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">\n        Puerto Rico\n       </a>\n       , Claudette quickly strengthened over the warm waters of the Caribbean, and its strongest winds were just shy of the threshold for hurricane intensity. Possibly due to its fast forward motion, the storm became disorganized and weakened. Conditions briefly became more favorable over the western Caribbean, and Claudette reached hurricane status for six hours on July 10. Soon after, the storm encountered unfavorable upper-level winds and weakened back into a tropical storm. In response to a break in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_hurricane#Steering_factors" title="Atlantic hurricane">\n        subtropical ridge\n       </a>\n       \xe2\x80\x94a high-pressure ridge that often prevents tropical systems from moving northward\xe2\x80\x94it turned to the west-northwest. Claudette made its first\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Landfall_(meteorology)" title="Landfall (meteorology)">\n        landfall\n       </a>\n       at\n       <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Morelos" title="Puerto Morelos">\n        Puerto Morelos\n       </a>\n       on the\n       <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula" title="Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula">\n        Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula\n       </a>\n       on July 11 as a tropical storm with 60\xc2\xa0mph (97\xc2\xa0km/h) winds.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Claudette_(2003).jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="498" data-file-width="600" height="183" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Hurricane_Claudette_%282003%29.jpg/220px-Hurricane_Claudette_%282003%29.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Hurricane_Claudette_%282003%29.jpg/330px-Hurricane_Claudette_%282003%29.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4c/Hurricane_Claudette_%282003%29.jpg/440px-Hurricane_Claudette_%282003%29.jpg 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Claudette_(2003).jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Hurricane Claudette making landfall at\n         <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">\n          Texas\n         </a>\n         on July 15\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Claudette wobbled to the northwest over the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">\n        Gulf of Mexico\n       </a>\n       and gradually restrengthened. Upper-level winds steadily decreased, and Claudette again became a hurricane late on July 14. Just prior to landfall, it strengthened quickly and picked up its forward motion in a more westerly direction, striking\n       <a href="/wiki/Port_O%27Connor,_Texas" title="Port O\'Connor, Texas">\n        Port O\'Connor, Texas\n       </a>\n       , on July 15 as a Category\xc2\xa01 hurricane on the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir-Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale">\n        Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale\n       </a>\n       with 90\xc2\xa0mph (150\xc2\xa0km/h) winds. Residents along and inland of the central Texas coast were caught off guard both by its intensity and its time of arrival. The hurricane was projected to make landfall in the evening hours of July 15, but instead came ashore before noon.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SRH_Claudette_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SRH_Claudette-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Furthermore, some initial estimates suggested that Claudette had reached Category\xc2\xa02 status, with sustained winds of up to 100\xc2\xa0mph (155\xc2\xa0km/h); these were based on unofficial observations which were not backed up by official data.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       As slow as Claudette was to strengthen, it was also slow to dissipate over land. Inland hurricane warnings were issued for several counties away from the coastline and winds gusted to 83\xc2\xa0mph (134\xc2\xa0km/h) at Victoria Regional Airport.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-SRH_Claudette_2-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-SRH_Claudette-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       It maintained tropical storm intensity for over 24\xc2\xa0hours after landfall, a rarity for such a weak storm; most tropical systems weaken rapidly after landfall, as they are separated from the warm waters that power them. The storm finally lost its low-level circulation early on July 17 over\n       <a href="/wiki/Chihuahua_(state)" title="Chihuahua (state)">\n        Chihuahua\n       </a>\n       , although its rainfall and upper-level circulation continued into the Pacific Ocean.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">\n        Preparations\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Claudette_(2003)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Mexican officials issued a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">\n        tropical storm warning\n       </a>\n       37\xc2\xa0hours prior to the storm\'s landfall between\n       <a href="/wiki/Chetumal" title="Chetumal">\n        Chetumal\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Cabo_Catoche" title="Cabo Catoche">\n        Cabo Catoche\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Quintana_Roo" title="Quintana Roo">\n        Quintana Roo\n       </a>\n       . The warning was upgraded to a hurricane warning about a day before landfall, but was downgraded when Claudette weakened just 13\xc2\xa0hours prior to its landfall in the Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The Mexican government declared a state of emergency in the projected path of the storm, and declared an evacuation order for 1,500 citizens in Quintana Roo. There, residents remained calm during the evacuation.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mexevac_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mexevac-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Tourists left nightclubs for supermarkets to stock up on, among other items,\n       <a href="/wiki/Beer" title="Beer">\n        beer\n       </a>\n       , which was banned at midnight. Schools were set up as shelters, while police forced tourists to remain in their hotels.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-mexevac2_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-mexevac2-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The consistent variation in the path of Claudette caused uncertainty over the strength and location of its final landfall. On July 13, two days prior to Claudette\'s eventual landfall, the National Hurricane Center issued a hurricane watch between\n       <a href="/wiki/Brownsville,_Texas" title="Brownsville, Texas">\n        Brownsville\n       </a>\n       and Port O\'Connor, Texas. By the next day, a hurricane warning existed from\n       <a href="/wiki/Baffin_Bay_(Texas)" title="Baffin Bay (Texas)">\n        Baffin Bay\n       </a>\n       to\n       <a href="/wiki/High_Island,_Texas" title="High Island, Texas">\n        High Island, Texas\n       </a>\n       , while a tropical storm warning extended from High Island to\n       <a href="/wiki/Intracoastal_City,_Louisiana" title="Intracoastal City, Louisiana">\n        Intracoastal City, Louisiana\n       </a>\n       . When it became apparent that Louisiana would not be affected significantly by the storm, the state\'s tropical storm warnings were canceled.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">\n        Texas\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Galveston_County,_Texas" title="Galveston County, Texas">\n        Galveston County\n       </a>\n       officials recommended evacuations for western\n       <a href="/wiki/Galveston_Island" title="Galveston Island">\n        Galveston Island\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Jamaica_Beach,_Texas" title="Jamaica Beach, Texas">\n        Jamaica Beach\n       </a>\n       , 24\xc2\xa0hours prior to Claudette\'s projected landfall. The Emergency Phone Notification System notified citizens in the evening to avoid evacuating during the night. Many citizens heeded the evacuation suggestion, some of whom remembered the flooding from\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_Storm_Frances_(1998)" title="Tropical Storm Frances (1998)">\n        Tropical Storm Frances\n       </a>\n       five years before.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jam_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-jam-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Hurricane Claudette also affected the\n       <a href="/wiki/2003_world_oil_market_chronology" title="2003 world oil market chronology">\n        oil industry\n       </a>\n       .\n       <a href="/wiki/ExxonMobil" title="ExxonMobil">\n        ExxonMobil\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Chevron_Corporation" title="Chevron Corporation">\n        Chevron\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/ConocoPhillips" title="ConocoPhillips">\n        ConocoPhillips\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Royal_Dutch_Shell" title="Royal Dutch Shell">\n        Shell Oil\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Marathon_Oil" title="Marathon Oil">\n        Marathon Oil\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Unocal_Corporation" title="Unocal Corporation">\n        Unocal Corporation\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Anadarko_Petroleum_Corporation" title="Anadarko Petroleum Corporation">\n        Anadarko Petroleum Corporation\n       </a>\n       all limited production and evacuated many of their workers. Chevron, which evacuated more than 1,800\xc2\xa0workers, returned many of its workers on the day when Claudette made landfall. Unocal closed 23\xc2\xa0rigs and platforms in the region. The combined closures stopped the daily production of 225,000 barrels (35,800\xc2\xa0m\n       <sup>\n        3\n       </sup>\n       ) per day of oil and 2\xc2\xa0billion cubic feet (57,000,000\xc2\xa0m\n       <sup>\n        3\n       </sup>\n       ) of natural gas, which accounted for 15% of the total production in the Gulf of Mexico.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">\n        Impact\n       </span>\n ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" damage due to its low intensity. In total, damage estimates rose to $180\xc2\xa0million (2003\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">\n        USD\n       </a>\n       , $198\xc2\xa0million 2006\xc2\xa0USD), and the storm caused one direct and two indirect deaths. One of the indirect deaths occurred from a man having a heart attack while surfing in the eastern Gulf of Mexico.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Caribbean_Sea">\n        Caribbean Sea\n       </span>\n       <span <p>\n       As a tropical wave, Claudette dropped torrential rainfall and brought strong winds to the island of\n       <a href="/wiki/Saint_Lucia" title="Saint Lucia">\n        Saint Lucia\n       </a>\n       . The\n       <a href="/wiki/George_F._L._Charles_Airport" title="George F. L. Charles Airport">\n        George F. L. Charles Airport\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/Castries" title="Castries">\n        Castries\n       </a>\n       reported a wind gust of 52\xc2\xa0mph (84\xc2\xa0km/h), while other parts of the island experienced nearly horizontal rainfall from the gusts. On the northern and eastern portions of the island, the strong winds ripped off roofs and downed trees across roadways.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stluc_7-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-stluc-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Overall, damage was light, and amounted to $1.1\xc2\xa0million (2003\xc2\xa0USD, $1.43\xc2\xa0million 2017\xc2\xa0USD).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stdam_8-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-stdam-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In the\n       <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">\n        Dominican Republic\n       </a>\n       , the outer bands of the storm caused moderate rainfall of up to 3 inches (76\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Santo_Domingo" title="Santo Domingo">\n        Santo Domingo\n       </a>\n       . Winds gusted up to 45\xc2\xa0mph (72\xc2\xa0km/h), knocking down trees, sign posts, and iron roofs. Also, flooding in metropolitan areas blocked traffic for cars and pedestrians.\n       <a href="/wiki/Banana" title="Banana">\n        Banana\n       </a>\n       and other fruit plantations in the southwestern portion of the country experienced severe damage, although exact damage totals are unknown.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-domdam_9-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-domdam-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       On\n       <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">\n        Jamaica\n       </a>\n       , the storm caused wind gusts of 45\xc2\xa0mph (72\xc2\xa0km/h) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Montego_Bay" title="Montego Bay">\n        Montego Bay\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Forecasters predicted high tides, strong waves, and up to 6 inches (150\xc2\xa0mm) of rain on the island, although exact post-storm totals are not known.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jam1_10-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-jam1-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Many fishermen moved their boats from the water to safety, while a cruise line diverted three ships away from Claudette\'s path.\n       <sup class="reference" Islands">\n        Cayman Islands\n       </a>\n       , where the storm remained 165 miles (266\xc2\xa0km) away at its closest point, rainfall totals ranged from 1 to 3 inches (25 to 76\xc2\xa0mm).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On the south side of\n       <a href="/wiki/Grand_Cayman" title="Grand Cayman">\n        Grand Cayman\n       </a>\n       , Claudette caused wave heights of around 10 feet (3.0\xc2\xa0m), while the western side received little precipitation. Also on Grand Cayman, the storm caused winds of 35\xe2\x80\x9340\xc2\xa0mph (56\xe2\x80\x9364\xc2\xa0km/h), causing minor tree damage but little else.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cayman_12-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-cayman-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       On Mexico\'s Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula, Claudette lashed strong waves against the coastline at\n       <a href="/wiki/Canc%C3%BAn" title="Canc\xc3\xban">\n        Canc\xc3\xban\n       </a>\n       , but overall caused light damage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-usa_13-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-usa-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       While crossing the peninsula, the storm dropped moderate rainfall, including a total of 3.22 inches (82\xc2\xa0mm) in Canc\xc3\xban.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Winds were moderate, causing the\n       <a href="/wiki/Canc%C3%BAn_International_Airport" title="Canc\xc3\xban International Airport">\n        Canc\xc3\xban International Airport\n       </a>\n       to delay several flights, though there were no cancellations.     <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Claudette_Damage_(2003).jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="768" <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Claudette_Damage_(2003).jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Overview of damage in\n         <a href="/wiki/Palacios,_Texas" title="Palacios, Texas">\n          Palacios\n         </a>\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Upon making landfall on the central Texas coast, Claudette\'s\n       <a href="/wiki/Storm_surge" title="Storm surge">\n        storm surge\n       </a>\n       reached a maximum height of 5.3 feet (1.6\xc2\xa0m) in\n       <a  reported a "storm tide"\xe2\x80\x94the overall rise of water over the mean low tide\xe2\x80\x94of 9.15 feet (2.79\xc2\xa0m). Claudette produced moderate rainfall across southern Texas, peaking at 6.5 inches (170\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Tilden,_Texas" title="Tilden, Texas">\n        Tilden\n       </a>\n       . On\n       <a href="/wiki/Matagorda_Island" title="Matagorda Island">\n        Matagorda Island\n       </a>\n       , the local weather station measured winds of 75\xc2\xa0mph (121\xc2\xa0km/h)\xe2\x80\x94the only official report of hurricane-force winds along the coastline. This report would suggest the storm was a low-end Category 2 hurricane, though this contradicted\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Hunters" title="Hurricane Hunters">\n        Hurricane Hunters\n       </a>\n       \' data, which pointed to a storm of a lesser intensity.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Severe\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beach_erosion" title="Beach erosion">\n        beach erosion\n       </a>\n       occurred from High Island to Freeport, though large\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Geo-tube&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Geo-tube (page does not exist)">\n        geo-tubes\n       </a>\n       reduced the erosion on Galveston Island and the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bolivar_Peninsula" title="Bolivar Peninsula">\n        Bolivar Peninsula\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-setex_14-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-setex-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The outer bands of the hurricane spawned two tornadoes. One was an F1 on the\n       <a href="/wiki/Fujita_scale" title="Fujita scale">\n        Fujita scale\n       </a>\n       that caused damage to several buildings in\n       <a href="/wiki/Palacios,_Texas" title="Palacios, Texas">\n        Palacios\n       </a>\n       , and the other damaged homes in\n       <a href="/wiki/Port_Lavaca,_Texas" title="Port Lavaca, Texas">\n        Port Lavaca\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Widespread flooding and gusty winds destroyed 204 houses along the southeast Texas coastline, most of which occurred in\n       <a href="/wiki/Matagorda_County,_Texas" title="Matagorda County, Texas">\n        Matagorda County\n       </a>\n       . In addition, the winds damaged 1,407 homes, with 144 of them being severely damaged. The wind also affected 147 businesses, of which 64 were either destroyed or severely damaged.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-setex_14-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-setex-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Strong winds downed numerous power lines, leaving around 74,000 residents without power in the immediate aftermath.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cnn_15-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-cnn-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Overall, Claudette caused $180\xc2\xa0million (2003\xc2\xa0USD, $234\xc2\xa0million 2017\xc2\xa0USD) in damage in Texas and one death from a fallen tree during the storm. Also, the storm was indirectly responsible for a death when a tree fell on a person in the aftermath of the storm.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Aftermath">\n        Aftermath\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Claudette_(2003)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Aftermath">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Claudette_Erosion.JPG">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="480" data-file-width="640" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f3/Claudette_Erosion.JPG/220px-Claudette_Erosion.JPG" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f3/Claudette_Erosion.JPG/330px-Claudette_Erosion.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f3/Claudette_Erosion.JPG/440px-Claudette_Erosion.JPG 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Claudette_Erosion.JPG" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Beach_erosion" title="Beach erosion">\n          Beach erosion\n         </a>\n         caused by Claudette\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       On July 17, President\n       <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">\n        George W. Bush\n       </a>\n       declared 18 south Texas counties as a federal disaster area. Within 2\xc2\xa0months of the storm, over 15,000\xc2\xa0citizens registered for assistance.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-aid_16-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-aid-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The government approved $34.8\xc2\xa0million (2003 USD, $45.3\xc2\xa0million 2017\xc2\xa0USD) in aid, of which nearly half went to basic house repairs. The\n       <a href="/wiki/Federal_Emergency_Management_Agency" title="Federal Emergency Management Agency">\n        Federal Emergency Management Agency\n       </a>\n       (FEMA) provided 75% of the $1.35\xc2\xa0million (2003 USD, $1.76\xc2\xa0million 2017\xc2\xa0USD) debris removal cost, with the other 25% being covered through local agencies.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-debris_17-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-debris-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       FEMA also provided $1.26\xc2\xa0million (2003 USD, $1.64\xc2\xa0million 2017\xc2\xa0USD) to repair geo-tubes on the Bolivar Peninsula in\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Galveston_County" title="Galveston County">\n        Galveston County\n       </a>\n       . The geo-tubes, which protect buildings along coastal areas, were severely damaged by the hurricane.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-geotube_18-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-geotube-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In western Texas, heavy rainfall of up to 5 inches (130\xc2\xa0mm) temporarily sparked widespread growth of wildflowers in a normally arid landscape. The rainfall restored the flow of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Rio_Grande" title="Rio Grande">\n        Rio Grande\n       </a>\n       at\n       <a href="/wiki/Big_Bend_National_Park" title="Big Bend National Park">\n        Big Bend National Park\n       </a>\n       , which had ceased in the area due to lack of rainfall. In addition, the precipitation increased the water level in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Amistad_Reservoir" title="Amistad Reservoir">\n        Amistad Reservoir\n       </a>\n       by more than 4 feet (1.2\xc2\xa0m).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-setexweather_19-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-setexweather-19">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n
