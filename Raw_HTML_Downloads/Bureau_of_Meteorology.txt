Bureau of Meteorology\n       </b>\n       (\n       <b>\n        BOM\n       </b>\n       ) is an Executive Agency of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Government_of_Australia" title="Government of Australia">\n        Australian Government\n       </a>\n       responsible for providing weather services to Australia and surrounding areas. It was established in 1906 under the Meteorology Act, and brought together the\n       <a href="/wiki/States_and_territories_of_Australia" title="States and territories of Australia">\n        state\n       </a>\n       meteorological services that existed before then. The states officially transferred their weather recording responsibilities to the Bureau of Meteorology on 1 January 1908.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ABC_1-0">\n        <a </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The Bureau of Meteorology is the main provider of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Weather_forecast" title="Weather forecast">\n        weather forecasts\n       </a>\n       , warnings and observations to the Australian public. The Bureau distributes weather images via\n       <a href="/wiki/Radiofax" title="Radiofax">\n        radiofax\n       </a>\n       and is responsible for issuing\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Flood_alerts" title="Flood alerts">\n        flood alerts\n       </a>\n       in Australia.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The Bureau\'s head office is in\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Melbourne_Docklands" title="Melbourne Docklands">\n        Melbourne Docklands\n       </a>\n       , which includes the Bureau\'s Research Centre, the Bureau National Operations Centre, the National Climate Centre, the Victorian Regional Forecasting Centre as well as the Hydrology and Satellite sections.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Regional offices are located in each state and territory capital. Each regional office includes a Regional Forecasting Centre and a Flood Warning Centre, and the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Perth,_Western_Australia" title="Perth, Western Australia">\n        Perth\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Darwin,_Northern_Territory" title="Darwin, Northern Territory">\n        Darwin\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Brisbane" title="Brisbane">\n        Brisbane\n       </a>\n       offices also house\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_Cyclone_Warning_Centre" title="Tropical Cyclone Warning Centre">\n        Tropical Cyclone Warning Centres\n       </a>\n       . Darwin office the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Volcanic_Ash_Advisory_Centre" title="Volcanic Ash Advisory Centre">\n        Volcanic Ash Advisory Centre\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Regional_Specialised_Meteorological_Centre" title="Regional Specialised Meteorological Centre">\n        Regional Specialised Meteorological Centre\n       </a>\n       (Analysis).\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Darwin_Airport_Met_Office.jpg">\n href="#cite_note-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and developed the\n       <a href="/wiki/Standard_Emergency_Warning_Signal" title="Standard Emergency Warning Signal">\n        Standard Emergency Warning Signal\n       </a>\n       used for warnings. The Bureau is responsible for\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_naming" title="Tropical cyclone naming">\n        tropical cyclone naming\n       </a>\n       for storms in waters surrounding Australia. Three lists of names used to be maintained, one for each of the western, northern and eastern Australian regions.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BOM_NAMES_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-BOM_NAMES-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       However, as of the start of the 2008\xe2\x80\x9309 Tropical Cyclone Year these lists have been rolled into one main national\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lists_of_tropical_cyclone_names#Australia" title="Lists of tropical cyclone names">\n        list of tropical cyclone names\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-BOM_NAMES_5-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-BOM_NAMES-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The regional offices are supported by the\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Bureau_National_Operations_Centre&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Bureau National Operations Centre (page does not exist)">\n        Bureau National Operations Centre\n       </a>\n       (BNOC) which is also located at the head office in\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Melbourne_Docklands" title="Melbourne Docklands">\n        Melbourne Docklands\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The Bureau maintains a network of field offices across the continent, on neighbouring islands and in\n       <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">\n        Antarctica\n       </a>\n       .
