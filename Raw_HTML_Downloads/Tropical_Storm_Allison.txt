  <p>\n       <b>\n        Tropical Storm Allison\n       </b>\n       was a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_low" title="Tropical low">\n        tropical storm\n       </a>\n       that devastated southeast\n       <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">\n        Texas\n       </a>\n       in June of the\n       <a href="/wiki/2001_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2001 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        2001 Atlantic hurricane season\n       </a>\n       . An arguable example of the "\n       <a href="/wiki/Brown_ocean_effect" title="Brown ocean effect">\n        brown ocean effect\n       </a>\n       ", Allison lasted unusually long for a June storm, remaining tropical or\n       <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_cyclone" title="Subtropical cyclone">\n        subtropical\n       </a>\n       for 15\xc2\xa0days, most of them over land dumping torrential rains. The storm developed from a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">\n        tropical wave\n       </a>\n       in the northern\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">\n        Gulf of Mexico\n       </a>\n       on June 4, 2001, and struck the upper Texas coast shortly thereafter. It drifted northward through the state, turned back to the south, and re-entered the Gulf of Mexico. The storm continued to the east-northeast, made landfall on\n       <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">\n        Louisiana\n       </a>\n       , then moved across the southeast United States and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mid-Atlantic_States" title="Mid-Atlantic States">\n        Mid-Atlantic\n       </a>\n       . Allison was the first storm since\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_Storm_Frances_(1998)" title="Tropical Storm Frances (1998)">\n        Tropical Storm Frances\n       </a>\n       in 1998 to strike the northern Texas coastline.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ivey_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ivey-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The storm dropped heavy rainfall along its path, peaking at over 40\xc2\xa0inches (1,000\xc2\xa0mm) in Texas. The worst flooding occurred in\n       <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">\n        Houston\n       </a>\n       , where most of Allison\'s damage occurred: 30,000 became homeless after the storm flooded over 70,000\xc2\xa0houses and destroyed 2,744\xc2\xa0homes. Downtown Houston was inundated with flooding, causing severe damage to hospitals and businesses. Twenty-three people died in Texas. Along its entire path, Allison caused $9 billion (2001\xc2\xa0USD) in damage and 41 deaths. Aside from Texas, the places worst hit were Louisiana and southeastern\n       <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">\n        Pennsylvania\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Following the storm, President\n       <a href="/wiki/George_W._Bush" title="George W. Bush">\n        George W. Bush\n       </a>\n       designated 75 counties along Allison\'s path as disaster areas, which enabled the citizens affected to apply for aid. Allison was the first Atlantic tropical storm to have its name\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricane names">\n        retired\n       </a>\n       without ever having reached hurricane strength.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="toc">\n       <div class="toctitle">\n        <h2>\n         Contents\n It moved westward across the\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">\n        Atlantic Ocean\n       </a>\n       , retaining little convection on its way. After moving across\n       <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">\n        South America\n       </a>\n       and the southwestern\n       <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">\n        Caribbean Sea\n       </a>\n       , the wave entered the eastern North\n       <a href="/wiki/Pacific_Ocean" title="Pacific Ocean">\n        Pacific Ocean\n       </a>\n       on June 1. A low-level circulation developed on June 2, while it was about 230\xc2\xa0miles (370\xc2\xa0km) south-southeast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Salina_Cruz" title="Salina Cruz">\n        Salina Cruz\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">\n        Mexico\n       </a>\n       . Southerly flow forced the system northward, and the wave moved inland on June 3. The low-level circulation dissipated, though the mid-level circulation persisted. It emerged into the Gulf of Mexico on June 4, and developed deep convection on its eastern side.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Early on June 5, satellite imagery suggested that a tropical depression was forming in the northwest Gulf of Mexico, which was furthered by reports of wind gusts as high as 60\xc2\xa0mph (95\xc2\xa0km/h) just a few hundred feet above the surface, towards the east side of the system.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       At 1200\xc2\xa0UTC on June 5, the disturbance developed a broad, low-level circulation, and was classified as Tropical Storm Allison, the first storm of the\n       <a href="/wiki/2001_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2001 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        2001 Atlantic hurricane season\n       </a>\n       . Some intensification was projected, though it was expected to be hindered by cool offshore sea surface temperatures.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Due to the cold-core nature of the center, Allison initially contained\n       <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_cyclone" title="Subtropical cyclone">\n        subtropical characteristics\n       </a>\n       . Despite this, the storm quickly strengthened to attain peak sustained winds of 60\xc2\xa0mph (95\xc2\xa0km/h), with tropical storm-force winds extending up to 230\xc2\xa0miles (370\xc2\xa0km) east of the center, and a minimum central pressure of 1000\n       <a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">\n        mbar\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_2-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The storm initially moved very little, and the presence of several small vortices from within the deep convection caused difficulty in determining the exact center location.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc2_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-disc2-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Later in the day, several different track forecasts arose. One scenario had the cyclone tracking westward into Mexico. Another projected the storm moving east towards southern Louisiana. At the time, it was noted that little rain or wind persisted near the center, but rather to the north and east.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Under the steering currents of a subtropical ridge that extended in an east\xe2\x80\x93west orientation across the southeast\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">\n        United States\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc2_5-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-disc2-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Allison weakened while nearing the Texas coastline, and struck near\n       <a href="/wiki/Freeport,_Texas" title="Freeport, Texas">\n        Freeport, Texas\n       </a>\n       with 50\xc2\xa0mph (80\xc2\xa0km/h) winds.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_2-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Inland, the storm rapidly weakened, and the\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">\n        National Hurricane Center\n       </a>\n       discontinued advisories early on June 6.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Shortly after being downgraded to a tropical depression, surface observations showed an elongated circulation with a poorly defined center, which had reformed closer to the deep convection.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n excessive rainfall, peaking at just over 40\xc2\xa0inches (1,033\xc2\xa0mm) in northwestern\n       <a href="/wiki/Jefferson_County,_Texas" title="Jefferson County, Texas">\n        Jefferson County\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wpc_9-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-wpc-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On June 7, the subtropical ridge off\n       <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">\n        Florida\n       </a>\n       weakened, while the ridge west of Texas intensified. This steered Tropical Depression Allison to make a clockwise loop, and the storm began drifting to the southwest. As the center reached\n       <a href="/wiki/Huntsville,_Texas" title="Huntsville, Texas">\n        Huntsville, Texas\n       </a>\n       , a heavy rain band began to back build from Louisiana westward into\n       <a href="/wiki/Liberty_County,_Texas" title="Liberty County, Texas">\n        Liberty County, Texas\n       </a>\n       , which had caused additional flooding.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-synoptic_10-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-synoptic-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At the time, the system had a minimum central pressure of about 1004\xc2\xa0mb and maximum sustained winds of about 10\xc2\xa0mph (16\xc2\xa0km/h).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Late on June 9 and early on June 10, Allison\'s remnants reentered reached the Gulf of Mexico and emerged over open waters.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The low once again became nearly stationary about 60\xc2\xa0mi (100\xc2\xa0km) south of\n       <a href="/wiki/Galveston,_Texas" title="Galveston, Texas">\n        Galveston, Texas\n       </a>\n       , and despite more favorable upper-level winds, it showed no signs of redevelopment.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Due to dry air and moderate westerly\n       <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">\n        wind shear\n       </a>\n       , the storm transformed into a subtropical cyclone. While the subtropical depression moved eastward, a new low level circulation redeveloped to the east, and Allison quickly made landfall on\n       <a href="/wiki/Morgan_City,_Louisiana" title="Morgan City, Louisiana">\n        Morgan City, Louisiana\n       </a>\n       on June 11.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_2-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       At around the same time, the surface center reformed to the east-northeast of its previous location, aligning with the mid-level circulation.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">\n        <a href="#cite_note-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Strong\n       <a href="/wiki/Thunderstorm" title="Thunderstorm">\n        thunderstorms\n       </a>\n       redeveloped over the circulation, and Allison strengthened into a subtropical storm over southeastern Louisiana.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_2-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The storm intensified further to attain sustained winds of 45\xc2\xa0mph (70\xc2\xa0km/h) and a minimum barometric pressure of about 1000\xc2\xa0mb near Mclain, Mississippi, accompanied by a well-defined\n       <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">\n        eye\n       </a>\n       -like feature.\n [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The depression drifted through North Carolina and sped to the northeast for a time in response to an approaching cold front.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">\n        <a href="#cite_note-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Though satellite and radar imagery show the system was well-organized, the system slowed and moved erratically for a period of time,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">\n        <a href="#cite_note-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       executing what appeared to be a small counterclockwise loop.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">\n        <a href="#cite_note-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The storm began tracking in a generally northeasterly direction, and crossed into the southern\n       <a href="/wiki/Delmarva_Peninsula" title="Delmarva Peninsula">\n        Delmarva Peninsula\n       </a>\n       on June 16.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">\n        <a href="#cite_note-19">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The subtropical remnants reached the Atlantic on June 17, and while located east of\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_City,_New_Jersey" title="Atlantic City, New Jersey">\n        Atlantic City, New Jersey\n       </a>\n       , winds began to restrengthen, and heavy rains formed to the north of the circulation. The low was interacting with a\n       <a href="/wiki/Weather_front" title="Weather front">\n        frontal boundary\n       </a>\n       , and started merging with it, as it accelerated to the northeast at 13\xc2\xa0mph (21\xc2\xa0km/h).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">\n        <a href="#cite_note-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The remnants of Allison briefly reintensified to a\n       <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_cyclone" title="Subtropical cyclone">\n        subtropical storm\n       </a>\n       through\n       <a href="/wiki/Baroclinity" </a>\n       </sup>\n       The remnants of the tropical storm were then absorbed by the frontal boundary by June 18, and eventually passed south of\n       <a href="/wiki/Cape_Race" title="Cape Tropical Storm Allison in Texas\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Shortly after the storm formed, officials in\n       <a href="/wiki/Galveston_County,_Texas" title="Galveston County, Texas">\n        Galveston County, Texas\n       </a>\n       issued a voluntary evacuation for the western end of\n       <a href="/wiki/Galveston_Island" title="Galveston Island">\n        Galveston Island\n       </a>\n       , as the area was not protected by the\n       <a href="/wiki/Galveston_Seawall" title="Galveston Seawall">\n        Galveston Seawall\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup Brazoria County\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws1_22-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nws1-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       When the\n class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_watches_and_warnings" title="Tropical cyclone watches and warnings">\n        Tropical Storm Warnings\n       </a>\n       from\n       <a Louisiana\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-disc1_23-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-disc1-23">\n         [23]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       After the storm made landfall,\n       <a href="/wiki/Flash_flood" title="Flash flood">\n        flash flood\n       </a>\n       watches and warnings were issued for numerous areas in eastern Texas.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws3_24-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nws3-24">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       During the flood event, the National Weather Service in Houston issued 99 flash flood warnings with an average lead time of 40\xc2\xa0minutes. With an average lead time of 24\xc2\xa0minutes, the National Weather Service in\n       <a href="/wiki/Lake_Charles,_Louisiana" title="Lake Charles, Louisiana">\n        Lake Charles, Louisiana\n       </a>\n       issued 47\xc2\xa0flash flood warnings. With an average lead time of 39\xc2\xa0minutes, the National Weather Service in New Orleans/Baton Rouge issued 87\xc2\xa0flash flood warnings, of which 30 were not followed by a flash flood.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nws_25-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nws-25">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Tallahassee,_Florida" title="Tallahassee, Florida">\n        Tallahassee, Florida\n       </a>\n       , a shelter opened the day before Allison\'s movement northward through the area, seven staff members housing 12\xc2\xa0people. Teams informed citizens in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Florida_Panhandle" title="Florida Panhandle">\n        Florida Panhandle\n       </a>\n       of flood dangers.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-flalpre_26-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-flalpre-26">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">\n        Impact\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_Storm_Allison&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Impact">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <table class="wikitable" style="float:right; The worst of the flooding occurred in\n       <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">\n        Houston\n       </a>\n       , Texas, where over 35\xc2\xa0inches (890\xc2\xa0mm) of rain fell. Allison caused over $9\xc2\xa0billion in damage (2001\xc2\xa0USD, $12\xc2\xa0billion 2014\xc2\xa0USD), making it the costliest tropical storm on record in the United States. The storm also killed 41\xc2\xa0people directly, including 27 who drowned. This ties Allison with a\n       <a href="/wiki/1917_Atlantic_hurricane_season#Tropical_Storm_Two" title="1917 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        tropical storm\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/1917_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1917 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1917\n       </a>\n       as the second-deadliest tropical storm to affect the US; only surpassed by the\n       <a href="/wiki/1925_Florida_tropical_storm" title="1925 Florida tropical storm">\n        1925 Florida tropical storm\n       </a>\n       which killed 73\xc2\xa0people.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TCR_2-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TCR-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Texas">\n        Texas\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_Storm_Allison&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Texas">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       Main article:\n       <a href="/wiki/Effects_of_Tropical_Storm_Allison_in_Texas" title="Effects of Tropical Storm Allison in Texas">\n        Effects of Tropical Storm Allison in Texas\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Combined with waves on top, areas of Galveston Island experienced a wall of water 8\xc2\xa0feet (2.5\xc2\xa0m) in height, creating\n       <a href="/wiki/Overwash" title="Overwash">\n        overwash\n       </a>\n       along the coastline.
