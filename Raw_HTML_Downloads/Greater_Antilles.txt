</table>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <b>\n        Greater Antilles\n       </b>\n       is a grouping of the larger islands in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">\n        Caribbean Sea\n       </a>\n       :\n       <a href="/wiki/Cuba" title="Cuba">\n        Cuba\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Hispaniola" title="Hispaniola">\n        Hispaniola\n       </a>\n       (containing\n       <a href="/wiki/Haiti" title="Haiti">\n        Haiti\n       </a>\n       and the\n       <a href="/wiki/Dominican_Republic" title="Dominican Republic">\n        Dominican Republic\n       </a>\n       ),\n       <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">\n        Puerto Rico\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Jamaica" title="Jamaica">\n        Jamaica\n       </a>\n       , and the\n       <a href="/wiki/Cayman_Islands" title="Cayman Islands">\n        Cayman Islands\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The Greater Antilles constitute nearly 90% of the land mass of the entire\n       <a href="/wiki/West_Indies" title="West Indies">\n        West Indies\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       as well as over 90% of its population. The remainder of the land belongs to the archipelago of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">\n        Lesser Antilles\n       </a>\n       , which is a chain of islands to the east (running north-south and encompassing the eastern edge of the Caribbean Sea where it meets the Atlantic Ocean) and south (running east-west off the northern coast of\n       <a href="/wiki/South_America" title="South America">\n        South America\n       </a>\n       ).\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The word\n       <i>\n        Antilles\n       </i>\n       originated in the period before the\n       <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">\n        European\n       </a>\n       conquest of the\n       <a href="/wiki/New_World" title="New World">\n        New World\n       </a>\n       - Europeans used the term\n       <i>\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Antilia" title="Antilia">\n         Antilia\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       as one of the mysterious lands featured on medieval charts, sometimes as an archipelago, sometimes as continuous land of greater or lesser extent, its location fluctuating in mid-ocean between the Canary Islands and Eurasia.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <a href="/wiki/Lucayan_Archipelago" title="Lucayan Archipelago">\n        Lucayan Archipelago\n       </a>\n       is not considered to be a
