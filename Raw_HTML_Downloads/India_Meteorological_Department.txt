It is the principal agency responsible for\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meteorological" title="Meteorological">\n        meteorological\n       </a>\n       observations,\n       <a href="/wiki/Weather_forecasting" title="Weather forecasting">\n        weather forecasting\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Seismology" title="Seismology">\n        seismology\n       </a>\n       . IMD is headquartered in Pune and operates hundreds of observation stations across\n       <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">\n        India\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Antarctica" title="Antarctica">\n        Antarctica\n       </a>\n       .Regional office are at\n       <a href="/wiki/Mumbai" title="Mumbai">\n        Mumbai\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Kolkata" title="Kolkata">\n        Kolkata\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Nagpur" title="Nagpur">\n        Nagpur\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Delhi" title="Delhi">\n        Delhi\n       </a>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       IMD is also one of the six\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Regional_Specialised_Meteorological_Centre" title="Regional Specialised Meteorological Centre">\n        Regional Specialised Meteorological Centres\n       </a>\n       of the\n       <a href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organization" title="World Meteorological Organization">\n        World Meteorological Organization\n       </a>\n       . It has the responsibility for forecasting, naming and distribution of warnings for\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        tropical cyclones\n       </a>\n       in the Northern Indian Ocean region, including the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Malacca_Straits" title="Malacca Straits">\n        Malacca Straits\n       </a>\n       , the\n       <a href="/wiki/Bay_of_Bengal" title="Bay of Bengal">\n        Bay of Bengal\n       </a>\n       , the\n       <a href="/wiki/Arabian_Sea" title="Arabian Sea">\n        Arabian Sea\n       </a>\n       and the\n       <a href="/wiki/Persian_Gulf" title="Persian Gulf">\n        Persian Gulf\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="toc">\n       <div   </a>\n        </li>\n       </ul>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="History">\n        History\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=India_Meteorological_Department&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       In 1686,\n       <a href="/wiki/Edmond_Halley" title="Edmond Halley">\n        Edmond Halley\n       </a>\n       published his treatise on the Indian summer\n       <a href="/wiki/Monsoon" title="Monsoon">\n        monsoon\n       </a>\n       , which he attributed to a seasonal reversal of winds due to the differential heating of the Asian land mass and the Indian Ocean. The first meteorological observatories were established in\n       <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">\n        India\n       </a>\n       by the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/British_East_India_Company" title="British East India Company">\n        British East India Company\n       </a>\n       . These included the\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Calcutta_Observatory&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Calcutta Observatory (page does not exist)">\n        Calcutta Observatory\n       </a>\n       in 1785, the\n       <a href="/wiki/Madras_Observatory" title="Madras Observatory">\n        Madras Observatory\n       </a>\n       in 1796 and the\n       <a href="/wiki/Colaba_Observatory" title="Colaba Observatory">\n        Colaba Observatory\n       </a>\n       in 1826. Several other observatories were established in India during the first half of the 19th century by various provincial governments.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <a href="/wiki/The_Asiatic_Society" title="The Asiatic Society">\n        The Asiatic Society\n       </a>\n       , founded in\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Calcutta" title="Calcutta">\n        Calcutta\n       </a>\n       in 1784 and in\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bombay" title="Bombay">\n        Bombay\n       </a>\n       in 1804, promoted the study of meteorology in India.\n       <a href="/wiki/Henry_Piddington" title="Henry Piddington">\n        Henry Piddington\n       </a>\n       published almost 40 papers dealing with\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Tropical_storms" title="Tropical storms">\n        tropical storms\n       </a>\n       from Calcutta between 1835 and 1855 in\n       <i>\n        The Journal of the Asiatic Society\n       </i>\n       . He also coined the term\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Cyclone" title="Cyclone">\n         cyclone\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       , meaning the coil of a snake. In 1842, he published his landmark thesis,\n       <i>\n        Laws of the Storms\n       </i>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-imd-mm_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-imd-mm-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       After a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        tropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       hit\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Calcutta" title="Calcutta">\n        Calcutta\n       </a>\n       in 1864, and the subsequent\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Famines" title="Famines">\n        famines\n       </a>\n       in 1866 and 1871 due to the failure of the monsoons, it was decided to organise the collection and analysis of meteorological observations under one roof. As a result, the\n       <b>\n        India Meteorology Department\n       </b>\n       was established in 1875.\n       <a href="/wiki/Henry_Francis_Blanford" title="Henry Francis Blanford">\n        Henry Francis Blanford\n       </a>\n       was appointed the first Meteorological Reporter of the IMD. In May 1889,\n       <a href="/wiki/John_Eliot_(meteorologist)" title="John Eliot (meteorologist)">\n        Sir John Eliot\n       </a>\n       was appointed the first\n       <i>\n        Director General of Observatories\n       </i>\n       in the erstwhile capital, Calcutta. The IMD headquarters were later shifted to\n       <a href="/wiki/Shimla" title="Shimla">\n        Shimla\n       </a>\n       in 1905, then to\n       <a href="/wiki/Pune" title="Pune">\n        Pune\n       </a>\n       in 1928 and finally to New Delhi in 1944.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-imd-establishment_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-imd-establishment-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       after\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Independence_of_India" title="Independence of India">\n        independence\n       </a>\n       on 27 April 1949.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wmo-members_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-wmo-members-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The agency has gained in prominence due to the significance of the monsoon rains on\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indian_agriculture" title="Indian agriculture">\n        Indian agriculture\n       </a>\n       . It plays a vital role in preparing the annual monsoon forecast, as well as in tracking the progress of the monsoon across India every season.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-bs-imd_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-bs-imd-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Organisation">\n        Organisation\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=India_Meteorological_Department&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Organisation">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       IMD is headed by the\n       <i>\n        Director General of Meteorology\n       </i>\n       . Dr. K. J. Ramesh is Director General of Meteorology since August 1 2016.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       IMD has 6\n       <i>\n        Regional Meteorological Centre\n       </i>\n       s, each under a Deputy Director General. These are located in\n       <a href="/wiki/Regional_Meteorological_Centre,_Chennai" title="Regional Meteorological Centre, Chennai">\n        Chennai\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Guwahati" title="Guwahati">\n        Guwahati\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Kolkata" title="Kolkata">\n        Kolkata\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Mumbai" title="Mumbai">\n        Mumbai\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Nagpur" title="Nagpur">\n        Nagpur\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/New_Delhi" title="New Delhi">\n        New Delhi\n       </a>\n       . There are also\n       <i>\n        Meteorological Centres\n       </i>\n       in every state capital. Other IMD units such as Forecasting Offices, Agrometeorological Advisory Service Centers, Flood Meteorological Offices, Area Cyclone Warning Centers and Cyclone Warning Centers are usually co-located with various observatories or meteorological center.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-imd-org_8-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-imd-org-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       IMD operates a network of hundreds of surface and glacial observatories, Upper Air (high altitude) stations,\n       <a href="/wiki/Ozone_layer" title="Ozone layer">\n        ozone\n       </a>\n       and radiation observatories and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Meteorological_radar" title="Meteorological radar">\n        meteorological radar\n       </a>\n       stations. Additional data is received from India\'s constellation of satellites, such as\n       <a href="/wiki/Kalpana-1" title="Kalpana-1">\n        Kalpana-1\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Megha-Tropiques" title="Megha-Tropiques">\n        Megha-Tropiques\n       </a>\n       and instruments on board the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IRS_series" title="IRS series">\n        IRS series\n       </a>\n       and the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/INSAT" title="INSAT">\n        INSAT\n       </a>\n       series of satellites.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-imd-satellites_9-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-imd-satellites-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Data and observations are also reported into the IMD network from meteorological instruments on board Indian merchant marine and\n       <a href="/wiki/Indian_Navy" title="Indian Navy">\n        Indian Navy\n       </a>\n       ships. IMD was the first organisation in India to deploy a message switching computer for supporting its global data exchange.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       IMD collaborates with other agencies such as the\n       <a href="/wiki/Indian_Institute_of_Tropical_Meteorology" title="Indian Institute of Tropical Meteorology">\n        Indian Institute of Tropical Meteorology\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Centre_for_Medium_Range_Weather_Forecasting" title="National Centre for Medium Range Weather Forecasting">\n        National Centre for Medium Range Weather Forecasting\n       </a>\n       and the\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Institute_of_Ocean_Technology" title="National Institute of Ocean Technology">\n        National Institute of Ocean Technology\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       IMD also operates seismic monitoring centres at key locations for earthquake monitoring and measurements.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Tasks">\n        Tasks\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=India_Meteorological_Department&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Tasks">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       IMD undertakes observations, communications, forecasting and weather services. In collaboration with the\n       <a href="/wiki/Indian_Space_Research_Organisation" title="Indian Space Research Organisation">\n        Indian Space Research Organisation\n       </a>\n       , the IMD also uses the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/IRS_series" title="IRS series">\n        IRS series\n       </a>\n       and the\n       <a href="/wiki/Indian_National_Satellite_System" title="Indian National Satellite System">\n        Indian National Satellite System\n       </a>\n       (INSAT) for weather monitoring of the Indian subcontinent. IMD was the first weather bureau of a developing country to develop and maintain its own satellite system.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       IMD is one of the 6 worldwide\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Regional_Specialised_Meteorological_Centre" title="Regional Specialised Meteorological Centre">\n        Regional Specialised Meteorological Centres\n       </a>\n       of the Tropical Cyclone Programme of the World Weather Watch of the\n       <a href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organization" title="World Meteorological Organization">\n        World Meteorological Organization\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-wmo-rsmc_10-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-wmo-rsmc-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       It is regional nodal agency for forecasting, naming and disseminating warnings about\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        tropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       in the Indian Ocean north of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Equator" title="Equator">\n        Equator\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="New_initiatives">\n        New initiatives\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=India_Meteorological_Department&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: New initiatives">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       The IMD launched System of Aerosol Monitoring and Research (SAMAR) in January 2016 to study the concentration of\n       <a href="/wiki/Black_carbon" title="Black carbon">\n        Black carbon\n       </a>\n       , radiative properties of\n       <a href="/wiki/Aerosol" title="Aerosol">\n        aerosols\n       </a>\n       , environmental visibility and their climatological impacts. It would contain a network of 16\n       <a href="/wiki/Aethalometer" title="Aethalometer">\n        aethalometers\n       </a>\n       , 12 sky radiometers and 12\n       <a href="/wiki/Nephelometer" title="Nephelometer">\n        nephelometers\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n
