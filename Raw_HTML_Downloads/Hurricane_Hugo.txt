</tr>\n       <tr style="display:none">\n        <td colspan="2">\n        </td>\n       </tr>\n      </table>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        Hurricane Hugo\n       </b>\n       was a powerful\n       <a href="/wiki/Cape_Verde_hurricane" title="Cape Verde hurricane">\n        Cape Verde\xe2\x80\x93type hurricane\n       </a>\n       that caused widespread damage and loss of life in\n       <a href="/wiki/Guadeloupe" title="Guadeloupe">\n        Guadeloupe\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saint_Croix" title="Saint Croix">\n        Saint Croix\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Puerto_Rico" title="Puerto Rico">\n        Puerto Rico\n       </a>\n       , and the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Southeast_United_States" title="Southeast United States">\n        Southeast United States\n       </a>\n       . It formed over the eastern Atlantic near the Cape Verde Islands on September 9, 1989. Hugo moved thousands of miles across the Atlantic, rapidly strengthening to briefly attain category 5 hurricane strength on its journey. It later crossed over Guadeloupe and St. Croix on September 17 and 18 as a category 4 hurricane. Weakening slightly more, it passed over Puerto Rico as a strong category 3 hurricane. Further weakening occurred several hours after re-emerging into the Atlantic, becoming downgraded to a category 2 hurricane. However, it re-strengthened into a category 4 hurricane before making landfall just slightly north of Charleston, on Isle of Palms on September 21 with 140\xc2\xa0mph sustained winds (gusts to more than 160\xc2\xa0mph). It had devolved to a remnant low near\n       <a href="/wiki/Lake_Erie" title="Lake Erie">\n        Lake Erie\n       </a>\n       by the next day. As of 2016, Hurricane Hugo is the most intense tropical cyclone to strike the East Coast north of Florida since 1900.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PrelimReport_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-PrelimReport-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Hurricane Hugo caused 34 fatalities (most by electrocution or drowning) in the Caribbean and 27 in South Carolina,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       left nearly 100,000 homeless, and resulted in $10\xc2\xa0billion (1989 USD) in damage overall, making it the most damaging hurricane ever recorded at the time.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pastcost_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-pastcost-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Of this total, $7\xc2\xa0billion was from the United States and Puerto Rico, ranking it as the costliest storm to impact the country at the time. Since 1989, however, it has been surpassed by multiple storms and now ranks as the eleventh costliest in the United States.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4">\n        <a id="Meteorological_history">\n        Meteorological history\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Hugo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Meteorological history">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hugo_1989_track.png">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="633" data-file-width="1024" height="136" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Hugo_1989_track.png/220px-Hugo_1989_track.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/Hugo_1989_track.png/330px-Hugo_1989_track.png 1.5x,         <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hugo_1989_track.png" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Map plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the\n         <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">\n          Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale\n         </a>\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Hurricane Hugo originated as a tropical wave (which also spawned\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Raymond_(1989)" title="Hurricane Raymond (1989)">\n        Hurricane Raymond\n       </a>\n       ), which moved off the west coast of Africa on September 9. Soon after moving off the African coast, it was classified as Tropical Depression Eleven southeast of the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Cape_Verde_Islands" title="Cape Verde Islands">\n        Cape Verde Islands\n       </a>\n       . Winds were initially 30\xc2\xa0mph (48\xc2\xa0km/h) but they reached 35\xc2\xa0mph (56\xc2\xa0km/h) soon after. Moving on a steady westward track at 18 knots (21\xc2\xa0mph, 33 k/h), Tropical Depression Eleven steadily intensified, becoming Tropical Storm Hugo on September 11 at 1800 UTC. On September 13, Hugo rapidly intensified, and reached hurricane strength 1265 miles (2035\xc2\xa0km) east of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Leeward_Islands" title="Leeward Islands">\n        Leeward Islands\n       </a>\n       . A low-pressure area to the south caused Hugo to gradually turn to the west-northwest, while the storm was slowly strengthening. Shortly after, Hurricane Hugo began to rapidly intensify; 24 hours after it was classified as a hurricane, it had become a category 2 hurricane. After this bout of rapid strengthening, Hugo began to rapidly deepen, becoming a major hurricane early the next morning.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PrelimReport_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-PrelimReport-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       After becoming a major hurricane, and maintaining Category 3 strength for a day, Hugo reached\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Category_4_hurricane" title="Category 4 hurricane">\n        Category 4\n       </a>\n       strength, and began to rapidly intensify again, while moving slowly west-northwest. Becoming a Category 5 Hurricane, its maximum sustained winds had increased to 160\xc2\xa0mph (260\xc2\xa0km/h) and the minimum central pressure had dropped to 918 millibars (27.1\xc2\xa0inHg). In the early hours of September 17, Hugo crossed in between\n       <a href="/wiki/Guadeloupe" title="Guadeloupe">\n        Guadeloupe\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Montserrat" title="Montserrat">\n        Montserrat\n       </a>\n       while its winds were near 140\xc2\xa0mph (230\xc2\xa0km/h), when hurricane-force winds extended only 45\xc2\xa0mi (72\xc2\xa0km) from the center. Less than 24 hours later, it made another landfall on the island of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/St._Croix" title="St. Croix">\n        St. Croix\n       </a>\n       , with the same intensity. That day, Hurricane Hugo also made landfalls in Puerto Rico, in\n       <a href="/wiki/Vieques,_Puerto_Rico" title="Vieques, Puerto Rico">\n        Vieques\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Fajardo,_Puerto_Rico" title="Fajardo, Puerto Rico">\n        Fajardo\n       </a>\n       , though it was slightly weaker.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PrelimReport_1-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-PrelimReport-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Hugo began to accelerate to the northwest soon after exiting eastern Puerto Rico. On September 18, the hurricane was located a couple of hundred miles east of Florida when it began a more northward track, in response to a steering flow associated with an\n       <a href="/wiki/Cold-core_low" title="Cold-core low">\n        upper-level low pressure area\n       </a>\n       that was moving across the southeastern United States. Hugo then began to strengthen again, and it reached a secondary peak at 1800 UTC on September 21 as a Category 4 hurricane. The maximum sustained winds were 140\xc2\xa0mph (230\xc2\xa0km/h), while the minimum central pressure was 944 millibars (27.9\xc2\xa0inHg). On September 22 at 0400 UTC, Hugo made landfall on\n       <a href="/wiki/Sullivan%27s_Island,_South_Carolina" title="Sullivan\'s Island, South Carolina">\n        Sullivan\'s Island, South Carolina\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ecu.edu_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ecu.edu-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       at its secondary peak as a Category 4 hurricane on the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_Hurricane_Scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson Hurricane Scale">\n        Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson Hurricane Scale\n       </a>\n       with 140-mph sustained winds and a central pressure of 934 millibars (27.6\xc2\xa0inHg).\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The storm continued inland, and weakened to a very strong tropical storm as it passed over\n       <a href="/wiki/Charlotte,_North_Carolina" title="Charlotte, North Carolina">\n        Charlotte, North Carolina\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ecu.edu_5-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ecu.edu-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Hugo continued on the weakening trend and weakened to a tropical storm later on the same day over central North Carolina. The storm continued weakening as it moved inland, and on September 23, the storm weakened to a remnant low. Its remnant low continued to accelerate north, and it reached the far northern Atlantic before dissipating on September 25.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PrelimReport_1-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-PrelimReport-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">\n        Preparations\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Hugo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Late on September 15, the\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Weather_Service" title="National Weather Service">\n        National Weather Service\n       </a>\n       Office in\n       <a href="/wiki/San_Juan,_Puerto_Rico" title="San Juan, Puerto Rico">\n        San Juan\n       </a>\n       , Puerto Rico issued a hurricane watch. On the following day, it was upgraded to a hurricane warning. In addition, the Civil Defense Office of Puerto Rico activated its Disaster Interagency Committee and began to evacuate coastal residents.\n       <a href="/wiki/Savannah,_Georgia" title="Savannah, Georgia">\n        Savannah\n       </a>\n       was evacuated in anticipation of Hugo, but saw no effects of the storm other than isolated and light showers. Had Hugo hit Savannah, it would have been the first major hurricane to make landfall in\n       <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(U.S._state)" title="Georgia (U.S. state)">\n        Georgia\n       </a>\n       since\n       <a href="/wiki/1898_Georgia_hurricane" title="1898 Georgia hurricane">\n        Hurricane Seven\n       </a>\n       of the\n       <a href="/wiki/1898_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1898 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1898 season\n       </a>\n       . Governor\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Carroll_Campbell" title="Carroll Campbell">\n        Carroll Campbell\n       </a>\n       of\n       <a href="/wiki/South_Carolina" title="South Carolina">\n        South Carolina\n       </a>\n       ordered an evacuation of the South Carolina coast in advance of the storm.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TT.26D_6-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TT.26D-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">\n        Impact\n       </span>\n       <span </td>\n        <td>\n        </td>\n       </tr>\n       <tr style="text-align:center;">\n        <td>\n         United States\n        </td>\n        <td>\n         37\n        </td>\n        <td>\n         $5.2\xc2\xa0billion\n        </td>\n        <td>\n        </td>\n       </tr>\n       <tr style="text-align:center;">\n        <th>\n         Total\n        </th>\n        <th>\n         99\n        </th>\n        <th>\n         ~$10\xc2\xa0billion\n        </th>\n       </tr>\n      </table>\n      <p>\n       Hugo caused nearly $7\xc2\xa0billion (1989 US$) in damage in the mainland United States and Puerto Rico. At the time it was the costliest hurricane in U.S. history, but was exceeded in 1992 by\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Andrew" title="Hurricane Andrew">\n        Hurricane Andrew\n       </a>\n       , and by seven other storms since then. It remains the eleventh costliest hurricane in U.S. history. An additional $3\xc2\xa0billion of damages was reported throughout the Caribbean. Therefore, total damages from the storm were near $10\xc2\xa0billion (1989 US$).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pastcost_3-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-pastcost-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Sources differ on the number of people killed by Hugo, with some citing the\n       <a href="/wiki/American_Meteorological_Society" title="American Meteorological Society">\n        American Meteorological Society\n       </a>\n       \'s figure of 49, and others claiming 56 deaths.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-deadly_8-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-deadly-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Caribbean">\n        Caribbean\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Hugo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Caribbean">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Severe damage was reported throughout the islands of the Caribbean. The storm caused an estimated $3\xc2\xa0billion in damages in the Caribbean (including $1.8\xc2\xa0billion in the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/USVI" title="USVI">\n        USVI\n       </a>\n       ).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-pastcost_3-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-pastcost-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The storm also took USVI television station\n       <a href="/wiki/WBNB-TV" title="WBNB-TV">\n        WBNB-TV\n       </a>\n       off the air by destroying their transmitter.\n      </p>\n      <h4>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Guadeloupe">\n        Guadeloupe\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Hugo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Guadeloupe">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h4>\n      <p>\n       At 1 am AST on September 17, 1989, Hurricane Hugo made a direct landfall on\n       <a href="/wiki/Grande-Terre" title="Grande-Terre">\n        Grande-Terre\n       </a>\n       in Guadeloupe, pounding the island with incredibly ferocious Category 4 sustained winds of 140\xc2\xa0mph (220\xc2\xa0km/h). A storm surge of up to eight\xc2\xa0ft (2.5 m) topped by high battering waves smashed ashore. Hugo wreaked massive devastation on the island, especially in\n       <a href="/wiki/La_D%C3%A9sirade" title="La D\xc3\xa9sirade">\n        La D\xc3\xa9sirade\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Grande-Terre" title="Grande-Terre">\n        Grande-Terre\n       </a>\n       . The hurricane damaged 30% of the buildings on the island and completely destroyed 10,000 homes (most of which were archaic houses), leaving a total of at least 90,000 or nearly 30% of the island\'s 340,000 people seriously affected, with 35,000 rendered homeless. 70% of the businesses sustained damages, including hotels, schools and churches. Five people died and 107 were injured. An additional seven people were killed three days after the storm, when a medical helicopter crashed while evacuating victims in\n       <a href="/wiki/La_D%C3%A9sirade" title="La D\xc3\xa9sirade">\n        La D\xc3\xa9sirade\n       </a>\n       . The storm almost completely destroyed (80%) the towns of\n       <a href="/wiki/Le_Moule" title="Le Moule">\n        Le Moule\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Saint-Fran%C3%A7ois,_Guadeloupe" title="Saint-Fran\xc3\xa7ois, Guadeloupe">\n        Saint-Fran\xc3\xa7ois\n       </a>\n       , on the island\'s eastern end. Debris blocked at least 30% of the island\'s roads. Agriculture suffered massive losses that took years to recover from, as Hugo flattened 100% of the banana crop, 50% of the sugar cane crop and destroyed nearly all of the island\'s coconut palms. In addition, most of Guadeloupe\'s fishing fleet was wiped out. Total damage to the island from Hugo amounted to 4 billions\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Francs" title="Francs">\n        francs\n       </a>\n       or $880 million (1989 USD).\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Hugo\'s winds knocked the airport control tower out of commission;\n       <a href="/wiki/Pointe-%C3%A0-Pitre_International_Airport" title="Pointe-\xc3\xa0-Pitre International Airport">\n        Raizet Airport\n       </a>\n       anenometer experienced 90\xc2\xa0mph (144\xc2\xa0km/h) sustained winds as well as a 117\xc2\xa0mph (187\xc2\xa0km/h) sustained gust before failing. Minimal pressure fell to 943 mb (27.79 inHg) as the eye passed after midnight.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-PrelimReport_1-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-PrelimReport-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In a French navy\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Marine_vessel" title="Marine vessel">\n        marine vessel\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/Pointe-%C3%A0-Pitre" title="Pointe-\xc3\xa0-Pitre">\n        Pointe-\xc3\xa0-Pitre\n       </a>\n       bay, gusts reported to reach 184\xc2\xa0mph (296\xc2\xa0km/h).\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Hugo was the strongest storm to impact Guadeloupe since\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Cleo" title="Hurricane Cleo">\n        Hurricane Cleo\n       </a>\n       (1964), and the worst storm since\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Inez" title="Hurricane Inez">\n        Hurricane Inez\n       </a>\n       (1966). In addition it was the strongest hurricane to hit the island since the legendary\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/1899_Hurricane_San_Ciriaco" title="1899 Hurricane San Ciriaco">\n        1899\n       </a>\n       , the longest-lived\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_tropical_cyclone" title="North Atlantic tropical cyclone">\n        Atlantic hurricane\n       </a>\n       of all time and the\n       <a href="/wiki/1928_Okeechobee_hurricane" title="1928 Okeechobee hurricane">\n        1928\n       </a>\n       storm.\n      </p>\n      <h4>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Montserrat">\n        Montserrat\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Hugo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Montserrat">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h4>\n      <p>\n       Strong winds from Hugo pounded the\n       <a href="/wiki/British_Overseas_Territories" title="British Overseas Territories">\n        British Dependent Territory\n       </a>\n       of\n       <a href="/wiki/Montserrat" title="Montserrat">\n        Montserrat\n       </a>\n       . Nearly every home on the island was damaged to some degree, leaving 11,000 out of 12,000\xc2\xa0people, over 90% of the islands residents, homeless. Numerous schools, churches, the hospital, the police department, the government headquarters, and the main power station were either destroyed or heavily damaged, disrupting electrical, water, and telephone service for weeks. A 180 feet (55\xc2\xa0m) stone jetty was destroyed at\n       <a href="/wiki/Plymouth,_Montserrat" title="Plymouth, Montserrat">\n        Plymouth\n       </a>\n       due to a storm surge of 20 feet (6.1\xc2\xa0m). Inland, rainfall up to 7 inches (180\xc2\xa0mm) created mudslides at the foot of\n       <a href="/wiki/Chances_Peak" title="Chances Peak">\n        Chances Peak\n       </a>\n       , destroying 21\xc2\xa0homes. Ten people were killed, 89\xc2\xa0were injured, and damage reached at least $260\xc2\xa0million, making it the most expensive hurricane in the history of Montserrat. Tourism and agriculture also suffered significantly.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Additionally, the local bat population was devastated, with an estimated 90 percent decrease in numbers after Hugo\'s passage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-cwmontserrat_10-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-cwmontserrat-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-washingtonpost_11-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-washingtonpost-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The species\n       <i>\n        <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Chiroderma_improvisum" title="Chiroderma improvisum">\n         Chiroderma improvisum\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       has not been seen on Montserrat since, and it is feared that it may be extinct on the island.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-montserratbats_12-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-montserratbats-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h4>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="United_States_Virgin_Islands">\n        United States Virgin Islands\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Hugo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: United States Virgin Islands">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h4>\n      <p>\n       The slower speed allowed Hugo to punish the island of\n       <a href="/wiki/Saint_Croix,_U.S._Virgin_Islands" title="Saint Croix, U.S. Virgin Islands">\n        St. Croix\n       </a>\n       with the worst beating of any location along the hurricane\'s destructive path. At 2 am local time on September 18, Hugo\'s eyewall struck St. Croix, bringing Category 4 winds, sustained at 140\xc2\xa0mph (220\xc2\xa0km/h). The hurricane\'s gusts were remarkably violent, and many residents witnessed tornado-like vortices barreling across the island as the hurricane raged about them. A storm surge of 2\xe2\x80\x933\xc2\xa0ft (0.61\xe2\x80\x930.91\xc2\xa0m), topped by battering waves 20\xe2\x80\x9323\xc2\xa0ft (6.1\xe2\x80\x937.0\xc2\xa0m) high, assaulted the coast, adding to the destruction. Two people were killed on St. Croix, 80 injured, and 90% of the buildings were damaged or destroyed. Damage estimates for St. Croix were astronomical, over $1 billion, and the island\'s entire infrastructure was virtually wiped out. Six weeks after the hurricane, only 25% of the public roads had been cleared, and only 25% of the island had power."\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hugo:_Day_10_13-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Hugo:_Day_10-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       On the island of\n       <a href="/wiki/Saint_Thomas,_U.S._Virgin_Islands" title="Saint Thomas, U.S. Virgin Islands">\n        St. Thomas\n       </a>\n       , the damage was bad but not nearly as extensive. Television station\n       <a href="/wiki/WBNB-TV" title="WBNB-TV">\n        WBNB-TV\n       </a>\n       channel 10 (the local\n       <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">\n        CBS\n       </a>\n       affiliate) had its transmitter completely destroyed by Hurricane Hugo. The station would not return to the air, because of the damages, and the owners\' inability to afford repairs. Many business were destroyed. West Indies Transport had operated several businesses in Krum Bay, St. Thomas, including a dry dock, a ship repair facility, and a barge towing company. In UNITED STATES v. WEST INDIES TRANSPORT INC 96 7063 WIT 96 7064 96 7065 it was written that "Hurricane Hugo seriously damaged some of the barges, shifting them from their permitted positions. West Indies Transport did not attempt to repair, reposition, or salvage these barges after the storm. Instead, it used these barges as docks, repair facilities, and housing for employees in their new unauthorized locations."\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14">\n        <a href="#cite_note-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The company officers were charged and convicted of environmental and other crimes and ordered to pay restitution in excess of caused $7\xc2\xa0billion in damage in the United States, surpassing\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Frederic" title="Hurricane Frederic">\n        Hurricane Frederic\n       </a>\n       of\n       <a href="/wiki/1979_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1979 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1979\n       </a>\n       as the costliest tropical cyclone in that country. Hugo itself was surpassed by\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Andrew" title="Hurricane Andrew">\n        Hurricane Andrew\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/1992_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1992 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1992\n       </a>\n       and several others over the next few decades.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17">\n        <a href="#cite_note-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       South Carolina suffered the worst impact, where Hugo made landfall as a Category\xc2\xa04 hurricane. In that state alone, 3,307\xc2\xa0single family homes were destroyed, 18,171\xc2\xa0were inflicted major damage, and 56,580\xc2\xa0sustained minor impact, while more than 12,600\xc2\xa0mobile homes and 18,000\xc2\xa0multi\xe2\x80\x93family houses were either damaged or destroyed.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-redcross_18-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-redcross-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Losses reached at least $5.9\xc2\xa0billion and there were thirty-five fatalities reported in South Carolina.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USGS_19-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-USGS-19">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata_20-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-stormdata-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       There was also significant impact in North Carolina, where 205\xc2\xa0structures were destroyed, 1,149\xc2\xa0were severely damaged, and 2,638\xc2\xa0suffered minor losses.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata_20-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-stormdata-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       One fatality occurred and damage in North Carolina reached about $1\xc2\xa0billion.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NCHazard_21-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-NCHazard-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h4>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Florida_and_Georgia">\n        Florida and Georgia\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Hugo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Florida and Georgia">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h4>\n      <p>\n       Impact in Florida was minimal. The highest sustained winds in the state reached only 21\xc2\xa0mph (34\xc2\xa0km/h) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Jacksonville,_Florida" title="Jacksonville, Florida">\n        Jacksonville\n       </a>\n       . At the same location, a trace of rainfall was also reported. Conditions were even lesser in\n       <a href="/wiki/St._Augustine,_Florida" title="St. Augustine, Florida">\n        St. Augustine\n       </a>\n       , though wind gusts were slightly higher than in Jacksonville. To the north in Georgia, minor storm surge was reported, with the highest measured as 1.4 feet (0.43\xc2\xa0m) above normal in\n       <a href="/wiki/Fort_Pulaski_National_Monument" title="Fort Pulaski National Monument">\n        Fort Pulaski\n       </a>\n       . Sustained winds of 58\xc2\xa0mph (93\xc2\xa0km/h) was reported at the\n       <a href="/wiki/Tybee_Island_Light" title="Tybee Island Light">\n        Tybee Island Lighthouse\n       </a>\n       , and this was the only record of tropical storm force winds in Georgia. Near tropical storm force winds were measured at the\n       <a href="/wiki/Savannah/Hilton_Head_International_Airport" title="Savannah/Hilton Head International Airport">\n        Savannah/Hilton Head International Airport\n       </a>\n       , along with 6.1 inches (150\xc2\xa0mm) of rain. High winds in\n       <a href="/wiki/Brunswick,_Georgia" title="Brunswick, Georgia">\n        Brunswick\n       </a>\n       downed five trees, one of which struck a house. Additionally, winds felled at least 50\xc2\xa0trees in the Savannah area, and two homes were struck. Scattered downed tree limbs were also reported in\n       <a href="/wiki/Hinesville,_Georgia" title="Hinesville, Georgia">\n        Hinesville\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Bryan_County,_Georgia" title="Bryan County, Georgia">\n        Bryan County\n       </a>\n       , though no damage occurred.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stormdata_20-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-stormdata-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h4>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="South_Carolina">\n        South Carolina\n  <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">\n        <a href="#cite_note-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner" A portion of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Ben_Sawyer_Bridge" title="Ben Sawyer Bridge">\n        Ben Sawyer Bridge\n       </a>\n       \xe2\x80\x93 linking\n       <a href="/wiki/Mount_Pleasant,_South_Carolina" title="Mount Pleasant, South Carolina">\n        Mount Pleasant\n       </a>\n       to\n       <a href="/wiki/Sullivan%27s_Island,_South_Carolina" title="Sullivan\'s Island, South Carolina">\n        Sullivan\'s Island\n       </a>\n       \xe2\x80\x93 collapsed due to strong winds and storm surge. The Red Cross estimates that 3,307\xc2\xa0single family homes were destroyed, 18,171\xc2\xa0were inflicted major damage, and 56,580\xc2\xa0sustained minor impact. Additionally, more than 12,600\xc2\xa0mobile homes and 18,000\xc2\xa0multi-family houses were either damaged or destroyed.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-redcross_18-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-redcross-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Damage from Hurricane Hugo in South Carolina was estimated at $5.9\xc2\xa0billion.\n       <sup class="reference" The ship had much damage to the public parts of the vessel, including the shops and the bridge; the swimming pools were all emptied to prevent large amounts of water shifting precariously as the ship pitched and tossed. Hugo moved quickly through western Virginia, West Virginia, and eastern Ohio to Erie, Pennsylvania. By the evening of September 22, it became an\n       <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">\n        extratropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       . Hugo caused schools in southwest Virginia to be closed for more than two weeks because of the wind and flooding damage. In the central portions of the state, winds downed several trees. The last death in relation to Hurricane Hugo occurred near\n       <a href="/wiki/Buffalo,_New_York" title="Buffalo, New York">\n        Buffalo\n       </a>\n       when the winds toppled a tree onto a motorist. The storm then moved northeastward across eastern Canada into the far North Atlantic Ocean.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Hugo:_Day_14_29-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Hugo:_Day_14-29">\n         [29]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h4>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="New_England">\n        New England\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Hugo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: New England">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h4>\n      <p>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Connecticut" title="Connecticut">\n        Connecticut\n       </a>\n       , wind gusts associated with the remnants of Hugo peaked at 49\xc2\xa0mph (79\xc2\xa0km/h) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Bridgeport,_Connecticut" title="Bridgeport, Connecticut">\n        Bridgeport\n       </a>\n       . The winds caused minor property damage, while falling trees and tree limbs briefly left up to 30,000\xc2\xa0customers without electricity. Gusty winds in\n       <a href="/wiki/Massachusetts" title="Massachusetts">\n        Massachusetts\n       </a>\n       left several thousand people without power, while also toppling trees and tree limbs, which caused minor damage to houses and cars.
