</tr>\n      </table>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        Tropical Storm Grace\n       </b>\n       was a weak\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        tropical storm\n       </a>\n       that struck\n       <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">\n        Texas\n       </a>\n       in the\n       <a href="/wiki/2003_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="2003 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        2003 Atlantic hurricane season\n       </a>\n       . The eleventh tropical depression and the seventh tropical storm of the season, Grace was also the weakest storm of the season. On August 30 the storm developed from a long-track\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">\n        tropical wave\n       </a>\n       in the western\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">\n        Gulf of Mexico\n       </a>\n       . Grace remained disorganized throughout its lifetime due to an upper level low to its west. The weak storm moved northwestward and made landfall on southeastern Texas. Grace quickly weakened over land, and dissipated on September 2 as it merged into a\n       <a href="/wiki/Cold_front" title="Cold front">\n        cold front\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In Texas, the storm dropped heavy rainfall, causing minor flash flooding damage. The cold front, combined with the remnants of the storm, dropped moderate to heavy rainfall from Texas through the Mid-Atlantic. The worst of the flooding occurred in\n       <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis" title="Indianapolis">\n        Indianapolis\n       </a>\n       , where record rainfall affected over 700 homes. Despite the rainfall, damage was minimal along its path.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n      </p>\n      <div class="toc" id="toc">\n       <div class="toctitle">\n        <h2>\n         Contents\n class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Grace_2003_track.png" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Map plotting the track and intensity of the storm, according to the\n         <a href="/wiki/Saffir%E2%80%93Simpson_scale" title="Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale">\n          Saffir\xe2\x80\x93Simpson scale\n         </a>\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       A strong\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_wave" title="Tropical wave">\n        tropical wave\n       </a>\n       accompanied with a low pressure system moved off the coast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">\n        Africa\n       </a>\n       on August 19. It moved quickly westward and organized, developing banding features and cirrus outflow near the blossoming convection. The wave nearly developed into a tropical cyclone on August 21, though its fast forward motion dislocated the low level circulation from the deep convection. The wave entered an area of dry air in the central\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">\n        Atlantic Ocean\n       </a>\n       , and by late on August 22 most of the convection dissipated from the system. On August 24, convection increased as the wave passed through the\n       <a href="/wiki/Lesser_Antilles" title="Lesser Antilles">\n        Lesser Antilles\n       </a>\n       , though strong southwesterly upper level\n       <a href="/wiki/Wind_shear" title="Wind shear">\n        wind shear\n       </a>\n       prevented further development. The tropical wave moved through the\n       <a href="/wiki/Caribbean_Sea" title="Caribbean Sea">\n        Caribbean Sea\n       </a>\n       , and developed deep convection due to more favorable conditions over the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Honduras" title="Gulf of Honduras">\n        Gulf of Honduras\n       </a>\n       on August 28. The wave crossed the\n       <a href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula" title="Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula">\n        Yucat\xc3\xa1n Peninsula\n       </a>\n       , and developed a surface low pressure area on the 29th in the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">\n        Gulf of Mexico\n       </a>\n       . Convection continued to organize, and the tropical wave developed into Tropical Depression Eleven on August 30 while located 335\xc2\xa0miles (540\xc2\xa0km) east-southeast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Corpus_Christi,_Texas" title="Corpus Christi, Texas">\n        Corpus Christi, Texas\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The depression moved to the northwest, and intensified into Tropical Storm Grace six hours after forming. Despite the increase in winds the center of circulation remained very broad, with\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Reconnaissance_Aircraft" title="Reconnaissance Aircraft">\n        Reconnaissance Aircraft\n       </a>\n       having difficulty pinpointing the center. In addition, an upper level low located over\n       <a href="/wiki/Brownsville,_Texas" title="Brownsville, Texas">\n        Brownsville, Texas\n       </a>\n       produced shear over the western portion of the system, which limited outflow to the east side. Forecasters predicted the upper level low to weaken, allowing for the possibility of Grace to intensify to a 65\xc2\xa0mph (100\xc2\xa0km/h) tropical storm over warm waters.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-gracedisc2_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-gracedisc2-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       However, the upper level low remained in place and continued to produce shear across the system. A new center of circulation reformed 115\xc2\xa0miles (185\xc2\xa0km) north of the original center, and made landfall near San Luis Pass on the southwestern portion of\n       <a href="/wiki/Galveston_Island" title="Galveston Island">\n        Galveston Island\n       </a>\n       on August 31 as a minimal tropical storm. Grace quickly weakened to a tropical depression over land, and after turning northeastward into\n       <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma" title="Oklahoma">\n        Oklahoma\n       </a>\n       the depression was absorbed by a\n       <a href="/wiki/Cold_front" title="Cold front">\n        cold front\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">\n        Preparations\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_Storm_Grace_(2003)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Three hours after forming, officials issued\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">\n        Tropical Storm Warnings\n       </a>\n       from\n       <a href="/wiki/High_Island,_Texas" title="High Island, Texas">\n        High Island\n       </a>\n       to\n       <a href="/wiki/Corpus_Christi,_Texas" title="Corpus Christi, Texas">\n        Corpus Christi, Texas\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Local\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Weather_Service" title="National Weather Service">\n        National Weather Service\n       </a>\n       offices requested a voluntary evacuation for western\n       <a href="/wiki/Galveston_Island" title="Galveston Island">\n        Galveston Island\n       </a>\n       including\n       <a href="/wiki/Jamaica_Beach,_Texas" title="Jamaica Beach, Texas">\n        Jamaica Beach\n       </a>\n       , the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Bolivar_Peninsula" title="Bolivar Peninsula">\n        Bolivar Peninsula\n       </a>\n       , and coastal areas of\n       <a href="/wiki/Brazoria_County,_Texas" title="Brazoria County, Texas">\n        Brazoria\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Matagorda_County,_Texas" title="Matagorda County, Texas">\n        Matagorda counties\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-srhtex_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-srhtex-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       though few residents heeded the warnings. Local emergency management officials predicted tides of up to 5\xc2\xa0feet (1.5\xc2\xa0m) above normal with coastal flooding.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jambeach_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-jambeach-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">\n        Impact\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_Storm_Grace_(2003)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Impact">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Grace_2003_rainfall.gif">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="842" data-file-width="940" height="269" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/Grace_2003_rainfall.gif/300px-Grace_2003_rainfall.gif" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/Grace_2003_rainfall.gif/450px-Grace_2003_rainfall.gif 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/Grace_2003_rainfall.gif/600px-Grace_2003_rainfall.gif 2x" width="300"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Grace_2003_rainfall.gif" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Rainfall totals for Grace\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Grace produced moderate amounts of rainfall along its path, peaking in southern\n       <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">\n        Texas\n       </a>\n       . The storm later merged with a cold front, which later dropped heavy rainfall in the Mid-West\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">\n        United States\n       </a>\n       . In addition to its impact on the United States, the outer\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rainbands" title="Rainbands">\n        rainbands\n       </a>\n       of Grace caused light rainfall in\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_(state)" title="Yucat\xc3\xa1n (state)">\n        Yucat\xc3\xa1n\n       </a>\n       and northern\n       <a href="/wiki/Tamaulipas" title="Tamaulipas">\n        Tamaulipas\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="South-Central_United_States">\n        South-Central United States\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_Storm_Grace_(2003)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: South-Central United States">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Upon making landfall, Tropical Storm Grace produced a light storm surge of 3.5\xc2\xa0feet (1\xc2\xa0m) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Matagorda_Island" title="Matagorda Island">\n        Matagorda\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=North_Jetty,_Texas&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="North Jetty, Texas (page does not exist)">\n        North Jetty\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">\n        Texas\n       </a>\n       . Wind gusts peaked at 53\xc2\xa0mph (85\xc2\xa0km/h) with sustained winds of 40\xc2\xa0mph (64\xc2\xa0km/h) at\n       <a href="/wiki/Sea_Rim_State_Park" title="Sea Rim State Park">\n        Sea Rim State Park\n       </a>\n       . Locations closer to where the storm made landfall reported below tropical storm force winds, with the exception of\n       <a href="/wiki/Galveston,_Texas" title="Galveston, Texas">\n        Galveston\n       </a>\n       which recorded a 40\xc2\xa0mph (64\xc2\xa0km/h) wind gust.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-tcr_1-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-tcr-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Rainfall was moderate to heavy across eastern\n       <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">\n        Texas\n       </a>\n       , peaking at 10.36\xc2\xa0inches (263\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Spindletop" title="Spindletop">\n        Spindletop Bayou\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rain_6-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rain-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The outer bands of Grace spawned a\n       <a href="/wiki/Waterspout" title="Waterspout">\n        waterspout\n       </a>\n       just south of the western tip of\n       <a href="/wiki/Galveston_Island" title="Galveston Island">\n        Galveston Island\n       </a>\n       , prompting the issuance of a\n       <a href="/wiki/Tornado_warning" title="Tornado warning">\n        tornado warning\n       </a>\n       . The waterspout dissipated before moving onshore. The storm also caused light beach erosion, though little occurred beyond the erosion caused by\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Claudette_(2003)" title="Hurricane Claudette (2003)">\n        Hurricane Claudette\n       </a>\n       one month prior.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-srhtex_3-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-srhtex-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Near the coast, high tides from the storm flooded piers, bulkheads, and low-lying areas.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jambeach_4-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-jambeach-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Further inland, heavy rainfall lead to flash flooding, covering roads and entering a few houses. Overall, damage was minor, and totaled to $113,000 (2003\xc2\xa0USD, $147,100 2016\xc2\xa0USD).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdc1_7-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ncdc1-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ncdc2_8-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-ncdc2-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Oklahoma" title="Oklahoma">\n        Oklahoma\n       </a>\n       , moisture from the storm, combined with a slow-moving cold front, produced heavy rainfall across the state, peaking at 8.98\xc2\xa0inches (228\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a class="new" href="/w/index.php?title=Courtney,_Oklahoma&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" title="Courtney, Oklahoma (page does not exist)">\n        Courtney\n       </a>\n       . Due to below normal precipitation by as much as 5 to 10\xc2\xa0inches (127 to 255\xc2\xa0mm), flooding was localized and overall minimal. Near\n       <a href="/wiki/Medford,_Oklahoma" title="Medford, Oklahoma">\n        Medford\n       </a>\n       , the rainfall lead to 2\xc2\xa0feet (0.6\xc2\xa0m) deep flooding on\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._Highway_81" title="U.S. Highway 81">\n        U.S. Highway 81\n       </a>\n       , forcing its closure. The rainfall was welcome in the state, and lessened the rainfall deficit.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oklanws_9-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-oklanws-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Tropical Storm Grace, combined with the slow-moving cold front, produced light to moderate rainfall totals across the southern United States, including isolated locations in\n       <a href="/wiki/Louisiana" title="Louisiana">\n        Louisiana\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Mississippi" title="Mississippi">\n        Mississippi\n       </a>\n       reporting over 3\xc2\xa0inches (76\xc2\xa0mm) and over 5\xc2\xa0inches (127\xc2\xa0mm) in northeastern\n       <a href="/wiki/Arkansas" title="Arkansas">\n        Arkansas\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rain_6-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rain-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Ohio_Valley_and_Eastern_United_States">\n        Ohio Valley and Eastern United States\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tropical_Storm_Grace_(2003)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Ohio Valley and Eastern United States">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Missouri" title="Missouri">\n        Missouri\n       </a>\n       , the cold front combined with moisture from the remnants of Grace brought temporary relief to a severe drought\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-missdrought_10-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-missdrought-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       by producing light to moderate rainfall of up to 5\xc2\xa0inches (127\xc2\xa0mm) in the southeastern portion of the state.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rain_6-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rain-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In\n       <a href="/wiki/Poplar_Bluff,_Missouri" title="Poplar Bluff, Missouri">\n        Poplar Bluff\n       </a>\n       , the rainfall caused severe flooding, resulting in rescues for people in trapped vehicles.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-paducnws_11-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-paducnws-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The moisture produced over 5\xc2\xa0inches (127\xc2\xa0mm) in northern\n       <a href="/wiki/Kentucky" title="Kentucky">\n        Kentucky\n       </a>\n       , as well.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rain_6-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rain-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Moisture from the remnants of Grace dropped heavy rainfall across central\n       <a href="/wiki/Indiana" title="Indiana">\n        Indiana\n       </a>\n       , including a record one-day total of 7\xc2\xa0inches (178\xc2\xa0mm) in\n       <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis" title="Indianapolis">\n        Indianapolis\n       </a>\n       , while other locations received over 9\xc2\xa0inches (228\xc2\xa0mm). Residents prepared sand bags to prevent overflowing rivers and creeks, though rising waters entered streets and over 700 homes. The rapid rainfall in Indianapolis backed up the sewage system, sending hundreds of millions of gallons of sewage into the streets. Following the deluge in the state, the governor declared a state of emergency for the state. Eight local\n       <a href="/wiki/American_Red_Cross" title="American Red Cross">\n        American Red Cross\n       </a>\n       chapters arrived to provide meals and aid to the affected people.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-indian_12-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-indian-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The remnants of Grace dropped moderate to heavy rainfall eastward through the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Mid-Atlantic_States" title="Mid-Atlantic States">\n        Mid-Atlantic States\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/New_England" title="New England">\n        New England\n       </a>\n       . Locations in extreme western\n       <a href="/wiki/Maryland" title="Maryland">\n        Maryland\n       </a>\n       and southeastern\n       <a href="/wiki/Virginia" title="Virginia">\n        Virginia\n       </a>\n       received over 5\xc2\xa0inches (127\xc2\xa0mm) of rain.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rain_6-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-rain-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In Maryland,\n       <a href="/wiki/Hagerstown,_Maryland" title="Hagerstown, Maryland">\n        Hagerstown\n       </a>\n       recorded 3.94\xc2\xa0inches (100\xc2\xa0mm), resulting in flash flooding. In\n       <a href="/wiki/Washington_County,_Maryland" title="Washington County, Maryland">\n        Washington County\n       </a>\n       , the system produced 1.09\xc2\xa0inches (27.8\xc2\xa0mm) of rain, a new daily record. Damage in Maryland, if any, was unknown.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-marland_13-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-marland-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n
