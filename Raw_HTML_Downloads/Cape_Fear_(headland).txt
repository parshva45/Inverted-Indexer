class="thumbimage" data-file-height="210" data-file-width="300" height="210" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/6/6b/Cape_Fear_Topo.jpg" width="300"/>\n        </a>\n        <div coast of North Carolina.\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Nasa_photo_cape_fear.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="550" data-file-width="600" height="275" </a>\n         </div>\n         Cape Fear in a NASA satellite photo, showing the estuary of the Cape Fear River.\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       <b>\n        Cape Fear\n       </b>\n       is a prominent\n       <a href="/wiki/Headland" title="Headland">\n        headland\n       </a>\n       jutting into the\n       <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">\n        Atlantic Ocean\n       </a>\n       from\n       <a href="/wiki/Bald_Head_Island,_North_Carolina" title="Bald Head Island, North Carolina">\n        Bald Head Island\n       </a>\n       on the coast of\n       <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">\n        North Carolina\n       </a>\n       in the southeastern\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">\n        United States\n       </a>\n       . It is largely formed of barrier beaches and the silty outwash of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Cape_Fear_River" title="Cape Fear River">\n        Cape Fear River\n       </a>\n       as it drains the southeast coast of North Carolina through an\n       <a href="/wiki/Estuary" title="Estuary">\n        estuary\n       </a>\n       south of\n       <a href="/wiki/Wilmington,_North_Carolina" title="Wilmington, North Carolina">\n        Wilmington\n       </a>\n       . Cape Fear is formed by the intersection of two sweeping arcs of shifting, low-lying beach, the result of\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Longshore_currents" title="Longshore currents">\n        longshore currents\n       </a>\n       which also form the treacherous, shifting\n       <a href="/wiki/Frying_Pan_Shoals" title="Frying Pan Shoals">\n        Frying Pan Shoals\n       </a>\n       , part of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Graveyard_of_the_Atlantic" title="Graveyard of the Atlantic">\n        Graveyard of the Atlantic\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <a href="/wiki/Dune" title="Dune">\n        Dunes\n       </a>\n       dominated by\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sea_oat" title="Sea oat">\n        sea oats\n       </a>\n       occur from the upper beach driftline back to the stable secondary dunes, where they mix with other grasses such as\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Saltmeadow_Cordgrass" title="Saltmeadow Cordgrass">\n        Saltmeadow Cordgrass\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Panic_grass" title="Panic grass">\n        panic grass\n       </a>\n       , as well as\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Seaside_goldenrod" title="Seaside goldenrod">\n        seaside goldenrod\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Spurge" title="Spurge">\n        spurge\n       </a>\n       and other herbs to form a stable salt-tolerant\n       <a href="/wiki/Grassland" title="Grassland">\n        grassland\n       </a>\n       .\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The Cape Fear estuary drains the largest watershed in North Carolina, containing 27% of the state\'s population.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <a href="/wiki/Giovanni_da_Verrazzano" title="Giovanni da Verrazzano">\n        Giovanni da Verrazzano\n       </a>\n       , the Italian explorer sailing for\n       <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">\n        France\n       </a>\n       , made landfall after crossing the Atlantic at or near Cape Fear on March 1, 1524.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The name comes from the 1585 expedition of Sir\n       <a href="/wiki/Richard_Grenville" title="Richard Grenville">\n        Richard Grenville\n       </a>\n       . Sailing to\n       <a href="/wiki/Roanoke_Island" title="Roanoke Island">\n        Roanoke Island\n       </a>\n       , his ship became embayed behind the cape. Some of the crew were afraid they would wreck, giving rise to the name Cape Fear.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       It is the fifth-oldest surviving English place name in the U.S.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Cape Fear was the landing place of General\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Henry_Clinton_(1730%E2%80%931795)" title="Henry Clinton (1730\xe2\x80\x931795)">\n        Sir Henry Clinton\n       </a>\n       during the\n       <a href="/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War" title="American Revolutionary War">\n        American Revolutionary War\n       </a>\n       on May 3, 1775. The 1962 film\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Cape_Fear_(1962_film)" title="Cape Fear (1962 film)">\n         Cape Fear\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       and its\n       <a href="/wiki/Cape_Fear_(1991_film)" title="Cape Fear (1991 film)">\n        1991 remake\n       </a>\n       were set at Cape Fear.\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline"
