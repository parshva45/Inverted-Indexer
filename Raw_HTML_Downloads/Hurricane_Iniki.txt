It moved westward across the unfavorable Atlantic Ocean and crossed\n       <a href="/wiki/Central_America" title="Central America">\n        Central America\n       </a>\n       into the Pacific on the August 28. The wave continued rapidly westward and remained disorganized. Conditions slowly became more favorable, and, as the convection concentrated around a center, the wave was classified Tropical Depression Eighteen-E on September 5. At this time, the wave was located 1700\xc2\xa0miles (2700\xc2\xa0km) southwest of\n       <a href="/wiki/Cabo_San_Lucas" title="Cabo San Lucas">\n        Cabo San Lucas\n       </a>\n       or 1550\xc2\xa0miles (2500\xc2\xa0km) east-southeast of\n       <a href="/wiki/Hilo,_Hawaii" title="Hilo, Hawaii">\n        Hilo\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CPHC_1992_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CPHC_1992-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Initially, the thunderstorm activity was not concentrated towards the center and thus the depression was not expected to intensify beyond minimal tropical storm strength.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The depression continued quickly westward and remained weak until September 8, when it slowed enough to strengthen to a tropical storm.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CPHC_1992_1-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CPHC_1992-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Iniki_08_sept_1992_0256Z.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="909" data-file-width="909" height="220" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c7/Iniki_08_sept_1992_0256Z.jpg/220px-Iniki_08_sept_1992_0256Z.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c7/Iniki_08_sept_1992_0256Z.jpg/330px-Iniki_08_sept_1992_0256Z.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c7/Iniki_08_sept_1992_0256Z.jpg/440px-Iniki_08_sept_1992_0256Z.jpg 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Iniki_08_sept_1992_0256Z.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Iniki (left) as a newly formed tropical storm next to\n         <a href="/wiki/1992_Pacific_hurricane_season#Hurricane_Orlene" title="1992 Pacific hurricane season">\n          Hurricane Orlene\n         </a>\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Located at the southern periphery of a\n       <a href="/wiki/Subtropical_ridge" title="Subtropical ridge">\n        subtropical ridge\n       </a>\n       , Iniki continued westward and strengthened over the unusually favorable central Pacific; it reached hurricane status on September 9 while 470\xc2\xa0miles (760\xc2\xa0km) south-southeast of Hilo. The subtropical ridge, which typically keeps hurricanes well away from the Hawaiian Islands, weakened due to an approaching upper level-trough and allowed Iniki to turn to the northwest. With very favorable upper-level outflow and warm water temperatures, Iniki steadily intensified, and attained major hurricane status on September 10 while south-southwest of the island chain.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CPHC_1992_1-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CPHC_1992-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       As Iniki turned to the north, it continued to strengthen, reaching a peak of 145\xc2\xa0mph (235\xc2\xa0km/h) winds on September 11 while 170\xc2\xa0miles (270\xc2\xa0km) south-southwest of\n       <a href="/wiki/Poipu,_Hawaii" title="Poipu, Hawaii">\n        Po\n        <span class="unicode">\n         \xca\xbb\n        </span>\n        ip\xc5\xab\n       </a>\n       on the island of Kaua\n       <span class="unicode">\n        \xca\xbb\n       </span>\n       i. It continued rapidly to the north-northeast, and made landfall on south-central Kauai early on the 11th with sustained winds of 145\xc2\xa0mph (235\xc2\xa0km/h), making Iniki a Category\xc2\xa04 hurricane on the Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale. The National Weather Service reported wind gusts of up to 175\xc2\xa0mph (280\xc2\xa0km/h). The highest recorded wind speed from Hurricane Iniki was a 227\xc2\xa0mph (365\xc2\xa0km/h) reading\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       from the Navy\'s Makaha Ridge radar station. According to the Honolulu Advertiser, that remarkable figure was recorded at a digital weather station whose wind gauging equipment blew off after taking the measurement during the storm. After crossing the island, Iniki rapidly accelerated north-northeastward, weakened rapidly, and was absorbed by a cold front as it lost tropical characteristics and became\n       <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">\n        extratropical\n       </a>\n       on September 13 about halfway between\n       <a href="/wiki/Alaska" title="Alaska">\n        Alaska\n       </a>\n       and Hawai\n       <span class="unicode">\n        \xca\xbb\n       </span>\n       i.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CPHC_1992_1-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CPHC_1992-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">\n        Preparations\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Iniki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       The\n       <a href="/wiki/Central_Pacific_Hurricane_Center" title="Central Pacific Hurricane Center">\n        Central Pacific Hurricane Center\n       </a>\n       (CPHC) failed to issue\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">\n        tropical cyclone warnings and watches\n       </a>\n       for the hurricane well in advance. For several days prior to the disaster, the CPHC and the news media forecast Iniki to remain well south of the island chain, with the only effect being some high surf conditions. Some of the standard international computer models were indicating a northward turn towards the populated Hawaiian Islands, but these were rejected by the CPHC forecasters. As late as early September 10, the CPHC suggested that Iniki would remain well to the south of the island group. It was not until a special bulletin was issued by the CPHC less than 24\xc2\xa0hours before landfall\xe2\x80\x94that any warning was given to the public.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-report_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-report-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       A hurricane watch was issued for Kaua\n       <span class="unicode">\n        \xca\xbb\n       </span>\n       i early on September 11 and was upgraded to a hurricane warning later that day.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CPHC_1992_1-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CPHC_1992-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Prior to Iniki\'s arrival in Kaua\n       <span class="unicode">\n        \xca\xbb\n       </span>\n       i, 8,000\xc2\xa0people were housed in shelters, many of whom remembered\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Iwa" title="Hurricane Iwa">\n        Hurricane Iwa\n       </a>\n       10\xc2\xa0years prior.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-assessment_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-assessment-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Because schools were canceled, traffic was light during the evacuation, and streets were clear by mid-morning. Rather than sending tourists to public shelters, two major hotels kept their occupants in the buildings during the storm\'s passage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-assessment_5-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-assessment-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The CPHC issued a tropical storm warning for\n       <a href="/wiki/Oahu" title="Oahu">\n        O\n        <span class="unicode">\n         \xca\xbb\n        </span>\n        ahu\n       </a>\n       on September 11 which was upgraded to a hurricane warning later that day.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CPHC_1992_1-6">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CPHC_1992-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Though not hit by the hurricane, Iniki\'s large wind field caused nearly 30,000\xc2\xa0people to evacuate to 110\xc2\xa0public shelters in O\n       <span class="unicode">\n        \xca\xbb\n       </span>\n       ahu. Public school buildings acted as shelters, and were for refuge only, meaning they did not provide food, cots, blankets, medications or other comfort items. Roughly one-third of O\n       <span class="unicode">\n        \xca\xbb\n       </span>\n       ahu\'s population participated in the evacuation, though many others went to the house of a family member or friend for shelter. The execution of the evacuations went well, beginning with the vulnerable coastal area. For those in need, vans and buses gave emergency transportation, while police manned certain overused intersections. The two main problems that occurred during the evacuation were lack of parking at shelters and exit routes for the coastlines.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-assessment_5-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-assessment-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">\n        Impact\n       </span>\n       <span Hurricane Iniki was the costliest hurricane to strike the state of Hawai\n       <span class="unicode">\n        \xca\xbb\n       </span>\n       i, causing $1.8\xc2\xa0billion in damage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-nhccost_6-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-nhccost-6">\n         [6]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Most damage was on the island of Kaua\n       <span class="unicode">\n        \xca\xbb\n       </span>\n       i, where the storm destroyed thousands of homes and left a large amount of the island without power, although O\n       <span class="unicode">\n        \xca\xbb\n       </span>\n       ahu also suffered significant damage. Iniki also was responsible for six deaths.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CPHC_1992_1-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CPHC_1992-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The hurricane nearly struck the Central Pacific Hurricane Center in\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Honolulu,_Hawaii" title="Honolulu, Hawaii">\n        Honolulu\n       </a>\n       . Had it hit there, Iniki, along with\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Andrew" title="Hurricane Andrew">\n        Hurricane Andrew\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Typhoon_Omar" title="Typhoon Omar">\n        Typhoon Omar\n       </a>\n       , would have struck each of the three\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Weather_Service" title="National Weather Service">\n        National Weather Service\n       </a>\n       offices responsible for tropical cyclone warnings within a two-month period.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-report_4-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-report-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span id="Kaua\xca\xbbi">\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Kaua.CA.BBi">\n        Kaua\n        <span class="unicode">\n         \xca\xbb\n        </span>\n        i\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Iniki&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Kaua\xca\xbbi">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Hurricane Iniki made landfall on the south-central portion of Kaua\n       <span class="unicode">\n        \xca\xbb\n       </span>\n       i island, bringing its dangerous inner core to the entire island.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CPHC_1992_1-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CPHC_1992-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Upon making landfall the hurricane produced storm tides of 4.5\xe2\x80\x936\xc2\xa0feet (1.4\xe2\x80\x931.8\xc2\xa0m), with some portions of the coastlines having high-water marks of up to 18\xc2\xa0feet (5.5\xc2\xa0m). In addition, strong waves of up to 35\xc2\xa0feet (10.5\xc2\xa0m) in height crashed along the southern coastline for several hours, causing a debris line of more than 800\xc2\xa0feet (250\xc2\xa0m) inland. Because it moved quickly through the island, there were no reports of significant rainfall.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-assessment_5-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-assessment-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Iniki.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="540" data-file-width="720" height="165" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a3/Hurricane_Iniki.jpg/220px-Hurricane_Iniki.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a3/Hurricane_Iniki.jpg/330px-Hurricane_Iniki.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a3/Hurricane_Iniki.jpg/440px-Hurricane_Iniki.jpg 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Hurricane_Iniki.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Hurricane Iniki making landfall on Kaua\n         <span class="unicode">\n          \xca\xbb\n         </span>\n         i\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Hurricane Iniki\'s making landfall during daylight hours, combined with the popularity of camcorders, led many Kaua\n       <span class="unicode">\n        \xca\xbb\n       </span>\n       i residents to record much of the damage as it was occurring. The footage was later used to create an hour-long video documentary.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7">\n        <a href="#cite_note-7">\n         [7]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Commercial air service was suspended.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tleft">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Iniki_Tree_Winds.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="334" data-file-width="490" height="150" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Iniki_Tree_Winds.jpg/220px-Iniki_Tree_Winds.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Iniki_Tree_Winds.jpg/330px-Iniki_Tree_Winds.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Iniki_Tree_Winds.jpg/440px-Iniki_Tree_Winds.jpg 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Iniki_Tree_Winds.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Wind damage to trees from Iniki\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Hurricane Iniki\'s high winds caused extensive damage in Kaua\n       <span class="unicode">\n        \xca\xbb\n       </span>\n       i. 1,421\xc2\xa0houses were destroyed, and 63 were lost from the storm surge and wave action. A total of 5,152\xc2\xa0homes were severely damaged, while 7,178 received minor damage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CPHC_1992_1-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CPHC_1992-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On the south coast, hotels and condominiums received severe damage as well. A few were restored quickly, though some took several years to be rebuilt. One hotel\xe2\x80\x94the\n       <a href="/wiki/Coco_Palms_Resort" title="Coco Palms Resort">\n        Coco Palms Resort\n       </a>\n       famous for Elvis Presley\'s\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Blue_Hawaii" title="Blue Hawaii">\n         Blue Hawaii\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       \xe2\x80\x94never reopened after the hurricane.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kauai_9-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-kauai-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Destroyed housing across the island left more than 7,000\xc2\xa0people homeless after the storm\'s passage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stats_10-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-stats-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Iniki_Damaged_Sidewalk.jpg">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="350" data-file-width="525" height="147" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/Iniki_Damaged_Sidewalk.jpg/220px-Iniki_Damaged_Sidewalk.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/Iniki_Damaged_Sidewalk.jpg/330px-Iniki_Damaged_Sidewalk.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/73/Iniki_Damaged_Sidewalk.jpg/440px-Iniki_Damaged_Sidewalk.jpg 2x" width="220"/>\n        </a>\n        <div class="thumbcaption">\n         <div class="magnify">\n          <a class="internal" href="/wiki/File:Iniki_Damaged_Sidewalk.jpg" title="Enlarge">\n          </a>\n         </div>\n         Sidewalk and tree damage from Iniki\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Iniki\'s high winds also downed 26.5% of the island\'s transmission poles, 37% of its distribution poles, and 35% of its 800\xc2\xa0mile (1300\xc2\xa0km) distribution wire system. The entire island lacked electricity and television service for an extended period of time.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stats_10-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-stats-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Electric companies restored only 20% of the island\'s power service within four weeks of Iniki, while other areas were without power for up to three months. Also affected by the storm was the agricultural sector.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CPHC_1992_1-10">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CPHC_1992-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Though much of the sugar cane was already harvested,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-stats_10-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-stats-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       what was left was severely damaged. The winds destroyed tender tropical plants like bananas and papayas and uprooted or damaged fruit and nut trees.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CPHC_1992_1-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CPHC_1992-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Most of Iniki\'s damage occurred in Kaua\n       <span class="unicode">\n        \xca\xbb\n       </span>\n       i. On the island, one person died when struck by debris, while another lost her life when a portion of her house fell on her. Offshore, two\n       <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">\n        Japanese\n       </a>\n       nationals died when their boat capsized. The reduced death toll was likely due to well-executed warnings and preparation. More than 100\xc2\xa0injuries can be attributed to Iniki, though most occurred in the aftermath of the hurricane.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-CPHC_1992_1-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-CPHC_1992-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Among those on Kaua\n       <span class="unicode">\n        \xca\xbb\n       </span>\n       i was filmmaker\n       <a href="/wiki/Steven_Spielberg" title="Steven Spielberg">\n        Steven Spielberg\n       </a>\n       , who was preparing for the final day of on-location shooting of the film\n       <i>\n        <a href="/wiki/Jurassic_Park_(film)" title="Jurassic Park (film)">\n         Jurassic Park\n        </a>\n       </i>\n       . He and the 130 of his cast and crew remained safely in a hotel during Iniki\'s passage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jurassic_11-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-jurassic-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-spielberg_12-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-spielberg-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       According to Spielberg, "every single structure was in shambles; roofs and walls were torn away; telephone poles and trees were down as far as the eye could see." Spielberg included footage of Iniki battering the Kaua\'i coastal walls as part of the completed film, where a tropical storm makes up a pivotal part of the plot. Members of the film\'s crew helped to clear some of the debris off of nearby roads.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-jurassic_11-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-jurassic-11">\n ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Upon passing by O\n       <span class="unicode">\n        \xca\xbb\n       </span>\n       ahu, Iniki produced tides of the most damage, with waves and storm surge flooding the second floor of beachside apartments.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oahu_13-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-oahu-13">\n   </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Damage on the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hawaii_Island" title="Hawaii Island">\n        Island of Hawai\xe2\x80\x98i\n        Storm surge\n         </a>\n         from Iniki\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Immediately after the storm, many were relieved to have survived the worst of the Category\xc2\xa04 hurricane; their complacency turned to apprehensiveness due to lack of information, as every radio station was out and there was no news available for several days. Because Iniki knocked out electrical power for most of the island, communities held parties to necessarily consume perishable food from unpowered refrigerators and freezers. Though food markets allowed those affected to take what they needed, many Kaua\n       <span class="unicode">\n        \xca\xbb\n       </span>\n       i citizens insisted on paying. In addition, entertainers from all of Hawai\n       <span class="unicode">\n        \xca\xbb\n       </span>\n       i, including\n       <a href="/wiki/Graham_Nash" title="Graham Nash">\n        Graham Nash\n       </a>\n       (who owns a home on the north shore of Kaua\xca\xbbi) and the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Honolulu_Symphony" title="Honolulu Symphony">\n        Honolulu Symphony\n       </a>\n       , provided free concerts to the victims.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kauai_9-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-kauai-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The film Jurassic Park was being filmed at the time, the sets used for the deleted scene where Mr Arnold goes to the shed to turn the power on were destroyed, the cast and crew took shelter in a hotel. Looting occurred in the aftermath of Iniki, though it was very minor. A group of\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Corps_of_Engineers" title="United States Army Corps of Engineers">\n        Army Corps of Engineers\n       </a>\n       , who experienced the looting of Hurricane Andrew just weeks before, were surprised at the overall calmness and lack of violence on the island. Although electrical power was restored to most of the island approximately six weeks following the hurricane, students returned to Kaua\xca\xbbi public schools two weeks after the disaster. Kaua\n       <span class="unicode">\n        \xca\xbb\n       </span>\n       i citizens remained hopeful for monetary aid from the government or insurance companies, though after six months they felt annoyed with the lack of help.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-kauai_9-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-kauai-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The military effectively provided aid for their immediate needs, though, and help arrived before local officials requested aid.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-fema_25-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-fema-25">\n         [25]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       <a href="/wiki/Amateur_radio_emergency_communications" title="Amateur radio emergency communications">\n        Amateur radio\n       </a>\n       proved to be extremely helpful during the three weeks after the storm, with volunteers coming from the neighboring islands as well as from around the Pacific to assist in the recovery. There was support of local government communications in Lihue in the first week of recovery\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-oahu_13-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-oahu-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       as well as a hastily organized effort by local operators to assist with the\n       <a href="/wiki/American_Red_Cross" title="American Red Cross">\n        American Red Cross\n       </a>\n       and their efforts to provide shelters and disaster relief centers across Kaua\xca\xbbi.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-redcross_26-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-redcross-26">\n         [26]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In the months after the storm, many\n       <a href="/wiki/Insurance" title="Insurance">\n        insurance\n       </a>\n       companies left Hawai\n       <span class="unicode">\n        \xca\xbb\n       </span>\n       i. To combat this,\n       <a href="/wiki/Governor_of_Hawaii" title="Governor of Hawaii">\n        State Governor\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/John_D._Waihee_III" title="John D. Waihee III">\n        John D. Waihee III\n       </a>\n       enacted the Hurricane Relief Fund in 1993 to help unprotected Hawai\n       <span class="unicode">\n        \xca\xbb\n       </span>\n       i residents. The fund was never needed for another Hawai\n       <span class="unicode">\n        \xca\xbb\n       </span>\n       i hurricane, and it was stopped in 2000 when insurance companies returned to the island.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-hawmoney_27-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-hawmoney-27">\n         [27]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       It is thought that Hurricane Iniki blew apart many\n       <a href="/wiki/Chicken_coop" title="Chicken coop">\n        chicken coops\n       </a>\n       , some possibly used to house fighting chickens; this caused a dramatic increase in the numbers of wild\n       <a href="/wiki/Chicken" title="Chicken">\n        chickens\n       </a>\n       roaming Kaua\n       <span class="unicode">\n        \xca\xbb\n       </span>\n       i.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28">\n        <a href="#cite_note-28">\n         [28]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The name Iniki was retired due to this storm, and was replaced with Iolana within the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Lists_of_tropical_cyclone_names#Central_Pacific" title="Lists of tropical cyclone  </sup>\n       Less than three days after Iniki struck,\n       <a href="/wiki/1992_Pacific_hurricane_season#Hurricane_Orlene" title="1992 Pacific hurricane season">\n        Hurricane Orlene\n
