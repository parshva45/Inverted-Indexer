 [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In his home <span class="mw-headline" id="Release">\n        Release\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a     </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       Lifehouse released "\n       <a href="/wiki/Flight_(Lifehouse_song)" title="Flight (Lifehouse song)">\n        Flight\n       </a>\n       " class="reference" id="cite_ref-8">\n        <a href="#cite_note-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On November 20, the band began a contest through online music design agency\n       <a href="/wiki/Creative_Allies" title="Creative Allies">\n        Creative class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Along with the track listing for the standard edition of the album, the first radio single "\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_(Lifehouse_song)" title="Hurricane (Lifehouse song)">\n        Hurricane\n       </a>\n       " was released for digital download the same day.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       On April 7, the album was made available for pre-order in a variety of collector\'s packages, with the release date moved back one week to May 26.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wish_14-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Wish-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Simultaneously, the acoustic ballad "Wish" was made available for immediate download on iTunes.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wish_14-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Wish-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On May 13, the band acoustically debuted "Yesterday\'s Son" for Billboard Magazine.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Yesterday_1-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Yesterday-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The standard edition of the album was featured on\n       <a href="/wiki/Pandora_Radio" title="Pandora Radio">\n        Pandora\n       </a>\n       Premieres for full streaming on May 19.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15">\n        <a href="#cite_note-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Track_History">\n        Track History\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Out_of_the_Wasteland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Track History">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       The majority of the record was written in 2013, however several previously-unreleased songs were recorded and included in the final release. "Wish" was written in 2001 and intended to be the final track on the band\'s 2002 album\n       <a href="/wiki/Stanley_Climbfall" title="Stanley Climbfall">\n        Stanley Climbfall\n       </a>\n       , before being replaced with "The Beginning"; played on tour in 2011 and recorded with Jim Cox in April 2014, Wade stated he was glad the song "finally made its way".\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Wish_14-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Wish-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16">\n        <a href="#cite_note-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       "Stardust" was a 2013 song written and performed by bassist\n       <a href="/wiki/Bryce_Soderberg" title="Bryce Soderberg">\n        Bryce Soderberg\n       </a>\n       with his side project KOMOX before being rerecorded as a Lifehouse song. "Central Park" was written in 2007 and originally tracked just prior to the March 2010 release of\n       <a href="/wiki/Smoke_%26_Mirrors_(Lifehouse_album)" title="Smoke &amp; Mirrors (Lifehouse album)">\n        Smoke &amp; Mirrors\n       </a>\n       , while "Angeline" was written prior to the\n       <a href="/wiki/Almer%C3%ADa" title="Almer\xc3\xada">\n        Almer\xc3\xada\n       </a>\n       sessions.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-About_Lifehouse_2-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-About_Lifehouse-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Angeline_17-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Angeline-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       "Wish", "Central Park", "Clarity", and "Angeline" were originally slated for Wade\'s shelved solo album,\n       <i>\n        Paper Cuts\n       </i>\n       , initially set for a 2013 release.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18">\n        <a href="#cite_note-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       "Hourglass" began in the early 1990s as an instrumental piece by producer\n       <a href="/wiki/Jude_Cole" title="Jude Cole">\n        Jude Cole\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Film_score_composer" title="Film score composer">\n        film score composer\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/James_Newton_Howard" title="James Newton Howard">\n        James Newton Howard\n       </a>\n       , before resurfacing during the\n       <i>\n        Out of the Wasteland\n       </i>\n       sessions; Cole suggested Wade write the lyrics and finish the song with Howard.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-19">\n        <a href="#cite_note-19">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Promotion">\n        Promotion\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Out_of_the_Wasteland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Promotion">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Lifehouse announced that they would be opening for\n       <a href="/wiki/Nickelback" title="Nickelback">\n        Nickelback\n       </a>\n       during the second leg of their\n       <a href="/wiki/No_Fixed_Address_Tour" title="No Fixed Address Tour">\n        No Fixed Address Tour\n       </a>\n       , from June 19 to August 29, 2015.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Yesterday_1-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Yesterday-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       On June 23, they announced that the tour had been cancelled due to the health of Nickelback lead singer\n       <a href="/wiki/Chad_Kroeger" title="Chad Kroeger">\n        Chad Kroeger\n       </a>\n       , and promised a European tour in the fall. The band began a headlining tour in Europe on September 15 with supporting act\n       <a href="/wiki/Raglans" title="Raglans">\n        Raglans\n       </a>\n       , stopped in\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Pasay_City,_Philippines" title="Pasay City, Philippines">\n        Pasay City, Philippines\n       </a>\n       on October 8, and continued in Australia and New Zealand from October 11 to October 19, 2015. With the departure of former touring guitarist and band member Ben Carey, Steve Stout became Lifehouse\' fourth touring member to date.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       The band debuted eight new tracks, including "Hurricane", "One for the Pain", "Flight", and "Runaways" as a full band, and "Firing Squad", "Alien", "Yesterday\'s Son", and "H2O" acoustically.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-20">\n        <a href="#cite_note-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       "Angeline", "Wish" and "Stardust" had debuted live in 2011, 2012, and early 2015, respectively.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Angeline_17-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Angeline-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-21">\n        <a href="#cite_note-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22">\n        <a href="#cite_note-22">\n         [22]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       During his solo acoustic set, Wade notably debuted several rare, never-performed\n       <a href="/wiki/Lifehouse_(band)#1996.E2.80.931999:_Blyss_and_formation_of_Lifehouse" title="Lifehouse (band)">\n        Blyss\n       </a>\n       demoes from the late 1990s, including "Joshua" and
