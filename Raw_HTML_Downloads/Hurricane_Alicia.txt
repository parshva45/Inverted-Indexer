hurricanes">\n        costliest\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone" title="Tropical cyclone">\n        tropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       in the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Atlantic" title="Atlantic">\n        Atlantic\n       </a>\n       since\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Agnes" title="Hurricane Agnes">\n        Hurricane Agnes\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/1972_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1972 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1972\n       </a>\n       . Alicia was the third depression, the first tropical storm, and the only major\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane" title="Hurricane">\n        hurricane\n       </a>\n       of the\n       <a href="/wiki/1983_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1983 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1983 Atlantic hurricane season\n       </a>\n       . It struck\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Galveston" title="Galveston">\n        Galveston\n       </a>\n       and\n       <a href="/wiki/Houston" title="Houston">\n        Houston\n       </a>\n       ,\n       <a href="/wiki/Texas" title="Texas">\n        Texas\n       </a>\n       directly, causing $1.7\xc2\xa0billion (1983\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">\n        USD\n       </a>\n       )\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gov_1-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Gov-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       in damage and killing 21 people; this made it the worst Texas hurricane since\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Carla" title="Hurricane Carla">\n        Hurricane Carla\n       </a>\n       in\n       <a href="/wiki/1961_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1961 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1961\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-prelim_2-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-prelim-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       In addition, Alicia was the first billion-dollar tropical cyclone in Texas history.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TexGov_3-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TexGov-3">\n         [3]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Hurricane Alicia was the first hurricane to hit the\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">\n        United States\n       </a>\n       mainland since\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Allen" title="Hurricane Allen">\n        Hurricane Allen\n       </a>\n       in August 1980. The time between the two storms totaled three years and eight days (1,103\xc2\xa0days).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-list_4-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-list-4">\n         [4]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Hurricane Alicia became the last major hurricane (Category\xc2\xa03 or higher) to strike Texas until the stronger\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_Bret_(1999)" title="Hurricane Bret (1999)">\n        Hurricane Bret in 1999\n       </a>\n       made landfall. Alicia was the first storm for which the\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Hurricane_Center" title="National Hurricane Center">\n        National Hurricane Center\n       </a>\n       issued landfall probabilities.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AliciaPg4_5-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-AliciaPg4-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Hurricane Alicia was notable for the delayed post storm evacuation of Galveston Island (since the eye of the storm traveled the evacuation route up\n       <a href="/wiki/Interstate_45" title="Interstate 45">\n        Interstate 45\n       </a>\n       from Galveston to Houston). The hurricane was also notable for the shattering of many windows in downtown Houston by loose gravel from the roofs of new skyscrapers and by other debris, prompting changes to rooftop construction developed\n       </a>\n       into Tropical Depression Three about 350\xc2\xa0miles (560\xc2\xa0km) south-southwest of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Mississippi_River_Delta" title="Mississippi River Delta">\n        Mississippi River Delta\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_8-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A few hours later, the Hurricane Hunters confirmed its development.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-9">\n        <a href="#cite_note-9">\n         [9]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Such development along the tail end of a cold front is more typical earlier or later in the hurricane season.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-METEOROLOGY_10-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-METEOROLOGY-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       After becoming a tropical cyclone, the depression was moving slowly westward, due to a\n       <a href="/wiki/Ridge_(meteorology)" title="Ridge (meteorology)">\n        ridge\n       </a>\n       to its north.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-prelim_2-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-prelim-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       A Hurricane Hunters flight late on August\xc2\xa015 reported that the depression reached winds of 50\xc2\xa0mph (80\xc2\xa0km/h); as a result, the NHC upgraded the cyclone to Tropical Storm Alicia. At the time of its upgrade, the storm was located in an area of higher than normal\n       <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_pressure" title="Atmospheric pressure">\n        atmospheric pressure\n       </a>\n       , although conditions favored further development.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11">\n        <a href="#cite_note-11">\n         [11]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Due to high pressures surrounding the storm, Alicia was a smaller than normal tropical cyclone; as a result, it produced stronger than normal winds, in comparison to its minimum central pressure.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-prelim_2-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-prelim-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The storm continued slowly to the west-northwest, and by August\xc2\xa017 attained hurricane status, about 160\xc2\xa0miles (255\xc2\xa0km) southeast of Galveston, Texas.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_8-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Shortly thereafter, an\n       <a href="/wiki/Eye_(cyclone)" title="Eye (cyclone)">\n        eye\n       </a>\n       became visible on radar,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12">\n        <a href="#cite_note-12">\n         [12]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       as the hurricane executed several small loops.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-METEOROLOGY_10-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-METEOROLOGY-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Its slow movement over warm waters, in addition to an\n       <a href="/wiki/Anticyclone" title="Anticyclone">\n        anticyclone\n       </a>\n       becoming established over the hurricane, caused Alicia to undergo\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rapid_deepening" title="Rapid deepening">\n        rapid deepening\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-prelim_2-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-prelim-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:177px;">\n        <a class="image"     </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       At 0600\xc2\xa0UTC on August\xc2\xa018, the winds reached 115\xc2\xa0mph (185\xc2\xa0km/h), just before Alicia made\n       <a class="mw-redirect" class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_8-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Upon moving ashore, the\n       <a href="/wiki/Gale" title="Gale">\n        gale\n       </a>\n       -force winds extended 125\xc2\xa0mi (201\xc2\xa0km) from the center,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13">\n        <a href="#cite_note-13">\n         [13]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and hurricane-force winds spread across an area from\n       <a href="/wiki/Freeport,_Texas" title="Freeport, Texas">\n        Freeport\n       </a>\n       to 60\xc2\xa0mi (97\xc2\xa0km) northeast.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-prelim2_14-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-prelim2-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Its\n       <a href="/wiki/Atmospheric_pressure" title="Atmospheric pressure">\n        atmospheric pressure\n       </a>\n       was 962\n       <a href="/wiki/Bar_(unit)" title="Bar (unit)">\n        mbar\n       </a>\n       (\n       <a href="/wiki/Pascal_(unit)" title="Pascal (unit)">\n        hPa\n       </a>\n       ; 28.41\n       <a href="/wiki/Inch_of_mercury" [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       and radar imagery indicated the presence of a rare double-eye structure.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-METEOROLOGY_10-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-METEOROLOGY-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Alicia quickly weakened, passing over central Houston with sustained winds of 80\xc2\xa0mph (130\xc2\xa0km/h). It accelerated toward the northwest, weakening to tropical storm status late on August\xc2\xa018 and to tropical depression status twelve hours later.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_8-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Tropical Depression Alicia moved into Oklahoma and interacted with an approaching\n       <a href="/wiki/Trough_(meteorology)" title="Trough (meteorology)">\n        frontal trough\n       </a>\n       . By 0600\xc2\xa0UTC on August\xc2\xa020, Alicia had transitioned into an\n       <a href="/wiki/Extratropical_cyclone" title="Extratropical cyclone">\n        extratropical cyclone\n       </a>\n       over northwestern Oklahoma, and by the next day it was no longer identifiable after merging with the trough over eastern Nebraska.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-prelim_2-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-prelim-2">\n         [2]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-HURDAT_8-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-HURDAT-8">\n         [8]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Preparations">\n        Preparations\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Alicia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Preparations">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">\n       See also:\n       <a href="/wiki/Tropical_cyclone_warnings_and_watches" title="Tropical cyclone warnings and watches">\n        Tropical cyclone warnings and watches\n       </a>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Several watches and warnings were issued in association with Alicia. The first ones were a gale warning and a\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hurricane_watch" title="Hurricane watch">\n        hurricane watch\n       </a>\n       for the area between\n       <a href="/wiki/Corpus_Christi,_Texas" title="Corpus Christi, Texas">\n        Corpus Christi, Texas\n       </a>\n       , and\n       <a href="/wiki/Grand_Isle,_Louisiana" title="Grand Isle, Louisiana">\n        Grand Isle, Louisiana\n       </a>\n       , issued on August 16. On August 17, a hurricane warning was issued for the coastline from Corpus Christi to\n       <a href="/wiki/Morgan_City,_Louisiana" title="Morgan City, Louisiana">\n        Morgan City, Louisiana\n       </a>\n       , and later for\n       <a href="/wiki/Port_Arthur,_Texas" title="Port Arthur, Texas">\n        Port Arthur, Texas\n       </a>\n       , southward.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-AliciaPg4_5-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-AliciaPg4-5">\n         [5]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Initially, however, residents did not take the warnings seriously. Galveston Mayor E. Gus Manuel, against the advice of Texas Governor\n       <a href="/wiki/Mark_White" title="Mark White">\n        Mark White\n       </a>\n       , ordered the evacuation of only low-lying areas.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TIME_15-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TIME-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       As a result, only 10 percent of the population living behind the seawall chose to leave when Alicia came ashore. In contrast, about 30 percent of Galveston\'s population evacuated the island when\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Allen" title="Hurricane Allen">\n        Hurricane Allen\n       </a>\n       threatened the eastern Texas coastline in 1980.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USA_Today_16-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-USA_Today-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Throughout the day, however, as the increasing winds began to cause damage in Galveston, people grew more concerned. The mayor finally ordered a widespread evacuation of the island after midnight on August 18, but by then, the bridges to the mainland were uncrossable.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TIME_15-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-TIME-15">\n         [15]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">\n        Impact\n </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <div class="thumb tright">\n       <div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;">\n        <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Alicia_1983_rainfall.gif">\n         <img alt="" class="thumbimage" data-file-height="872" data-file-width="668" height="287"      </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       Galveston reported\n       <span class="frac nowrap">\n        7\n        <span class="visualhide">\n        </span>\n        <sup>\n         3\n        </sup>\n        \xe2\x81\x84\n        <sub>\n         4\n        </sub>\n       </span>\n       <a href="/wiki/Inch" title="Inch">\n        in\n       </a>\n       (197\n       <a 10\xc2\xa0in (250\xc2\xa0mm). Centerville reported over 8 inches (200\xc2\xa0mm), with Normangee and Mexia reporting over 7\xc2\xa0in (180\xc2\xa0mm).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-prelim5_17-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-prelim5-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Maximum rainfall in the Houston area in Harris County was about 10\xe2\x80\x9311\xc2\xa0in (250\xe2\x80\x93280\xc2\xa0mm), while 8\xc2\xa0in (200\xc2\xa0mm) of rain was reported in\n       <a href="/wiki/Leon_County,_Texas" title="Leon County, Texas">\n        Leon County\n       </a>\n       and 9\xc2\xa0in (230\xc2\xa0mm) in the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Sabine_River_(Texas-Louisiana)" title="Sabine River (Texas-Louisiana)">\n        Sabine River\n       </a>\n       area. High gusts were reported throughout Texas, with a maximum gust of 125\xc2\xa0mph (201\xc2\xa0km/h) reported on the\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard" title="United States Coast Guard">\n        Coast Guard\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Coast_Guard_Cutter" title="United States Coast Guard Cutter">\n        cutter\n       </a>\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_United_States_Coast_Guard_cutters" title="List of United States Coast Guard cutters">\n        <i>\n         Buttonwood\n        </i>\n        (WLB-306)\n       </a>\n       stationed at the northeastern tip of Galveston Island.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-METEOROLOGY_10-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-METEOROLOGY-10">\n         [10]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Pleasure Pier reported tides of 8.67\xc2\xa0ft (2.64\xc2\xa0m), with Pier 21 reporting a little over 5.5\xc2\xa0ft (1.7\xc2\xa0m).\n       <a href="/wiki/Baytown,_Texas" title="Baytown, Texas">\n        Baytown, Texas\n       </a>\n       , reported 10\xe2\x80\x9312\xc2\xa0ft (3.0\xe2\x80\x933.7\xc2\xa0m) tides, and Morgan City reported 12.1\xc2\xa0ft (3.7\xc2\xa0m), the highest recorded as a result of Alicia.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-prelim5_17-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-prelim5-17">\n         [17]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The storm also caused extensive disruption of power services. A\n       <a href="/wiki/Paul_Simon" title="Paul Simon">\n        Paul Simon\n       </a>\n       -\n       <a href="/wiki/Art_Garfunkel" title="Art Garfunkel">\n        Art Garfunkel\n       </a>\n       reunion concert scheduled for the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Houston_Astrodome" title="Houston Astrodome">\n        Houston Astrodome\n       </a>\n       was canceled due to the coming storm.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Twenty-three tornadoes were reported in association with Alicia. Fourteen of those were located in the Galveston and\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Hobby_Airport" title="Hobby Airport">\n        Hobby Airport\n       </a>\n       area. The other nine were concentrated around Tyler to Houston, Texas,\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-prelim2_14-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-prelim2-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       ranging around F2 on the\n       <a href="/wiki/Fujita_scale" title="Fujita scale">\n        Fujita scale\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USA_Today_16-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-USA_Today-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       A major oil spill occurred around\n       <a href="/wiki/Texas_City,_Texas" title="Texas City, Texas">\n        Texas City\n       </a>\n       , and an ocean-going tugboat capsized 50 miles (80\xc2\xa0km) off the Sabine Pass coast.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LateTexCanes_18-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-LateTexCanes-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The Coast Guard Air Station Houston (AIRSTA) weathered Alicia with little damage, and afterwards AIRSTA\'s helicopters assisted residents with evacuation, supply, and survey flights.\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       Sixty gallons of water had to be removed from the\n       <a href="/wiki/National_Weather_Service" title="National Weather Service">\n        National Weather Service\n       </a>\n       (NWS) office in Galveston;\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LateTexCanes_18-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-LateTexCanes-18">\n         [18]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       this weather office also temporarily lost its radar.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Army_19-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Army-19">\n         [19]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Houston suffered billions of dollars in damage. Thousands of glass panes in downtown skyscrapers were shattered by gravel blown off rooftops.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-USA_Today_16-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-USA_Today-16">\n         [16]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Although Alicia was a small Category 3 hurricane, a total of 2,297 dwellings were destroyed by Alicia, with another 3,000+ experiencing major damage. Over ten thousand dwellings had minor damage.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-prelim2_14-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-prelim2-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       In Galveston, the western beach had its public beach boundary shifted back about 150\xc2\xa0ft (46\xc2\xa0m).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Texas_Hurricanes_of_the_80.27s_20-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Texas_Hurricanes_of_the_80.27s-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       About 5 feet (1.5\xc2\xa0m) of sand was scoured, leaving beachfront homes in a natural vegetation state. This moved many beachfront homes onto public beach, and the Attorney General\'s office declared that they were in violation of the\n       <a href="/wiki/Texas_Open_Beaches_Act" title="Texas Open Beaches Act">\n        Texas Open Beaches Act\n       </a>\n       and forbade the repair or rebuilding of those homes.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Texas_Hurricanes_of_the_80.27s_20-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Texas_Hurricanes_of_the_80.27s-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       The\n       <a href="/wiki/United_States_Army_Corps_of_Engineers" title="United States Army Corps of Engineers">\n        Corps of Engineers\n       </a>\n       stated that if the Galveston Sea Wall had not been there, that another $100\xc2\xa0million (1983 USD; $240\xc2\xa0million 2017\xc2\xa0USD) in damage could have occurred.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Texas_Hurricanes_of_the_80.27s_20-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Texas_Hurricanes_of_the_80.27s-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Also, if Alicia had been the size of\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Carla" title="Hurricane Carla">\n        Hurricane Carla\n       </a>\n       from\n       <a href="/wiki/1961_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1961 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1961\n       </a>\n       , damage could have easily doubled or possibly tripled.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Texas_Hurricanes_of_the_80.27s_20-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Texas_Hurricanes_of_the_80.27s-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       Alicia damaged chemical and petrochemical plants in Houston.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Plants_21-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Plants-21">\n         [21]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h3>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Elsewhere">\n        Elsewhere\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Alicia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Elsewhere">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h3>\n      <p>\n       As Alicia progressed northward, it produced heavy precipitation in several other areas.  helicopter\n        </div>\n       </div>\n      </div>\n      <p>\n       The Red Cross provided food and shelter to 63,000 people in the hurricane\'s wake, costing about $166\xc2\xa0million (1983\xc2\xa0USD; $399\xc2\xa0million 2017\xc2\xa0USD).\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-prelim2_14-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-prelim2-14">\n         [14]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Texas_Hurricanes_of_the_80.27s_20-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Texas_Hurricanes_of_the_80.27s-20">\n         [20]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       FEMA gave out $32\xc2\xa0million (1983\xc2\xa0USD; $76.9\xc2\xa0million 2017\xc2\xa0USD) to Alicia\'s victims and local governments; $23\xc2\xa0million (1983 USD; $55.3\xc2\xa0million 2017\xc2\xa0USD) of that was for picking up debris spread after the storm.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gov_1-1">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Gov-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       More than 16,000 people sought help from FEMA\'s disaster service centers. The\n       <a href="/wiki/Small_Business_Administration" title="Small Business Administration">\n        Small Business Administration\n       </a>\n       , aided with 56 volunteers, interviewed over 16,000 victims, and it was predicted that about 7,000 loan applications would be submitted. The Federal Insurance Agency had closed over 1,318\n       <a href="/wiki/Flood_insurance" title="Flood insurance">\n        flood insurance\n       </a>\n       cases from Alicia\'s aftermath, however only 782 received final payment.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gov_1-2">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Gov-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <p>\n       On September 23 and September 24, 1983, in the wake of Alicia, two subcommittees of the\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/U.S._House_of_Representatives" title="U.S. House of Representatives">\n        U.S. House of Representatives\n       </a>\n       held hearings in Houston. The hearing on September 23 were to examine the primary issues of the NWS during Alicia, the effectiveness of the NWS in current procedures, and the use of the NWS. The second hearing, which occurred on September 24, was to discuss the damage and recovery efforts during Alicia.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gov_1-3">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Gov-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       During the September 23 hearing, witnesses agreed that the NWS did well before and during the emergency caused by Alicia. NWS forecasters also testified in which they said they gratified themselves that their predictions were well "on target" and that the local emergency plans had worked so well, which saved many lives. Mayor Gus Manuel on Galveston claimed that the NWS did an excellent job during Alicia. He was also very impressed about their landfall predictions on August 17.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gov_1-4">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Gov-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       During the September 24 hearing, evidence was presented which demonstrated the need for improving readiness to cope with disasters, such as Alicia. Mayor Manuel mentioned that his town needed stronger building codes, which were under review.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Gov_1-5">\n        <a href="#cite_note-Gov-1">\n         [1]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="Retirement">\n        Retirement\n       </span>\n       <span class="mw-editsection">\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         [\n        </span>\n        <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hurricane_Alicia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Retirement">\n         edit\n        </a>\n        <span class="mw-editsection-bracket">\n         ]\n        </span>\n       </span>\n      </h2>\n      <p>\n       Due to the severe damage, the name "Alicia" was\n       <a href="/wiki/List_of_retired_Atlantic_hurricane_names" title="List of retired Atlantic hurricane names">\n        retired\n       </a>\n       in the spring of 1984 by the\n       <a href="/wiki/World_Meteorological_Organization" title="World Meteorological Organization">\n        World Meteorological Organization\n       </a>\n       , and will never be used again for an\n       <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_tropical_cyclone" title="North Atlantic tropical cyclone">\n        Atlantic hurricane\n       </a>\n       . It was the first name to be retired since\n       <a href="/wiki/Hurricane_Allen" title="Hurricane Allen">\n        Hurricane Allen\n       </a>\n       in 1980.\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-retire_23-0">\n        <a href="#cite_note-retire-23">\n         [23]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n       It was replaced with "Allison" for the\n       <a href="/wiki/1989_Atlantic_hurricane_season" title="1989 Atlantic hurricane season">\n        1989 season\n       </a>\n       .\n       <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24">\n        <a href="#cite_note-24">\n         [24]\n        </a>\n       </sup>\n      </p>\n      <h2>\n       <span class="mw-headline" id="In_popular_culture">\n        In popular culture\n       </span>\n
