2013 atlantis 2016 music video hurricane on youtube hurricane is a song by american recording artist bridgit mendler from her debut studio album hello my name is 2012 the song was released as a promotional single to the itunes store as single of the week on october 22 2012 and was later announced to be the second single from the album 3 on february 12 2013 the song was released to top 40 radio 4 the song received positive reviews from music critics praising the reggae influence and vocals and rapping skills which have been compared to cher lloyd lily allen and karmin it debuted and problems playing this file see media help a midtempo pop song hurricane exhibits elements of reggae fusion and r b built on a beat and multi tracked harmonies the instrumentation includes slow bouncing keyboard tones and drums the song was written by mendler and american songwriters emanuel kiriakou evan kidd bogart and andrew goldstein and produced by kiriakou and goldstein lyrically the song gives ode to a disaster love and reconciliation and perseverance in love vocals span from the low note of f 3 to the high note of f 5 5 in interview for coup de main magazine mendler commented about recording a rap song for the first time she said i loved recording you know the rap in hurricane that was pretty fun it was very experimental for me i done much rapping before it was not a deliberate decision we had written the song and i had come in to record it and then the guys were like why we just try rapping this part so i did and then it kind of just worked and we decided to keep it so not intentional but sometimes things just happen in the moment 6 critical reception edit hurricane has been compared to the works of cher lloyd and lily allen the song has received positive critical acclaim from music critics tim sendra of allmusic said hurricane has clever fairy tale lyrics and an impassioned vocal and a sassy rap section 7 girls life magazine stated mendler brings the beat in hurricane where she belts out some sweet raps about being in the middle of a stormy relationship 8 kai of embrace you magazine claimed that the track title was quite ironic when currently experiencing one of mother conception of the said thing and later said that interestingly enough i love this song the acoustic opener caught me instantly and addicted to her oh oh the play on natural disasters as metaphors for the way she feels about her love interest once again showcases how witty she is as an artist the fast paced pop rock driven track is both catchy and substantial 9 music
